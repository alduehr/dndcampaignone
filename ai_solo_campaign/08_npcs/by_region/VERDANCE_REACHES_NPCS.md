## AI Use

NPC roster for **the Verdance Reaches** — the river-road corridor up the Verdance, SE of Caradril, the player's **first step into the wider continent** (Ring 2; Lvl 8–12). This is a placeholder region: settlements here are **light NPC-facing anchors** (river-towns, lord's halls, barge-wharves), not full gazetteers. The Verdance Reaches are *transitional* — frontier roughness giving way to the older, richer, more settled Orrun. People here are river-traders, petty lords, toll-keepers, bargefolk, and the first agents of the inland Ledger and Remnant.

**Player-Facing fields are surfaceable; Secret/DM-Only fields are earned through play.** Reuse the three established figures from `FAR_CONTINENT_NPCS.md` (Crane, Hadwin Vael, Mossa Drenn) — given full profiles here. No NPC knows the apex truth (the harvest / Hollow Court). Death-rite failures are **just beginning to be noticed here** — fresher, less denied than in the Reach, because the Verdance Reaches sit closer to the spreading edge of the crisis but have not yet been hit hard.

**Cultural voice:** river-trade practicality over frontier fatalism. People here say "up-river" (richer, civilized) and "down-river" (the cold frontier, faintly pitied). Talk of money, tolls, water-levels, and which lord holds which stretch. Less death-haunted than the Reach; more worldly.

> Light anchors used below: **Marrowfen Stair** (a stepped river-town of locks and wharves, the corridor's hub), **Lord's Wend** (a petty lord's hall-town), **Cresswater** (a barge-village), **the Nine Locks** (the great staircase of locks that gives the corridor its name).

## MAJOR NPCs

### VR-M1. Factor-General Wessel Crane — the Ledger's inland reach
- **Region/Base:** Verdance Reaches; seat at Marrowfen Stair, circuits the whole corridor and down to Caradril.
- **Public Role:** Cinder Ledger regional head for the Verdance corridor — Mother Calla Vyre's *superior* in the inland trade, the Ledger's authority **above** Caradril.
- **Faction:** Cinder Ledger (senior; inland directorate).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The single most powerful Ledger officer the player can reach short of the Glassmere heartland; he sets the directives that come *down* to Vyre and Caradril. To meet him is to glimpse how large the Ledger really is.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A spare, grey, immaculately patient man in a plain coat worth more than a barge, who listens to your whole pitch without a flicker and then prices it exactly.
- **Voice:** Quiet, unhurried, surgical; speaks in clean ledgered sentences. "Down-river thinks small. Tell me what you actually want." Never raises his voice; the threat is in the calm.
- **Private Motivation:** Consolidate the entire Verdance corridor's relic-and-salt trade under the Ledger before the Glassmere League's banks can move in from up-river. He is fighting a *two-front* trade war — the frontier below him, the League above.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the Glassmere bankers (Sefwy Holt) will absorb the Ledger from above before he can secure the corridor; that the down-river "rite trouble" he's hearing about will spook the relic-market he's built his career on.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Reliable, deniable agents who can do in the corridor what salaried clerks can't — settle a stubborn lord, recover a lost cargo, sniff out who's leaking Ledger business to the League.
- **What he can offer the player:** Ledger backing of a scale Vyre can't match — letters of credit good up-river, introductions into the Glassmere League, deep relic-market access, real money.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Lean on a petty lord blocking the locks; intercept a League agent; quietly assess whether the "down-river failures" are a market risk worth pulling out over.
- **What he knows about his region:** Every toll, lord, and cargo on the Verdance; the Ledger's true inland scale; the League's encroachment; that Vyre's Caradril operation is a frontier outpost he tolerates.
- **What he misunderstands:** He reads the death-rite failures purely as a *market signal* — a thing that moves relic prices — never as a real spreading catastrophe. He is too far up-river to feel it yet.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Cold market calculation: are relics about to get scarcer (buy now) or worthless (sell now)? He'd send an agent to *assess*, not to help.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Pure asset-evaluation. Ruins are inventory. He funds Remnant access only where it improves Ledger salvage.
- **Relationship Links:** Superior to **Mother Calla Vyre** and **Provost Anneth Vell** (Caradril); trade-rival of **Banker-Magistra Sefwy Holt** (Glassmere); employs corridor factors incl. **Toll-Reeve Halsa Wend** (below); coolly aware of **Sub-Lector Hadwin Vael's** Remnant work and funds it selectively.
- **Player-safe description:** A senior inland merchant-banker of immense reach and immaculate calm.
- **DM-Only:** *(secret)* Crane has begun quietly **divesting** the Ledger's deepest down-river relic positions — he smells something wrong in the frontier "failures" and is protecting the Ledger's balance sheet, which means he may *abandon* the Reach to its fate to save the inland trade. He does **not** know the harvest exists; he's a banker reading a bad omen as a bad market. A player who grasps this can either warn the frontier or profit from his retreat.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — consolidates the corridor, the Ledger's inland grip tightens, Vyre gains a harder master. *Helped* — becomes a powerful (mercenary) patron with continental reach. *Betrayed/exposed* — a relentless, patient enemy who can freeze the player's credit across the whole river.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble/Spymaster-like with resources** (AC 13, ~40 HP; commands factors, guards, and credit, not a war-band). Solo danger: low in person, very high economically. Noncombat: out-bargain, out-inform, or align with the League against him.
- **Tags:** [faction:cinder-ledger, role:authority, role:trade-contact, far-continent, major]

### VR-M2. Lord Ennis Marrow — petty lord of the Nine Locks
- **Region/Base:** Lord's Wend, holding the Nine Locks stretch of the Verdance.
- **Public Role:** The minor lord whose family has held the great lock-staircase for six generations and taxes every barge that climbs it.
- **Faction:** Independent (petty nobility); Ledger-indebted, League-courted.
- **Political/Social Importance:** Controls the chokepoint of the entire corridor — no cargo reaches up-river except past his locks. Small lord, large lever.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A florid, anxious nobleman in slightly-too-grand clothes, who talks loudly of his ancient family while a clerk whispers debt figures in his ear.
- **Voice:** Blustering, status-anxious, name-dropping ancestors; turns wheedling the moment money or threat appears. "Do you know how long the Marrows have held these locks?"
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the locks his family's, keep up appearances, and not be quietly bought out by either the Ledger below or the League above — both of whom hold his debts.
- **Fear/Pressure:** Insolvency; that the Ledger (Crane) or the League (Holt) will call in his debts and take the locks; that the locks themselves — old Concord works — are "going wrong."
- **What he wants from outsiders:** A champion, a loan, a lever — anyone who can break his creditors' grip without taking the locks for themselves.
- **What he can offer the player:** Free passage up-river, a noble patron and roof, his family's old records (some pre-date the corridor's settlement), the political weight of a border-lord.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Recover a debt owed *to* him; embarrass a creditor; investigate the "wrongness" in the lock-works before it ruins a barge season.
- **What he knows about his region:** The corridor's noble politics; who owes whom; the locks' old engineering; the up-river / down-river trade rivalry from a lord's-eye view.
- **What he misunderstands:** He thinks the lock-works' "wrongness" is mere decay and ill-luck; he refuses to connect it to the down-river rite-failures he dismisses as frontier superstition.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Embarrassed dismissal — "down-river nonsense" — until it touches *his* locks, then panic.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Proprietary pride (the locks are Concord-built and he profits from them) mixed with growing dread that the old works are stirring.
- **Relationship Links:** Indebted to **Wessel Crane** (Ledger) and courted by **Sefwy Holt** (Glassmere); employs **Lockmaster Dob Cress** (below); rival of neighboring **Lord's Wend** gentry; his old records interest **Hadwin Vael** (Remnant).
- **Player-safe description:** An over-proud, under-funded river-lord clinging to an ancestral chokepoint.
- **DM-Only:** *(complication)* The Nine Locks are a **minor Concord water-node** — the "wrongness" Marrow dismisses is the same waking the player saw at frontier nodes, now reaching up-river. He sits on an early M2/M5-style corroboration **further inland than the player has yet seen**, proving the crisis is spreading — without knowing what it is.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — bought out; the Ledger or League takes the locks. *Helped* — a grateful (if unreliable) noble ally and a free door up-river. *Betrayed* — a sputtering, vindictive minor enemy who can close the corridor to the player.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble-like** (AC 15 w/ household guard, ~22 HP; commands 6–10 **Guard-like** retainers). Solo danger: low personally, moderate via his guard. Noncombat: handle his debt politics, expose or befriend his creditors.
- **Tags:** [role:authority, role:noble, far-continent, major]

### VR-M3. River-Captain Mossa Drenn — mistress of the great barges
- **Region/Base:** The Verdance, Caradril to the Glassmere League; home-wharf at Marrowfen Stair.
- **Public Role:** Owner-captain of the largest independent barge-train on the Verdance; the woman who actually moves the corridor's cargo.
- **Faction:** Independent (river-trade); works *for* whoever pays — Ledger, lords, League.
- **Political/Social Importance:** The player's most reliable way **further inland** — and a clear-eyed, unaligned read on the whole corridor that no faction officer will give them.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A weathered, river-tanned woman with a steersman's squint and a deckhand's bark, who sizes up cargo and people with the same fast eye.
- **Voice:** Brisk, profane, river-shrewd; measures everything in water and weather. "Up-river's two weeks if the locks behave and the lord's paid. Down-river I don't run no more — bad water."
- **Private Motivation:** Keep her barge-train independent and unowned as the Ledger and League both try to put trade-captains on a leash. Stay free, stay paid.
- **Fear/Pressure:** Being forced into one faction's livery; the "bad water" down-river spreading up the Verdance and ruining her routes.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Paying passengers and cargo, an extra blade or two against river-pirates, and word of which stretches have gone "bad" before she runs them.
- **What she can offer the player:** Fast, unaligned passage up-river to the Glassmere League (or back down to Caradril); river-intelligence; introductions among bargefolk all along the Verdance.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Guard a valuable cargo through a pirate stretch; carry a private message up-river the factions mustn't see; help her dodge a Ledger contract that's really a leash.
- **What she knows about her region:** Every wharf, pirate-stretch, lord, and toll on the Verdance; which factions are squeezing the bargefolk; *exactly* how far up-river the "bad water" has crept.
- **What she misunderstands:** She calls the spreading rite-failures "bad water" — a sailor's omen — and routes around it without theorizing. She's a precise witness who doesn't speculate.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Pragmatic avoidance — she stops running "bad-water" stretches and warns other captains. A clean, unaligned M5-style witness to the *spread*.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Indifferent unless it's in the water; the old causeways and drowned locks are just hazards on her charts.
- **Relationship Links:** Hires to (and resists) **Wessel Crane**; ferries **Hadwin Vael** up-river on his Remnant rounds; friendly rival of Caradril's **Skipper Wenna Roke** (Pale Coast); knows **Mossa's bargefolk network** down to **Bargemaster Tibb** at Kettle Bridge.
- **Player-safe description:** A tough, independent river-captain who'll take your coin and tell you the truth about the water.
- **DM-Only:** *(discoverable)* Drenn keeps a private chart marking every stretch where the rites have failed and the dead have "come up wrong" along the Verdance — an inland map of the crisis's spread, drawn from a working sailor's caution, not understanding. The cleanest single artifact proving the crisis is **moving up-river**.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — stays independent and useful but distant. *Helped* — a trusted, recurring cross-continent ferry and ally. *Betrayed* — refuses the player passage and warns every wharf against them.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Veteran/Scout-like** (AC 14, ~38 HP; crew of 8–12 **Guard/Bandit-like** bargehands she can rally). Solo danger: moderate (her deck, her crew); low alone. Noncombat: pay her, earn her, guard her cargo.
- **Tags:** [role:trade-contact, role:travel-guide, far-continent, major]

### VR-M4. Sub-Lector Hadwin Vael — the Remnant's inland chapter
> Name note: a common "Vael" — **not kin** to Archivist Quorrin Vane, Imris Vane-Tolm, Sub-Lector Oneth Vael (Saint Veddow's), or Hollin Vane (Heartlands). "Vael"/"Vane" are common scholarly surnames; keep full names.
- **Region/Base:** Verdance Reaches; circuits the corridor's ruins, corresponds with Caradril and the Glassmere.
- **Public Role:** Concord Remnant scholar heading the order's Verdance chapter — the Remnant's presence *between* frontier and heartland.
- **Faction:** Concord Remnant (chapter sub-lector; corresponds with Quorrin Vane).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The bridge between the frontier Remnant the player may know (Briss, Quorrin) and the Remnant's deeper institutional self up-river. A door to the order's larger ambitions.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A travel-worn scholar with mud to the knee and a barge-satchel of rubbings, who's plainly happier in a ruin than a hall.
- **Voice:** Eager, over-precise, prone to lecturing; warms instantly to anyone curious about the old works. "You've *seen* a live node? Up-river we only have rubbings — tell me everything."
- **Private Motivation:** Map and read every Concord work along the Verdance before the Ledger strips them for salvage — and earn standing in the Remnant's up-river hierarchy.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the Ledger (his sometime-funder, Crane) will gut the ruins he's racing to study; that what he's piecing together about the corridor's nodes is something the order's leaders will *take from him* and hush.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** First-hand accounts and rubbings from frontier nodes the player has actually entered — the Remnant up-river works mostly from copies.
- **What she can offer:** Concord Script translation, scholarly context for anything the player has found, an introduction to the Remnant's heartland chapter, and a relatively *honest* read of the old works.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Escort him to a corridor ruin; bring him a rubbing or relic from a frontier node; carry his findings up-river past the Ledger's eyes.
- **What he knows:** Concord Script and corridor ruin-lore; the Remnant's inland structure and ambitions; which corridor nodes are stirring.
- **What he misunderstands:** Like most Remnant scholars, he believes the old works are a *recoverable inheritance* — knowledge and power to restore the Concord's order. He has no idea the works are a harvest engine; his idealism is a dupe's idealism.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Fascinated and alarmed — he correctly links them to the waking nodes but interprets it as "the old works reactivating," a thing to *study and control*, never to fear and destroy.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Reverent hunger. This is his life's work.
- **Relationship Links:** Corresponds with **Quorrin Vane** & **Lector Briss** (Caradril Remnant); funded (warily) by **Wessel Crane**; ferried by **Mossa Drenn**; up-river contact of **the Glassmere Remnant** and **Crown-Reclaimer Hollin Vane** (Heartlands) — a chain of Remnant ambition leading to the Concord's ruined heart.
- **Player-safe description:** A keen, road-worn Concord scholar mapping the river's old ruins.
- **DM-Only:** *(secret, gated)* Hadwin has independently deduced that the corridor's nodes are a **connected network** "switching on" up-river from the frontier — an inland echo of the truth — but reads it as restoration, not harvest. If the player ever earns deep Remnant trust, he is a clean conduit toward the Heartlands chain (Hollin Vane) — and a man who could be **horrified into an ally** if shown what the works really do.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — keeps cataloguing, the Remnant's inland reach deepens. *Helped* — a valuable scholar-ally and translator with continental contacts. *Disillusioned* (if shown the truth) — a defector who could turn the Remnant's inland chapter against its own leadership.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Mage/Scholar-like** (AC 12, ~30 HP; a few utility/divination spells, no battle-magic). Solo danger: very low. Noncombat: his whole value is knowledge, translation, and contacts.
- **Tags:** [faction:concord-remnant, role:scholar, role:memory-keeper, far-continent, major]
