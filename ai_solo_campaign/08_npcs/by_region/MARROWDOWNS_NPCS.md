## AI Use

NPC roster for **the Marrowdowns** — the settled chalk-down sheep-country and barrow-fields of old Orrun (S-central uplands), a patchwork of manorial holds and shire-moots (Lvl 6–10). This is **pastoral mid-continent**: rolling green downs, old barrow-mounds, sheep and wool, and orderly old-fashioned manorial law — a gentler, more *ordinary* region than the haunted frontier or the alien south, useful as a route south and a place of barrow-ruins and quiet old custom.

**Player-Facing surfaceable; Secret/DM-Only earned.** Reuse the two established figures (Shire-Reeve Aldous Penmark, Moot-Mother Senna Crale) — full profiles here. Senna Crale is the **southern-downs node of the continental old-songs network** (M6-oblique folk-thread). The Marrowdowns are mid-distance from the crisis — death-rite failures register as **the barrow-dead "growing restless,"** read through old down-country barrow-superstition. No NPC knows the apex truth. The **barrow-fields are pre-Concord** (older than the Concord, like the Saltmere's drowned towns) — a resonant echo, NOT the keystone.

**Cultural voice:** orderly, old-fashioned, custom-bound, dryly proud. Downs-folk speak of "the shire," "the moot," "the old barrows," "good ground and good custom," "the way it's always been done." Conservative, law-abiding, suspicious of the new but courteous to the well-mannered.

> Light anchors: **Marrowmoot** (the shire-town where the moots gather), **Penmark Hold** (Aldous Penmark's manor), **the Barrow-Fields** (the ancient burial-downs), **Wether** (a wool-trade village).

## MAJOR NPCs

### MD-M1. Shire-Reeve Aldous Penmark — the downs' landed law
> Name note: "Aldous" is shared with Reke, Cray, Penhew, Frome (common given name); "Penmark" is his hold — **not kin** to the Caradril "Penhallow" Charter House nor Ser Aldous Penhew of the Vale. Keep full names.
- **Region/Base:** Penmark Hold and Marrowmoot; circuits the shire.
- **Public Role:** Shire-Reeve of the central Marrowdowns — the senior landed official who keeps the old manorial law and presides over the shire-moot, the closest thing the downs have to a governor.
- **Faction:** Independent (landed gentry / shire-law).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The orderly authority of a whole settled region; the player's gateway to the downs' law, gentry, and wool-wealth, and a courteous contrast to the frontier's rough councils.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A dignified, grey-bearded landed gentleman in good plain wool, with the unhurried courtesy of a man whose family has kept this law for centuries, who weighs a stranger by their manners first.
- **Voice:** Measured, courteous, custom-citing, fair; speaks of precedent and propriety. "We do things by custom in the shire, friend — old custom, tested custom. It has kept the downs peaceful while the world burned elsewhere. Mind it, and you're welcome." Conservative, decent.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the shire orderly, prosperous, and unchanged — to hand his children the same peaceful, well-governed downs his fathers handed him.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the world's troubles (the Ledger's reach, the frontier's "ghost-talk," the barrow-dead's restlessness) will breach the shire's long peace; that change he can't control is coming.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Good conduct and respect for shire-custom; help with troubles his constables can't handle (barrow-robbers, a wolf-pack, a dispute beyond the moot).
- **What he can offer the player:** The shire's law, hospitality, and gentry-network; the wool-trade's wealth; honest, orderly support; a safe, civilized base and a route south.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Deal with barrow-robbers desecrating the old mounds; settle a dispute the moot can't; investigate why the barrows have "gone restless."
- **What he knows:** The shire's law, gentry, and wool-trade; the barrow-fields' old customs and superstitions; the downs' quiet politics.
- **What he misunderstands:** He frames the barrow-dead's restlessness as old down-country superstition flaring up — barrow-robbers disturbing what should be let lie — with no notion it's part of a continental crisis.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Orderly concern in the shire's idiom — "the barrows have grown restless; the old custom of letting them lie is being broken" — a far-region corroboration through *barrow-superstition*, blamed on robbers, not understood.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** The barrow-fields (pre-Concord) are sacred old ground to be respected and left alone; Concord roads cross the downs but are just roads to him.
- **Relationship Links:** Presides over the shire-gentry and the moot; works with **Moot-Mother Senna Crale** (the downs' grief-keeper) on the barrow-troubles; opposes **Cinder Ledger** wool-and-relic encroachment; the **Concord Remnant** wants his barrow-fields; **(DM)** an orderly witness to the barrow-dead's stirring.
- **Player-safe description:** The dignified shire-reeve who keeps the downs' old law and long peace.
- **DM-Only:** *(corroboration via barrow-superstition)* Aldous Penmark frames the crisis through the downs' **pre-Concord barrow-lore** — the dead growing restless, blamed on grave-robbers. The barrow-fields are *older than the Concord* (a resonant echo like the Saltmere's drowned towns), never the keystone. He's a decent, orderly potential ally and a route south; he knows no apex truth.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the shire keeps its peace, the barrow-troubles worsen quietly. *Allied* — a courteous, orderly ally and a civilized southern base. *Crossed* — the shire's law (and the gentry's weight) turns politely cold.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble/Knight-like** (AC 16 w/ retainers, ~38 HP; commands shire-constables, **Guard/Veteran-like**). Solo danger: low in person, moderate via the shire-watch. Noncombat: mind his custom; earn him by *helping the shire*.
- **Tags:** [role:authority, role:ally, far-continent, major]

### MD-M2. Moot-Mother Senna Crale — the downs' grief-keeper
> Name note: **no kin** to Mother Sennet, Goodwife Sennet, or the Senns of the League/Emberfell — "Crale" is a downs surname; keep full names.
- **Region/Base:** Marrowmoot and the Barrow-Fields; the downs' old faith.
- **Public Role:** Eldest grief-keeper of the Marrowdowns — the Mourners' Circle's southern-downs elder, who tends the shire's dead and keeps the old barrow-custom.
- **Faction:** Mourners' Circle (downs eldest); the **southern-downs node of the continental old-songs network**.
- **Political/Social Importance:** Carries the downs' deep memory and the M6-oblique old-songs thread at the settled south's edge — and she's the one who *understands* the barrow-restlessness as more than robbery.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A serene, chalk-dust-grey old woman with a barrow-keeper's quiet authority, who tends the ancient mounds and the shire's dead with a custom older than the shire itself.
- **Voice:** Soft, grave, song-and-custom-laced, downs-slow; speaks of "the old barrows," "letting them lie," "the grey hands of the old songs." "The barrows have lain quiet a long age, child. The shire blames robbers. But the *songs* say what wakes the old dead — and it's not spades." Patient, knowing, unafraid.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the barrow-custom and old songs alive — and keep the old dead lying quiet, as the songs' warning comes true.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the barrow-dead's stirring is what the old songs warned of — "the grey hands" returning; that the shire's complacency (blaming mere robbers) means no one will be ready.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Respect for the old dead and the barrows; news of the wider Mourner-network; a hand to carry the songs and the warning on.
- **What she can offer the player:** The downs' grief-lore and barrow-custom; the **old songs** (M6-oblique folk-fragment); the southern-downs key to the continental network; a knowing, humane ally who takes the crisis seriously.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Tend a restless barrow properly; carry a song or token to another elder; investigate (and *not merely rob*) a stirring barrow.
- **What she knows:** The barrow-custom and old songs; the continental Mourner-network (she corresponds with the other elders — including the scaffolded letters to Combe and Wend); that the barrow-dead's stirring matches the songs' warning, not robbers.
- **What she misunderstands:** She holds the true fragment ("the grey hands took the dead") as folk-memory and applies it to the barrows — but the *barrows are pre-Concord*, older than "the grey hands," so she half-conflates two old dooms; she doesn't know the mechanism or that the harvesters survive.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Knowing, sorrowful alarm — the songs warned of this; she's the downs' lone true-seer against the shire's "it's only robbers."
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** The songs name "the grey hands" of the Concord-age; the barrows are older still; she tends both with care and warns folk from disturbing either.
- **Relationship Links:** Continental Mourner-network node — corresponds with **Mother Ezrith Combe** (Vale), **Grandmother Wend** (Hollowmere), **Wenna Lowwater** (Glassmere), **Onn Greenward** (Hethewald), **Tide-Mother Sera**, **Salt-Mother Tess Brackhold**, **Mother Sela Reed**, **Grave-Sister Onn Crale**; works with **Aldous Penmark** on the barrow-troubles; **(DM)** the southern-downs old-songs keeper.
- **Player-safe description:** The serene old barrow-keeper who tends the downs' ancient dead and the old songs.
- **DM-Only:** *(M6-oblique, network)* Senna Crale is the **southern-downs anchor of the old-songs network** — the same gated folk-truth, here applied to the pre-Concord barrow-fields (which she half-conflates with "the grey hands"). Keep her fragment oblique; she knows no mechanism, Court, or keystone. The barrows are pre-Concord echoes, never the keystone. A humane, knowing ally and the proof the truth survives in song in the settled south.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the songs pass on; the barrows grow restless and the shire keeps blaming robbers. *Helped* — a knowing ally and network-key. *Lost* — the downs strand of the old-songs frays.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Priest/Druid-like, frail** (AC 11, ~26 HP; barrow-grief rites, *Gentle Repose*, *Speak with Dead* [mourning], low-level divine). Solo danger: none; will not fight. Noncombat: lore, network, moral clarity.
- **Tags:** [faction:mourners-circle, role:religious, role:memory-keeper, role:ally, far-continent, major, mystery-folk-thread]

### MD-M3. Barrow-Master Doss Wether — the grave-robber-turned-guide
> Name note: takes the wool-village as surname; common downs custom.
- **Region/Base:** The Barrow-Fields and Wether; circuits the downs.
- **Public Role:** Publicly a barrow-antiquary and "barrow-warden"; privately the downs' most successful barrow-robber, who knows the ancient mounds inside out.
- **Faction:** Independent (barrow-delver / grave-robber); a grey figure.
- **Political/Social Importance:** The one person who actually *opens* the barrows and lives — invaluable for the downs' deep lore or loot, and the flashpoint of the barrow-restlessness (his digging is what the shire blames, and what's *worsening* the stirring).
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A genial, chalk-stained, soft-spoken man with an antiquary's patter and a robber's eyes, who calls grave-robbing "rescuing history" and almost believes it.
- **Voice:** Smooth, scholarly-roguish, self-justifying; dresses theft in antiquarian finery. "The barrows are *history*, friend. Better I rescue it than let it rot under the chalk. That the shire would *hang* me for it is mere... custom." Charming, mercenary, increasingly uneasy.
- **Private Motivation:** Loot the barrows' ancient gold and relics before the shire catches him or the Remnant claims them — and lately, get out before the *barrows themselves* turn on him.
- **Fear/Pressure:** The shire's gallows (barrow-robbing is a capital crime under old custom); Senna Crale's quiet condemnation; and, newly, *the barrow-dead waking when he digs* — they never used to.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** A partner, a buyer, or a guard for his digs — and someone to tell him he's not mad about the barrow-dead stirring.
- **What he can offer the player:** The only survivable access to the deep barrows; the downs' ancient pre-Concord lore and loot; a guide to the barrow-fields' secrets.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Guard a barrow-dig; help him reach a deep mound; fence what he finds; verify (or *stop*) the waking dead.
- **What he knows:** The barrow-fields' insides better than any living soul; the pre-Concord barrow-relics; that the barrow-dead now wake when disturbed — recently, and worsening.
- **What he misunderstands:** He has no theory and dreads getting one — but his eyewitness account of the barrow-dead waking is the downs' most concrete corroboration of the crisis, through its *oldest* (pre-Concord) ruins.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** He doesn't name rites — he speaks of "the old dead getting up when I open the mounds," the same thing from inside a barrow. The downs' most chilling witness — and an unwitting *cause* of the local worsening.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Pure antiquarian greed; the barrows (pre-Concord) are his living, and Concord salvage too if he can get it.
- **Relationship Links:** Hunted (when caught) by **Aldous Penmark's** law; condemned by **Senna Crale**; fences to the **Cinder Ledger** and the **Concord Remnant** (who covet the barrows); a grey delver like the Saltmere's **Oss** and the Reach's deep-scavengers; **(DM)** his digging *worsens* the local barrow-stirring.
- **Player-safe description:** The charming antiquary-robber who plunders the downs' ancient barrows.
- **DM-Only:** *(grey witness / unwitting aggravator)* Doss Wether is the **barrow witness** to the pre-Concord dead waking — corroborating the crisis at the downs' oldest ruins, and (importantly) an *unwitting local aggravator*: his robbing disturbs the barrows the old custom kept lying. The barrows are pre-Concord, never the keystone. A grey contact, a lore-source, and a moral knot (his looting is part of the problem). He knows no apex truth.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — keeps robbing; the barrow-stirring worsens; the shire's hunt closes in. *Helped* — a grey guide to the barrows' lore. *Stopped/turned* — ceasing his digs eases the local stirring (a clean small win).
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Scout/Expert-like, delver** (AC 14, ~40 HP; expertise in stealth, traps, barrow-lore; not a frontline fighter). Solo danger: low offensively; losing him in a deep barrow is lethal *to the player*. Noncombat: hire, partner, fence for, or stop him.
- **Tags:** [role:criminal, role:travel-guide, role:scholar, role:exploiting-failures, far-continent, major]

Problem: barrow-robbers and rustlers plague the roads. Knows: the downs' routes and dangers; the road south. Link: serves the wool-trade. Enters: overland travel hire; a route south.

Problem: her old charms "don't bind the dead like they did." Knows: downs folk-lore; the barrow-superstitions (M2/M5-oblique). Link: respected by Senna Crale. Enters: a folk-lore/healing scene; an oblique omen.
- **[independent] Horse-Reeve Edony Marrow** — *(common Marrow; the shire's mounted lawkeeper)* Captains the shire's small troop of mounted constables who patrol the down-roads and barrow-edges. *A brisk, saddle-hard gentlewoman in good plain riding-leathers who would sooner ride a problem down than debate it at the moot.* Trait: impatient with custom, devoted to order. Problem: barrow-robbers and rustlers outride her thin troop, and the "waking" barrows now frighten her horses. Knows: the down-roads and who travels them; which barrows even the horses shun (an M5 animal-omen at scale). Link: serves **Aldous Penmark** but chafes at his slow shire-law; works with **Constable-Sergeant Brann Penmark**; would deputize a capable outsider. Enters: a mounted-patrol/escort scene; a pursuit of barrow-robbers; the player's quickest road-law contact and a route south on horseback. **(DM)** Her horses' refusal to pass certain barrows is a clean, concrete M5 corroboration that needs no theory.
