## AI Use

NPC roster for **the Saltmere Reaches** — a dying brackish inland-sea basin of salt-clans and drowned towns, ringed by salt-flats (the Bonepan Flats) and haunted-reputation hills (the Ghostmark Range), a fallen, isolated realm (Lvl 11–15). The Saltmere is **shrinking**; whole towns lie under its receding water, and the salt-clans say they "went down" in an older fall. This is a brooding, isolated, half-abandoned land — useful as a place of **drowned-town lore, an older echo of catastrophe, and a thinly-peopled frontier of salt-clans and scavengers.**

**Player-Facing surfaceable; Secret/DM-Only earned.** Reuse the two established figures (Salt-Clan Matriarch Bryd Saltmere, Drowned-Town Delver Oss) — full profiles here. **CRITICAL:** the Saltmere's "older drowning" is a **pre-Concord echo** — it *corroborates the theme* (catastrophe and the dead going down) but is **NOT** the keystone, **NOT** the Hollow Court's seat, and **NOT** the harvest mechanism. It is a separate, older, half-legendary fall. No NPC knows the apex truth; the drowned-town lore is oblique flavor that *resonates* with the campaign secret without confirming it. The Gravecallers have a plausible presence here (the failing/drowned dead draw them) — keep it subtle and DM-labeled.

**Cultural voice:** spare, fatalistic, salt-dry, clan-proud. Saltmere folk speak of "the going-down," "the drowned ones," "the clans," "the white flats," "the lake takes and the lake gives." A people living among their own ruins, proud and dwindling, superstitious about the drowned towns and the white salt-flats.

> Light anchors: **Brackhold** (the largest surviving salt-clan hold, Bryd's seat), **the Drowned Towns** (the receding lake's submerged ruins), **the Bonepan Flats** (the white salt-badlands), **Saltcairn** (a Ghostmark-edge clan-town).

## MAJOR NPCs

### SR-M1. Salt-Clan Matriarch Bryd Saltmere — head of the surviving clans
> Name note: **no kin** to Salt-Mother Bryd of the Pale Coast (Cobble Strand) — "Bryd" is a shared salt-folk name across distant regions; keep full names ("Matriarch Bryd Saltmere" vs. "Salt-Mother Bryd").
- **Region/Base:** Brackhold, the greatest surviving salt-clan hold.
- **Public Role:** Matriarch of the dominant salt-clan and de facto leader of the Saltmere's dwindling clan-confederacy — the iron old woman holding a fallen realm together.
- **Faction:** Independent (salt-clan confederacy leader).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The closest thing the Saltmere has to a ruler; the player's gateway to the salt-clans, the drowned towns, and the basin's brooding lore.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A gaunt, salt-white-haired matriarch in clan-grey, hard as the flats and twice as unyielding, who measures a stranger against a hundred years of decline.
- **Voice:** Spare, grave, fatalistic-proud; speaks for "the clans" and "the going-down." "The lake is taking us back, stranger, town by town, as it took the old ones. We hold what we can and we don't ask why." Few words, all weight.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the surviving clans alive and unbroken as the Saltmere shrinks — and keep the drowned towns' dead, and their old secret, left well alone.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the clans will finally scatter and the realm die for good; that the **drowned dead are stirring** as they did "in the going-down"; that scavengers (and worse) disturbing the drowned towns will wake what the clans have left buried for a century.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Respect for the clans and the drowned dead; help against the scavengers and raiders bleeding the dying realm; and, secretly, anyone who'll *leave the drowned towns alone.*
- **What she can offer the player:** Clan-welcome and passage through the basin; salt-clan strength and guides; the drowned-town lore (oblique); an honest, fatalistic read on the fallen realm.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Drive off drowned-town looters; settle a clan-feud; recover clan-folk lost on the flats — or *stop* a delver before they wake the wrong drowned town.
- **What she knows:** The salt-clans, the basin, and the drowned towns; the "going-down" lore (an older, pre-Concord fall); that the drowned dead stir worse of late.
- **What she misunderstands:** She frames everything through the clans' "going-down" myth — an older catastrophe distinct from the Concord — and connects the recent stirring to it, not to the frontier crisis she's never heard of. Her lore is a *resonant echo*, not the truth.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Recognition in her own idiom — "the drowned ones stir, as in the going-down" — a far-region corroboration through an **older, separate catastrophe-myth.** Powerful thematic resonance; never confirmation.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** The Saltmere's ruins are *older than* the Concord (the "going-down" predates it); she regards Concord salvage as latecomer's trash atop the old drowned towns, and shuns both.
- **Relationship Links:** Holds the clan-confederacy against scattering; tolerates (warily) **Drowned-Town Delver Oss**; opposes the **Cinder Ledger's** salt-and-relic buyers; **(DM)** the drowned dead's stirring draws **Gravecaller** interest she'd violently reject; kin-in-name only to **Salt-Mother Bryd** (Coast).
- **Player-safe description:** The iron matriarch holding the dying salt-realm's clans together.
- **DM-Only:** *(thematic echo, NOT the keystone)* Matriarch Bryd's "going-down" is a **pre-Concord catastrophe-myth** that *rhymes* with the campaign secret (the dead going down, an older fall) without being it. She is the campaign's clearest signal that **catastrophe and the troubled dead are older and wider than the Concord** — deepening the theme, never the climax. The drowned towns are pre-Concord ruins; never let them become the harvest, the Court's seat, or the keystone. She knows no apex truth.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the clans dwindle; the realm dies slowly off-screen. *Allied* — a grim, loyal frontier ally and a guide to the drowned towns' lore. *Crossed* — the salt-clans bar a hostile realm to the player.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Veteran/Clan-Chief-like** (AC 15, ~56 HP; commands hard **Guard/Veteran-like** clan-warriors). Solo danger: high in her hold, moderate alone. Noncombat: respect the clans and the drowned dead; earn her by *helping the realm*.
- **Tags:** [role:authority, role:ally, far-continent, major]

### SR-M2. Drowned-Town Delver Oss — the scavenger of the sunken ruins
- **Region/Base:** The Drowned Towns and the receding lake-edge.
- **Public Role:** The most renowned (and reviled) of the Saltmere's drowned-town delvers — who dives and loots the submerged ruins the clans hold sacred.
- **Faction:** Independent (ruin-scavenger); a grey, mistrusted figure.
- **Political/Social Importance:** The one person who actually *goes into* the drowned towns and returns — invaluable to anyone (player included) who wants the basin's deep lore or loot, and a flashpoint with the clans who forbid it.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A wiry, salt-bleached scavenger with a diver's lungs and a grave-robber's nerve, who treats the sacred drowned towns as his private larder.
- **Voice:** Dry, sardonic, secretive; gives nothing for free. "The clans say leave the drowned ones be. The clans are starving on principle. I eat what the lake gives up." Guarded, mercenary, oddly haunted.
- **Private Motivation:** Get rich (or famous) off the drowned towns before the lake recedes too far or the clans stop him — and, beneath the greed, a creeping need to understand what he keeps *finding* down there.
- **Fear/Pressure:** The clans' wrath; the lake's dangers; and, increasingly, *the drowned dead themselves* — they've begun to move when he dives, as they never did before.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** A partner, a buyer, or a bodyguard for his dives — and someone to tell him he's not mad about the drowned dead waking.
- **What he can offer the player:** The only survivable access to the drowned towns; the basin's deep loot and lore; a guide to the lake-floor ruins.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Guard a dive; help him reach a deep drowned town; fence what he finds; verify the dead are stirring.
- **What he knows:** The drowned towns' insides better than any living soul; the "going-down" relics (pre-Concord); that the drowned dead are waking.
- **What he misunderstands:** He has no theory and dreads getting one — but his eyewitness account of the drowned dead stirring is the basin's most concrete corroboration of the spreading crisis, through its *oldest* ruins.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** He doesn't name rites — he speaks of "the drowned ones moving down there," the same thing from the lake-floor. The most chilling, concrete Saltmere witness.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Pure scavenger's appraisal; the drowned towns predate the Concord and he loots both indiscriminately.
- **Relationship Links:** Reviled (but tolerated) by **Matriarch Bryd**; fences to the **Cinder Ledger's** relic-buyers; **(DM)** his waking-dead reports and the drowned towns draw **Gravecaller** interest; a grey contact like the Reach's deep-delvers (Dredd, "Badger" Crick).
- **Player-safe description:** The notorious scavenger who loots the Saltmere's sacred drowned towns.
- **DM-Only:** *(corroboration via pre-Concord ruins)* Oss is the **drowned-town witness** to the dead stirring at the basin's oldest ruins — corroborating the crisis's reach and the "older fall" theme. But the drowned towns are pre-Concord; he must never find the keystone or the Court there. His haunting is real; keep it unexplained. A natural Gravecaller-recruitment risk (the waking dead).
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — keeps looting until the dead or the clans take him. *Helped* — a grey guide to the drowned towns and their lore. *Lost* — the basin's deep access dies with him.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Scout/Diver-like, expert** (AC 14, ~44 HP; expertise in diving, stealth, ruin-survival; not a frontline fighter). Solo danger: low offensively; losing him in a drowned town is lethal *to the player*. Noncombat: hire, partner, fence for him.
- **Tags:** [role:criminal, role:travel-guide, role:scholar, far-continent, major]

### SR-M3. Salt-Mother Tess Brackhold — the basin's grief-keeper
> Name note: "Brackhold" is the hold-surname; "Salt-Mother" is her rite-title (cf. Coast's Salt-Mother Bryd — distinct person, shared salt-folk title). Keep full names.
- **Region/Base:** Brackhold and the clan-shrines of the receding lake-edge.
- **Public Role:** Eldest grief-keeper of the salt-clans — who tends the basin's dead (and re-tends the drowned ones) by the clans' old salt-rites, blending Mourner custom with the "going-down" mourning.
- **Faction:** Mourners' Circle (salt-clan eldest); the **Saltmere node of the continental old-songs network**, fused with local drowning-lore.
- **Political/Social Importance:** Carries the basin's deepest memory — and the M6-oblique old-songs thread at its most resonant, where the continental Mourner-truth meets the local "going-down" myth.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A salt-crusted, sorrowful old woman in clan-grey who tends the lake-edge shrines of the drowned with a grief that seems to reach back a hundred years.
- **Voice:** Soft, dirge-slow, salt-and-song-laced; mourns "the drowned ones, old and new." "We have sung the drowned down since the going-down, child. Lately the song doesn't hold them. As it didn't, then." Patient, unafraid, deep with old sorrow.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the salt-rites and old songs alive for the basin's dead — and keep the true memory passing, where the continental songs and the local "going-down" lore meet.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the dwindling clans will let the rites die; that the drowned dead's stirring means "the going-down comes again"; that the old songs' century-old warning is proving true here, at the basin's bones.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Respect for the drowned dead; news of the wider Mourner-network; a hand to carry the songs and the warning on.
- **What she can offer the player:** The basin's grief-lore and salt-rites; the **old songs** (M6-oblique) fused with the unique "going-down" drowning-lore; the Saltmere key to the continental network; a knowing, humane ally.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Honor a drowned grave; carry a song or token to another elder; *stop* the delvers from waking the wrong drowned dead.
- **What she knows:** The salt-rites and old songs; the continental Mourner-network (she corresponds with the other elders); the "going-down" lore; that the drowned dead stir as the songs warned.
- **What she misunderstands:** She holds the true fragment ("the grey hands took the dead") *and* the local "going-down" myth, and intuits they're related — closer than most to the deep pattern — but as folk-memory, oblique, unprovable; she doesn't know the mechanism or that the harvesters survive, and conflates the older fall with the present crisis.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Deep, expectant grief — the songs *and* the "going-down" both warned of this; she's the basin's truest, saddest witness.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** The songs name "the grey hands"; the local lore names the "going-down" older still; she mourns at the edge of both and shuns the drowned towns.
- **Relationship Links:** Continental Mourner-network node — corresponds with **Mother Ezrith Combe**, **Grandmother Wend**, **Senna Crale**, **Wenna Lowwater**, **Onn Greenward**, **Tide-Mother Sera**, **Grave-Sister Onn Crale**; honored by **Matriarch Bryd**; grieves the delvers' (Oss's) disturbances; **(DM)** her shrines are a Gravecaller-temptation site she'd reject.
- **Player-safe description:** The sorrowful old keeper of the salt-clans' rites for the drowned, old and new.
- **DM-Only:** *(M6-oblique, network, deepest resonance)* Salt-Mother Tess is where the **continental old-songs network meets the local "going-down" myth** — the campaign's strongest hint that the troubled-dead pattern is *older and wider than the Concord*, carried in folk-memory. Keep her fragments oblique; she does not know the harvest, the Court, or the keystone, and her conflation of the older fall with the present crisis is a *folk* intuition, not the truth. A humane, knowing ally.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the songs pass on; the basin's rites keep failing. *Helped* — a knowing ally and the basin's network-key. *Lost* — the Saltmere strand of the old-songs frays, and the unique "going-down" lore with it.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Priest/Acolyte-like, frail** (AC 10, ~26 HP; salt-grief rites, *Gentle Repose*, *Speak with Dead* [for mourning], low-level divine). Solo danger: none; will not fight. Noncombat: lore, network, moral clarity.
- **Tags:** [faction:mourners-circle, role:religious, role:memory-keeper, role:ally, far-continent, major, mystery-folk-thread]

### Brackhold (the great salt-clan hold)
- **[independent] Clan-Warden Doss Brackhold** — *(Bryd's son; the hold's war-leader)* Defends Brackhold. *A hard, weary warrior holding a shrinking border.* Trait: grim duty. Problem: too few warriors, too many raiders. Knows: the hold's defenses; the basin's dangers. Link: Bryd's blood and sword. Enters: any defense scene.
- **[independent] Salt-Master Sera Brackhold** — *(Bryd's daughter; runs the salt-works)* Manages the clan's salt-trade. *A sharp, practical woman keeping the hold fed on salt.* Trait: trade-shrewd, clan-proud. Problem: the Ledger squeezes the salt-price. Knows: the salt-trade; the Ledger's reach. Link: Bryd's heir-apparent. Enters: a salt-trade scene; an anti-Ledger lever.
- **[Cinder Ledger] Factor Iss Tallow** — *(common Tallow; the Ledger's salt-and-relic buyer)* The Ledger's man in the basin. *A cold, persistent factor the clans loathe.* Trait: relentless lowball. Problem: the clans resist; the drowned towns spook his agents. Knows: the Ledger's basin designs; the drowned-relic trade. Link: opposed by the clans; fences from Oss. Enters: a Ledger-trade scene.
- **[independent] Welcomer Mab Saltcairn** — *(from Saltcairn)* The hold's host to rare outsiders. *A weathered, watchful woman who decides who's fed.* Trait: hospitality as caution. Problem: outsiders usually mean trouble. Knows: how to win the clans' trust. Link: Bryd's envoy. Enters: the social gateway; "wary of outsiders" softened.

### The Drowned Towns & the Bonepan Flats
- **[independent] Lake-Pilot Brann Saltmere** — *(common Saltmere)* Guides boats over the drowned towns. *A salt-bleached boatman who reads the receding water.* Trait: superstitious, indispensable. Problem: the water-lines shift; the drowned dead stir. Knows: the lake's safe channels; where the dead wake (M5-resonant). Link: ferries Oss (reluctantly). Enters: a lake-crossing scene; a drowned-dead witness.
- **[Gravecaller-adjacent] Wanderer "the Drowned Sister"** — *(DM-watch)* A grieving outsider drawn to the stirring dead. *A hollow-eyed woman who "hears" the drowned ones.* Trait: an eerie, sorrowful calm. Problem: she's drawing the bereaved toward forbidden "listening." Knows: the drowned-dead phenomena; cult-fragments. Link: gravitates to the drowned towns; **(DM)** a Gravecaller emissary/recruiter. Enters: a subtle cult-seed scene; keep DM-labeled and oblique.
- **[independent] Flats-Guide Sef Bonepan** — *(takes the flats as surname)* Crosses the white salt-badlands. *A mirage-wise, laconic guide who never loses the trail.* Trait: trusts the flats over people. Problem: the flats kill the unwary; raiders prowl them. Knows: the Bonepan crossings; the basin's edges. Link: serves any who pay. Enters: a salt-flats travel scene.
- **[independent] Drowned-Relic Fence Doll Tallow** — *(common Tallow; no kin to Iss)* Buys what the delvers raise. *A quick, nervy dealer in drowned-town finds.* Trait: greed over caution. Problem: some finds are "wrong" and she knows it. Knows: the drowned-relic trade; who buys. Link: fences for Oss; sells to the Ledger. Enters: a fence scene; a "wrong relic" (M2) lead.

### Saltcairn & the Ghostmark edge
- **[independent] Clan-Elder Tess Saltcairn** — Eldest of the Ghostmark-edge clan-town. *A proud, failing matriarch watching her town empty.* Trait: clings to old dignity. Problem: Saltcairn is dying faster than Brackhold. Knows: the Ghostmark's haunted reputation; the basin's decline. Link: defers to Bryd. Enters: a dying-town aid hook.
- **[Concord Remnant] Sub-Lector Wenn Cairn** — *(common Cairn)* A lone Remnant scholar studying the "going-down." *A nervy, fascinated scholar far from his order.* Trait: brave curiosity, no clan-sense. Problem: the clans forbid the drowned towns he covets. Knows: that the "going-down" predates the Concord (a key distinction); Script. Link: corresponds with the Reliquary (Glassmere). Enters: a Remnant-in-the-basin scene; the "older fall" lore-keeper.
- **[independent] Ghostmark-Hermit "Old Salt"** — A recluse in the haunted hills. *A salt-mad old hermit who "talks to the going-down."* Trait: cracked, oracular, harmless. Problem: half-believes his own madness. Knows: the basin's oldest lore (oblique, unreliable). Link: shunned by the clans. Enters: an atmospheric lore-source; a fair red-herring or oblique truth.
- **[independent] Salt-Caravan Master Marek Bonepan** — *(takes the flats as surname)* Heads the salt-trains that haul the basin's one export out to the wider south. *A broad, sun-cured trader-captain who's crossed the white flats a thousand times and trusts no road he hasn't walked.* Trait: caravan-pride; counts everything in barrels and water-skins. Problem: flats-raiders ("Mirage" and her kind) and the Ledger's lowballing are strangling the salt-trade the clans live on. Knows: the routes out of the dying basin and who buys salt in the south; which trains have vanished and where. Link: serves the clans' survival; rival of **Factor Iss Tallow** (Ledger); hires flats-guides like **Sef Bonepan**. Enters: a salt-trade/escort hook; the player's clearest land-route *out* of the Saltmere and an anti-Ledger lever. **(DM)** Carries gossip from the wider south back into the isolated basin — a clean way to connect Saltmere lore to the larger crisis the clans never hear of.
