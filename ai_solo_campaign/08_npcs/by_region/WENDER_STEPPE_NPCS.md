## AI Use

NPC roster for **the Wender Steppe** — a cold open grass-sea ridden by horse-clans the Concord never tamed (N-central, beyond the Highmark Spine; Lvl 8–13). This is **ungoverned wildland** and, crucially, **a people outside the Concord's whole history** — the steppe-clans were never part of the Concord, never harvested, and carry no Concord ruins or rites. They are the campaign's clearest "outside view": a culture to whom the entire crisis is a *foreign* affliction of the settled south. NPC coverage skews **mobile** — clan-camps, caravans, riders, and seasonal migration, not fixed towns (3 major, 12 secondary, 30 minor).

**Player-Facing surfaceable; Secret/DM-Only earned.** Reuse the two established figures (Clan-Speaker Tamur Wend-Khar, Wind-Singer Esha) — full profiles here. **No NPC knows the apex truth.** Because the clans were never Concord, death-rite failures are **alien to them** — "the settled-folk's sickness," a sky-omen the seers read with dread but not understanding. The steppe is the campaign's reminder that the world is larger than the Concord's shadow.

**Cultural voice:** distinct, NON-Orrun. Steppe-clans use horse-and-sky imagery, kin-names, and a proud, plain directness. They speak of "the long grass," "the riding," "the clans," "the Sky," "the settled-folk" (faintly pitied), "winter-camp" and "summer-riding." Hospitable to guests by sacred custom, deadly to raiders. Names follow clan-conventions (given-name + clan, e.g. "Wend-Khar," "of the Grey Mare clan"), deliberately distinct from Orrun surnames.

> Light anchors: **the Winter-Camp at Cold Springs** (the largest clans' cold-season gathering), **the Summer-Riding** (the migrating clan-host), **the Sky-Stones** (an ancient steppe shrine-circle, NOT Concord), **the Spine-Foot trade-meet** (where steppe and settled trade at the mountains' edge).

## MAJOR NPCs

### WS-M1. Clan-Speaker Tamur Wend-Khar — voice of the steppe-clans
> Name note: **no kin** to the Reach Wends — "Wend-Khar" is a steppe clan-name (coincidental root); always full name. He speaks for a confederacy, he does not rule it.
- **Region/Base:** The Winter-Camp at Cold Springs; rides with the Summer-Riding.
- **Public Role:** Clan-Speaker — chosen voice of the steppe-clans' loose confederacy, who speaks for the clans to outsiders and at the great gatherings without ruling any clan but his own.
- **Faction:** Independent (steppe horse-clan confederacy).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The player's gateway to the entire steppe-people; without the Speaker's guest-welcome, the steppe is a deadly trackless sea; with it, a vast ally outside the Concord's whole world.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A weather-hardened, far-eyed clan-lord in fur and lacquered leather, mounted as if born to it, who greets a guest with sacred courtesy and a rider's frank appraisal.
- **Voice:** Plain, proud, sky-and-horse-imaged, direct; courteous to guests, blunt with raiders. "The long grass answers to no crown, settled-one. The clans ride free, as we always have. You come as guest? Then you are kin while you ride with us. Come as raider, and the Sky will not find your bones." Few words, much weight.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the clans free and united-enough to ride as they always have — against the settled south's creeping trade, the harsh winters, and the clan-feuds that fracture the confederacy.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the settled-folk's trade (and the Ledger's reach) will tame the free clans; that the clan-feuds will break the confederacy; that the seers' growing dread — of a "sickness in the settled-lands' dead" drifting toward the grass — might reach the steppe.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Guest-honor and fair trade; a strong arm against raiders or a rival clan; and, from a frontier guest, *the truth of the settled-folk's omens* his seers fear.
- **What he can offer the player:** Guest-welcome and safe passage across the trackless steppe; the clans' horses, riders, and strength; an honest outside view of the whole crisis; mounts and steppe-craft.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Ride against raiders or a feuding clan; carry the Speaker's word to a rival; help his seers read the "settled-sickness" omen.
- **What he knows:** The steppe, its clans, feuds, and migrations; the settled-folk only as distant traders; that his seers read a growing dread in the Sky from the south.
- **What he misunderstands:** The crisis is wholly foreign to him — a "settled-folk's sickness" with no bearing on the free clans, who bury no dead in Concord ground and keep no Concord rites. He's the campaign's clearest "this is not our doom" voice — which is *almost* true, and may not stay so.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Distant unease — "a sickness of the settled-lands' dead, that the Sky shows my seers" — pitied, feared at a remove, not understood. The outside view.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Indifference shading to disdain — the Concord was a settled-folk's empire that never touched the free grass; its ruins are "the settled-dead's stone bones," nothing to a rider.
- **Relationship Links:** Speaks for the clan-confederacy; relies on the seer **Wind-Singer Esha**; rival of feuding clan-lords; trades (warily) with the **Cinder Ledger's** edge-agents at Spine-Foot; **(DM)** the campaign's "people outside the Concord" — a vast, free, uncomprehending world.
- **Player-safe description:** The free-riding Speaker of the steppe horse-clans, courteous to guests and deadly to raiders.
- **DM-Only:** *(outside-view, world-scale)* Tamur Wend-Khar embodies **the world beyond the Concord** — a whole free people the harvest never touched, to whom the crisis is foreign. He widens the player's sense of the world and offers a vast wildland ally. The steppe has **no Concord ruins and no harvest-history** — keep it clean of them; the Sky-Stones shrine is an *independent* steppe holy site, never Concord. He knows no apex truth; his seers' dread is the crisis seen *from entirely outside.*
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the clans ride free; settled-trade and feuds press on off-screen. *Allied* (guest-honor earned) — a vast steppe ally and free passage. *Crossed* (guest-law broken) — the whole grass-sea turns lethal.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 — Mounted Veteran/Chieftain-like** (AC 16, ~80 HP; mounted lance-and-bow, commands many **Scout/Veteran-like** horse-warriors). Solo danger: extreme on the open steppe with his riders; moderate alone. Noncombat: honor guest-law; earn him as kin.
- **Tags:** [role:authority, role:ally, far-continent, major]

### WS-M2. Wind-Singer Esha — the steppe seer
> Name note: a steppe given-name (no clan suffix used publicly for a seer); distinct from Orrun "Esha"-minors. Established figure, full profile here.
- **Region/Base:** Rides with the clans; keeps vigil at the Sky-Stones.
- **Public Role:** The clans' most honored Wind-Singer — a steppe seer who reads the Sky's omens, sings the clans' dead to the wind, and counsels the Speaker.
- **Faction:** Independent (steppe seer); kin-in-spirit (very oblique) to the Mourners' grief-keeping, by parallel not contact.
- **Political/Social Importance:** The steppe's spiritual authority and the one figure who *reads* the crisis as a Sky-omen — the steppe's oblique early-warning, and a strange far echo of the Mourners' role.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A wind-burned figure of indeterminate age, hung with feather and bone-charms, whose eyes seem fixed on a horizon no one else can see, and who speaks the Sky's omens in song.
- **Voice:** Sung, fey, sky-imaged, unhurried; speaks in omen and wind-metaphor. "The Sky has gone *thin* in the south, settled-one. Something pulls at the dead down there, like wind through torn felt. My clan is far from it. But the wind... the wind carries far." Eerie, kind, far-seeing.
- **Private Motivation:** Read the Sky truly for the clans' good — and understand the growing wrongness in the southern sky that troubles every Wind-Singer's vigil.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the "thinning sky" in the south is drifting north toward the free grass; that the omens mean the clans' age of freedom is ending; that she alone among the clans senses how vast the wrongness is.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** A frontier guest's first-hand truth of the "settled-sickness," to test against the Sky's omens — she suspects they are the same thing seen two ways.
- **What she can offer the player:** The steppe's omen-lore; an outside, oblique read on the crisis (the "thinning sky" — a genuine far-echo of the Quiet Country thinning, kept very oblique); the clans' spiritual welcome; sky-craft and weather-reading.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Carry her omen-warning to the settled-folk; help her vigil at the Sky-Stones; tell her, truly, what the south's dead do.
- **What she knows:** The Sky's omens and the steppe's spirit-lore; that the southern sky has "gone thin" and pulls at the dead; the clans' deepest history (which contains no Concord).
- **What she misunderstands:** Her "thinning sky" omen is the crisis seen through steppe-cosmology — genuinely *perceiving* something true (the thinning) without any framework to name it; she has no concept of the harvest, the Concord, or the Quiet Country.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Sung dread — "the Sky thins, the dead are pulled" — the steppe's oblique perception of the same crisis, from wholly outside. The most *poetically* true far-region read, and the most deniable.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Indifference; the Concord is settled-folk's stone, beneath the Sky's notice and hers — except that the "thinning" *began* in their lands.
- **Relationship Links:** Counsels **Tamur Wend-Khar**; honored across the clans; **(DM)** her "thinning sky" is a deeply-oblique far echo of the Quiet Country's depletion — a strange spiritual parallel to the Mourners' old songs, reached independently, never by contact.
- **Player-safe description:** The honored steppe seer who reads the Sky and sings the clans' dead to the wind.
- **DM-Only:** *(oblique cosmic echo — handle with care)* Wind-Singer Esha *perceives* the crisis as a "thinning of the southern Sky" — a deeply-oblique, deniable far-echo of the **Quiet Country thinning** (the harvest's deepest consequence), reached through steppe-cosmology with no framework to name it. This is the campaign's most cosmic far-region hint; keep it **extremely oblique** and never let her name the harvest, the Quiet Country, or the Court. She corroborates the *theme's* vast reach (even the free steppe's sky feels it) without any confirmation. A clean, eerie, humane ally.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — sings her omens; the thinning sky deepens. *Helped* — an eerie, knowing ally and an oblique cosmic witness. *Lost* — a strange far perception of the truth is silenced.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Druid/Diviner-like** (AC 12, ~32 HP; omen-divination, weather, low-level nature-magic; not a fighter). Solo danger: low. Noncombat: omen-lore, the oblique cosmic read, spiritual welcome.
- **Tags:** [role:religious, role:memory-keeper, role:ally, far-continent, major, mystery-cosmic-echo]

### WS-M3. Raid-Lord Borr of the Black Horse — the steppe's reaver
> Name note: "Borr of the Black Horse clan" — steppe clan-naming; distinct from Orrun names.
- **Region/Base:** The far steppe and the Spine-Foot trade-meet's edges; a raiding clan-host.
- **Public Role:** A feared raid-lord of the Black Horse clan — who rejects the Speaker's confederacy and rides to raid the settled edge and rival clans alike.
- **Faction:** Independent (steppe raider / dissident clan).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The steppe's danger made flesh — the raider a player most likely *clashes* with at the settled edge, a rival to the Speaker's confederacy, and a wild card who could be fought, fled, or (hard) turned.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A scarred, grinning reaver on a great black horse, hung with the trophies of raids, who measures a settled stranger as either prey, sport, or — rarely — a worthy rider.
- **Voice:** Bold, mocking, raid-proud, sky-defiant; scorns the Speaker's "tame talk." "Wend-Khar bows to settled-trade and calls it freedom. The Black Horse *takes.* That is the old way, the Sky's way. Which are you, settled-one — prey, or sport?" Fierce, theatrical, not without honor to those who earn it.
- **Private Motivation:** Ride free and take freely as the old way demands — and prove the Black Horse the steppe's true heart against the Speaker's "soft" confederacy.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the confederacy (or the settled garrisons) will combine to break him; that the seers' "thinning sky" omen is a doom even his defiance can't outride; that his own brutal lieutenants will turn the raiding to senseless slaughter.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Tribute, sport, or a worthy rival; he respects strength and nerve, despises weakness and bowing.
- **What he can offer the player:** The far steppe's raider-network and routes; deadly cavalry as ally or enemy; a wild, honor-bound (if dangerous) path across the ungoverned grass; the *old* steppe ways the Speaker has softened.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Ride a raid (against the settled or a rival clan); prove themselves in a contest of horse and nerve; choose his way over the Speaker's.
- **What he knows:** The far steppe's raid-routes and rival clans; the settled edge's weak points; the old steppe ways.
- **What he misunderstands:** The crisis is beneath his scorn — "settled-folk's troubles" — though the seers' dread unsettles even him; he'd ride *away* from an omen, never toward understanding it.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Defiant dismissal masking unease — "let the settled-dead rot; the Black Horse rides free" — but the "thinning sky" troubles his own seers, and he hates that.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Scorn; settled stone is for burning or ignoring.
- **Relationship Links:** Rival of **Tamur Wend-Khar's** confederacy; raids the settled edge and the **Cinder Ledger's** Spine-Foot caravans; his brutal lieutenant strains his honor; **(DM)** the steppe's wild-card danger and the "old way" foil to the Speaker.
- **Player-safe description:** The fierce raid-lord of the Black Horse, who rejects the confederacy and rides to take by the old way.
- **DM-Only:** *(wild-card / foil)* Raid-Lord Borr is the steppe's **danger and dissent** — the "old way" foil to the Speaker's confederacy, a rival a player can fight, flee, or (hard) earn. Not a faction; a dissident clan-host. He's also a steppe-internal clock (his raiding could fracture the confederacy). He knows and cares nothing for the apex truth.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — raids the edge; may fracture the confederacy or be broken off-screen. *Earned* (very hard) — a fierce, honor-bound steppe ally and the old ways' path. *Opposed* — a deadly cavalry enemy across the open grass.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 — Mounted Berserker/Veteran-like** (AC 15, ~90 HP; mounted greataxe-and-bow, commands a hard **Berserker/Scout-like** raid-host). Solo danger: extreme mounted with his riders; high alone. Noncombat: tribute, a contest of nerve, or split him from his lieutenant.
- **Tags:** [role:criminal, role:outlaw, role:antagonist, far-continent, major]

### The Spine-Foot trade-meet (where steppe meets settled)
- **[Cinder Ledger] Edge-Factor Iss Karr** — *(common Karr; the Ledger's steppe-edge trader)* Trades settled-goods for steppe horses and furs. *A shrewd, cold factor working the trade-meet.* Trait: relentless bargaining. Problem: the clans distrust him; raiders threaten his caravans. Knows: the steppe-edge trade; the Ledger's reach. Link: trades with Tamur, raided by Borr. Enters: a trade-meet scene; the Ledger's edge.
- **[independent] Trade-Meet Keeper Doss Spine** — *(takes the place as surname; settled-born)* Runs the neutral trade-meet. *A grizzled, even-handed broker between two worlds.* Trait: scrupulous neutrality. Problem: caught between clans, raiders, and settled-greed. Knows: the steppe-settled trade; both sides' moods. Link: serves all, owned by none. Enters: a neutral-ground scene; a bridge between worlds.
- **[independent] Caravan-Master Mab Wether** — *(common Wether; a settled drover braving the steppe-edge)* Hauls goods to the trade-meet. *A bold, road-hard teamster who dares the raiders.* Trait: nerve and grievance. Problem: Borr's raiders bleed her caravans. Knows: the route to the steppe-edge; the raid-dangers. Link: trades at Spine-Foot; raided by Borr. Enters: caravan travel hire; a raid-threat witness.
- **[independent] Steppe-Trader "Three-Furs" Borr-Tai** — *(steppe clan-name)* A clan-trader who deals with the settled. *A canny, well-traveled rider who knows both worlds.* Trait: cosmopolitan for a steppe-man. Problem: distrusted by both sides as too-settled. Knows: the steppe and the settled-edge both; the trade. Link: trades at Spine-Foot. Enters: a cross-world contact; a mobile rumor.

### Region-wide (the open grass)
- **[independent] Sky-Stones Keeper Old Wend-Mor** — *(steppe clan-name)* Tends the ancient steppe shrine-circle. *A wind-cracked old keeper of the clans' oldest holy site.* Trait: reverence for the Sky-Stones. Problem: the Stones "hum strange" of late. Knows: the steppe's deepest spirit-lore; the Sky-Stones (NOT Concord — an *independent* steppe site). Link: honored by the seers. Enters: a steppe-shrine scene; the steppe's own ancient holy lore.
- **[independent] Lone-Rider "Cold" Esha-Mor** — *(steppe clan-name)* A solitary steppe scout-for-hire. *A silent, far-ranging rider who guides the lost across the grass.* Trait: trusts the grass over people. Problem: the trackless steppe kills the unguided. Knows: the steppe's hidden ways and water. Link: rides for any with guest-honor. Enters: a steppe-guide hire; the survival-key.
- **[independent] Kin-Mother Tamur-Sai** — *(no kin to Clan-Speaker Tamur Wend-Khar despite the shared name-root; a separate clan's matriarch)* The eldest matriarch of a great clan and keeper of its bloodlines, marriages, and old debts. *A broad, iron-spined grandmother who sits at the heart of the Winter-Camp and remembers every alliance and grudge for four generations.* Trait: weaves the clans together by marriage and memory the way the Speaker does by word. Problem: the feuds (Feud-Lord Kessa's chief among them) and a hard winter strain the kin-bonds she's spent a life knotting. Knows: every clan's lineage, alliance, and old wrong; who can be wed or reconciled to whom; the human map *beneath* the Speaker's confederacy. Link: a power behind **Tamur Wend-Khar's** confederacy; could mediate **Feud-Lord Kessa's** grudge; honored by **Wind-Singer Esha**. Enters: a clan-politics/feud-reconciliation scene; the social key to binding (or splitting) the steppe-clans. **(DM)** The unglamorous true glue of the confederacy — winning her does more to unite the steppe than winning the Speaker.
