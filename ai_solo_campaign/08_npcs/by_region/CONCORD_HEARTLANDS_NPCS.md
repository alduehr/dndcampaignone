## AI Use

NPC roster for **the Concord Heartlands / the Ruin'd Crown** — the shattered surface seat of the old Custodian Concord, astride the Greatspine passes; a vast contested ruin everyone covets and no one holds (Lvl 13–17, late-game placeholder). This region has a **low living population** but a rich ecology of **ruin-edge survivors, scavengers, scholars, soldiers, refugees, cultists, and rival expeditions** drawn to the Crown. Coverage skews toward those people, not toward farms and shops.

**CRITICAL SECRECY:** This is the Concord's *surface* fallen-capital country. It is **NOT** the Concord Deep (the buried harvest-network) and **NOT** the Under-Shrine / Drowned Keystone beneath Hollowmere. The Heartlands **corroborate** the campaign theme (the Concord's grandeur and fall) but are **not** the keystone and **not** the Hollow Court's seat. **No NPC here knows the apex truth.** The far-region grandeur must NEVER pull the climax away from Hollowmere. NPCs may know *more surface history* than frontier folk — the Concord was vast, it fell deliberately-seeming, its inner circle vanished — but the harvest, the Hollow Court, and the buried keystone stay DM-only and are never confirmed here.

**Cultural voice:** ruin-haunted gravity. People here live among the bones of the world's greatest civilization and know it. They speak of "the Crown," "the old hands," "the work that ate them." Survivors are hard, watchful, and superstitious about which ruins to enter. Refugees from contested ground drift through. Reuse the two established figures from `FAR_CONTINENT_NPCS.md` (Hollin Vane, "Old Crown" Mab) — full profiles here.

> Light anchors: **Crownmouth** (a fortified scavenger-town at the ruin's edge, the only real settlement), **the Ruin'd Crown** (the fallen capital ruins themselves), **the Greatspine passes** (contested mountain roads), **the Pilgrim Camps** (shanty-camps of refugees and relic-seekers).

## MAJOR NPCs

### CH-M1. Crown-Reclaimer Magister Hollin Vane — the Remnant's expedition-lord
> Name note: **not kin** to Quorrin Vane, Hadwin Vael, Oneth Vael, or Imris Vane-Tolm — common scholarly surname; keep full names.
- **Region/Base:** Crownmouth and the Ruin'd Crown; the Remnant's heartland expedition seat.
- **Public Role:** The Concord Remnant's senior field-commander at the Crown — the scholar-magister leading the order's greatest expedition, to reclaim the Concord's fallen capital.
- **Faction:** Concord Remnant (inner-circle / heartland expedition-lord; above the frontier sub-lectors).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The most powerful Remnant figure the campaign authors — the order's grand ambition made flesh. If the player follows the Remnant's chain up-river and inland, it ends at Hollin Vane and the Crown.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A commanding, hawk-eyed magister in field-worn scholar's robes over light armor, who treats the greatest ruin in the world like a problem he intends to solve.
- **Voice:** Imperious, visionary, certain; speaks of "restoring what was lost" as a destiny. "The Concord did not die. It was *interrupted*. We will finish the sentence." Brooks little doubt.
- **Private Motivation:** Reclaim the Crown, recover the Concord's lost knowledge and power, and **rebuild the Concord's order with himself among its restorers** — to end the world's slow decay by restoring its lost greatness.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That a rival (the Ledger's salvagers, the Emberfell theocracy's agents, the salt-clans, freelance delvers) will reach the Crown's deepest vaults first; that the order's frontier chapters are stumbling onto truths he hasn't sanctioned; that the Crown itself is more dangerous than even he admits — his expeditions keep vanishing in the deep ruins.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Capable, deniable agents to do what scholars can't — secure a deep ruin, recover a specific relic, eliminate a rival expedition, or bring him first-hand reports from frontier nodes (he hungers for the live-node data the frontier player has).
- **What he can offer the player:** The Remnant's full institutional power — knowledge, Concord Script mastery, magic, money, and a place in the "restoration"; access to the Crown's catalogued ruins; the truest available reading of Concord history *as the Remnant understands it.*
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Clear a rival from a vault; recover a "keystone-fragment" relic; carry his sealed orders down-river to Quorrin Vane; suppress (or investigate) what the frontier chapters are uncovering.
- **What he knows:** Concord history and Script at the deepest scholarly level; the Crown's layout and its dangers; the Remnant's true ambition; that the Concord's *inner circle* "vanished rather than died" in the fall (he reads this as tragedy, not as the Hollow Court).
- **What he misunderstands:** **Everything that matters.** He believes the Concord was a benevolent order whose knowledge can be *restored* for good. He does not know it was a harvest engine, does not know the Custodians survive as the Hollow Court, does not know the keystone is under Hollowmere. He is the campaign's grandest **dupe** — a brilliant idealist who would, if he succeeded, hand the world straight back to its harvesters.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Reads them as proof the old works are "reawakening" and must be *reclaimed and mastered* — the worst possible conclusion, and the Remnant's core error writ large.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Worshipful ambition. The Crown is the altar of his life's purpose.
- **Relationship Links:** Senior to **Quorrin Vane**, **Hadwin Vael**, **Lector Briss**, **Sabine Ferrant** (the whole Remnant chain leads up to him); rival to **Wessel Crane** & the Ledger's salvagers; opposed by the **Emberfell theocracy's** relic-agents; hires **"Old Crown" Mab** as a guide; **(DM)** unknowingly the Hollow Court's most useful potential tool.
- **Player-safe description:** A formidable scholar-magister leading the great expedition to reclaim the Concord's fallen capital.
- **DM-Only:** *(apex-adjacent — handle with extreme care)* Hollin Vane is the figure most likely to **stumble toward the truth from the wrong end** — if his expedition reaches a deep Crown vault that records the harvest, he would try to *master* it, not destroy it. He is a late-game **escalation lever**: the Remnant succeeding at the Crown is one of the campaign's bad endings. He must **never** be allowed to confirm the Hollow Court or the Hollowmere keystone in narration; the Crown's deepest secret is a *record of what the Concord did*, not the living machine. Keep him a magnificent, dangerous idealist.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the expedition grinds on; the Remnant's reach at the Crown deepens; an off-screen escalation clock. *Allied* — a patron of immense power whose goals quietly endanger everything. *Opposed/disillusioned* — if shown the true nature of the works, either a horrified ally or a man who refuses to believe and becomes a final-act obstacle.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 — Archmage/Magister-like, boss-class** (AC 15, ~120 HP; high-level Concord-attuned spellcasting, ruin-wards, a guard of **Veteran/Knight-like** expedition-soldiers). Solo danger: **extreme** — a level-13+ threat. Noncombat: he is far more useful as a misguided patron than a fight; persuasion, truth-revelation, and faction-leverage are the real tools.
- **Tags:** [faction:concord-remnant, role:authority, role:scholar, boss-class, far-continent, major, apex-adjacent]

### CH-M2. Warden Lyssa Crownmouth — captain of the scavenger-town
- **Region/Base:** Crownmouth, the fortified town at the ruin's edge.
- **Public Role:** Elected war-captain of Crownmouth — the hard woman who keeps the only real settlement at the Crown alive against ruin-horrors, raiders, and rival expeditions.
- **Faction:** Independent (Crownmouth's free militia); plays the factions against each other.
- **Political/Social Importance:** The de facto ruler of the only safe ground at the Crown; every expedition, the player included, must come through her town and her terms.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A scarred, plain-spoken woman in salvaged Concord-plate, with a soldier's economy and a survivor's distrust, who has buried more delvers than she's welcomed.
- **Voice:** Terse, dry, command-hardened; weighs newcomers as future corpses until proven otherwise. "Crownmouth's rules keep you alive. The Crown's rules just kill you slower."
- **Private Motivation:** Keep Crownmouth free and standing — not owned by the Remnant, the Ledger, the theocracy, or any expedition that thinks her town is a base to be requisitioned.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That one of the great factions will simply *take* Crownmouth; that the ruin is "waking" worse — her people's dead won't stay buried, and the horrors come closer each season.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Capable hands who'll fight for the town without trying to own it; honest word of which expeditions mean Crownmouth harm.
- **What she can offer the player:** The only safe base at the Crown — lodging, supply, ruin-guides, salvage-market, and a hard-eyed honest read on every faction operating here.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Drive off a ruin-horror threatening the walls; break up a faction's attempt to seize the town; recover townsfolk lost in the near ruins.
- **What she knows:** The Crown's edge-ruins and their dangers; every expedition's strength and intent; that Crownmouth's *own dead* have begun to rise wrong (a frontier-style failure, now here).
- **What she misunderstands:** She thinks the ruin is simply "hungry, old, and waking" — a haunted place getting worse. She has no theory and wants none; she just wants her people to stop dying.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Grim, immediate, practical — she's lived it longest, here at the source-country. Her dead-won't-rest problem is the **oldest and worst** of any region's, which makes Crownmouth a powerful late-game corroboration of how far the crisis reaches — without anyone here understanding it.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Wary respect and hard-won fear. The Crown gives Crownmouth its salvage *and* its horrors; she takes both as facts of life.
- **Relationship Links:** Holds the line against **Hollin Vane's** expedition's encroachment; tolerates **"Old Crown" Mab** (her best guide); trades with the Ledger's salvagers and the Mourner **Grave-Sister Onn Crale** (below); resists the Emberfell agents; respected by refugee-leaders in the Pilgrim Camps.
- **Player-safe description:** The iron war-captain who keeps the last safe town at the Ruin'd Crown alive.
- **DM-Only:** *(corroboration)* Crownmouth is the campaign's strongest *living* proof that the crisis is **continent-wide and worst near the Concord's old heart** — but it is surface-history horror, not the keystone. Keep her dead-rising problem severe but **unexplained**; she must never learn the cause. A high-level player who reaches her gains powerful thematic confirmation and a staunch potential ally.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — Crownmouth holds or falls off-screen; if it falls, the Crown loses its only safe ground. *Allied* — a fierce, loyal frontier ally and a base of operations. *Crossed* — she bars the player from the only safe town at the deadliest ruin in the world.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 — Veteran/Knight-Captain-like** (AC 18 salvaged plate, ~95 HP; commands a militia of **Guard/Veteran-like** fighters; Concord-salvage gear). Solo danger: high in her town; deadly with her militia. Noncombat: earn her trust by *helping the town*, not by deals.
- **Tags:** [role:authority, role:ally, far-continent, major]

### CH-M3. Father Casian Ord — the heretic-priest of the Pilgrim Camps
- **Region/Base:** The Pilgrim Camps, the refugee-and-seeker shanties below Crownmouth.
- **Public Role:** A wandering Three Thresholds priest turned camp-shepherd, tending the thousands who come to the Crown to seek relics, the dead, or sanctuary.
- **Faction:** Three Thresholds (lapsed/schismatic); quietly grief-keeping like the Mourners; **(DM)** a Gravecaller-adjacent thread.
- **Political/Social Importance:** The moral voice of the camps — the only authority the desperate relic-seekers and refugees actually heed. Where Crownmouth has the walls, Ord has the people's hearts.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A gaunt, gentle, fever-bright priest in patched vestments who moves through the camps blessing the dying and the dead with equal, exhausted tenderness.
- **Voice:** Soft, sorrowful, scripture-worn fraying into something rawer; "the Door is *stuck*, friend, here above all places — and I have begun to wonder if we should be the ones to open it." A reassurance that keeps slipping toward heresy.
- **Private Motivation:** Ease the suffering of the camps' dead and grieving — and, increasingly, *understand why* the Last Door fails worst here, at the Concord's heart.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That his faith is a lie; that the dead he buries and they will not stay; that the answer he's circling toward is damnation.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** A listener; news of the wider crisis; and — secretly — anyone who knows why the rites fail and whether the dead can be *freed*.
- **What he can offer the player:** The trust and knowledge of the camps (thousands of seekers know fragments); sanctuary among the refugees; folk-rites that still half-work; and an honest, anguished read on the spiritual crisis at its worst.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Help the camps' dead find rest; recover a lost pilgrim from the ruins; bring him word of the crisis's true shape — and, if trusted, help him test a forbidden "freeing."
- **What he knows:** The camps' rumors and the seekers' fragmentary lore; that the Last Door fails worse at the Crown than anywhere; oblique scraps that the Concord's dead are "still here, still held" (M6-oblique, surface-history flavor).
- **What he misunderstands:** He half-grasps a fragment — that the dead are *held*, not freed — and is being drawn, like the Gravecallers, toward "freeing" them by force. He has no idea this would be catastrophe; his compassion is leading him toward the same tragic error.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** This is his whole crisis of faith. He lives in their worst concentration and is breaking under it — the most *spiritually* advanced reaction of any far-region NPC, and the campaign's clearest far-region echo of the Gravecaller temptation.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Haunted dread shading into forbidden curiosity; he has begun to enter the edge-ruins seeking "the held dead."
- **Relationship Links:** Tolerated by **Warden Lyssa Crownmouth** (she needs him to keep the camps calm); kin-in-grief to the Mourner **Grave-Sister Onn Crale**; **(DM)** quietly in contact with a Gravecaller emissary drawn to the Crown's failing dead; pities and fears **Hollin Vane's** reclamation.
- **Player-safe description:** A gentle, failing priest shepherding the refugee camps below the great ruin.
- **DM-Only:** *(secret, gated)* Ord is the **far-region mirror of the Gravecaller tragedy** — compassion sliding toward catastrophic "freeing." If a Gravecaller cell ever reaches the Crown (plausible: the worst failures draw them), Ord is their natural convert. He must **never** confirm the harvest or the keystone; his fragment is the *folk-tragic* half-truth (the dead are held), not the mechanism. A late-game player could save him from his error — or be pulled into it.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — slides toward heresy; if Gravecallers reach him, the camps become a far-region cult-flashpoint. *Helped* — eased back from the brink; a saintly, knowledgeable ally. *Pushed* — becomes a tragic far-region "Breaking" instigator at the Crown.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Priest/Acolyte-like** (AC 11, ~33 HP; low-level divine and grief-magic; *Speak with Dead* if he falls fully to the cult). Solo danger: very low; he will not fight. Noncombat: his value is moral, social, and as a tragic mystery-mirror.
- **Tags:** [faction:three-thresholds, faction:gravecallers-adjacent, role:religious, role:harmed-by-failures, far-continent, major, mystery-mirror]

### CH-M4. "Old Crown" Mab — the ruin-guide who always comes back
- **Region/Base:** Crownmouth and the deep Ruin'd Crown.
- **Public Role:** The legendary ruin-guide — the one delver who has gone deepest into the Crown and returned, again and again, when no one else does.
- **Faction:** Independent (master ruin-guide); sells her skill to all, owes loyalty to none.
- **Political/Social Importance:** The single most valuable person at the Crown for anyone who actually wants to *enter the deep ruins and survive* — including a high-level player. Every faction courts her; she belongs to none.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A small, sinewy, grey-cropped old woman hung with salvaged charms and chalk, who looks at the greatest ruin in the world the way a shepherd looks at a familiar, dangerous hill.
- **Voice:** Laconic, dry, riddling; gives warnings as proverbs. "The Crown lets you in easy. Coming out's the toll. Mark your turns or it keeps you." Rarely explains; expects you to listen.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep walking the Crown — it's all she knows and all she loves — and keep coming back, which means never taking a client who'll get her killed by greed.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the Crown is changing — passages she's known for thirty years now "go wrong," the dead in the deep ruins move where they never did. Even she is starting to lose her certainty.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Clients with sense, not greed; and someone to confirm she's not mad — that the deep Crown really *is* waking, not just her old eyes failing.
- **What she can offer the player:** Survivable passage into the deepest catalogued ruins; the truest map of the Crown that exists (in her head); warnings that keep a high-level party alive.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Help her recover the body of a client the Crown "kept"; verify a changed passage; carry her hard-won knowledge out so it isn't lost when she finally doesn't come back.
- **What she knows:** The deep Ruin'd Crown better than any living soul; which vaults kill and which keep; that the deep dead are stirring as never before in her lifetime.
- **What she misunderstands:** She has no theory and doesn't want one — but her *observation* is razor-accurate: the Crown is waking. She is the campaign's most authoritative far-region witness to the deep ruins reactivating, purely from a guide's craft.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** She doesn't speak of rites — she speaks of the deep dead *moving*, which is the same thing seen from the bottom of the world's greatest ruin. The most chilling, concrete corroboration available.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Total practical mastery and zero reverence. The Crown is her workplace, her home, and (she suspects) her eventual grave.
- **Relationship Links:** Hired by **Hollin Vane** (warily — she doesn't trust his greed) and by **Warden Lyssa Crownmouth** (whom she respects); the only guide refugees in the camps will trust; **(DM)** her observations are the deep-ruin counterpart to Mossa Drenn's river-chart and Mab's tally back in the Reach — a network of unaligned witnesses to the spread.
- **Player-safe description:** The legendary old guide who walks the deepest Crown and lives.
- **DM-Only:** *(corroboration)* Mab is the **deep-ruin witness** that confirms, at the highest level, that the Concord's works are reactivating across the continent. But she is a *surface*-ruin guide — she has **never** been to (and does not know of) the buried Concord Deep or the Hollowmere keystone, and must never be allowed to "know the way" there. Her knowledge is of the *fallen capital*, not the living machine.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — keeps guiding until the Crown finally keeps her. *Helped* — the player's lifeline into the deepest ruins and a beloved, gruff ally. *Lost* — if she dies in the deep, the Crown becomes far deadlier and her irreplaceable map dies with her.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Scout/Veteran-like, expert** (AC 15, ~52 HP; expertise in ruin-survival, traps, stealth; not a frontline fighter but very hard to kill or trap). Solo danger: low offensively, but losing her in the deep is lethal *to the player*. Noncombat: she is a guide and a witness; hire and heed her.
- **Tags:** [role:travel-guide, role:scholar, role:ally, far-continent, major]
