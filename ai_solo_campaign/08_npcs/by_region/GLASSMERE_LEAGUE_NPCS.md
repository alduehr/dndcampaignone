## AI Use

NPC roster for **the Glassmere League** — a confederacy of free river-cities grown rich on the Glasswater, the continent's main trade artery; the **"real" urban Orrun** the player only glimpses past Caradril (Lvl 9–13). This is the **Ledger's true heartland** and a place of banks, guilds, civic intrigue, and old wealth — Caradril writ several times larger and older. High-density region: it is a *city-world*, so coverage is deep (5 major, 18 secondary, 47 minor).

**Player-Facing fields surfaceable; Secret/DM-Only fields earned in play.** Reuse the three established figures from `FAR_CONTINENT_NPCS.md` (Oren Glass, Sefwy Holt, "the Glass Ear") — full profiles here. **The "Glassmere League" is a pre-existing placeholder local power, NOT a new major faction** — its officers are civic/mercantile officials, not a new faction structure. The campaign's seven major factions are unchanged; here they have *reach* (the Ledger is strong, the Remnant has a great chapter, the Mourners have an old urban presence).

**No NPC knows the apex truth.** Death-rite failures are **barely a rumor here** — the League is far from the spreading edge and comfortably skeptical; "down-river superstition" is the prevailing attitude, which makes the League a place where the crisis is *denied by prosperity.* A handful of NPCs (memory-keepers, a worried banker, a Remnant scholar) take it seriously.

**Cultural voice:** confident, cosmopolitan, mercantile. League folk are proud of their freedom, their charters, their banks, and their distance from the "rough north." They speak of "the League," "the floor" (the exchange), "good paper" (credit), "the river feeds us." Politeness as a negotiating posture; condescension toward frontier folk that the worldly ones hide and the vulgar ones don't.

> Light anchors: **Glassmere** (the league-capital city, greatest of the river-cities), **the Floor** (its famous merchant-exchange), **the Three Bridges** (the city's banking quarter), **the Reliquary** (the Remnant's great League chapter-house), **Lowwater** (the poor riverside district), and the smaller league-cities of **Sennfort** and **Cairnwater** referenced as needed.

## MAJOR NPCs

### GL-M1. League-Factor Oren Glass — the League's trade-envoy
> Name note: "Glass" is the league-capital's namesake civic surname (like "Caradril" families) — common here; keep full names.
- **Region/Base:** Glassmere city; circuits the League and down-river to Caradril.
- **Public Role:** The Glassmere League's senior trade-envoy — the diplomat-merchant who speaks for the league-cities to the outside world, including the frontier.
- **Faction:** Glassmere League (civic/mercantile leadership — *not* one of the seven major factions; the League is a local confederacy power).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The face of the wider, richer Orrun. To the frontier player, Oren Glass *is* the League — the first real continental power to take notice of them.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A genial, immaculately dressed man of middle years with a politician's memory for names and a banker's eye for leverage, who makes the frontier feel small and the League feel inevitable.
- **Voice:** Warm, expansive, flattering, never quite committing; turns every conversation toward what the League can do *for* you (and get from you). "The League has always had room for useful friends. Tell me — what are you, exactly?"
- **Private Motivation:** Extend the League's commercial reach down-river — to fold the Verdance corridor and ultimately Caradril's charter-trade into the League's orbit, peacefully if possible.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the Cinder Ledger (whose true heartland is *in* the League's own cities) will out-maneuver the civic government from within; that the League's famous unity is more fragile than it looks.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Useful frontier agents and intelligence; a foot in Caradril's door; leverage against the Ledger's overreach.
- **What he can offer the player:** Introductions to the League's highest circles; League trade-charters and backing; the full resources of a continental power that finds the player useful.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Carry a League overture to Caradril's council; gather intelligence on the Ledger or a rival city; quietly undercut a Ledger position on the Floor.
- **What he knows:** The League's politics, the river-trade, and the wider continent's commerce; the Ledger's true scale (which alarms him); Caradril's strategic value.
- **What he misunderstands:** He sees the frontier purely as a *market and a buffer* — a poor, superstitious edge to be exploited and absorbed. He has no inkling the world's gravest crisis is centered there; he'd laugh at the idea.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Dismissive cosmopolitan skepticism — "frontier superstition, bad for trade if it spreads." He'd suppress the *rumor* to protect commerce before he'd ever investigate it.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Civic pride (the League's wealth flows on Concord-built canals and roads) without curiosity; ruins are infrastructure and salvage, not mystery.
- **Relationship Links:** Civic rival-and-uneasy-partner of **Banker-Magistra Sefwy Holt** (the bank vs. the city); contends with the **Cinder Ledger's** heartland power (and with **Wessel Crane** down-river); employs **"the Glass Ear"** for intelligence; courts Caradril's **Tidewater Council**.
- **Player-safe description:** The smooth, powerful envoy who speaks for the great river-league.
- **DM-Only:** *(political)* Oren Glass is the player's clearest demonstration that **the Cinder Ledger's true home is the League, not the frontier** — the Ledger the player fights in the Reach is a tendril of a continental organism rooted here. Glass is locked in a quiet struggle to keep the civic government from being captured by the Ledger he can't fully control. He is **not** evil and **not** a Court agent — a worldly power-broker whose blind spot is the frontier crisis.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the League's reach grows down-river off-screen; Caradril edges into its orbit. *Allied* — a continental patron whose backing transforms the player's reach. *Crossed* — a smiling, implacable opponent who can close the wider Orrun's doors.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble/Diplomat-like with vast resources** (AC 13, ~44 HP; commands money, agents, and civic guard, not a war-band). Solo danger: low in person, immense politically. Noncombat: this is pure intrigue — leverage, alliance, betrayal.

### GL-M2. Banker-Magistra Sefwy Holt — the League's merchant-bank
- **Region/Base:** The Three Bridges, Glassmere's banking quarter.
- **Public Role:** Magistra of the greatest of the League's merchant-banks — the financier whose credit moves the continent's trade and whose ambition rivals the Cinder Ledger's.
- **Faction:** Glassmere League (banking magnate); the **Ledger's chief rival**.
- **Political/Social Importance:** The one power that could undercut or absorb the Cinder Ledger — and thus the most strategically important ally (or enemy) the player can find against the faction squeezing the frontier.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A severe, brilliant woman of austere elegance who listens to a proposal the way a surgeon studies a patient, and prices risk in real time behind her eyes.
- **Voice:** Precise, cool, economical; speaks in terms of risk, return, and leverage; rare dry wit. "The Ledger thinks it owns the north. I think it has overextended. Both of us cannot be right." Says exactly what she means and no more.
- **Private Motivation:** Break the Cinder Ledger's relic-and-salt monopoly and seize the inland trade for her own bank — to make her house the continent's first power.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the Ledger's monopoly is too entrenched; that her aggressive expansion has over-leveraged her bank; that the frontier "trouble" the Ledger is exposed to could, if real, crater the relic-market she's also betting on.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Anyone who can damage the Ledger — proof of its abuses, disruption of its monopoly, intelligence from inside its operations.
- **What she can offer the player:** A continental financial power *aligned against the player's frontier enemy* — funding, credit, legal weight, and the leverage to actually hurt the Cinder Ledger where it's strongest.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Bring her proof of Ledger malfeasance (which the frontier player may *have*); disrupt a Ledger cargo or charter; turn a Ledger officer.
- **What she knows:** The continent's high finance; the Ledger's true structure and weak points; that the Ledger's relic-monopoly rests on a supply (frontier "old glass") she doesn't fully understand.
- **What she misunderstands:** She sees the frontier crisis only as **counterparty risk** — a threat to the relic-market's value, not a real catastrophe. She'd hedge against it, never investigate it.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Pure risk-calculation: does this make the relic-trade more valuable (scarcity) or worthless (panic)? She wants *data*, for her book, not for the world.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Asset-valuation, identical to the Ledger's — which is why the two are such mirror-rivals.
- **Relationship Links:** Arch-rival of the **Cinder Ledger** (Mother Calla Vyre, **Wessel Crane**); civic rival of **Oren Glass** (bank vs. city); employs agents like **Ondine Holt** (Verdance) and corresponds with anyone who can hurt the Ledger; **(DM)** the single most useful external lever against the frontier's chief faction.
- **Player-safe description:** The formidable banker whose ambition is to break the Cinder Ledger and rule the continent's trade.
- **DM-Only:** *(strategic lever)* Sefwy Holt is the campaign's **clean continental counterweight to the Cinder Ledger** — a frontier player armed with M3 proof of Ledger abuses can turn that proof into real continental leverage *through her.* She is amoral, not evil; allying with her trades one monopolist for another, a genuine choice. She must never learn the harvest truth; she'd try to *corner the market* on it.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the bank-vs-Ledger war grinds on off-screen; the relic-market wobbles. *Allied* — a powerful patron against the Ledger, at the price of empowering her. *Crossed* — she withdraws the one external lever that could break the frontier's enemy.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble/Magnate-like with resources** (AC 13, ~40 HP; commands bank-guards, agents, and credit). Solo danger: low in person, immense financially. Noncombat: leverage, proof, alliance — never a fight.
- **Tags:** [faction:glassmere-league, role:authority, role:faction-opponent, far-continent, major]

### GL-M3. Spy-Broker "the Glass Ear" — the League's information-master
- **Region/Base:** Glassmere city; everywhere and nowhere — works through cut-outs.
- **Public Role:** The League's pre-eminent information-broker — the unseen merchant of secrets whose network rivals (and trades with) Caradril's Hush.
- **Faction:** Independent (information-broker); sells to the League, the banks, the Ledger, and anyone who pays.
- **Political/Social Importance:** The continent's clearing-house of secrets; the fastest way for a frontier player to *learn what's really happening* up-river — and the fastest way to be sold out.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** Never the same person twice — a different soft-spoken go-between each time, each insisting they only "carry a message for a friend who values discretion."
- **Voice (of the cut-outs):** Mild, deferential, maddeningly indirect; every answer a question, every fact a commodity. "My principal might know such a thing. My principal might be persuaded to share it. The question, friend, is what you carry to trade."
- **Private Motivation (the true broker):** Know everything, owe nothing, be irreplaceable — to remain the indispensable hub no faction can do without and none can find.
- **Fear/Pressure:** Exposure (the Ear's whole power is anonymity); a rival network (the Hush, or a faction's spies) mapping the cut-out chain.
- **What it wants from outsiders:** New, exclusive intelligence — and the frontier player carries the rarest commodity on the continent: first-hand knowledge of the crisis no one up-river believes.
- **What it can offer the player:** Almost any secret on the continent, for a price; the location of a person, the contents of a deal, the weakness of a faction; a back-channel to anyone.
- **What it might ask the player to do:** Carry a sealed message; verify a rumor in person; trade frontier intelligence for League secrets.
- **What it knows:** Effectively everything that is *known* on the continent — which, crucially, does **not** include the harvest, because no one alive knows it to sell. The Ear can confirm what factions *do*, never what the Concord truly *was.*
- **What it misunderstands:** Nothing it would admit — but it has bought the "frontier failures" rumor cheap and shelved it as low-value superstition. It underestimates the one secret it can't source.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Commercial neutrality — it would *buy and resell* the rumor if a buyer emerged, and is faintly intrigued that no one up-river will pay for it.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Indifferent except as it touches who's buying what. Lore is only valuable if someone pays for it.
- **Relationship Links:** Trades and competes with Caradril's **the Bellman / the Hush**; sells to **Oren Glass**, **Sefwy Holt**, the **Ledger**, and the **Remnant** alike; **(DM)** a potential conduit by which the frontier crisis-intelligence could finally reach (and alarm) the wider continent.
- **Player-safe description:** The League's faceless master of secrets, reachable only through ever-changing intermediaries.
- **DM-Only:** *(network)* The Glass Ear is the player's tool for *information warfare at continental scale* — and a clean way to dramatize that **the apex truth is genuinely unknown**: even the being who knows everything knowable cannot sell the harvest, because no living source possesses it. The Ear's identity is deliberately undefined; never reveal a "true face" unless the campaign demands it.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — a recurring, neutral information-source. *Cultivated* — an invaluable (mercenary) intelligence ally. *Crossed* — the player's secrets become someone else's weapon.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — works through Spy/Assassin-like cut-outs** (no single stat block; cut-outs are **Spy-like**, AC 12, ~22 HP; the true broker is never in the room). Solo danger: low directly, high indirectly (betrayal, sold secrets). Noncombat: trade, never fight — fighting a cut-out tells you nothing.
- **Tags:** [role:criminal, role:rumor-spreader, role:scholar, far-continent, major]

### GL-M4. Reliquary-Lector Mareth Senn — the Remnant's great League chapter
> Name note: "Senn" (and the city "Sennfort") are common League surnames — **no kin** to Mother Sennet, Goodwife Sennet, or Senna Crale; keep full names.
- **Region/Base:** The Reliquary, the Concord Remnant's grand chapter-house in Glassmere.
- **Public Role:** Lector of the Remnant's wealthiest, oldest chapter — the scholarly heart of the order's *institutional* face, where the frontier chapters' findings are gathered, judged, and hoarded.
- **Faction:** Concord Remnant (senior institutional lector; peer of Sabine Ferrant, junior to Hollin Vane's field-command).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The Remnant's *brain* — where the order decides what its scattered chapters' discoveries mean and what to do with them. A frontier player's findings, sent up the chain, end up judged here.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A poised, formidably learned woman in the Remnant's grey-and-silver, surrounded by the greatest private collection of Concord relics on the continent, who treats knowledge as both treasure and weapon.
- **Voice:** Measured, erudite, quietly imperious; speaks of "the work," "the inheritance," "responsible stewardship." "The frontier finds things. We decide what they *mean.* That is the difference between a digger and a scholar."
- **Private Motivation:** Gather, control, and interpret all the Remnant's discoveries — and guide the order toward *reclaiming* the Concord's power "responsibly," with the institutional Remnant (not Hollin Vane's reckless field-expedition) in command.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That Hollin Vane's grandiose Crown expedition will seize the order's destiny (and its dangers) before the institution can vet it; that the frontier chapters are uncovering things that should be *contained*, not published; that the Ledger will buy the relics she means to study.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Frontier findings delivered to *her*, not to the field-zealots; capable agents to retrieve relics before the Ledger does; discretion.
- **What she can offer the player:** The deepest Concord scholarship on the continent (short of the Crown); Script and Old-Custodial expertise; the Remnant's institutional resources; honest *interpretation* of what the player has found — filtered through the order's self-serving idealism.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Carry a frontier relic or rubbing to the Reliquary unseen; assess whether a frontier chapter (Briss, Quorrin) is "going too far"; recover a relic from the Ledger.
- **What she knows:** Concord history and language at the highest scholarly level; the Remnant's true ambitions and internal factions (institution vs. Hollin Vane's field-command); the relic-trade's scholarly value.
- **What she misunderstands:** The same fatal error as all the Remnant — she believes the Concord's works are a *recoverable inheritance for good*, not a harvest engine. She is *cautious* about it (her instinct to "contain" is closer to right than Vane's "reclaim"), but she draws the wrong conclusion: contain to *control*, not to destroy.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Scholarly alarm and *containment instinct* — she correctly suspects the old works are reactivating and wants the knowledge controlled by the Remnant. Closer to the truth than most, still on the wrong side of it.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Reverent stewardship; the Reliquary is a temple to it.
- **Relationship Links:** Institutional peer of **Sabine Ferrant** (Caradril); receives reports up the chain from **Quorrin Vane**, **Lector Briss**, **Hadwin Vael**; **rival** of field-commander **Hollin Vane** over the order's direction; coldly opposed to the **Cinder Ledger's** relic-buying; **(DM)** the Remnant figure most likely to *correctly fear* the works while still wanting them.
- **Player-safe description:** The learned lector who heads the Remnant's grand chapter and guards the continent's greatest relic-collection.
- **DM-Only:** *(faction-internal lever)* Mareth Senn embodies the Remnant's **"contain vs. reclaim" schism** — she is the institutional caution against Hollin Vane's reckless ambition. A clever player can play the two against each other. She is the Remnant figure who could, shown the full truth, most plausibly turn toward *destruction* — but her pride and the order's purpose make her a hard convert. She must never confirm the harvest; her knowledge is deep history and language, not the live mechanism.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the Remnant's institution and field-command drift toward open schism. *Allied* — the player's best scholarly resource and a lever inside the Remnant. *Crossed* — the order's full institutional weight turns against the player.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Mage/Archivist-like** (AC 12, ~46 HP; high-level divination/abjuration utility, ward-craft, no battle-magic by preference). Solo danger: low; defended by Remnant wards and guards. Noncombat: scholarship, interpretation, faction-leverage.
- **Tags:** [faction:concord-remnant, role:scholar, role:memory-keeper, far-continent, major]

### GL-M5. Mother Wenna Lowwater — the old urban Mourners' eldest
> Name note: "Lowwater" is a district-surname (folk take the district's name) — common; keep full names.
- **Region/Base:** Lowwater, Glassmere's poor riverside district.
- **Public Role:** Eldest of the Mourners' Circle's old urban presence in the League — the grief-keeper of Glassmere's forgotten poor, in a city that worships money and ignores the dead.
- **Faction:** Mourners' Circle (urban eldest; the folk-faith's continental memory-keeper).
- **Political/Social Importance:** Small in the city's eyes, large in the campaign's — she is the **League node of the continent-wide Mourner old-songs network**, the folk-faith thread that quietly carries the truest fragments across Orrun.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A tiny, ancient woman with river-grey eyes and hands stained from grave-work, who tends Lowwater's pauper-dead with a dignity the rich quarters have forgotten.
- **Voice:** Soft, grave, song-laced; speaks in the Mourners' half-sung cadence and old refrains. "The League counts coins. We count the dead. Only one of those numbers ever comes due." Patient; unafraid of the powerful.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the old rites alive for the poor the city discards — and keep the Mourners' true memory (carried in the old songs) safe and passing, hand to hand, across the generations and the continent.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the city's wealth and the official faith will let the old folk-rites die; that the "down-river trouble" in the songs is coming true — and that the League's complacency means no one will be ready.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Respect for the dead and the old ways; news of the frontier (she *takes the failures seriously* when no one else in the League does); a hand to carry the songs on.
- **What she can offer the player:** The Mourners' continental network and lore; the **old songs**, which carry the truest folk-fragments of what the Concord did to the dead (M6-oblique — the *folk-memory* layer, not the mechanism); sanctuary among Lowwater's poor; an honest, unafraid ally in a city of liars.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Carry a song, a message, or a grave-token to a Mourner elder in another region; help the poor whose dead won't rest; honor a rite the city would mock.
- **What she knows:** The Mourners' old-songs and folk-history; the network of grief-keepers across Orrun (she corresponds, in songs and tokens, with Combe, Wend, Senna Crale, Onn Greenward, and the rest); that the failing dead are an *old* doom returning, sung of for a century.
- **What she misunderstands:** The old songs carry a *true fragment* — that "the grey hands took the dead before the roads fell" — but as folk-memory, oblique and unprovable, not as the harvest mechanism. She believes it; she cannot demonstrate it; she does not know the Hollow Court survives.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** The **most clear-eyed of any League NPC** — she has *expected* this for a century, because the songs warned of it. She is the city's lone believer.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Sorrowful knowing — the old songs name the Concord's works as the place "the grey hands" did their taking. She walks wide of them.
- **Relationship Links:** Continental Mourner-network node — corresponds (in songs/tokens) with **Mother Ezrith Combe** (Vale), **Grandmother Wend** (Hollowmere), **Senna Crale** (Marrowdowns), **Onn Greenward** (Hethewald), **Odila Cress** (Verdance), **Grave-Sister Onn Crale** (the Crown); pities the city's official **Three Thresholds** clergy; quietly defies **Oren Glass's** "bad for trade" suppression of the failure-rumors.
- **Player-safe description:** The ancient Mourner-eldest who keeps the old death-rites alive among Glassmere's forgotten poor.
- **DM-Only:** *(M6-oblique, network)* Wenna Lowwater is the **League anchor of the campaign-wide Mourner old-songs network** — the folk-faith thread that carries the truest (oblique, gated) fragment of the harvest-truth across the whole continent. She is the clean far-region proof that **the truth survives in folk-memory, not in scholarship or finance**, and that the Mourners are the campaign's quiet keepers of it. Keep her fragment oblique ("the grey hands took the dead"); she does **not** know the mechanism, the Hollow Court, or the keystone. She corroborates M6 at the folk layer and is a powerful, humane potential ally.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the old songs pass quietly on; the League stays complacent. *Helped* — a beloved, knowing ally and a key to the continental Mourner-network. *Lost* — if she dies untended, a strand of the old-songs memory frays.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Priest/Acolyte-like, frail** (AC 10, ~24 HP; folk grief-rites, *Gentle Repose*, *Bless*, low-level divine). Solo danger: none; she will never fight. Noncombat: her whole value is lore, network, and moral clarity.
- **Tags:** [faction:mourners-circle, role:religious, role:memory-keeper, role:ally, far-continent, major, mystery-folk-thread]
