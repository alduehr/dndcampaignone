## AI Use

NPC roster for **the Hethewald Free Holds** — a confederacy of reclusive forest free-holds and outlaw camps strung along the Hethe, the great eastern trade-river, in the vast Hethewood (Lvl 7–12). This is **the Tollwood writ large**: deep old-growth forest, forest-customs, river-tolls, outlaws, and deep Concord ruins. The campaign's Tollwood is the Hethewood's far-NW finger, so these people share a cultural root with Tollwood folk but are more independent, more various, and proud of governing themselves.

**Player-Facing surfaceable; Secret/DM-Only earned.** Reuse the two established figures (Hold-Speaker Bram Hethe, Forest-Mother Onn Greenward) — full profiles here. Onn Greenward is the **eastern node of the continent-wide Mourner old-songs network** (M6-oblique folk-thread). No NPC knows the apex truth. Death-rite failures register here as **the forest-customs "going wrong"** — the old bargains with the wood that kept the dead quiet are slipping, read through a folk-pagan lens rather than the official faith's.

**Cultural voice:** spare, wary, free-proud, rule-bound (like Tollwood but with more swagger). Hethewald folk say "the holds answer to no crown," "the wood's own law," "we keep the old bargains," "stay to the river or stay armed." Suspicious of outsiders, fiercely loyal once earned.

> Light anchors: **Hethemoot** (the largest free-hold, where the holds gather to council), **Greenward** (Onn's grove-village), **Tollreach** (a river-toll outlaw camp on the Hethe), **the Old Holds** (deep-wood Concord ruins).

## MAJOR NPCs

### HW-M1. Hold-Speaker Bram Hethe — voice of the free holds
> Name note: "Hethe" is the great forest/river's name, taken as a surname by its free folk — common; keep full names.
- **Region/Base:** Hethemoot, the great gathering-hold.
- **Public Role:** Elected Speaker of the Hethewald free-holds' moot — the closest thing the fiercely independent forest has to a leader, who *speaks for* the holds without ruling them.
- **Faction:** Independent (free-hold confederacy leader).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The player's gateway to the whole forest confederacy; nothing moves through the Hethewald with the holds' goodwill except through Bram, and against their goodwill not at all.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A broad, green-clad woodsman with a longbow he never seems to set down and a slow, weighing courtesy, who measures an outsider against the wood's own law before he'll trust a word.
- **Voice:** Spare, grave, proverb-laced; speaks for "the holds," rarely for himself. "The holds answer to no crown, friend. We answer to the wood, and the wood remembers." Few words, all weighed.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the holds free and the old forest-bargains kept — against the Ledger's logging-greed, the river-tolls' lawlessness, and any crown or league that would tame the wood.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the holds' unity (always fragile) will shatter under outside pressure; that the **forest-customs are failing** — the old bargains that kept the deep wood's dead and powers quiet are slipping, and no one knows why.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Respect for the wood's law; allies against the Ledger's encroachment; and, secretly, anyone who can tell him *why the old bargains are failing.*
- **What he can offer the player:** Safe passage and welcome through the whole confederacy; forest-guides, hold-sanctuary, and the holds' considerable woodland strength; honest forest-lore.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Drive off a Ledger logging-incursion; mediate a feud between holds; investigate a deep-wood ruin where the "bargains" are failing worst.
- **What he knows:** The forest, its holds, its feuds, and its old bargains; the Hethe's outlaw-tolls; the deep Old Holds (Concord ruins) the wood-folk shun.
- **What he misunderstands:** He frames the failing dead entirely through forest-paganism — "the old bargains are breaking, the wood's gone restless" — and connects it to the Concord ruins only as cursed places to avoid, never as the cause's source.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Grave alarm in the forest's own idiom — the bargains are slipping, the deep dead stir. A clean far-region corroboration through a *pagan/folk* lens distinct from the official faith and the Mourners.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Inherited dread — the Old Holds are forbidden ground where "the grey hands worked"; the holds keep their bargains partly to keep those places asleep (the Tollwood "Old Mast" logic, writ large).
- **Relationship Links:** Allied-in-faith with **Forest-Mother Onn Greenward** (the wood's grief-keeper); opposes the **Cinder Ledger's** logging arm (and **Factor Hesk Dorr's** eastern equivalent); wary of **Tollreach's** outlaw-tollers; kin-in-custom to Tollwood's **Goodwife Sennet** and **Brann the Guide** (the same forest-culture's NW finger).
- **Player-safe description:** The grave, free-proud Speaker of the eastern forest's independent holds.
- **DM-Only:** *(corroboration, folk-pagan)* Bram is the far-region proof that the crisis reads the **same everywhere but is understood differently** — here as failing *forest-bargains*, the pagan cousin of the Mourners' old songs and the official faith's failing rites. The Old Holds are surface-Concord ruins (like the Tollwood's gated sites), NOT the keystone. He must never learn the mechanism; his "grey hands" is the same oblique folk-fragment Coldhearth holds.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the holds hold, warily; Ledger pressure mounts off-screen. *Allied* — the forest confederacy as a powerful woodland ally. *Crossed* — the whole wood turns hostile; no safe passage east.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Scout/Veteran-like, archer** (AC 15, ~58 HP; longbow expertise, ambush tactics; commands many **Scout/Archer-like** hold-folk in the wood). Solo danger: high in the forest, low in the open. Noncombat: earn the wood's law; respect the bargains.
- **Tags:** [role:authority, role:ally, far-continent, major]

### HW-M2. Forest-Mother Onn Greenward — the wood's grief-keeper
- **Region/Base:** Greenward, her grove-village deep in the Hethewood.
- **Public Role:** Eldest grief-keeper of the Hethewald — the Mourners' Circle's eastern elder, who tends the forest-folk's dead by the old grove-rites.
- **Faction:** Mourners' Circle (eastern eldest); the **eastern node of the continental old-songs network**.
- **Political/Social Importance:** Small in the holds' politics, large in the campaign's — she carries the M6-oblique folk-truth at the forest's eastern edge and links the Hethewald to the continent-wide Mourner network.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A small, moss-grey woman wreathed in the smell of leaf-mould and woodsmoke, who tends the grove-graves with an ancient, unhurried tenderness and seems to listen to the trees.
- **Voice:** Soft, song-laced, forest-slow; speaks in grove-rite cadence and old refrains. "We give the dead to the wood and sing them down, and the wood keeps them — when the bargain holds. Lately... the wood is hungry." Patient, unafraid.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the grove-rites and the old songs alive, and keep the forest's dead at rest as the bargains slip — and pass the true memory on.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the grove-rites are failing (they are); that the deep wood is waking; that the songs' old warning — of the "grey hands" who took the dead before the roads fell — is coming true.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Respect for the dead and the grove; news from the wider Mourner network; a hand to carry the songs and the warning on.
- **What she can offer the player:** The forest's grief-lore and the **old songs** (the M6-oblique folk-fragment); grove-sanctuary; the eastern key to the continental Mourner-network; an honest, knowing ally.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Help a grove-grave that won't keep; carry a song or token to another elder; honor the bargains at a failing deep-wood site.
- **What she knows:** The grove-rites and old songs; the continental Mourner-network (she corresponds in songs/tokens with Combe, Wend, Senna Crale, Wenna Lowwater, the rest); that the failing dead are an *old* doom the songs warned of.
- **What she misunderstands:** Like all the network, she holds the true fragment ("the grey hands took the dead") as folk-memory, oblique and unprovable; she doesn't know the mechanism or that the harvesters survive.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Clear-eyed, sorrowful, *expectant* — the songs warned of this. One of the campaign's truest far-region witnesses, at the folk layer.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** The songs name the Old Holds as where "the grey hands worked"; she keeps wide of them and warns others.
- **Relationship Links:** Continental Mourner-network node — corresponds with **Mother Ezrith Combe** (Vale), **Grandmother Wend** (Hollowmere), **Senna Crale** (Marrowdowns), **Mother Wenna Lowwater** (Glassmere), **Grave-Sister Onn Crale** (the Crown); allied-in-faith with **Bram Hethe**; kin-in-custom to Tollwood's **Goodwife Sennet**.
- **Player-safe description:** The ancient grove-keeper who sings the eastern forest's dead to rest.
- **DM-Only:** *(M6-oblique, network)* Onn Greenward is the **eastern anchor of the continental old-songs network** — the same humane, gated folk-truth carried by every Mourner-eldest across Orrun. Keep her fragment oblique ("the grey hands"); she does not know the harvest, the Hollow Court, or the keystone. A clean, knowing potential ally and a thread that quietly proves the truth survives in song.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the songs pass on; the grove-rites keep failing. *Helped* — a beloved, knowing ally and a network-key. *Lost* — a strand of the eastern old-songs frays.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Druid/Priest-like, frail** (AC 11, ~30 HP; grove grief-rites, *Speak with Plants*, *Gentle Repose*, low-level nature/divine). Solo danger: none; will not fight. Noncombat: lore, network, moral clarity.
- **Tags:** [faction:mourners-circle, role:religious, role:memory-keeper, role:ally, far-continent, major, mystery-folk-thread]

### HW-M3. Toll-Lord "Greenfinger" Maddoc — the Hethe's outlaw-toller
> Name note: "Maddoc" is distinct from the "Maddox" outlaw-name family (Eel, Carp, Crow) — keep spelling.
- **Region/Base:** Tollreach, the outlaw river-toll camp on the Hethe.
- **Public Role:** The most powerful of the Hethe's outlaw toll-lords — who taxes the eastern trade-river from a hidden camp the holds tolerate and the merchants curse.
- **Faction:** Independent (outlaw river-tolls); a grey power, like Tollwood's Tollmen writ large.
- **Political/Social Importance:** Controls a stretch of the continent's eastern trade-artery; the player must deal with him to move freely on the Hethe, and he's a turnable grey contact (Cady Renn's eastern cousin in kind).
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A lean, green-cloaked outlaw with a longbow and an easy grin, who steps from the trees to "discuss the river's toll" with the manners of a host and the eyes of a wolf.
- **Voice:** Charming, theatrical, mock-courteous; robs you politely. "The Hethe's a free river, friend — free for those who pay its keepers. Shall we discuss the rate, or the alternative?" Genuinely amused; not gratuitously cruel.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep his stretch of the river his, get rich, and stay one step ahead of the merchant-guilds, the holds' patience, and the rival toll-lords — ideally without becoming the murderer his trade tempts him toward.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the Ledger and the merchant-guilds will hire force enough to clear him; that the holds will withdraw their tolerance; that his own crueler lieutenants will turn the tolls to slaughter.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** A cut, a clever scheme, or a strong arm — and amusement; he respects nerve and wit.
- **What he can offer the player:** Safe passage on his river-stretch, the outlaw-network's intelligence, smuggling routes, and a turnable grey ally against the Ledger.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Help him rob a Ledger barge; deal with a rival toll-lord; carry contraband the guilds are watching for.
- **What he knows:** The Hethe's traffic, hidden channels, and outlaw-network; who ships what; the deep-wood routes the merchants don't.
- **What he misunderstands:** He treats the deep-wood ruins purely as good hiding-places and dismisses the "bargains failing" as the holds' superstition — until a deep-wood camp of his goes "wrong" and he stops laughing.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Mercenary avoidance shading into unease — he relocates camps away from "bad wood," and is the player's grey-network read on *which* deep-wood is going wrong.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Opportunistic (good hideouts) until they turn dangerous; then superstitious avoidance.
- **Relationship Links:** Tolerated (warily) by **Bram Hethe**; rival of other Hethe toll-lords; preys on the **Cinder Ledger's** eastern cargo (and **Factor Hesk Dorr's** logging-trains); his crueler lieutenant **Skell-the-Younger** (below) strains his control; kin-in-kind to Tollwood's **Cady Renn** / **Skell**.
- **Player-safe description:** The charming, dangerous outlaw-lord who taxes the great eastern river.
- **DM-Only:** *(turnable grey)* Greenfinger is the Hethewald's Cady Renn — a robber with a line he hasn't crossed, turnable by a player who offers a better game. His cruel lieutenant is the dark mirror (the Skell role). He's also a useful grey-network witness to the deep-wood's "going wrong." Not a faction; a local outlaw power.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the tolls go on; if his cruel lieutenant rises, the river-robbery turns murderous. *Turned* — a roguish ally and a free river. *Opposed* — a clever, deadly forest enemy.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Scout/Bandit-Captain-like, archer** (AC 15, ~50 HP; longbow ambush, commands 8–12 **Bandit/Scout-like** outlaws). Solo danger: high in ambush, moderate face-to-face. Noncombat: pay, charm, out-scheme, or split him from his lieutenant.
- **Tags:** [role:criminal, role:outlaw, role:trade-contact, far-continent, major]

Problem: wants the tolls turned murderous and the lordship for himself. Knows: the camp's strength; the cruel routes. Link: Greenfinger's second and rival. - **[independent] Smuggler-Wife Doll Tull** — *(common Tull)* Runs contraband past the guilds. *A fast-talking, fearless smuggler-matron.* Trait: charming, mercenary, loyal to coin. Problem: the guilds and rival tollers squeeze her. Knows: the smuggling routes; who buys what. Link: deals with Greenfinger. Enters: a smuggling contact; a black-market in.
- **[Cinder Ledger] Cutter-Captain Vask Dorr** — *(kin to Hesk Dorr; the logging-camp guard)* Protects the Ledger's timber-trains. *A hard, underpaid camp-guard captain.* Trait: company-loyal but cracking. Problem: the holds and outlaws both hunt his trains; the deep-wood terrifies his men. Knows: the logging-camps; the deep-wood's "wrongness" (turnable witness). Link: Hesk Dorr's man. Enters: a logging-camp scene; a turnable guard.

Problem: his rites fail worst in the Hethewald. Knows: the official faith's forest failures (M1/M5). Link: defers to the folk-Mourners. Enters: a faith scene; an outsider-priest witness.
- **[independent] Hold-Feud Matriarch Senna Briar** — *(takes the bramble as surname)* Head of a hold feuding with Greenward. *A proud, vengeful elder nursing an old grudge.* Trait: long memory for wrongs. Problem: her feud blinds her to the real crisis. Knows: the holds' deep feuds; a false-lead grievance. Link: rival of Doss Greenward. Enters: a feud-mediation hook; a fair red-herring.
