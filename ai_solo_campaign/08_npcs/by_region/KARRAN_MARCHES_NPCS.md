## AI Use

NPC roster for **the Karran Marches** — a jagged outlaw-and-mining frontier walling the northeast in the Karran Teeth, a land of ungoverned wildland and petty warlord holds (Lvl 9–14). This is **a second, harsher frontier mirroring the Sundering Reach** — but where the Reach is fen and quiet decay, the Karran is cold stone, mines, feuds, and the open rule of the strong. Useful as a place of mercenaries, ore, outlaw-holds, and a rough mirror of the player's home frontier.

**Player-Facing surfaceable; Secret/DM-Only earned.** Reuse the two established figures (Warlord "Iron" Brask, Ore-Factor Mully Karr) — full profiles here. **No NPC knows the apex truth.** The Karran has scattered Concord ruins (the old empire reached even here, thinly), so death-rite failures register — read through a brutal frontier lens as "the old dead in the deep mines and old forts won't stay down." The Gravecallers have a plausible toehold here (a lawless frontier with stirring dead).

**Cultural voice:** hard, blunt, strength-respecting, feud-bound. Karran folk speak of "the holds," "the strong hand," "the deep dark" (mines), "old iron" (Concord ruins), "the feud." No courtesy wasted; respect for strength and straight dealing; contempt for soft southern law.

> Light anchors: **Brask's Hold** (Iron Brask's fortress-town, the largest hold), **Karran-Gate** (a mining-and-mercenary town at the range's mouth), **the Deep Cuts** (the great ore-mines), **the Old Iron forts** (scattered Concord ruins in the peaks).

## MAJOR NPCs

### KM-M1. Warlord "Iron" Brask — the strongest petty warlord
> Name note: **no kin** to War-Rider Captain Borr-Sai of the steppe or the "Brask Tarn" pass-warden of the Heartlands — common hard-frontier name; keep full names.
- **Region/Base:** Brask's Hold, his fortress-town at the range's heart.
- **Public Role:** The strongest of the Karran's petty warlords — "Iron" Brask, who holds the largest fortress, the richest mines, and the deadliest mercenary band in the Marches.
- **Faction:** Independent (petty warlord).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The closest thing the ungoverned Karran has to a ruler; the player's gateway to the mining-frontier's holds, ore, and mercenaries, and a rough mirror of frontier power-by-strength.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A massive, scarred warlord in battered Concord-salvage plate, with a fortress at his back and a mercenary host at his call, who respects exactly one thing: strength backed by straight dealing.
- **Voice:** Blunt, hard, contemptuous of weakness, oddly honest; says what he means and expects the same. "The Marches have no law but the strong hand, and mine's the strongest. I don't lie and I don't forgive a lie. Deal straight, fight straight, and we'll get on." Brutal, but not treacherous.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep his hold the strongest, his mines the richest, and his word worth fearing — and hold the Marches against the rival warlords, the southern factions' creeping reach, and the deep dark's growing wrongness.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That a coalition of rival warlords (or the Ledger's gold) will break him; that the **deep mines and old forts are "going wrong"** — his diggers come back changed, the old dead in the Old Iron forts stir; that whatever's in the deep is beyond even his strong hand.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Strength he can use and trust — mercenaries, a champion, a straight-dealing agent; he pays well and keeps his word.
- **What he can offer the player:** The Karran's mercenary strength, ore-wealth, and hold-sanctuary; straight-dealing patronage; a rough frontier base and a mirror-ally.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Break a rival warlord; clear a "gone-wrong" deep mine or Old Iron fort; champion his hold in a feud.
- **What he knows:** The Marches' warlords, mines, and feuds; the Old Iron forts (Concord ruins) the holds shun; that the deep dark is "going wrong" — diggers changed, old dead stirring.
- **What he misunderstands:** He frames the deep-wrongness as "bad ground and old curses" — a frontier hazard to be fought or avoided — with no notion it's part of a continental crisis. A harder, prouder Dredgemaster Vole.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Grim, practical alarm — "the deep dead won't stay down, the old forts have gone bad" — a far-region corroboration through a *brutal mining-frontier* lens. He'd send armed men, not scholars.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** The Old Iron forts are salvage *and* hazard — he strips them for plate and ore but his men dread the deepest, which "have gone bad."
- **Relationship Links:** Rival of the other Karran warlords; employs **Ore-Factor Mully Karr** and sells through her; resists the **Cinder Ledger's** gold and the **Concord Remnant's** prying at the Old Iron forts; **(DM)** a strong-hand witness to the deep going wrong; a rough mirror of **Dredgemaster Osrin Vole** (Harrowgast).
- **Player-safe description:** The iron-handed warlord who holds the strongest fortress in the northeast frontier.
- **DM-Only:** *(corroboration via mining-frontier; Reach-mirror)* "Iron" Brask frames the crisis through the **Karran's deep mines and Old Iron forts** — the dead stirring in scattered Concord ruins at the far NE. These are thin, peripheral Concord works (the empire reached even here), NOT the keystone. He's a straight-dealing potential patron and a rough mirror of the player's home frontier. He knows no apex truth; keep the deep-wrongness unexplained.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — holds the Marches by strength; the deep-wrongness and feuds worsen off-screen. *Allied* — a straight-dealing warlord-patron and a frontier base. *Crossed* — a deadly, honest enemy with a fortress and a host.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 — Warlord/Champion-like, boss-class** (AC 18 salvage-plate, ~100 HP; greatsword, commands a mercenary host of **Veteran/Berserker-like** fighters). Solo danger: extreme in his hold; high alone. Noncombat: deal straight; earn him by strength and honesty.
- **Tags:** [role:authority, role:antagonist, role:ally, boss-class, far-continent, major]

### KM-M2. Ore-Factor Mully Karr — the frontier metals-trader
> Name note: "Karr" is a common frontier name (cf. Nessa Coyle's buyers, Doss Karr of the Crown) — keep full names.
- **Region/Base:** Karran-Gate and Brask's Hold; the mines and the trade-road south.
- **Public Role:** The Marches' foremost metals-trader — who buys the warlords' iron and "old iron" salvage and sells it south, the commercial pulse of the mining-frontier.
- **Faction:** Independent (metals-trader); Ledger-courted.
- **Political/Social Importance:** Controls the Karran's trade-flow and is the player's likeliest practical contact — and a turnable source on the warlords, the mines, and the "old iron" relics seeping south.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A sharp, leather-clad woman with an assayer's eye and a hard bargainer's smile, who weighs ore and people with the same fast, fair-but-ruthless judgment.
- **Voice:** Brisk, blunt, trade-shrewd, dryly funny; talks weights, grades, and prices. "Iron's iron, friend — I pay for the grade, not the story. Now the *old* iron, the Concord salvage... that I pay strange prices for, and don't ask why it's strange." Fair, mercenary, watchful.
- **Private Motivation:** Master the Karran's metals-trade and get rich — including the lucrative, dangerous "old iron" (Concord salvage, some of it Remembrance-relic) the warlords' deep mines turn up.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the Cinder Ledger will swallow her trade; that the warlords' feuds will cut her routes; that the "old iron" she trades is "going wrong" — some pieces "hum," and her deep-mine suppliers come back changed.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Reliable guards, buyers, and intelligence; muscle for her caravans against the feuds and raiders.
- **What she can offer the player:** The Karran's ore and salvage trade; "old iron" (Concord relics, oblique); routes south; an honest-mercenary read on the warlords and mines.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Guard a metals-caravan; assess a "gone-wrong" deep mine; broker between feuding warlords; move "old iron" south discreetly.
- **What she knows:** The Karran's metals-trade, mines, and warlord-politics; the "old iron" salvage (some of it Remembrance-relic, which she doesn't understand); which deep mines have "gone wrong."
- **What she misunderstands:** She treats the "humming old iron" as merely valuable-and-strange contraband and the deep-mine sickness as bad ground — no notion of Remembrance, the harvest, or the Concord ruins she's profiting from.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Mercenary unease — she avoids the worst "old iron" and notes which deep mines turn out changed men; an oblique M2/M3 corroboration through the metals-trade.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Pure salvage-appraisal; the Old Iron forts are her richest, strangest stock.
- **Relationship Links:** Buys from **"Iron" Brask** and the warlords; courted (and squeezed) by the **Cinder Ledger's** northern arm; her "old iron" interests the **Concord Remnant**; her deep-mine "wrongness" mirrors **Mully Karr ↔ Nessa Coyle/Osrin Vole** (Harrowgast); **(DM)** an unwitting harvester-relic conduit at the NE.
- **Player-safe description:** The sharp metals-trader who runs the northeast frontier's ore-and-salvage trade.
- **DM-Only:** *(relic-supply, oblique; trade-pulse)* Mully Karr is the Karran's **"old iron"/Concord-salvage conduit** — proof the Remembrance-relic phenomenon reaches even the far NE, here as "humming old iron" from the deep mines and Old Iron forts. She's a clean practical contact, a turnable source, and an oblique M2/M3 corroboration. She knows no meaning; keep the "old iron" oblique.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the metals-trade trickles on, the "old iron" seeping south. *Helped* — a fair, useful trade-ally and relic-source. *Crossed* — she withdraws the frontier's trade-access and warns the holds against the player.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Guard/Veteran-like with resources** (AC 14, ~42 HP; commands caravan-guards and toughs). Solo danger: moderate. Noncombat: trade, guard-work, intelligence.
- **Tags:** [role:trade-contact, role:criminal, role:exploiting-failures, far-continent, major]

### KM-M3. Reclaimer-Captain Wenna Stone — the Remnant's hard-frontier expedition
> Name note: takes the peaks as surname; common Karran custom. Distinct from any "Stair/Strand" name.
- **Region/Base:** The Old Iron forts and Karran-Gate; the Remnant's NE expedition.
- **Public Role:** Captain of the Concord Remnant's Karran expedition — a hard, soldierly scholar leading the order's risky hunt for the Old Iron forts' relics in warlord country.
- **Faction:** Concord Remnant (field-captain; the order's NE reach).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The Remnant's presence at the far NE, and the player's likeliest Remnant contact here — a tougher, more pragmatic scholar than the soft southern lectors, operating in lawless country.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A weather-hardened woman in scholar's grey over hard-traveling leather, more soldier than sage in bearing, who treats the deadly Old Iron forts as a campaign to be won.
- **Voice:** Clipped, pragmatic, scholar-soldier; little of the southern Remnant's idealism, much of its hunger. "The forts are full of the old work, Captain, and full of dead that won't lie still. I lose people every season. But what's *in* them is worth it. The order says so." Hard, driven, tiring of the cost.
- **Private Motivation:** Recover the Old Iron forts' relics for the Remnant before the warlords strip them or the Ledger buys them — and prove the NE expedition worth its mounting cost in lives.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the forts' stirring dead will overwhelm her teams; that the order's leadership (Mareth Senn, Hollin Vane) is spending her people for relics they'll just hoard; that what she's recovering is something that should be *feared*, not catalogued.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Capable hands for the deadly forts; intelligence on the warlords; relief from the order's reckless quotas.
- **What she can offer the player:** Concord Script and relic-lore; the Old Iron forts' (dangerous) access; a hard, honest Remnant scholar's read — closer to disillusionment than her southern peers.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Help clear a fort's stirring dead; recover a specific relic; carry her doubts up the chain to the order's leadership.
- **What she knows:** Concord Script and the Old Iron forts; the Remnant's NE aims and its mounting cost; that the forts' dead stir worse each season.
- **What she misunderstands:** Like all the Remnant, she believes the works are a recoverable inheritance — but the *cost* is eroding her faith; she's the rare Remnant scholar half-ready to conclude the works should be feared, making her turnable.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Soldier's grim recognition — the forts' dead stir, her people die — closer than her southern peers to *fearing* rather than coveting the works. A turnable Remnant conscience at the far NE.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Hungry, but increasingly haunted; the Old Iron forts have cost her too much.
- **Relationship Links:** Serves the Remnant's chain (up to **Mareth Senn**, Glassmere); buys fort-access (warily) from **"Iron" Brask**; rivals **Mully Karr's** salvage; **(DM)** the Remnant figure most worn-down toward doubt by the forts' real danger — a turnable conscience.
- **Player-safe description:** The hard-bitten Remnant captain leading the order's deadly expedition into the frontier's old forts.
- **DM-Only:** *(turnable Remnant conscience)* Wenna Stone is the Remnant's **most disillusion-prone field officer** — ground down by the Old Iron forts' real cost toward fearing the works, where her southern peers covet them. A frontier player who shows her the truth could turn her. The Old Iron forts are peripheral Concord ruins, NOT the keystone. She knows no apex truth yet.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — keeps spending her people on the forts. *Allied* — a hard, knowledgeable Remnant ally and a turnable conscience. *Disillusioned* — a defector who could turn the NE expedition against the order's leadership.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Veteran/War-Mage-like** (AC 16, ~50 HP; some Concord-attuned utility-magic, soldierly fighting, commands **Guard/Scout-like** expedition-hands). Solo danger: moderate-high (capable, with troops). Noncombat: scholarship, fort-access, a turnable conscience.
- **Tags:** [faction:concord-remnant, role:scholar, role:faction-opponent, far-continent, major, turnable]

Problem: the dead won't keep; the holds barely heed her. Knows: which dead won't stay down (M5, far-NE); a thin thread to the old-songs network. Link: kin-in-faith to the network (distant). - **[independent] Fort-Guide "Old Iron" Doss** — A veteran who survives the Old Iron forts. *A scarred, laconic survivor who guides delvers into the forts and back.* Trait: trusts caution over courage. Problem: even he's losing the forts to the stirring dead. Knows: the Old Iron forts' insides (M2/M6 fragments); a living warning. Link: hired by Brask, the Remnant, and Mully Karr alike. Enters: a fort-delve guide; an M-line clue and an M10-style omen.
- **[independent] Pass-Toll Warlord Hessa Teeth** — *(takes the range as surname; a minor warlord)* Holds a fortified toll-gate on the chief pass *out* of the Marches toward the settled south, taxing every caravan, mercenary band, and refugee that crosses. *A lean, scar-cheeked woman in mismatched salvage-mail who rules her gate with a crossbow and a fair-but-merciless tariff.* Trait: scrupulous greed; keeps her word and her toll exactly. Problem: rival warlords covet her gate, and the southbound refugees fleeing the "going-wrong" deep are more than her gate can process. Knows: who comes and goes between the Marches and the south; which holds are failing and sending their people out (a population-flow witness). Link: a lesser warlord wary of **"Iron" Brask**; taxes **Mully Karr's** ore-caravans; rival of **"Red" Karr**. Enters: a pass-crossing/toll scene; the player's gateway between the Karran and the settled south; a turnable lesser-warlord lever. **(DM)** The refugees backing up at her gate are a quiet, concrete sign the deep-wrongness is driving folk out — corroboration with no theory attached.
