## AI Use

NPC roster for **the Sunmark** — a warm southern land of sacred groves and grove-keeper tribes in the Sunmark Wilds (S, between the southern plains and the Greatspine; Lvl 8–13). This is a **gentler, warmer "other" south** with a *living old faith*: the grove-tribes keep an ancient, vital nature-religion that long predates and sits apart from the Concord, the Three Thresholds, and the Mourners. The campaign's most peaceful, lush, non-death-haunted region — useful as a place of green sanctuary, living old faith, and a southern contrast to the cold ruin-frontier.

**Player-Facing surfaceable; Secret/DM-Only earned.** Reuse the two established figures (Grove-Keeper Sael Sunmark, Sun-Singer Doll) — full profiles here. **No NPC knows the apex truth.** The Sunmark's living grove-faith *genuinely tends its dead well* (the grove-rites still hold), so the crisis is **barely present** here — the rare faintest sign read as "the southern groves' deep memory stirring." The Sunmark is a place where the player sees a faith that *works*, a poignant contrast to the failing north.

**Cultural voice:** warm, gentle, grove-imaged, reverent, slow. Sunmark folk speak of "the groves," "the green," "the grove-keepers," "the sun and the soil," "the old green memory." Hospitable, peaceable, slow to anger, wary of those who'd cut or take from the sacred groves. A living, confident faith — not the north's frightened denial.

> Light anchors: **the Great Grove at Sunhollow** (the tribes' holy heart and gathering-place), **the Grove-Camps** (the grove-tribes' settlements), **the Sunward Wilds** (the deep sacred forest), **the Green Roads** (pilgrim-and-trade paths through the groves).

## MAJOR NPCs

### SK-M1. Grove-Keeper Sael Sunmark — guardian of the Great Grove
> Name note: "Sael" is a given-name (cf. the distinct northern Saels — Domic, Brunt, Goodman); "Sunmark" is taken from the land. **No kin** to northern Saels; keep full name.
- **Region/Base:** The Great Grove at Sunhollow; circuits the grove-camps.
- **Public Role:** Eldest Grove-Keeper — chief guardian of the Great Grove and spiritual head of the grove-tribes, who keeps the living old faith and speaks for the green.
- **Faction:** Independent (grove-tribe spiritual leader; a living ancient nature-faith).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The player's gateway to the grove-tribes and their living faith; without the Grove-Keeper's welcome the sacred wilds are closed, with it a green sanctuary and an ally of a faith that still *works.*
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A serene, sun-warmed elder crowned in living leaf, who moves through the Great Grove as through a beloved home and greets a respectful stranger with deep, unhurried welcome — and a clear eye for those who'd take from the green.
- **Voice:** Warm, slow, grove-imaged, reverent; speaks of the green as kin. "The groves remember, traveler — every leaf, every grave, every name we give the green. In the north, they say, the dead grow restless. Here the green still holds them. Come, walk gently, and be welcome." Gentle, confident, unafraid.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the Great Grove and the living faith whole and vital — against the southern plains' creeping cut-and-trade, the factions' covetous reach, and the faint stirring in the groves' deep memory.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the settled south (and the Ledger, the Remnant) will cut, take, or defile the sacred groves; that the faint wrongness the Sun-Singers sense in the groves' "deep memory" is the north's failing reaching even the living green.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Respect for the groves and the green; a hand against those who'd cut or defile them; and, from a northern guest, the truth of "the restless dead" — to know if the living faith can still hold against it.
- **What he can offer the player:** Green sanctuary and grove-welcome; the grove-faith's living lore and rites (a faith that *works* — rare and precious); grove-guides, healing, and the tribes' peaceable strength; a poignant contrast to the failing north.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Drive off grove-cutters or relic-takers; honor a grove-rite; investigate the faint "deep memory" stirring before it touches the living green.
- **What he knows:** The groves, the tribes, and the living faith; the southern plains' encroachment; that the Sun-Singers sense a faint, distant wrongness — "the green's deep memory stirring."
- **What he misunderstands:** He frames the faint stirring as the groves' own "deep memory" troubling — a living-faith idiom — and the north's crisis as a *northern* failing of inferior rites; he doesn't grasp it as one continental affliction that the living green has merely, so far, withstood.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Gentle, knowing concern from a position of *strength* — "the north's rites fail; ours still hold; but the green's deep memory stirs, and I would know why." The one far-region voice speaking from a faith that *works* — a powerful, hopeful contrast.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** The grove-faith long predates and stands *apart from* the Concord; its ruins are "the stone-folk's dead works" the groves have grown over and reclaimed — pitied, not revered.
- **Relationship Links:** Spiritual head of the grove-tribes; relies on the seer **Sun-Singer Doll**; resists the **Cinder Ledger's** and **southern plains'** cutting; the **Concord Remnant** covets the groves' over-grown ruins; **(DM)** the rare far-region faith that *still works*, a hopeful mirror to the broken north.
- **Player-safe description:** The serene grove-keeper who guards the south's Great Grove and its living old faith.
- **DM-Only:** *(living-faith contrast — hopeful)* Grove-Keeper Sael is the campaign's **faith-that-works** — the living green still holds its dead where the Concord's stone rites fail. This is a deliberate, hopeful contrast and a thematic hint that *proper, consensual rites for the dead are possible* — relevant to the endgame's "transform the harvest into something just" path, kept very oblique. The grove-faith predates and stands apart from the Concord; keep the groves clean of Concord-keystone connection. He knows no apex truth; the "deep memory stirring" is a faint living-faith perception, never the mechanism.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the groves hold; the plains' cutting and the faint stirring press on off-screen. *Allied* — a green sanctuary, a knowing ally, and a glimpse of a faith that works. *Crossed* (the groves defiled) — the peaceable tribes turn the living wilds against the defiler.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Druid/Priest-like (potent)** (AC 13, ~48 HP; living grove-magic, *Speak with Plants*, *Plant Growth*, *Gentle Repose*, mid-level nature-divine; defends the grove fiercely if forced). Solo danger: low unless the groves are attacked, then high (the wilds answer him). Noncombat: respect the green; his value is sanctuary, living lore, and hope.
- **Tags:** [role:authority, role:religious, role:ally, far-continent, major, mystery-hopeful-contrast]

### SK-M2. Sun-Singer Doll — the grove-tribes' seer
> Name note: a grove given-name (cf. the many distinct northern "Dolls" — minor color-NPCs); this is the established far-continent figure, full profile here. No kin.
- **Region/Base:** The Sunward Wilds and the Great Grove; keeps the deep-grove vigils.
- **Public Role:** The most honored Sun-Singer — the grove-tribes' seer who sings the groves' deep memory, tends the green's oldest places, and counsels the Grove-Keeper.
- **Faction:** Independent (grove seer); kin-in-spirit (oblique, by parallel) to the Mourners' grief-keeping.
- **Political/Social Importance:** The grove-faith's spiritual depth and the one figure who *senses* the faint far stirring — the Sunmark's oblique early-perception, and a southern echo of the Mourners' role through an entirely living faith.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A leaf-crowned singer with bark-brown skin and green-flecked eyes, who seems half-rooted in the grove, and sings the green's memory in a voice like wind through leaves.
- **Voice:** Sung, gentle, green-imaged, deep-rooted; speaks in grove-memory and growing-metaphor. "The green remembers all who lie in it, northern one — names and griefs a thousand years deep. Of late... a far root aches. Something tugs at the deep green memory, from your cold lands. The groves hold. But I sing, and I listen." Serene, far-perceiving, kind.
- **Private Motivation:** Sing the groves' deep memory truly and keep the green's old roots whole — and understand the faint far ache troubling the deepest grove-vigils.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the "far root-ache" from the north is the failing reaching even the living green; that the groves' deep memory — which holds a thousand years of the dead — could itself be "tugged at"; that she alone senses how far the wrongness reaches.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** A northern guest's truth of "the restless dead," to test against the groves' deep memory — she senses they share a root.
- **What she can offer the player:** The grove-faith's deep memory-lore; an oblique, living-faith read on the crisis ("a far root aches" — a genuine far-perception of the deep pattern, kept very oblique); grove-craft and the green's welcome.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Carry the groves' warning to the north; help her vigil at a troubled deep-grove; tell her, truly, what the cold lands' dead do.
- **What she knows:** The groves' deep memory and the living faith's oldest lore; that a "far root aches" from the north, tugging at the deep green memory; that the grove-rites still hold the dead (and why — the living faith's strength).
- **What she misunderstands:** Her "far root-ache" is the crisis perceived through grove-cosmology — a genuine sensing of the deep pattern (the harvest's pull on the dead) without any framework to name it; she has no concept of the harvest, the Concord, or the Quiet Country.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Sung, rooted concern — "a far root aches; the deep green memory is tugged" — the Sunmark's oblique living-faith perception of the same crisis, from a faith that holds. The most *hopeful* and the most poetic far-region read.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** The grove-faith predates the Concord; its over-grown ruins are "old stone the green reclaimed," and she sings their dead to rest where the Concord's own rites failed them — a quietly profound act.
- **Relationship Links:** Counsels **Grove-Keeper Sael Sunmark**; honored across the tribes; **(DM)** her "far root-ache" is, like the steppe's "thinning sky" and the Mourners' old songs, an independent far perception of the crisis — here from a *living, working* faith, the most hopeful of all.
- **Player-safe description:** The honored grove-seer who sings the south's deep green memory and tends its oldest places.
- **DM-Only:** *(oblique perception + hopeful contrast — handle with care)* Sun-Singer Doll *perceives* the crisis as "a far root-ache tugging the deep green memory" — a deeply-oblique far-echo of the harvest's pull on the dead, reached through a *living, working* grove-faith. With Grove-Keeper Sael she is the campaign's clearest hint that **rites for the dead can still hold** — a hopeful thread toward the endgame's "just/consensual" path, kept extremely oblique. Never let her name the harvest, the Quiet Country, or the Court. A clean, serene, humane ally and the south's most hopeful voice.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — sings the deep memory; the far root-ache deepens. *Helped* — a serene, knowing ally and an oblique, hopeful witness. *Lost* — a living-faith perception of the truth is silenced.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Druid/Diviner-like** (AC 12, ~34 HP; deep-memory divination, *Speak with Plants*, low-mid nature-magic; not a fighter). Solo danger: low. Noncombat: memory-lore, the oblique hopeful read, the green's welcome.
- **Tags:** [role:religious, role:memory-keeper, role:ally, far-continent, major, mystery-hopeful-echo]

### SK-M3. Plains-Factor Doss Sunward — the southern cut-and-trade lord
> Name note: takes the wilds as surname; he is a *settled-plains* man working the grove-edge, distinct from the grove-tribes. Common "Doss" given-name.
- **Region/Base:** The Green Roads and the southern plains' edge; the grove-edge trade.
- **Public Role:** The foremost settled-plains factor working the Sunmark's edge — who buys the groves' rare resin, timber, and herbs, and presses the grove-tribes to *cut and sell* more.
- **Faction:** Independent (settled-plains trade-lord); Ledger-allied.
- **Political/Social Importance:** The chief threat *to* the groves and thus the player's likeliest source of conflict here — the settled world's covetous reach into the living green, and a turnable-or-opposable trade-power.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A prosperous, smooth plains-merchant in southern linen, all warmth and reason, who speaks of the groves as "resources the tribes waste" with a smile that never reaches his eyes.
- **Voice:** Smooth, reasonable, persuasive, faintly contemptuous of the tribes; dresses greed as progress. "The groves are *wealth*, friend — resin, timber, rare herbs the whole south wants — and the grove-keepers let it rot for superstition. I merely... encourage efficiency." Plausible, persistent, hollow.
- **Private Motivation:** Open the sacred groves to the plains' trade and make his fortune — to break the grove-keepers' "superstitious" hold on the south's richest green.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the grove-tribes (and the living wilds themselves) will resist him for good; that the Ledger, his ally, will cut him out; that there's *something to* the grove-keepers' warnings about defiling the deep groves.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Agents to pressure or trick the grove-tribes into trade; muscle against grove-resistance; intelligence on the groves' wealth.
- **What he can offer the player:** Southern-plains trade and wealth; the grove-edge goods; a route to the settled south; a (corrupting) patronage.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Pressure a grove-camp into selling; clear grove-resistance to a logging-venture; "negotiate" access to a sacred grove.
- **What he knows:** The grove-edge trade and the groves' wealth; the southern plains' politics; the Ledger's southern reach.
- **What he misunderstands:** He dismisses the grove-keepers' warnings entirely as superstition guarding profit — until a defiled deep-grove "goes wrong" and the living wilds answer; he has no notion the green's "deep memory" is real.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Indifference to "northern" failures; nervous dismissal of the grove-keepers' "deep memory stirring" as a tactic to keep him out — until it isn't.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Salvage-and-clearance interest; the over-grown ruins are obstacles to logging, or salvage to sell.
- **Relationship Links:** Opposed by **Grove-Keeper Sael Sunmark** and the tribes; allied with the **Cinder Ledger's** southern arm; rival of honest grove-edge traders; **(DM)** the settled world's covetous reach into the one faith that still works — the chief Sunmark antagonist.
- **Player-safe description:** The smooth plains-merchant who would open the sacred groves to the saw and the market.
- **DM-Only:** *(antagonist / defiler)* Doss Sunward is the **threat to the living green** — the settled-greed that would break the one faith that still holds its dead. Opposing him is a clean Sunmark hook and protects the hopeful contrast the groves represent. He's a turnable-or-opposable trade-lord, not a faction. If he defiles a deep grove, the living wilds answer (a Sunmark clock). He knows no apex truth and would scoff at it.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — presses the groves; cutting and conflict worsen off-screen. *Turned* (hard) — an uneasy honest trade-partner. *Opposed* — the groves' defense becomes the player's cause; a persistent, plausible enemy.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Noble/Merchant-like with resources** (AC 13, ~38 HP; commands hired toughs and loggers, **Guard/Bandit-like**). Solo danger: moderate via his hirelings. Noncombat: oppose, expose, out-bargain, or (hard) reform him.
- **Tags:** [role:trade-contact, role:antagonist, role:exploiting-failures, far-continent, major]
