## AI Use

NPC roster for **the Emberfell Theocracy / the Ashfast** — a fire-and-ash theocracy in the volcanic Emberfell highlands (SE-central), where the fire-priests read the volcanoes as divine and guard old buried works (Lvl 12–16). This is a **conceptually alien** late-game power: a foreign faith, a different cosmology, a people who burn their dead rather than bury them — which gives the death-rite crisis a strange new shape here. The Ashfast guards **its own pre-Concord secrets** beneath the ash (the Cindern Waste's buried works).

**Player-Facing surfaceable; Secret/DM-Only earned.** Reuse the two established figures (Ash-Hierophant Vole Cindra, Pyre-Warden Sef Embren) — full profiles here. **CRITICAL:** the Emberfell's "buried works" and its theocratic secrets *resonate* with the campaign theme (an order guarding old Concord-adjacent works, a faith built atop a hidden truth) but are **a separate southern power with its own history** — NOT the keystone, NOT the Hollow Court, NOT the harvest. The theocracy's fire-faith genuinely *keeps its dead from lingering* by burning them, so the crisis manifests differently and is partly *masked* here — a useful contrast. No NPC knows the apex truth.

**Cultural voice:** fervent, formal, fire-imaged, certain. Ashfast folk speak of "the Pyre," "the Ashfast" (their faith/fortress-state), "the Mountain's will," "the unburnt" (a horror — dead not given to fire), "ash to ash, never to linger." Zealous certainty; suspicion of foreign faiths and the "linger-cursed" north.

> Light anchors: **Ashfast** (the great fortress-temple-city at the Emberfells' heart, the theocracy's seat), **Cinderhold** (a volcanic mining town), **the Ash Roads** (pilgrim-ways through the volcanic highland), **the Forbidden Waste** (the Cindern Waste's edge, where the buried works lie).

## MAJOR NPCs

### EF-M1. Ash-Hierophant Vole Cindra — the fire-theocracy's ruler
> Name note: **no kin** to any "Vole" of the north (Osrin, Casmir, Senna) — coincidental foreign root; keep full name. "Cindra" is the Ashfast's holy-line surname.
- **Region/Base:** Ashfast, the fortress-temple at the Emberfells' heart.
- **Public Role:** Ash-Hierophant — supreme fire-priest and ruler of the Emberfell Theocracy, who reads the volcanoes as the Mountain's divine will and rules the Ashfast in its name.
- **Faction:** Independent (theocratic ruler; a foreign fire-faith — *not* one of the seven major factions).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The absolute authority of a whole alien southern power; to deal with the Ashfast at all is to deal, ultimately, with the Hierophant. A late-game gatekeeper to the Emberfells' secrets.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A tall, ash-marked figure in robes of red and char, with eyes that seem to hold furnace-light, who regards a northern outsider as one might regard the linger-cursed: with pity, suspicion, and absolute certainty.
- **Voice:** Formal, oracular, fire-imaged, unshakably certain; speaks the Mountain's will as fact. "Your northern dead *linger* because you bury them in cold earth like seeds of rot. We give ours to the Pyre. The Mountain takes them clean. This is not faith, outlander. It is observation." Imperious, missionary.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the Ashfast pure, the Mountain appeased, and the buried works beneath the ash **guarded and unopened** — and, increasingly, understand why the Mountain "speaks" louder of late.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the buried works the Ashfast guards are stirring (the same continental waking, masked by fire-faith); that foreign powers (the Remnant, the Ledger, the player) will breach the Forbidden Waste; that the Mountain's growing "wrath" means the old secret the theocracy was *founded to contain* is failing.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Almost nothing — the Ashfast needs no one — except, perhaps, the destruction or expulsion of those who'd breach the Forbidden Waste; and, secretly, an outsider's-eye answer to why the buried works stir.
- **What he can offer the player:** The Ashfast's knowledge of fire-rite (which genuinely *settles* the dead), its guarded lore of the buried works (oblique, gated), and the resources of a theocratic state — *if* the player proves no threat to the Pyre's secrets.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Destroy or drive off Remnant/Ledger agents breaching the Waste; recover a relic stolen from the buried works; *not ask questions* about what the Ashfast guards.
- **What he knows:** The fire-faith and the Mountain; that the buried works beneath the ash hold something the theocracy was founded to keep sleeping; that the dead linger *less* here because of the Pyre (a true, important contrast).
- **What he misunderstands:** He believes the Ashfast's secret is *theirs alone* — a southern volcanic mystery — with no notion it's part of a continent-wide pattern centered in the far north. He's guarding a real, related secret while blind to the whole.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Theological certainty that *northern* failures are the wages of cold-earth burial — and quiet terror that the *Mountain's* signs (the works stirring) mean the Ashfast's own containment is failing. The crisis here is masked by fire and reframed as the Mountain's wrath.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** The Ashfast guards buried works it considers *its* holy charge to contain — fierce, forbidding secrecy. (These are a *related but distinct* southern complex, never the keystone.)
- **Relationship Links:** Commands **Pyre-Warden Sef Embren** (his enforcer) and the whole theocracy; sends agents like **Brother Vurr Cindra** to watch the Concord Heartlands; violently opposes the **Concord Remnant's** and **Cinder Ledger's** breaches of the Waste; **(DM)** guards a southern secret that *rhymes* with — but is not — the harvest.
- **Player-safe description:** The fire-crowned ruler of the volcanic theocracy, certain, forbidding, and utterly alien to the north.
- **DM-Only:** *(thematic mirror, NOT the keystone)* The Ash-Hierophant guards **a southern complex of buried works** that *resonates* with the campaign secret — an order founded to contain old works, now stirring — but is a **separate, distinct, southern thing.** It deepens the theme (the Concord's reach was vast; others guard fragments) without being the climax. The Pyre genuinely settling the dead is a *real worldbuilding contrast* (fire severs lingering) that an insightful player could even use. He must never confirm the harvest, the Court, or the Hollowmere keystone; never let the Forbidden Waste become "the real" endgame.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the Ashfast guards its secret; the Mountain's "wrath" grows off-screen. *Allied* (hard-won) — a forbidding theocratic ally and a key to the fire-rite's contrast-lore. *Crossed* — the full zeal of a theocratic state turns on the player as a Waste-breaching heretic.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 — High-Priest/Archmage-like, boss-class** (AC 16, ~110 HP; fire/divine high-level casting, theocratic guard of **Veteran/Cultist-like** pyre-soldiers, ash-warded fortress). Solo danger: **extreme** in the Ashfast. Noncombat: prove no threat to the Pyre; the fight is unwinnable on his ground — diplomacy, stealth, or avoidance.
- **Tags:** [role:authority, role:religious, boss-class, far-continent, major, thematic-mirror]

### EF-M2. Pyre-Warden Sef Embren — the theocracy's enforcer
- **Region/Base:** Ashfast and the Ash Roads; the Forbidden Waste's edge.
- **Public Role:** Pyre-Warden — commander of the Ashfast's fire-soldiers and inquisitors, the theocracy's sword against heretics, foreign agents, and "the unburnt."
- **Faction:** Independent (Ashfast enforcer; theocratic military).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The Ashfast's reach and danger made flesh — the figure a player most likely *meets* (and clashes with) when the theocracy notices them, and the gatekeeper of the Forbidden Waste.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A scarred, ash-tattooed warrior-priest in lacquered red armor, with a zealot's stillness and a soldier's economy, who watches an outsider the way one watches a spreading fire.
- **Voice:** Clipped, hard, scripture-edged; threats as liturgy. "Cross into the Waste and you are unburnt already, outlander. I will see you given to the Pyre — for your sake and the Mountain's." Cold certainty, no cruelty for its own sake.
- **Private Motivation:** Guard the Forbidden Waste and the Ashfast's purity absolutely — and prove his faith equal to the Mountain's growing wrath.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the buried works he guards are stirring beyond the wardens' power; that he'll fail the Hierophant and the Mountain; that the heretics he burns are right about *something.*
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Their absence from the Waste, or their compliance — and, in the rare worthy, a fellow-warrior's respect.
- **What he can offer the player:** Safe-conduct on the Ash Roads (if they prove no heretics); the wardens' lore of the Waste's dangers; a hard, honorable (if zealous) ally against those breaching the Ashfast's charge.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Help hunt heretics or foreign agents in the Waste; recover what looters stole from the buried works; submit to the Pyre's law.
- **What he knows:** The Forbidden Waste's dangers and the buried works' edge; the theocracy's military; that the wardens are losing ground to "the Mountain's wrath."
- **What he misunderstands:** He frames the stirring works entirely as a test of faith and the Mountain's wrath, never as a continental crisis; his zeal blinds him to the pattern even as he fights its symptoms.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Inquisitor's certainty — failures are heresy or the unburnt's curse — paired with grim alarm at the Waste's own stirring. He'd *burn* a lingering dead, not study it.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** The buried works are his sacred charge to guard and keep sealed; he kills to keep them so.
- **Relationship Links:** Serves **Ash-Hierophant Vole Cindra**; commands the pyre-soldiers and inquisitors; clashes with **Remnant** and **Ledger** agents in the Waste; a hard mirror of the Wardens' "guard the seal" role *without* their doubt; **(DM)** guards the southern complex, ignorant of its meaning.
- **Player-safe description:** The zealous warrior-priest who guards the theocracy's forbidden ground.
- **DM-Only:** *(enforcer / Waste-gate)* Sef Embren is the **Ashfast's sword and the Forbidden Waste's gate** — the obstacle and possible hard-won ally for any player drawn to the southern works. He is a *fervent, undoubting mirror* of the Ashen Wardens (guarding old works they don't understand), useful for contrast. He must never grasp or confirm the apex truth; the Waste is a distinct southern complex.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — guards the Waste; the wardens lose ground off-screen. *Won over* (very hard) — a fierce theocratic ally. *Opposed* — a relentless, deadly inquisitor-hunter.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 — Inquisitor/Veteran-Priest-like** (AC 18 lacquered plate, ~85 HP; fire-divine smites, commands **Veteran/Cultist-like** pyre-soldiers). Solo danger: **high** — a level-12+ threat with troops. Noncombat: prove no heresy; respect the Waste; earn a warrior's regard.
- **Tags:** [role:religious, role:antagonist, far-continent, major]

### EF-M3. Ember-Sage Mira Cindra — the theocracy's heretic-scholar
> Name note: "Cindra" holy-line surname; she is a junior collateral of the Hierophant's line, distinct person.
- **Region/Base:** Ashfast's lore-vaults and (secretly) the Forbidden Waste's edge.
- **Public Role:** Senior ember-sage — keeper of the Ashfast's deepest lore of the buried works, charged with *understanding* what the wardens merely guard.
- **Faction:** Independent (Ashfast scholar); secretly heterodox.
- **Political/Social Importance:** The one figure in the theocracy actually *studying* (not just guarding) the buried works — and thus the player's likeliest source of the Ashfast's true secrets, at great risk to them both.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A quiet, intense ash-robed scholar with ink-and-soot-stained hands, who speaks the orthodoxy aloud but watches an outsider with the hunger of someone with forbidden questions.
- **Voice:** Measured, careful, double-layered — orthodox in public, probing in private. "The Mountain's will is not to be questioned." (then, alone, low) "...and yet the works *change*. The old texts did not warn of this. Tell me — what do your northern dead *do*?" Curiosity straining against fear.
- **Private Motivation:** Understand what the Ashfast was truly founded to contain — even at the cost of heresy — before the buried works' stirring overwhelms the theocracy's certainties.
- **Fear/Pressure:** Discovery by Pyre-Warden Embren (heresy means the Pyre); that her questions will prove the faith a lie; that the works will wake before anyone understands them.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Knowledge the Ashfast forbids itself — especially a northerner's first-hand account of lingering dead and stirring works, which she suspects connects to *her* secret.
- **What she can offer the player:** The Ashfast's true lore of the buried works (gated, dangerous); the fire-rite's real mechanics (how the Pyre severs lingering); a heretic-ally inside a closed theocracy.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Smuggle her a forbidden text or relic from the Waste; carry her questions north; help her reach a buried-works site Embren guards — secretly.
- **What she knows:** The Ashfast's deepest forbidden lore; that the buried works are *changing* in ways the founding-texts never warned; that the fire-rite *works* against lingering for reasons the orthodoxy mystifies.
- **What she misunderstands:** She's closer than any Ashfast figure to grasping that her secret connects to something larger — but she frames it within volcanic theology and has no concept of the harvest, the Concord's true nature, or the north's centrality. A brilliant scholar circling a truth from the wrong cosmology.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Forbidden fascination — she *correlates* the Waste's stirring with rumors of northern failures and is the rare far-region scholar piecing the pattern together, at deadly personal risk.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Heretical hunger; she'd *enter* the Forbidden Waste if she dared, where Embren would *seal* it.
- **Relationship Links:** Outwardly serves **Ash-Hierophant Vole Cindra**; secretly dreads and evades **Pyre-Warden Sef Embren**; would correspond (forbidden) with the **Concord Remnant** (Mareth Senn, Hadwin Vael) if she could; **(DM)** the Ashfast figure most able to *contribute a piece* to the continental puzzle.
- **Player-safe description:** A devout-seeming ember-sage who guards (and quietly questions) the theocracy's deepest lore.
- **DM-Only:** *(scholar-bridge, gated)* Mira Cindra is the **bridge between the Emberfell secret and the wider truth** — a heretic-scholar who could, with a frontier player's knowledge, help piece together that the buried works are part of the Concord's continental network. But the Emberfell complex remains a *distinct southern thing*; she contributes a corroborating fragment, never the keystone or the Court. Keep her revelations gated and oblique; protect her heresy (exposure means death).
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — questions in secret; may be discovered and burned off-screen. *Allied* — a priceless, endangered heretic-scholar. *Exposed* — martyred, and a powerful indictment of the theocracy's blindness.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Mage/Scholar-like** (AC 12, ~36 HP; fire-divine utility, ward-lore; not a battle-caster). Solo danger: low; her danger is *political* (heresy). Noncombat: her value is forbidden knowledge and the fire-rite contrast.
- **Tags:** [role:scholar, role:memory-keeper, role:faction-opponent, far-continent, major, mystery-bridge]

### EF-M4. Cinder-Master Doss Ashfast — the volcanic mining-lord
> Name note: takes the fortress-state's name as surname (common for high officials); distinct from the place "Ashfast."
- **Region/Base:** Cinderhold, the volcanic mining town.
- **Public Role:** Cinder-Master of Cinderhold — the lord who runs the theocracy's volcanic mines (obsidian, sulfur, rare ash-metals, and "old glass" salvaged near the Waste).
- **Faction:** Independent (theocratic mining-lord); Ledger-tempted.
- **Political/Social Importance:** Controls the Emberfells' material wealth and the dangerous trade in salvage from near the buried works — the player's likeliest *practical* contact (and a crack in the theocracy's purity, where outside money seeps in).
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A soot-grimed, hard-handed industrialist in ash-priest trappings he wears lightly, who counts obsidian and "old glass" with a merchant's eye behind the orthodoxy.
- **Voice:** Blunt, practical, faith-as-formality; talks ore and trade, not theology. "The Mountain provides, the priests say. *I* dig it out. Obsidian, sulfur, and the old glass we're not supposed to sell — but coin is coin, even here." Pragmatic, quietly corrupt.
- **Private Motivation:** Get rich off the Emberfells' wealth — including the forbidden "old glass" (Remembrance relics) dug near the Waste — without the Pyre-Wardens catching him.
- **Fear/Pressure:** Pyre-Warden Embren discovering his forbidden salvage-trade; the mines (which edge the Waste) "going wrong" — his deep crews come back "ash-touched" and strange.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Discreet buyers and fences for the forbidden "old glass," and muscle that won't talk to the priests.
- **What he can offer the player:** Volcanic goods, obsidian and ash-metal gear, forbidden Remembrance-relic salvage, and a corrupt insider's route around the theocracy's controls.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Smuggle "old glass" out past the Pyre-Wardens; deal with a deep-mine problem; keep his forbidden trade secret.
- **What he knows:** The Emberfells' mines and trade; that "old glass" comes up near the Waste and the priests forbid selling it; that the deep mines are "going wrong" (thin-touch's southern face).
- **What he misunderstands:** He treats the "old glass" purely as forbidden contraband worth good coin and the deep-mine sickness as bad air — no notion of Remembrance, the harvest, or the works he's digging beside.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Indifferent to "northern" failures; alarmed only that his deep crews come back "ash-touched and wrong" (a southern thin-touch). A grubby, oblique corroboration.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Pure salvage-greed; he digs near the Waste for profit and bribes the wardens to look away.
- **Relationship Links:** Outwardly faithful to **Vole Cindra**; bribes around **Sef Embren**; secretly trades with the **Cinder Ledger's** southern arm (Iss Mallorn's reach); his deep-mine sickness echoes **Dredgemaster Osrin Vole's** Harrowgast (a northern parallel); **(DM)** an unwitting harvester-relic supplier.
- **Player-safe description:** The soot-handed mining-lord who works the volcano's wealth and quietly sells what he shouldn't.
- **DM-Only:** *(southern relic-supply, oblique)* Doss Ashfast is the Emberfells' **forbidden Remembrance-relic source** — proof the "old glass"/relic phenomenon is continent-wide, here dug *beside* the theocracy's guarded works and sold against the Pyre's law. His deep-mine "ash-touched" sickness is the southern thin-touch. He knows nothing of the meaning; he's a corrupt industrialist atop a related secret. A clean practical contact and an oblique M2/M3 corroboration.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the forbidden trade trickles on. *Helped* — a corrupt practical ally and relic-source. *Exposed* (to the wardens) — ruined and Pyre-bound, a lever or a casualty.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Guard/Veteran-like with resources** (AC 14, ~46 HP; commands miners and toughs). Solo danger: moderate in Cinderhold. Noncombat: trade, bribe, blackmail.
- **Tags:** [role:trade-contact, role:criminal, role:exploiting-failures, far-continent, major]
