## AI Use

NPC roster for **the Sallowmarch Protectorate** — a fever-ridden delta of rice, reed, and smuggling in the Mardenflow's mouth (S coast), held under the distant claim of an absent southern crown (Lvl 10–14). This is a region of **foreign occupation, delta-poverty, and quiet defiance**: a distant empire's garrison-rule laid over a marsh-people who endure it, smuggle around it, and outlast it. A different politics (imperial protectorate vs. free frontier), a different climate (hot, wet, malarial).

**Player-Facing surfaceable; Secret/DM-Only earned.** Reuse the two established figures (Protector-Legate Vorr Sallow, Marsh-Healer Doll Fenn) — full profiles here. The Sallowmarch is **far from the frontier crisis**; death-rite failures are unknown here, masked further by the delta's grim familiarity with death (fever takes so many that lingering would be noticed late). A few NPCs (a delta Mourner, a fen-witch) take the first faint signs seriously.

**Cultural voice:** two registers. The **occupiers** speak stiff imperial formality ("the Protectorate," "the crown's peace," "your compliance is noted"). The **delta-folk** speak soft, wet, fatalistic marsh-cant ("the fen keeps its own," "the fever's fair — it takes all sorts," "the crown's far and the water's near"). Quiet, enduring defiance beneath courtesy.

> Light anchors: **Fenward** (the protectorate's garrison-capital, the crown's seat), **Reedmouth** (a delta smuggling-village), **the Rice Sallows** (the flooded paddy-country), **the Fever Channels** (the deep delta backwaters).

## MAJOR NPCs

### SM-M1. Protector-Legate Vorr Sallow — the distant crown's officer
> Name note: takes the region's name as title-surname (imperial custom); distinct from the place.
- **Region/Base:** Fenward, the garrison-capital.
- **Public Role:** Protector-Legate — the absent southern crown's chief officer in the Sallowmarch, ruling the delta in a far monarch's name.
- **Faction:** Independent (a distant crown's imperial administration — foreign occupation, *not* one of the seven major factions).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The face of foreign empire in Orrun's south — a different *kind* of power than the frontier's councils and merchant-states, and the player's introduction to the world's larger crowns.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A stiff, fever-pale officer in the crown's faded finery, sweating in a climate he hates, ruling a marsh he despises in the name of a court half a world away.
- **Voice:** Clipped, imperial, weary-formal; the language of administration and disdain. "The Protectorate maintains the crown's peace, the crown's roads, and the crown's revenue. The delta-folk maintain their resentment. We tolerate each other. *You* — explain your business." Cold courtesy over deep fatigue.
- **Private Motivation:** Survive his posting, send the crown its revenue, and earn a recall home before the fever or the delta-folk's patience kills him.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the delta's quiet defiance will become open revolt; that the smugglers (and the Ledger) are bleeding the crown's revenue past forgiveness; that he'll die in this marsh, forgotten by the court he serves.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Competent, deniable agents to do what his fever-thinned garrison can't — break a smuggling ring, overawe a defiant village, recover the crown's stolen revenue.
- **What he can offer the player:** The Protectorate's authority and resources; legal passage and protection; a door to the distant crown and the wider imperial world; information on the south.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Smash a smuggling operation; intimidate a rebellious village; hunt a bandit or a tax-thief; carry the crown's word to a recalcitrant town.
- **What he knows:** The Protectorate's administration; the delta's politics and defiance; the smuggling that bleeds the crown; the distant empire's reach.
- **What he misunderstands:** The frontier crisis is beyond his horizon entirely; he'd see a "lingering dead" report as delta-folk superstition or fever-delirium, beneath an imperial officer's notice.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Dismissive imperial skepticism — "marsh-fever fancy" — unless it threatened the crown's revenue or order.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** The Drowned Steps (the rumored sea-ruin off the delta) are a curiosity and a salvage-revenue question, nothing more.
- **Relationship Links:** Rules over the delta-folk (Doll Fenn and the rest) with cold distance; his garrison clashes with **smugglers** (Reedmouth's Maddox-kin); resented by the free **Hollow Gulf Ports** (his crown claims their delta trade); the **Cinder Ledger** both bribes and undercuts him; door to the distant southern crown.
- **Player-safe description:** The weary imperial legate who rules the fever-delta for a far-off crown.
- **DM-Only:** *(world-scale foreign power)* Vorr Sallow embodies **the wider world's empires** — a different governance-model that broadens the player's sense of Orrun and the world beyond. Not evil, not faction-bound; a tired functionary of a distant crown, blind to the north. Through him a high-level player glimpses imperial politics and the southern crown. He knows no apex truth.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the Protectorate grinds on; revolt or recall looms off-screen. *Allied* — an imperial patron and a door to the southern crown. *Crossed* — the occupation's full (if fever-weakened) weight turns on the player.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble/Officer-like with garrison resources** (AC 16 w/ guard, ~40 HP; commands **Guard/Veteran-like** soldiers, fever-thinned). Solo danger: moderate via his garrison, low in person. Noncombat: comply, bargain, or play him against the delta-folk.
- **Tags:** [role:authority, far-continent, major]

### SM-M2. Marsh-Healer Doll Fenn — the delta's plague-doctor & quiet leader
> Name note: "Fenn" is a common delta surname; distinct from any northern name.
- **Region/Base:** Reedmouth and the Fever Channels; circuits the delta.
- **Public Role:** The delta's most renowned plague-doctor — a fever-healer whose craft and standing make her the marsh-folk's true (unofficial) leader, where the crown's officers are merely tolerated.
- **Faction:** Independent (delta healer & folk-leader); quietly anti-occupation.
- **Political/Social Importance:** The real authority the delta-folk *follow*, beneath the crown's nominal rule — the player's gateway to the marsh-people, their knowledge, and their quiet resistance.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A calm, sleeves-rolled woman with fever-steady hands and a marsh-wise gaze, who moves through the deadly delta dispensing cures and quiet authority in equal measure.
- **Voice:** Soft, dry, marsh-fatalistic, kind beneath; speaks in the delta's wet cadence. "The fever's fair, friend — it takes the crown's men and the fen-folk all alike. Only thing in this delta that *is* fair. Now — what ails you, or what do you want?" Patient, unfooled.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep her delta-folk alive against the fever and the crown's neglect — and keep the marsh-people's quiet strength and old ways intact under the occupation.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That a worse plague (or the crown's cruelty) will break the delta; that the **first faint signs of the dead "not settling"** she's begun to notice mean a new sickness she can't name — one no marsh-cure touches.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Respect for the delta-folk; medicines and knowledge the marsh lacks; and a hand against the crown's worst excesses.
- **What she can offer the player:** Unmatched fever-craft and marsh-medicine (rare healing the frontier lacks); the delta-folk's trust, guides, and hidden ways; an honest, unaligned read on the occupation.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Bring her a rare medicine or knowledge; help a village the crown is crushing; investigate the strange new "unsettled dead" she's seen.
- **What she knows:** The delta's fevers and cures; the marsh-folk's politics and smuggling; the deep channels; the first faint signs of the crisis reaching even here.
- **What she misunderstands:** She's the rare far-south figure to *notice* the dead "not settling" — but she frames it as a new, unknown sickness, with no concept of the harvest or its source.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Clinical alarm — a *new disease* she can't cure, distinct from fever. The southernmost early-clinical-witness, framed as medicine, not mystery.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Marsh-wary; the Drowned Steps are "drowned ground, best left," and she warns folk from them.
- **Relationship Links:** The delta-folk's true leader, against the distant rule of **Vorr Sallow**; works with the delta Mourner **Tide-Mother's southern kin** (below) and the smugglers of Reedmouth; her fever-craft is sought as far as the **Pale Coast** (Doll Fenn was scaffolded as reaching the Coast); **(DM)** the south's clinical early-warning.
- **Player-safe description:** The marsh-wise plague-doctor the delta-folk follow, healer and quiet leader both.
- **DM-Only:** *(clinical early-warning, southern)* Doll Fenn is the **far-south's earliest, faintest witness** to the crisis — noticing the dead "not settling" as a new sickness. She's a clean, humane potential ally, a source of rare healing, and the delta's real power beneath the crown. She knows no mechanism; keep her observation clinical and oblique. Through her, a frontier player's knowledge could connect the "new sickness" to the truth.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — keeps her people alive; the occupation grinds on. *Allied* — a beloved healer-ally and the delta's key. *Crossed* — the delta-folk close ranks against the player.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Priest/Healer-like** (AC 11, ~34 HP; potent healing/disease-craft, low-level divine; not a fighter). Solo danger: none; she heals, not fights. Noncombat: her value is healing, trust, and the delta-network.
- **Tags:** [faction:independent, role:religious, role:harmed-by-failures, role:ally, far-continent, major]

### SM-M3. Reed-Boss "Heron" Maddox — the delta smuggler-king
> Name note: common outlaw "Maddox" (cf. Eel/Carp/Crow) — distinct delta figure; keep epithet.
- **Region/Base:** Reedmouth and the Fever Channels' hidden ways.
- **Public Role:** The delta's foremost smuggler — "the Heron," who moves contraband through the Fever Channels the crown's boats can't follow, and bleeds the Protectorate dry.
- **Faction:** Independent (delta smuggler-king); a grey power and quiet resistance.
- **Political/Social Importance:** Controls the delta's hidden trade and the marsh-folk's defiance-by-smuggling; the player's contraband contact and a turnable grey ally against the occupation.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A lean, still, long-legged smuggler who waits in the reeds like the heron he's named for, then strikes a deal (or a throat) before you've seen him move.
- **Voice:** Soft, patient, marsh-sly, never wasted; lets silence do half his talking. "The crown's boats draw three feet. The Channels run two. That's the whole of the delta's politics, friend. I go where the crown can't." Quiet menace, dry humor.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the Channels his, bleed the occupation, get rich, and protect the marsh-folk who shelter him — without becoming the crown's excuse to burn the delta.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the crown will find force enough to dredge the Channels; that the Ledger's smuggling-arm will swallow his trade; that a rival smuggler will betray the marsh-folk to the legate.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** A cut, a clever scheme, or a strong arm — and proof they're no crown's-informer.
- **What he can offer the player:** Passage through the Fever Channels the crown can't follow; the delta's contraband and intelligence; a turnable grey ally against the occupation; the marsh-folk's hidden network.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Run a cargo past the garrison; sabotage a crown patrol; deal with a rival smuggler or a marsh-traitor.
- **What he knows:** The delta's hidden ways and contraband; the crown's patrols and weaknesses; who among the marsh-folk can be trusted.
- **What he misunderstands:** He treats the delta's deep drowned ruins (and the Drowned Steps) as good hiding-holes, dismissing the marsh-witches' unease — until a Channel-hideout goes "wrong."
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Mercenary marsh-wariness — he avoids "bad water" hideouts the witches warn of, and is the player's grey-network read on where the delta's "going strange."
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Opportunistic (hideouts) until they turn dangerous; then marsh-superstition.
- **Relationship Links:** Shelters among **Doll Fenn's** delta-folk; preys on **Vorr Sallow's** garrison-revenue; rivals the **Cinder Ledger's** southern smuggling-arm; kin-in-kind to the Reach's **Gull Heddwyn** and the Hethe's **Greenfinger**; **(DM)** a grey witness to the delta's first crisis-signs.
- **Player-safe description:** The patient, dangerous smuggler-king who rules the delta's hidden waters.
- **DM-Only:** *(turnable grey, anti-occupation)* "Heron" Maddox is the Sallowmarch's smuggler-resistance — a grey contact turnable against the crown, like Gull or Greenfinger. He's also a useful grey-network witness to the delta "going strange." Not a faction; a local outlaw power and quiet resistance.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the smuggling-war with the crown simmers on. *Turned* — a marsh-sly ally and a free hidden delta. *Opposed* — a deadly, unseen enemy in the reeds.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Scout/Assassin-like, ambusher** (AC 15, ~48 HP; stealth-and-poison ambush, commands **Bandit/Scout-like** smugglers in the reeds). Solo danger: high in the Channels, moderate ashore. Noncombat: pay, scheme, prove you're no informer.
- **Tags:** [role:criminal, role:outlaw, role:trade-contact, far-continent, major]

### SM-M4. Fen-Witch Mother Sela Reed — the delta's grief-and-marsh keeper
> Name note: "Reed" is a common delta surname; distinct from any northern name. She blends the Mourners' rites with delta marsh-lore.
- **Region/Base:** The Rice Sallows and the Fever Channels; the delta-folk's old faith.
- **Public Role:** The delta's eldest fen-witch — grief-keeper and marsh-seer who tends the drowned and fever-dead by the delta's old rites, fused with the Mourners' Circle's grief-craft.
- **Faction:** Mourners' Circle (delta eldest); the **Sallowmarch node of the continental old-songs network**, blended with marsh-lore.
- **Political/Social Importance:** Carries the delta's deep memory and the M6-oblique old-songs thread at the south's wet edge — the spiritual counterpart to Doll Fenn's medicine.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A reed-thin, fever-bright old woman hung with marsh-charms and grave-tokens, who tends the delta's countless dead with a sorrow as wide as the water.
- **Voice:** Soft, wet, song-and-charm-laced, marsh-slow; speaks of "the fen's own," "the drowned and the burned-out," "the old water-songs." "The fen takes so many, child, that we sing them down by the hundred. Lately... some don't go down. As the old songs feared." Patient, deep with sorrow.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the delta's grief-rites and old water-songs alive against the fever's toll and the crown's contempt — and keep the true memory passing on.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the delta's endless dying will outpace the rites; that the dead "not going down" — which Doll Fenn calls a new sickness — is what the old water-songs warned of; that the south is unready.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Respect for the delta's dead; news of the wider Mourner-network; a hand to carry the songs and the warning on.
- **What she can offer the player:** The delta's grief-lore and water-rites; the **old songs** (M6-oblique) fused with marsh-lore; the Sallowmarch key to the continental network; a knowing, humane ally.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Honor a drowned or fever grave; carry a song or token to another elder; help with the dead who "won't go down."
- **What she knows:** The delta's water-rites and old songs; the continental Mourner-network (she corresponds with the other elders); that the failing dead match the songs' century-old warning.
- **What she misunderstands:** She holds the true fragment ("the grey hands took the dead") as folk-memory, oblique and unprovable, and connects it to the dead "not going down" — but doesn't know the mechanism or that the harvesters survive.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Sorrowful recognition — the water-songs warned of this; she and Doll Fenn (medicine and rite) together are the delta's twin early-witnesses.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** The songs name "the grey hands"; she dreads the Drowned Steps the crown covets and warns folk from them.
- **Relationship Links:** Continental Mourner-network node — corresponds with **Mother Ezrith Combe**, **Grandmother Wend**, **Senna Crale**, **Wenna Lowwater**, **Onn Greenward**, **Tide-Mother Sera Mardenmouth**, **Salt-Mother Tess Brackhold**, **Grave-Sister Onn Crale**; works with healer **Doll Fenn** (rite + medicine); ignored by **Vorr Sallow**; sheltered by **Heron Maddox's** marsh-folk.
- **Player-safe description:** The reed-thin old fen-witch who sings the delta's countless dead to rest.
- **DM-Only:** *(M6-oblique, network, southern)* Mother Sela Reed is the **Sallowmarch anchor of the old-songs network** — the same gated folk-truth, here fused with delta water-lore. With Doll Fenn she forms the south's twin early-warning (rite + medicine). Keep her fragments oblique; she knows no mechanism, Court, or keystone. A humane, knowing ally and the proof the truth survives in song even at the fever-delta's edge.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the songs pass on; the rites strain under the fever's toll. *Helped* — a knowing ally and network-key. *Lost* — the southern delta strand of the old-songs frays.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Druid/Priest-like, frail** (AC 11, ~28 HP; marsh-grief rites, *Gentle Repose*, *Water Breathing*, low-level nature/divine). Solo danger: none; will not fight. Noncombat: lore, network, moral clarity.
- **Tags:** [faction:mourners-circle, role:religious, role:memory-keeper, role:ally, far-continent, major, mystery-folk-thread]
