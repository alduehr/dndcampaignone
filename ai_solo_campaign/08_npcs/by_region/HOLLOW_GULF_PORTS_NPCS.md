## AI Use

NPC roster for **the Hollow Gulf Ports** — a string of great warm-water southern port city-states ringing the Hollow Gulf where the Mardenflow delta meets the Calm Reach (Lvl 10–15). This is **the maritime south**: rival merchant ports, the gateway to Surren and the wider world overseas, where the continent's trade meets the sea. A different climate, a different politics — warm, cosmopolitan, ocean-facing, with foreign sailors, southern goods, and the smell of the open sea. High-density region: it is a *port-world* (5 major, 18 secondary, 47 minor).

**Player-Facing surfaceable; Secret/DM-Only earned.** Reuse the two established figures (Port-Mistress Ive Calder, Privateer-Captain Roke Mallin) — full profiles here. The Hollow Gulf is **far from the frontier crisis** — death-rite failures are essentially **unknown here**, a thing of the cold north no one credits. This is the campaign's most *normal* region, a relief from the death-haunted frontier, useful as a place that shows the player how much of the world simply doesn't know. A very few NPCs (a worried Mourner, a far-sailing captain who's seen the north) take it seriously.

**Cultural voice:** warm, brash, polyglot, sea-superstitious. Gulf folk are loud, mercantile, and proud of their ports' rivalries; they swear by tides and saints of the sea, haggle hard, and treat northerners as exotic and slightly pitiable. Talk of "the Gulf," "the lanes" (sea-routes), "the warm trade," "Surren-side," "the season" (sailing weather).

> Light anchors: **Calderport** (the greatest of the Gulf ports, Ive Calder's city-state), **Saltgate** (a rival port), **the Reach Lanes** (the open-sea shipping lanes south), **the Foreign Quarter** (where Surren and overseas traders dock), **the Mardenmouth** (the delta-mouth wharves).

## MAJOR NPCs

### HG-M1. Port-Mistress Ive Calder — ruler of the greatest Gulf port
- **Region/Base:** Calderport, the greatest city-state on the Hollow Gulf.
- **Public Role:** Port-Mistress (elected merchant-ruler) of Calderport — the most powerful of the rival Gulf port-lords, mistress of the south's greatest harbor.
- **Faction:** Independent (Gulf city-state ruler).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The first true *maritime power* the player meets — the south's gateway, and the player's door to overseas trade, Surren, and the wider world beyond Orrun.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A sun-browned, gold-ringed woman of magnificent self-possession, dressed in southern silks, who rules a harbor of a thousand ships from a counting-house terrace overlooking the Gulf.
- **Voice:** Warm, commanding, mercantile-shrewd; flatters and prices in the same breath; loves a bold proposal. "The Gulf made me, friend, and I made Calderport. Tell me what a hard northerner wants in a warm city — and what you'll pay."
- **Private Motivation:** Keep Calderport first among the Gulf ports — ahead of Saltgate and the rest — and master the overseas trade to Surren before her rivals or the inland League can.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That a rival port (Saltgate) or the Cinder Ledger's southern arm will break Calderport's primacy; that the privateers she licenses will slip her leash; that the distant Sallowmarch crown's reach will tighten on the delta trade.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Bold, capable agents for the work her own people can't be seen doing — settle a trade-war, run a delicate cargo, deal with a rival or a too-free privateer.
- **What she can offer the player:** The south's greatest harbor and its resources — ships, overseas passage (to Surren and beyond), warm-trade wealth, a maritime patron of the first rank.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Sabotage a rival port's deal; recover a hijacked cargo; rein in or eliminate a privateer (her own Roke Mallin, perhaps); carry a sealed offer to an overseas power.
- **What she knows:** The Gulf's port-rivalries, the sea-lanes, and the overseas trade; the southern reach of the inland factions; the way to Surren.
- **What she misunderstands:** The frontier crisis is *beneath her notice* — cold northern superstition, irrelevant to the warm trade. She is the campaign's clearest demonstration that the wider world neither knows nor cares.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Genuine non-comprehension — "the dead won't *what*?" — followed by polite dismissal. If the rumor ever reached the Gulf's trade, she'd suppress it for the market's sake.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Mild commercial interest (the Drowned Steps off the coast are a rumored salvage-prize) without dread; the Concord is ancient history to a sea-facing merchant.
- **Relationship Links:** Rival of **Saltgate's port-lord** and the other Gulf city-states; licenses (and fears) **Privateer-Captain Roke Mallin**; contends with the **Cinder Ledger's** southern arm; the **Glassmere League** (Oren Glass) covets her overseas trade; door to **Surren** and the overseas powers.
- **Player-safe description:** The magnificent merchant-ruler of the south's greatest port.
- **DM-Only:** *(world-scale lever)* Ive Calder is the player's **gateway to the overseas world** and the campaign's proof that **the crisis is, so far, contained to the north** — a deliberate counterweight to frontier doom. She is not evil and not faction-bound; a worldly power-broker whose blind spot is the north entirely. Through her, a high-level player could reach Surren or marshal maritime resources — but she'd never spend a copper on a "northern ghost story."
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — Calderport's primacy and the Gulf's trade-wars roll on off-screen. *Allied* — a maritime patron of immense reach. *Crossed* — the south's greatest harbor closes to the player.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble/Magnate-like with naval resources** (AC 14, ~46 HP; commands ships, harbor-guard, and privateers, not a personal war-band). Solo danger: low in person, immense by sea. Noncombat: trade, alliance, and the leverage of a thousand hulls.
- **Tags:** [role:authority, role:trade-contact, far-continent, major]

### HG-M2. Privateer-Captain Roke Mallin — the Gulf's licensed sea-raider
- **Region/Base:** The Reach Lanes and the Gulf ports; flagship *the Salt Crow*.
- **Public Role:** The most feared and famous of Calderport's licensed privateers — a sea-raider with the Port-Mistress's letter of marque and a reputation that outruns his ship.
- **Faction:** Independent (privateer; Calder-licensed, increasingly free).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The man who actually controls the southern sea-lanes' danger — the player's likeliest sea-passage, escort, or enemy on the open Gulf, and a wild card straining at Ive Calder's leash.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A grinning, sea-scarred captain in a salt-stiff coat hung with foreign coins, who treats the whole Gulf as his hunting-ground and every meeting as a wager.
- **Voice:** Bold, profane, theatrical, sea-superstitious; spins everything into a tale. "The lanes are mine, the Crow is fast, and the Port-Mistress's paper says it's all legal. Sail with me or sail careful, friend — your pick."
- **Private Motivation:** Be the freest, richest, most legendary captain on the southern sea — and quietly slip from a licensed privateer into a true sea-lord answering to no port.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That Ive Calder will revoke his marque and turn the Gulf's navies on him; that a rival privateer or an overseas fleet will best him; that the far-sailing rumors he's heard from Surren-side of "a wrongness in the north" might be more than a tale (he's the rare Gulf figure who's *heard* of it, sailing wide).
- **What he wants from outsiders:** A bold crew, a fat prize, a clever scheme, and a good story; he respects nerve above all.
- **What he can offer the player:** Fast sea-passage anywhere on the Gulf or to Surren; the sea-lanes' intelligence; a privateer's strength; smuggling and salvage; a roguish maritime ally.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Crew a daring raid or salvage (the Drowned Steps tempt him); run a blockade; help him slip Calder's leash without open war.
- **What he knows:** The southern sea-lanes, the overseas routes to Surren, and the Gulf's privateers; far-sailors' tales — including faint, dismissed rumors of "the north gone wrong."
- **What he misunderstands:** He's *heard* the northern rumor (rare for the Gulf) but files it as a sailor's ghost-story among many; if pressed by a frontier player who knows it's real, he's one of the few here who might *believe* it.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Sea-superstitious curiosity rather than scorn — he's heard stranger tales true. A rare far-region NPC who could be *convinced*, making him a useful bridge for spreading the alarm by sea.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Treasure-hunger, especially the rumored Drowned Steps; he'd salvage a Concord ruin for the prize and the story.
- **Relationship Links:** Licensed (and chafing) under **Ive Calder**; rival of other privateers and the Gulf navies; deals with smugglers and overseas captains; far-sailing contact toward **Surren** and the **Sundered Isles**; a sea-link the player could use to carry the frontier's warning outward.
- **Player-safe description:** The bold, famous privateer who rules the southern sea-lanes by letter of marque.
- **DM-Only:** *(bridge)* Roke Mallin is the campaign's rare far-region NPC who *might believe* the frontier crisis — sailors trade in true-strange tales — making him a potential bridge to alarm the wider maritime world late-game. He's a wild card straining Calder's authority (a Gulf clock). Keep his "north gone wrong" knowledge as vague sailor's-rumor, never confirmed truth.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — raids the lanes, edges toward sea-lordship; if he slips Calder's leash, the Gulf erupts. *Allied* — a roguish, invaluable sea-captain. *Crossed* — a deadly enemy on every lane the player must sail.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 — Pirate-Captain/Veteran-like** (AC 16, ~75 HP; cutlass-and-pistol-bow, commands a crew of **Bandit/Veteran-like** sailors and a fast ship). Solo danger: high at sea with his crew, moderate ashore. Noncombat: charm, wager, hire, or out-sail him.
- **Tags:** [role:trade-contact, role:travel-guide, role:criminal, far-continent, major]

### HG-M3. Saltgate Port-Lord Doss Saltgate — the great rival
> Name note: port-lords take their city as surname — common Gulf custom; keep full names.
- **Region/Base:** Saltgate, Calderport's chief rival city-state.
- **Public Role:** Port-Lord of Saltgate — ruler of the Gulf's second city and Ive Calder's bitterest rival for the south's trade.
- **Faction:** Independent (Gulf city-state ruler).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The other pole of Gulf politics; a player can play Calderport and Saltgate against each other, and Saltgate is the natural ally for anyone Ive Calder has crossed.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A lean, hawk-faced man of cold elegance and colder ambition, who rules Saltgate like a man certain he should rule the whole Gulf.
- **Voice:** Clipped, proud, scornful of Calderport; courts the player as a tool against his rival. "Calder calls herself first of the Gulf. Saltgate will outlive her. The question is which side of that you'd rather be on."
- **Private Motivation:** Supplant Calderport as the Gulf's first city — by trade, intrigue, or, if it comes to it, by sea-war.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That Calder's wealth and her privateers will keep Saltgate forever second; that the Cinder Ledger or the League will pick a winner over his head; that his own ambition will start a war Saltgate can't win.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Anyone who can hurt Calderport — intelligence, sabotage, a cargo redirected, a privateer turned.
- **What he can offer the player:** Saltgate's harbor and resources as a base *against* Calder; a rival maritime patron; the leverage of Gulf rivalry.
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Undercut a Calderport deal; turn or sink a Calder privateer; carry a hostile overture to an overseas power.
- **What he knows:** The Gulf's rivalries from the underdog's angle; Calderport's weaknesses; the southern trade.
- **What he misunderstands:** Like all the Gulf, the frontier crisis is invisible to him — he'd only care if Calder cared, as a lever.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Total indifference unless it could embarrass Calderport.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Salvage-interest, identical to Calder's; the Drowned Steps tempt him too.
- **Relationship Links:** Arch-rival of **Ive Calder**; courts the **Cinder Ledger's** southern arm and the **Glassmere League** against her; would hire **Roke Mallin** away if he could; natural ally to anyone Calder has wronged.
- **Player-safe description:** The cold, ambitious port-lord who would make Saltgate first of the Gulf.
- **DM-Only:** *(rivalry lever)* Doss Saltgate is the **counterweight to Ive Calder** — the south's politics in two poles, fully playable against each other. Allying with him is a clean alternative to Calderport. Not evil, not faction-bound; an ambitious rival whose blind spot is the north, like all the Gulf.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the Calder-Saltgate rivalry simmers toward trade-war. *Allied* — a powerful rival patron and base against Calderport. *Crossed* — a cold enemy controlling the Gulf's second harbor.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble/Magnate-like with naval resources** (AC 14, ~42 HP; ships, harbor-guard, intrigue). Solo danger: low in person, high by sea. Noncombat: rivalry, alliance, intrigue.
- **Tags:** [role:authority, role:trade-contact, role:faction-opponent, far-continent, major]

### HG-M4. Tide-Mother Sera Mardenmouth — the sea-Mourners' eldest
> Name note: takes the delta-mouth as surname — common; keep full names. The Gulf Mourners blend the Circle's rites with southern sea-saint worship.
- **Region/Base:** The Mardenmouth wharves and the delta-edge; the Gulf's old grief-keeping.
- **Public Role:** Eldest of the Gulf's sea-Mourners — keeper of the southern death-rites for the drowned and the sea-dead, in a faith blending the Mourners' Circle with the south's sea-saints.
- **Faction:** Mourners' Circle (southern/maritime eldest); the **Gulf node of the continental old-songs network**.
- **Political/Social Importance:** Small in port-politics, large in the campaign's network — the southernmost anchor of the old-songs thread, and the rare Gulf voice who'd take a frontier player's crisis seriously.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A weathered, salt-cured old woman in tide-blue, who tends the wharf-shrines of the drowned with a serenity the brawling port has no time for.
- **Voice:** Soft, tide-slow, song-and-saint-laced; speaks of "the sea's own," "the saints of the deep," "the long tide." "The Gulf forgets its dead between cargoes, friend. We remember. The sea remembers longest of all." Calm, unafraid of the loud and the rich.
- **Private Motivation:** Keep the sea-rites and old songs alive among a port that worships profit — and keep the southern dead at rest, and the true memory passing on.
- **Fear/Pressure:** That the Gulf's wealth and bustle will let the old rites die; that the far-north rumors she's heard from sailors *match the old songs' warning* — and the south is utterly unready and unbelieving.
- **What she wants from outsiders:** Respect for the sea-dead; news of the north (she *connects* the sailors' rumors to the songs); a hand to carry the warning on.
- **What she can offer the player:** The Gulf's grief-lore and sea-rites; the **old songs** (M6-oblique folk-fragment); the southern key to the continental Mourner-network; and the rare far-region ear that *believes* the frontier crisis.
- **What she might ask the player to do:** Honor the sea-dead; carry a song or token to another elder; help spread the warning the Gulf won't hear.
- **What she knows:** The sea-rites and old songs; the continental Mourner-network (she corresponds with the other elders); that sailors' tales of "the north gone wrong" echo the songs' century-old warning.
- **What she misunderstands:** She holds the true fragment ("the grey hands took the dead") as folk-memory and now *correlates* it with sailors' northern rumors — closer than most to sounding an alarm — but cannot prove it and doesn't know the mechanism or the surviving harvesters.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Believing dread — she's the Gulf's lone NPC who hears the sailors' rumors and *connects them to the old songs.* The southern early-warning the port ignores.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** The songs name the Concord's works; she dreads the rumored Drowned Steps the port-lords covet, and warns sailors from them.
- **Relationship Links:** Continental Mourner-network node — corresponds with **Mother Ezrith Combe**, **Grandmother Wend**, **Senna Crale**, **Wenna Lowwater**, **Onn Greenward**, **Grave-Sister Onn Crale**; the only Gulf figure who'd warn **Roke Mallin** the northern tales are true; ignored by **Ive Calder** and **Doss Saltgate**.
- **Player-safe description:** The serene old keeper of the Gulf's death-rites for the drowned and the sea's own.
- **DM-Only:** *(M6-oblique, network, southern alarm)* Tide-Mother Sera is the **southernmost anchor of the old-songs network** and the Gulf's lone believer — the link by which the frontier warning could reach the sea-world (via Roke Mallin). Keep her fragment oblique; she doesn't know the harvest, the Court, or the keystone. A humane, knowing ally and the proof that even at the far warm south, the truth survives in song.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — the songs pass on; the Gulf stays oblivious. *Helped* — a knowing ally and the southern network-key; possibly the catalyst that finally makes the maritime world *listen.* *Lost* — the southern strand of the old-songs frays.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Priest/Acolyte-like, frail** (AC 10, ~26 HP; sea-grief rites, *Gentle Repose*, *Water Breathing*, low-level divine). Solo danger: none; will not fight. Noncombat: lore, network, moral clarity.
- **Tags:** [faction:mourners-circle, role:religious, role:memory-keeper, role:ally, far-continent, major, mystery-folk-thread]

### HG-M5. Foreign-Factor Hadiz of Surren — the overseas merchant
> Name note: Surren names follow a different (foreign) convention — single given name + homeland; deliberately distinct from Orrun surnames.
- **Region/Base:** The Foreign Quarter of Calderport; trades between the Gulf and Surren overseas.
- **Public Role:** The most prominent of the overseas merchants in the Gulf — a factor of distant Surren who deals in southern and foreign goods and embodies the world *beyond* Orrun.
- **Faction:** Independent (overseas merchant; foreign).
- **Political/Social Importance:** The player's living glimpse of the wider world — the proof that Orrun is one continent among others, and a potential door to Surren itself.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A poised, richly-robed foreigner with an accent like warm spice and a merchant's universal smile, surrounded by goods and gods no Orrun-born has names for.
- **Voice:** Courteous, melodious, faintly amused by Orrun's parochialism; speaks of "the old lands" (Surren) and "your young continent." "You northerners think Orrun is the world. Surren was old when the Concord was a child. There is *so* much you do not know."
- **Private Motivation:** Master the Orrun trade for his Surren house — and, privately, satisfy an old scholar's curiosity about Orrun's strange ruin-haunted history (Surren has its own ancient lore).
- **Fear/Pressure:** Being cheated or trapped in a foreign land's politics; that the Gulf's rivalries (Calder vs. Saltgate) will catch his house in the crossfire.
- **What he wants from outsiders:** Reliable Orrun partners and intelligence; safe passage to inland goods; and, curiously, *stories of the north's ruins* — he collects Orrun's strange history.
- **What he can offer the player:** Overseas goods, foreign knowledge, passage toward Surren, and a perspective on Orrun no native holds — including, perhaps, *Surren's own ancient memory* of similar things (gated, oblique).
- **What he might ask the player to do:** Broker an inland deal; carry a cargo or message overseas; bring him tales and relics of Orrun's deep history for his collection.
- **What he knows:** The overseas trade and the world beyond Orrun; Surren's ancient lore (which *may* hold its own oblique echo of harvest-like history — a deep, gated, far-future thread); the Gulf's foreign politics.
- **What he misunderstands:** He treats Orrun's ruin-history as exotic antiquarian curiosity — he has no idea the frontier crisis is live, and would find the idea thrilling rather than alarming.
- **Reaction to death-rite failures:** Scholarly fascination — "how *fascinating*; Surren has tales not unlike it" — the campaign's one hint that the harvest-theme may be older and wider than Orrun, kept deeply oblique and never developed.
- **Reaction to Concord ruins / old roads / memory:** Avid antiquarian interest; he'd pay well for Concord lore and relics for his Surren patrons.
- **Relationship Links:** Trades through **Ive Calder's** Foreign Quarter; courted by **Doss Saltgate**; deals with **Roke Mallin** for passage; a door to **Surren** and the overseas world; **(DM)** a deeply-gated far-future thread of "Surren's own old lore."
- **Player-safe description:** The cultured overseas merchant of distant Surren, trading in goods and knowledge from beyond the sea.
- **DM-Only:** *(world-horizon, deeply gated)* Hadiz embodies **the world beyond Orrun** and carries the campaign's faintest, most optional hint that harvest-like history may not be Orrun's alone — a horizon-thread to keep *extremely* oblique and never confirm or develop; it must never pull focus from Hollowmere. Primarily he is a colorful gateway to the overseas world and a buyer of Orrun's lore. He knows nothing of the live crisis.
- **Evolution:** *Ignored* — a recurring exotic merchant-contact. *Cultivated* — a door to Surren and the wider world; a far-future horizon-ally. *Crossed* — loses the player their overseas reach.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble/Merchant-like with foreign resources** (AC 13, ~32 HP; foreign guards, overseas wealth). Solo danger: low. Noncombat: trade, knowledge, passage.
- **Tags:** [role:trade-contact, role:scholar, role:welcomes-outsiders, far-continent, major, world-horizon]
