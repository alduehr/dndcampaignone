## AI Use

Stage 9 major NPCs for **Caradril**, the first major city and mid-game hub (Lvl 5–12). These deepen the city's power structure beyond the foundation majors seated here (Vyre, Quorrin, Briss) and the 15 district secondaries. **Player-Facing blocks are surfaceable; Secret/DM-Only blocks are earned.** Numbering continues from MAJOR_NPCS.md (C25+). Mechanics use reference profiles. **No NPC here is a new faction, god, or central mystery;** they populate the existing Tidewater Council, Charter Houses, Ledger, Remnant, Salt Syndicate, the Hush, and the temple/guilds.

> Several already exist as *secondaries* (Voss, Dann, Tolm, Kade, Vell, Dree, Ysarra, Sorrel, the Bellman) — those remain. The figures below are NEW majors plus two elevations clearly marked.

## C25. Magister Otho Brail — the council's swing vote
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Tidewater Council (independent magister). **Location:** the Magisterium.
- **First Impression:** A shrewd, owlish magister of middling charter who never speaks first and is courted by everyone because his vote decides things.
- **Public Role:** The Tidewater Council's perennial swing vote; the man both Tolm and Dann must win.
- **Voice:** Careful, conditional, fond of "it depends"; weighs every word like coin.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Stay relevant and un-ruined; back whichever side leaves him strongest.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** He privately *believes the Reach crisis is real* (a frightened Warden petitioner reached him) but won't spend his vote on it without cover. He keeps a secret tally of who has bribed whom on the council — leverage he hoards. Turnable by the player with proof and protection; the cleanest lever to actually *move the council*.
- **Fear:** Picking the losing side; his bribe-tally surfacing.
- **Leverage:** His decisive vote; his knowledge of every councilor's price.
- **What he knows:** The true balance of the council; who's bought; the ignored Reach petitions' contents.
- **What he wants from the player:** Cover — proof that backing reform/the Reach won't ruin him.
- **What he can offer:** The swing vote (the key to `Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER` and any council action); the bribe-tally.
- **How he can harm the player:** Sell their secrets to the higher bidder; vote against them out of fear.
- **Relationship Links:** Courted by Tolm and Dann; wary of Voss's weakness; quietly contacted by the ignored Wardens.
- **Current Attitude:** Noncommittal, watchful, available to the best-prepared suitor.
- **Possible Evolution:** The vote that saves or dooms the Reach petition; exposed and ruined; or a cautious recurring ally.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble-like** (AC 12, ~27 HP). Noncombatant; fights with his vote.
- **Related:** `Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER`; Dann, Tolm, Voss, the Wardens.

## C26. Charter-Lady Imris Vane-Tolm — the reforming heir
> Surname note: "Vane-Tolm" is a cadet branch married into House Tolm; **not** kin to Archivist Quorrin **Vane** (common name). Keep full name.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Charter Houses (heir to a junior House). **Location:** the Magisterium; the Counting-Quays.
- **First Impression:** A young, impatient noblewoman in plain rich cloth who treats her elders' politics as a sinking ship she means to leave.
- **Public Role:** Heir to a junior Charter House; an ambitious reformer who courts the Ledger's *methods* while despising the old Houses' sloth.
- **Voice:** Crisp, modern, faintly contemptuous; "the Houses are antiques; I intend to be useful."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Drag her House (and the city) into the future; make her name before the old order collapses.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** She has quietly invested in the relic-trade through Ledger cutouts and is **horrified** by what she's begun to suspect the relics *are* (an early-Caradril M3 conscience-crisis among the powerful). She'll fund a player who can get her the truth — or bury it if the truth would ruin her.
- **Fear:** Being on the wrong side of history; her relic-money damning her.
- **Leverage:** A junior House's wealth without its caution; Ledger contacts.
- **What she knows:** The relic-trade's reach into the Houses; who's invested; the Ledger's methods.
- **What she wants from the player:** Discreet investigation of the relics she's bought into; deniable agency.
- **What she can offer:** Funding, a House's resources, a foothold among the young nobility, an M3 thread inside the elite.
- **How she can harm the player:** Buy their silence or their ruin if exposure threatens her; she's pragmatic to a fault.
- **Relationship Links:** Cadet of House Tolm (chafes under Esren); courts Ledger methods (uneasy with Vyre); rival-peer of Dann's idealism.
- **Current Attitude:** Transactional, curious, secretly anxious.
- **Possible Evolution:** A powerful young ally who turns on the relic-trade; a coverer-up if cornered; a casualty of the Houses' fall.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble-like** (AC 13, ~31 HP; commands House guards, Guard-like). Noncombatant.
- **Related:** M3; Esren Tolm, Vyre, Dann; the Ashmarket relic-trade.

## C27. Sister-Commander Veil Ansele — the Tide-Watch's iron hand
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Tide-Watch (second to Captain Kade; commands the Quays and Magisterium wards). **Location:** citywide; barracks in the Magisterium.
- **First Impression:** A flint-eyed watch-commander whose courtesy is a closed gate; she has never once been seen to hurry.
- **Public Role:** Kade's hard-edged second; the Watch's enforcer where Kade is its conscience.
- **Voice:** Clipped, formal, final; "the law is the law, and I am the law's hand."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Order, by the book, without exception — including for the rich.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** She is *more honest than Kade fears and harder than the council likes* — she has a private file on Syndicate and Hush operations the council won't let her act on. She is incorruptible, which makes her dangerous to everyone: a player who breaks the law cleanly will face her; a player who *exposes* corruption can hand her the warrant she's been denied.
- **Fear:** Being ordered to look away one time too many.
- **Leverage:** Command of the city wards; her uncorrupted warrant-file.
- **What she knows:** The Watch's real intelligence on the Syndicate, the Hush, and smuggling; who the council protects.
- **What she wants from the player:** Either compliance — or actionable proof she can finally move on.
- **What she can offer:** The full weight of the Watch *against* a target the player exposes; lawful protection.
- **How she can harm the player:** Relentless, fair pursuit if they break the law; she does not bribe and does not forget.
- **Relationship Links:** Kade's second (mutual respect, friction over mercy); despised by Sorrel and the Bellman; distrusted by the corrupt council.
- **Current Attitude:** Cold, correct, watchful. Respects nothing but proof and procedure.
- **Possible Evolution:** The lawful hammer the player aims at the Syndicate/Hush; an obstacle if the player turns criminal; a casualty if the corrupt move against her.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 — Veteran/Knight-like** (AC 18, ~85 HP; Leadership; commands squads of **Guard/Veteran-like** watch). Solo danger: high; **never a default fight** — she arrests, she does not brawl for sport. Noncombat: comply, exonerate, or feed her a warrant.
- **Related:** city law; `Q_QUIET_COIN`, `Q_THE_BELLMANS_PRICE`; Kade, Sorrel, the Bellman.

## C28. Hierarch Casmir Vole — the relic-faith schismatic
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Three Thresholds clergy (a dissident faction under Mother Ysarra). **Location:** Highmourn; the Threshold Cathedral's lower chapels.
- **First Impression:** A magnetic, hollow-cheeked priest whose sermons on "the mercy owed the dead" draw crowds Ysarra wishes were smaller.
- **Public Role:** A rising, popular under-priest preaching that the city must *face* the failing rites, not hush them.
- **Voice:** Resonant, urgent, prophetic; "we have lied to the dead, and the dead have stopped believing us."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Force the Church to confront the crisis honestly; comfort the terrified faithful.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Vole has been quietly **studying the failed rites and is one bad idea from heresy** — he's begun to wonder if the dead *want* to be spoken to (a hair's breadth from Gravecaller thinking) and has met, once, with a Gravecaller emissary in the city. He doesn't yet grasp where that road ends. He can become a reform-ally, a city Gravecaller-bridge, or a catastrophe of misplaced compassion.
- **Fear:** That the Church's lie is damning the dead; that his own questions are heresy.
- **Leverage:** A devoted popular following; the moral high ground over Ysarra's cover-up.
- **What he knows:** The pattern of the city's failed rites (he's mapping them); the cover-up's shape; the Gravecaller contact.
- **What he wants from the player:** Allies and evidence to force the Church's honesty; answers about the dead.
- **What he can offer:** A popular movement, sanctuary, a city M1/M5 path, a Gravecaller bridge (dangerous).
- **How he can harm the player:** His movement can ignite panic; his drift toward the Gravecallers can implicate allies.
- **Relationship Links:** Under (and against) Mother Ysarra; quietly aligned with Grave-Magister Quayle's conscience; courted by a Gravecaller emissary; popular in the Sill.
- **Current Attitude:** Fervent, searching, glad of a sympathetic ear.
- **Possible Evolution:** The Church's honest reformer (ally), a city Gravecaller-bridge (grey), or a panic-igniting demagogue.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Priest-like** (AC 13, ~33 HP; *Cure Wounds, Bless, Spirit Guardians, Speak with Dead* he's only begun to dare). Noncombatant; his power is the crowd.
- **DM-Only Notes:** A clean way to bring the Gravecaller theme *into the city* without importing the fen cult. Keep his heresy a slow slide.
- **Related:** M1, M5; Secret 7 (the cover-up); Ysarra, Quayle, the Gravecallers, Renna Sill.

## C29. Grandmaster Edrin Cole — the foundry-guilds' overlord
> Surname note: "Edrin Cole" (Caradril guild-grandmaster) is distinct from "Cole Ashby" (Gravecaller, given name) and "Factor Maris Cole" (Coast Ledger). Common surname; keep full names.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** craft guilds (Grandmaster of the Crucible's combined guilds; above Foundress Beck Harrow). **Location:** the Crucible.
- **First Impression:** A massive, burn-scarred old master with a guildmaster's chain and the stillness of a man who has decided things for thirty years.
- **Public Role:** Grandmaster of Caradril's craft guilds; the Crucible's real power; a maker of the city's wealth.
- **Voice:** Low, deliberate, proud; speaks of the city as something he *built*.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** The guilds' supremacy; Caradril's industry; his legacy in iron and glass.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Cole has knowingly let the **relic-smelting** continue under Harrow because it's wildly profitable and the Ledger pays — and he has *seen* the "incidents" (a casting that screamed, thin-touched workers) and chosen profit. He is not a believer in anything but the guilds; he is the city's most powerful **willful denier** of the M3 horror, and the man who can *stop the smelting* if turned, threatened, or out-profited.
- **Fear:** The guilds losing primacy; the incidents reaching the council and shutting his foundries.
- **Leverage:** Control of the city's foundries and the relic-smelting; guild votes; deep pockets.
- **What he knows:** Exactly what's being smelted and why; the incidents; the Ledger/Remnant proxy-war over relics; who profits.
- **What he wants from the player:** Discretion, useful work, or to be left to his furnaces.
- **What he can offer:** Master-crafted gear/commissions, guild backing, the power to halt (or hide) the relic-smelting.
- **How he can harm the player:** Guild blacklisting; hired enforcers; burying evidence in the furnaces.
- **Relationship Links:** Above Beck Harrow (uses her as a buffer); paid by the Ledger (Vyre/Vell); his foreman Ordo is bribed by the Remnant; resented by the Sill labor (Renna).
- **Current Attitude:** Imperious, transactional, untroubled — for now.
- **Possible Evolution:** Turned to shut the smelting (a major M3 win); a hardened obstacle; a man undone by one incident too many.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Guard/Veteran-like with resources** (AC 14, ~40 HP; commands guild enforcers, **Guard/Thug-like**). Noncombatant by preference; fights with the guild, not his fists.
- **Related:** M3; `Q_THE_SMELTING`; Beck Harrow, Vyre, Ordo, Renna Sill.

## C30. Lector-General Sabine Ferrant — the Remnant's true believer
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Concord Remnant (inner circle; Quorrin's rival-peer). **Location:** the Lantern Reach; the Lamplighters' Hall.
- **First Impression:** A radiant, exacting scholar-orator who can make a lecture-hall weep for a civilization that died a century before they were born.
- **Public Role:** The Remnant's Lector-General — its institutional head and the public face of its mission to "restore the Concord."
- **Voice:** Soaring, certain, evangelical; "we are the memory of a kinder world, and memory is a duty."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Rebuild the Concord's wisdom and order; lead the Remnant to its destiny.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Ferrant is the Remnant's **true believer in the inner circle** — she knows (with Quorrin) that the Concord harvested the dead, and unlike Quorrin's cold ambition, she believes restarting it would be a **holy restoration of the natural order**. She is the *ideological* engine of the "seize/restore the harvest" path where Quorrin is its strategist — and the two quietly distrust each other (Quorrin wants power; Ferrant wants vindication). Disillusioning Briss strikes at her recruiting; turning Ferrant against Quorrin (or exposing their rift) can fracture the Remnant.
- **Fear:** That the Concord's sacrifice was meaningless; that Quorrin will seize what should be sanctified.
- **Leverage:** The Remnant's institutional loyalty; its idealist rank-and-file; the Lamplighters' Hall.
- **What she knows:** The harvest truth; the Remnant's plan; the rift with Quorrin; the Sealed Archive's contents (partial).
- **What she wants from the player:** A convert or a useful instrument for "the restoration"; the player kept ignorant of the cost.
- **What she can offer:** Remnant resources, Concord scholarship, a seat near the "seize/restore" endgame path.
- **How she can harm the player:** The Remnant's full institutional weight; turning the idealist rank-and-file against an exposer.
- **Relationship Links:** Inner-circle peer and rival of Quorrin Vane; above Briss (whose naivety she finds *useful*) and Dree (her enforcer-archivist); courted by Vyre's monopoly overture (which she'd rather sanctify than sell).
- **Current Attitude:** Welcoming, evangelical, probing for a convert.
- **Possible Evolution:** The "restore the harvest" claimant if Quorrin falls; the wedge that splits the Remnant; a true-believer final rival at the shrine.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 — Mage/Priest-like** scaled to tier (AC 13–15, ~55–95 HP; *Counterspell, Hold Person, Sending, Wall of Force*, Remembrance relics; inspires followers). Wins by conviction and resources; never a cheap ambush.
- **DM-Only Notes:** Use her to give the Remnant *two* heads — Quorrin (ambition) and Ferrant (faith) — so disillusioning the rank-and-file actually matters. Their rift is a player lever.
- **Related:** M6, M9; the "seize/restore" ending; Quorrin, Briss, Dree, Vyre.

## C31. "Mother" Silque — the Syndicate's hidden head
> Elevation/depth note: builds *above* "Tidewife" Sorrel (a Syndicate boss). Sorrel runs streets; Silque owns the trade.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Salt Syndicate (the true head, above Sorrel). **Location:** the Sill and the Sunken Wards' dry edges; rarely seen.
- **First Impression (when finally met):** A small, elegant, unhurried woman who pours your tea herself and lets you realize, slowly, how little of the conversation you control.
- **Public Role:** A rumor — "Mother" of the Salt Syndicate, the quiet-coin trade's true owner; most assume she's a story Sorrel tells.
- **Voice:** Soft, gracious, patient; "drink your tea, dear. We have time, and the dead have nothing but."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Own the quiet-coin trade utterly; keep the Syndicate fed and invisible.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Silque **understands the quiet-coin relics better than anyone outside the Court or the Remnant inner circle** — she has quietly experimented, keeps several "talking" relics, and has begun to suspect the trade touches something far larger than profit. She is *curious*, not zealous: she would deal with the Court, the Remnant, *or* the player for the right price and the right answers. The criminal underworld's window onto M3/M6 — and a wild card no faction controls.
- **Fear:** Being made a tool of powers she can't see; the relics' "wrongness" reaching her.
- **Leverage:** The whole quiet-coin trade; "talking" relics; the Wards' smuggling routes; everyone's vices.
- **What she knows:** The relic-trade's true depth; which relics "remember"; routes into the Sunken Wards; the city's appetites.
- **What she wants from the player:** Answers about the relics; a clever, deniable agent; a stake in whatever the trade really is.
- **What she can offer:** Black-market relics (incl. clue-relics), Wards routes, criminal muscle, a uniquely *informed* underworld ally.
- **How she can harm the player:** The Syndicate's full shadow-weight; a debt that never closes; selling them to a rival.
- **Relationship Links:** True head over Sorrel; rival of the Bellman (the Hush); uneasy truce with the Ledger (Vyre); hunted by Sister-Commander Ansele; a buyer the Remnant and the Court would both court.
- **Current Attitude:** Gracious, patient, intensely curious about a capable newcomer.
- **Possible Evolution:** An informed criminal ally on the M3/M6 path; a dangerous rival claimant for relic-power; the player's debt-holder.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 — Assassin/Mastermind-like** (AC 15, ~65 HP; *poisons, evasion*, commands Spy/Assassin crews; never fights fair). Solo danger: high if crossed in her own ground; she prefers leverage to blades. Noncombat: deal, trade answers, out-inform her.
- **DM-Only Notes:** She is the underworld's *intelligent* take on the central mystery — a counterweight to the zealots and ideologues. Keep her unaligned; she's a price, not a cause.
- **Related:** M3, M6; the quiet-coin trade; Sorrel, the Bellman, Vyre, the Sunken Wards.

## C32. Magister Ophir Lensk — the Reach's enemy on the council
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Tidewater Council (the faction that wants the Reach *kept poor and useful*). **Location:** the Magisterium.
- **First Impression:** A handsome, reasonable magister who speaks of the frontier with warm condescension and votes to keep it on its knees.
- **Public Role:** Leader of the council bloc that profits from the Reach's dependence; the chief obstacle to any Caradrese aid.
- **Voice:** Genial, paternal, dismissive; "the Reach is a child that must not be spoiled with charity."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Keep the Reach a cheap, controlled hinterland that feeds Caradrese profit.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Lensk is **personally invested in the Ledger's Reach operations** (relic-salvage, the toll-farms, the salt-trade) and quietly takes a cut — exposing this is the player's lever to break his bloc. He is **not** part of the conspiracy and knows nothing of the harvest; he is ordinary greed wearing a statesman's smile. A clean, hateable, *mundane* antagonist for the city's politics — a foil to the cosmic stakes.
- **Fear:** The Reach's crisis becoming Caradril's problem (and his profits drying up); his Ledger cut exposed.
- **Leverage:** A council bloc; trade contracts; the city's complacent self-interest.
- **What he knows:** The Reach's economic worth to Caradril; his own (and others') Ledger cuts; how to bury a petition.
- **What he wants from the player:** Nothing — he'd prefer they go back where they came from; a useful tool against rivals at most.
- **What he can offer:** (To a corrupt player) lucrative contracts and protection; otherwise, obstruction.
- **How he can harm the player:** Council obstruction; trade blacklisting; framing reform as ruin.
- **Relationship Links:** Bloc-rival of Dann; ally-of-convenience to Tolm against the Ledger *and* a secret Ledger profiteer (he plays both); contemptuous of the Reach petitioners.
- **Current Attitude:** Dismissive, untroubled, faintly amused by an upjumped frontier agent.
- **Possible Evolution:** Broken by exposure of his Ledger cut; a recurring political nemesis; a reluctant convert if the crisis reaches the city.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble-like** (AC 12, ~27 HP). Noncombatant.
- **DM-Only Notes:** He is the *mundane* villain the city needs so not every antagonist is cosmic. His exposure feeds `Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER` and Brail's swing vote.
- **Related:** `Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER`; Dann, Brail, Tolm, Vyre.
