## AI Use

**Light NPC scaffolds for the full-continent placeholder regions.** These are *map-authoritative placeholders only* — 2–4 named figures per distant region who could be encountered far-future, or (more usefully) who **send agents, rumors, letters, or trade-goods into the campaign frontier**. Do NOT deep-build these regions or invent major lore from these stubs. Each figure has a name, role, one hook, and one way they can reach into the early campaign. **No new factions, gods, central mysteries, or artifacts.** Combat is left as a one-word tag; stat fully only if the player ever reaches them.

> Primary use: a **rumor source, a letter, a visiting agent, or a far-trade contact** — the horizon reaching inward. Keep them thin.

## Verdance Reaches (up the Verdance, inland from Caradril; Lvl 8–12)
- **Factor-General Wessel Crane** — Cinder Ledger regional head. Hook: Vyre's superior in the inland trade; the Ledger's reach *above* Caradril. Inward: sends Ledger directives/auditors down to Caradril. [noncombat]
- **Sub-Lector Hadwin Vael** — Concord Remnant scholar. Hook: corresponds with Quorrin; the Remnant's inland chapter. Inward: letters and rival scholars arriving in Caradril. [low]
- **River-Captain Mossa Drenn** — Verdance trade-skipper. Hook: runs the great river-barges. Inward: a lift further inland; far-trade rumor. [guard-like]

## Glassmere League (central, on the Glasswater; mercantile city-league; Lvl 9–13)

## Usage Notes
- **Do not deep-build** from these. They are horizon-figures: a rumor, a letter, a visiting agent, a far-trade good.
- Two threads are *deliberately* seeded for far-future continuity (keep oblique): the **Mourner old-songs network** extends south (Senna Crale), east (Onn Greenward), and to the Sunmark — the M6 folk-truth is continent-wide; and the **Ledger/Remnant** have reach *above* Caradril (Crane, Hadwin Vael). Neither is to be advanced in early play.
- Surname-collision guards ("no kin") are noted; honor them.
