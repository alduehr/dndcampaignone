## AI Use

Stage 9 pass-2 major NPCs (M33–M50). These bring the major roster to **50**, filling the gaps the first 32 left: the **underrepresented Reach settlements** (Harrowgast, Kettle Bridge, Saltmargin, Greywater Holm, Candlewick), **Caradril districts without a major anchor** (the Sill, Ashmarket, the Hush, Sunken Wards), **third majors for thin factions** (Wardens, Compact, Ledger, Gravecallers), and **social-play / cross-pressure / moral-dilemma figures** who are not faction-aligned villains.

**Player-Facing blocks are surfaceable; Secret / DM-Only blocks are earned through play.** Mechanics use D&D 5e/2024-compatible reference profiles, not copied stat blocks. Numbering continues from `CARADRIL_MAJOR_NPCS.md` (C25–C32) as M33+. **No NPC here is a new faction, god, central mystery, or artifact** — all populate the existing world. Hollow Court remains DM-only; none of these NPCs knows the apex truth.

> Every major below answers the four-question test: *Why would the player talk to them? What can they provide? What might they hide? What pressure are they under?*

## M33. Dredgemaster Osrin Vole — Harrowgast's mine-lord
> Surname note: "Osrin Vole" (Harrowgast mine-lord) is distinct from Hierarch Casmir **Vole** (Caradril) and Mother Senna **Vole** (Caradril Mourner) — common frontier surname; keep full names.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Independent (mine-owner; Ledger-contracted). **Location:** Harrowgast, the spoil-heaps and the Deep Adit head.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A broad, grey-dusted man with a cough he ignores and a foreman's voice, who counts every barrow of "old glass" out of his ground like it owes him.
- **Public Role:** Owner of Harrowgast's deepest workings; the man who decides who digs, who eats, and who goes into the Deep Adit.
- **Voice:** Loud, blunt, proud; talks about the mountain as a debtor he means to collect from.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Make the strike of his life out of the Heights' relic-salvage before the seam (or his lungs) gives out.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Vole *knows* his deepest crews are coming back "thin" (thin-touch) and going missing in the Deep Adit, and he is hiding it from the Compact and the families because stopping the dig would ruin him. He's begun bonding desperate men into the deep against their will (debt-bondage). A grubby M10/Deep-Adit witness and a squeeze-victim of his own greed — **not** a conspirator, just a man who chose profit over his people.
- **Fear:** The mountain "taking" the rest of his crews; the Compact shutting the Adit; the cough that's killing him.
- **Leverage:** Controls the only deep workings and the relic-flow out of Harrowgast; holds men's debts.
- **What he knows:** Exactly how deep is too deep; who's gone thin and who's vanished (M10); what the deep "old glass" really fetches; the Deep Adit's lower reaches.
- **What he wants from the player:** Muscle to keep his bonded men digging, or a "guide" to bring back the missing — and his silence kept.
- **What he can offer:** Deep-Adit access, relic-salvage, mine-gear, paid (grim) work; an M10 deep-dread witness if pressed.
- **How he can harm the player:** His foremen and bonded toughs; turning Harrowgast against an outsider "scaring off the work"; sealing the Adit with the player inside.
- **Relationship Links:** Contracts with the Ledger (Nessa Coyle buys his salvage); employs Dig-boss Hennet (who's near breaking over it) and "Thin" Coll; Overseer Brack (Compact) looks the other way; despised by Toolwright's-widow Sarn Garn and Cold-Drum Vey, who keep the disappeared-tally.
- **Current Attitude:** Transactional, defensive, increasingly desperate. Tests whether you're a threat to the dig.
- **Possible Evolution:** Shut down and exposed (an M10 win + a freed bonded crew); a hardened obstacle who seals the Adit; or a man the deep finally takes, his office found empty.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Guard/Veteran-like with resources** (AC 14, ~45 HP; commands 4–6 **Thug/Guard-like** foremen). Solo danger: moderate (in his own town/mine); low on open ground. Noncombat: buy him out, expose the disappearances to Brack/the families, free the bonded men, out-guide him into the Adit. **Scaling:** add bonded-crew numbers / deep-Adit hazards.
- **DM-Only Notes:** He makes Harrowgast a *place with an owner* and gives the Deep Adit a human gate. His secret is a clean M10 corroboration of "Thin" Coll's omen. Never let him name the harvest — he thinks the mountain is simply "bad ground."
- **Related:** M3, M10; the Deep Adit; Hennet, "Thin" Coll, Nessa Coyle, Brack.

## M34. Bridge-Keeper Mahalf Dunmore — Kettle Bridge's true power
> Kin note: kin to the minor NPC "Keeper Dunmore" (the Drowned Kettle inn) — Mahalf is his elder brother and the family's head; the inn is the family's lesser holding.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Independent (toll-and-ferry family); Compact-licensed, Ledger-indebted. **Location:** Kettle Bridge, the span and the family's bridge-house.
- **First Impression:** A heavyset, watchful man who greets every traveler by trade and tonnage, and seems to own the bridge the way a spider owns a web.
- **Public Role:** Head of the family that has kept Kettle Bridge's span, ferry, and toll for four generations; the man you must satisfy to cross the Mirewend here.
- **Voice:** Genial, proprietary, always pricing; "the bridge has a price, friend — it always has."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Keep the bridge his family's, and keep it standing as the river rises and the broken arch worsens.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Mahalf is quietly drowning in Ledger debt (Bly's toll-farm is squeezing him out) and has started letting the Salt Syndicate's smugglers and Gull's punts pass the broken arch unlogged for coin — and he's heard the "singing" under the span (the Sunken Tollhouse) and is terrified the bridge sits on something wrong. A turnable river-gatekeeper and an M2/M5 witness sitting *on top of* a Concord node, who'd back whoever can break Bly's squeeze.
- **Fear:** Losing the bridge to the Ledger; the broken arch (and whatever sings under it) taking the whole span.
- **Leverage:** Controls the only safe Mirewend crossing for miles; knows every cargo that passes.
- **What he knows:** Who and what crosses the river (M3 smuggling); the broken arch and the "singing" beneath it (M2/M5, the Sunken Tollhouse); Bly's and Cray's corners.
- **What he wants from the player:** Help breaking Bly's toll-farm squeeze, or escorting/clearing whatever's fouling the broken arch — and a cut, of course.
- **What he can offer:** Guaranteed crossing, river-traffic intelligence, an in to the Sunken Tollhouse, an anti-Ledger lever at the river-choke.
- **How he can harm the player:** Close the crossing to them; sell their movements to Cray, Bly, or the Syndicate; "lose" them at the broken arch.
- **Relationship Links:** Squeezed by Quenna Bly (Ledger toll-farmer) and rival of Bailiff Cray; uses Bargemaster Tibb's crews; brother to Innkeep Dunmore; lets Gull Heddwyn pass.
- **Current Attitude:** Cordial, mercenary, quietly cornered. Warms fast to anyone who threatens Bly.
- **Possible Evolution:** Freed from the Ledger and turned honest ally at the river-gate; a casualty if the arch (or the Syndicate) collapses on him; a smuggling-lord if the player ignores him.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Commoner/Guard-like with resources** (AC 13, ~28 HP; commands bridge-toughs and ferrymen, **Guard-like**). Noncombatant by trade. Solo danger: low. Noncombat: break Bly's squeeze, clear the arch, out-bid the Syndicate.
- **DM-Only Notes:** He anchors Kettle Bridge (which had only secondaries) and turns the river-crossing into a recurring node with its own politics. His "singing under the span" is a low-key M2/M5 on-ramp to the Sunken Tollhouse dungeon.
- **Related:** M2, M3, M5; the Sunken Tollhouse; Quenna Bly, Cray, Tibb, Gull, the Salt Syndicate.

## M35. Pan-Mother Decca Rull — *(elevated to Major; Saltmargin labor anchor)*
> Elevated from secondary (`SECONDARY_NPCS.md`). Decca already exists as the Saltmargin pan-foreman; Stage 9 raises her to major as the Reach's labor conscience and the player's cleanest non-faction working-class ally. The secondary stub remains as quick-reference.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Independent (salt-pan labor leader; no faction allegiance). **Location:** Saltmargin, the salt-pans and the workers' rows.
- **First Impression:** A salt-burned, broad-shouldered woman with cracked hands and a level stare who speaks for three hundred pan-workers and means every word.
- **Public Role:** Foreman and unofficial leader of Saltmargin's salt-pan crews; the one the workers follow and the wharf-factors fear.
- **Voice:** Blunt, grievance-ready, dryly funny; "fair wage for foul work — that's the whole of it."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** A fair price for the salt and a fair wage for the crews, against Marlow's rigged scale and the Ledger's squeeze.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Decca quietly holds the workers' evidence that Wharf-Factor Iss Marlow rigs the scale and skims relic-salvage (M3 proof, kept hidden via Net-wife Sereth), and she's *this* close to a strike that could either crack the Ledger's salt-corner open or get the pan-rows burned. She is honest to a fault — which makes her a dangerous lever: hand her proof and protection and she'll move the whole town; betray her and she'll never trust an outsider again.
- **Fear:** Leading her people into ruin; the Ledger breaking the crews the way it breaks farms.
- **Leverage:** Three hundred workers who'll move when she says; the hidden evidence against Marlow.
- **What she knows:** The real salt tonnage and Marlow's skim (M3); who among the desperate is drifting to Gull's smuggling; the pan-rows' loyalties.
- **What she wants from the player:** Proof against Marlow she can act on, and a hand strong enough that the strike doesn't end in blood.
- **What she can offer:** The labor-force as muscle or witnesses, an M3 corroboration against the Ledger, safe lodging among the crews, an honest working-class ally in a region of grey patrons.
- **How she can harm the player:** Turn Saltmargin's workers against a strikebreaker or a Ledger tool; her strike can lock down the salt-trade the player may need.
- **Relationship Links:** Backed by Net-wife Sereth (holds her evidence); opposed by Iss Marlow and (above him) the Ledger; respected by Pan-Mother's parallel Headman Strand on the Coast; wary of Gull Heddwyn poaching her desperate.
- **Current Attitude:** Guarded, fair, testing. Opens fully to anyone who proves they're not the Ledger's creature.
- **Possible Evolution:** The leader of a successful strike that cracks the Ledger's salt-monopoly (an M3/Monopoly-clock win); a martyr if the strike turns violent; a broken, exiled foreman if betrayed.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 — Commoner/Guard-like** (AC 13, ~26 HP; rallies crowds; can field dozens of **Commoner/Guard-like** workers as a mob/witness-force, not a war-band). Solo danger: low personally, high as a mob-mover. Noncombat: her whole value is social — proof, protection, solidarity.
- **DM-Only Notes:** She is the campaign's clearest *labor* lever and a non-grey ally amid Saltmargin's compromised officials. Her strike is a Monopoly-clock pressure valve the player can trigger.
- **Related:** M3, Monopoly clock; Iss Marlow, Net-wife Sereth, Gull Heddwyn, the Cinder Ledger.

## M36. Fenwife "Mother Osset" — the Greyfens Gravecaller cell-mother
> The Gravecallers' third major (beyond the Tallow Man / Knell / Cole Ashby), giving the cult depth at the *cell* level and a face in the Greywater Holm / Greyfens spread. Name note: "Osset" is her cult-name; she has no registered collision.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Gravecallers (cell-mother of the Greyfens/Greywater Holm cell). **Location:** The Drowned Blind and the fen-edge around Greywater Holm.
- **First Impression:** A stout, sorrowful fen-woman with reed-stained hands who tends the bereaved like a midwife of grief — and who never seems surprised by what the dead say.
- **Public Role:** The "comforting" elder of the Greyfens cult-cell; where Knell *recruits*, Mother Osset *keeps* — she runs the day-to-day rites of the drowned and the failed-buried.
- **Voice:** Warm, doubled-soft like all the cult, motherly; "hush now — they're not gone, only waiting for a last word."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Comfort the grieving of the dying holm; give the drowned and failed-buried "their last word."
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Osset genuinely keeps a *cell* of channeled dead at the Drowned Blind and has begun to suspect, like Knell, that the dead she comforts are using the cult — but unlike Knell she's too deep and too needed to leave; she shelters Cole Ashby's radicals against her better judgment and could be the player's path to (or warning against) "the Breaking." She knows the fen-routes to the shrine's approaches that even Drane fears. A humane, tragic mid-level cult face — the cult's *mercy* made a person, sitting one bad decision from catastrophe.
- **Fear:** That Cole's radicals will do something the dead can't be called back from; that she's damned the grieving she meant to save.
- **Leverage:** The Holm's grief-stricken trust her; she holds the Blind's rites and the deep-fen approaches.
- **What she knows:** The cult's cell-network and the Drowned Blind's rites; the deep-fen routes toward the shrine (M5); that Cole has breaking-tools (and dreads it); fragments of what the dead "say" (M6-oblique).
- **What she wants from the player:** Someone to hear the dead honestly, and (secretly) someone to stop Cole before "the Breaking."
- **What she can offer:** A gentler door into the Gravecallers than Cole; the deep-fen approaches; an M5/M6-oblique window; a defector-in-waiting against the radicals.
- **How she can harm the player:** Her cell can turn the grieving Holm against an outsider; her rites can draw worse things; her shelter of Cole's radicals endangers anyone near.
- **Relationship Links:** Cell-mother under the Tallow Man; kin-in-spirit to Sister Knell (both wavering); shelters Cole Ashby's radicals; tolerated by heretic Mourner Elder Mabb Tay; runs Fenwife Sho and Den Hask's near-recruits.
- **Current Attitude:** Gentle, watchful, sorrowful; tests whether you mock the dead before she opens any door.
- **Possible Evolution:** A defector who helps the player stop "the Breaking"; a casualty of Cole's radicalism; or, if pushed, the keeper who lets the catastrophe happen.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 lite — Cult Fanatic/Priest-like** (AC 12, ~38 HP; *Speak with Dead* at will, *Calm Emotions, Hold Person, Inflict Wounds*; calls a spectral Remembrance like the Tallow Man, weaker). Solo danger: moderate in the fen; flees rather than dies cheaply. Noncombat: she *wants* an honest listener — talk, don't fight.
- **DM-Only Notes:** She gives the Gravecallers a third dimension between the Tallow Man's mystery, Knell's recruiting, and Cole's zealotry: the *cell's* compassionate, compromised daily face. Keep her M6 fragments oblique; she does not know the apex truth.
- **Related:** M5, M6 (oblique); the Drowned Blind, Mother Tongue Pool; the Tallow Man, Knell, Cole Ashby, Mabb Tay, Drane.

## M37. Hayward-Elder Tomas Greel — *(elevated to Major; Candlewick / the Script gate)*
> Elevated from secondary (`SECONDARY_NPCS.md`). Tomas Greel already exists as Candlewick's village head; Stage 9 raises him to major because Candlewick is the campaign's quiet **Concord Script gate** (the Whispering Cairn archive-stone) and he is its human lock. The secondary stub remains.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Reachward Compact (Candlewick hayward); Remnant-tempted. **Location:** Candlewick and the Whispering Cairn.
- **First Impression:** A careful, soft-spoken village elder with ink on his fingers and a worried courtesy, who weighs a stranger the way he weighs a contract.
- **Public Role:** Hayward (village head) of Candlewick, keeper of its peace and its strange old "humming stone."
- **Voice:** Measured, hedging, fond of "we must be careful here"; the most literate man in the south Reach and quietly proud of it.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Keep Candlewick safe and prosperous, and protect the old archive-stone he half-venerates and half-fears.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Greel sits on the **Whispering Cairn's archive-stone** — a clear Concord Script source (an M6/M9 fragment) — and the Remnant (via Briss/Lector-correspondence, through his daughter Juna) has offered to *buy* it. He's tempted by the money and terrified of the blasphemy, and the stone has begun to "hum" worse as the crisis deepens. He's the gatekeeper to one of the cleanest written-truth fragments outside Caradril — and a man being quietly worked by the Remnant. He does not know what the Script says; he only knows the stone matters.
- **Fear:** Selling something holy and damning his village; the stone "waking"; being out of his depth among scholars and factions.
- **Leverage:** Controls access to (and the fate of) the archive-stone; the village's trust.
- **What he knows:** The archive-stone's location and behavior (M2/M6 environmental); the Remnant's offer and reach into the village; that "the stone's gone wrong" lately.
- **What he wants from the player:** Honest counsel on what to do with the stone — sell it, hide it, or have it *read* by someone he trusts more than the Remnant.
- **What he can offer:** Access to the Whispering Cairn and the archive-stone (a major M6/M9 clue gate); lodging; a sincere Compact ally; an early read on the Remnant's recruiting methods.
- **How he can harm the player:** Sell the stone out from under them to the Remnant; close the Cairn; his caution can stall a clue-trail at a critical moment.
- **Relationship Links:** Father of Juna Greel (his Remnant-tempted heir); courted by Lector Briss's correspondence; serves the Compact (uneasy with Reke's distant schemes); leans on Chandler-master Yorrin Tay and Chapel-clerk Vesna.
- **Current Attitude:** Cautious, courteous, conflicted. Trusts slowly; values honesty over coin.
- **Possible Evolution:** Entrusts the stone to the player (opening the Script-fragment cleanly); sells it to the Remnant (a clue-trail setback the player must chase to Caradril); or his village is dragged into the Remnant's orbit through Juna.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble/Commoner-like** (AC 11, ~20 HP; literate, high Insight). Noncombatant. Solo danger: none. Use a Noble-like social profile.
- **DM-Only Notes:** He makes Candlewick a real Script-gate node so the player isn't forced to Caradril for early written truth. Keep the stone's content gated to Act 3 per `REVELATION_MAP.md`; Greel is the *access*, not the *answer*.
- **Related:** M2, M6, M9; the Whispering Cairn; Juna Greel, Lector Briss, Yorrin Tay, the Concord Remnant.

## M38. Renna Sill — *(elevated to Major; the Sill / thin-born advocate)*
> Elevated from secondary (`SECONDARY_NPCS.md`). Renna already exists as the Sill's community leader; Stage 9 raises her to major as Caradril's poor-quarter conscience, the city's living M1/M5/M10 echo, and the anchor for the district that had no major. The secondary stub remains.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Independent (the Sill's refugee/thin-born advocate). **Location:** Caradril, the Sill (the poor riverside undercity) and the Brass Ferry.
- **First Impression:** A fierce, exhausted woman with a drowned-quarter's whole grief on her shoulders, who counts her dead by name and dares the city to look away.
- **Public Role:** The unelected leader and advocate of the Sill — the voice of Caradril's refugees, thin-born, and forgotten poor.
- **Voice:** Fierce, plain, unsleeping; "no one drowns in my district unseen. Not one."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Protect the Sill's people — the refugees fleeing the frontier crisis, the thin-born the city despises, the poor the rites have failed.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Renna shelters a Reach "wrong-come-back" (a failed-rite revenant fled to the city) and a worsening cluster of thin-born — the clearest *city-side* window on the thinning and the harvest's leakage (M1/M5/M10), made human. She hides them from the Watch, the Church, and the Syndicate alike, and she's begun to grasp that the frontier "rumors" are real and coming for the city. She doesn't know the cause — but she's the player's surest path to *seeing* what's happening, made personal, in Caradril itself.
- **Fear:** The city "cleansing" the Sill of its thin-born; the day the frontier crisis reaches her district in full.
- **Leverage:** The loyalty of the Sill's hundreds; sanctuary the powerful can't reach; the city's guilty conscience.
- **What she knows:** Who in the city is "coming back wrong" or going thin (M1/M5/M10); which refugees carry frontier news; the Sill's hidden ways and the Brass Ferry routes.
- **What she wants from the player:** Protection for her people, supplies, and someone who'll *believe* the Sill's dead and dying matter — and act.
- **What she can offer:** Sanctuary in the Sill (a safe base the powerful avoid), the city's most direct M1/M5/M10 evidence, refugee intelligence from across the frontier, a network of the overlooked.
- **How she can harm the player:** The Sill closes ranks against a betrayer; she can expose a player who uses the poor cynically; her sheltered "wrong-come-back" is a danger if mishandled.
- **Relationship Links:** Allied with Widow Tace (shelters refugees) and Boat-priest Wen Marrick; quietly sympathized with by Captain Kade (Watch) and Almoner Brother Wend; threatened by the Syndicate's reach into the Sill and the Church's contempt; a city echo of Grandmother Wend's compassion.
- **Current Attitude:** Wary, fierce, desperate for a powerful ally who isn't using her.
- **Possible Evolution:** The player's city-side conscience and M1/M5/M10 mentor; a martyr if the Sill is "cleansed"; the organizer of the poor against the complacent city.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Commoner-like** (AC 12, ~22 HP; rallies the Sill's crowds). Noncombatant; her power is people and moral weight. Solo danger: none personally. Fallback: Commoner who summons a protective mob.
- **DM-Only Notes:** She brings the *central mystery* into the city at human scale without naming it — the thinning made a face. Keep her sheltered revenant a careful, gated scene, never a combat set-piece by default.
- **Related:** M1, M5, M10; the Sill, the Brass Ferry, the Sunken Wards; Widow Tace, Wen Marrick, Kade, the Salt Syndicate.

## M39. Auctioneer Cassia Mourn — the Ashmarket's relic-queen
> Surname note: "Cassia Mourn" is a Caradril trade-family name; **no kin** to the Mourners' Circle (the shared root is coincidental city/folk naming). Keep full name; she is emphatically *not* a Mourner.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Independent (the Ashmarket's licensed relic-auction house); Ledger- and Syndicate-courted. **Location:** Caradril, the Ashmarket (relic/salvage district).
- **First Impression:** A glittering, theatrical auctioneer in salvage-pearl finery whose gavel sets the price of every relic in the city, and who can read a buyer's want across a crowded floor.
- **Public Role:** Mistress of the Ashmarket's great relic-auction house — the legitimate face of the relic-and-salvage trade, where "old glass" becomes wealth.
- **Voice:** Honeyed, performative, razor-quick on a number; "a bid, a bid — who'll honor the dead with a bid?"
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Own the relic-market; make the Ashmarket *the* place the continent's salvage is priced and sold.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Cassia has quietly realized that some relics she sells "remember" — that the quiet-coin trade and her legitimate floor handle the *same* dread substance (M3) — and rather than recoil (like Imris) or hoard (like Silque), she sees a *monopoly of authentication*: if she alone can certify which relics are "real," she controls the trade above Ledger, Syndicate, and Remnant. She is the relic-economy's most dangerous *neutral*: she'll sell to anyone, certify for anyone, and bury anyone who threatens her floor. A clean M3 commercial window and a wild-card rival to Vyre, Silque, and the Remnant — driven by ambition, not zeal.
- **Fear:** A rival (Silque, the Ledger, or the Remnant) cornering authentication before she does; the relics' "wrongness" becoming public and crashing her market.
- **Leverage:** Sets the price and provenance of every legal relic in Caradril; her floor is where every faction must come to buy or sell openly.
- **What she knows:** Who buys and sells relics and for what (M3); which relics "read wrong" (her appraisers, incl. Hawk-eyed Sala, flag them); the seam between the legal and quiet-coin trades.
- **What she wants from the player:** A discreet, capable agent to source rare relics and *quietly verify* which ones "remember" — and to remove rivals to her authentication monopoly.
- **What she can offer:** Legal relic-access and appraisal (the cleanest M3 commercial path), market intelligence on every faction's relic-dealings, wealth, a neutral foothold in the city's trade.
- **How she can harm the player:** Blacklist them from the market; tip a rival faction onto them; her ambition makes her loyalty worthless if they become inconvenient.
- **Relationship Links:** Rival-courtier of both Vyre (Ledger) and "Mother" Silque (Syndicate) for the relic-trade's control; employs Appraiser Mooren and the "thin-touch"-sensitive fence Hawk-eyed Sala; courted by the Remnant for authentication; watched by Sister-Commander Ansele.
- **Current Attitude:** Charming, transactional, coolly ambitious. Everyone is a buyer, a seller, or an obstacle.
- **Possible Evolution:** The relic-economy's kingmaker (an M3 hub the player can use or must break); a casualty of the Ledger/Syndicate/Remnant three-way once she's too powerful; a reluctant exposer if the "wrongness" threatens her market.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Noble/Spy-like with resources** (AC 13, ~30 HP; commands market-guards and bought informants, **Guard/Spy-like**). Noncombatant; fights with price, provenance, and information. Solo danger: low personally, high commercially.
- **DM-Only Notes:** She makes the Ashmarket a *named* power and gives M3 a purely commercial, ambition-driven face distinct from Vyre's monopoly-zeal and Silque's curiosity. She is a *price*, not a cause — keep her unaligned.
- **Related:** M3; the Ashmarket; Vyre, "Mother" Silque, the Remnant, Appraiser Mooren, Hawk-eyed Sala, Ansele.

## M40. The Bellman — *(elevated to Major; the Hush / information apex)*
> Elevated from secondary (`SECONDARY_NPCS.md`). The Bellman already exists as Caradril's information broker; Stage 9 raises him to major as the city's leverage-hub, the anchor of the Hush (a district-faction with no major), and the player's costly shortcut into M3/M6/M8. The secondary stub remains.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** The Hush (its head and chief broker). **Location:** Caradril, citywide; never the same room twice (a voice, a coat, a bell-token).
- **First Impression:** You rarely see him plainly — a grey coat, a low voice from the next table, a small brass bell-token left where a name should be. By the time you've placed him, he's pricing what you came to hide.
- **Public Role:** The city's premier information broker; "the Bellman" of the Hush, who knows what everyone would pay to keep quiet.
- **Voice:** Unhurried, precise, never quite seen; "everything's known to someone, friend. I simply know the someone."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Know everything; owe nothing; sell knowledge at the price the buyer can least afford to refuse.

## M47. Toll-Boss Cady Renn — *(elevated to Major; Tollwood / the Tollmen)*
> Elevated from secondary (`SECONDARY_NPCS.md`). Cady Renn already exists as the Tollmen's leader; Stage 9 raises her to major as Tollwood's grey-power anchor (the region was thin on majors), the face of the minor Tollmen faction, and a recurring moral-grey ally-or-obstacle on the East Road. The secondary stub remains.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** The Tollmen (leader); ex-Reachward Compact. **Location:** Tollwood, Tollstone Cross and the East Road.
- **First Impression:** A weathered, level-eyed woman with a soldier's bearing gone to banditry, who names her toll like a fair price and watches your hands while she says it.
- **Public Role:** Boss of the Tollmen — the East Road's toll-bandits; the woman every Tollwood traveler must satisfy or evade.
- **Voice:** Dry, tired, soldier-plain; "it's a toll, not a robbery — pay it and the road stays open. That's more than the Compact gives you."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Keep her people fed and the road *hers*, since the Compact abandoned it; keep the toll from becoming the slaughter Skell wants.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Renn is a Compact deserter who took to the road when the council abandoned Tollwood's protection — and she's the *better* of two evils: she actually keeps worse predators (and the deep-wood's dangers, and Skell's murderousness) off the East Road, charging toll instead of killing. She's losing control of her own gang to her murderous lieutenant **Skell**, who plots to oust her and turn the toll into open slaughter and relic-robbing. She'd take a pardon and honest road-warden work in a heartbeat if the Compact returned — and she's the player's clearest path to *fixing* the East Road: turn her (against Skell, back toward the Compact) and the road heals; break her and Skell's bloodier reign begins. A grey, sympathetic frontier-power, **not** a cult or conspiracy.
- **Fear:** Skell's coup and the slaughter that follows it; her people starving if the toll fails; dying a bandit after a soldier's life.
- **Leverage:** Controls the East Road's choke; a gang of road-toughs (loyalists and Skell's faction); knowledge of the wood's dangers.
- **What she knows:** The East Road and its real dangers (deep-wood predators, the cult's spread, the relic-traffic); Skell's coup-plan (she sees it coming); which travelers the Ledger marks; the Compact's abandonment of Tollwood.
- **What she wants from the player:** Help against Skell, or a path back to legitimacy — a pardon, the Compact's return, honest road-warden work for her people.
- **What she can offer:** Safe passage on the East Road (solo-safety through a dangerous region), the gang as muscle or guides against worse threats, intelligence on the wood and the relic-traffic, a turnable grey-power that can *heal* the road.
- **How she can harm the player:** Toll, detain, or rob them; set the gang on them; if she falls to Skell, the road becomes lethal.
- **Relationship Links:** Leads the Tollmen over loyal Toll-Sergeant Brec, Quartermaster Old Vey, and the turn-lever clerk Wenny — *against* her murderous lieutenant **Skell**; abandoned by the Compact (would return to Capt. Brannoch Vey / Magister-Reeve Sela Coalmont's authority); preys (reluctantly) on the road's traffic, including the Ledger's (Hesk Dorr).
- **Current Attitude:** Wary, transactional, quietly hopeful for a way out; respects competence and straight dealing, despises cruelty (Skell's kind).
- **Possible Evolution:** Pardoned and turned road-warden, the East Road healed (a clean regional win); deposed and killed by Skell (the road's bloodier turn); a recurring grey toll-power if left alone.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 lite — Veteran/Spy-like** (AC 16, ~58 HP; commands the Tollmen — a mix of **Bandit/Thug/Scout-like** road-toughs; tactically sharp from her soldier days). Solo danger: moderate-high *at the toll with her gang*; low one-on-one and negotiable. **Always telegraphed** (the toll-station and her reputation precede her). Noncombat: pay the toll, turn her against Skell (via Brec/Wenny), broker her pardon, out-scout the road. **Scaling:** adjust gang size; Skell's faction can be a separate, nastier fight.
- **DM-Only Notes:** Tollwood needed a non-Mourner major and a face for the Tollmen; Renn is a grey ally-or-obstacle whose turn *fixes a region*. She pairs with Skell (secondary) as the toll-war's moral axis. She is firmly *frontier-grey*, never tied to cult or harvest.
- **Related:** Tollwood clocks, the toll-war; the East Road; Skell, Toll-Sergeant Brec, Old Vey, Wenny, Brannoch Vey, Hesk Dorr, Magister-Reeve Sela Coalmont.

## M48. Harbor-Reeve Ond Falk — *(elevated to Major; Pale Coast authority)*
> Elevated from secondary (`SECONDARY_NPCS.md`). Ond Falk already exists as Wrackmouth's harbor governor; Stage 9 raises him to major as the Pale Coast's anchor of *legitimate* civic authority (the region was thin on majors, and its other majors are a rite-keeper and a folk-elder), the overwhelmed official trying to hold a port together against the drowned-tide, the wreckers, and the Ledger. The secondary stub remains.
- **Tier:** Major (noncombatant authority). **Faction:** Reachward Compact-adjacent (Wrackmouth's harbor-moot reeve). **Location:** Pale Coast, Wrackmouth harbor and the moot-hall.
- **First Impression:** A square, grey-faced man with a sleepless stare and salt in his beard, who runs a harbor that's quietly coming apart and is too proud and too tired to admit it.
- **Public Role:** Harbor-Reeve of Wrackmouth — the Coast's senior civic authority, governor of the port, head of the harbor-moot.
- **Voice:** Clipped, harried, defensively proud; "the harbor functions. It *functions*. Don't tell me otherwise where the crews can hear."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Keep Wrackmouth working and its people from panic as the sea turns wrong, the wreckers prey, and the Ledger squeezes.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Falk *half-knows the drowned-tide is real and worsening* — he's seen the death-tolls his niece compiles, the "wrong-come-back" drowned, the wreckers' false-lights — and he is desperately *managing fear* instead of facing the cause, because facing it would mean admitting the port (his life's work) sits at the edge of something he can't fix. He's not corrupt like the Ledger or grey like the wreckers; he's an overwhelmed honest man choosing denial to keep order. He'll back a player who can give him a *solvable* problem (the wreckers, the Ledger's gouge) and resist anyone who forces the *unsolvable* one (the drowned-tide, the harvest) into the open before he's ready. The Coast's civic conscience, cracking under a crisis no reeve can govern. He does **not** know the cause; he knows the symptoms and can't bear them.
- **Fear:** Wrackmouth panicking and emptying; being the reeve who "let the harbor die"; the drowned-tide proving beyond any law to fix.
- **Leverage:** Civic authority over the port (the moot, the harbor-watch, the docks); the trust (and the fear) of the harbor folk.
- **What he knows:** The true drowned-tide death-toll (M5, via his niece Hessa); the wreckers' pattern (M3); the Ledger's salt-and-relic squeeze; which "lost" ships were really wrecked.
- **What he wants from the player:** Solvable wins he can *act* on — break the wreckers, ease the Ledger's gouge, settle a panic — without forcing the unfaceable truth into the open prematurely.
- **What he can offer:** Civic authority and legitimacy on the Coast (warrants, the harbor-watch, official sanction), the moot's resources, an honest (if frightened) ally, the harbor as a base; pointers to the wreckers and the Ledger's salvage-fraud.
- **How he can harm the player:** Suppress or deny what threatens the port's calm; his denial can stall the truth; he can lawfully obstruct a player who forces panic.
- **Relationship Links:** Uncle to Moot-Clerk Hessa Falk (who compiles the death-toll he won't face); overwhelmed counterpart to Salt-Mother Bryd (who faces what he denies); squeezed by Ledger factor Maris Cole; plagued by Wreck-Master Quint's wreckers; leans on honest skipper Wenna Roke and tide-reader Mabon Crale; the Coast's civic mirror of the Vale's Tindle (denial as governance) but more sympathetic.
- **Current Attitude:** Harried, proud, defensive; warms to anyone bringing *solvable* help, resists anyone forcing the unfaceable.
- **Possible Evolution:** An honest authority who (with the player) breaks the wreckers and the Ledger's gouge and finally faces the drowned-tide; a broken reeve if Wrackmouth panics and empties; a denier whose obstruction the player must work around.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Commoner/Veteran-like** (AC 13, ~30 HP; commands the harbor-watch, **Guard/Veteran-like**). Noncombatant by role. Solo danger: low personally. Use a Noble/Veteran civic-authority profile.
- **DM-Only Notes:** The Coast needed a major of *legitimate civic authority* to balance its rite-keeper (Bryd) and folk-elder majors; Falk is the overwhelmed-honest-governor archetype, a sympathetic denier the player can lever toward the truth. He pairs with Bryd as the Coast's "denial vs. facing it" axis.
- **Related:** M3, M5; the drowned-tide, the wreckers; Hessa Falk, Salt-Mother Bryd, Maris Cole, Wreck-Master Quint, Wenna Roke, Mabon Crale.

## M49b. Sub-Lector Tamsin Orr — *(elevated to Major; the honest Remnant / Script gate)*
> Elevated from secondary (`SECONDARY_NPCS.md`). Tamsin Orr already exists as the honest Concord-Script tutor; Stage 9 raises her to major as the *innocent, sympathetic* face of the Remnant (a counterweight to Quorrin/Ferrant), the cleanest Script-gate ally, and a moral lever (her disillusionment, like Briss's, can crack the Remnant). The secondary stub remains.
- **Tier:** Major (noncombatant). **Faction:** Concord Remnant (junior scholar; Briss's student). **Location:** Caradril, the Lantern Reach; lectures in the Reach.
- **First Impression:** A bright, ink-smudged young scholar who lights up at a scrap of old script and treats a stranger's questions as the best part of her day.
- **Public Role:** A junior Remnant Script-reader and teacher — the patient, generous one who actually *teaches* outsiders to read Concord Script.
- **Voice:** Eager, precise, kind; "oh — oh, this glyph means *keeping*, do you see? They had a *word* for it. Isn't that extraordinary?"
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Recover and share the Concord's lost knowledge; teach; be worthy of the scholarship she loves.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Tamsin is *wholly innocent* of the inner circle's aim — a purer dupe even than Briss, because she's young and has given the Remnant her whole heart. She is the cleanest, most reliable path for the player to *learn Concord Script* (a major clue-gate for M2/M6/M9) without the Remnant's strings. And she is a moral fault line: she's begun translating fragments that *trouble* her ("why does the Concord write of 'keeping' the dead?"), and if the player shows her proof of what her order's leaders intend, her disillusionment — like Briss's — can help shatter the Remnant from the bottom up. Protecting her innocence, or breaking her heart with the truth, is a real choice. She does **not** know the harvest; she's translating her way toward it, trustingly.
- **Fear:** That the knowledge she loves might be monstrous; that her teachers are not who she believes; failing the scholarship that gives her life meaning.
- **Leverage:** The clearest no-strings path to literacy in Concord Script; the innocent rank-and-file's trust.
- **What she knows:** Concord Script (she can *teach* it — the key clue-gate); the troubling fragments she's begun to translate (M6-adjacent); the Remnant's public face and its junior membership.
- **What she wants from the player:** A student, a friend, and (without knowing it) someone honest enough to tell her the truth before her order does worse with it.
- **What she can offer:** **Concord Script literacy** (the cleanest M2/M6/M9 clue-gate ally in the game), honest scholarship, archive navigation, an innocent inside-view of the Remnant.
- **How she can harm the player:** Only by accident — her innocence can lead her (and the player) into the inner circle's notice; if betrayed or used cynically, she withdraws the one no-strings Script path.
- **Relationship Links:** Student of Lector Briss (shares his sincere blindness); under (and unknowingly endangered by) Quorrin Vane and Lector-General Ferrant; mentored-from-afar by other honest readers (Reader Sefwy Oale, Bram Oale); idolized by Page Wenna; the Remnant's innocent heart, mirroring Briss one generation down.
- **Current Attitude:** Warm, eager, trusting; opens instantly to anyone who shares her love of the work, wounded by anyone who exploits it.
- **Possible Evolution:** The player's loyal Script-mentor and, once disillusioned with proof, a bottom-up Remnant reformer (an ally outcome); a heartbroken casualty of the truth; an innocent the inner circle silences when she translates too much.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Scholar/Acolyte-like** (AC 11, ~16 HP; expert Concord Script, History, Investigation; minimal magic). Noncombatant. Solo danger: none. Fallback: noncombatant who flees; her value is teaching and trust.
- **DM-Only Notes:** She is the *innocent, non-faction-villain* social-play major the brief asked for and the cleanest Script-gate (so the player isn't dependent on the compromised inner circle for literacy). Her disillusionment is a second lever (with Briss) on the Remnant. Keep her translating *toward* the truth, never reaching the apex herself.
- **Related:** M2, M6, M9; Concord Script gate; Lector Briss, Quorrin Vane, Ferrant, Sefwy Oale, Page Wenna; parallels Briss.

## M50b. Wreck-Master Tomas Quint — *(elevated to Major; the Coast's grey predator)*
> Elevated from secondary (`SECONDARY_NPCS.md`). Tomas Quint already exists as the wreckers' leader; Stage 9 raises him to major as the Pale Coast's combat-relevant grey antagonist (the region had a rite-keeper, a folk-elder, and a civic reeve as majors, but no *predator*), the face of the wrecking trade, and a turnable-or-fightable moral-grey power feeding the relic-chain. The secondary stub remains.
> Surname note: given name "Tomas" is shared with Tomas Brevin (Hollowmere) and Wreck-Master *Tomas* Quint — distinct people; keep surnames.
- **Tier:** Major (combat-relevant). **Faction:** The wreckers (leader); Ledger-fed, Syndicate-linked. **Location:** Pale Coast, the wild coast — sea-caves, false-light reefs, and Wrackmouth's shadows.
- **First Impression:** A soft-spoken, courteous man with a drowned-sailor's calm and a coil of rope always near his hand, who calls dead men's salvage "the sea's bounty" without a flicker.
- **Public Role:** Master of the Coast's wreckers — the false-light men who lure ships onto the reefs and strip what (and who) washes up.
- **Voice:** Soft, gentle, chillingly reasonable; "the sea takes them, friend. We only gather what she leaves. Where's the sin in tidiness?"
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Run the wrecking and salvage trade profitably; keep his ruined crew fed by the sea that ruined them.
- **Secret / DM-Only:** Quint is the Coast's grey moral knot. Half his crew are *salt-folk the Ledger's gouge and the drowned-tide already ruined* — he gives the desperate a way to eat, which makes him a *protector* of his people even as he murders strangers for cargo. He feeds **sea-relics** (Remembrance relics salvaged from wrecks and sea-shrines) up the chain to Maris Cole (Ledger) and on to Silque's Caradril operation (M3) — the maritime relic-trade made personal. And he's begun to notice the relics he salvages "behave wrong" and the drowned "come back" worse, which unsettles even him. He can be **fought** (a clean combat antagonist the Coast needed), **turned** (his ruined crew would take honest work and a pardon, like Renn's Tollmen, if the Ledger's gouge and the drowned-tide were eased), or **exposed** (his trade fed by the Ledger's squeeze). Not a cultist or conspirator — a frontier predator and reluctant protector profiting off a crisis he half-fears. He does **not** know the harvest; he knows the relics are "wrong" and sells them anyway.
- **Fear:** The sea turning on *him*; his ruined crew starving if the wrecking stops; the relics' "wrongness" reaching him; the drowned he's stripped "coming home."
- **Leverage:** The false-light reefs and sea-caves; a crew of desperate, dangerous salt-folk; the sea-relic supply that feeds the Ledger's coastal arm.
- **What he knows:** The wrecking pattern and which "lost" ships were taken (M3); the sea-relic→Cole→Caradril chain (M3, the maritime spine); the sea-caves and false-light reefs; that the relics and the drowned are "behaving wrong" (M5-oblique).
- **What he wants from the player:** To be left to his trade, or paid off; failing that, that they don't bring the moot or the Wardens down on him; he'll deal if the price (or the alternative for his crew) is right.
- **What he can offer:** (To a grey player) sea-salvage, the sea-caves' routes, the relic-chain's coastal end; (if turned) the maritime M3 spine exposed from inside, his crew redirected to honest work; (if fought) a clean, telegraphed Coast antagonist.
- **How he can harm the player:** False-lights and reef-ambush at sea; his ruined, dangerous crew; selling the player's movements to Cole or the Syndicate; the sea itself as his weapon.
- **Relationship Links:** Feeds Ledger factor Maris Cole and, through fence-captain Mully Dree, Silque's Caradril chain (M3); hated by Salt-Mother Bryd and the Cobble Strand folk; preys on the shipping Harbor-Reeve Falk can't protect; his ruined crew drawn from the salt-gouge's victims (Salt-raker Tibba's brother and the like); a grey mirror of Toll-Boss Renn (a ruined-crew leader who can be turned).
- **Current Attitude:** Courteous, watchful, dangerous; weighs whether the player is a customer, an obstacle, or a threat to his crew's survival.
- **Possible Evolution:** Turned (with the player) to honest salvage and a pardon, the maritime M3 spine cut and the wrecking stopped (a Coast win); a fought-and-broken predator; a recurring grey sea-power if left alone — escalating as the drowned-tide worsens.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 — Bandit-Captain/Veteran-like** (AC 15, ~70 HP; net + cutlass, *Leadership*; commands a crew of **Thug/Scout/Bandit-like** wreckers; fights on his own reefs and in the surf). Solo danger: high *on his coast/at sea*; lower on land or alone. **Always telegraphed** (false-lights, missing ships, his reputation). Noncombat: pay him, turn him with a pardon + relief for his crew (via Falk/Bryd), expose his Ledger-fed chain, out-sail him with Wenna Roke/Mabon Crale. **Scaling:** crew size and sea-hazards (false-lights, surf, the drowned-tide).
- **DM-Only Notes:** The Coast needed a *combat-relevant grey antagonist*; Quint is a fightable-or-turnable predator whose trade is fed by the Ledger's squeeze and whose crew are the crisis's victims — so opposing him raises the question of *what happens to his ruined people*. He carries the maritime M3 spine. Keep him a frontier predator, never a cultist.
- **Related:** M3, M5 (oblique); the wrecking trade, the sea-relic chain; Maris Cole, Mully Dree, "Mother" Silque, Salt-Mother Bryd, Harbor-Reeve Falk, Wenna Roke; parallels Toll-Boss Renn.
