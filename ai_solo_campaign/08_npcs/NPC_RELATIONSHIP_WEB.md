## AI Use

Load when an NPC dies, leaves, is exposed, or allies with the player — to know **who reacts and how**. Covers major NPCs and the most connected secondary NPCs only (minor NPCs are not webbed). DM-only ties are marked **(DM)** and must not be surfaced. Pairs with `FACTION_RELATIONSHIP_MAP.md` (faction-level) and `NPC_SECRET_LEDGER.md` (what each hides).

> Convention: **A → B** = "A relates to B." Public ties are player-safe; **(DM)** ties are hidden.

## Faction Clusters (who knows whom inside each faction)

### Ashen Wardens
- Othetha (senior, secret-keeper) ← Hale, Pell, Sirrin, Adwen, Verge, Sef, Ošric defer to her line.
- Hale ↔ Pell (gruff mentor/student); both mothered by Sirrin at the Ashwalk Rest.
- Adwen (Vale), Verge (Coast), Sef (Coast wanderer), Ošric (Tollwood wanderer): **each independently holds a seal-fragment (DM)** — they rarely meet; the player is often the only one connecting them.

### Cinder Ledger
- Vyre (matriarch) → Vell (Caradril deputy), Domic Sael (Hollowmere factor), and the regional factors: Marlow (Saltmargin), W. Pell (Vale), Cole (Coast), Coyle (Harrowgast), Bly (Kettle Bridge).
- Vell → Riis (clerk, **turnable, holds deal-proof — DM**), Tallytooth Ren (Hollowmere muscle).
- **(DM)** Vyre ↔ Quorrin Vane (secret monopoly negotiation) — the apex Ledger tie; Domic, Riis, and Vell each glimpse a piece.
- Cole → Quint (wreckers) and Dree ("Cuttle," Wrack fence); Marlow → Gull (smuggler, deniably).

### Mourners' Circle
- Wend (eldest, Hollowmere) ↔ kin-in-spirit to Combe (Vale), both Sennets, Bryd (Coast), Mam Tace, Mabb Tay, Ansel.
- Wend → Iola (granddaughter, **secret Gravecaller bridge — DM**).
- Combe ↔ Mam Tace (honest Vale voice); Sennet (S.V.) → Petronel (deputy, conscience-lever).
- Bann Oester (grave-digger) serves Wend's network Reach-wide.

### Reachward Compact
- Coalmont (Hollowmere reeve) → Hesk (sergeant), Pevin Oss (clerk); rival of Cray (Kettle Bridge).
- **(DM)** Reke (councilor) is the Court's agent on the council; outwardly a peer of Coalmont, secretly steering against her.
- Regional: Tindle (Vale moot) → Frost (road-wardens); Brannoch Vey (Tollwood road-warden); Falk (Coast harbor-moot).

### Concord Remnant
- Quorrin Vane (inner circle) → Wessel Dree (Archive gatekeeper, Reclaimer), Veska Dunn (field reclaimer), Oneth Vael (Vale), Maren Voll (Coast).
- Briss (public face, **dupe**) → Tamsin Orr (student, innocent); Briss reveres Quorrin (doesn't know his aim).
- **(DM)** Quorrin ↔ Vyre (monopoly talks); Quorrin suspects/races the Hollow Court.

### Gravecallers
- The Tallow Man (speaker, **channels a real witness — DM**) ↔ Sister Knell (recruiter, **wants out — DM**) ↔ Cole Ashby (radical, **has breaking-tools — DM**).
- Iola Wend is their secret bridge into the Mourners; Tomas Brevin sought them out (Wren).

### Hollow Court (DM-ONLY)
- Veyl (leader) ↔ Maire (doubter) ↔ Orre (hawk). Veyl persuades, Maire wavers, Orre pushes for force.
- **(DM)** Court → Reke (knowing agent) → Sefra Quick (unwitting cutout) → the player. The steering chain.

## Settlement Clusters (who knows whom locally)

### Hollowmere (the hub)
- Halla Coalmont (inn) is sister to **Reeve Marda Coalmont** — the key family tie; the inn is the town's ear.
- Tomas & Wren Brevin (the opening crisis); Wat (apprentice) serves Tomas.
- Sefra Quick works everyone; Domic Sael + Tallytooth Ren run the Ledger corner; Grandmother Wend + Iola at the Green; Sexton Marrow digs.
- Reke moves among them all, genial and trusted (**DM: steering them**).

### Kettle Bridge
- Cray (bailiff, rival of Coalmont) uses Bly (toll) and the watch; Tibb's bargemen lose men at the broken arch; Bann Oester buries them; Old Marrock's grandson is the **(DM)** Gravecaller cutout.

### Caradril (city power-map)
- **Council:** Voss (weak First Magister) chaired over Dann (reform) vs. Tolm (old money). **(DM)** Voss took Ledger coin.
- **Ledger vs. Houses:** Vyre/Vell vs. Tolm — open rivalry; Tolm would use the player vs. Vyre.
- **Remnant:** Quorrin → Dree; Briss → Orr (the honest/innocent pair).
- **Crime:** Sorrel (Salt Syndicate) vs. the Bellman (the Hush) — rival shadow-powers; uneasy truce with the Ledger.
- **Temple:** Ysarra over Quayle (Quiet Houses) — **(DM)** Ysarra runs the raise-dead cover-up; Quayle is the crack.
- **Sill:** Renna Sill + Widow Tace shelter refugees; Pater Dunk guides the Wards; Kade (Watch) quietly sympathetic.

### Ashgarden Vale
- Tindle (cover-up) vs. Combe (truth) — the Vale's moral axis; Frost serves Tindle but is breaking.
- W. Pell (Ledger) squeezes Brunt (Tilbrook); Mam Tace + Combe carry the honest line.
- Saint Veddow's: Sennet (hides failures) over Petronel (conscience); Adwen (seal) wary of Oneth Vael (Remnant).

### Tollwood
- Hartfell: Dorr (Ledger cutting) vs. Vey (road-warden) and Brann (guide, won't serve Dorr); Dunnal captains Dorr's camp-guard, near refusing.
- Coldhearth: Goodwife Sennet (deep secret) leads the woodfolk; Gethin + Brann defer to her.
- Tollstone Cross: Renn (turnable toll-boss) vs. Skell (murderous lieutenant) — the toll-war axis; Wenny (clerk) is the turn-lever.

### Pale Coast
- Wrackmouth: Cole (Ledger salt/relics) vs. Falk (overwhelmed harbor-moot) and Roke (honest skipper, squeezed); Voll (Remnant) courts access.
- Cobble Strand: Bryd (rite-keeper) over Petronel-parallel; Strand (worldly elder) defers to Bryd; Ferec is the **(DM)** cult-seed.
- Quint (wreckers) feeds Cole; hated by Bryd and the moot; Crale + Roke + Sef try to keep folk off the killing tides.

## Cross-Region Ties (travelers, kin, rivals who span the map)

- **The Ledger spine:** Vyre (Caradril) ties every regional factor into one chain — the player can follow it region to region.
- **The Mourner old-songs network:** Wend ↔ Combe ↔ both Sennets ↔ Bryd ↔ Mam Tace — one faith, one buried memory, five regions.
- **The wandering Wardens:** Sef (Coast), Ošric (Tollwood), Pell (Reach roam), Adwen/Verge (resident) — the order's thin thread across the world.
- **Smuggling/relic underground:** Gull (Reach) → Gravecaller buyer + Coast fence (Dree "Cuttle") → Cole; Sorrel's Syndicate (Caradril) overlaps. A black-market through-line.
- **Surname families (NOT kin unless noted):** the "Pells" (Warden Pell, Wymar Pell, Pell Oster, Ale-wife Pell) are **unrelated** — common frontier surname. The two **Sennets** are distinct people. **Wren Hollowmere** ≠ **Wren-of-the-Wood**. Keep epithets.

## Blackmail & Leverage Map (DM)

- **The Bellman** holds leverage on the Charter Houses and fragments of the Vyre–Quorrin deal — he is a leverage *hub*; buying from him puts the player in his debt.
- **Riis** and **Domic Sael** are leverage *against Vyre* (deal-proof); protecting them is a clock-race (both can be silenced).
- **Pevin Oss** is leverage against **Reke** (doctored filings) — but only if protected.
- **Tolm** offers the player leverage vs. Vyre, at the price of becoming his tool.
- **Frost / Petronel / Quayle** are conscience-leverage *inside cover-ups* — turn them with proof and protection, not coin.
- **Cole** (the radical) is a *threat* lever: he can be pointed at the shrine (catastrophe) or talked down.
- **The Bellman** (now major) is the city's leverage *hub*: he holds fragments of the Vyre–Quorrin deal, the correspondent thread, and the Sealgate route — every secret bought deepens the player's Hush-debt.
- **Decca Rull / Net-wife Sereth** hold the workers' proof against **Marlow** (M3) — protect them; the strike is a Monopoly-clock lever.
- **Mauld Tallow** is leverage *against the Ledger from inside* (an enforcer's conscience + operational knowledge) — turn him with proof + a better offer; the Ledger will destroy him if it learns he wavers.
- **Sela Coalmont** is the *lawful* lever against the basin scheme (she can act where Marda can't) — but she needs proof and is one rung from Reke's reach.
- **Onaia Veen's clinical record** is leverage every faction wants (credible corroboration of the crisis) — a thing to protect, seize, or race for.
- **Ser Aldous Penhew's** relic-ground is an M3 site-gate; his cross-pressure makes him a patron the player can win *or* a coverer-up if forced.
- **Cady Renn / Tomas Quint** are turnable grey-crew leaders (Tollmen / wreckers) — a pardon + relief for their ruined people flips them; betrayal hardens them.
- **Toll-Sergeant Brec / Toll-clerk Wenny** are the levers that turn Renn against **Skell**.
