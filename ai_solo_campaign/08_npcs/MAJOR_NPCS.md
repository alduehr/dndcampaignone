## AI Use

The 20 major NPCs of the campaign foundation. **Player-Facing** blocks are safe to surface. **Secret / DM-Only** blocks must be earned through play — never reveal them directly. Voice notes are for the DM. Mechanical profiles use D&D 5e/2024-compatible reference profiles, not copied stat blocks; scale to the encounter.

> Secrecy convention: every NPC has a player-safe "first impression / public role" and a DM-only secret + true motivation.

## 1. Warden Sister Othetha
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Ashen Wardens (senior, Reach circle). **Location:** Roams the Reach; based near Hollowmere.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A spare, ash-cloaked woman with burn-scarred hands and a voice worn smooth by a thousand funerals. She is gentle with the grieving and unyielding about the dead.
- **Public Role:** The Reach's most respected rite-keeper; the one sent for when a rite fails badly.
- **Voice:** Speaks in short, certain blessings; pauses before answering anything about the *cause* of the failures.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Lay the restless dead; protect the living from them.
- **Secret / True Motivation (DM-Only):** She is the **sole local keeper of the Wardens' true purpose**: the order exists to keep the harvest *paused* and the drowned shrine *sealed*. Her oath forbids her from explaining why. She is quietly terrified by the basin-drainage scheme.
- **Fear:** That the shrine will reopen and she will have to choose between her oath of silence and the truth.
- **Leverage:** Her oath; her guilt over the dead she cannot reach; her need for trustworthy help.
- **What she knows:** The seal exists; the shrine must stay closed; fragments of the true rites. (A late-arc confession source.)
- **What she wants from the player:** Help with rites; eyes on the basin scheme; eventually, someone to trust with the truth.
- **What she can offer:** Rite knowledge, Warden safe-houses, moral authority, the campaign's deepest insider revelation.
- **Possible evolution:** Breaks her oath to warn the player (Act 3–4); dies guarding the shrine; or becomes the player's key ally for the "seal it" ending.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 2 noncombat — **Priest-like** (AC 13, ~33 HP, high Insight/Religion, *Cure Wounds, Lesser Restoration, Gentle Repose, Spirit Guardians*). Non-combatant unless cornered.

## 2. Warden Brother Hale
- **Tier:** Major (companion-class). **Faction:** Ashen Wardens (young). **Location:** Hollowmere and the road.
- **First Impression:** A road-worn young man with kind eyes and chapped lips, always slightly out of breath, carrying too much.
- **Public Role:** Junior Warden; the player's likely first friendly contact and guide to the rites.
- **Voice:** Earnest, over-explains, apologizes, then says something quietly wise.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Prove himself; ease suffering; not let anyone down.
- **Secret (DM-Only):** He is starting to *doubt the order* — he suspects the elders are hiding something about the failures and feels guilty for thinking it. He does **not** know the true secret.
- **Fear:** That he's not strong enough for this crisis; that the order is lying to him.
- **What he can offer:** A loyal, low-power **traveling companion**; rite assistance; an in to the Wardens.
- **Possible evolution:** Grows into a true Warden; learns the secret and is shattered; dies protecting the player (a gut-punch the DM can deploy carefully).
- **Mechanics:** Companion ally — **Priest/Acolyte-like** (AC 13, ~22 HP scaling; *Cure Wounds, Bless, Sacred Flame*). Designed to support, not solve. See companion rules in `DND_MECHANICS_REQUIREMENTS.md`.

## 3. Mother Calla Vyre
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Cinder Ledger (matriarch). **Location:** Caradril HQ; visits Hollowmere counting-house.
- **First Impression:** An immaculate, ageless woman who remembers your name, your debts, and your weaknesses, and smiles as if they were gifts.
- **Public Role:** Head of the Reach's bank and supplier; the most powerful merchant in the region.
- **Voice:** Warm, precise, transactional — every sentence sounds like a generous offer with a hidden clause.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Grow the Ledger; secure her house's future.
- **Secret / True Motivation (DM-Only):** She intends to **monopolize the Remembrance** and sell immortality. She is secretly negotiating with the Concord Remnant's inner circle (Quorrin). She doesn't yet grasp the full horror — she sees the deal of the millennium.
- **Fear:** Irrelevance; being outmaneuvered; the truth being worse than profitable.
- **Leverage:** Half the Reach owes her; she holds a relic that "remembers" the harvest.
- **What she wants from the player:** A capable, deniable agent — and the player's debt.
- **Possible evolution:** Becomes the "seize it / sell it" endgame claimant; or, horrified by the full truth, an unlikely ally against the Court.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 2 noncombat — **Noble-like** with deep resources (AC 12, ~27 HP). Non-combatant; fights with money and people.

## 4. Factor Domic Sael
- **Tier:** Major (secondary-plus). **Faction:** Cinder Ledger (Hollowmere agent). **Location:** Hollowmere counting-house.
- **First Impression:** A pleasant, tired man with ink-stained cuffs who always seems to be doing you a small favor.
- **Public Role:** The Ledger's local factor; arranges loans, buys salvage, hires for odd jobs.
- **Voice:** Friendly, deflecting, fond of "between us, of course."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Keep the Hollowmere books balanced; rise in the Ledger.
- **Secret (DM-Only):** He thinks Vyre's relic obsession is **reckless and dangerous** and is quietly documenting it — he could be turned against her, or could leak the vault relic to the player.
- **Fear:** Being blamed when Vyre's scheme goes wrong.
- **What he can offer:** Early paid work, loans, gossip, and (if turned) access to the counting-house vault relic.
- **Possible evolution:** Becomes the player's inside man at the Ledger; or is silenced for knowing too much.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 3+ — **Commoner/Spy-lite** (AC 12, ~16 HP). Not a fighter.

## 5. Grandmother Wend
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Mourners' Circle (eldest, Hollowmere). **Location:** Hollowmere cemetery cottage.
- **First Impression:** A tiny, sharp-eyed old woman with dirt under her nails and a voice like wind through reeds, who sees more than she says.
- **Public Role:** Eldest grief-keeper of Hollowmere; washes the dead, sings the passings.
- **Voice:** Speaks in proverbs and half-songs; answers questions with older questions.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Let the dead rest; protect the grieving; keep the old ways.
- **Secret / True Motivation (DM-Only):** She carries the folk-memory that **"the Concord ate the dead"** — encoded in old songs she can teach the worthy. She would see the harvest *ended*, and some of her elders are stockpiling tools to destroy a shrine.
- **Fear:** That the songs will be lost; that the dead will never rest again.
- **Leverage:** Total community trust; the old songs (major clue).
- **What she wants from the player:** Proof the player honors the dead before she shares anything.
- **Possible evolution:** Becomes the moral conscience of the campaign; key ally for the "let the dead rest" endings; or her militant faction goes too far.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 2 noncombat — **Acolyte/Priest-like** (AC 11, ~22 HP, high Insight/Religion). Non-combatant.

## 6. Iola Wend
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Mourners' Circle (Wend's granddaughter) / secret Gravecaller contact. **Location:** Hollowmere.
- **First Impression:** A young woman with her grandmother's eyes and a grief she can't put down, restless in a way the Circle's patience can't soothe.
- **Public Role:** Wend's apprentice; future Circle elder.
- **Voice:** Quick, frustrated, suddenly tender; argues with tradition.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Actually *do something* about the dead, not just mourn them.
- **Secret (DM-Only):** She has secretly contacted the **Gravecallers**, drawn to their promise of *answers and action*. She is the living bridge between the two enemy faiths — and a moral fault line.
- **Fear:** Becoming the helpless mourner she's watched her grandmother be.
- **What she can offer:** A quiet door to the Gravecallers; insight into both factions; a friendship that complicates loyalties.
- **Possible evolution:** Joins the Gravecallers fully; brokers an alliance; or is pulled back by the player.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 3+ — **Commoner/Acolyte-lite** (AC 11, ~14 HP).

## 7. Reeve Marda Coalmont
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Reachward Compact (Hollowmere reeve). **Location:** Hollowmere council hall.
- **First Impression:** A broad, weather-beaten woman with a permanent frown of responsibility and a fairness people rely on.
- **Public Role:** Hollowmere's reeve; the honest backbone of the Compact.
- **Voice:** Blunt, fair, exhausted; hates pretty words.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Keep Hollowmere alive and lawful through the crisis.
- **Secret (DM-Only):** She **distrusts the basin-drainage scheme and Councilor Reke** but can't prove anything and fears looking like an obstructionist. She is desperate for an outsider she can trust to investigate.
- **Fear:** That she'll approve something that destroys the town she's sworn to protect.
- **Leverage:** Legal authority; the town watch; her good name.
- **What she wants from the player:** An honest pair of hands for jobs the watch can't manage; eventually, evidence against the basin scheme.
- **Possible evolution:** The player's staunchest lawful ally; or breaks under the politics; or is removed by Reke.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 2 — **Veteran/Noble hybrid** (AC 15, ~40 HP, can fight if cornered). Prefers law to violence.

## 8. Councilor Aldous Reke
- **Tier:** Major (key hidden antagonist, Act 1–2). **Faction:** Reachward Compact (senior councilor) / **knowing Hollow Court agent**. **Location:** Hollowmere council hall.
- **First Impression:** A genial, silver-haired statesman who listens beautifully and makes everyone feel heard.
- **Public Role:** Respected senior councilor; champion of "progress" and the basin-drainage project.
- **Voice:** Smooth, reasonable, always framing the next step as obvious and modest.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Resettlement, water rights, the Reach's prosperity.
- **Secret / True Motivation (DM-Only):** He is a **knowing servant of the Hollow Court**, steering the Compact to drain the basin and reopen the drowned shrine. He is partly Remembrance-touched (extended life, subtle powers). He believes the Court's restored harvest will *save* civilization. He arranged much of the player's path without the player knowing.
- **Fear:** Exposure before the basin drains; the Court's disappointment.
- **Leverage:** Political skill; procedural control; the Court's backing.
- **What he wants from the player:** To channel the player's energy *toward* the shrine while keeping them ignorant of why.
- **Possible evolution:** The Act 2 reveal of the first true agent; flees rather than dies cheaply; can be turned, exposed, or killed.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 2 social / Tier 1 if forced — **Mage/Spy-like** (AC 12, ~45 HP, *Hold Person, Counterspell, Misty Step/Dimension Door, Suggestion*). Telegraphed; escapes cheap death.

## 9. The Tallow Man
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Gravecallers (speaker). **Location:** Hidden, near the Greyfens.
- **First Impression:** A figure in wax-stained robes whose face is hard to remember, whose voice sometimes seems to belong to someone else.
- **Public Role:** The dreaded "voice" of the Gravecallers — said to speak with the dead.
- **Voice:** Soft, doubled — as if a second, older voice rides beneath his.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Free the dead; teach the living what the dead know.
- **Secret / True Motivation (DM-Only):** He genuinely **channels a real Remembrance — a Quietfall-era witness** who knows the Concord's sin. That witness has its *own* agenda and may be using him. The Tallow Man is the richest truth-source in Acts 1–2, and a danger.
- **Fear:** That the dead he carries will not let him go.
- **Leverage:** The dead's testimony; forbidden knowledge.
- **What he wants from the player:** A living agent willing to hear the dead and act.
- **Possible evolution:** Mentor on the synthesis path; tragic casualty; or consumed by the Remembrance he channels.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 1 — **Cult Fanatic/Warlock-like** (AC 12, ~45 HP, *Speak with Dead* at will, *Hold Person, Phantasmal Force*; calls a spectral Remembrance). Flees into the fens; not a cheap-death ambusher.

## 10. Sister Knell
- **Tier:** Major (secondary-plus). **Faction:** Gravecallers (recruiter). **Location:** Moves between towns and the fens.
- **First Impression:** A soft-spoken woman in grey who finds you exactly when your grief is loudest.
- **Public Role:** The gentle face that draws the bereaved toward the Gravecallers.
- **Voice:** Comforting, patient, quietly persuasive; never pushes, always opens a door.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Ease grief; bring the lost to the cult's comfort.
- **Secret (DM-Only):** She **fears the Gravecallers are being used by the very Remembrances they channel** and is secretly looking for a way out — or someone to confirm her dread.
- **What she can offer:** A humane way into the Gravecallers; a wavering insider; warnings.
- **Possible evolution:** Defects to the player; becomes the cult's conscience; or is silenced by the radicals.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 2 noncombat — **Acolyte-like** (AC 11, ~20 HP, *Speak with Dead, Calm Emotions*).

## 11. Cole Ashby
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Gravecallers (radical). **Location:** The fens; the shrine's approaches.
- **First Impression:** A lean young man vibrating with conviction, who looks at the living as if they're in the way of the dead.
- **Public Role:** The cult's most fervent zealot.
- **Voice:** Fast, righteous, brittle; quotes the dead like scripture.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Free every soul, now, whatever it costs.
- **Secret (DM-Only):** He has acquired **Concord breaking-tools** and a partial map of the shrine, and he intends to shatter the keystone — risking the campaign's catastrophe ending. He hides (even from himself) that he doesn't understand the consequences.
- **Fear:** That he is too late and the dead will be enslaved forever.
- **What he can offer / threaten:** A door to "the Breaking"; or a reckless rival racing the player to the shrine.
- **Possible evolution:** The trigger of the destruction ending; talked down by the player; or a martyr.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 1 lite — **Berserker/Acolyte-like** zealot (AC 13, ~30 HP). Dangerous when committed.

## 12. Archivist Quorrin Vane
- **Tier:** Major (key rival, Act 3+). **Faction:** Concord Remnant (inner circle). **Location:** Caradril; agents in the Reach.
- **First Impression:** A precise, courteous scholar whose curiosity feels like being read.
- **Public Role:** The Remnant's most revered scholar and reader of Concord Script.
- **Voice:** Measured, generous with knowledge, faintly condescending; treats horror as a fascinating problem.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Restore the Concord; recover its wisdom for the world.
- **Secret / True Motivation (DM-Only):** He **knows the Concord harvested the dead** and intends to **seize and restart the harvest himself**, becoming the new Custodian. He suspects the Hollow Court exists and is racing it to the shrine.
- **Fear:** That the Hollow Court — or the player — reaches the keystone first.
- **Leverage:** The only fluent Concord Script; the Caradril archives (the clearest written proof of the truth).
- **What he wants from the player:** A useful field agent and relic-finder, kept ignorant of his true aim.
- **Possible evolution:** The "seize it / new Concord" endgame claimant; exposed to his own idealists; or a final rival at the shrine.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 1 boss-class — **Mage/Archmage-like** scaled to tier (AC 12–15, ~60–110 HP; *Counterspell, Wall of Force, Scrying, Sending*; Remembrance relics). Wins by knowledge; never a cheap ambush.

## 13. Lector Briss
- **Tier:** Major (secondary-plus). **Faction:** Concord Remnant (idealist public face). **Location:** Caradril; lectures in the Reach.
- **First Impression:** A bright, generous teacher who lights up explaining the Concord's lost roads and gentleness.
- **Public Role:** The Remnant's beloved public voice and recruiter.
- **Voice:** Enthusiastic, kind, a little naive; believes the best of everyone, including Quorrin.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Share the Concord's wisdom; rebuild a kinder world.
- **Secret (DM-Only):** He is a **sincere dupe** — he has no idea his inner circle plans to seize the harvest. If shown proof, his disillusionment can **shatter the Remnant** and turn him into a powerful ally.
- **What he can offer:** Teaches the player **Concord Script** (a major clue gate); archive access; honest scholarship.
- **Possible evolution:** Becomes the player's ally and the Remnant's reformer; or is crushed by the truth.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 2 noncombat — **Scholar/Acolyte-like** (AC 11, ~18 HP). Non-combatant.

## 14. Custodian Veyl
- **Tier:** Major (final antagonist / final voice). **Faction:** Hollow Court (leader). **Location:** The deepest under-shrine beneath Hollowmere. **Secrecy: DM-ONLY existence until late.**
- **First Impression (only when finally met):** A figure of still grey light in the shape of a person, dignified and unbearably patient, who speaks as if continuing a conversation begun a hundred years ago.
- **Public Role:** A legend; "the one who waits below." Believed long dead.
- **Voice:** Calm, sorrowful, certain; never raises the voice; reasons with you as a peer.
- **Motivation (DM-Only — there is no player-safe version until the endgame):** Restart the harvest "mercifully" to save all souls from the slow thinning, ruling as an immortal steward. Veyl **designed the Quietfall** and carries a century of grief and conviction.
- **Secret:** Veyl is a **preserved Remembrance of the original Custodian who designed the deliberate Quietfall.** The campaign's apex truth.
- **Fear:** That the sacrifice of the Quietfall was for nothing; that the dead are doomed either way.
- **What Veyl wants from the player:** Agreement. Veyl would rather **convince** the player to accept the merciful harvest than destroy them.
- **Possible evolution:** Final ally (synthesis), final enemy (seal/destroy), or final patron (join). Beatable by persuasion, sabotage, alliance, or combat.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 4 solo boss — **Lich/Wraith-like Remembrance-being** built with explicit **weaknesses and non-combat win conditions** (severing the shrine, Maire's defection, the player's chosen artifact/ally). Detailed in Stage 13/15. Never a pure damage wall.

## 15. Custodian Maire
- **Tier:** Major (hidden lever). **Faction:** Hollow Court (the doubter). **Location:** The under-shrine. **Secrecy: DM-ONLY.**
- **First Impression (only when met):** A flickering, uncertain light among the Court's stillness, the one who looks away when Veyl speaks.
- **Public Role:** Unknown to the world.
- **Motivation (DM-Only):** Maire **secretly believes the Court should let itself end** — that a century of waiting only proved the dead deserve release, not rule. Maire is wracked with guilt over the original harvest.
- **Secret:** Maire is the **key to the synthesis ending** — a Custodian willing to help the player transform or end the harvest justly, against Veyl.
- **What Maire offers the player:** The inside path to defeating or transforming the Court without a pure brute-force assault.
- **Possible evolution:** The player's unlikely endgame ally; destroyed by Veyl for heresy; or the one who enacts the merciful end.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 4 Remembrance-being, but **aids the player** in the endgame; not an enemy if the player reaches them. Use as a "win condition" NPC.

## 16. Sefra Quick
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Independent (Hollowmere fixer); **unwitting Hollow Court cutout**. **Location:** Hollowmere (the Drowned Lantern tavern).
- **First Impression:** A quick-smiling, quick-talking fixer who knows everyone's business and offers you a job before you've set down your pack.
- **Public Role:** Hollowmere's information broker and odd-job fixer; the player's likely **first contact**.
- **Voice:** Rapid, charming, deflects with jokes, never quite answers the second question.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Make coin; stay useful to everyone; survive the frontier.
- **Secret / True Motivation (DM-Only):** A **mysterious well-paying patron** (a Hollow Court cutout, via Reke) hired Sefra to **ensure a capable newcomer investigates the failed rite and the shrine** — the player is being *steered*, and Sefra doesn't know by whom or why. Sefra suspects the money is "wrong" and is uneasy.
- **Fear:** That the patron's money will get her killed; that she's done something terrible without knowing.
- **Leverage:** Knows the town; owes and is owed favors.
- **What she wants from the player:** A reliable hand for the patron's "investigation" — and, later, help getting clear of the patron.
- **Possible evolution:** The thread the player pulls to discover they were steered (a mid-arc trust crisis); becomes a loyal ally once she confesses; or vanishes when the patron tidies up.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 2 — **Spy-like** (AC 13, ~22 HP, Stealth/Deception/Insight). Survivor, not a brawler.

## 17. Wren Hollowmere
- **Tier:** Major (the opening's living mystery). **Faction:** None. **Location:** Hollowmere; the Brevin cottage; later the fens. **Secrecy: mixed — her *condition* is visible; its *cause* is DM-only.**
- **First Impression:** A pale, soft-spoken woman recently buried — and now standing in her own doorway, remembering her life *almost* right.
- **Public Role:** A beloved local (a midwife) who died of fever and whose rite **failed**: she came back. The town is terrified and grieving at once.
- **Voice:** Warm but lagging, like a song sung half a beat behind; repeats endearments; forgets she died.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Finish something unfinished; care for her husband Tomas; not be sent away.
- **Secret (DM-Only):** Wren is a **partial Remembrance-revenant** — living proof the harvest is restarting. The "unfinished thing" she clings to is a fragment of memory the shrine *failed to fully take*. Speaking with her carefully yields the campaign's first real clue toward the shrine. She is not malevolent; she is a person caught in the machine.
- **Fear (as she has any):** Being released before she's "done"; Tomas's fear of her.
- **What she offers:** The opening mystery; the first clue; a moral test (release her, protect her, or use her).
- **Possible evolution:** Released at rest (level-up beat); becomes a recurring tragic clue-bearer; or is taken back by the shrine.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 1 lite when threatening — flavor a **wrong-come-back revenant** (ghost/specter-like, AC 12, ~30 HP), but **she is not a combat encounter by default**. She is a roleplay/clue scene; combat only if the player or town forces it. Telegraph; offer non-combat resolution (a true rite).

## 18. Tomas Brevin
- **Tier:** Major (secondary-plus; the opening's emotional anchor). **Faction:** None. **Location:** Hollowmere; the Brevin cottage.
- **First Impression:** A hollow-eyed cooper clutching his dead-not-dead wife's hand, begging anyone not to take her away again.
- **Public Role:** Wren's husband; a respected craftsman; the man at the center of the opening crisis.
- **Voice:** Cracked, pleading, swinging between hope and horror.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Keep Wren; undo her death; make the town stop being afraid.
- **Secret (DM-Only):** He has **secretly sought out the Gravecallers** for a way to keep Wren — a desperate act that pulls the player toward that faction and endangers the town.
- **Fear:** Losing Wren a second time; that he's damning her by holding on.
- **What he offers:** The human stakes of letting go; a hook into the Gravecallers; a relationship that makes the campaign's theme personal.
- **Possible evolution:** Learns to release Wren (catharsis); falls deeper into the Gravecallers; or breaks entirely.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 3 — **Commoner** (AC 11, ~9 HP). Not a fighter; an emotional center.

## 19. Halla Coalmont
- **Tier:** Major (hub NPC). **Faction:** Independent; sister to Reeve Marda Coalmont. **Location:** The Drowned Lantern (Hollowmere's inn).
- **First Impression:** A loud, generous innkeeper who feeds you before she trusts you and trusts you faster than she should.
- **Public Role:** Keeper of the Drowned Lantern, Hollowmere's social hub; the player's likely base.
- **Voice:** Booming, gossipy, warm, secretly observant.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Keep her inn full, her town fed, and her sister out of trouble.
- **Secret (DM-Only):** She **overhears everything** in Hollowmere and quietly worries about Reke and the basin scheme on her sister's behalf — a low-key, reliable **rumor and clue conduit** for the player, and an early warning system.
- **What she offers:** Lodging, food, rumors (the rumor table lives at her bar), introductions, a safe base, motherly protection.
- **Possible evolution:** The player's home-base ally; a casualty that raises the stakes; a key witness against Reke.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 3 — **Commoner/Guard-lite** (AC 12, ~16 HP; surprisingly tough with a cudgel). Not a combatant by trade.

## 20. Old Sashe
- **Tier:** Major (the wilderness expert / solo-safety guide). **Faction:** Independent fen-guide; loose Mourners' Circle ties. **Location:** A stilt-hut at the Greyfens' edge.
- **First Impression:** A weathered, ageless peat-cutter who talks to the fog and somehow always knows where the firm ground is.
- **Public Role:** The Reach's best fen-guide; the only one who'll lead folk into the Greyfens and bring them back.
- **Voice:** Slow, dry, riddling; names every pool and stone like old friends.
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Keep the fens' fragile balance; guide the worthy; warn off the foolish.
- **Secret (DM-Only):** Sashe has seen the **Remembrances gathering and drifting toward the drowned shrine** for years and has quietly mapped their movement — a unique clue source pointing straight at the shrine, offered only to someone who respects the fens and the dead.
- **Fear:** That the fens — and the dead in them — are being pulled somewhere terrible.
- **What Sashe offers:** Safe passage through the Greyfens (a key **solo-safety** resource), a map of the dead's drift (a shrine clue), survival lore.
- **Possible evolution:** The player's guide to the shrine; a guardian who falls; a quiet sage of the synthesis ending.
- **Mechanics:** Tier 2 — **Scout/Druid-lite** (AC 14, ~30 HP, Survival/Nature/Stealth expert, a little primal magic: *Pass Without Trace, Entangle*). Capable but avoids fights.

# Stage 9 — Major NPC Expansion

> These NPCs fill gaps the foundation 20 left open: a non-Ledger/Compact early Reach antagonist, Ring 1 regional anchors elevated to major, and Caradril's full power-structure (council, Charter Houses, crime, temple, guild, Remnant inner circle). **Player-safe blocks are surfaceable; Secret/DM-Only blocks are earned.** Mechanics use reference profiles, not copied stat blocks. Numbering continues 21+.

## 21. Hewett Drane, "the Reedwarden"
- **Tier:** Major (early Reach antagonist; non-faction). **Faction:** Independent (a self-appointed "protector" with a private armed band). **Location:** The Greyfens' inner channels; a fortified reed-island camp.
- **First Impression (player-safe):** A huge, soft-voiced fenman in a coat of stitched hides who speaks of the marsh as *his*, and of strangers as guests who haven't yet earned leaving.
- **Public Role:** A feared fen-strongman who "keeps order" in the lawless Greyfens — part guide, part toll-taker, part warlord; the Compact pretends he doesn't exist.
- **Voice:** Slow, courteous, possessive; calls the fen "the lady" and everyone "guest."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Keep the fens his; profit from anyone crossing them; be the one strong thing in a drowning land.
- **Secret / True Motivation (DM-Only):** Drane has been quietly **robbing the drowned dead and the failed-rite graves** for grave-goods and "old glass," selling to Gull and the Ledger — and he's started losing men to the drift himself. He half-believes the fen is "feeding," and feeds it bodies to keep his luck. A grubby, dangerous obstacle who sits astride the player's route to the shrine, **not** a Court agent — a frontier predator profiting off the crisis (M3/M5 grit).
- **Fear:** The fen turning on *him*; being made to leave the only place he matters.
- **Leverage:** Controls the safest deep-fen channels; holds hostages/debts; knows where Sashe won't go.
- **What he knows:** The deep-fen routes; which graves he's robbed (M5 evidence); that the dead "drift toward the deep water."
- **What he wants from the player:** Tribute, service, or to be left alone; failing that, to make an example.
- **What he can offer:** Deep-fen passage (rival to Sashe), grave-goods, blunt local muscle — at a price and a moral cost.
- **How he can harm the player:** Ambush in his own marsh; turning fen-folk against them; holding a needed person hostage.
- **Relationship Links:** Buys from / sells to Gull Heddwyn and (via cutouts) Nessa Coyle/the Ledger; despised by Old Sashe and the Mourners; the Compact ignores him.
- **Current Attitude:** Territorial, transactional. Tests newcomers; respects strength, punishes disrespect.
- **Possible Evolution:** A defeated/turned local power who guides the player to the shrine's approaches; a recurring fen-tyrant; or fen-drift fodder himself (his camp found empty).
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 1 — Berserker/Veteran-like** (AC 15, ~75 HP, two-handed maul + net; Reckless; commands 3–5 **Bandit/Thug-like** fenmen). Solo danger: high *in the fen*, telegraphed (his tolls and reputation precede him); low if met on open road. Noncombat: pay the toll, out-guide him with Sashe, turn his frightened men, expose his grave-robbing to the Mourners. **Scaling:** drop the band / add fen-hazards.
- **DM-Only Notes:** Use him to make the Greyfens feel *owned* and the route to the shrine *contested* without invoking any major faction. His grave-robbing is a clean M5 evidence source.
- **Related:** M3, M5; the Greyfens sites; Old Sashe, Gull, the Mourners.

## 22. Mother Ezrith Combe — *(elevated to Major; Ashgarden Vale anchor)*
> Combe already exists as a secondary in `SECONDARY_NPCS.md`; Stage 9 **elevates** her to major as the Vale's moral anchor and a node of the M6 folk-truth network. Use this fuller profile; the secondary entry remains as a quick-reference stub.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Mourners' Circle (eldest, Ashgarden Vale). **Location:** Orchardmere, the Old Circle grave-garden.
- **First Impression:** A stern, grief-worn old woman with hands stained from washing the dead and eyes that weigh you before you speak.
- **Public Role:** The Vale's most respected grief-keeper; the one who says aloud what the comfortable Vale would rather not hear.
- **Voice:** Proverbs and flat truths; "the dead are owed, and the Vale's stopped paying."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Let the Vale's dead rest truly; break the cover-up and the relic-fraud.
- **Secret / True Motivation (DM-Only):** Holds the **fullest Vale version of the "Concord ate the dead" folk-memory** (M6; gated to Act 3) and knows some Mourner elders Reach-wide are stockpiling shrine-breaking resolve. She is the Vale twin of Grandmother Wend, and a key to the "let the dead rest" endings.
- **Fear:** That the Vale's pride will doom it the way denial doomed the old roads; that the songs die with her.
- **Leverage:** The Vale's deepest community trust; the old songs.
- **What she knows:** Every hushed Vale failure; the relic-fraud's blasphemy; the gated folk-truth.
- **What she wants from the player:** Proof of respect for the dead before she opens the old songs; an honest agent against Tindle's cover-up and Pell's quarrying.
- **What she can offer:** The Vale Mourner network, the M6 folk-path, moral authority, safe houses among the devout.
- **How she can harm the player:** Turn the devout Vale against a desecrator; withhold the one path she guards.
- **Relationship Links:** Kin-in-spirit to Wend (Reach), both Sennets, Bryd (Coast), Mam Tace; at open odds with Tindle and W. Pell; conscience-pull on Frost and Petronel.
- **Current Attitude:** Watchful, testing. Warmer fast to anyone who honors a grave.
- **Possible Evolution:** The Vale's conscience and the player's M6 mentor; a martyr if the cover-up turns ugly; or radicalized toward the shrine-breakers.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Priest-like** (AC 12, ~30 HP; high Insight/Religion; *Cure Wounds, Gentle Repose, Spirit Guardians* if the dead are threatened). Noncombatant by choice.
- **DM-Only Notes:** She and Wend should *feel* like the same faith across two regions — reuse cadence. Gate her apex song per `REVELATION_MAP.md`.
- **Related:** M5, M6; Orchardmere, Saint Veddow's; Wend, Bryd, Tindle, W. Pell.

## 23. Goodwife Sennet — *(elevated to Major; Tollwood anchor)*
> Elevated from secondary. The Tollwood's wisest voice and keeper of the gated deep-wood secret.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Mourners' Circle forest-elder. **Location:** Coldhearth, the Elder's Hut, deep in the Tollwood.
- **First Impression:** A still, ancient woman whose silences carry more than most people's words; the wood seems to listen when she speaks.
- **Public Role:** Coldhearth's forest-elder and keeper of the old bargain that keeps the deep wood asleep.
- **Voice:** Spare, weighted; long pauses; "the wood keeps its rules, or it keeps you."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Keep the deep wood and her people safe; uphold the bargain (offer at the toll-shrines, never cut the marked trees).
- **Secret / True Motivation (DM-Only):** Holds the **deep secret of the Old Mast's pre-Concord presence** (gated regional landmark-power, NOT the Hollow Court) and the oblique "the grey lords took the dead before the roads fell" witness (M5/M6, gated). She knows Dorr's cutting and the cult's spread are waking what should sleep.
- **Fear:** The bargain breaking in her lifetime; the wood's old presence stirring; her people paying for outsiders' greed.
- **Leverage:** The woodfolk's absolute trust; the only living knowledge of the bargain.
- **What she knows:** The wood's deep history and rules; the oblique pre-Concord/harvest half-memory; how the cutting and the cult endanger it.
- **What she wants from the player:** Respect for the wood's rules; help curbing Dorr's deep-cutting and the cult before the bargain fails.
- **What she can offer:** The Tollwood M5/M6 fragment, safe deep-wood guidance (with Brann), the woodfolk's aid.
- **How she can harm the player:** Refuse the wood to the disrespectful; the woodfolk close ranks; the guides won't take them deep.
- **Relationship Links:** Leads the woodfolk; Gethin and Brann defer to her; kin-in-faith to Combe/Bryd/Wend; wary of the sheltered Gravecallers.
- **Current Attitude:** Reserved, patient, testing respect for "the rules."
- **Possible Evolution:** The Tollwood's sage-ally on the gated Old Mast path; a casualty if the bargain breaks; the voice that warns of the deep-wood catastrophe.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Druid/Priest-like** (AC 12, ~33 HP; Nature/Survival/Insight; minor primal magic *Speak with Animals, Entangle, Gust*). Power is knowledge and the wood's regard, not combat.
- **DM-Only Notes:** Distinct from Mother Sennet of Saint Veddow's (shared surname, different people). The Old Mast presence is a **regional power**, never named as the Court or a new god.
- **Related:** M5, M6 (gated), Tollwood clocks; the Old Mast; Brann, Combe, Hesk Dorr.

## 24. Salt-Mother Bryd — *(elevated to Major; Pale Coast anchor)*
> Elevated from secondary. The Coast's rite-keeper and honest maritime mystery-window.
- **Tier:** Major. **Faction:** Mourners' Circle (Cobble Strand). **Location:** Cobble Strand, the Tide-Shrine, under the Drowned Lamp.
- **First Impression:** A gaunt, grave woman with salt-cracked hands who looks like she hasn't slept since the tide turned.
- **Public Role:** Keeper of the salt-and-tide death-rites; the one who settles the sea's drowned dead.
- **Voice:** Tide-slow, salt-dry, sorrowful; "the sea keeps its own — but it's keeping too many now."
- **Motivation (player-safe):** Settle the sea's drowned; hold the strand at peace as the drowned-tide rises.
- **Secret / True Motivation (DM-Only):** Holds the **fullest coastal old songs** — "the Lamps kept the drowned for the Concord" (M5/M6 oblique, gated) — and has watched the drowned "come home" toward the Drowned Lamp faster every season (M5). The Coast's honest M2/M5 window and moral conscience.
- **Fear:** The day the salt-and-tide rite fails outright and the sea gives back everyone it took.
- **Leverage:** The only one who can teach the salt-and-tide rite; the coast's deepest trust.
- **What she knows:** The salt-and-tide rite; the drowned's homing toward the Lamp (M5); the gated old songs (M6).
- **What she wants from the player:** Respect for the drowned and the rite; help against the wreckers and the relic-trade fouling the dead.
- **What she can offer:** The salt-and-tide rite (a real tool vs. coastal restless dead), the Coast M5/M6 path, the Mourner network's coastal arm.
- **How she can harm the player:** Withhold the rite and the songs; turn the devout coast against a desecrator.
- **Relationship Links:** Kin-in-faith to Wend/Combe/both Sennets; defers to Headman Strand on worldly matters; hates the wreckers (Quint); wary of the Remnant (Voll) and the Wardens' silence (Verge/Sef).
- **Current Attitude:** Exhausted, grave, testing. Opens to genuine reverence for the dead.
- **Possible Evolution:** The Coast's conscience and rite-mentor; broken by a mass drowned-tide event; the voice for the "let the sea's dead rest" outcome.
- **Mechanics:** **Tier 2 noncombat — Priest-like** (AC 12, ~30 HP; Religion/Insight; salt-and-tide rite + *Gentle Repose, Cure Wounds*). Noncombatant.
- **DM-Only Notes:** The Lamp's "presence" and the drowned-tide are gated **maritime** echoes, never the Court. Keep her songs oblique until Act 3.
- **Related:** M2, M5, M6 (gated); the Drowned Lamp; Wenna Roke, Mabon Crale, Quint, Verge.
