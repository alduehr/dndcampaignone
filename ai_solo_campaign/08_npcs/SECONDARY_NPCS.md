## AI Use

Secondary NPCs of the Sundering Reach — recurring local characters tied to settlements, factions, dungeons, and quests created in Stage 3. **First Impression / public role is player-safe; Secret/Complication is DM-only.** Combat Relevance gives a fallback stat profile per `DND_MECHANICS_REQUIREMENTS.md` Tier 2. ## Hollowmere

### Clerk Pevin Oss — Reachward Compact records-keeper *(registered)*
- **Location:** Hollowmere council hall, records office. **Role:** Compact clerk; keeper of surveys, filings, and votes.
- **First Impression:** A pinched, ink-smelling man who guards his ledgers like a dragon and flinches at raised voices.
- **Motivation:** Keep his records perfect and his head down; avoid being blamed for anything.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** He has *noticed* that the basin "water-rights" filings don't add up and Reke's surveys are doctored — but he's too frightened of Reke to say so. He'll talk to someone who protects him. **Holds C-M2-3 / C-M4-2.**
- **Relationship Link:** Answers to Coalmont; quietly afraid of Reke; respects honest authority.
- **What They Know:** The doctored filings; the real (alarming) basin survey; who voted which way.
- **How They Enter Play:** Hooks 4/5 (the basin investigation); the player needs the records, and Pevin needs courage or cover.
- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Commoner** (noncombatant). Will hide, not fight.

### Tallytooth Ren — Cinder Ledger enforcer *(registered)*
- **Location:** Hollowmere counting-house and streets. **Role:** Domic's "collections" man; the Ledger's local muscle.
- **First Impression:** A big, gold-toothed man with a friendly grin and very still eyes, who calls everyone "friend."
- **Motivation:** Get paid; rise in the Ledger; avoid actual violence if a threat will do.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** He's softer than he looks — he hates squeezing grieving families and is one bad collection away from quitting (or being turned). Knows more Ledger dirt than he lets on.
- **Relationship Link:** Works for Domic Sael; muscle for Vyre's interests; uneasy ally of the watch.
- **What They Know:** Who owes the Ledger; the vault's guard schedule; that "something's off" with Vyre's relic-buying.
- **How They Enter Play:** Hook 10 (debt-squeeze), the Ledger Vault heist (catch-and-intimidate obstacle), or as a turnable enforcer.
- **Combat Relevance:** **Thug/Spy-like** (AC 13, ~40 HP, grapple + intimidation). Catches and threatens; rarely kills. Solo danger: low-moderate, telegraphed.

### Reeve's Sergeant Hesk — Compact watch *(town watch leader)*
- **Location:** Hollowmere; the watch barracks. **Role:** Coalmont's right hand; runs the town watch.
- **First Impression:** A square, scarred woman who's seen too much fen-death and trusts competence over words.
- **Motivation:** Keep Hollowmere orderly; protect Coalmont; survive the crisis.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** Loyal to Coalmont over the council; would quietly help the player against Reke if Coalmont asked.
- **Relationship Link:** Coalmont's enforcer; wary of Reke; respects the Wardens.
- **What They Know:** Town crime, the watch's limits, who's been seen where (useful for tailing Reke).
- **How They Enter Play:** Deputizing the player; backup on lawful jobs; the watch's response to player crimes.
- **Combat Relevance:** **Veteran-like** (AC 17, ~58 HP). Competent; the toughest lawful fighter the player meets early.

## Kettle Bridge

### Bailiff Aldwin Cray — Reachward Compact *(town head)*
- **Location:** Kettle Bridge, Compact House. **Role:** Resident bailiff; governs the bridge-town.
- **First Impression:** A crisp, ambitious official who quotes regulations and eyes you as a problem to be filed.
- **Motivation:** Win Coalmont's reeve post in Hollowmere; keep the bridge profitable and quiet.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** He privately knows the river-drownings are unnatural but suppresses it because solving "ghost stories" won't advance his career — and a scandal might. He'll authorize an *off-books* investigation to keep his hands clean.
- **Relationship Link:** Rival of Coalmont; uses Bly; commands Sergeant-equivalent watch.
- **What They Know:** The drowning reports he's buried; bridge politics; the toll-farm's books.
- **How They Enter Play:** "The Broken Arch" and "The Bailiff's Ladder" hooks; a morally grey patron.
- **Combat Relevance:** none-low — **Noble/Spy-like** (AC 13, ~27 HP). Fights with authority, not steel.

### Toll-farmer Quenna Bly — Cinder Ledger
- **Location:** Kettle Bridge, the Toll-Span. **Role:** Runs the toll and grain-mill; the Ledger's bridge agent.
- **First Impression:** A brisk, river-weathered woman who counts your coin twice and misses nothing.
- **Motivation:** Maximize the toll-farm; quietly grow rich on river-salvage.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** She fishes Concord salvage from the river (including from the Sunken Tollhouse) and sells it upstream — an M3 thread and a crime the Mourners would punish.
- **Relationship Link:** Ledger (Vyre's interests); buys from desperate boatmen; mistrusts Cray.
- **What They Know:** What comes up from the river; who buys salvage; the Sunken Tollhouse exists (and pretends it doesn't).
- **How They Enter Play:** "Toll and Trouble," the Sunken Tollhouse (her looting), the salvage trade.
- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Commoner/Spy-lite** (AC 12, ~16 HP). Hires others; doesn't fight.

### Bargemaster Tibb — independent
- **Location:** Kettle Bridge, the Wet Ward. **Role:** Leads the bargemen; knows the river.
- **First Impression:** A burly, superstitious riverman missing two fingers, who drops his voice near the water.
- **Motivation:** Keep his crews alive; he's losing men at the broken arch.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** One of his bargemen is a Gravecaller cutout; Tibb half-knows and is afraid to root it out (he's lost too many to scorn anyone who offers comfort).
- **Relationship Link:** Bargemen; uneasy with Cray; the drowned men's families.
- **What They Know:** The river-drownings firsthand; the broken arch; the Sunken Tollhouse entrance.
- **How They Enter Play:** "The Broken Arch" (guide/witness), the Wet Ward, the Gravecaller river-thread.
- **Combat Relevance:** **Commoner/Guard-lite** (AC 12, ~16 HP). Brawler, not a soldier.

### Warden Pell — Ashen Wardens *(registered)*
- **Location:** Kettle Bridge and the Ashwalk Rest; roams. **Role:** Veteran rite-keeper; bitter and overworked.
- **First Impression:** A gaunt, grey-stubbled Warden with a drinker's nose and a stare that's laid too many dead.
- **Motivation:** Do the work; but he's started to *resent* the order's "don't ask why."
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** Pell **suspects the Wardens are hiding something** about the failures (he's right) but lacks the truth (Othetha's secret). He's a volatile, sympathetic crack in the order — a path toward Secret 8 that doesn't require Othetha's trust, but he can also blow up dangerously.
- **Relationship Link:** Othetha (chafes under her silence); Hale (gruff mentor); the Mourners.
- **What They Know:** Decades of rites; that they're failing *worse near water* (M5); that the elders won't explain.
- **How They Enter Play:** "Pell's Doubt" (Ashwalk Rest); a failed-rite call; a bitter informant.
- **Combat Relevance:** **Priest/Veteran-hybrid** (AC 14, ~38 HP, can fight and rite). The most combat-capable Warden the player meets early; avoids fights but won't run from the dead.

### Bann Oester — Mourners' Circle *(registered)*
- **Location:** Kettle Bridge, Two Houses Lane; serves the Reach. **Role:** Grave-digger and corpse-tender for the Mourners.
- **First Impression:** A quiet, spade-handed man with earth permanently in his knuckles and a gentleness that unsettles.
- **Motivation:** Bury the dead right; lately, he can barely keep up, and the river-drowned won't stay buried.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** He's begun digging the river-dead's graves *higher and deeper* and salting them double, improvising against the leakage — and he's noticed they still "pull toward the water" (M5). He's frightened and faithful and would trust a respectful player with what he's seen.
- **Relationship Link:** Mourners' Circle (Wend's network); works near Bly and the bargemen.
- **What They Know:** That the buried dead drift; grave-craft; which graves "won't keep" (a physical M5 clue, like Wren's).
- **How They Enter Play:** Any Kettle Bridge burial scene; "The Broken Arch"; a Mourner-trust path.
- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Commoner** (noncombatant). Strong but no fighter.

## Saltmargin

### Wharf-Factor Iss Marlow — Cinder Ledger
- **Location:** Saltmargin, the Weigh-House. **Role:** Sets the salt rate; the Ledger's western agent.
- **First Impression:** A smooth, salt-pale man with a beautiful scale and a beautiful smile, both rigged.
- **Motivation:** Profit on the salt panic; skim salvage; impress Vyre.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** He skims Concord salvage out of the Pale Coast trade before it reaches Hollowmere (M3 thread), and a mislabeled Remembrance relic sits in his cellar he's too scared to ship. Domic Sael has noticed the shortfalls.
- **Relationship Link:** Reports up to Vyre; rivals Domic; uses Gull's smugglers without admitting it.
- **What They Know:** The salt trade's truth (salt only *slows* failing rites); the skim; the cellar relic.
- **How They Enter Play:** "Light on the Scale," the salt hooks, the Ledger monopoly thread.
- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Spy/Noble-lite** (AC 12, ~20 HP). Hires toughs; flees confrontation.

### Pan-foreman Decca Rull — independent labor
- **Location:** Saltmargin, the Pans. **Role:** Leads the salt-pan crews.
- **First Impression:** A brine-cracked, blunt woman with forearms like cables and a grievance she'll share for free.
- **Motivation:** Fair wages for brutal work; she's ready to strike and choke the Reach's salt.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** She suspects Marlow is robbing the trade and would feed the player evidence to bring him down — but a strike would hurt every grieving family in the Reach, a real moral cost.
- **Relationship Link:** The pan-crews; hates Marlow; sympathetic to the inland villages.
- **What They Know:** The skim; the pans; the true cost of the salt panic.
- **How They Enter Play:** The strike threat; "Light on the Scale"; a labor-vs-Ledger pivot.
- **Combat Relevance:** **Commoner/Guard-lite** (AC 12, ~16 HP). Tough; leads a crowd, not a battle.

### "Gull" Heddwyn — smuggler
- **Location:** Saltmargin, the Tar Wharf (and the fen's Bone Weir). **Role:** Moves contraband, grave-goods, and the occasional relic.
- **First Impression:** A lean, laughing figure who appears at your elbow at night with exactly what you didn't say you wanted.
- **Motivation:** Profit; stay one step ahead of everyone.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** Gull's grave-goods line runs to a Gravecaller buyer and a Pale Coast fence — a hidden thread linking Saltmargin → the Greyfens → the Coast. Gull doesn't care what they smuggle, which makes them dangerous.
- **Relationship Link:** Smuggler network; buys from Bly and Marlow's leavings; sells to Gravecallers.
- **What They Know:** Every back-route in the western Reach; who buys forbidden things.
- **How They Enter Play:** "Grave-Goods," night-wharf offers, a fixer/black-market contact.
- **Combat Relevance:** **Spy-like** (AC 13, ~22 HP, Stealth/Sleight). Slippery; vanishes rather than fights.

## Greywater Holm

### Elder Mabb Tay — Mourners' Circle
- **Location:** Greywater Holm, the Crooked Longhall. **Role:** Village elder; the holm's only authority.
- **First Impression:** A bowed, hollow-eyed old woman who has buried too many and stopped pretending it's enough.
- **Motivation:** Spare her village more grief; she's lost faith the rites can be saved.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** She **tolerates the Gravecaller cell** because they can quiet a failed rite when no one else can — and she's ashamed and frightened of what that means. A quietly heretical Mourner; a gentle door to the cult and a tragic figure.
- **Relationship Link:** Wend's distant kin and rival in temperament; shelters Sister Knell's work; trusts Sashe.
- **What They Know:** The rites fail worst here (M5); the cell exists; the old grief-songs (partial M6).
- **How They Enter Play:** Any Greywater scene; the failing-rites hooks; the Gravecaller door.
- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Acolyte/Priest-like** (AC 11, ~20 HP). Noncombatant.

### Fowler Den Hask — independent / half-recruited
- **Location:** Greywater Holm and the fen blinds. **Role:** Fowler; knows the Greyfens' hidden ways.
- **First Impression:** A wiry, watchful man who talks to his decoys and not much to people.
- **Motivation:** Keep fowling the fen that's killing his neighbors; he's half-drawn to the cell's comfort.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** He's been to the Drowned Blind and is wavering on joining the Gravecallers; he'll guide a player there if trusted — or warn the cell if betrayed.
- **Relationship Link:** Knell is working on him; knows Sashe's routes; the cell.
- **What They Know:** The fen blinds; the Drowned Blind's location; the drift (M5).
- **How They Enter Play:** A secondary fen-guide (cheaper, riskier than Sashe); the Gravecaller door.
- **Combat Relevance:** **Scout-like** (AC 13, ~16 HP, Survival/Stealth). Avoids fights.

## Candlewick

### Hayward Tomas Greel — Reachward Compact
- **Location:** Candlewick. **Role:** Village head; prosperous farmer-official.
- **First Impression:** A comfortable, careful man proud of his tidy village and slow to believe the crisis is real.
- **Motivation:** Keep Candlewick orderly and prosperous; he's tempted by Remnant coin.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** He holds a secret Remnant offer to "purchase and protect" the archive-stone — selling the Reach's most accessible Concord text. He's not corrupt, just provincial and tempted.
- **Relationship Link:** The Compact; hosts Briss; the Remnant's quiet courtship.
- **What They Know:** The stone's value to outsiders; village politics; that his village's rites still "hold" (M5 geography, un-interpreted).
- **How They Enter Play:** "The Sold Stone," the archive-stone hooks, a Vale-edge patron.
- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Noble/Commoner-like** (AC 12, ~22 HP). Noncombatant.

### Chandler-master Yorrin Tay — independent craft
- **Location:** Candlewick, the Tallow Works. **Role:** Makes the Reach's candles and funeral tapers.
- **First Impression:** A beeswax-scented, exacting craftsman proud that his tapers light every Reach funeral.
- **Motivation:** Keep the works running; supply the Mourners; protect his trade.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** He's noticed the funeral tapers "burn wrong" at failed rites (gutter and weep) and quietly fears his candles are blamed for the failures — a folk-clue that the failures are real and spreading.
- **Relationship Link:** Supplies the Mourners Reach-wide; Candlewick pride.
- **What They Know:** Where every funeral happens (taper orders = a map of the dead); the "wrong-burning" omen.
- **How They Enter Play:** Supply runs; a folk-clue source; a Mourner connection.
- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Commoner** (noncombatant).

## Harrowgast & the Sunder Heights

### Overseer Brack — Reachward Compact
- **Location:** Harrowgast. **Role:** Town boss; keeps order by force.
- **First Impression:** A scarred ex-miner with fists like maul-heads and no patience for ghost-talk.
- **Motivation:** Hit the salvage quota; keep the rough town from boiling over.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** He's losing control — the deep-dread is emptying his dig-crews — and rules harder to hide his fear, risking a revolt. He'd accept outside help he'd never ask for.
- **Relationship Link:** Nominal Compact authority; rivalry with the Ledger and Remnant crews; commands miner-toughs.
- **What They Know:** The town; the "thin" returnees he refuses to discuss; the Deep Mouth.
- **How They Enter Play:** Harrowgast governance; the Ledger–Remnant conflict; the Deep Adit.
- **Combat Relevance:** **Thug/Veteran-hybrid** (AC 14, ~45 HP). Brawler town-boss; tough but not lethal-minded.

### Ledger buyer Nessa Coyle — Cinder Ledger
- **Location:** Harrowgast, the Spoil-Heaps weigh-post. **Role:** Buys "old glass" salvage for the Ledger.
- **First Impression:** A sharp, fur-wrapped woman who weighs a relic and your worth in the same glance.
- **Motivation:** Corner the Heights salvage before the Remnant does; impress Vyre.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** She's losing the bidding war to the Remnant's deeper pockets and is desperate enough to hire rough help — including the player — to secure "old glass" by any means.
- **Relationship Link:** Ledger (Vyre); bitter rival of the Remnant crews; uses miner-toughs.
- **What They Know:** "Old glass" is more than rock (M3); the Remnant's interest; the Deep Adit's value.
- **How They Enter Play:** "Old Glass," "The Bidding War," a Ledger patron in the north.
- **Combat Relevance:** none-low — **Spy/Noble-lite** (AC 13, ~22 HP). Hires muscle.

### Dig-boss Hennet — independent labor
- **Location:** Harrowgast, the Open Adits. **Role:** Leads the digging crews.
- **First Impression:** A capable, sweat-soaked foreman whose hands have started to shake.
- **Motivation:** Keep his crews working and alive; he's terrified of the deep.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** He saw something in the deep tunnels he can't describe and is hiding his terror to keep his job; he'll beg the player to go where his crews won't (the Deep Adit).
- **Relationship Link:** Brack's foreman; the crews trust him; the "thin" returnees haunt him.
- **What They Know:** The deep tunnels; what level the fear starts; the way to the Deep Mouth.
- **How They Enter Play:** "The Crew That Won't Dig"; the Deep Adit guide/petitioner.
- **Combat Relevance:** **Guard/Commoner-like** (AC 13, ~22 HP). Works, doesn't fight.

### Reclaimer-agent Veska Dunn — Concord Remnant
- **Location:** The Sunder Heights; the Deep Adit's approaches. **Role:** Quorrin's field salvage-master; leads a Remnant crew.
- **First Impression:** A calm, gloved scholar-in-the-field who treats a haunted ruin like a library and you like a footnote.
- **Motivation:** Reach the Deep Adit; secure relics and proof for the Remnant; she believes she's saving knowledge.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** She is a knowing servant of the inner Remnant's aim (seize the harvest) — but rationalizes it as preservation. A field-level face of the Reclamation clock and a recurring rival; she'd rather hire or outpace the player than kill them.
- **Relationship Link:** Reports to Quorrin Vane (major); rivals the Ledger; commands hired bravos.
- **What They Know:** The Deep Adit is a harvest node (M6/M7 foreshadow); Concord Script; the Remnant's plan (partial).
- **How They Enter Play:** The Deep Adit, the Whispering Cairn (rival), the Heights race; a recurring Act 1–2 rival who escalates.
- **Combat Relevance:** **Scout/Mage-lite** (AC 13, ~30 HP, *Mage Armor, Misty Step, Shatter*; light caster). Wins by maneuver; flees cheap death. Solo danger: moderate, telegraphed.

## The Ashwalk Rest

- **Location:** The Ashwalk Rest. **Role:** Runs the waystation; the order's quartermaster-of-mercy.

## Caradril (Stage 4 — First Major City)

> Caradril is the SE city-state (~10–12 days from Hollowmere), the Cinder Ledger's HQ and the Concord Remnant's seat. Major NPCs Calla Vyre, Quorrin Vane, and Lector Briss are seated here (see `MAJOR_NPCS.md`). These fifteen secondary NPCs populate its districts. Player-safe role vs. DM secret kept separate; Combat Relevance per Tier 2.

### Magister Halloran Voss — Tidewater Council (First Magister)
- **Location:** Caradril, the Magisterium (Magisters' Hall). **Role:** Head of the ruling council.
- **First Impression:** A genial, well-fed man who agrees with whoever spoke last and dreads having to decide.
- **Motivation:** Keep Caradril calm, prosperous, and himself unblamed; avoid the Reach crisis being "his problem."
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** He has quietly taken Ledger money and is compromised on the relic-trade vote; he is not part of the conspiracy, only weak — a pressure point, and the path to the unidentified "Magisterium correspondent" thread (he may, without knowing, be the conduit Reke writes to, or know who is — keep this a lead).
- **Relationship Link:** Chairs the council; beholden to the Charter Houses and (secretly) the Ledger; outmaneuvered by Tolm, pushed by Dann.
- **What They Know:** Council business; which magisters owe whom; the pile of ignored Reach petitions.
- **How They Enter Play:** `Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER`; any council hearing; the correspondent thread.
- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Noble-like** (AC 12, ~27 HP), noncombatant.

### Magister Sefa Dann — Tidewater Council (reform bloc)
- **Location:** Caradril, the Magisterium / the Wardmoot. **Role:** Reform magister; the player's lever to make the council act.
- **First Impression:** A sharp, tired woman of modest charter who says the unwelcome true thing in a room that wants comfort.
- **Motivation:** Force Caradril to heed the Reach crisis and rein in the relic-trade before it's too late.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** She is genuinely good and genuinely outgunned; allying with her risks her ruin (Tolm and the Ledger will move against her). A sincere ally who needs the player's evidence and muscle to survive politically.
- **Relationship Link:** Leads the Wardmoot reformers; rival of Tolm; courted by the ignored Wardens; would ally with Briss if disillusioned.
- **What They Know:** Who blocks reform and why; the relic-trade's spread; the ignored Warden warnings.
- **How They Enter Play:** `Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER`, the relic-trade vote, carrying the Reach's warning to the council.
- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Noble/Acolyte-like** (AC 12, ~22 HP), noncombatant.

### Charter-Lord Esren Tolm — Charter Houses (old money)
- **Location:** Caradril, the Magisterium. **Role:** Head of the oldest Charter House; the Ledger's rival.
- **First Impression:** A cold, courteous aristocrat who treats the city as his family's property on loan to everyone else.
- **Motivation:** Preserve the old Houses' dominance; humble the upstart Cinder Ledger; keep the comfortable order.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** He hates the Ledger enough to *help the player against Vyre* — for his own ends, not justice. A dangerous ally of convenience; he'd ruin the player the moment they stop being useful.
- **Relationship Link:** Rival of Vyre and the Ledger; blocks Dann's reform; commands old-House clients and Tide-Watch favor.
- **What They Know:** The Houses' secrets; the Ledger's overreach; council leverage.
- **How They Enter Play:** `Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER`; anti-Ledger schemes; a treacherous patron.
- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Noble-like** (AC 13, ~31 HP), noncombatant; fights with charters and clients.

### Captain Yorell Kade — Tide-Watch (city watch)
- **Location:** Caradril, citywide (barracks in the Magisterium). **Role:** Commands the city watch.
- **First Impression:** A square, scarred professional who enforces the law as written and resents how it's written for the rich.
- **Motivation:** Keep the city orderly; do his job honestly within a corrupt frame.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** Privately disgusted by how the Watch favors the Quays over the Sill; he'll quietly back a player who does the right thing where the law won't — a turnable lawful ally.
- **Relationship Link:** Answers to the council; harder on the Sill by order, not conviction; respects competence.
- **What They Know:** City crime; who's bribable; movements and incidents (useful for tails/leads).
- **How They Enter Play:** Deputization, bail, the Watch's response to player crimes; backing the player off-record.
- **Combat Relevance:** **Knight/Veteran-like** (AC 18, ~90 HP, Leadership). The city's toughest lawful fighter; never a default fight.

### Provost Anneth Vell — Cinder Ledger (Caradril deputy)
- **Location:** Caradril, the Ledger Keep (Counting-Quays). **Role:** Runs the Keep; Vyre's Caradril right hand.
- **First Impression:** A precise, unsmiling woman who has read your debts before you sit down.
- **Motivation:** Run the Keep flawlessly; protect Vyre's interests; rise.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** She knows about the Quorrin deal and the deep-vault relic and disapproves of neither — but she fears it's going wrong and keeps her own insurance. Colder and more loyal than Domic Sael; harder to turn, but corruptible by self-interest.
- **Relationship Link:** Vyre's deputy; commands Ledger muscle and clerks (incl. Coll Riis); rival of Tolm's House.
- **What They Know:** The Keep's secrets; the deal's shape; the deep vault.
- **How They Enter Play:** `Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER`; the deep-vault job; Ledger gatekeeper.
- **Combat Relevance:** none-low — **Spy/Noble-like** (AC 14, ~30 HP); commands muscle, rarely fights.

### Underfactor Coll Riis — Cinder Ledger (turnable clerk)
- **Location:** Caradril, the Ledger Keep / the Last Tally. **Role:** Mid-level Ledger clerk on the deal-accounts.
- **First Impression:** A nervous, decent young clerk who drinks alone and checks the door too often.
- **Motivation:** Do right without getting killed; he stumbled onto what the deal *is*.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** He has copied ledger-pages proving the Vyre–Quorrin monopoly deal (an Archive-free M3/M6 clue path) and is terrified. He'll turn for safety, coin, or conscience — and may be silenced if the player dawdles.
- **Relationship Link:** Under Vell; appalled by what he's filing; no allies until the player.
- **What They Know:** The deal-papers; who meets whom; the deep-vault's existence.
- **How They Enter Play:** `Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER`; a frightened informant; a clock-race to protect him.
- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Commoner**, noncombatant (will flee/hide).

### Sub-Lector Tamsin Orr — Concord Remnant (honest scholar)
- **Location:** Caradril, the Lantern Reach (Lamplighters' Hall). **Role:** Briss's student; a Concord Script tutor.
- **First Impression:** An eager, generous young scholar who'd teach a stone to read if it asked nicely.
- **Motivation:** Share knowledge; honor the Concord; she has no idea of the inner circle's aim.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** Wholly innocent — the player's cleanest, strings-free way to *learn Concord Script* (the key clue-gate) if they'd rather not lean on Briss or risk Quorrin. May later be endangered by what she unknowingly enables.
- **Relationship Link:** Briss's student; reveres Quorrin (a dupe like Briss); friendly to curious newcomers.
- **What They Know:** Concord Script; the Stacks' catalogue; nothing of the Reclaimers.
- **How They Enter Play:** `Q_THE_LANTERN_AND_THE_LAMP`; Script tutelage; translations.
- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Commoner/Acolyte-like** (AC 11, ~14 HP), noncombatant.

### Keeper-Archivist Wessel Dree — Concord Remnant (Reclaimer)
- **Location:** Caradril, the Lantern Reach (the Sealed Archive). **Role:** Guards the inner-circle vault.
- **First Impression:** A soft-spoken, immovable old archivist whose courtesy never once reaches his eyes.
- **Motivation:** Keep the Sealed Archive's truth from the unworthy; serve Quorrin's vision.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** A knowing Reclaimer; he knows exactly what the Archive proves (C-M6-1/C-M9) and will lie, delay, and report intruders. The human lock on the Act-3 truth cache; also the keeper of the Wards back-way's secret.
- **Relationship Link:** Quorrin's loyalist; despises Briss's naivety; commands the Archive's wards and guards.
- **What They Know:** The Archive's contents (apex-adjacent — DM); the Wards back-way; who's been asking.
- **How They Enter Play:** `Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE`; the Archive gate; a Reclaimer obstacle.
- **Combat Relevance:** low — **Mage/Acolyte-like** (AC 12, ~30 HP, *Alarm, Hold Person, Misty Step*); raises the alarm rather than brawls.

### Mother Ysarra — Three Thresholds clergy (Cathedral high priest)
- **Location:** Caradril, Highmourn (the Threshold Cathedral). **Role:** Leads the city's great temple.
- **First Impression:** A serene, commanding priest whose calm is a wall built carefully against panic.
- **Motivation:** Keep Caradril's faith — and the Cathedral's authority — steady through the failures.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** She is **covering up the city's raise-dead failures** (Secret 7) to prevent panic, hushing away "wrong-come-backs." Not malicious — frightened and institutional. Exposing or turning her is a key city-internal M1/M5/M6 lever.
- **Relationship Link:** Clergy and Mourners defer to her; pressured by the council to keep calm; opposes the relic-trade.
- **What They Know:** Every failed rite in Caradril; the buried folk-memory of the harvest (genteel, denied).
- **How They Enter Play:** `Q_THE_FUNERAL_THAT_WOULDNT_TAKE`; healing services; the cover-up.
- **Combat Relevance:** none-low — **Priest-like** (AC 13, ~33 HP, low-level divine magic), noncombatant.

### Grave-Magister Lon Quayle — Quiet Houses (funeral official)
- **Location:** Caradril, Highmourn (the Quiet Houses). **Role:** Runs the civic cemetery and funeral works.
- **First Impression:** A grave, meticulous man whose composure has begun, lately, to crack.
- **Motivation:** Lay Caradril's dead to rest properly — and he can no longer pretend it's working.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** A troubled witness to the failing rites; conscience-bound and frightened of Ysarra and the council. He can be *turned into the player's proof* that the crisis has reached Caradril.
- **Relationship Link:** Answers to Ysarra; oversees the gravediggers; quietly sympathetic to the ignored Wardens.
- **What They Know:** Which graves "won't keep"; which raisings failed; where the hushed-up cases lie.
- **How They Enter Play:** `Q_THE_FUNERAL_THAT_WOULDNT_TAKE`; a turnable witness.
- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Commoner/Acolyte-like** (AC 11, ~16 HP), noncombatant.

### Foundress Beck Harrow — craft guilds (foundry-guild mistress)
- **Location:** Caradril, the Crucible (Harrow's Foundry). **Role:** Leads the foundry-guild; runs the relic-smelting.
- **First Impression:** A broad, soot-streaked, plain-spoken master who built her wealth and is starting to fear what it's built on.
- **Motivation:** Profit and her guild's rise — and, lately, to know what her foundry has been melting.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** Her relic-smelting causes "incidents" (M3 by accident; thin-touched workers; a casting that holds a voice). Profit-driven but increasingly scared; a turnable witness, a warning, or a reckless rival.
- **Relationship Link:** Guild power-bloc; paid by the Ledger to smelt evidence; her foreman (Ordo) is bribed by the Remnant; the Sill workers resent her.
- **What They Know:** What's being smelted and why; the incidents; the Ledger/Remnant proxy-war over relics.
- **How They Enter Play:** `Q_THE_SMELTING`; gear commissions; an M3 witness.
- **Combat Relevance:** low — **Guard/Commoner-like** (AC 13, ~26 HP); a tough crafter, not a duelist.

### "Tidewife" Sorrel — Salt Syndicate (boss)
- **Location:** Caradril, the Ashmarket / the Sill. **Role:** Runs the relic black market and smuggling.
- **First Impression:** A warm, motherly fence with a grandmother's smile and a drowned river's patience.
- **Motivation:** Control the quiet-coin trade; keep the Syndicate fed; stay below the Watch.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** She sells relics that hold the dead **without grasping what they are** (M3 made commercial); a specific quiet-coin relic in her keeping "remembers" a recent Reach death. Unsettled by the strange goods; would deal with the player as buyer, fence, or rival.
- **Relationship Link:** Boss of the Syndicate; uneasy truce with the Ledger; rival of the Hush; uses Ashmarket fences.

### The Bellman — the Hush (information broker)
- **Location:** Caradril, citywide (based in the Sill). **Role:** Leads the city's information/blackmail network.
- **First Impression:** Never quite seen plainly — a voice, a coat, a bell-token left behind; people swear he's three different people.
- **Motivation:** Know everything; sell it carefully; owe no one.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** The Hush brokers fragments of the Vyre–Quorrin deal, the Magisterium correspondent thread, and the Sealgate route into the Sunken Wards — fair, costly shortcut-clues. Every secret bought from him carries a hidden price (a favor, a debt, a name). A dangerous convenience.
- **Relationship Link:** Rival of the Syndicate; feared by the Houses (he knows their secrets); the Sill's shadow-power.
- **What They Know:** Nearly everything, in fragments — for a price.
- **How They Enter Play:** `Q_THE_BELLMANS_PRICE`; a clue-shortcut with strings.
- **Combat Relevance:** none-low — **Spy-like** (AC 14, ~22 HP); never fights, always knows the exit; protected by knowing too much.

### Renna Sill — the Sill (community leader)
- **Location:** Caradril, the Sill (the Refugee Boards). **Role:** Speaks for the poor and the thin-born refugees.
- **First Impression:** A fierce, exhausted woman who has decided no one drowns in her district unseen.
- **Motivation:** Shelter the refugees; force the city to see the thin-born; survive the Hush and the Watch.
- **Secret / Complication (DM):** She shelters a "wrong-come-back" refugee from the Reach (a humane M1/M5 echo in the city) and is the player's clearest window onto the **thinning spreading** (M10 made local). A genuine ally who needs help she can't get from anyone above the river.

- **What They Know:** The refugees' stories; who's come back wrong; the Sill's truths the city denies.

- **Combat Relevance:** none — **Commoner**, noncombatant.

### Old Pater Dunk — independent (Sunken Wards guide)
- **Location:** Caradril, the Sill (the Sealgate). **Role:** The delver who knows the dry routes below the city.
- **First Impression:** A leathery, lantern-carrying old man who talks to the dark and answers it.
