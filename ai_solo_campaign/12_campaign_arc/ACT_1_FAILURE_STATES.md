## AI Use

**Mixed secrecy — DM-facing.** The Act 1 failure playbook. **Core rule: every failure REDIRECTS play; none ends the campaign.** When the player fails, loses, flees, antagonizes, or gets caught, find the matching case below and use its four parts: **(1) immediate consequence**, **(2) the redirected path forward**, **(3) which clocks advance** (`../02_runtime_state/WORLD_CLOCKS.md`), and **(4) what NPCs do / what options remain**. Failure costs time, trust, resources, or safety, and *changes the world* — it never blocks all progress. Keep DM-Only causes hidden (the harvest, the steering, the Court); narrate only the visible consequence. Pairs with `../01_runner_protocol/FAILURE_AND_CONSEQUENCES.md`.

> **Solo-play floor:** no Act 1 failure may strand the player with zero leads, zero allies, or zero ways to earn levels. If a failure removes one door, this file opens another.

## Failure Case 1 — The Opening Crisis Goes Unresolved (Wren / the failed funeral)

**Trigger:** The player leaves Wren's situation hanging, botches it, or refuses the opener entirely.

- **Immediate consequence (player-safe):** Hollowmere's fear curdles. One of three branches fires (DM picks by what the player did): **(a)** a frightened mob forms to "put her down"; **(b)** Tomas, desperate, turns to the Gravecallers to keep her (Secret 19 surfaces as *a knock at his door*); or **(c)** the Wardens are forced to act without the player. The town's mood cools from warm toward frightened (NPC_MEMORY: Halla, Wend, Coalmont note it).
- **Redirected path forward:** Each branch is a *new hook*. The mob → a rescue/mediation scene and a Mourner-trust opportunity. Tomas-to-cult → the Gravecaller door opens early (Hook 7 / `Q_ACT1_GRAVECALLER_A_LAST_WORD`). Wardens-act → Hale seeks the player for the *next* failing rite (Hook 9 / `Q_ACT1_WARDEN_THE_TRUE_RITE`). R1 is still reachable through M5/M2 routes regardless.
- **Clocks:** #9 Wren's Fate advances a stage; if branch (b), #5 The Breaking ticks; if the mob harms her, #3 Wardens Break ticks.
- **NPCs / options:** Sefra still has work; Coalmont still needs an honest hand (Hook 5); the level 1→2 milestone can still be earned by closing *any* first community problem (`ACT_1_MILESTONES.md`). Nothing is lost but Hollowmere's easy warmth.

## Failure Case 2 — A Dead or Lost Patron

**Trigger:** A key quest-giver dies, vanishes, is silenced, or is alienated past repair (Sefra, Domic, Coalmont, Wend, Reke-as-patron, Briss, Sashe).

- **Immediate consequence:** That door narrows. (DM-Only: if **Sefra** is the casualty, the steering thread doesn't end — the patron "tidies up," which is itself an Act 2 shock seed; M8 stays reachable via follow-the-money. If **Domic** dies, the Ledger replaces him with the colder Tallytooth/Provost line.)
- **Redirected path forward:** Every patron has a **backup giver for the same content** (see `ACT_1_NPC_GUIDE.md` "Re-Entry Points"): Wardens via Hale → Pell or Sirrin; Compact via Coalmont → Sergeant Hesk or Bailiff Cray (Kettle Bridge); Mourners via Wend → Iola or Mabb Tay (Greywater); Ledger via Domic → the counting-house itself; fens via Sashe → Den Hask; scholarship via Briss → Sub-Lector Tamsin Orr (later, in Caradril). The *quests and clues* survive the *person*.
- **Clocks:** the relevant faction clock ticks (a lost honest patron tends to *advance* the harmful clock — e.g. losing Coalmont advances #2 Basin Drains; losing Wend advances #4 Circle Turns Militant).
- **NPCs / options:** an alienated patron may become a *rival* (a richer scene) rather than a blank. Re-earning trust is a future quest, not a dead end.

## Failure Case 3 — A Burned Faction Bridge

**Trigger:** The player antagonizes, betrays, or publicly humiliates a faction (e.g. exposes the Gravecallers to a purge, robs the Ledger, defies the Compact, mocks the Mourners, crosses the Remnant).

- **Immediate consequence:** That faction's attitude drops (FACTION_STATE), and it acts *against* the player within its Act 1 means: the Ledger calls debts and sends Tallytooth (Threat 3); the Compact watches/fines/jails (see Case 7); the Mourners go cold and close their songs; the Gravecallers' radicals (Ashby) mark the player an enemy; the Remnant's crews race harder (Threat 5).
- **Redirected path forward:** **Burning one faction opens its rivals.** Crossing the Ledger warms the Mourners and Compact toward an anti-Ledger ally; exposing the Gravecallers earns Compact favor; defying the Compact endears the player to the Gravecallers or the squeezed families (Hook 10). The Reach's seven-way tension means there is *always* another patron who likes an enemy of your enemy.
- **Clocks:** the burned faction's clock usually advances (an angry faction pushes its agenda); a rival faction's standing improves.
- **NPCs / options:** a burned bridge is rebuildable through a later make-good quest. R1 clues are spread across *all* factions, so no single faction's enmity blocks the mystery. (DM: never let faction enmity become a death-spiral — enforcers threaten and escalate per Threat 3, they don't assassinate a L1–4 PC.)

## Failure Case 4 — Imprisoned or Detained

**Trigger:** The player is caught at a serious crime (robbing the Ledger vault, aiding outlawed Gravecallers, assaulting an official, sabotage) and the watch takes them.

- **Immediate consequence (player-safe):** Detention by the Tide-Watch's Reach equivalent — Sergeant Hesk's town watch or Bailiff Cray's bridge-watch. Gear may be impounded; a fine or term looms.
- **Redirected path forward — the jail-arc out (always available):**
  - **Talk/law:** plead the case to Coalmont (fair-minded) — a Persuasion/Insight scene, possibly a favor owed.
  - **Bail/coin:** the Ledger (Domic) or a patron posts bail — at the price of a debt or job.
  - **Deal:** the watch offers release in exchange for a dangerous task (deputization; investigate something the watch can't).
  - **Quiet exit:** a Gravecaller/smuggler contact (Gull) or a sympathetic guard arranges an escape — making the player owe the underworld.
- **Clocks:** time passes in the cell — every Act 1 clock ticks modestly while the player is held; #9 Wren's Fate and #2 Basin Drains are the most time-sensitive.
- **NPCs / options:** imprisonment *creates* relationships (a debt to a patron, a deal with the watch, an underworld favor). The player leaves with a new obligation and an intact path to R1. Never a campaign-ender.

## Failure Case 5 — Fled Town Without Resolving the Opening

**Trigger:** The player abandons Hollowmere early — heads for Kettle Bridge, Saltmargin, the Heights, or even toward Caradril — leaving the opening crisis behind.

- **Immediate consequence:** Hollowmere's crisis evolves *without* the player (Case 1 branches fire offstage). But the Reach is deep-built — **the crisis is everywhere.** The fleeing player meets the *same R1 pattern* in the next settlement: failing rites at Kettle Bridge (Bann Oester's drifting river-graves, the Sunken Tollhouse), Greywater Holm's worst-in-the-Reach failures, "thin" miners at Harrowgast, salt-panic at Saltmargin.
- **Redirected path forward:** the regional quests (`09_quests/regional_quests/`) are the open-world net: `Q_THE_BROKEN_ARCH`, `Q_THE_SALT_RUN`, `Q_THE_HOLM_THAT_WONT_BURY_ITS_DEAD`, `Q_RACE_NORTH`, etc. all deliver Act 1 content and R1 routes (especially M5/M2) far from Hollowmere. The player can run the entire early game elsewhere and still arrive at "the basin is the source."
- **Clocks:** #9 Wren's Fate resolves offstage (with consequences the player hears about later); other clocks advance on the regional timescale.
- **NPCs / options:** Hale, Sashe, and the Wardens travel the Reach and will cross the player's path again; the Ashwalk Rest is a reliable re-entry hub anywhere central. Fleeing changes *where* Act 1 happens, not *whether* it happens.

## Failure Case 6 — Failed a Dungeon and Retreated

**Trigger:** The player enters a Concord site (Peat Chapel, Sunken Tollhouse, Whispering Cairn, Ledger Vault, Barrow, Deep Adit), gets overwhelmed or spooked, and pulls out.

- **Immediate consequence:** The objective there is unmet *for now* — a relic falls to a rival, an inscription stays unread, a haunt stays unlaid, thin-touch lingers (reversible — `SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md`). The player keeps their life and what they learned getting there.
- **Redirected path forward:** **Retreat is a designed option, not a loss.** The site persists; the player can return better-prepared (an ally, a Warden, rite-salt, a scout of the route). If a *rival* claimed the prize (Threat 5), that reroutes the clue to a new scene (buy it back, steal it, expose the rival) rather than deleting it. Every dungeon's clue has alternate paths (`ACT_1_CLUE_TRAILS.md`), so a failed delve never blocks R1.
- **Clocks:** the relevant faction clock may tick (a rival who grabbed the relic advances #6 or #7); the master clock is unmoved by a *retreat*.
- **NPCs / options:** Sirrin (Ashwalk Rest) heals and resupplies; Sashe guides a safer return; Hale assists a second attempt. Retreat costs time and maybe a prize — never the campaign.

## Failure Case 7 — Antagonized the Authorities (Compact / a town)

**Trigger:** The player repeatedly defies the Compact, sabotages the basin works clumsily, or makes a public enemy of Reke before they can prove anything.

- **Immediate consequence:** The watch tightens on the player (fines, surveillance, a warning from Hesk); Reke, *smiling*, isolates them politically and uses the friction to paint them as a troublemaker (DM-Only: this serves Reke — but he stays the reasonable politician; do not expose him).
- **Redirected path forward:** the player pivots to the **honest Compact** (Coalmont, Hesk) who *need* an outsider precisely because the official channels are compromised — turning friction into the `Q_ACT1_COMPACT_THE_REEVES_DOUBT` alliance. Or the player goes *around* the law (Gravecaller/smuggler routes, the Mourners' community trust). Crossing the authorities openly makes Reke's quiet enemies into the player's friends.
- **Clocks:** #2 Basin Drains advances (Reke exploits the disorder); if the player has alienated Coalmont too, it advances faster.
- **NPCs / options:** even a watched player can work through allies and the night-side of the Reach. The R3 reveal (Reke's treachery) is *delayed*, not denied — Act 2 still delivers it.

## Failure Case 8 — Caught With the Gravecallers

**Trigger:** The player is seen meeting, aiding, or joining the outlawed cult and the Compact learns of it.

- **Immediate consequence:** The player is suspect under the Compact's anti-Gravecaller law (a purge may be looming — `Q_ACT1_GRAVECALLER_A_LAST_WORD`). Lawful NPCs cool; a fine, detention (Case 4), or expulsion threatens; the Mourners are conflicted (they share the grief but not the crime).
- **Redirected path forward:**
  - **Lean in:** the player accepts the cult's protection — gaining the Knock, fen routes, and the richest Act 1–2 *truth-source* (capped at R1 fragments) at the cost of Compact standing.
  - **Lean out:** the player gives the Compact something (information, a captured radical, distance) to clear themselves — gaining Compact favor and a cult enemy (Ashby).
  - **Play both:** with Insight/Deception, the player walks the line (Iola Wend and Sister Knell are the soft bridges).
- **Clocks:** #5 The Breaking advances if a purge fires (the cult radicalizes, Ashby ascendant); #4 Circle Turns Militant may tick if Mourners are pushed toward the cult.
- **NPCs / options:** being "caught" forces a *choice of allegiance*, which is good play, not a dead end. Either path keeps R1 fully reachable (the drift/Wren/political routes are untouched by cult standing).

## Failure Case 9 — Lost the Opening Mystery Clue

**Trigger:** The player loses, destroys, or never finds the *specific* first clue they were chasing (Wren is "taken back" by the shrine before they speak with her; a relic is smelted; a filing is burned; Sashe's map is lost).

- **Immediate consequence:** That one clue is gone (status `missed` in `CLUE_INDEX.md`).
- **Redirected path forward:** **the three-clue rule guarantees redundancy.** Every Act 1 conclusion (R1) has 3–4 independent paths (`ACT_1_CLUE_TRAILS.md`). Lost Wren? The drift (M5) and the basin shape (M2) still deliver the personal-and-pattern frame. Lost the filings? Coalmont's testimony and surveillance still build the political route. Lost the relic? The Gravecallers' channeled dead and the Ledger's other relics remain. **No single clue is load-bearing.**
- **Clocks:** losing a clue to a *faction* (smelted relic, buried filing) advances that faction's clock; losing it to the *shrine* (Wren taken back) ticks #1/#9 and is itself a chilling new clue.
- **NPCs / options:** the DM should visibly route the player to an alternate path ("you've lost this thread, but old Sashe sends word…"). A lost clue makes the world feel dangerous and alive; it never stops the investigation.

## General Redirect Principles (apply to any Act 1 failure)

- **Cost, don't block.** Failure spends time, trust, coin, safety, or a prize — never the campaign or all forward motion.
- **Convert failure into a new scene.** A dead patron → a rival or an heir; a burned faction → its enemies; a jail → a debt; a lost clue → an alternate path.
- **Advance a clock, visibly.** Let the player *see* the world move (the vote slips, the cult swells, the Ledger tightens) so consequences feel real (`WORLD_CLOCKS.md`, `CONSEQUENCES.md`).
- **Protect the solo floor.** Always leave at least one ally reachable, one lead live, and one way to earn the next level (`ACT_1_MILESTONES.md`).
- **Hold the line on secrecy.** Failures may *raise the stakes* but must never blurt R2+ truth (Remembrance-as-substance, Reke's master, the steering employer, the Concord's sin, the Court). Cap at R1.
- **Record it.** Update `CONSEQUENCES.md`, `FACTION_STATE.md`, `WORLD_CLOCKS.md`, `NPC_MEMORY.md`, and the relevant quest's State Updates so the failure persists and pays off later.
