## AI Use

**Mixed secrecy — DM-facing arc spine.** Load at campaign start and whenever planning an Act 1 session, choosing what to surface, or checking pacing for levels 1–4. This is an **open-world kit, not a track**: it tells the DM what is prepared and how the early game can branch, so the player can pull any thread (or none of them) and still reach authored content. Player-safe narration is marked; everything under **DM-Only** must be earned through play. Act 1's revelation cap is **R1 only** (the failures have a source and a pattern) per `../11_mysteries_and_secrets/REVELATION_MAP.md` — never surface Remembrance-as-substance (M3), the Concord's sin (M6), the deliberate Quietfall (M9), or the Hollow Court (M7) in Act 1.

## One-Paragraph Shape (DM)

A newcomer arrives in Hollowmere as a death-rite fails publicly (Wren). Levels 1–4 are local, eerie, and personal: the player resolves Wren's situation one way or another, picks up early jobs from the town and the factions' local faces, reaches a first Concord site, and slowly learns the failures are **not random — they have a source, and the source is the basin.** No faction's true agenda is exposed yet; Reke reads as a reasonable politician; the shrine reads as a hazard, not a conspiracy. The act ends when the harvest's *reality* (a source, a pattern, a thing under the water) becomes undeniable and the player faces a first faction-level decision about the basin scheme.

## Multiple Routes Into the Main Mystery (M0)

The central question — *why won't the dead rest, and who is doing it?* — is approached in Act 1 only at the **R1 layer** (a source and a pattern). At least three independent Act 1 routes deliver that frame, so no single faction, NPC, or roll gates it:

1. **The personal route (M1):** Wren herself. Speaking with her, a Warden examining her, Wend's songs, or her grave's waterward drift all show *something is holding the dead here* — not random decay.
2. **The wilderness route (M5):** Old Sashe's drift-map; the Drift-Line in the Greyfens; grave-drift at Greywater/Kettle Bridge. The dead **move toward the basin.**
3. **The political route (M4 shape):** The basin scheme keeps pointing everyone at the water; Coalmont's unease and the fabricated water-rights filings show *someone wants the basin opened.* (Reke's true allegiance stays hidden — Act 2.)
4. **The exploration route (M2 shape):** A first Concord site (Peat Chapel; the basin shore; a cairn) shows the Concord built *something* here, drowned and leaking, with the basin at its heart.

The player needs only ~2 of these to leave Act 1 understanding "the failures have a source, and it is the basin." See `../11_mysteries_and_secrets/ACT_1_CLUE_TRAILS.md`.

## The Act 1 Threat (DM-Only)

Act 1 has **no named boss.** Its antagonist is twofold and quiet:
- **The leakage itself** — the reawakening harvest as faceless dread: failing rites, drifting dead, the cold off the basin. Embodied in recurring threats (`../13_encounters_and_bestiary/ACT_1_THREATS.md`).
- **The reopening push** — Councilor Reke steering the Compact to drain the basin, presented entirely as reasonable progress. In Act 1 he is a *charming politician with a project*, not an exposed traitor. The player may grow suspicious (Hooks 4/5) but the reveal of his allegiance is **Act 2 (R3)**.

Cole Ashby (Gravecaller radical) is a secondary Act 1 pressure: a reckless rival inching toward the basin who can become a complication if the player ignores the fens.

## What Changes If the Player Ignores Each Major Thread

The world is consequential whether or not the player engages. (Clocks: `../02_runtime_state/WORLD_CLOCKS.md`. Detailed redirects: `ACT_1_FAILURE_STATES.md`.)

| Ignored Thread | Clock | What Happens (Act 1 timescale) |
|---|---|---|
| **Wren** (Hook 1) | #9 Wren's Fate | Town fear curdles; a mob forms OR Tomas turns to the Gravecallers OR the Wardens are forced to act. Hollowmere cools from warm base toward frightened. New hooks open (7, the mob). |
| **The basin scheme** (Hooks 4/5) | #2 Basin Drains | The vote goes Reke's way; drainage is approved, then begins; ruins start to surface. Raises the master clock (#1). |
| **The failing rites** (Hook 9) | #3 Wardens Break | A rite goes unanswered; someone is harmed; the Wardens strain and start refusing the worst sites. |
| **The Mourners** (Hook 2) | #4 Circle Turns Militant | Grief turns to anger; relic-caravans get blocked; the Circle drifts toward the Gravecallers. |
| **The Gravecallers** (Hook 7) | #5 The Breaking | Cells swell; radicals gather tools; Cole Ashby moves toward the basin. |
| **The Ledger** (Hooks 3/10) | #6 Monopoly Closes | The Ledger out-buys all salvage; a squeezed family is ruined; debt-grip tightens. |
| **Sefra / the steering** | #8 Steering Unravels | Sefra keeps nudging the player toward the shrine; her unease grows; the patron eventually moves to "tidy up" (Act 2 shock). |

> No ignored thread *ends* the game. Each evolves into a new situation and new hooks. The DM should let consequences land visibly so the player feels the world moving.

## Recommended Default Opening

Use **Opener A: "The Failed Funeral"** (`../16_ai_session_packs/OPENING_SCENES.md`) — drops the player into Wren's failing rite, introduces Sefra, Wend, Tomas, and the tone, with social/ritual/investigation choices and zero forced combat. Openers B (the fixer's job) and C (lost on the Concord road) are equally prepared alternatives. After the opener, follow the player's chosen door above.

## Early Foreshadowing of Levels 5–8 (Place, Don't Explain)

Seed these so Act 2 lands hard. **Foreshadow only — never resolve in Act 1:**

- **Relics "remember" people.** In Act 1 a salvage relic (Peat Chapel; Ledger vault rumor) is *uncanny* — it shows a dead face, feels cold-wrong. Do NOT yet state it *stores a person* (that's R2/M3, Act 2).
- **Someone is buying the dead's leavings.** The Ledger's salvage-buying and a squeezed family hint at a market in grief — the shape of the Monopoly without the word "Remembrance."
- **Reke is too smooth.** Plant small inconsistencies (the water-rights story that doesn't add up, his lone night-walks to the shore) the player can bank for Act 2's R3.
- **The Gravecallers know something.** Knell's and the Tallow Man's hints in Act 1 are fragments and feelings, not the harvest's history.
- **The scholar's letters.** Briss's Concord Script is presented as "lost gentleness" — the player can begin *learning the gate* that unlocks Act 3's archives.
- **The basin is the center.** Every route bends toward the water. By Act 1's end the player should *feel* the basin is the heart of it, without knowing why.

## Level-Up Shape (Summary; Full Triggers in ACT_1_MILESTONES.md)

Milestone leveling (`../03_canon/LEVELING_ASSUMPTIONS.md`), earnable by **multiple approaches** — combat is never required.

| To Level | Earned When (any qualifying path) |
|---|---|
| 1 → 2 | Wren's situation is resolved one way or another (released, sheltered, fought, hidden, or the crisis otherwise concludes) — OR an equivalent first personal/community problem is closed. |
| 2 → 3 | The player completes a first real quest **and** commits to pulling a faction or basin thread (picks a direction). |
| 3 → 4 | The player reaches and survives a **first Concord site** (Peat Chapel, basin shore ruin, or a cairn) and grasps that the failures have a *source*. |
| 4 → 5 (unlocks Act 2) | The player faces a **first faction-level decision about the basin scheme** and the harvest's *reality* becomes undeniable — see Act 2 triggers below. |

---

## What Unlocks Act 2 (Multiple Possible Triggers)

Act 2 (levels 5–8, "Conspiracy") begins when **any** of these crystallizes — the DM uses whichever the player's path produces:

1. The player **takes a side on the basin scheme** (helps Reke, opposes him with Coalmont, or moves to sabotage/sway the vote) — the political door to R3.
2. The player **studies a relic or contacts the Gravecallers** in earnest, ready to learn what Remembrance *is* (R2/M3 — held until Act 2).
3. The player **reaches the basin shore / a surfacing ruin** and confronts that the thing under the water is enormous, intact, and built (M2 shape hardens).
4. **Clocks force it:** if the player is passive, the Basin clock (#2) and Wardens clock (#3) advance until the surfacing shrine and active factions make the conspiracy undeniable on their own.

> Act 2's content lives in Stage 15 (`ACT_2_LEVELS_5_8.md`). Act 1 only needs to deliver the player *to the threshold* of the conspiracy, with R1 understood and a basin-stance chosen or forced.

## Failure Redirects (Summary; Full in ACT_1_FAILURE_STATES.md)

Every major Act 1 failure **changes the world rather than ending the game**: a dead/burned patron reroutes to another door; an imprisoned player gets a jail-arc out; a failed dungeon retreat costs time and a clue, not the campaign; antagonizing a faction makes an enemy but opens its rivals. ## Solo-Play Spine (Always On)

- **Telegraph everything.** No ambush kills a level-1 PC before a meaningful choice.
- **Allies on tap:** Hale (rites/companion), Old Sashe (fens), the town watch (Hollowmere), Halla (base/rumors), Wend (folk lore).
- **Noncombat is the default resolution** for the dead (true rite / parley) and for politics (investigation / persuasion).
- **Retreat always exists** from every site (`../13_encounters_and_bestiary/ACT_1_THREATS.md`, zone tables).
- **The starting safety net** (`../16_ai_session_packs/SOLO_SAFETY_START.md`) backstops the first sessions.
