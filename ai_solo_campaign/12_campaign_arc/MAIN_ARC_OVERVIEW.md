## Purpose

**DM-ONLY.** The full shape of the main story arc, levels 1–20: the hidden truth, escalation structure, major revelations, and how player choices alter the outcome. Do not quote to the player.

## AI Use

Load at the start of a new act, when a major revelation approaches, or when calibrating what the player has discovered.

---

## Campaign Premise (DM-Facing)

A newcomer arrives in the failing frontier town of Hollowmere just as the world's oldest fear comes true on its doorstep: the dead will not rest. Pulled into investigating a botched funeral, the player gradually uncovers that the lingering of the dead is not decay or divine wrath but the **reawakening of a hidden machine** — the Custodian Concord secretly harvested the dead for centuries, destroyed itself a century ago to let the drained afterlife refill, and is now, through its surviving members (the Hollow Court), switching the harvest back on. The campaign is the player's journey from "why won't this corpse rest?" to "who gets to decide the fate of every soul that has ever died?"

## The Hidden Truth

(Full detail in `../03_canon/DM_ONLY_CANON.md`.) In one line: **the Concord harvested the dead, the Quietfall was a deliberate pause, and the surviving Custodians — the Hollow Court — are restarting the harvest beneath Hollowmere now.**

## The Central Conflict

Multiple powers want to control the **Remembrance** (the harvestable substance of the dead), each certain they alone are right:
- **Hollow Court** — restart the harvest "responsibly" and rule as immortal stewards.
- **Concord Remnant (inner circle)** — seize the harvest to rebuild the Concord's empire.
- **Cinder Ledger** — monopolize and sell it (immortality as a commodity).
- **Gravecallers** — destroy it entirely to free the dead (and risk catastrophe).
- **Mourners' Circle / Ashen Wardens** — keep it sealed; let the dead rest.

The player becomes the swing vote. The endgame is a **choice about the Remembrance**, not just a boss fight.

## Arc Shape

### Act 1 — Levels 1–4: The Reach and the Failing Rite
Local stakes. A funeral fails; a body (Wren) comes back wrong. The player investigates Hollowmere, meets the factions' local faces, reaches a first Concord site, and learns the failures are *spreading and have a source*. Foreshadow the harvest; reveal nothing of the Hollow Court yet. **Act 1 threat:** the reawakening leakage and whoever is quietly pushing to reopen the drowned shrine (Councilor Reke, unrevealed). Detailed in `ACT_1_LEVELS_1_4.md` (Stage 5).

### Act 2 — Levels 5–8: Factions and the Spreading Harvest
The wider Reach opens. Factions actively recruit, oppose, and deceive the player. First real revelation: **Remembrance is a substance, and someone is collecting it.** The player picks early sides. The drowned shrine becomes the obvious center of everything. First Hollow-Court *agent* exposed (not the Court itself). Detailed in `ACT_2_LEVELS_5_8.md` (Stage 15).

### Act 3 — Levels 9–12: The Truth of the Concord
Major revelations fall: **the Concord harvested the dead; the Quietfall was deliberate.** The player travels beyond the Reach (Caradril, archives, other regions) to confirm it. Faction war heats up over the reopening shrine. The player's Act 1–2 choices compound into allies and enemies. Detailed in `ACT_3_LEVELS_9_12.md` (Stage 15).

### Act 4 — Levels 13–16: The Hollow Court
The apex truth: **the Custodians survived as Remembrance and are restarting the harvest.** The player makes contact with the Hollow Court (Veyl). The Court makes its case — it is *persuasive*, not cartoonish. Last major choice points crystallize. Detailed in `ACT_4_LEVELS_13_16.md` (Stage 15).

### Act 5 — Levels 17–20: The Fate of the Remembrance
Resolution. The player enacts a decision about the Remembrance at the drowned shrine. Endgame states (below). Epilogues reflect accumulated choices. Detailed in `ACT_5_LEVELS_17_20.md` (Stage 15).

## Major Revelation Points

| Revelation | Approx. Level | How Discovered | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| The dead are leaking from a *source*, not randomly. | 2–4 | First Concord site; Wren; Gravecaller hint. | Reframes the crisis as a mystery with an answer. |
| Remembrance is a harvestable substance; someone collects it. | 5–7 | Relic study; Gravecaller teaching; Ledger salvage. | Turns horror into conspiracy. |
| The Concord harvested the dead. | 9–11 | Concord archives; Warden Othetha; Remnant records. | Destroys the "noble fallen order" myth. |
| The Quietfall was deliberate. | 9–12 | Same sources; Hollow Court hints. | Reframes the world's founding trauma. |
| The Hollow Court survives and is restarting the harvest. | 13–15 | Under-shrine; converging clues; Veyl. | Names the apex power. |
| The player was steered here. | 6–12 (flexible) | Sefra Quick's secret; tracing the patron. | Personal stakes; trust crisis. |

## Player Choice Branches (Endgame States)

The Act 5 decision about the Remembrance. The player can pursue (or be pushed toward) one of:

1. **Seal it** (Wardens/Mourners path) — re-seal the shrine, let the dead rest, accept that resurrection mostly ends and civilization loses a crutch. Quiet, costly, humane.
2. **Restart it controlled** (Hollow Court path) — let the Court resume a "gentler" harvest; stability and immortality for some, the dead consumed forever. Powerful, monstrous, tempting.
3. **Seize it** (Remnant or Ledger path) — hand the machine to a single living faction; empire or monopoly; new tyranny.
4. **Destroy it** (Gravecaller path) — shatter the harvest and free the dead; resurrection ends entirely and the living/dead boundary destabilizes; chaotic liberation.
5. **Transform it** (the synthesis / hardest path) — the player invents/enables a *consensual* system where the dead choose; requires uniting unlikely allies and is only reachable through deep play. The "best" ending, not guaranteed.

No ending is labeled "correct" in play. Each has real costs. Detailed branching in `ENDGAME_STATES.md` (Stage 15).

## Villain / Antagonist Arc

The antagonist *escalates by revelation*, not by power level alone:
- **Acts 1–2:** faceless dread (the leakage) + a hidden human pusher (Reke).
- **Acts 2–3:** rival factions become active antagonists based on player choices.
- **Acts 3–4:** the Concord's sin and the Hollow Court emerge as the true apex.
- **Act 4–5:** **Custodian Veyl** of the Hollow Court — the campaign's final voice. Not a monster: a grieving, certain, century-old idealist who believes consuming the dead is the only mercy left. The final confrontation can be combat, debate, sabotage, or persuasion.

## Solo-Play Note

Every act offers combat, social, stealth, and investigation paths. The arc never requires winning a fight to advance — revelations can be reached multiple ways (see `../11_mysteries_and_secrets/MYSTERY_WEB.md`). Telegraph apex threats; the Hollow Court is beatable by cleverness, alliance, or choice, not just damage.
