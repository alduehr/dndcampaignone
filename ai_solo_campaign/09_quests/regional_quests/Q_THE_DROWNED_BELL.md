## AI Use
Develops PC-H4. A low-level rite-and-investigation quest centered on Wrackmouth's Tide-Bell Mourners, whose salt-and-tide rites can no longer keep pace with the rising drowned-tide. The player helps the rites hold, learns *why* the tide is worsening (oblique M5), and chooses whether to honor the dead or let the Gravecallers fill the gap. A gentle on-ramp to the Coast's maritime-harvest thread that never touches the apex.

## Quest Type
Regional / Mystery (rite-support)

## Level Range
2–4. Solo danger: low-moderate (the drowned dead; the tide). Telegraphed and largely avoidable.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Assist the salt-and-tide rite (settle a restless drowned one): Religion DC 13 (DC 15 if the dead is older/worse).
- Read why the tide is worsening (the drowned "come home"): Survival/Insight DC 13.
- Persuade the overstretched Mourners to accept help / coordinate: Persuasion DC 12.
- Spot the Gravecaller stepping in where the rites fail: Insight DC 13.
- Time work around the tide (the drowned rise with it): Survival DC 13.

## Starting Hook
The Tide-Bell Mourners of Wrackmouth — who ring the drowned-bell when a body comes in — are exhausted and falling behind: more drowned are coming home than they can settle, and some are coming back *wrong*. A Mourner, Headman Strand, or Salt-Mother Bryd (sending word from Cobble Strand) asks the player to help the rites keep pace before a stretch of coast turns truly haunted.

## Location
Wrackmouth's Tide-Bell shrine and harbor, the drowned-cairns along the cliff-road, and Cobble Strand (`WRACKMOUTH.md`, `PALE_COAST_SITES.md`, `COBBLE_STRAND.md`).

## Involved NPCs
The Tide-Bell Mourners, Salt-Mother Bryd (the rite's master; Cobble Strand), Headman Corl Strand, Net-wife Senna (watching for her drowned son), a Gravecaller stepping into the gap, Skipper Wenna Roke (ferry between sites).

## Involved Factions
Mourners' Circle (the failing rites; the player's likely allies), Reachward Compact (the thin harbor-watch, anxious about panic), Gravecallers (the answering drowned; filling the rite-gap to recruit).

## Surface Problem
Help the Mourners settle the rising tide of drowned dead before the coast turns haunted.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The drowned-tide is worsening because the coastal Concord nodes (the Drowned Lamp chief among them) are warming as the keystone reawakens, *pulling the drowned "home"* faster than any rite can settle them (oblique, maritime M5). The Gravecallers thrive precisely where the salt-rites fail. **Cap at R1: the player can learn the tide is worsening and the drowned "come home" toward the old lights — never the harvest mechanism, the node's purpose, or the Court.**

## Objectives
1. Help the rites settle the backlog of drowned. 2. Learn why the tide is worsening (oblique M5). 3. Decide who fills the gap — the Mourners (honor) or the Gravecallers (the easy, dangerous answer).

## Possible Approaches
- **Rite-support:** learn enough of the salt-and-tide rite (from Bryd) to settle drowned ones yourself, easing the backlog.
- **Investigation:** trace the worsening to the warming Lamp/sea-shrines (sets up "What the Lamp Holds," PC-H3).
- **Organizing:** rally fishers and the moot to give the Mourners hands and salt, so the rites hold.
- **Faction choice:** expose and check the Gravecaller stepping in — or quietly let them, with consequences.

## Obstacles
The sheer number of drowned; the tide's timing; the Mourners' exhaustion and pride; a Gravecaller offering an easier (worse) comfort to the grieving; the rising salt-price making rite-salt dear (ties to "The Salt-Price").

## Important Scenes
- The drowned-bell ringing again before the last rite is even finished.
- A drowned one who comes home "wrong," and Bryd's rite to settle it (or fail to).
- A grieving widow (Senna) choosing between the slow Mourner rite and the Gravecaller's quick, false comfort.

## Clues
- **C-PC-M5-1** (reuse) — the drowned "come home" toward the old lights with the worsening tide (Survival/Insight DC 13). Oblique, maritime M5.
- The salt-price spike starving the rites (ties to "The Salt-Price," PC-H1).
- The Gravecaller current strongest where the rites fail (Insight; the cult-door).

## Combat Options
Restless/wrong drowned (specter/ghost/"drowned-one"-like, AC 12–13, ~22–36 HP); near-shore ones are layable by the rite (Religion DC 13) rather than fought; a confronted Gravecaller is Cultist/Acolyte-like (AC 12, ~16 HP), social-first. Telegraphed; the tide is as dangerous as any dead. See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/PALE_COAST_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
The whole quest can be carried by rite-work, salt-lore, organizing, and persuasion; the dead are settled, not slain.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Bryd teaches and backs the rite (sanctuary and aid); the dead are layable, not lethal, near shore; the tide is telegraphed by the tide-tables and the bell; retreat to town is always open; the Lamp/Skerries stay gated.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Wrackmouth and Cobble Strand. Soft pressure: each tide brings more drowned; let the backlog grow and a stretch of coast turns haunted (and the Gravecaller current strengthens) — the Coast clock.

## Rewards
- The rites holding; a stretch of coast at peace; the Mourners' deep gratitude and Bryd's trust (a key Coast ally and the salt-and-tide rite taught).
- The oblique M5 read and a lead toward the Lamp (PC-H3/PC-H9).
- ~40–70 gp (moot/Mourner thanks) or rite-salt, blessed salt, and a standing welcome on the Coast.

## Consequences
## If The Player Succeeds
The rites keep pace; the coast quiets; Bryd and the Mourners trust the player; the Gravecaller current is checked locally.
## If The Player Partially Succeeds
The worst of the backlog is settled but the tide keeps rising; the player understands the cause and has the lead to the Lamp.
## If The Player Ignores It
The rites fail, a stretch of coast turns haunted, the Gravecallers claim the grieving, and the Coast clock ("The Drowned Tide Rises") advances.
## If The Player Uses Excessive Force
Fighting the drowned instead of settling them (or strong-arming the Gravecaller's flock) hardens the grieving against the Mourners and feeds the cult's grievance.

## Failure State
If the rites can't be made to hold, the haunting spreads and Bryd grieves — but the cause (the warming lights) and the path forward (the Lamp) are now known, and the thread reopens. No dead end.

## Scaling Notes
- L2: a backlog of gentle drowned; rite-and-organize weighted; the cult passive.
- L3: a "wrong" drowned or two; the cult actively recruiting in the gap.
- L4: an older/worse drowned (Wraith-like, AC 13, ~36 HP) needs the full rite or careful handling; the cause-thread (the Lamp) presses.
