## AI Use
The Harrowgast / Sunder Heights mid-tier quest. The Concord Remnant is racing to reach a deep harvest node (the Deep Adit); the player can beat them, sabotage them, ally with them, or sell "old glass" to the highest bidder. The clearest Act 1–2 view of the Reclamation clock and a direct M3/M6 payoff.

## Quest Type
Regional / Dungeon / Faction

## Level Range
3–5. Solo danger: moderate-high (cold, falls, tougher dead, a faction rival, the thinning aura). Telegraphed throughout.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Learn what the deep crews fear (Hennet): Insight DC 13.
- Negotiate dig-access or out-bid the Remnant: Persuasion DC 15–18.
- Survive the Heights / Deep Adit hazards: Survival/Athletics DC 13–15; thinning Con save DC 14.
- Read the Relay-Vault Script (gated): Investigation DC 14.
- Quiet the node non-violently ("closing word"): Concord Script DC 15, or true rite Religion DC 15.

## Starting Hook
In Harrowgast, dig-boss Hennet's crews won't dig deeper, "old glass" fetches double from Caradril scholars, and men who go too deep come back "thin." Someone is racing to reach whatever's down there. The Ledger and the Remnant both want a capable hand.

## Location
Harrowgast → the Sunder Heights sites → the Deep Adit (see dungeon file).

## Involved NPCs
Dig-boss Hennet (petitioner), Veska Dunn (Remnant rival), Nessa Coyle (Ledger patron), Overseer Brack (town boss), "Thin" Coll (the warning), a Warden if the player brings one.

## Involved Factions
Concord Remnant (racing for the node — the Reclamation clock embodied), Cinder Ledger (wants the relic supply), Ashen Wardens (would quiet the node if they knew).

## Surface Problem
Find out what's in the deep tunnels and who should control the "old glass."

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The Deep Adit is a secondary harvest node (M6/M7 foreshadow); "old glass" is raw Remembrance relics (M3 direct); the Remnant is racing to claim it for Quorrin's plan to seize the harvest. The "thin" miners are early thinning evidence (M10 background).

## Objectives
1. Investigate the deep (why the crews fear it).
2. Reach the Deep Adit (beat, join, or trail the Remnant).
3. Decide the node's and the relics' fate (quiet it, claim it, sell it, deny it).

## Possible Approaches
- **Exploration/combat:** delve the Adit; face its keepers (or quiet them non-violently).
- **Faction:** ally with Coyle (Ledger) or Dunn (Remnant) — each with a moral price.
- **Sabotage:** deny the Remnant the node (collapse a drift, expose their aim).
- **Mercy:** quiet the node with the "closing word" or a true rite (slows the harvest).

## Obstacles
The Heights' cold and falls; the Remnant crew at the Fear-Line; the thinning aura; the Relay-Vault keepers; the moral weight of the relics.

## Important Scenes
- "Thin" Coll repeating his half-sentence in the Cold Drum.
- A three-way standoff at the Fear-Line (player, Remnant crew, the fearful miners).
- The Relay-Vault: the harvest stated in the Concord's own carved words.

## Clues
- **C-M3-1/C-M3-3** ("old glass" stores the dead — direct).
- **C-M6/M9 fragment** (the Relay-Vault Script).
- **M10 background** (the "thin" phenomenon).

## Combat Options
Remnant crew (Veska Dunn + 2 bravos; AC 13–14, ~16–30 HP) — **social/stealth first**. Relay-Vault keepers (1–2 wraith-like, AC 13, ~36 HP) — **two non-combat outs** (closing word / true rite). Highland Remembrances and predators en route (telegraphed).

## Noncombat Options
Negotiate access, out-bid, ally, sabotage, or quiet the node by ritual/Script — the dungeon's climax has explicit non-combat resolutions.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Harrowgast resupply (telegraphed: "buy gear, this is dangerous"); Hennet/Coll as warnings; open retreat throughout; the keepers are bound to the vault (can't pursue); thinning is reversible.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe (rough) rest at Harrowgast; no rest in the aura-zones; no magical healing north — supply matters. Time pressure: the Remnant is *actively racing* — delay hands them the node (advances the Reclamation clock toward Act 3).

## Rewards
- "Old glass" Remembrance relics (M3; valuable, hot, thin-touch risk).
- A tier-2-appropriate magic item from the Adit (see `REWARDS_BY_LEVEL.md` when built).
- The Relay-Vault rubbing (M6/M9 clue, portable).
- Slowed Reclamation clock if the node is quieted or the Remnant is denied; Ledger or Remnant favor if allied.

## Consequences
- **Quiet the node:** Heights leakage and the thinning ease; the Reclamation clock slows; a major hidden win.
- **Claim it for a faction:** that faction surges (Ledger monopoly or Remnant reclamation) — power with a price.
- **Ignore:** the Remnant takes it; Quorrin gains relic-supply and proof; the thinning spreads.
- **Excessive looting:** the player risks lasting thin-touch and arms factions with relics.

## Failure State
If the player is beaten to the Adit, the Remnant holds it — but the player can still infiltrate, sabotage, or expose them later (Act 2–3), and M3/M6 remain reachable. A setback, not a wall.

## Follow-Up Hooks
The Vyre–Quorrin monopoly deal; the Caradril archives (Act 3, full M6/M9); the thinning as a campaign-wide dread; a recurring Veska Dunn rivalry.

## Scaling Notes
- L3: one keeper, Veska alone, softer thinning (DC 12).
- L5–6: two keepers + summons, full Remnant crew contesting the vault, Reach-wide-feeling thinning aura.
