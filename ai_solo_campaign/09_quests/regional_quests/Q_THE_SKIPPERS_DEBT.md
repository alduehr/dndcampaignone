## AI Use
Develops PC-H19. A relationship-and-leverage quest: Skipper Wenna Roke — the player's honest lift to Caradril and guide to the coves — is being squeezed by Factor Cole's Ledger debt and pressured to run for the wreckers. Helping her out from under it secures a recurring ally, the water-route to Caradril, and a documentary lead on Cole's sea-relic trade (M3). A character-driven quest that turns the Coast's solo-safety skipper from a service into a friend.

## Quest Type
Regional / Personal (ally) / Faction (against the Ledger)

## Level Range
2–4. Solo danger: low-moderate (Ledger toughs; wreckers; the sea). Mostly social and investigative.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Understand the debt-trap (how Cole's terms are rigged): Investigation DC 13.
- Negotiate Roke's debt down / call Cole's bluff: Persuasion DC 14 (DC 16 against Cole himself).
- Find proof Cole's terms are predatory or his cargo is contraband: Investigation DC 14 (Stealth DC 13 if it means the weigh-house).
- Resist or out-talk the wreckers pressuring Roke to run for them: Intimidation/Persuasion DC 13.
- Sail a hard passage to clear the debt by honest work: Survival/seamanship DC 14.

## Starting Hook
Wenna Roke — blunt, tide-wise, already half-trusted if the player has sailed with her (PC-H2) — finally admits she's drowning in Cole's debt: he set the salt-price and the harbor-fees so an honest skipper can't make the note, and his men keep hinting she could "run a quiet cargo" to clear it. She doesn't want to become the wreckers' or Cole's creature. She'll owe the player her loyalty if they help her find another way.

## Location
Wrackmouth harbor, Cole's weigh-house, the coves, and the coastal route (`WRACKMOUTH.md`, `PALE_COAST_SITES.md`, `TRAVEL_ROUTES_RING1.md`).

## Involved NPCs
Skipper Wenna Roke (the ally to save), Factor Maris Cole (the creditor; M3), Harbormaster Oll (bribable; looks away from Cole's hulls), Fence "Cuttle" Dree (the wreckers' contact pressuring her), Harbor-Reeve Ond Falk (the moot, if the player goes lawful), Innkeep Sella Vane (knows which skippers run for the wreckers).

## Involved Factions
Cinder Ledger (Cole's debt-trap and sea-relic trade), Reachward Compact / harbor-moot (lawful recourse, such as it is).

## Surface Problem
Help an honest skipper clear or escape a Ledger debt before it forces her into the wreckers' service.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
Cole's debt-trap isn't personal — it's how he turns honest skippers into deniable carriers for his sea-relic trade (M3), the same trade that feeds Vyre's Monopoly clock. The "quiet cargo" Dree dangles is Remembrance "sea-relics." Saving Roke honestly denies Cole a carrier and can yield proof of the trade. **Cap at R1/R2: the player can expose the sea-relic smuggling and the debt-trap; never the harvest, the node's purpose, or the Court.**

## Objectives
1. Understand the debt-trap. 2. Get Roke out from under it (pay it, negotiate it, beat it with honest work, or expose Cole). 3. Decide how hard to move on Cole — and gain Roke as a loyal ally and the water-route.

## Possible Approaches
- **Pay/negotiate:** clear or renegotiate the note (coin, a hard honest passage, or leverage on Cole).
- **Investigate:** prove the terms are predatory or the "quiet cargo" is contraband; take it to the moot (ties to "The Salt-Price" / "The Wreckers").
- **Honest work:** sail a dangerous, well-paid passage *with* Roke to clear the debt straight.
- **Confront:** out-bluff Cole or scare off Dree's pressure — risky, but it frees her.

## Obstacles
Cole's rigged terms and reach; Dree and the wreckers' pressure; the bribable harbormaster; the sea on a hard passage; the temptation (for Roke) of the easy, dirty money.

## Important Scenes
- Roke laying out the rigged note at a harbor table, ashamed and angry.
- Dree's smiling pressure — "one quiet run and you're clear, skipper."
- The choice: clean money the hard way, or Cole's clean-looking trap.

## Clues
- **C-PC-M3-1** (reuse, if the player digs) — the "quiet cargo" is a sea-relic that stores a drowned person (Arcana/Religion DC 13, or experiential). M3.
- Cole's salvage-ledger / rigged-terms pattern (Investigation; ties to "The Salt-Price," PC-H5).

## Combat Options
Cole's salt-toughs / Dree's wreckers if the player forces it (Thug/Bandit-like, AC 12–13, ~22–32 HP; morale-driven; turnable); telegraphed and avoidable. The quest is built for social/investigative resolution. See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/PALE_COAST_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Negotiation, investigation, lawful process, and honest seamanship all resolve it; no fight is required.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Roke herself is the safety valve she becomes — saving her secures a reliable lift and guide; the moot and Sella Vane back a careful player; Cole prefers deniability to open violence; retreat is always open.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Wrackmouth. Pressure: Cole's note comes due on a date; if the player waits past it, Roke either loses her boat or takes Dree's dirty run (and becomes harder to save / lost as an ally).

## Rewards
- Roke freed and loyal: a recurring honest ally, the **water-route to Caradril**, and a guide to the coves/Skerries (a major solo-safety gain).
- Proof of Cole's trade (M3 leverage; feeds "The Salt-Price" / the Monopoly clock).
- ~50–90 gp from the cleared honest passage, or Roke's lifelong free passage along the coast.

## Consequences
## If The Player Succeeds
Roke keeps her boat and her honesty; Cole loses a carrier; the player gains a key ally and the water-route; Cole's trade is a notch more exposed.
## If The Player Partially Succeeds
Roke is freed but indebted to the player instead, or Cole's trade is glimpsed but not proven — still an ally, with a thread left open.
## If The Player Ignores It
Roke takes Dree's dirty run and becomes the wreckers'/Cole's creature (lost as a clean ally; the water-route now comes with strings), and Cole's trade grows.
## If The Player Solves It Nonviolently
Best outcome — a clean win that wins Roke's whole-hearted loyalty and quietly damages Cole.

## Failure State
If the player can't free Roke, she's compromised but alive; the water-route still exists but on the wreckers' terms, and the player can still move against Cole later (the M3 thread reopens). No dead end.

## Scaling Notes
- L2: a social/negotiation quest; Dree merely tempts.
- L3: Dree's pressure turns to threats; a hard honest passage is on offer.
- L4: Cole's toughs lean on Roke openly; the M3 proof is within reach and worth grabbing.
