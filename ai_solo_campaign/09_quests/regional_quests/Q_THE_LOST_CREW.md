## AI Use
Develops TW-H19. A search-and-survival quest in the mid-wood: a Ledger logging crew pushed too deep toward the Old Mast and did not come back. The player must follow them in, learn what the deep-cutting woke, and decide what to do with the survivors and the truth. A standalone mid-wood quest that makes the regional "Old Mast Stirs" clock concrete and human, points obliquely at M5/M6, and feeds "The Marked Oaks" without requiring it.

## Quest Type
Regional / Search-and-Rescue

## Level Range
3–5. Solo danger: moderate (the mid-wood; the old dead; getting lost). The Old Mast itself stays gated and off-limits.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Track the crew's cut-line into the wood: Survival DC 14 (DC 12 with Brann or a Coppice-Shrine offering).
- Read the abandoned cut-front (what happened): Investigation DC 13.
- Calm or reach a panicked survivor: Insight/Persuasion DC 13.
- Avoid getting lost following the trail back out: Survival DC 14.
- Resist the deep-wood dread near the cut they made too deep: Wis DC 13.
- Recognize the cut tree was a marked/shrine-tree (the taboo broken): Religion/Survival DC 12 (lower if the player learned the forest-rules; TW-H21).

## Starting Hook
Cutter-foreman Vask (drunk and frightened) or Factor Hesk Dorr (annoyed at "deserters" eating his profit) wants someone to go find a logging crew that went east to a fresh cut a week ago and never reported back. Dorr calls it desertion; Vask, quieter, says the wood "took them," and won't say more sober.

## Location
The eastern woodcutter camps and the deep cut-front toward the Old Mast edge (`TOLLWOOD_SITES.md` — the Woodcutter Camps, the Edge of the Old Mast). Coldhearth and Hartfell are the havens.

## Involved NPCs
Cutter-foreman Vask (the giver; saw the front), Factor Hesk Dorr (the Ledger's denial), Brann the Guide (solo-safety), Goodwife Sennet / Old Gethin (the forest-rules and what was broken), the surviving crewmen, the Gravecaller cell (who may have reached the survivors first).

## Involved Factions
Cinder Ledger (the cutting that caused this; Dorr suppresses it), Mourners' Circle / woodfolk (warned them; want the marked tree atoned), Gravecallers (drawn to the freshly-restless dead and to any survivor who "heard" them).

## Surface Problem
Find the missing logging crew and bring back who you can.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The crew cut a marked (shrine-)tree at the deepest front, breaking the forest-bargain. The cut roused old forest dead and a thread of the deep wood's watchfulness; men panicked, scattered, and were lost to the wood (exposure, the dark, the dead's lure) rather than slaughtered. One or two survivors are alive but marked — and the Gravecaller cell may already be whispering to them about "what the grey lords took." **This is the Old Mast Stirs clock made personal and the wood's rule made literal. Gate it: deliver the deep's pull, the broken taboo, and an oblique M5/M6 fragment — never the harvest's full truth, never the Court, never the Old Mast's interior.**

## Objectives
1. Track the crew to the cut-front. 2. Read what happened. 3. Find the survivor(s). 4. Get them (and the truth) back — and decide what to tell Dorr and the woodfolk.

## Possible Approaches
- **Woodcraft:** hire Brann / make the offerings; reach the front and back without becoming lost yourself.
- **Investigation:** read the abandoned cut, the broken marked tree, the scattered trail; reconstruct the panic.
- **Compassion:** find and talk down the marked survivor before the cult or the wood takes them fully.
- **Confrontation (optional):** if the cell has reached a survivor first, parley or oppose them.

## Obstacles
The mid-wood and getting lost; the freshly-roused old dead; the deep-wood dread; a survivor half-lured by the dead or the cult; Dorr's pressure to call it desertion and bury it.

## Important Scenes
- The abandoned cut-front: axes dropped, a great marked tree half-felled, the offering-marks defaced.
- A survivor found hollow-eyed at the offering-edge, listening to something the player can't hear.
- The reckoning with Dorr (bury it as desertion?) and with the woodfolk (atone the broken tree?).

## Clues
- **C-TW-M5-1** (reuse) — the old forest dead "drift wrong" and rouse where the bargain is broken (Survival/Insight DC 13; oblique M5).
- The broken marked tree proves the woodfolk's rule is *real*, not superstition (ties to TW-H1 "The Marked Oaks").
- A survivor's babble of "grey lords" and "the dead that remember" (oblique M6 fragment; gated; deliver as dread, not exposition).

## Combat Options
Roused old forest dead (specter/ghost/will-o'-wisp-like, AC 12–13, ~22–36 HP; harder to lay than Reach dead — true rite Religion DC 15, or retreat) and, if the cell contests a survivor, Gravecaller cultists (Cultist/Acolyte-like, AC 12, ~16–22 HP). All telegraphed; the dead are avoidable; the quest is search-and-rescue, not a clearance. See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/TOLLWOOD_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Tracking, reading the front, atoning the broken tree at a Coppice Shrine (quiets the roused dead by custom — Religion/Wis DC 13), and talking the survivor back all resolve it without a fight.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Brann guides and halves the getting-lost risk; the Coppice-Shrine offerings ease the wood; the survivor can be reached by words; retreat to Coldhearth/Hartfell is always open; the Old Mast interior is hard-gated (the trail ends at the edge, never within). Telegraph the dread and the depth constantly.

## Rest And Time Pressure
No safe rest in the deep mid-wood; Coldhearth and Hartfell are havens; an honored Coppice Shrine allows a guarded rest. Time pressure: each day the survivor drifts further toward the dead/the cult, and the roused dead spread a little nearer the camps (the Old Mast Stirs clock).

## Rewards
- Survivor(s) brought back: the camp-crews' and Vask's gratitude; Dorr's grudging coin (~50–80 gp) if he can't deny it; a woodfolk debt if the tree is atoned.
- The broken-tree proof: hard leverage for "The Marked Oaks" (TW-H1) against Dorr's denial.
- An oblique M5/M6 fragment and a clear, lived read of the deep wood's danger.

## Consequences
## If The Player Succeeds
Survivor(s) home, the marked tree atoned, the roused dead quieted; the woodfolk trust the player; the cutting's danger is now undeniable (helps stop it).
## If The Player Partially Succeeds
The crew's fate is known and the proof recovered, but a survivor is lost to the wood or half-claimed by the cult (recoverable later, with cost).
## If The Player Ignores It
The crew is written off as deserters; the roused dead spread toward the camps; the cult claims any survivor; Dorr cuts on, and the Old Mast Stirs clock advances.
## If The Player Solves It Nonviolently
Best outcome — atonement and persuasion quiet the wood and save the survivor without bloodshed; the woodfolk hold the player in high regard.

## Failure State
If the player can't find or reach the survivor in time, the crew is lost and the wood grows a notch more dangerous — but the broken-tree truth and the clock-warning are clear, and the thread reopens (a worse cut, a bolder cell). No dead end; the player can still pursue "The Marked Oaks."

## Scaling Notes
- L3: a straightforward search; the dead are dread and few; the cult is absent or passive.
- L4: the dead actively contest the front; one survivor is half-lured; the cell is present.
- L5: the cell actively claims a survivor; the roused dead press toward the camps; ties directly into "The Marked Oaks" and the Old Mast clock.
