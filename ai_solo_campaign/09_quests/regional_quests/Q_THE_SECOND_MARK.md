## AI Use
Develops Hook 7 ("A Knock in the Night") into the Greyfens. The gentle door to the Gravecallers via Sister Knell and the Knock-mark — a primary M3/M6 truth-source and a moral fork (the cult is persecuted, partly right, and dangerously extreme).

## Quest Type
Regional / Mystery / Faction (forbidden)

## Level Range
2–4. Solo danger: low-to-moderate (the fen; a possible Compact purge; the cult prefers to talk).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Read the Knock-marks: Investigation DC 12 (or learn from Iola/Knell).
- Earn an invitation to the cell: Persuasion DC 12 (lower if grieving/curious).
- Navigate to the Drowned Blind: Survival DC 14 (Sashe/Den Hask negate).
- Learn the dead's testimony (Tallow Man): forbidden contact (roleplay; Insight DC 13 to grasp).
- Avoid betraying the cell to a purge: social/moral choice.

## Starting Hook
Someone is knocking at grieving doors in Greywater Holm after dark, offering "a last word" — and a second mark has appeared beneath the star on half the village's doors. The Compact wants the Gravecallers purged; the grieving want comfort. Find out who they are before the watch does.

## Location
Greywater Holm (the Knock-marks) → the Greyfens (the Drowned Blind / Mother Tongue Pool).

## Involved NPCs
Sister Knell (the gentle door — major), Elder Mabb (who tolerates them), the Tallow Man (the truth-source — major), Cole Ashby (the radical danger — major), Iola Wend (secret bridge — major), Den Hask (half-recruited guide).

## Involved Factions
Gravecallers (the cell), Mourners' Circle (overlapping radicals), Compact (would purge them).

## Surface Problem
Find out who's contacting the bereaved and what they want.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The Gravecallers genuinely speak with the dead and have learned fragments of the truth (M3/M6): the Concord *ate the dead*. Sister Knell is the humane door; the Tallow Man channels a real Quietfall-era witness (M6/M9, gated). Cole Ashby's radicals want to shatter the shrine (catastrophe). The player can learn, expose, protect, or oppose them.

## Objectives
1. Decode the Knock-marks / find the cell.
2. Make contact (Knell, then the Tallow Man).
3. Decide: learn from them, protect them, expose them, or oppose the radicals.

## Possible Approaches
- **Social/forbidden:** follow Knell's gentle door; hear the dead's testimony.
- **Investigation:** trace the marks and the contacts.
- **Protective:** shield the cell from a Compact purge (earns deep trust).
- **Oppositional:** expose the cell to the Compact (a purge; the cult radicalizes).

## Obstacles
The cult's secrecy; the Compact's hostility; the radicals' danger (Cole Ashby); the moral weight of trafficking with the dead.

## Important Scenes
- A grey-cloaked Knell at a grieving door, gentle and grieving herself.
- The Drowned Blind: the cell's hidden meeting, wary and hopeful.
- The Tallow Man at Mother Tongue Pool, his doubled voice naming "the door under the water."

## Clues
- **C-M3-2 / C-M5-3** (the Gravecallers' teaching).
- **C-M2-4** (the channeled dead name "the door under the water").
- **C-M6-3 / C-M9-3** (act-gated, Act 3) — the Quietfall-witness's full testimony. **Give fragments only in Act 1–2.**

## Combat Options
The cult prefers to talk or flee. If betrayed/attacked: 2–4 grief-mad recruits (Cultist/Commoner-like, AC 11–12, ~10–22 HP), Cole Ashby (T1 lite, AC 13, ~30 HP) if cornered, the Tallow Man (T1, flees into the fens). All telegraphed; none ambush.

## Noncombat Options
The entire quest can be social and investigative; the intended path is *contact and learning*, not combat.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Knell is a non-threatening door; Sashe/Den Hask guide the fen; the cult flees rather than fights; the player can withdraw and choose a different stance anytime.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest at Greywater Holm; no safe rest in the fen. Time pressure: a Compact purge may be imminent (the player can hasten or prevent it); the radicals inch toward the shrine (Gravecaller clock).

## Rewards
- **A primary truth-source** (M3/M6 fragments — among the richest Act 1–2 clues).
- Gravecaller trust (forbidden lore, fen routes, the dead's testimony) if protected.
- A moral compass-point: the player's stance on the dead and the harvest.

## Consequences
- **Learn/protect:** the player gains the cult's truth and trust; the Gravecallers become a (dangerous) resource; Knell may eventually defect to them.
- **Expose:** a purge scatters the cell; survivors radicalize (Gravecaller clock jumps); the player loses a truth-source and gains the cult's enmity.
- **Oppose the radicals:** may prevent a catastrophe but loses the gentle door; Cole Ashby becomes a rival.

## Failure State
If contact fails or the cell scatters, M3/M6 remain reachable (relics, the Deep Adit, the Barrow, the archives), and the Gravecallers resurface elsewhere (the fen is wide). No dead end — but a botched purge accelerates "the Breaking."

## Follow-Up Hooks
The Barrow of Nine Doors (the dead's deeper testimony); the Tallow Man's witness (Act 3, M6/M9); Cole Ashby's "Breaking" (a race to the shrine); Iola Wend's bridge between the faiths.

## Scaling Notes
- L2: gentle, Knell-led, no purge.
- L4: a live Compact purge in progress (a rescue/standoff); Cole Ashby actively dangerous; the Tallow Man offers the gated witness (give a fragment, hold the apex).
