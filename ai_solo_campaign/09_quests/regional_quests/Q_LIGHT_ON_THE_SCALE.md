## AI Use
A Saltmargin investigation into Wharf-Factor Marlow's salvage-skim — an M3/Monopoly-clock thread and a turn-an-NPC opportunity (Decca Rull, Domic Sael).

## Quest Type
Regional / Investigation / Faction

## Level Range
2–3. Solo danger: low (social/stealth; enforcer threat telegraphed).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Get Decca's evidence: Persuasion DC 13.
- Catch the rigged scale / skim: Investigation DC 15.
- Search Marlow's cellar (the hidden relic): Stealth DC 14, Investigation DC 13.
- Get Marlow to confess/back down: Persuasion DC 20, or DC 13 with hard evidence.

## Starting Hook
Decca Rull (or Domic Sael, noticing Hollowmere shortfalls) suspects Marlow is robbing the salt-and-salvage trade. Prove it.

## Location
Saltmargin: the Weigh-House, the Pans, Marlow's cellar, the Tar Wharf.

## Involved NPCs
Iss Marlow (target), Decca Rull (ally/evidence), Domic Sael (Hollowmere link), Gull Heddwyn (smuggler who handles the skimmed goods), Net-wife Sereth (hides Decca's evidence).

## Involved Factions
Cinder Ledger (Marlow vs. Domic internal crack), Mourners' Circle (hate the skim of grave-relics).

## Surface Problem
Expose a corrupt factor cheating the salt trade.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
Marlow skims **Concord salvage** (Remembrance relics) before it reaches Hollowmere — direct evidence of the Ledger's relic-hunger (M3) and Vyre's monopoly scheme. A mislabeled relic hides in his cellar.

## Objectives
1. Gather evidence (rigged scale, skim records, the cellar relic).
2. Decide what to do with it (expose, blackmail, hand to Domic/Coalmont/Mourners).

## Possible Approaches
- **Social:** turn Decca, Sereth, or a guilty clerk.
- **Stealth:** the cellar and the records at night.
- **Mercantile:** trace the skimmed goods through Gull.
- **Legal:** bring proof to Domic (Ledger internal) or the Compact.

## Obstacles
Marlow's rigged systems; Tallytooth-style toughs (2–3 **Thug-like**, AC 11, ~32 HP) if caught; Gull's price for information.

## Important Scenes
- The beautiful crooked scale, weighed twice.
- The cellar: a Remembrance relic Marlow's too scared to ship (M3 in hand).
- The choice: ruin Marlow, blackmail him, or expose the whole Ledger thread.

## Clues
- **C-M3-3-adjacent** (the cellar relic stores a dead person).
- The skim ties Saltmargin → the Ledger monopoly (Secret 12) and the Vyre–Quorrin deal.

## Combat Options
Marlow's toughs if the player is caught burgling — telegraphed catch-and-eject, not lethal. Avoidable entirely.

## Noncombat Options
The entire quest is solvable by investigation, stealth, and social leverage. No fight needed.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Multiple evidence paths; the player can retreat and try social vs. stealth; getting caught means consequence (heat), not death.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest at the Knotted Hand. Light time pressure: Marlow may ship the cellar relic to Caradril (advancing the Monopoly/Reclamation clocks) if the player dawdles.

## Rewards
- The cellar Remembrance relic (M3 clue + valuable/hot).
- Leverage over Marlow (a turned factor, a bribe, or his downfall).
- Decca's alliance; Domic's gratitude (Ledger inside-man path); Mourners' goodwill.

## Consequences
- **Expose:** Marlow falls; the salt trade fairens (relief Reach-wide); Vyre is quietly furious.
- **Blackmail:** the player gains a Ledger asset but is complicit.
- **Ignore:** the skim continues; relics flow to Caradril; the Monopoly clock advances.

## Failure State
If evidence is lost or Marlow buys the player off, the relic still surfaces later through the Ledger thread (M3 reachable elsewhere). Decca may strike anyway, choking the Reach's salt — a new regional crisis.

## Follow-Up Hooks
Turning Domic Sael (the Ledger Vault); the Vyre–Quorrin deal (Act 2); Decca's strike; Gull's "Grave-Goods."

## Scaling Notes
- L2: lenient DCs, no toughs.
- L4: Marlow has real protection (a Spy-like agent and 3 toughs); the relic is guarded; a Remnant buyer competes.
