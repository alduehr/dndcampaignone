## AI Use
The developed form of Hook 8. Escort Lector Briss to the Whispering Cairn to translate an inscription — the campaign's primary **Concord Script gate** and a foundational M6/M9 seed. Pairs with "The Sold Stone." A low-combat, high-payoff escort/protect/translate quest.

## Quest Type
Regional / Travel / Investigation

## Level Range
2–3. Solo danger: low (a protect-the-fragile-NPC escort; one possible rival/scavenger encounter).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Protect Briss en route: Perception DC 13 (spot trouble); combat or evasion as needed.
- Solve the cairn's lock-glyph: Concord Script, or Investigation DC 14 with a rubbing.
- Read the census-wall (gated): Investigation DC 12 with Script/Briss/Comprehend Languages.
- Begin learning Concord Script: downtime with Briss (no single roll).

## Starting Hook
Lector Briss, the Remnant's kindly scholar, needs an escort to a Concord archive-cairn on the South Road and protection while he translates. Good pay — and the chance to learn to read the dead language yourself.

## Location
Candlewick → the Whispering Cairn (South Road; see dungeon file).

## Involved NPCs
Lector Briss (the scholar/dupe — major), a Remnant snatch-team or scavengers (rivals), the warding cairn-Remembrance (if the niche is forced).

## Involved Factions
Concord Remnant (Briss's order — and its hidden agents), Ashen Wardens (would value the inscription's warning).

## Surface Problem
Get Briss to the cairn, keep him safe, and let him translate.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The cairn's census-wall is a fragment of the harvest-truth (M6/M9 seed): the Concord *recorded and kept the dead's patterns*. Briss reads it as fascinating heritage, not horror — he is a sincere dupe (Secret 13). His inner circle would want this; he doesn't know that. Teaching the player Concord Script here unlocks most of the campaign's deep clues.

## Objectives
1. Escort Briss to the cairn.
2. Protect him through the translation (and any rivals).
3. Read the census-wall; begin learning Concord Script.

## Possible Approaches
- **Escort/combat:** guard Briss from scavengers or a Remnant snatch-team.
- **Scholarly:** assist the translation; learn the Script.
- **Stealth:** avoid rivals entirely; translate quietly.
- **Social:** plant doubt in Briss about his order (toward Secret 13).

## Obstacles
The road's dangers; rival salvagers; the language gate; the warding glyph (if the niche is forced).

## Important Scenes
- Briss tracing the carved census-wall, delighted, then quietly disturbed by a line.
- A rival crew arriving mid-translation — negotiate, hide the find, or fight.
- The player's first real Concord Script lesson (a campaign-opening moment).

## Clues
- **C-M6/M9 fragment** (the census-wall; language-gated).
- **Concord Script tutelage** (the key that opens most deep clues).
- Briss as a future ally lever (Secret 13).

## Combat Options
A Remnant snatch-team (Veska Dunn or a courier + bravos; AC 13–14, ~16–30 HP) or scavengers (CR 1/8) — telegraphed, avoidable by stealth or negotiation. The warding Remembrance (specter-like, AC 12, ~17 HP) only if the niche is forced; layable.

## Noncombat Options
Stealth, negotiation, and scholarship resolve the quest; protecting a fragile NPC is the core challenge, not killing.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Briss is a (weak) caster ally; the cairn is scoutable and has open exits; rivals prefer to claim, not kill; the player can leave with a partial reading.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest at Candlewick (with real healing). Time pressure: rivals may reach the cairn first if the player dawdles; a complete reading requires uninterrupted time.

## Rewards
- **Concord Script tutelage** (the campaign's most useful clue-key).
- A census-wall rubbing (portable M6/M9 fragment).
- Briss's trust/gratitude (Secret 13 groundwork); Remnant payment.

## Consequences
- **Success:** the player can read Concord Script (unlocking M2/M6/M9 clue sites Reach-wide) and holds a key M6/M9 fragment.
- **Rivals win:** the cairn is looted/erased (M6/M9 still reachable elsewhere; Reclamation clock advances); Briss is dejected.
- **Disillusion Briss:** seeds the Remnant's fracture (Secret 13).
- **Ignore:** the Script gate stays closed for now (reachable later via Briss elsewhere or the Caradril archives).

## Failure State
If Briss is hurt or the translation fails, he survives (he's too useful to kill cheaply) and will try again; the Script gate and the M6/M9 fragment remain reachable. No dead end.

## Follow-Up Hooks
Learning more Concord Script (every gated clue opens); "The Sold Stone"; the Caradril archives (Act 3, full M6/M9); Briss's disillusionment arc; the Roofless Hall / Cold Cairns (Heights Script sites).

## Scaling Notes
- L2: scavenger trouble at most; gentle.
- L3–4: a coordinated Remnant snatch-team; the warding Remembrance is a full specter if the niche is forced; higher stakes on the rubbing.
