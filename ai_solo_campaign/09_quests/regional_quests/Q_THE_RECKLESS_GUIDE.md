## AI Use
Develops TW-H11. A rescue/redemption quest in the mid-wood: Wren-of-the-Wood, a young Coldhearth guide, has gone too deep toward the Old Mast, drawn by the deep dead. A human-scale way into the Hanging Oaks / deep-wood threads with personal stakes.

## Quest Type
Regional / Personal / Rescue

## Level Range
4–5. Solo danger: moderate (the mid-wood; the deep dead's pull).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Track Wren into the wood: Survival DC 14 (lower with Brann).
- Reach her at the Hanging Oaks without rousing the grove: Wis DC 13 + offering.
- Talk her back from the deep's pull: Insight/Persuasion DC 15.
- Resist the deep dead's lure yourself: Wis DC 13.

## Starting Hook
Old Gethin or Goodwife Sennet, grim with worry, asks the player to bring Wren-of-the-Wood home: she went toward the Hanging Oaks days ago, chasing what the deep dead "told her," and hasn't returned.

## Location
The mid-wood, the Hanging Oaks, the Old Mast edge.

## Involved NPCs
Wren-of-the-Wood (the lost guide), Old Gethin / Sennet (givers), the Gravecaller cell (who drew her), Brann the Guide.

## Surface Problem
Find Wren and bring her back alive and sane.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
Wren was lured deeper by the deep dead and the cell's promise of "what the grey lords took" — the same oblique M5/M6 pull. She's not possessed, but *marked* by the deep wood and half-convinced to go to the Old Mast. **Gate it: the player sees the deep's *pull* and a fragment of the cult's lore, not the apex.**

## Objectives
1. Track Wren. 2. Reach her safely. 3. Talk her back (or save her from the cult / the deep).

## Possible Approaches
- **Compassion:** reach Wren and talk her back from the pull.
- **Confront the cell:** if the Gravecallers are holding/encouraging her, parley or oppose them.
- **Woodcraft:** use the forest-rules and Brann to reach her without becoming lost yourself.

## Obstacles
The mid-wood and getting lost; the deep dead's lure (on Wren and the player); the cell's influence; Wren's own fascination.

## Important Scenes
- Wren's trail leading deeper than any guide should go.
- Finding her at the offering-edge, half-listening to the deep dead.
- Her choice — and the player's argument for the living world over the dead's whispers.

## Clues
- The deep's pull on the young and grieving (M5 oblique; the wood's danger).
- The cell's lore that drew her (oblique M6 fragment).

## Combat Options
Old forest dead and (if hostile) the cell — both telegraphed and avoidable; the quest is a rescue, not a fight. See the Hanging Oaks dungeon.

## Noncombat Options
Tracking, woodcraft, and persuasion carry the quest.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Brann guides; the forest-rules ease the wood; Wren can be talked back; retreat to Coldhearth is open; the Old Mast itself stays gated (Wren is found at the edge, not within).

## Rest And Time Pressure
No safe rest in the mid-wood; Coldhearth is the haven. Time pressure: each day Wren drifts closer to the Old Mast (and the cult's deeper plans).

## Rewards
- Wren saved: Coldhearth's deep gratitude; a young guide ally; ~40–60 gp or woodcraft gear.
- A fragment of the cult's lore (oblique M6) and a clear read of the deep's danger.

## Consequences
- **Saved:** Wren returns marked but alive; the village trusts the player; the cult's pull is checked.
- **Lost to the deep/cult:** Wren joins the Gravecallers or is lost toward the Old Mast (a grief that hardens Coldhearth, and a cult gain).
- **Ignored:** Wren is lost; Coldhearth blames the outsiders; the cell gains a deep-wood guide.

## Failure State
If the player can't reach or convince Wren in time, she's drawn into the cell — recoverable later, with cost — not killed outright. The thread feeds the Hanging Oaks/Old Mast quests. No dead end.

## Scaling Notes
- L4: a straightforward rescue; the deep dead are dread, not a fight.
- L5: the cell actively contests Wren; the deep's pull is stronger; the Old Mast clock presses.
