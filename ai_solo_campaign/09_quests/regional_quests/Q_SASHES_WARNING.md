## AI Use
Develops Hook 6 ("What the Fens Carry"). Old Sashe — the Reach's fen-guide and a unique M5 clue-source (Secret 20) — will show a respectful player the dead drifting toward the basin. Also the quest that wins Sashe as a solo-safety guide for the Greyfens.

## Quest Type
Regional / Mystery / Exploration

## Level Range
2–4. Solo danger: moderate (the Greyfens), low with Sashe guiding.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Earn Sashe's respect: Persuasion DC 13 (DC 8 if the player has honored the dead).
- Navigate the fen (with Sashe negates; without): Survival DC 14.
- Recover the lost peat-cutter: Survival/Investigation DC 13.
- Interpret the drift (M5): Insight/Investigation DC 12 (Sashe explains freely if trusted).

## Starting Hook
A peat-cutter has vanished in the Greyfens. Old Sashe, at his hut on the fen's edge, will help recover the body before the fog and the dead claim it — and, if the player proves worthy, show them something he's told no one believes.

## Location
Greywater Holm / Sashe's hut → the Greyfens (the Drift-Line; see wilderness file).

## Involved NPCs
Old Sashe (guide, clue-source), the lost peat-cutter (dead or drifting), Elder Mabb (the village), Den Hask (secondary fen-guide if Sashe is refused).

## Involved Factions
Mourners' Circle (the dead), Ashen Wardens (would want the drift-evidence).

## Surface Problem
Recover a lost peat-cutter from the Greyfens.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
Sashe has watched the Remembrances **drift toward the basin** for years and mapped it (Secret 20) — the single most direct M5 field-proof, pointing straight at the shrine. The lost cutter has joined the drift. Sashe shares this only with someone who respects the fen and the dead.

## Objectives
1. Win Sashe's trust (or hire Den Hask as a riskier alternative).
2. Recover the cutter (body or drifting Remembrance).
3. Witness and understand the drift (M5).

## Possible Approaches
- **Respect-first:** prove yourself to Sashe (honor the dead, listen, don't bluster).
- **Exploration:** track the cutter through the fen.
- **Ritual:** lay the drifting cutter (true rite) — mercy and a clue at once.
- **Alternative:** if Sashe refuses, Den Hask guides (cheaper, riskier, cult-adjacent).

## Obstacles
The fen's hazards (fog, sinking ground, will-o'-wisps); Sashe's wariness of the disrespectful; the emotional weight of the drift.

## Important Scenes
- Sashe refusing a blustering traveler, opening to a kind one.
- The Drift-Line: dozens of the dead standing in the water, all facing the basin.
- Laying the lost cutter, who "remembers being pulled toward the water."

## Clues
- **C-M5-1 / C-M2-2** (the drift; Sashe's map) — the clearest Act 1 shrine-pointer.
- The cutter's testimony (a human M5 confirmation).

## Combat Options
Drifting Remembrances (specter-like, AC 12, ~17–22 HP) — **non-hostile; lay or pass them by**. Fen predators (CR 1/4) optional, telegraphed. With Sashe, combat is almost always avoidable.

## Noncombat Options
Respect, navigation, ritual, and listening. The drift is witnessed, not fought.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
**Sashe himself is the safety valve** — winning him makes the Greyfens survivable. He warns, guides, and never leads the player into a fight they can't avoid.

## Rest And Time Pressure
No safe rest in the open fen; Greywater Holm / Sashe's hut for recovery. Time pressure: the longer the cutter drifts, the closer to the basin (and the harder to recover/lay) — a gentle clock.

## Rewards
- **Old Sashe as a guide ally** (the Greyfens' key solo-safety resource) — the real prize.
- The M5 clue/map (a direct line toward the shrine).
- Mourners' and village goodwill; the cutter's family's gratitude.

## Consequences
- **Success:** the player gains Sashe, the drift-evidence, and a powerful early lead on the central mystery.
- **Disrespect Sashe:** he refuses; the player must risk the fen alone or with Den Hask (cult-adjacent danger).
- **Ignore:** the cutter joins the drift unrecovered; Sashe's evidence goes unwitnessed (M5 still reachable elsewhere, but the clearest path is missed).

## Failure State
If the recovery fails or Sashe is alienated, the M5 clue remains available (Wren's grave, Greywater's rites, Gravecaller lore), and Sashe may still be won later by genuine respect. No dead end.

## Follow-Up Hooks
The Drowned Blind / Gravecallers; the Barrow of Nine Doors (deep drift); the basin shore (M2); Sashe as a guide to the shrine itself (late).

## Scaling Notes
- L2: gentle; Sashe guides; minimal predators.
- L4: the fen is more active (more dead, a will-o'-wisp gauntlet); the cutter has nearly reached a dangerous drift-knot (the Barrow).
