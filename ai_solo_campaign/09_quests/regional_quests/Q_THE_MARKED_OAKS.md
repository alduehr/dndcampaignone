## AI Use
Develops TW-H1. The master Tollwood quest: the Ledger's deep-cutting (Factor Hesk Dorr) is felling the marked oaks and waking the deep wood. The player must stop, expose, or redirect it before the regional clock ("The Old Mast Stirs") runs. Combines social, investigation, and woodland-survival; the deep secret stays gated.

## Quest Type
Regional / Faction (vs. Cinder Ledger)

## Level Range
3–5. Solo danger: moderate (the deep wood, the loggers).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Convince Hartfell's leaders the cutting is dangerous: Persuasion DC 15 (DC 12 with woodfolk testimony/evidence).
- Turn Road-Warden Vey against Dorr: Persuasion/Insight DC 14.
- Confront/outmaneuver Dorr: Persuasion DC 16 / Investigation DC 14 (find his cut-orders).
- Survive the deep-cutting front: Survival DC 14; offering at a Coppice Shrine eases the wood.

## Starting Hook
Brann the Guide (or Edda Sorrel, or the woodfolk) tells the player Dorr is cutting the *marked* oaks now — the trees the old bargain forbids — and the wood has "gone wrong out east." Crews are vanishing. Someone must stop it before the wood wakes.

## Location
Hartfell (the Timber-Weigh, the camps) and the deep-cutting front toward the Old Mast.

## Involved NPCs
Factor Hesk Dorr (Ledger; driving the cutting), Road-Warden Captain Brannoch Vey (turnable), Goodwife Edda Sorrel / Goodwife Sennet (woodfolk), Brann the Guide.

## Involved Factions
Cinder Ledger (profit), Mourners'/woodfolk (the bargain), Compact (the road and order).

## Surface Problem
Stop the deep-cutting before the wood becomes deadly.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The marked oaks ward the Old Mast's sleep; cutting them wakes the **pre-Concord presence** (regional clock), whose restlessness *resents the harvest reawakening* (oblique M5/M6). Dorr is greedy, not a Court agent — **commercial denial, not conspiracy.** **Gate the deep secret; the player sees the *effects* (waking wood, vanishing crews), not the presence's nature, unless they go to the Old Mast.**

## Objectives
1. Confirm the cutting is the cause. 2. Build the case (woodfolk testimony, vanished crews, Dorr's orders). 3. Stop, expose, or redirect it.

## Possible Approaches
- **Political:** turn Vey and Hartfell's leaders against the cutting (halt it lawfully).
- **Economic:** expose the danger/losses so the Ledger pulls back (Dorr's own crews are dying).
- **Direct:** disrupt the front (sabotage, scare off crews) — risky, makes a Ledger enemy.
- **Spiritual:** invoke the bargain with the woodfolk and a Coppice-Shrine rite (the old way).

## Obstacles
Dorr's profit and denial; the deep wood's growing danger; Hartfell's dependence on the timber-trade; the Ledger's reach.

## Important Scenes
- A marked oak felled, and the wood's response — dread, restless dead, a vanished cutter.
- Dorr dismissing a shaking woodsman's warning over the timber-ledgers.
- Vey, at the barred east gate, admitting he keeps the rules he enforces against.

## Clues
- The vanished crews and the waking wood (regional clock; effects).
- Dorr's cut-orders pushing toward the Old Mast (Investigation; the human cause).
- The woodfolk's bargain and the marked oaks (M5/M6 oblique shape; gated).

## Combat Options
Ledger logger-toughs (Thug/Scout-like, AC 12–13, ~32 HP) if the player disrupts the front; old forest dead near the cutting (Specter-like, hard to lay). Both telegraphed; combat is costly and avoidable.

## Noncombat Options
Political, economic, and spiritual approaches all resolve it without a pitched fight.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Brann guides; Coppice-Shrine offerings ease the wood; the player can build the case from town without going deep; retreat to Hartfell is always open.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Hartfell/Coldhearth and at honored shrines; none in the deep wood. **Real time pressure:** the regional clock advances with each cutting-season — but it's a clock, not a hard fail.

## Rewards
- The cutting halted (or redirected): the wood quiets (the clock arrested); woodfolk and Mourners' deep gratitude; Vey as an ally.
- ~80–120 gp (woodfolk/Compact reward) or a Ledger favor if the player brokers a profitable redirect.
- The gateway to the deeper Tollwood threads (the Bargain, the Old Mast) earned through trust.

## Consequences
- **Stopped:** the Old Mast clock is arrested; the deep wood stabilizes; the Ledger resents the lost timber.
- **Redirected:** a compromise — cutting moves away from the marked stands; partial win.
- **Failed/ignored:** Dorr cuts on; the Old Mast stirs; the deep dead spread to the road; the East Road to Caradril grows perilous.

## Failure State
If the player can't stop Dorr, the cutting continues and the regional clock advances — but the thread reopens (the woodfolk, a vanished-crew disaster, or Vey turning at last). The deep wood worsens; it does not dead-end the campaign.

## Scaling Notes
- L3: social/investigation-weighted; the deep-cutting front is dread, not a fight.
- L5: the front is dangerous (old dead, marked-tree taboo-backlash); Dorr more entrenched; the clock closer.
