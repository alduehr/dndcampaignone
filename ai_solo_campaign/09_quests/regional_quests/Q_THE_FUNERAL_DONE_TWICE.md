## AI Use
Develops AV-H1. The Vale's gentle introductory mystery: a reburied matriarch won't stay down, and the town would rather the player make the problem quietly disappear than ask why. The player's first taste of the Vale's denial (M1/M5 echo, R1-capped).

## Quest Type
Regional / Mystery

## Level Range
1–3. Solo danger: low (one telegraphed, layable Remembrance).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Get the family to admit the rite failed: Insight/Persuasion DC 14 (DC 10 with Mother Combe's blessing).
- Read the grave-drift toward the lake: Survival/Investigation DC 12.
- True rite to settle the matriarch: Religion DC 13 (DC 11 with a Mourner's aid).
- Pry into the Reeve's suppressed reburial-records: Investigation DC 14.

## Starting Hook
A grieving Orchardmere family quietly asks the player to "see to" their mother, who was buried with full rites, dug up and reburied once already, and is again surfacing toward the lake. They are ashamed and frightened, and want it done without the moot hearing.

## Location
Orchardmere — the Old Circle grave-garden, the lakeside, the family's home.

## Involved NPCs
The grieving family (minor), Mother Ezrith Combe (Mourners; ally), Moot-Reeve Halsa Tindle (running the cover-up), Goodwife Orrel (knows the reburials).

## Involved Factions
Mourners' Circle (wants the dead truly settled), the moot/Compact (wants it hushed).

## Surface Problem
Lay a matriarch who will not stay buried.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The rite "failed" because the harvest's leakage has reached the Vale (M5) — she drifts toward the water like the Reach's dead drift toward the basin. The Reeve has hushed three such cases this autumn to protect the pilgrim trade. **Cap at R1: the failures have a source and a pattern; do not name the harvest.**

## Objectives
1. Learn the truth the family is hiding.
2. Settle the matriarch (a true rite, not a blade).
3. Decide what to do with what you learn (tell Combe, confront the Reeve, or stay quiet).

## Possible Approaches
- **Compassion:** win the family's trust, perform/arrange a true rite with Combe's help.
- **Investigation:** trace the grave-drift and the prior reburials; uncover the Reeve's pattern.
- **Politics:** take the cover-up to Combe or the moot (consequences either way).

## Obstacles
The family's shame; the Reeve's pressure to stay quiet; the matriarch's sorrowful resistance to an ordinary rite.

## Important Scenes
- The family confessing, in shame, that "she's been done over twice already."
- The matriarch surfacing at dusk by the lake, facing the water (M5, visceral but gentle).
- Mother Combe, deciding whether to trust the player with more.

## Clues
- The grave-drift toward the lake (C-AV-M5-1; mirrors the Reach's basin-drift).
- The Reeve's pattern of quiet reburials (M1/M5; the cover-up).

## Combat Options
None required. If provoked, the matriarch's Remembrance defends weakly (Specter-like, AC 12, ~17 HP, chill touch ~1d6) and is far better settled by a rite. Telegraphed.

## Noncombat Options
Compassion, a true rite, investigation, and political maneuver are the whole quest.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Combe will aid the rite; the matriarch is non-hostile; the family wants help, not a fight; the player can always walk away and the quest reopens.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rests in Orchardmere throughout. Soft pressure: each night the matriarch drifts nearer the lake (more visible, more talked-of), nudging the Vale clock — but no hard fail.

## Rewards
- Mother Combe's trust (the gateway to the Vale's deeper Mourner threads, incl. the M6 path).
- The family's lasting gratitude and ~25 gp or goods.
- If the cover-up is documented: leverage over the Reeve (and a Compact/moot complication).

## Consequences
- **Success (rite):** the matriarch rests; the family is grateful; Combe warms.
- **Partial:** she's settled but the cover-up stands; the pattern continues elsewhere.
- **Ignored:** she reaches the lake and draws other dead toward it; the Vale clock advances; the family despairs.
- **Excessive force:** "destroying" the dead without a rite horrifies the family and the Mourners (relationship hit).

## Failure State
If the player can't settle her, the matriarch joins the lakeside drift and the case becomes a public scare — which actually *cracks* the cover-up (a different door opens: AV-H2). No dead end.

## Follow-Up Hooks
"The Moot's Silence" (AV-H2); "Saint's Bones" (AV-H3); deeper Mourner trust (M6, gated); Combe's errand south (AV-H20).

## Scaling Notes
- L1: pure social/ritual; the matriarch is gentle.
- L3: add a second hushed case, a wrathful edge to the matriarch if disrespected, and the Reeve actively obstructing.
