## AI Use
Develops AV-H11. The Vale's gentlest on-ramp: an honest, poor village admits its dead won't rest and asks for plain help. The cleanest, lowest-stakes brush with M5 in Ring 1, with Mam Tace as a warm folk-mentor.

## Quest Type
Regional / Mystery (M5, gentle)

## Level Range
1–2. Solo danger: very low.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Read which grave is drifting: Survival/Investigation DC 12.
- Perform/assist a true rite with Mam Tace: Religion DC 12 (DC 10 with her aid).
- Comfort a frightened villager: Insight/Persuasion DC 11.

## Starting Hook
Old Mam Tace, Tilbrook's grave-singer, shows the player the loaf left on a fresh grave — found moved toward the river three mornings running. "The grave-field won't hold this winter," she says. "Will you help me sing one down proper?"

## Location
Tilbrook — the grave-field and the Ammet bank.

## Involved NPCs
Old Mam Tace (Mourners; mentor), the grieving family (minor), Ale-wife Genna Brunt (rumor).

## Surface Problem
Settle a grave that won't stay closed.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The dead here drift toward water like the Reach's dead drift toward the basin (M5) — the Vale is now leaking, *later and weaker than the Reach* (corroborates the failures radiate from the keystone). Mam Tace, unlike Orchardmere, says so plainly. **R1 cap: a source and a pattern; no harvest.**

## Objectives
1. Find the drifting grave. 2. Settle it with a true rite. 3. Hear Mam Tace's honest read of the Vale.

## Possible Approaches
- Ritual (with Mam Tace), compassion (the family), or investigation (the drift pattern). All valid; combine freely.

## Obstacles
The family's grief; the drift's pull resisting an ordinary rite; the winter cold.

## Important Scenes
- The loaf, found moved toward the river again at dawn.
- Mam Tace singing the passing as the player assists — the Vale's gentlest true rite.
- Her plain words: "It started up north and it's coming to us. Don't let the fat towns tell you different."

## Clues
- The grave-drift toward water (C-AV-M5-1).
- Mam Tace's honest testimony (M5; the Vale's honest window).

## Combat Options
None. If badly disrespected, the grave's Remembrance stirs weakly (Specter-like, AC 12, ~13 HP) — better sung down. Telegraphed.

## Noncombat Options
The entire quest.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Mam Tace leads the rite; the dead are gentle; Tilbrook is safe; the quest cannot dead-end.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest at the Plough & Star. Soft pressure: each night the drift worsens (Vale clock nudge), no hard fail.

## Rewards
- Tilbrook's trust (the village's quiet backing in later Ledger conflicts).
- Mam Tace as an ally and folk-mentor; ~10–15 gp or goods; a true-rite blessing.
- The honest M5 read (a clue most of the Vale hides).

## Consequences
- **Success:** the grave rests; Tilbrook warms; Mam Tace opens up.
- **Ignored:** the grave-field "won't hold"; more dead drift riverward; the Vale clock advances; Widow Hessit's despair deepens (cult-seed).

## Failure State
If the rite doesn't take, the grave joins a small riverside drift — sad, not catastrophic — and Mam Tace tries again with the player. No dead end.

## Follow-Up Hooks
"The Marks on the Doors" (AV-H12); "A Last Word in the Vale" (AV-H13); Mam Tace's link to Orchardmere's Mother Combe (M6 path, gated).

## Scaling Notes
- L1: exactly as written — soft and warm.
- L2+: add a second drifting grave and a clearer drift-pattern toward the Ammet/lake.
