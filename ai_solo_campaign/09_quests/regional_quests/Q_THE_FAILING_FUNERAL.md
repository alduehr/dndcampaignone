## AI Use
The developed, **repeatable** form of Hook 9. A funeral fails publicly somewhere in the Reach; help a Warden (Hale) complete the rite or contain it. The Reach's core Act 1 texture — each failure nearer the basin is *worse* (M5), and helping builds Warden trust toward Secret 8. Reusable in any settlement.

## Quest Type
Regional / Side (repeatable)

## Level Range
1–3 (scalable). Solo danger: low-to-moderate (a single telegraphed Remembrance; worse near the basin).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Assist/complete the true rite: Religion DC 13 (auto with a Warden's help; DC 15 alone).
- Calm a frightened crowd/family: Persuasion/Insight DC 13.
- Diagnose why this rite failed (M5): Religion/Medicine DC 13.
- Contain a Remembrance that escapes: varies (chase/lay; see below).

## Starting Hook
A funeral fails before the mourners' eyes — the dead won't go down. Brother Hale (or a lone Warden) is there, exhausted and overstretched, and needs a steady hand to complete the rite or keep the grief from turning to panic.

## Location
Any Reach settlement (Hollowmere, Reedford, Greywater, Candlewick, Kettle Bridge) or roadside. **DM picks by proximity; failures are worse nearer the basin.**

## Involved NPCs
Brother Hale (the overstretched Warden — major/companion), the grieving family (improv/minor), a Mourner-keeper, the failed-rite Remembrance.

## Involved Factions
Ashen Wardens (the rite), Mourners' Circle (the grief).

## Surface Problem
Help complete a failing funeral rite and keep the situation from breaking.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
Rites fail because the harvest is reawakening (M5); the failure is *worse the nearer the basin*, a pattern a watchful player or Hale will notice over repeated funerals. Each helped rite builds Warden trust (toward Secret 8) and, cumulatively, slows the Wardens Break clock.

## Objectives
1. Stabilize the scene (the dead, the crowd, the family).
2. Complete the rite (or contain the Remembrance).
3. (Over time) notice the geographic pattern (M5).

## Possible Approaches
- **Ritual:** assist or complete the true rite (the core path).
- **Social:** calm the family/crowd; prevent a mob.
- **Investigation:** track *where* failures cluster (M5).
- **Containment:** if the Remembrance escapes, lay it before it harms anyone.

## Obstacles
The rite's resistance (the pull); a frightened crowd; Hale's exhaustion; the risk of a Remembrance loose in a settlement.

## Important Scenes
- The casket-lid shifting; the dead sitting up, gentle and wrong; the family's scream.
- Hale's hands shaking as he tries again, grateful for help.
- The dawning realization (over several funerals) that the basin is the cause.

## Clues
- **C-M5 (cumulative)** — failures cluster and worsen toward the basin.
- **C-M1-2-adjacent** — a Warden examining the dead ("something holds them here"; Secret 8 seed).

## Combat Options
A single failed-rite Remembrance (specter/ghost-like, AC 12, ~17–30 HP scaling) — **non-combat primary** (true rite); combat only if it escapes and threatens, and even then it's telegraphed and layable. Hale assists.

## Noncombat Options
Ritual and social de-escalation resolve it; combat is the fallback, not the path.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Hale (a companion ally) is usually present; the Remembrance is sorrowful, not an ambusher; retreat and fetch help is always possible; failures near the basin are flagged as harder (telegraphed).

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in whatever settlement hosts the funeral. Time pressure: an unresolved failure can become a loose Remembrance (a new minor threat) and shakes the community (advances local dread / the Wardens Break clock).

## Rewards
- Warden trust (toward Secret 8 and a slowed Wardens Break clock).
- Community goodwill in that settlement.
- Cumulative M5 insight (the geographic pattern — a quiet but crucial clue).
- Hale's deepening friendship (toward his companion arc).

## Consequences
- **Success:** the dead rests; the community steadies; the Wardens gain a trusted helper.
- **Partial:** the rite half-takes; the dead is "quieted" but uneasy (a return scene later).
- **Ignore/fail:** the Remembrance escapes into the fens/roads (a new minor threat); the community panics; the Wardens fray.

## Failure State
A failed funeral that goes uncontained seeds a loose Remembrance and a frightened settlement — a complication, not a dead end, and itself an M5 data-point. The quest recurs naturally as the crisis worsens.

## Follow-Up Hooks
"Pell's Doubt" / Secret 8 (the order's hidden purpose); "The Holm That Won't Bury Its Dead" (the worst version, at Greywater); the basin scheme (the cause); Hale's companion arc.

## Scaling Notes
- L1: weak Remembrance (~17 HP); Hale does most of the work.
- L3: stronger, basin-adjacent failure (~30 HP), a near-mob, and a clearer M5 pattern; consider a relic or a tougher rite (two successes).
- **Repeatable:** vary the settlement, the deceased, and the complication each time; escalate as clocks advance.
