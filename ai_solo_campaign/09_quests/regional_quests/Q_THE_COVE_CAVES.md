## AI Use
Develops PC-H10/H12. The Coast's tide-and-social infiltration: clear the wreckers from the cove caves, trace the sea-relic smuggling (M3), and find (or save) the Gravecaller current and young Ferec. Combines stealth, the tide-puzzle, and a moral choice; combat is the worst option.

## Quest Type
Regional / Faction (wreckers / Gravecallers) / Dungeon

## Level Range
3–5. Solo danger: moderate (the tide; the wreckers).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Time the tide for the caves: Survival DC 14.
- Sneak past the wrecker lookout: Stealth DC 13.
- Get the wreckers' salvage-ledger: Investigation DC 12.
- Talk young Ferec back from the current: Insight/Persuasion DC 14.
- Lay the cave's drowned haunting: Religion DC 13.

## Starting Hook
The village (or Bryd, or Net-wife Senna) asks the player to deal with the cove caves: the wreckers prey on the cove, they fence the drowned's own things (breaking the salt-rites), and young Ferec — grieving his drowned father — has been slipping down there to "hear the dead."

## Location
The Wreckers' Caves (`THE_WRECKERS_CAVES.md`), under the Drowned Lamp headland.

## Involved NPCs
The Wreck-Master (wreckers), the Gravecaller current speaker, young Ferec (grieving lad), Salt-Mother Bryd, Net-wife Senna.

## Surface Problem
Break the wreckers' hold and find what's drawing the grieving into the caves.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The wreckers fence Concord "sea-relics" (M3) through Cole's chain; the Gravecaller current uses the deepest cave (where the drowned answer) to recruit the grieving like Ferec; the caves leak toward the Lamp's node (M5 oblique). **Cap at R1/R2: M3 and the cult-door; never the Court.**

## Objectives
1. Time the tide and get into the caves. 2. Break/expose the wreckers (the salvage-ledger). 3. Find the Gravecaller current; save or learn from it; reach Ferec.

## Possible Approaches
- **Stealth/tide:** infiltrate at the right tide; get the ledger; reach the Deep Cave quietly.
- **Social:** turn or expose the wreckers; parley the Gravecaller current.
- **Protective:** prioritize Ferec — talk him out before the current claims him.
- **Force:** storm the caves (the failure path — the tide and numbers punish it).

## Obstacles
The flooding tide-maze; the wrecker lookout and gang; the moral pull of the answering drowned; Ferec's grief.

## Important Scenes
- Timing the tide into the dark, dripping caves.
- The wreckers' plunder — and the drowned's own keepsakes among it.
- The Listening Pool, the drowned answering, and Ferec half-convinced to stay.

## Clues
- The wreckers' salvage-ledger (M3; the sea-relic chain to Cole).
- The Gravecaller current's teaching ("the drowned are called home"; M5/M6 oblique fragment).

## Combat Options
Wreckers (Bandit/Thug-like, AC 12–13) and the Wreck-Master (Veteran/Spy-like, AC 15, ~45 HP); a drowned haunting (Specter-like, layable). All telegraphed; combat is the worst path. See the dungeon.

## Noncombat Options
Stealth, tide-timing, social, and ritual carry the quest.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The tide limits pursuit; the wreckers ransom rather than kill; the current parleys; Ferec can be saved; retreat at the right tide is open.

## Rest And Time Pressure
No safe rest in the caves; Cobble Strand is a short climb at low tide. The tide is the recurring clock.

## Rewards
- The wreckers' hold broken (honest fishing restored; the village's gratitude).
- The salvage-ledger (M3; leverage on Cole — feeds "The Salt-Price").
- Ferec saved; a Gravecaller-current contact (forbidden lore) or its recruiting checked; the wreckers' plunder (some owed to wreck-victims' kin).

## Consequences
- **Broken + Ferec saved:** the cove is freed; the cult-current checked; the Mourners honor the player.
- **Exploited (current's way):** more lore, but the cult gains and Ferec is lost.
- **Ignored:** the wreckers and the relic-trade flourish; Ferec is claimed by the current; the cove stays closed.

## Failure State
If the player can't clear the caves, the wreckers persist but the salvage-ledger / M3 truth and the cult-door stand, and the thread reopens. If caught by tide or wreckers, stranded or ransomed, not killed. No dead end.

## Scaling Notes
- L3: stealth/tide-weighted; the wreckers prefer threats.
- L5: a Ledger enforcer, a wrathful drowned, a tighter tide; higher relic stakes.
