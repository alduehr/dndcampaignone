## AI Use
Develops Hook 10 ("Debts and the Drowned") into a basin-shore quest. The drainage scheme's land-grab threatens the basin-island fisher-families; their plight ties Ledger debt, the basin scheme, and the master plot (M2/M4) into a human, moral pivot.

## Quest Type
Regional / Faction / Social

## Level Range
2–4. Solo danger: low-to-moderate (enforcers threaten; the basin shore is eerie but daylight-safe).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Untangle the debt paperwork: Investigation DC 14.
- Spot that the debt traces to the basin land-grab: Investigation/Insight DC 15.
- Face down Ledger enforcers (Tallytooth Ren): Intimidation/Persuasion DC 14, or a telegraphed fight.
- Rally the Reed Holm families / Mourners: Persuasion DC 13.

## Starting Hook
A basin-island family ruined by a failed-rite tragedy is being squeezed by Ledger debt — and the debt, oddly, is tied to the basin "resettlement." They beg the player for help before they're driven off their holm.

## Location
Hollowmere and the Reed Holms (basin-shore islands); the counting-house; the drainage works.

## Involved NPCs
A ruined Reed Holm family (use minor NPCs/improv), Factor Domic Sael (the paperwork), Tallytooth Ren (the squeeze), Reeve Coalmont (sympathetic), Mourners' Circle (furious).

## Involved Factions
Cinder Ledger (the debt/land-grab), Compact (whose scheme it is), Mourners' Circle (the dead and the displaced).

## Surface Problem
Save a family from ruinous Ledger debt and eviction.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The debt is a *lever* of the basin-drainage land-grab (M4): clearing the Reed Holms is part of exposing the shrine. The family's tragedy traces back to a failed rite (M5) and the scheme that profits from the crisis. Helping them is a direct push against the Basin clock and a window into how Reke and the Ledger weaponize grief.

## Objectives
1. Investigate the debt and the eviction.
2. Trace it to the basin scheme.
3. Resist it (legally, socially, or by exposing the land-grab).

## Possible Approaches
- **Legal:** untangle and challenge the debt (Domic, Coalmont, the records).
- **Social:** rally the families and the Mourners against the eviction.
- **Investigation:** expose the land-grab's link to the shrine scheme (M4).
- **Confrontation:** face down the enforcers (telegraphed; avoidable).

## Obstacles
The Ledger's legal machinery; Ren's pressure; the families' fear; the scheme's political momentum.

## Important Scenes
- The family's eviction notice, stamped with the resettlement seal.
- Ren on the holm's plank-walk, all smiles and threat.
- The shore itself — the surfacing shapes, the eerie water — making the stakes literal.

## Clues
- **C-M4 support** (the debt → land-grab → shrine scheme).
- **C-M2 (shore)** if the player goes to the works/shore (the surfacing ruin).

## Combat Options
Tallytooth Ren + 1–2 toughs (Thug-like, AC 11–13, ~32–40 HP) — **catch/intimidate, not kill**; telegraphed; avoidable by paperwork or social pressure.

## Noncombat Options
Legal challenge, social mobilization, exposure — all viable; no fight required.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Daylight shore (the deep basin is telegraphed off-limits); enforcers threaten before they strike; Coalmont and the Mourners as allies; retreat to town anytime.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Hollowmere. Time pressure: the eviction has a deadline; the Basin clock advances as the scheme proceeds.

## Rewards
- The family's safety (and a loyal basin-island contact/punt-access).
- Mourners' goodwill; Coalmont's trust; a Domic lever (if he helps undermine the debt).
- An M4 clue and a slowed Basin clock (a real win against the master plot).

## Consequences
- **Save them:** the Reed Holms hold; the scheme stalls a step; the Mourners rally behind the player.
- **Fail/ignore:** the families are displaced or drowned; the basin clears for drainage (Basin clock jumps); a grief that fuels the Gravecallers and the Circle's militancy.
- **Side with the Ledger:** coin and Ledger favor, at a moral cost and a strengthened scheme.

## Failure State
If the family is ruined, the player gains a hard M4 lesson (the scheme's human cost) and a furious Mourners' Circle — and the eviction itself becomes evidence for a later anti-Reke case. No dead end.

## Follow-Up Hooks
"The Reeve's Trust" (Hook 5; M4); the basin shore survey (Hook 4); the Circle turning militant; turning Domic.

## Scaling Notes
- L2: paperwork-and-persuasion; Ren alone.
- L4: the Ledger fields more muscle and tighter legal traps; the eviction is near; tie directly to the basin vote.
