## AI Use
Develops TW-H2/H4. The Tollwood's classic frontier quest: the East Road bandits (the Tollmen) and their fraying leadership. Built for *multiple solutions* — clear, broker, turn, or co-opt — with a sympathetic boss (Renn) and a murderous lieutenant (Skell). Controls a Caradril travel artery.

## Quest Type
Regional / Faction (the Tollmen)

## Level Range
2–4. Solo danger: moderate (a real bandit chokepoint; lethal if Skell takes over or the player forces a fight).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Negotiate passage / refuse the toll: Persuasion/Intimidation DC 13.
- Turn Renn from the racket: Persuasion DC 15 (DC 12 with an honest-work offer from Vey/Hartfell).
- Expose or exploit Skell's plot: Insight DC 14 / Investigation DC 13.
- Defeat the Tollmen in a fight: hard solo — use terrain, the road's chokepoint, or split them.

## Starting Hook
Road-Warden Captain Brannoch Vey (or a robbed trader, or Hartfell's traders) asks the player to do something about Tollstone Cross — the toll is choking the Caradril trade, and word is the bandits are turning ugly.

## Location
Tollstone Cross (the chain, the Broken Arch, the Honest Corner).

## Involved NPCs
Toll-Boss Cady Renn (turnable; ex-road-watch), Skell (the murderous lieutenant), Smith Doune / Ale-wife Pell (honest residents), Captain Brannoch Vey (patron).

## Involved Factions
Reachward Compact (wants the road clear), Cinder Ledger (wants it cheap), the Tollmen (want to survive).

## Surface Problem
Open the East Road and end (or fix) the toll.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
Renn is a deserter from a *Compact road-watch the Compact abandoned* — she runs the toll as a hard protection racket that genuinely keeps worse dangers (and the deep dead) off the road. Skell wants to escalate to murder and is one season from ousting her. **The "right" answer isn't simply killing the bandits** — it's choosing what kind of order the road gets.

## Objectives
1. Read the Tollmen's situation (Renn vs. Skell). 2. Choose an approach. 3. Resolve the road's control.

## Possible Approaches
- **Turn Renn:** offer her a way back to honest road-watch work (Vey's deputation) — she takes the toll-folk legitimate; the road gains a real warden.
- **Break Skell:** expose/oust the lieutenant before he seizes control (saves Renn's better order).
- **Clear them:** drive or defeat the Tollmen (opens the road, but the wood/rival bandits fill the gap).
- **Co-opt:** take the toll yourself (coin and control, at a cost to your standing with Vey/Hartfell).

## Obstacles
The chokepoint terrain; the bandits' numbers (a solo PC can't simply out-fight them all); Skell's agitation; the residents caught in the middle.

## Important Scenes
- The chain across the road, Renn naming a toll, Skell behind her itching for blood.
- Renn, alone, admitting she'd trade the toll for honest work.
- Skell making his move — and the player deciding who the road belongs to.

## Clues
- Renn's road-watch past (Insight/Doune; she's not a simple villain).
- Skell's plot to seize and escalate (Investigation/eavesdropping).
- The Drowned Vault's leakage drawing the dead here (links to TW-H5; the local drift).

## Combat Options
The Tollmen (Bandit/Scout-like, AC 12–13, 6–10), Renn (Veteran/Spy-like, AC 15, ~45 HP), Skell (Thug/Berserker-like, AC 13, ~40 HP). **Solo-danger high if forced** — use the chokepoint, split them, or ally with Renn against Skell. Morale breaks under Renn; lethal under Skell.

## Noncombat Options
Negotiation, turning Renn, exposing Skell, paying the toll, or co-opting it all avoid a pitched fight.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The toll is the default (pay and pass); Renn prefers coin to blood; the player can recruit Renn against Skell rather than fight all the Tollmen; retreat down the road is open.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Rough/uneasy rest at Tollstone Cross; Hartfell/Kettle Bridge are the real havens. Soft pressure: each week Skell gains ground (toward a murderous takeover) and the road-trade chokes.

## Rewards
- The road opened/stabilized: Vey's and Hartfell's gratitude; the Caradril route secured; ~60–100 gp or a Compact deputation.
- If Renn is turned: a grey ally controlling the road (a recurring asset).
- The Drowned Vault thread unlocked (TW-H5).

## Consequences
- **Renn turned / Skell broken:** the road gains real order; the Tollmen go semi-legitimate; a durable win.
- **Cleared:** the road opens briefly; the wood and rival bandits move in; a fragile win.
- **Co-opted:** the player holds the toll (power and coin, lost standing with the lawful).
- **Ignored:** Skell ousts Renn; the Tollmen turn road-pirate; the East Road to Caradril chokes; Hartfell weakens.

## Failure State
If the player fails to resolve it, the toll endures (or worsens under Skell), but the road stays passable for a price and the thread reopens via Vey or a road-pirate atrocity. No dead end.

## Scaling Notes
- L2: emphasize negotiation; the Tollmen prefer the toll.
- L4: Skell's faction is stronger; a fight is genuinely dangerous; the takeover is imminent.
