## AI Use
Develops AV-H12. A social/justice quest: stop or expose the Ledger's foreclosures on Tilbrook and uncover *why* Pell forecloses shrine-land in particular (M3 — the relic-quarrying at the Pellow Grange). Combat-light; sympathy and evidence are the weapons.

## Quest Type
Regional / Faction (vs. Cinder Ledger)

## Level Range
2–3. Solo danger: low-moderate (the grange infiltration).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Spot Pell's foreclosure pattern (shrine-land only): Investigation/Insight DC 13.
- Rally or steady the village: Persuasion DC 13.
- Face down Pell's enforcers without bloodshed: Intimidation/Persuasion DC 14.
- Obtain the Pellow dig-ledger: Stealth DC 13 / Investigation DC 12 (see the dungeon).

## Starting Hook
Miller Sael Brunt, marks creeping across his village, asks the player to help: "Pell's taking the farms one by one. And he only ever takes the ones with old stones in the fields. Why?"

## Location
Tilbrook and the Pellow Grange.

## Involved NPCs
Miller Sael Brunt (giver), Factor Wymar Pell (Ledger; antagonist), Foreman Drust (grange), Widow Hessit (a casualty).

## Surface Problem
Stop the Ledger from beggaring Tilbrook.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
Pell forecloses farms *over Concord field-shrines* so the Ledger can quarry their Remembrance relics unobserved (M3) and ship them south (Monopoly clock). The Pellow Grange is the active dig. **R2/M3 act-paced; at low level, deliver the *pattern* and the experiential relic-truth, not the full collector-conspiracy frame.**

## Objectives
1. Uncover the foreclosure pattern. 2. Investigate the Pellow Grange. 3. Stop, expose, or leverage the scheme.

## Possible Approaches
- **Legal/social:** expose the pattern to the moot, the Mourners, or a Compact official.
- **Direct:** face down the enforcers; rally the village to resist.
- **Infiltration:** get the dig-ledger from the grange (the proof).
- **Cynical:** cut your own deal with Pell (coin, lost goodwill).

## Obstacles
Pell's legal cover and charm; the enforcers; the village's fear and debt.

## Important Scenes
- Enforcers nailing a mark to a door as the village watches.
- The realization that every foreclosed farm has an old stone in its fields.
- The grange dig by lamplight — the dead being quarried for profit.

## Clues
- The shrine-land foreclosure pattern (M3 pattern).
- The Pellow dig-ledger (M3 documentary; the relics logged by grave).

## Combat Options
The grange crew (Thug-like, AC 11, ~32 HP) and Drust (Spy/Thug-like, AC 13, ~40 HP) — all prefer threats; break on morale; combat is the worst path. See `THE_PELLOW_GRANGE.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Investigation, social exposure, rallying the village, and infiltration carry the quest.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The grange is scoutable; the enforcers ransom rather than kill; the village backs a careful player; the player can gather proof and leave.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Tilbrook. Soft pressure: each week another mark appears and another relic-cart goes south (Monopoly clock).

## Rewards
- Tilbrook's loyalty; Miller Brunt's alliance; the Mourners' goodwill if the relics are returned for release.
- M3 understanding (act-capped).
- Leverage over Pell / the Ledger; or coin if the player deals.

## Consequences
- **Stop/expose:** foreclosures halt; the Vale relic-supply falters (Monopoly clock eases); Pell an enemy.
- **Deal:** coin and a Ledger favor; Tilbrook and the Mourners turn cold.
- **Ignored:** the mill and shrine-farms fall to the Ledger; relics flow south; Tilbrook empties; Hessit turns to the Gravecallers.

## Failure State
If Pell wins the legal fight, the player still has the dig-ledger and the experiential M3 truth; the thread reopens via the Mourners or Caradril. No dead end.

## Follow-Up Hooks
"Saint's Bones" (AV-H3); "The Ledger's Quiet Cart" (AV-H19); "A Last Word in the Vale" (AV-H13); the Monopoly clock toward Caradril.

## Scaling Notes
- L2: enforcers prefer intimidation; the grange lightly held.
- L3: add a Ledger enforcer; sharpen the legal trap; raise the relic stakes.
