## AI Use
Develops PC-H6. The wider wrecking-trade quest: the wreckers who lure or strip ships along the wild coast and feed the sea-relic chain. A regional-justice quest with multiple solutions, the wreckers' strand and caves as sites, and a moral spectrum from victims' justice to grim sympathy for desperate coast-folk.

## Quest Type
Regional / Faction (the wreckers)

## Level Range
3–5. Solo danger: moderate (the wreckers; the coast; the tide).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Investigate the wrecking (lures, salvage, the chain): Investigation DC 13.
- Win a wrecker's trust or turn one: Persuasion/Insight DC 14.
- Catch the wreckers in the act / disable a false-light: Stealth DC 13 / Survival DC 14.
- Bring the wreckers to the moot's justice: Persuasion DC 15.

## Starting Hook
The harbor-moot, a wreck-victim's grieving kin, or Bryd (furious at the broken salt-rites) asks the player to stop the wreckers: ships are dying on the rocks too often, and what washes up is being stripped before the dead are even cold.

## Location
The wreckers' strand, the cove caves, and the Skerries' reefs (`PALE_COAST_SITES.md`, `THE_WRECKERS_CAVES.md`).

## Involved NPCs
The Wreck-Master, the wreckers, Harbor-Reeve Ond Falk (the moot), Bryd (the broken rites), a wreck-victim's kin, Skipper Wenna Roke (an honest counter-witness).

## Involved Factions
Reachward Compact/moot (law), Cinder Ledger (quietly profits from the salvage; Cole), Mourners' (the broken salt-rites).

## Surface Problem
Stop the wrecking and bring the wreckers to account.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The wreckers feed Cole's sea-relic chain (M3) and break the Coast's salt-rites; some are desperate coast-folk the salt-gouge ruined (grim sympathy, not pure villainy). The false lights run out to the Skerries (gated). **Cap at R1/R2: M3 and the human cost; never the Court.**

## Objectives
1. Investigate the wrecking and its chain. 2. Stop it (catch, turn, expose, or break the wreckers). 3. Decide the wreckers' and victims' fates.

## Possible Approaches
- **Lawful:** gather evidence; bring the wreckers to the moot's justice.
- **Investigative:** trace the salvage-chain to Cole (ties to "The Salt-Price").
- **Direct:** disable the false-lights; catch them in the act; break their hold (the caves).
- **Sympathetic:** turn the desperate wreckers (offer them honest work / expose the gouge that drove them).

## Obstacles
The wreckers' violence and numbers; the coast and tide; Cole's quiet protection; the moral grey of desperate folk.

## Important Scenes
- A ship dying on the false-lit rocks, and the wreckers descending on the wreck.
- A drowned victim stripped of even their grave-goods — Bryd's fury.
- A wrecker who'd quit if there were any other way to feed their family.

## Clues
- The false-light wrecking method (Survival; the Skerries link, gated).
- The salvage-chain to Cole (M3; ties to "The Salt-Price").

## Combat Options
The wreckers (Bandit/Thug-like) and the Wreck-Master (Veteran/Spy-like, AC 15, ~45 HP); telegraphed; morale-driven; turnable. The coast and tide are as dangerous as the wreckers. See the dungeon.

## Noncombat Options
Investigation, lawful process, turning the desperate, and disabling the lures all resolve it without a massacre.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Roke and the moot back a careful player; the wreckers can be turned or brought to justice rather than slain; the tide and coast are navigable with care; retreat is open.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Wrackmouth/Cobble Strand. Soft pressure: each storm risks another wreck; the salvage flows to Cole (Monopoly clock).

## Rewards
- The wrecking stopped; the coast and honest fishing safer; the moot's and Bryd's gratitude.
- The salvage-chain exposed (M3; leverage on Cole).
- ~80–120 gp (moot/victims' reward) or turned wreckers as wary contacts.

## Consequences
- **Stopped/justice:** the wild coast is safer; the salt-rites honored; Cole's chain checked.
- **Sympathetic/turned:** the desperate are redeemed (and the gouge that drove them exposed) — a deeper win that implicates Cole.
- **Ignored:** the wrecking worsens, more ships die, the salvage flows to Cole, and the Coast clock advances.

## Failure State
If the player can't stop the wreckers, the wrecking persists but the chain to Cole and the human cost are clear, and the thread reopens (a worse wreck, the moot, Bryd). No dead end.

## Scaling Notes
- L3: investigation/social-weighted; one wrecker cell.
- L5: a Ledger enforcer; the Skerries link surfaces; a harder, sea-bound confrontation.
