## AI Use
A gentle level-1 waypoint quest at Reedford. A sorrowful Remembrance haunts the ford; resolving her is a soft on-ramp to M5 and a thread linking Reedford's haunting to Kettle Bridge's Sunken Tollhouse upstream. Ideal as a first or second session of open-world play.

## Quest Type
Regional / Mystery / Social

## Level Range
1–2. Solo danger: very low (one non-hostile haunting; no required combat).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Get Dob to describe the sighting: Persuasion DC 10.
- Approach/parley the grey woman: Insight/Persuasion DC 12.
- Lay her with a true rite: Religion DC 12.
- Trace her connection upriver: Investigation/Insight DC 13.

## Starting Hook
At Reedford, Ferryman Dob won't cross the ford at dusk — he and two children have seen "a grey woman" standing in the water. The hamlet's first rite has stuttered. They ask the passing player, hopefully, for help.

## Location
Reedford: the ford and ferry, the bank-stone shrine.

## Involved NPCs
Ferryman Dob (witness), Goodwife Nan (village voice), Widow-mourner Sel (the failing rite), the grey woman (the Remembrance — a drowned soul drifting down from Kettle Bridge).

## Involved Factions
Mourners' Circle (the rite), Ashen Wardens (if fetched).

## Surface Problem
Deal, kindly, with the grey woman at the ford.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The grey woman is a Remembrance drifting **down-river from the Sunken Tollhouse** at Kettle Bridge (M5; links the two sites). She is sorrowful, not malevolent — a person caught in the current, headed (wrongly) toward the basin. Reedford's stuttering rite is the leakage reaching this far. A small, clear proof that the crisis is in the water and *moving*.

## Objectives
1. Investigate the sighting (listen to Dob, the children, Sel).
2. Resolve the grey woman (lay her, guide her, or simply understand her).
3. Optionally, trace the haunting upriver (toward Kettle Bridge).

## Possible Approaches
- **Social:** speak with the grey woman; she remembers drowning.
- **Ritual:** a true rite (yourself or a fetched Warden) lays her.
- **Investigation:** follow the river-link to the Tollhouse (M5 chain).
- **Folk:** help Widow Sel steady the bank-stone rite.

## Obstacles
The hamlet's fear; the ford's dusk-danger; the gentle difficulty of *talking to a ghost*.

## Important Scenes
- Dob refusing to ferry at dusk, pointing at the empty water.
- The grey woman in the shallows, calling a name, half-remembering her death.
- The hamlet's relief — or grief — when she's resolved.

## Clues
- **C-M5 (gentle)** — the dead drift down-river toward the basin; the crisis is moving.
- The river-link to the Sunken Tollhouse (a thread the curious player can follow upstream).

## Combat Options
The grey woman (specter/ghost-like, AC 12, ~17 HP) — **non-combat by default**; she does not attack unless attacked. A first, safe demonstration that the dead can be *helped*, not just fought.

## Noncombat Options
Listening, ritual, folk-craft — the entire quest resolves without combat.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
No required fight; the grey woman is sorrowful and slow; a Warden can be fetched from the Ashwalk Rest or Kettle Bridge; retreat is trivial.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest at Goodwife Nan's hearth. Light time pressure: the autumn flood may close the ford, and the haunting worsens slowly if ignored.

## Rewards
- The hamlet's deep gratitude (a friendly waypoint for future travel).
- A gentle M5 clue and a thread toward the Sunken Tollhouse.
- A first, formative experience of *mercy as the answer* — shaping the player's stance toward the dead.

## Consequences
- **Resolve her:** Reedford steadies; the ford reopens; the player learns the crisis travels by water.
- **Ignore:** more drifting dead join her; the hamlet abandons the ford (breaking the central road); the haunting spreads.
- **Attack her:** a needless, sad fight; the village is shaken by the violence.

## Failure State
If unresolved, the haunting persists and grows — a recurring, escalating Reedford problem that eventually closes the ford and reroutes Reach travel onto dangerous fen paths. The M5 clue remains reachable. No dead end.

## Follow-Up Hooks
The Sunken Tollhouse / "The Broken Arch" (the source upstream); the basin drift (Sashe); the failing rites across the Reach.

## Scaling Notes
- L1: exactly as written — gentle and safe.
- L3+: two or three drifting dead at the ford; a Warden needed; a clearer push to investigate the upstream source.
