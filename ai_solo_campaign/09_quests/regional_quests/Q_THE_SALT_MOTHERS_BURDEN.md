## AI Use
Develops PC-H8. The Coast's gentle, honest M5/M2 window: Salt-Mother Bryd's drowned-cairns won't stay quiet, and she teaches the player the salt-and-tide rite while the truth of the waking Lamp comes clear. The Coast's Tilbrook/Loaf-That-Moves equivalent, with Bryd as a warm mentor and the old songs as a gated reward.

## Quest Type
Regional / Mystery (M5/M2 oblique) / Social

## Level Range
2–4. Solo danger: low (one or more layable drowned; the tide).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Learn the salt-and-tide rite from Bryd: Religion DC 12 (she teaches the worthy).
- Settle a drowned-cairn: Religion DC 14 (DC 12 with Bryd).
- Read the drowned's "homing" toward the Lamp: Survival/Insight DC 13.
- Earn Bryd's old songs (gated): Persuasion/Insight DC 15.

## Starting Hook
Salt-Mother Bryd, gaunt and grave to the bone, asks the player's help: the drowned-cairns won't hold, the sea sends its dead back faster than she can sing them down, and she can't keep the strand at peace alone.

## Location
Cobble Strand — the strand, the cairns, the Tide-Shrine; the Lamp headland in view.

## Involved NPCs
Salt-Mother Bryd (Mourners; mentor), the drowned and their families, Net-wife Senna, young Ferec (at the edges).

## Surface Problem
Help settle the drowned the rites can no longer hold.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The drowned "come home" toward the waking Drowned Lamp — the maritime node leaking (M5 oblique). Bryd's old songs hold "the Lamps kept the drowned for the Concord" (M5/M6 oblique). **Cap at R1: a source and a pattern, the Lamp as a cold waking shape; never the harvest mechanism, never the Court.**

## Objectives
1. Learn the salt-and-tide rite. 2. Settle the restless drowned. 3. (Gated) earn Bryd's old songs and the read on the Lamp.

## Possible Approaches
- **Ritual:** learn and perform the salt-and-tide rite (the right way; the Coast's signature).
- **Compassion:** help the drowned's grieving families (Senna, others).
- **Investigation:** trace the drowned's homing toward the Lamp (M5/M2).

## Obstacles
The rising drowned-tide; the families' grief; the Lamp's pull resisting the rite; the cold sea.

## Important Scenes
- A drowned man returning on the tide to his own cairn, and the village's grave, unsurprised welcome.
- Bryd teaching the player the salt-and-tide rite by lamplight.
- Bryd, weighing whether to sing the player the old songs — and what they say about the Lamp.

## Clues
- The drowned's homing toward the Lamp (C-PC-M5-1).
- Bryd's old songs (gated oblique M5/M6 — "the Lamps kept the drowned").

## Combat Options
None required. A restless drowned defends weakly only if disrespected (Specter-like, AC 12, ~22 HP); the salt-and-tide rite is the answer. Telegraphed.

## Noncombat Options
The entire quest is ritual, compassion, and investigation.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Bryd leads the rites; the drowned are tragic, not monstrous; Cobble Strand is safe; the Lamp itself stays gated to a later quest.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Cobble Strand. Soft pressure: each tide brings more drowned home; the cairns "won't hold" (Coast clock nudge), no hard fail.

## Rewards
- The salt-and-tide rite (a real tool for the whole Coast thereafter).
- Bryd's deep trust and (gated) her old songs (oblique M5/M6).
- The Strand's gratitude; ~25–40 gp or goods.
- The on-ramp to "What the Lamp Holds."

## Consequences
- **Success:** the strand quiets; Bryd opens up; the player can keep the rites.
- **Ignored:** the cairns fail, the strand is haunted toward abandonment, and the grieving (Ferec) turn to the Gravecallers; the Coast clock advances.

## Failure State
If a rite doesn't take, the drowned joins a strand-haunting — sad, not catastrophic — and Bryd tries again with the player. No dead end.

## Follow-Up Hooks
"What the Lamp Holds" (PC-H3); "The Cove Caves" (PC-H10); "The Songs of the Tide" (PC-H11); Bryd's link to the Reach/Vale Mourners (M6 path, gated).

## Scaling Notes
- L2: one cairn, gentle; the rite the focus.
- L4: multiple drowned, a clearer Lamp-ward homing, a wrathful drowned if disrespected.
