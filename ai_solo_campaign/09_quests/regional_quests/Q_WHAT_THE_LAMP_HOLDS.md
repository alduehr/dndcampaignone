## AI Use
Develops PC-H3/H7/H9. The Coast's keystone quest: explore the Drowned Lamp and learn the lighthouses were maritime harvest-nodes (M2/M5/M6 oblique fragments). Gated to level 4–6 with the tide as the core challenge; the Lamp-Keeper is the climax. Rivals (Remnant, Cole) contest the prize.

## Quest Type
Regional / Mystery (M2/M5/M6 oblique fragment) / Dungeon

## Level Range
4–6. Solo danger: moderate-to-high (the tide; the Lamp-Keeper). Telegraphed.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Time the tide for the lower node: Survival DC 14.
- Read the Lamp's Script: Concord Script (reader required).
- Settle the Lamp-Keeper by rite: Religion DC 15 (DC 13 with Bryd's salt-and-tide aid).
- Reach the Keeper's records: Investigation DC 14.

## Starting Hook
The waking Lamp draws the player: Bryd's dread ("the Lamp's waking"), the Remnant's scholarly hunger to study it, the player's own curiosity from the drowned-tide, or Cole's offer to buy "anything off the Lamp." The Lamp Stair climbs from Cobble Strand to the ruin.

## Location
The Drowned Lamp (`THE_DROWNED_LAMP.md`), above Cobble Strand.

## Involved NPCs
Salt-Mother Bryd (the salt-and-tide rite; the honest read), a Remnant scholar (wants the Lamp), Cole's salvagers (want the relics), the Lamp-Keeper (climax).

## Involved Factions
Mourners'/Bryd (let the drowned rest), Concord Remnant (study it; Reclamation), Cinder Ledger (strip its relics; Monopoly clock).

## Surface Problem
What is the Drowned Lamp, and why is it waking?

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The Lamp was a **maritime harvest-node** — it kept the sea's drowned for the Concord — and is warming as the keystone reawakens, pulling the drowned home (M2/M5/M6 oblique). **Strictly gated: fragments and danger only; never the harvest's full purpose pre-Act-3, never the Hollow Court. The Sea-Shaft is off-limits/lethal.**

## Objectives
1. Reach the Lamp and time the tide. 2. Read its truth (Script, records, the Keeper). 3. Survive/settle the Keeper; decide who gets the node and the relics.

## Possible Approaches
- **Ritual/Mourner:** go with Bryd's salt-and-tide rite (the safest, richest path — she aids against the Keeper).
- **Scholarly/Remnant:** ally with the Remnant scholar (opens the Script, advances their interest).
- **Salvage/Ledger:** strip the relics for Cole (coin, complicity, the Monopoly clock).
- **Solo/curious:** the player's own delve, tide-timed.

## Obstacles
The tide (the core challenge — the lower node floods); the cold and leakage; the Lamp-Keeper; the moral weight of the kept drowned; the rival factions.

## Important Scenes
- The lamp-room with no lamp — *it was never a lighthouse* (M2 shape).
- The tide rising in the Settling Hall, forcing the player out.
- The Lamp-Keeper's challenge: fight, rite, or parley.

## Clues
- The shrine-focus and Script (M2/M3/M5 fragment; the maritime node).
- The keeper's records (M6 oblique fragment; conditional).
- A sea-relic (M3).

## Combat Options
The Lamp-Keeper (Wraith/ghost-like, AC 13, ~52 HP at L5) and drowned dead — but the salt-and-tide rite or parley resolves the Keeper better and yields richer clues. The Keeper is bound to the node; retreat (and the tide) ends any fight. See the dungeon.

## Noncombat Options
Tide-timing, Script-reading, the rite, and parley carry the quest.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Bryd as rite-aid; the tide forces the player out (built-in valve); open retreat up the Lamp Stair; the Sea-Shaft telegraphed lethal so a low-level PC can't over-reach.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Cobble Strand below / the upper tower at low tide. No rest below. The tide is a hard, recurring clock within the delve.

## Rewards
- M2/M5/M6 oblique fragments (major, act-capped clues).
- Bryd's deep trust / a keeper's tide-token (coastal rite-aid) if befriended.
- Remnant favor or Ledger coin if allied (with strings).
- Sea-relics (M3; the recurring moral choice).

## Consequences
- **With Bryd/Mourners:** the secret is shared, the drowned respected; the node stays controlled.
- **For the Remnant:** they begin working the node (a coastal Reclamation site; a future problem).
- **For the Ledger:** Cole strips the relics (Monopoly clock advances).
- **Forced/reckless:** the node leaks worse; the drowned-tide spikes.

## Failure State
If the player can't reach the lower node (tide, the Keeper), they keep the M2 telegraph (no lamp; a cold Concord node; the drowned homing) and the thread reopens. If they flee the Keeper ahead of the tide, they escape with a fragment and a thin-touch scare. No dead end.

## Scaling Notes
- L4: soften the Keeper; lean on the tide and the tragedy.
- L5 (intended): as written.
- L6+: the node louder; rivals already inside; the Sea-Shaft gated regardless.
