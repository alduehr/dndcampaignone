## AI Use
A Warden-faction quest at the Ashwalk Rest. Bitter veteran Warden Pell suspects the order is hiding something about the failures (he's right). A path toward Secret 8 that doesn't require Othetha's late-arc trust — and a way to advance or slow the Wardens Break clock.

## Quest Type
Regional / Faction / Mystery

## Level Range
2–4. Solo danger: low-to-moderate (Warden work on the dangerous roads; no required combat in town).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Get Pell to share his suspicions: Persuasion/Insight DC 12 (lower if you've helped the Wardens).
- Investigate the pattern of failures (M5): Investigation DC 13.
- Avoid provoking Othetha's guard: Insight DC 14 (she watches anyone probing the secret).
- Help a road-rite that's failing worse near water: Religion DC 13.

## Starting Hook
At the Ashwalk Rest, Warden Pell — gaunt, half-drunk, and bitter — mutters that "the order knows why the dead won't rest, and won't say." He'll trust a player who shares the work and the danger. He wants help proving the elders are hiding something.

## Location
The Ashwalk Rest and the Concord roads (failing road-rites; the pattern).

## Involved NPCs
Warden Pell (the doubter — secondary), Hospitaller Sirrin (keeps the faith), Warden Sister Othetha (the secret-keeper — major; watchful), Brother Hale (also quietly doubting — major).

## Involved Factions
Ashen Wardens (their internal crisis of faith).

## Surface Problem
Help a bitter Warden prove the order is hiding something.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The order **was founded to keep the harvest paused** and guard the seal; only Othetha locally knows (Secret 8). Pell senses the secret without grasping it — the failures get *worse near water* (M5), the rites are deliberately *incomplete*, and the elders forbid asking why. Pursuing Pell's doubt is a real, alternate path toward Secret 8 — but pushing too hard alerts Othetha, who is bound by oath and frightened.

## Objectives
1. Earn Pell's trust (share the work).
2. Investigate the pattern of failures (M5; the rites' strangeness).
3. Decide what to do with the suspicion (press it, steady Pell, or take it to Othetha).

## Possible Approaches
- **Investigation:** map where and how rites fail (M5; the rites are *incomplete*).
- **Social:** draw out Pell, Hale, or (carefully) Othetha.
- **Service:** help on the roads to earn the order's trust (and slow the Wardens Break clock).
- **Confrontation:** push Othetha directly (risky; she deflects, but a trust-bond may form).

## Obstacles
The order's "don't ask why" wall; Othetha's guarded oath; Pell's volatility (he may go too far); the danger of the road-rites themselves.

## Important Scenes
- Pell, deep in his cups, half-confessing his heresy.
- A road-rite failing worse near a Star-Stone or the river (M5 in action).
- Othetha watching the player too closely — the secret-keeper sensing a probe.

## Clues
- **C-M5-4** (rites fail worse near water — a Warden's observation; Secret 8 seed).
- The rites are *deliberately incomplete* (a strangeness pointing at the order's true purpose).
- An early, partial line toward C-M6-2 (Othetha's eventual confession) — **fragment only; the full confession is Act 3, trust-gated.**

## Combat Options
None required in town. Helping road-rites may mean laying a single telegraphed Remembrance (specter-like, AC 12, ~22 HP; true rite resolves it). Solo-safe.

## Noncombat Options
The quest is investigative, social, and service-based; no combat path is required.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The Ashwalk Rest is a sanctuary; Wardens assist on the roads; no forced combat; the secret is gated so the player can't blunder into the apex truth too early.

## Rest And Time Pressure
The Rest is the Reach's best non-town safe rest and minor healing. Time pressure: the Wardens Break clock — every road-rite the player helps with slows it; neglect advances it.

## Rewards
- **A real path toward Secret 8** (the seal/true purpose) — fragments now, the full confession later.
- Warden trust (resupply, sanctuary, Hale as a companion, Othetha's eventual confidence).
- A slowed Wardens Break clock if the player aids the order.

## Consequences
- **Steady Pell + serve the order:** the Wardens hold longer; Othetha begins to trust the player (toward her Act 3 confession).
- **Push too hard:** Pell breaks publicly (a scandal); Othetha clams up or is endangered; the Wardens Break clock jumps.
- **Ignore:** Pell's bitterness festers; the order frays unaided; the seal weakens unnoticed.

## Failure State
If Pell goes too far or Othetha is alienated, Secret 8 remains reachable (the Caradril archives, the Gravecaller witness, the old songs), and a chastened player may rebuild Warden trust. No dead end — but a public Warden scandal accelerates their collapse.

## Follow-Up Hooks
Othetha's confession (Act 3; C-M6-2/C-M9-2); the basin shore (the seal she guards); the old songs (Wend; M6); the Wardens Break endgame (the seal's fate).

## Scaling Notes
- L2: gentle; fragments only.
- L4: the Wardens Break clock is advanced (Wardens refusing the Greyfens); Pell is on the edge; Othetha is closer to her breaking point (a fuller, but still gated, Secret 8 fragment).
