## AI Use
Develops PC-H21. A small, intimate, low-level quest: a drowned fisher has "come home wrong," and his family won't let the Mourners settle him — they want him back. The player must navigate a grieving household, a wrong-come-back at the hearth, and a Gravecaller offering the family false hope. A tight, human first-Coast quest that teaches the salt-and-tide rite, the cult-door, and the cost of refusing the dead their rest. Pairs with "The Drowned-Bell."

## Quest Type
Regional / Personal (a household) / Mystery (gentle)

## Level Range
2–3. Solo danger: low (one wrong-come-back; one grieving family; a cult-tempter). Intimate, not a clearance.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Read what's wrong with the returned man (a wrong-come-back, not the man): Insight/Medicine DC 13.
- Reach the grieving family / win them to the truth: Persuasion DC 13 (DC 15 against their hope).
- Perform or assist the salt-and-tide rite to settle him: Religion DC 13 (DC 15 if the family resists).
- See through the Gravecaller's false comfort: Insight DC 13.
- Calm the wrong-come-back without harm: Persuasion/Wis DC 13.

## Starting Hook
In Cobble Strand, a drowned fisher came home on the tide three nights ago — and got up, and walked back to his own door. His widow and son have taken him in and barred the Mourners from the house: he sits by the cold hearth, he knows their names, and they will not give him up. Salt-Mother Bryd, Net-wife Senna, or Headman Strand asks the player to help — gently — before it ends in grief or worse.

## Location
A fisher's cottage in Cobble Strand; the Tide-Shrine; the cove and tide-line (`COBBLE_STRAND.md`, `PALE_COAST_SITES.md`).

## Involved NPCs
The drowned fisher (the wrong-come-back), his widow and son (the grieving family), Salt-Mother Bryd (the rite; sanctuary), Net-wife Senna (kin; watching for her own drowned son), a Gravecaller offering the family "a way to keep him," Headman Strand.

## Involved Factions
Mourners' Circle (the rite; the truth that the dead must rest), Gravecallers (the cult-door; "you don't have to let him go").

## Surface Problem
Help a family whose drowned man has come home wrong — settle him, or lose them to false hope and danger.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
He is a wrong-come-back — a botched, leaked return, worse as the drowned-tide rises (oblique, maritime M5; the warming nodes pulling the drowned "home" faster than they can be settled). He is not the man; he is a hungering echo wearing him, and he will turn on the family as he hollows out. The Gravecaller knows this and offers the family a "binding" that only prolongs the horror and recruits the grieving. **Cap at R1: the player learns the dead are coming back wrong and worse, and that the cult preys on it — never the harvest, the node, or the Court.**

## Objectives
1. Understand what came home. 2. Reach the grieving family. 3. Settle the wrong-come-back (rite or, if forced, gently). 4. Keep the family from the Gravecaller's false comfort.

## Possible Approaches
- **Compassion:** sit with the family, earn their trust, and let them choose to let him rest.
- **The rite:** learn/assist Bryd's salt-and-tide rite to settle him with dignity.
- **Investigation:** show the family (and yourself) what he truly is now, gently.
- **Confront the tempter:** expose the Gravecaller's false "binding" before the family takes it.

## Obstacles
The family's grief and hope; the wrong-come-back's worsening hunger; the Gravecaller's plausible, kind-sounding lie; the danger of forcing the issue (a panicked family, a turning revenant).

## Important Scenes
- The drowned man at the cold hearth, knowing the player's name in a wet, wrong voice.
- The widow's plea — "he's still my husband, he came back to us."
- The moment the echo slips — a flicker of hunger — and the family must finally see.

## Clues
- **C-PC-M5-1** (reuse) — the drowned come back, and come back *wrong*, more as the tide rises (Insight/witnessed). Oblique, maritime M5.
- The Gravecaller's "binding" is the cult-door, not a cure (Insight; ties to PC-H12 "A Word with the Drowned").

## Combat Options
The wrong-come-back if it turns (Specter/Wraith-like, AC 13, ~30–36 HP; telegraphed by the slipping echo; settle-able by the rite rather than fought) and the Gravecaller if confronted (Cultist/Acolyte-like, AC 12, ~16 HP, social-first). Built to be resolved without a brawl in the family's home. See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/PALE_COAST_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Compassion, the rite, and persuasion carry the quest; the best resolution is the family choosing to let him rest.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Bryd backs the rite and offers sanctuary; the wrong-come-back is one telegraphed foe, not a swarm; the family can be won with patience; retreat is always open; the cult-tempter is talk, not ambush.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest at the Tide-Shrine and in the village. Pressure: each day the echo hollows the man further and grows more dangerous; the longer the family clings, the worse the parting — and the more the Gravecaller's "binding" tempts them.

## Rewards
- The family at peace and the man settled with dignity; Bryd's and the village's gratitude; the **salt-and-tide rite** learned (a durable Coast tool).
- The oblique M5 read and the cult-door recognized (sets up PC-H12).
- ~30–50 gp or blessed salt, a fisher-family's standing welcome, and Senna's trust.

## Consequences
## If The Player Succeeds
The man rests, the family grieves cleanly, the Gravecaller is denied a recruit; the village trusts the player; Bryd opens her deeper threads (the old songs, the Lamp).
## If The Player Partially Succeeds
The man is settled but the grieving family blames the player, or takes a small step toward the cult — a thread left open.
## If The Player Ignores It
The wrong-come-back hollows and turns on the family (tragedy), or the Gravecaller "binds" him and claims the household; the cult-current strengthens and the Coast clock advances.
## If The Player Uses Excessive Force
Cutting down the man in front of his family without the rite or their consent makes an enemy of the household and the village and feeds the Gravecaller's grievance.

## Failure State
If the player can't settle him in time, he turns and is put down hard or the cult claims the family — a grief, not a dead end; the wrong-come-back pattern and the cult-door are now known, and the larger Coast threads (the Bell, the Lamp) reopen.

## Scaling Notes
- L2: a gentle, social quest; one slow wrong-come-back; the cult merely whispers.
- L3: the echo turns faster and harder; the Gravecaller actively works the family; ties into "A Word with the Drowned" (PC-H12) and "The Drowned-Bell."
