## AI Use
Develops PC-H1/H5. The master Coast quest and the upstream end of the Saltmargin salt thread: break or expose Factor Maris Cole's salt-gouge and the sea-relic trade that funds it. Connects the whole frontier's salt-grievance to the Ledger's Monopoly clock (M3). Social/investigation-led.

## Quest Type
Regional / Faction (vs. Cinder Ledger)

## Level Range
2–4. Solo danger: low-moderate (Cole's enforcers; the Wreckers' Caves if pursued).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Rally/organize the salt-workers: Persuasion DC 14.
- Get Cole's salvage-ledger (the sea-relic proof): Investigation DC 13 / Stealth DC 13 (via the Weigh-House or the Wreckers' Caves).
- Force/expose Cole: Persuasion DC 20 (DC 13 with the ledger).
- Negotiate a fairer price through the moot: Persuasion DC 15.

## Starting Hook
The salt-workers (near a strike) or the harbor-moot ask the player to do something about Cole's price — the gouge is starving the Coast and the whole frontier pays it. An honest skipper hints the gouge hides something worse: where the relics come from.

## Location
Wrackmouth (the Weigh-House, the Moot-Stair, the Wrack) and the Wreckers' Caves.

## Involved NPCs
Factor Maris Cole (Ledger; antagonist), Harbor-Reeve Ond Falk (the moot), a salt-worker leader, an honest skipper (Wenna Roke), Fence "Cuttle" Dree.

## Involved Factions
Cinder Ledger (the gouge and the sea-relic trade), Mourners'/salt-workers (gouged), Compact/moot (governs).

## Surface Problem
Bring down (or fairly fix) the salt-price.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The gouge is real *and* a cover: Cole funds and profits from the sea-relic trade (Remembrance relics; M3) salvaged off the Lamp and the wrecks, feeding Vyre's Monopoly clock. The salt panic (as at Saltmargin) inflates value while salt only *slows* failing rites. **Cap at R1/R2: the player can grasp "someone is collecting the dead's relics for profit" (M3) without the full machine; never the Court.**

## Objectives
1. Document or organize against the gouge. 2. Trace the sea-relic trade (the salvage-ledger). 3. Break, expose, or leverage Cole.

## Possible Approaches
- **Organize:** rally the salt-workers and moot to force a fair price.
- **Investigate:** get the salvage-ledger (Weigh-House or Wreckers' Caves) — the M3 proof and the Monopoly thread.
- **Expose:** take the relic-trade to the Mourners, the moot, or Caradril.
- **Leverage:** use the ledger to force Cole's hand (a fair price, or your own cut — a moral fork).

## Obstacles
Cole's charm, deniability, and enforcers; the Coast's dependence on the salt-trade; the wreckers guarding the relic-chain.

## Important Scenes
- A near-riot at the Weigh-House as a crew comes home empty and broke.
- Cole, all generous warmth, offering the player coin to bring him "anything off the Lamp."
- A night in the Wrack, the salvage-ledger in reach, the wreckers between.

## Clues
- The manufactured salt-panic (M5 context; salt only slows the dead).
- Cole's salvage-ledger (M3; sea-relics by wreck/shrine; the Monopoly clock's coastal arm).

## Combat Options
Cole's salt-toughs/wreckers (Thug/Bandit-like, AC 11–13, ~32 HP) if the player pushes; telegraphed, avoidable, morale-driven. See the Wreckers' Caves.

## Noncombat Options
Organizing, investigation, exposure, and negotiation carry the quest.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The salt-workers and an honest skipper back a careful player; the ledger is scoutable; Cole prefers ruin to violence; retreat is open.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Wrackmouth/Cobble Strand. Soft pressure: each week Cole ships more relics south (Monopoly clock) and the salt-workers edge toward a strike that would choke the frontier's salt.

## Rewards
- A fairer salt-price (the Coast and the frontier's rites eased) and the salt-workers' loyalty.
- The salvage-ledger (M3; major leverage and a Caradril-bound thread).
- ~80–120 gp or a moot/worker reward; or coin if the player takes Cole's cut (at a moral cost).

## Consequences
- **Broke/exposed:** the gouge eases; the relic-trade is checked; the Monopoly clock slows; Cole an enemy.
- **Leveraged for self:** coin and power, lost standing with the workers and Mourners.
- **Ignored:** the gouge tightens, the relic-trade flows south, the salt-workers strike, and the whole frontier's rites worsen.

## Failure State
If Cole evades exposure, the player keeps the salvage-ledger / experiential M3 truth, and the thread reopens via the Mourners, the strike, or Caradril. No dead end.

## Scaling Notes
- L2: organizing/investigation-weighted; enforcers prefer threats.
- L4: a Ledger enforcer; the wreckers contest the ledger; the strike imminent.
