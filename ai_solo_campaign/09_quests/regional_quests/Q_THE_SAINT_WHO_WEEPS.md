## AI Use
Develops AV-H8. The investigation that exposes the Vale's central denial: the famous "rest easy at Saint Veddow's" promise is failing, and the keepers hide it. Delivers M1/M5 echoes and the M2 *telegraph* of the node — and leads naturally to "The Sealed Door."

## Quest Type
Regional / Mystery (M1/M5 echo; M2 telegraph)

## Level Range
2–4. Solo danger: low (town); the tomb itself is a separate, gated quest.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Get a keeper to admit the rests are failing: Insight/Persuasion DC 15 (DC 12 with a pilgrim's plea or evidence).
- Notice the lower-cell failures hidden from pilgrims: Investigation DC 13.
- Calm a near-panic on the hill: Persuasion DC 15.
- Sense the wrong-cold off the sealed door: Wis DC 12.

## Starting Hook
A distraught pilgrim's "rested" father has come back wrong; the shrine-keepers hush it. The pilgrim (or Mother Sennet herself, cracking) begs the player to find out why the saint's promise is failing.

## Location
Saint Veddow's Rest — the Shrine Crown, the Lower Cells, Relic Row.

## Involved NPCs
A frightened pilgrim (minor), Mother Sennet (shrine-keeper; hiding it), Warden Sister Adwen (knows more than she says), Sub-Lector Oneth Vael (circling).

## Surface Problem
Why is the saint failing to settle the dead?

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The shrine is a capped Concord node beginning to warm (M2); its "rest" was real Concord-work, and as the keystone reawakens, it fails (M1/M5 echo). The keepers hide it to save the pilgrim trade. **R1-capped at low level: a source and a pattern, the node as a cold shape under the hill — not the harvest.**

## Objectives
1. Confirm the rests are failing. 2. Find where the keepers hide it (the Lower Cells). 3. Trace the wrongness to the sealed door (the node telegraph).

## Possible Approaches
- **Social:** press the keepers, the hosteler, the pilgrims.
- **Investigative:** find the Lower Cells and the hidden failures.
- **Spiritual:** witness a rite "not take" at the flower-altar.

## Obstacles
The keepers' denial; the pilgrim trade's vested interest; a crowd one failure from panic.

## Important Scenes
- A blessing-rite that visibly "won't take."
- The Lower Cells, where the failed-rest pilgrims are quietly kept.
- The wrong-cold off the sealed inner-tomb door, and Adwen's unspoken dread.

## Clues
- The failing rests (M1/M5 echo).
- The hidden Lower Cells (the cover-up).
- The cold sealed door (C-AV-M2-1; the node telegraph) — leads to "The Sealed Door."

## Combat Options
None required. A failed-rest pilgrim's Remembrance (Specter-like, AC 12, ~22 HP) is layable by a true rite; never an ambush.

## Noncombat Options
Investigation, social, and ritual; no fight needed.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The Warden House for aid; the dead are layable; the crowd is calmable; the tomb itself is gated to a later quest.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in the Warden House and hostels. Soft pressure: each failed rest risks a public panic that would ruin the pilgrim trade and radicalize the Vale.

## Rewards
- M1/M5/M2 clues (the failing promise; the cold node).
- Mother Sennet's or Adwen's trust; a pilgrim's gratitude; ~25–40 gp.
- The on-ramp to "The Sealed Door."

## Consequences
- **Success:** the player understands the shrine is failing and why-ish; trust opens.
- **Public:** if a failure becomes public, the pilgrim trade collapses, beggaring and radicalizing the Vale (Remnant + Gravecaller openings).
- **Ignored:** the keepers' cover-up fails on its own timeline, worse and unguided.

## Failure State
If the keepers stonewall, the player still witnesses a failing rite and the cold door — the M2 telegraph stands and the thread reopens via Adwen, the pilgrim, or a public failure. No dead end.

## Follow-Up Hooks
"The Sealed Door" (AV-H9); "Adwen's Doubt" (AV-H7); "Saint's Bones" (AV-H3); "Crowd of the Faithful" (AV-H10).

## Scaling Notes
- L2: gentle; one hidden failure, one cold-door telegraph.
- L4: multiple failures; a near-panic scene; Adwen closer to breaking her silence.
