## AI Use
A political quest. Bailiff Cray of Kettle Bridge wants to discredit Reeve Coalmont and take her Hollowmere post; he hires the player. A moral-choice quest that intersects the basin-scheme politics (M4) and tests the player's loyalties within the Compact.

## Quest Type
Regional / Faction / Personal (moral)

## Level Range
2–4. Solo danger: low (social/investigation; consequences are political, not lethal).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Read Cray's true aim: Insight DC 13.
- Dig dirt on Coalmont (there's little): Investigation DC 16 (she's clean — fabrication required).
- Turn the job back on Cray / mediate: Persuasion DC 15.
- Spot how Reke benefits from Compact infighting: Insight DC 14.

## Starting Hook
Bailiff Cray, smooth and regulation-bound, hires the player for "an audit" of Reeve Coalmont's competence — really a hunt for ammunition to take her job. He pays well and frames it as serving good order.

## Location
Kettle Bridge (Cray's seat) and Hollowmere (Coalmont's). The Compact's politics.

## Involved NPCs
Bailiff Cray (grey patron), Reeve Coalmont (honest target), Sergeant Hesk (Coalmont's loyal watch), Clerk Pevin Oss (records), Councilor Reke (the unseen beneficiary).

## Involved Factions
Reachward Compact (its internal politics).

## Surface Problem
Find (or manufacture) cause to replace Coalmont with Cray.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
Coalmont is clean — the only honest senior official and the player's potential staunchest lawful ally (and a quiet M4 ally against Reke). **Reke benefits from any Compact infighting:** a weakened or distracted Coalmont can't oppose the basin scheme. Cray is an unwitting tool of that dynamic. Helping Cray indirectly serves the Hollow Court.

## Objectives
1. Investigate Coalmont (and discover she's clean).
2. Decide: serve Cray, protect Coalmont, expose the dynamic, or play both.

## Possible Approaches
- **Serve Cray:** fabricate or exaggerate (a betrayal with consequences).
- **Protect Coalmont:** warn her, ally with her, turn the job around.
- **Mediate:** broker a truce that keeps both useful.
- **Expose:** show that the infighting serves Reke (a sharp M4 insight).

## Obstacles
Cray's coin and plausibility; Coalmont's lack of dirt (forcing a moral line); Hesk's protectiveness; the temptation of an easy payday.

## Important Scenes
- Cray laying out the "audit" as obvious public service.
- Coalmont, exhausted and honest, unaware she's being undermined.
- The realization that this squabble is exactly what Reke needs.

## Clues
- **C-M4 support:** Compact infighting weakens the anti-basin bloc — a structural M4 insight (who benefits?).
- Coalmont as a clean ally (Secret 7 confirmation).

## Combat Options
None by design. If the player threatens or is threatened, the watch (Hesk; Veteran-like) responds, but this is a political quest, not a fight.

## Noncombat Options
The entire quest is social, investigative, and moral. No combat path.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
No lethal risk; the worst outcome is a political consequence (an enemy, a lost ally). The player can disengage at any point.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rests in either town. Time pressure: the basin vote looms — distracting Coalmont now (per Cray) helps Reke win it (advances the Basin clock).

## Rewards
- **Protect Coalmont:** her lasting alliance (the player's best lawful ally; M4 partner) and Hesk's respect.
- **Serve Cray:** coin and a Kettle Bridge patron — but a hidden assist to the Hollow Court and a weakened Coalmont.
- **Expose the dynamic:** a real M4 insight and the gratitude of the honest bloc.

## Consequences
- **Protect/expose:** Coalmont stays strong; the anti-basin bloc holds; Reke is quietly thwarted.
- **Serve Cray:** Coalmont weakened or removed; the Basin clock jumps; the player is complicit (and may not know it).
- **Ignore:** Cray finds another tool; the infighting proceeds without the player; Coalmont distracted.

## Failure State
There is no hard fail — only which way the politics tip. If the player botches it, Coalmont may distrust them (losing a key ally), but M4 remains solvable via records, surveillance, and the Reeve's own suspicions.

## Follow-Up Hooks
"The Reeve's Trust" (Hook 5; allying with Coalmont against Reke); the basin vote; Reke's exposure (M4/Secret 9); Cray's continued ambition.

## Scaling Notes
- L2: pure social.
- L4: Cray escalates to dirty tricks (planted evidence, a bribed witness) the player must counter; the basin vote becomes imminent.
