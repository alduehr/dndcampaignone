## AI Use

The authored **major campaign questlines** — large, multi-session arcs that carry the level 1–20 story and the major faction/regional plots. Each is a spine the AI DM can run across many sessions, NOT a railroad: every one has multiple approaches, failure states that continue play, and ignore-consequences. Load the relevant questline when the player commits to its thread. **DM-Only sections are never surfaced to the player.** The apex truth (Hollow Court / the harvest / the Under-Shrine / the Drowned Keystone) must stay DM-only and is gated to Act 3–4 per `../11_mysteries_and_secrets/REVELATION_MAP.md`.

Quest IDs: `Q_MAJOR_NNN_SLUG`. Where a questline subsumes existing Stage-1–8 quests, those are listed under "Component Quests" — the major quest is the connective arc, not a replacement.

# Q_MAJOR_001 — The Body That Came Back Wrong

**Act / Level:** Act 1 / 1–4. Solo danger: low→moderate. **Quest Type:** Investigation / mystery (campaign opener).
**Region / Start:** Sundering Reach, Hollowmere.
**Secrecy:** mixed. **Lead factions:** Mourners' Circle, Ashen Wardens (Cinder Ledger and Reachward Compact circle).
**Primary NPCs:** Wren Hollowmere (Brevin), Tomas Brevin, Grandmother Wend, Iola Wend, Warden Brother Hale, Warden Sister Othetha, Reeve Marda Coalmont, Sefra Quick.
**Secondary NPCs:** Halla Coalmont, Old Sashe, Councilor Aldous Reke, Factor Domic Sael.
**Component quests:** `regional_quests/Q_THE_FAILING_FUNERAL.md`, `act_1_quests/Q_ACT1_MOURNER_SALT_AND_SONG.md`, `act_1_quests/Q_ACT1_WARDEN_THE_TRUE_RITE.md`.

### Starting Hooks
- The default campaign opener (Hook 1): a funeral fails publicly on Mourner's Green; the buried woman is seen walking. The town wants it quietly fixed.
- A grieving husband (Tomas) begs a stranger for help no priest will give.
- A Mourner apprentice (Iola) pulls the player aside: "The eldest says it isn't the first."

### Player-Facing Premise
A woman was buried with full rite and has come back — not as a monster, but wrong: she remembers dying and clings to something unfinished. The rite "won't take." The player is asked to find out why and to settle her, while the town's leaders all want different things: silence, salt, scholarship, or profit.

### DM-Only Truth
Wren is a partial Remembrance-revenant — leakage from the reawakening drowned shrine given form (M1, fed by M5). Her rite fails because the machine under the basin is *pulling* at her. This is the campaign's first thread of M0. **Do not name the shrine, the harvest, or the Hollow Court here.** Reke is already quietly steering the basin scheme; Sefra Quick is a Hollow Court cutout who "happened" to point the player at this job (M8 seed) — neither is exposed in Act 1.

### Objectives
1. Learn what is actually wrong with Wren (M1 — needs ~2 clue paths).
2. Choose how to settle her: a true rite, a Mourner's song, a Gravecaller's last word, or refuse and protect her.
3. (Optional) Notice that this is not isolated — rites are failing across the Reach, worse near the water (M5 seed).

### Possible Approaches
- **Social:** Talk with Wren; she is lucid and frightened, not hostile. Persuasion/Insight DC 12.
- **Ritual:** Have a Warden examine her (Religion/Medicine DC 13) — "something holds her."
- **Folk:** Grandmother Wend reads the signs from the old songs.
- **Physical:** Examine her grave; soil and salt drift toward the basin (Investigation/Survival DC 12).
- **Forbidden:** A Gravecaller offers to ask her directly what she wants (opens M5/M3 early).

### Important Scenes
- The failed funeral on Mourner's Green; the crowd's fear.
- A quiet conversation with Wren in the Brevin home — she is the heart of the quest.
- The factions' competing offers: Othetha wants it sealed and silent; Wend wants it mourned right; Reke wants it forgotten; the Ledger wants the "relic" angle.

### Clues (cross-ref CLUE_INDEX)
C-M1-1 (Wren, social), C-M1-2 (Warden exam), C-M1-3 (Wend's songs), C-M1-4 (grave drift); seeds C-M5-2.

### Combat Options
Optional only. If a rite is botched or a Gravecaller pushes too far, Wren can flare into a **Restless Remembrance** (Act 1 threat profile, `ACT_1_THREATS.md`) — telegraphed, non-lethal-by-default, calmable. No required fight.

### Noncombat Options
The entire quest is solvable through conversation, ritual, and choice. Settling Wren peacefully is the intended spine.

### Solo-Play Safety Valves
Wren is not a monster; she warns before she flares. The Ashwalk Rest and the Drowned Lantern are safe rests. Any single faction's help suffices; no path is mandatory.

### Rest And Time Pressure
Soft clock: each day Wren is unsettled, the town's fear rises and the Mourners' clock ticks. No hard timer.

### Rewards
~30–60 gp or goods; Mourners' goodwill (toward Wend's trust / Secret 15); a Warden contact (Hale); the husband Tomas as a grateful recurring ally. [STAGE_14_FINALIZE: a minor keepsake-charm if settled by song.]

### Consequences
- **Settled peacefully:** the town exhales; the player is "the one who fixed it" — every faction now courts them. Opens Q_MAJOR_002 and the faction chains.
- **Settled by force:** Wren is destroyed; Tomas is broken; Mourners cool; the Gravecallers mark the player as a destroyer.
- **Refused / protected:** Wren persists as a recurring NPC; the town fears the player; a unique long-thread (Wren as a living clue to M1/M5).
- **Ignored:** Wren wanders to the basin; rites worsen; the opener reappears as a darker crisis hook.
- **Botched:** a public flare injures bystanders; the Compact wants a scapegoat (failure-redirect, not campaign end).

### Failure State
If the player cannot settle Wren, she drifts basin-ward and the problem spreads to a second family (new hook). The mystery is not blocked — every clue path remains open elsewhere.

### Follow-Up Hooks
Q_MAJOR_002 (the basin); Q_MAJOR_005 (the Gravecallers, if contacted); Q_MAJOR_006 (the Wardens' secret); Sefra's "patron" thread (Q_MAJOR_007 seed).

### Scaling Notes
L1: pure social/ritual. L3–4: add a second failed rite, a Ledger relic-buyer at the graveside, and a stronger flare.

### State Updates
On resolution: ACTIVE_QUESTS, NPC_MEMORY (Wren, Tomas, Wend, Hale, Othetha), FACTION_STATE (Mourners/Wardens shift by approach), KNOWN_CLUES (M1 clues), CONSEQUENCES, WORLD_CLOCKS (Mourners / Wardens Break).

# Q_MAJOR_002 — What Rises in the Basin

**Act / Level:** Act 1–2 / 3–6. Solo danger: moderate. **Quest Type:** Investigation / political / exploration.
**Region / Start:** Sundering Reach, Hollowmere → basin shore.
**Secrecy:** mixed. **Lead factions:** Reachward Compact (Cinder Ledger funds it; Ashen Wardens resist).
**Primary NPCs:** Reeve Marda Coalmont, Councilor Aldous Reke, Clerk Pevin Oss, Old Sashe, Warden Sister Othetha, Factor Domic Sael.
**Component quests:** `regional_quests/Q_THE_REED_HOLMS.md`, `regional_quests/Q_SASHES_WARNING.md`, the `REACHWARD_COMPACT/` chain (QC1–QC4).

### Starting Hooks
- The Compact is pushing a "water-rights" works project in the basin; families on the Reed Holms face eviction.
- Old Sashe warns that the dead are drifting toward the water — and something under it is rising.
- A councilor (Reke) hires the player to "survey what's surfacing."

### Player-Facing Premise
Someone on the council is determined to drain and "develop" the drowned basin. The justification keeps changing. People are being pushed off their land, a fen-guide swears the dead are being pulled waterward, and a ruin is surfacing in the shallows. The player must find out what is really being uncovered — and who benefits.

### DM-Only Truth
Reke (a Hollow Court agent — **never named as such in Act 1–2**) is engineering the basin works to reopen the drowned shrine, the harvest's keystone node (M2/M4). The "water rights" filing is fabricated; Pevin Oss's records don't support it. The surfacing ruin is a peripheral node; the keystone is deeper and stays sealed until late arc (full build Stage 12).

### Objectives
1. Discover who is pushing the scheme and the true reason (M4 — ~2 paths).
2. Reach and read the surfacing ruin (M2 — needs a Script reader).
3. Decide the fate of the Reed Holms families and the works.

### Possible Approaches
- **Political:** Back Coalmont; trace the votes (Investigation/Insight DC 13).
- **Paper trail:** Get into Pevin Oss's records; the filings don't add up (Investigation DC 14).
- **Surveillance:** Tail Reke to the basin shore at night (Stealth/Perception).
- **Exploration:** Reach the surfacing ruin and bring a Script reader (gates to Q_MAJOR_003).
- **Mercantile:** Press Domic Sael — the Ledger funds it and he grumbles it's "not about water."

### Important Scenes
- The Reed Holms eviction standoff (moral pivot).
- The night the player watches Reke stand alone at the waterline.
- The surfacing ruin's drowned doorway, marked in Concord Script.

### Clues
C-M4-1/2/3/4, C-M2-1/2/3, C-M5-1/2; seeds the M8 "why does this keep pointing at the shrine" pattern.

### Combat Options
Compact watch or hired toughs may try to clear the Holms (Frontier Toughs profile). Basin hazards (cold water, unstable ruin) over monsters. Telegraphed; avoidable by negotiation.

### Noncombat Options
The whole arc is winnable by politics, evidence, and exposure. The eviction can be stopped in council, not by force.

### Solo-Play Safety Valves
Sashe guides the fens safely; the deep basin is explicitly off-limits early (warning signs). Coalmont and Othetha are allies who can shield the player politically.

### Rest And Time Pressure
The Basin Scheme clock (WORLD_CLOCKS) advances if ignored: the works proceed, the eviction happens, the node opens further. Stoppable, not a hard timer.

### Rewards
Compact favor or Coalmont's lasting trust; the Holms families as allies; an early, partial read of the drowned shrine's nature (a major M2 step); [STAGE_14_FINALIZE: salvage relic from the surfacing ruin].

### Consequences
- **Scheme exposed/stopped:** Reke is checked (but not unmasked); the works halt; the Basin clock pauses; Reke escalates covertly (Q_MAJOR_007 / Hollow Court QH chain advances).
- **Scheme allowed:** the works proceed; the node opens further; the Reach destabilizes; Act 2 darkens.
- **Holms saved:** loyal allies, Mourner/Compact goodwill.
- **Holms lost:** the player can still pursue the truth, but the human cost compounds.

### Failure State
If the player can't stop the scheme, it proceeds — but exposure attempts leave a paper trail others (Remnant, Gravecallers) can pick up. The truth remains reachable.

### Follow-Up Hooks
Q_MAJOR_003 (reading the ruin), Q_MAJOR_007 (who is Reke really serving), Q_MAJOR_028 (the faction war for the shrine).

### Scaling Notes
L3: surveillance and politics. L5–6: armed eviction, a real node-guardian hazard, higher Ledger involvement.

### State Updates
ACTIVE_QUESTS, FACTION_STATE (Compact/Ledger/Wardens), WORLD_CLOCKS (Basin Scheme), NPC_MEMORY (Reke, Coalmont, Oss, Sashe), KNOWN_CLUES (M2/M4/M5), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_MAJOR_003 — The Reader of the Old Script

**Act / Level:** Act 1–3 / 2–10 (long arc). Solo danger: low→moderate. **Quest Type:** Scholarly mystery / faction (Concord Remnant).
**Region / Start:** Sundering Reach (Candlewick / Whispering Cairn) → Caradril (Lantern Reach).
**Secrecy:** mixed. **Lead faction:** Concord Remnant.
**Primary NPCs:** Lector Briss, Sub-Lector Tamsin Orr, Archivist Quorrin Vane, Keeper-Archivist Wessel Dree, Lector-General Sabine Ferrant, Veska Dunn.
**Component quests:** `regional_quests/Q_THE_SCHOLARS_REQUEST.md`, `regional_quests/Q_THE_SOLD_STONE.md`, `city_quests/Q_THE_LANTERN_AND_THE_LAMP.md`, `city_quests/Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE.md`, the `CONCORD_REMNANT/` chain.

### Starting Hooks
- A visiting Remnant scholar (Briss) needs an escort and a local who can reach ruins he can't.
- A village wants to sell an inscribed archive-stone; a Mourner wants it kept.
- Inscriptions keep turning up that no one local can read.

### Player-Facing Premise
The ruins everywhere are covered in a dead language — Concord Script. One faction of scholars can read it, and they want the player to help them find, translate, and recover the inscriptions. As the player learns the Script (the campaign's key clue-gate), the inscriptions start telling a darker story than the Remnant's public version of history.

### DM-Only Truth
The Remnant's public face is preservation; its inner circle (the Reclaimers — Vane, Dree, Ferrant) wants to *seize* the harvest and rebuild the Concord (M6/M9 → endgame "seize" branch). Briss is an idealist who will eventually be disillusioned (Secret 13). The Script is the gate to M6 (the Concord harvested the dead) and M9 (the Quietfall was deliberate) — **both gated to Act 3 / L9–12.** Early translations reveal only that the Concord "kept the dead's pattern" (M3), not the full sin.

### Objectives
1. Gain access to Concord Script (via Briss/Orr — the clue-gate for half the campaign).
2. Recover and translate key inscriptions across the arc.
3. Eventually reach the Sealed Archive in Caradril — the clearest proof of M6/M9.
4. Decide whether to trust the Remnant's leadership with what is found.

### Possible Approaches
- **Scholarly:** Learn the Script as a downtime track; do translation work.
- **Exploration:** Recover inscriptions from the Whispering Cairn, Buried Cloister, coastal/Vale nodes.
- **Social:** Navigate the Remnant's internal split (idealist Briss vs. Reclaimer inner circle).
- **Heist/intrigue:** Reach the Sealed Archive past Dree's gatekeeping (Act 3).

### Important Scenes
- First successful translation — the player can suddenly *read the ruins*.
- The archive-stone's fate (sell to the Ledger, keep, or give to the Remnant — each has cost).
- The Sealed Archive: the written proof of the harvest and the planned Quietfall (Act 3 payoff).

### Clues
C-M3-4, C-M6-1, C-M9-1, C-M9-4, C-M2-1 (reading the shrine). This questline is the primary **scholarly** path to the deep history.

### Combat Options
Minimal; ruin hazards and the occasional Rival Salvage Crew (Ledger competitors). The Reclaimers prefer to outmaneuver, not fight.

### Noncombat Options
Almost entirely investigation, translation, and faction politics.

### Solo-Play Safety Valves
Briss is a patient teacher and protective ally early. The Script can be acquired multiple ways (Briss, Orr, captured texts) so no single NPC death blocks it.

### Rest And Time Pressure
Long-fuse. The Reclamation clock advances if the Ledger or Reclaimers get ahead; the player can slow it by controlling what's translated and shared.

### Rewards
Concord Script literacy (a major campaign capability); Remnant favor; access to the Lantern Reach and (Act 3) the Sealed Archive; [STAGE_14_FINALIZE: a translation-aiding relic].

### Consequences
- **Trust the Remnant fully:** they advance toward the "seize" endgame; the player is complicit unless they later break with them.
- **Expose the Reclaimers:** Ferrant/Vane become enemies; Briss may defect to the player.
- **Keep the knowledge:** the player becomes the swing holder of the deep history.

### Failure State
If the player loses the Script gate (e.g., alienates the whole Remnant), alternate readers exist (captured Gravecaller knowledge, a turned scholar). The deep history stays reachable — slower.

### Follow-Up Hooks
Q_MAJOR_011 (Sealed Archive), Q_MAJOR_019 (Glassmere's grand chapter), Q_MAJOR_012 (the Heartlands reclamation), endgame "seize" branch.

### Scaling Notes
L2–4: escort/translation in the Reach. L9–12: Caradril archive intrigue, Reclaimer confrontation.

### State Updates
ACTIVE_QUESTS, FACTION_STATE (Remnant), WORLD_CLOCKS (Reclamation), NPC_MEMORY (Briss, Orr, Vane, Dree, Ferrant), KNOWN_CLUES (M3/M6/M9), CONSEQUENCES, PLAYER_CHARACTER (Script literacy).

# Q_MAJOR_004 — The Ledger's Long Hunger

**Act / Level:** Act 1–4 / 2–14 (campaign-long). Solo danger: low→moderate (social/economic). **Quest Type:** Faction / intrigue / moral.
**Region / Start:** Sundering Reach (Hollowmere/Saltmargin) → Caradril (Counting-Quays) → Coast/Verdance/Glassmere.
**Secrecy:** mixed. **Lead faction:** Cinder Ledger.
**Primary NPCs:** Mother Calla Vyre, Factor Domic Sael, Provost Anneth Vell, Underfactor Coll Riis, Collector-Captain Mauld Tallow, Factor-General Wessel Crane, Banker-Magistra Sefwy Holt.
**Component quests:** `regional_quests/Q_LIGHT_ON_THE_SCALE.md`, `city_quests/Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER.md`, `city_quests/Q_QUIET_COIN.md`, the `CINDER_LEDGER/` chain.

### Starting Hooks
- A profitable but morally grey salvage job from the Ledger (Domic Sael).
- A rigged scale / debt-trap that's strangling a town.
- A quiet buyer paying too much for "old glass."

### Player-Facing Premise
The Cinder Ledger is everywhere: salt, debt, salvage, charters. They pay well and ask the player to do increasingly compromised work. The deeper the player goes, the clearer it becomes that the Ledger isn't just trading relics — it's cornering a market in something it doesn't fully understand, and a conscience or two inside the bank is starting to crack.

### DM-Only Truth
The Ledger is racing to monopolize Remembrance relics (M3) and, through Vyre's secret deal with Quorrin Vane, to control the knowledge of what they are (M3/M6 — endgame "seize/sell" branch). They do **not** yet know the apex truth (Hollow Court). Domic Sael and Coll Riis are the turnable consciences (Secret-ledger entries). The Monopoly clock is the Ledger's escalation.

### Objectives
1. Work Ledger jobs and learn the shape of the relic monopoly (M3).
2. Discover the Vyre–Vane deal (M3/M6 link) via a turned factor or a heist.
3. Decide: help the Ledger corner the market, break the monopoly, or turn its consciences.

### Possible Approaches
- **Mercantile:** Play the Ledger's game and rise within it.
- **Intrigue:** Turn Coll Riis or Domic Sael; extract the deal-proof.
- **Heist:** The Ledger Vault (Hollowmere) / the deep-vault (Caradril) relic.
- **Opposition:** Feed evidence to the Compact, Mourners, or a rival bank (Holt of Glassmere).

### Important Scenes
- A debt-eviction the player is asked to enforce (moral pivot).
- A factor's confession (Domic or Coll) — the conscience cracks.

The campaign's resolution; an epilogue reflecting every accumulated choice; the player's legend.

**Region / Start:** Verdance Reaches (Marrowfen Stair / the Nine Locks).
**Secrecy:** mixed. **Lead faction:** Cinder Ledger (inland directorate).
**Primary NPCs:** Factor-General Wessel Crane, Lord Ennis Marrow, River-Captain Mossa Drenn, Sub-Lector Hadwin Vael.
**Component quests:** (new questline; ties to Q_MAJOR_004).

### Starting Hooks
- The river road from the Reach to Caradril is the Ledger's inland artery — and its relic-smuggling corridor.
- A petty river-lord (Marrow) taxes the locks and squeezes everyone.
- An independent barge-captain (Drenn) will help the player move against the Ledger — for a cut.

### Player-Facing Premise
The Verdance is the great river that carries coin, salt, and quieter cargo between the Reach and the wider world. The Ledger runs it through a chain of locks and a river-lord's tolls. The player works (or wars) along the river — escorting, smuggling, exposing, or seizing the trade that moves Remembrance relics inland.

### DM-Only Truth
The Verdance corridor is the Ledger's inland relic-smuggling route (M3 at scale); Factor-General Crane runs the directorate that feeds Caradril and Glassmere. Sub-Lector Hadwin Vael (Remnant, inland chapter — NOT kin to Quorrin Vane) shadows the trade for the Reclaimers. The river is the connective tissue between the Reach crisis and the continental market.

### Objectives
1. Map the Ledger's river relic-corridor (M3).
2. Choose a side among Crane, Marrow, and Drenn.
3. Control, expose, or exploit the trade.

### Possible Approaches
- **Mercantile:** Run cargo for the Ledger and rise.
- **Smuggling:** Move relics with Drenn against the directorate.
- **Opposition:** Expose the corridor to the Compact/Remnant.
- **Political:** Break the river-lord's toll-grip.

### Important Scenes
- The Nine Locks — a chokepoint of coin and contraband.
- A barge boarded; relics found among the salt.
- Crane's cold, courteous offer to make the player rich.

### Clues
M3 (relic corridor), the Ledger's continental logistics, a Remnant shadow (Vael).

### Combat Options
Caravan guards, river-pirates, lock-toughs. Moderate; telegraphed; the river favors maneuver.

### Noncombat Options
A mercantile/intrigue arc; mostly negotiation and smuggling.

### Solo-Play Safety Valves
Drenn provides safe passage and an escape route; the river is navigable around danger.

### Rest And Time Pressure
The Monopoly clock (inland branch) advances if the corridor runs unopposed.

### Rewards
Coin, river contacts, a route to Glassmere; or a blow to the Ledger; [STAGE_14_FINALIZE: a corridor manifest as evidence].

### Consequences
- **Corridor controlled by player:** leverage over the Ledger's inland trade.
- **Corridor exposed:** the Compact/Remnant move; the Ledger retaliates.
- **Ignored:** relics flow inland; the market grows.

### Failure State
A lost barge or blown cover reshuffles the river's powers but never blocks the M3 paper-trail.

### Follow-Up Hooks
Q_MAJOR_019 (Glassmere), Q_MAJOR_004 (the Ledger), Q_MAJOR_011 (the Archive).

### Scaling Notes
L8–10: river jobs. L11–13: the corridor war, Crane's directorate.

# Q_MAJOR_026 — The Grove That Will Not Harvest

**Act / Level:** Act 3–4 / 8–13. Solo danger: moderate. **Quest Type:** Religious / cultural (Sunmark); hopeful contrast.
**Region / Start:** Sunmark (the Great Grove at Sunhollow / the Grove-Camps).
**Secrecy:** mixed. **Lead faction:** the grove-faith (living tradition).
**Primary NPCs:** Grove-Keeper Sael Sunmark, Sun-Singer Doll, Plains-Factor Doss Sunward, Greenway-Warden Tamsin Greenway.
**Component quests:** (new questline).

### Starting Hooks
- A grove-faith that "lets the dead become the green" has no restless-dead problem at all.
- A cut-and-trade lord (Sunward) threatens the sacred groves.
- The grove-keepers may hold the seed of a different answer to the whole crisis.

### Player-Facing Premise
In the warm south, grove-tribes practice a living faith in which the dead become part of the land and rest easily. Where every other region the player has seen is haunted, the Sunmark is at peace — until outsiders' greed and the spreading crisis begin to threaten even this. The player encounters the campaign's most hopeful answer.

### DM-Only Truth
The grove-faith is a **hopeful contrast** — a genuinely *functional* way of letting the dead rest that owes nothing to the Concord (it neither harvests, seals, nor destroys; it *releases*). This is the cultural seed of the endgame's **"transform"** synthesis (M10): proof that a consensual, peaceful relationship with the dead is possible. **No Concord node; the contrast is the point.** The grove is threatened by the spreading harvest leak (a warning that even peace isn't safe).

### Objectives
1. Learn the grove-faith's peaceful way (the "transform" seed).
2. Protect the sacred groves from the cut-and-trade threat.
3. Carry the grove's lesson toward the endgame.

### Possible Approaches
- **Spiritual:** Learn the grove-faith as a seeker.
- **Protection:** Defend the groves from Sunward's logging/trade.
- **Cultural:** Earn the grove-keepers' trust.

### Important Scenes
- A grove-rite where the dead truly rest.
- The cut-and-trade threat at the grove's edge.
- The Sun-Singer's vision of a different world.

### Clues
The "transform" seed (M10); living proof that a peaceful answer exists.

### Combat Options
Trade-lord's hired cutters, grove-edge skirmishes, wilds hazards. Moderate; telegraphed.

### Noncombat Options
Spiritual and protective play; the groves are defended as much by custom as by force.

### Solo-Play Safety Valves
The grove-keepers shelter the player; the Sunmark is the campaign's gentlest far-region; retreat available.

### Rest And Time Pressure
A "the groves are threatened / the leak spreads south" clock advances with neglect.

### Rewards
The campaign's most hopeful insight (the "transform" path); grove allies; [STAGE_14_FINALIZE: a grove-blessing].

### Consequences
- **Groves protected:** the "transform" seed survives; a hopeful ally for the endgame.
- **Groves lost:** even the peaceful answer falls; the endgame darkens.

### Failure State
Failing to protect the groves still imparts the lesson (the player has seen that peace is possible) — the "transform" path remains reachable, harder.

### Follow-Up Hooks
Q_MAJOR_015 (the "transform" endgame), Q_MAJOR_014 (Maire's synthesis), Q_MAJOR_027 (Hethewald's free holds).

### Scaling Notes
L8–10: learning the faith. L11–13: defending the groves.

### State Updates
ACTIVE_QUESTS, FACTION_STATE (grove-faith), WORLD_CLOCKS (regional), NPC_MEMORY, KNOWN_CLUES (M10 seed), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_MAJOR_027 — The Free Holds Vote

**Act / Level:** Act 3 / 7–12. Solo danger: moderate. **Quest Type:** Political / confederacy (Hethewald Free Holds).
**Region / Start:** Hethewald Free Holds (Hethemoot / Greenward / Tollreach).
**Secrecy:** mixed. **Lead faction:** the free-holds confederacy.
**Primary NPCs:** Hold-Speaker Bram Hethe, Forest-Mother Onn Greenward, Toll-Lord "Greenfinger" Maddoc.
**Component quests:** (new questline).

### Starting Hooks
- A confederacy of free forest-holds must vote on how to face the spreading crisis.
- An outlaw river-toller (Maddoc) holds the confederacy's trade hostage.
- A grove grief-keeper (Onn) carries old songs that could sway the vote.

### Player-Facing Premise
The Hethewald is a confederacy of fiercely independent forest free-holds, governed by moot and vote. As the crisis spreads, the holds must decide — together — how to respond: seal their old ruins, trade their relics, or hold to the old forest-ways. The player becomes a voice (or a kingmaker) in a continental-scale democratic crisis.

### DM-Only Truth
The Hethewald's "Old Holds" are deep-wood Concord ruins (forbidden; a Tollwood-parallel — peripheral nodes, NOT the keystone). Forest-Mother Onn's old songs are an M6 folk-path. The vote is a microcosm of the endgame choice (seal/sell/keep-the-old-ways) at regional scale — a rehearsal for M10 that lets the player test the consequences of each path safely. **No apex.**

### Objectives
1. Win standing in the confederacy.
2. Sway the vote on how to face the crisis.
3. Resolve the outlaw toll-lord's blockade.

### Possible Approaches
- **Political:** Work the moot and the Speaker.
- **Folk:** Use Onn's old songs to move the holds.
- **Opposition:** Break Maddoc's toll-blockade.

### Important Scenes
- A great moot where the holds argue the crisis.
- Onn's song that changes minds.
- Maddoc's toll-camp on the river.

### Clues
M6 folk-path (Onn's songs); a regional rehearsal of M10 (the endgame choice).

### Combat Options
Toll-lord's outlaws, deep-wood hazards, hold-feuds. Moderate; telegraphed.

### Noncombat Options
Political, folk, and diplomatic play throughout.

### Solo-Play Safety Valves
A hold sponsor shelters the player; the deep Old Holds are gated/warned.

### Rest And Time Pressure
A "the confederacy fractures / the crisis spreads" clock advances with neglect.

### Rewards
A regional rehearsal of the endgame choice; free-holds allies; [STAGE_14_FINALIZE: a hold-token].

### Consequences
- **Vote: seal:** the holds steady; a "seal it" rehearsal and ally.
- **Vote: trade:** relics flow; the Ledger gains.
- **Vote: old ways:** the holds endure but isolate.

### Failure State
A deadlocked vote leaves the holds divided — a regional consequence, not a dead end; Onn's songs remain.

### Follow-Up Hooks
Q_MAJOR_017 (Tollwood parallel), Q_MAJOR_026 (Sunmark), Q_MAJOR_015 (the endgame rehearsed here).

### Scaling Notes
L7–9: winning standing. L10–12: the vote, the blockade.

# Q_MAJOR_028 — The Faction War for the Shrine

**Act / Level:** Act 3–4 / 11–16. Solo danger: high. **Quest Type:** Convergent faction war / climax setup. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Region / Start:** Sundering Reach (convergent — all factions descend on Hollowmere/the basin).
**Lead faction:** all seven major factions.
**Primary NPCs:** every faction lead the player has cultivated (Vyre, Ferrant/Vane, the Tallow Man/Ashby, Othetha, Wend, Coalmont, and — DM-only — Reke/Veyl).
**Component quests:** convergence of all faction chains + Q_MAJOR_002/006/011/014.

### Starting Hooks
- The drowned shrine is reopening; every faction races to control or destroy it.
- The player's accumulated allies and enemies all converge on the Reach.
- The basin works (Q_MAJOR_002) reach their climax.

### Player-Facing Premise
Everything the player has built across the campaign collides at the drowned shrine. The Ledger, the Remnant, the Gravecallers, the Wardens, the Mourners, and the Compact all move on the basin at once — and the player, the one figure trusted or feared by all, must shape the war that decides who reaches the shrine first.

### DM-Only Truth
This is the Act 3–4 convergence that sets up Q_MAJOR_014 (the Court beneath) and Q_MAJOR_015 (the endgame). The Hollow Court (Reke/Veyl) is the hidden hand behind the basin's reopening; the faction war is the surface storm over the buried apex. The player's prior choices (which factions they aided/opposed) determine the war's sides and which endings stay reachable. **The Court is revealed here only if the player has assembled M4+M6+M9 — otherwise the war reads as a faction scramble with a hidden mover.**

### Objectives
1. Marshal the player's coalition (or play factions against each other).
2. Control who reaches the shrine first.
3. Position for the descent (Q_MAJOR_014) and the endgame (Q_MAJOR_015).

### Possible Approaches
- **Coalition:** Unite allied factions behind one goal.
- **Divide-and-rule:** Set rivals against each other.
- **Decisive:** Reach the shrine first yourself.
- **Restraint:** Stop everyone — keep the basin sealed a while longer.

### Important Scenes
- The factions massing on Hollowmere.
- A council-of-war (or a betrayal) among the player's allies.
- The race to the basin's reopening doorway.

### Clues
Convergence of M2/M4/M6/M9 → M7 (the Court, gated); all prior threads pay off.

### Combat Options
Faction war-bands, the basin's awakening guardians, betrayals. High; telegraphed; the player commands allies, not soloing.

### Noncombat Options
Coalition-building, negotiation, and maneuver can shape the war without the player fighting every battle.

### Solo-Play Safety Valves
The player fights *with* a coalition gathered over 20 levels; allies absorb the danger; retreat and regroup are always available.

### Rest And Time Pressure
The Harvest Restarts master clock drives the war's tempo; the basin reopens on a schedule the player can accelerate or resist.

### Rewards
Control of the shrine's approach; the coalition for the endgame; [STAGE_14_FINALIZE: war-spoils / the means to enact a choice].

### Consequences
- **Coalition wins:** the player dictates the descent and the endgame's terms.
- **Rivals win the race:** a faction reaches the shrine first; the player must wrest control back in Act 5.
- **Sealed again:** the war ends in a fragile pause; the apex waits.

### Failure State
Losing the race doesn't end the campaign — it sets the player up to reach the shrine *after* a rival, reframing Q_MAJOR_014/015 as a recovery rather than a triumph.

### Follow-Up Hooks
Q_MAJOR_014 (the Court beneath), Q_MAJOR_015 (the fate of the Remembrance).

### Scaling Notes
L11–13: the factions mass. L14–16: the war and the race to the shrine.

### State Updates
ACTIVE_QUESTS, FACTION_STATE (all), WORLD_CLOCKS (Harvest Restarts + all faction clocks), NPC_MEMORY (all leads), KNOWN_CLUES (convergence → M7 gated), CONSEQUENCES.

## Anti-Railroad / Solo-Safety Note

No major quest requires winning a fight to advance; every revelation has 3+ paths at campaign scale (see MYSTERY_WEB). The apex (M7/Hollow Court) is gated behind M4+M6+M9 convergence and never named before Act 3–4. Every questline has a failure state that continues play and an ignore-consequence that evolves the world (faction clocks). The player is expected to arrive at high-tier questlines with a coalition gathered across the campaign — the solo-safety valve at every scale.
