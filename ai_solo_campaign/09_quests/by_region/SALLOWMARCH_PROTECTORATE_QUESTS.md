## AI Use

Seven developed quests for the Sallowmarch Protectorate (a fever-delta of rice, reed, and smuggling under an absent crown; Lvl 10–14). Cast from `08_npcs/by_region/SALLOWMARCH_PROTECTORATE_NPCS.md`. The delta is far from the crisis; failures are masked by the fever's grim familiarity with death — Doll Fenn (medicine) and Sela Reed (rite) are the south's twin faintest early-witnesses. Feeds Q_MAJOR_021 (The Fever Delta). Anchors: Fenward, Reedmouth, the Rice Sallows, the Fever Channels.

# Q_SP_001_THE_CROWNS_PEACE — The Crown's Peace

**Quest Type:** Faction favor / social infiltration / moral dilemma. **Level Range:** 10–13; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 16 (Vorr Sallow); Insight DC 16 (the delta-folk's defiance); Deception DC 17; Investigation DC 15.
**Starting Hook:** Protector-Legate Vorr Sallow — sweating in a climate he hates — hires a competent outsider to do what his fever-thinned garrison can't: break a smuggling ring or overawe a defiant village for "the crown's peace."
**Location:** Fenward (the garrison-capital); a defiant delta village.
**Primary NPCs:** Vorr Sallow (giver), Marsh-Healer Doll Fenn (the delta's true leader), "Heron" Maddox (the smuggler target), garrison officers.
**Factions:** Sallowmarch Protectorate (crown), independent (delta-folk).
**Surface Problem:** Enforce the crown's order against the delta's quiet defiance.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** A moral vise: the crown's "peace" is occupation; Doll Fenn is the delta's real authority and a humane potential ally; "Heron" Maddox is a turnable grey resistance. The player chooses whose side they're really on. Vorr is blind to the frontier crisis entirely. No harvest content.
**Objectives:** 1) Take or refuse Vorr's commission. 2) Resolve it (enforce, subvert, or broker). 3) Decide where the player's loyalty lies.
**Approaches:** Enforce for the crown; secretly aid the delta-folk; broker a truce between crown and village; expose a corrupt garrison officer.
**Obstacles:** The crown's cold authority; the delta-folk's solidarity; the fever; the moral weight of occupation.
**Important Scenes:** Vorr ruling a marsh he despises; Doll Fenn's quiet authority; a village standing silent against the crown.
**Clues:** The crown's reach and the delta's defiance (foreign-power thread).
**Combat Options:** Garrison soldiers (**Guard/Veteran-like**, fever-weakened) or smugglers (**Bandit/Scout-like**) depending on the side taken — avoidable by negotiation.
**Noncombat Options:** Enforcement-by-presence, subversion, brokering, exposure — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Multiple sides; the delta and the Channels offer escape; turnable contacts; no forced fight.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Rests in Fenward or with the delta-folk; a commission-deadline.
**Rewards:** Vorr's authority/protection (if served) OR the delta-folk's trust (if subverted); coin; a door to the southern crown.
**Consequences:** Serving the crown tightens the occupation. Aiding the delta builds toward the delta-folk as allies (Q_SP_003/004). Brokering a truce pleases no one fully but eases the delta.
**Failure State:** A botched commission costs Vorr's favor but opens the delta-folk's; reopen via Doll Fenn; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SP_003 (Heron Maddox); Q_SP_004 (Doll Fenn); Q_HGP_008 (the crown's customs).
**Scaling Notes:** L10 — a village dispute. L13 — an occupation flashpoint and a garrison crackdown.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Protectorate/delta-folk), RELATIONSHIPS (Vorr, Doll Fenn), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_021), `../../08_npcs/by_region/HOLLOW_GULF_PORTS_NPCS.md`.

# Q_SP_002_THE_NEW_SICKNESS — The New Sickness

**Quest Type:** Investigation / death-rite failure (faint, clinical) / mystery. **Level Range:** 11–14; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Medicine DC 17 (the "new sickness"); Insight DC 16; Religion DC 16 (Sela Reed's rite); Investigation DC 17.
**Starting Hook:** Marsh-Healer Doll Fenn is the rare far-south figure to *notice* the dead "not settling" — a new sickness no marsh-cure touches. She seeks anyone with knowledge of it; the frontier player may have it.
**Location:** Reedmouth; the Fever Channels; the Rice Sallows.
**Primary NPCs:** Doll Fenn (giver/ally, clinical witness), Fen-Witch Mother Sela Reed (rite-witness), a stricken family, garrison doctor.
**Factions:** Independent (delta healers), Mourners' Circle (Sela Reed).
**Surface Problem:** Diagnose and treat a "new sickness" — the dead not settling — at the fever-delta's edge.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Doll Fenn (medicine) and Sela Reed (rite) are the south's TWIN faintest early-witnesses. The "new sickness" is the crisis reaching even here, masked by the fever's grim death-familiarity. Sela's "grey hands took the dead" is M6-oblique folk-memory ONLY. NEVER explain the mechanism; keep it clinical and oblique.
**Objectives:** 1) Confirm the "new sickness" is real (vs. fever). 2) Help settle a "won't-go-down" death (rite + medicine). 3) Decide whether to connect it to the frontier truth.
**Approaches:** Clinical investigation with Doll Fenn; a settling-rite with Sela Reed; bring frontier knowledge to bear; correlate the twin witnesses.
**Obstacles:** The fever masking the signs; the delta's familiarity with death; the cause unknowable here.
**Important Scenes:** Doll Fenn's fever-steady hands meeting a case she can't cure; a "won't-go-down" body; Sela Reed's grave, song-laced fragment.
**Clues:** The dead "not going down" (M5, far-south faintest); Sela's oblique "grey hands" fragment (M6-oblique); the twin medical+rite witness.
**Combat Options:** A single "won't-go-down" Remembrance manifestation (scaled, AC 11) — settle-able by rite; telegraphed and rare.
**Noncombat Options:** Medicine, rite, investigation — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Non-violent settling; Doll Fenn and Sela Reed assist; retreat to the village; the threat is faint.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests; no hard clock (the delta is far from the edge).
**Rewards:** Doll Fenn's trust (rare healer-ally; rare delta medicine); Sela Reed's trust (southern network-key, far later); a far-south data-point; the twin-witness correlation.
**Consequences:** Helping the twin witnesses builds the delta's belief and the old-songs network (Q_MAJOR_021). Ignored — the "new sickness" spreads quietly and the delta dies a little faster.
**Failure State:** If a death isn't settled, a delta crisis-hook opens; the healers persist; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SP_004 (Doll Fenn); the continental old-songs network (Sela Reed); frontier-truth correlation.
**Scaling Notes:** L11 — one faint case. L14 — a delta outbreak of "won't-go-down" dead.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (M5/M6-oblique south), RELATIONSHIPS (Doll Fenn, Sela Reed), FACTION_STATE (Mourners), NPC_MEMORY.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_021), `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/CLUE_INDEX.md`.

# Q_SP_003_THE_HERON_AND_THE_CHANNELS — The Heron and the Channels

**Quest Type:** Smuggling / escort / turnable outlaw. **Level Range:** 11–14; solo danger: high in the Channels. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 16 (turning Heron Maddox); Stealth DC 17 (the Channels); Survival DC 16 (the marsh); Insight DC 15.
**Starting Hook:** Reed-Boss "Heron" Maddox — the patient smuggler-king who "goes where the crown can't" — offers passage through the Fever Channels, a cut, or a turnable alliance against the occupation.
**Location:** Reedmouth; the Fever Channels.
**Primary NPCs:** "Heron" Maddox (outlaw/possible ally), Doll Fenn (whose folk he shelters), a rival smuggler or a crown patrol.
**Factions:** Independent (Heron's network), Sallowmarch Protectorate (the crown he bleeds).
**Surface Problem:** Deal with Heron Maddox — hire him, run a cargo, or turn him.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Heron is the delta's turnable grey resistance (Gull/Greenfinger's southern cousin) and a grey-network witness to where the delta's "going strange" (a Channel-hideout gone "wrong"). No central-mystery content beyond an oblique "bad water" note.
**Objectives:** 1) Win Heron's trust/passage. 2) Run a job or turn him against the crown. 3) Navigate the deadly Channels.
**Approaches:** Hire him for passage; run a cargo past the garrison; turn him into an anti-occupation ally; deal with a rival smuggler or a marsh-traitor.
**Obstacles:** The Channels' lethal terrain; Heron's caution (prove you're no informer); the crown's patrols.
**Important Scenes:** Heron waiting in the reeds "like the heron he's named for"; a night-run through the Channels; a hideout gone "wrong."
**Clues:** A Channel-hideout "going strange" (M5-oblique grey-network witness).
**Combat Options:** A crown patrol (**Guard/Veteran-like**) or a rival smuggler (**Bandit/Scout-like**) — avoidable by stealth; the Channels themselves are the main danger.
**Noncombat Options:** Hiring, scheming, proving-trust, sabotage — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Heron guides through the Channels (vital ally); multiple resolutions; retreat by water; prove-no-informer is non-violent.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Rests at Reedmouth; a cargo-run deadline.
**Rewards:** Passage through the crown-proof Channels; a marsh-sly ally; the delta's contraband and intelligence; coin.
**Consequences:** Turning Heron gains a hidden delta and bleeds the occupation. Betraying him to the crown is profitable but burns the marsh-folk. Ignored — the smuggling-war simmers.
**Failure State:** A blown run — Heron loses respect but isn't an enemy-for-life; the player can earn it back; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SP_001 (the crown); Q_SP_002 (the marsh "going strange"); the delta underworld.
**Scaling Notes:** L11 — a smuggling run. L14 — a crown crackdown and a Channel-hideout horror.
**State Updates:** RELATIONSHIPS (Heron, Doll Fenn), FACTION_STATE (Protectorate/delta), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `../../08_npcs/by_region/SALLOWMARCH_PROTECTORATE_NPCS.md`.

# Q_SP_004_THE_FEVER_AND_THE_FEN — The Fever and the Fen

**Quest Type:** Rescue / protection / wilderness survival. **Level Range:** 10–13; solo danger: moderate–high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Medicine DC 16; Survival DC 17 (the deep delta); Constitution saves (the fever); Persuasion DC 15.
**Starting Hook:** Marsh-Healer Doll Fenn is overwhelmed: a worse plague (or the crown's neglect) threatens to break a delta village, and folk are lost in the Fever Channels.
**Location:** A delta village; the Rice Sallows; the Fever Channels.
**Primary NPCs:** Doll Fenn (giver/ally), Mother Sela Reed (rites for the dying), Heron Maddox (the hidden ways), a stricken village.
**Factions:** Independent (delta-folk), Mourners' Circle, Sallowmarch Protectorate (neglectful).
**Surface Problem:** Save a village from plague (and the crown's neglect); recover the lost.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** A humane survival/rescue quest cementing Doll Fenn as a rare healer-ally and the delta-folk as a people worth saving. The "new sickness" (Q_SP_002) may complicate it (oblique). No harvest content.
**Objectives:** 1) Bring medicine/aid to the village. 2) Recover the lost from the Channels. 3) Confront the crown's neglect (or work around it).
**Approaches:** Gather/deliver rare medicine; lead a Channel rescue (with Heron); rite the dying with Sela Reed; pressure or bypass the crown for relief.
**Obstacles:** The deadly fever (Con saves); the Channels' terrain; the crown's indifference; the "new sickness" complicating cures.
**Important Scenes:** Doll Fenn "dispensing cures and quiet authority"; a Channel rescue under fever-mist; the crown turning away.
**Clues:** The "new sickness" complicating the fever (links Q_SP_002, oblique).
**Combat Options:** Marsh predators or a Channel hazard — environmental; avoidable with Heron's guidance.
**Noncombat Options:** Medicine, rescue, rite, persuasion — combat largely avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Doll Fenn/Heron guide and heal; clear retreat; the fever is survivable with care; no forced fight.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Limited safe ground; the plague/the lost as a hard-ish clock.
**Rewards:** The delta-folk's deep trust; Doll Fenn as a healer-ally (rare delta medicine); Sela Reed's regard; a saved village.
**Consequences:** Saving the village earns the delta as allies (Q_MAJOR_021). The crown's exposed neglect is a lever. Ignored — the village breaks and the delta mistrusts outsiders.
**Failure State:** The village lost → grief and a moral indictment of the crown; the delta-folk thread persists; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SP_001 (the crown's neglect as a lever); Q_SP_002 (the new sickness); the old-songs network.
**Scaling Notes:** L10 — a contained outbreak. L13 — a delta-wide plague and the "new sickness" compounding it.
**State Updates:** RELATIONSHIPS (Doll Fenn, Sela Reed, Heron), FACTION_STATE (delta-folk/crown), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_021).

# Q_SP_005_THE_REVENUE_THAT_VANISHED — The Revenue That Vanished

**Quest Type:** Investigation / faction (crown vs. smuggler) / political leverage. **Level Range:** 11–14; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Investigation DC 17 (the missing revenue); Insight DC 16; Persuasion/Deception DC 17; Stealth DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** The crown's revenue is vanishing into the delta; Vorr Sallow wants it found, the smugglers want it kept hidden, and a corrupt garrison officer may be skimming both.
**Location:** Fenward; the Rice Sallows; a customs-post.
**Primary NPCs:** Vorr Sallow (giver/investigator), a corrupt garrison officer, "Heron" Maddox (smuggler-suspect), Crown-clerk (turnable).
**Factions:** Sallowmarch Protectorate (crown), independent (smugglers).
**Surface Problem:** Find the crown's vanishing revenue.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The revenue is skimmed by a corrupt crown officer AND bled by Heron's smuggling — a political puzzle the player can resolve in several directions. Pure occupation-politics; no central-mystery content. The Ledger's southern arm may be involved (oblique Q_MAJOR_004 link).
**Objectives:** 1) Trace the missing revenue. 2) Identify the culprit(s). 3) Decide who takes the fall (and who benefits).
**Approaches:** Follow the paper-trail; turn a crown clerk; pin it on the corrupt officer; protect (or expose) Heron; play the crown against the smugglers.
**Obstacles:** The corrupt officer's cover; the smugglers' silence; Vorr's expectation; the delta's complicity.
**Important Scenes:** A doctored customs-ledger; a corrupt officer's panic; Heron's plausible deniability.
**Clues:** The crown's revenue-bleed (occupation-politics); a possible Ledger-southern-arm hand (oblique).
**Combat Options:** A cornered corrupt officer's loyal soldiers (**Guard-like**) if exposed by force — avoidable.
**Noncombat Options:** Investigation, exposure, turning informants, brokering — the whole quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; multiple culprits and resolutions; turnable clerk; the Channels for escape.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests; an audit-deadline.
**Rewards:** Vorr's favor (if the revenue's found); a lever over the crown or the smugglers; coin; a turned clerk.
**Consequences:** Pinning the officer cleans the garrison (and may free Heron). Protecting Heron keeps the resistance alive. The Ledger's hand, if exposed, links Q_MAJOR_004.
**Failure State:** The revenue lost / the wrong person blamed → an injustice the player can later right; play continues.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SP_001/003; Q_MAJOR_004 (the Ledger's southern arm).
**Scaling Notes:** L11 — a skim. L14 — a garrison-corruption scandal with a Ledger angle.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (crown/smugglers/Ledger), RELATIONSHIPS, CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `../../08_npcs/by_region/SALLOWMARCH_PROTECTORATE_NPCS.md`.

# Q_SP_006_THE_DROWNED_GROUND — The Drowned Ground

**Quest Type:** Ruin / haunted site (oblique) / faction race. **Level Range:** 12–14; solo danger: high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Survival DC 17 (the deep delta); Religion/Arcana DC 18 (the drowned ground — gated); Investigation DC 16; Stealth DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** Smugglers and salvagers covet a "drowned ground" in the deep delta — old, half-sunken ruins the fen-witches warn against — and the Ledger or a port-lord wants it salvaged before a rival does.
**Location:** The Fever Channels' deepest backwaters; a drowned-ground ruin.
**Primary NPCs:** "Heron" Maddox (a hideout there gone "wrong"), Mother Sela Reed (warns against it), a Ledger salvager, Doll Fenn (the marsh-wise read).
**Factions:** Cinder Ledger (salvage), independent (smugglers/salvagers), Mourners' Circle (warn).
**Surface Problem:** Reach and salvage (or investigate) the drowned ground.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The drowned ground is a peripheral Concord delta-work (M2/M5-oblique) — NOT the keystone. The wrongness there corroborates the crisis reaching the deep south without explaining it. Sela Reed's warning telegraphs the danger. Keep the deepest ruin unexplained.
**Objectives:** 1) Reach the drowned ground. 2) Salvage/investigate (decide how deep). 3) Survive what stirs there.
**Approaches:** A careful delve with Heron's guidance; heed Sela Reed and go cautious; race the Ledger salvager; rite the stirring dead.
**Obstacles:** The deadly deep delta; the ruin's "wrongness"; the Ledger's rival crew; stirring drowned dead.
**Important Scenes:** A hideout gone "wrong" in the deep Channels; a half-sunken ruin "humming"; a Ledger crew racing in.
**Clues:** A far-south Concord work waking (M2 continental); drowned dead stirring (M5-oblique); never the mechanism.
**Combat Options:** Drowned-dead guardians or a Ledger salvage-crew (scaled L12–14, telegraphed) — escapable by retreat; deeper = deadlier.
**Noncombat Options:** Careful salvage, retreat, rite, racing — the deep is optional.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Heron guides; clear retreat; Sela's warning telegraphs danger; turning back is rewarded with survival.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Rests at the delta's edge; tide/flood windows and the Ledger race as pressure.
**Rewards:** Concord delta-salvage (coin + possibly a relic [STAGE_14_FINALIZE]); a far-south M2 data-point; faction favor by choice.
**Consequences:** Going too deep risks death and "waking" the ground further. A cautious delve yields salvage and a clean clue. The Ledger winning the race feeds the monopoly (Q_MAJOR_004).
**Failure State:** Forced retreat — partial salvage and the knowledge the deep south has a "waking" ruin; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SP_002 (the new sickness); Q_HGP_007 (the Drowned Steps parallel); Q_MAJOR_004.
**Scaling Notes:** L12 — shallow salvage. L14 — the deep ruin's full danger and a real guardian-boss.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (M2/M5 far-south), INVENTORY, FACTION_STATE (Ledger), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_SP_007_THE_RICE_MOOT — The Rice Moot

**Quest Type:** Low-mid local color / council dispute / social. **Level Range:** 10–12; solo danger: low. **Secrecy:** player-safe.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 15–16; Insight DC 15; Investigation DC 14.
**Starting Hook:** A rice-village's moot (the delta-folk's quiet self-rule beneath the crown) is deadlocked over water, debt, or a family feud — and an even-handed outsider could break the tie.
**Location:** The Rice Sallows; a delta village.
**Primary NPCs:** Rice-Moot Speaker Pell Sallows (village-speaker), Doll Fenn (respected mediator), feuding villagers, a crown tax-collector.
**Factions:** Independent (delta-folk self-rule), Sallowmarch Protectorate (the crown's shadow).
**Surface Problem:** Break a village-moot deadlock (water-rights, a debt, or a feud).
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Humane delta-texture showing the marsh-people's quiet self-rule and endurance beneath the occupation. Introduces recurring delta contacts. No central-mystery content.
**Objectives:** 1) Understand the dispute. 2) Broker a resolution the moot accepts. 3) Keep the crown out of it.
**Approaches:** Mediate the feud; find a fair water/debt compromise; expose a cheat; shield the village from a crown tax-grab.
**Obstacles:** Old grudges; scarce water; the crown's looming tax; the village's wariness of outsiders.
**Important Scenes:** The moot deadlocked in the wet heat; Doll Fenn lending quiet weight; a crown collector hovering.
**Clues:** None central (delta self-rule color).
**Combat Options:** None expected; a tax-collector's escort if it turns to force — avoidable.
**Noncombat Options:** Mediation, compromise, exposure — the whole quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; multiple resolutions; the player can decline; Doll Fenn assists.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests; a moot-deadline (the rice-crop / flood season).
**Rewards:** The village's goodwill; Pell Sallows and the delta-folk as recurring contacts; a foothold in the delta.
**Consequences:** A fair resolution endears the player to the delta. Failure deepens a feud and the village's distrust.
**Failure State:** The moot fractures / the crown grabs the tax → a grievance-thread; play continues.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SP_001/004; delta self-rule politics.
**Scaling Notes:** Low-mid throughout; raise the crown-tax stakes if pursued.
**State Updates:** RELATIONSHIPS (Pell Sallows, Doll Fenn), FACTION_STATE (delta-folk), NPC_MEMORY.
