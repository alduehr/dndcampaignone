## AI Use

Seven developed quests for the Sunmark (a warm southern land of sacred groves and grove-keeper tribes with a *living* old nature-faith; Lvl 8–13). Cast from `08_npcs/by_region/SUNMARK_NPCS.md`. The Sunmark is the campaign's most peaceful, lush region — and crucially the place where **a faith for the dead still WORKS.** The crisis is barely present (the faintest "far root-ache"). This is a hopeful contrast and a very oblique hint that *proper, consensual rites for the dead are possible* (relevant to the endgame "just" path). Keep it extremely oblique; the groves are NOT Concord and never connect to the keystone. No NPC knows the apex truth. Anchors: the Great Grove at Sunhollow, the Grove-Camps, the Sunward Wilds, the Green Roads.

# Q_SUN_001_THE_SAW_AT_THE_GROVE — The Saw at the Grove

**Quest Type:** Faction-opposition / environmental defense / moral conflict. **Level Range:** 8–11; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 16 (Doss Sunward / the tribes); Investigation DC 16 (the logging scheme); Insight DC 16; Nature DC 15.
**Starting Hook:** Plains-Factor Doss Sunward is pressing a grove-camp to "cut and sell" their sacred trees, backed by the Cinder Ledger's coin — and Grove-Warden Sela Green asks a respectful outsider to stop the cutters before the green is defiled.
**Location:** A threatened grove-edge; the Grove-Camps; the Green Roads.
**Primary NPCs:** Grove-Warden Sela Green (giver), Plains-Factor Doss Sunward (antagonist), Resin-Trader "Honest" Mab Sunward (the fair-trade alternative), Southern-Agent Iss Sunward (Ledger backer), cutter-foreman Vask (a turnable conscience, minor).
**Factions:** Cinder Ledger (edge-trade, antagonist), independent (grove-tribes).
**Surface Problem:** Stop Doss Sunward's cutters from defiling a sacred grove.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** A clean environmental/moral quest — settled greed (Ledger-backed) against a living faith. Protecting the groves protects the campaign's hopeful contrast (a faith that works). If a deep grove is defiled, the living wilds answer (a Sunmark clock). No central-mystery content; the faith's *working* is the oblique hint, never explained.
**Objectives:** 1) Understand the cutting scheme. 2) Stop or redirect it (defense, exposure, honest-trade alternative). 3) Protect the grove and the tribes' trust.
**Approaches:** Defend the grove with Sela Green; expose Doss Sunward's Ledger backing; offer the fair-trade alternative (Honest Mab); turn the cutter-foreman (Vask); out-bargain or shame Doss.
**Obstacles:** Doss's plausible "progress" rhetoric; the Ledger's coin; the cutters' livelihood; the tribes' wariness of any outsider.
**Important Scenes:** Doss Sunward dressing greed as "efficiency"; the cutters at a grove-edge; Honest Mab showing trade done *with* the tribes' consent.
**Clues:** None central (the Ledger's southern edge-arm — Q_MAJOR_004; the living faith's worth is shown, not explained).
**Combat Options:** Doss's hired loggers/toughs (**Guard/Bandit-like**) if it turns to force; the living wilds answer a defiler (DM, scaled) — both avoidable.
**Noncombat Options:** Defense, exposure, honest-trade, turning a conscience, bargaining — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; Sela Green and the tribes as allies; multiple resolutions; the wilds themselves help defend.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests in the grove-camps; a logging-contract soft-clock.
**Rewards:** The tribes' trust and grove-welcome; Honest Mab as a fair-trade ally; Sela Green's friendship; the green protected; a glimpse of a faith that works.
**Consequences:** Saving the grove earns the tribes and protects the hopeful contrast. Letting it fall defiles the green (a Sunmark clock; the wilds may answer terribly).
**Failure State:** The grove cut → the tribes grieve and harden; the living wilds may retaliate; reopen as a grove-defense arc; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SUN_002 (the deep groves' "ache"); Q_SUN_005 (Doss's wider scheme); Q_MAJOR_004 (the Ledger).
**Scaling Notes:** L8 — one grove-edge. L11 — a logging-venture and a defiled deep grove's answer.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Ledger edge-arm, grove-tribes), RELATIONSHIPS (Sela Green, Doss Sunward, Honest Mab), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_004), `../../08_npcs/by_region/SUNMARK_NPCS.md`.

# Q_SUN_002_THE_FAR_ROOT_ACHE — The Far Root-Ache

**Quest Type:** Mystery (oblique) / vigil / lore. **Level Range:** 9–13; solo danger: low–moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Insight DC 17 (Sun-Singer Doll's vigil); Nature/Religion DC 17 (the deep memory); Survival DC 16 (the deep wilds); Persuasion DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** Sun-Singer Doll senses a "far root-ache" tugging at the groves' deep memory from the cold north — and she will share what she perceives only with a northern guest who tells her, truly, what the restless dead up there do.
**Location:** The Sunward Wilds; the deep-grove vigils; the Great Grove.
**Primary NPCs:** Sun-Singer Doll (giver/oracle), Grove-Keeper Sael Sunmark (the living faith's strength), Sun-Singer Apprentice "Greenleaf" (the young seer, secondary), Grove-Guide Sef Green (the deep-wilds route).
**Factions:** Independent (grove-faith), oblique kin-thread to the Mourners.
**Surface Problem:** Help Sun-Singer Doll understand the faint wrongness troubling the deepest groves.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Doll *perceives the crisis* as "a far root-ache tugging the deep green memory" — a deeply-oblique far-echo of the harvest's pull on the dead, reached through a *living, working* grove-faith. With Sael, she's the campaign's clearest hint that **rites for the dead can still hold** (the endgame "just" path). Keep it EXTREMELY oblique. Never let her name the harvest, the Quiet Country, or the Court. The faith *working* is the point.
**Objectives:** 1) Earn Doll's trust (share the north's truth). 2) Help her vigil at a troubled deep-grove. 3) Carry the groves' warning (and hope) north, or learn from how the living faith holds.
**Approaches:** Tell Doll, truly, of the restless dead; keep a deep-grove vigil with her; investigate why the *living* rites still hold (the hopeful clue); carry her warning to the Mourner-network (oblique parallel).
**Obstacles:** Doll's careful obliqueness; the deep wilds' danger; the difficulty of naming what neither party fully understands.
**Important Scenes:** Doll singing the deep memory and faltering at "the far root-ache"; a deep-grove where the green still *holds* its dead (the hopeful contrast, vivid); the moment her ache rhymes with the player's frontier knowledge.
**Clues:** The crisis perceived from a living, working faith (the "far root-ache" — extremely oblique); the living rites *holding the dead* where Concord stone rites fail (the hopeful hint — handle with care); a far-region independent perception of the deep pattern.
**Combat Options:** Deep-wilds hazards or a grove-guardian beast (scaled L9–13, telegraphed) — avoidable by Sef Green's guidance.
**Noncombat Options:** Vigil, lore, persuasion, careful travel — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** A near-combatless lore quest; Sef Green's guidance in the deep wilds; the groves themselves shelter the player; retreat is easy.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests in the groves; no hard clock (a soft deepening of the ache).
**Rewards:** Doll's serene trust and the groves' deepest memory-lore; the hopeful contrast made explicit (oblique); a living-faith perspective on the crisis; the green's lasting welcome.
**Consequences:** Sharing the truth deepens Doll's (and the player's) oblique understanding. The hopeful contrast advances the endgame "just" thread. Ignoring it lets the ache deepen.
**Failure State:** If Doll isn't earned, her perception stays closed — reopen via Sael or the apprentice; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SUN_001 (protecting the deep groves she vigils); Q_MAJOR_001 (the deep mystery); the Mourner old-songs parallel.
**Scaling Notes:** Lore-weight scales with the player's progress on the central mystery; minimal combat scaling.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (oblique far-echo, hopeful-contrast), RELATIONSHIPS (Doll, Sael), FACTION_STATE (grove-faith).
**Related:** `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/MYSTERY_WEB.md`, `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_001).

# Q_SUN_003_THE_LOST_PILGRIMS — The Lost Pilgrims

**Quest Type:** Rescue / travel / investigation. **Level Range:** 8–11; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Survival DC 16 (the Green Roads/deep wilds); Investigation DC 16 (where they vanished); Insight DC 15; Perception DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** Greenway-Warden Tamsin Greenway keeps the Green Roads safe, but pilgrims keep wandering into the "aching" deep wilds and not returning — and she needs a capable hand to find them before more are lost.
**Location:** The Green Roads; the troubled deep-groves; the Sunward Wilds.
**Primary NPCs:** Greenway-Warden Tamsin Greenway (giver), Grove-Guide Sef Green (the deep-wilds route), Grove-Healer Sun-Sister Onn (the rescued's "green-sickness"), lost-pilgrim "Sun" Mott (a survivor, minor).
**Factions:** Independent (grove-tribes); brushes Doss Sunward's bandits.
**Surface Problem:** Find and rescue the pilgrims lost in the deep wilds.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Tamsin's record of where pilgrims vanish **maps the faint southern edge of the crisis** without any theory — the "aching" deep-groves are where the far root-ache is faintly felt. The lost are mostly lost to ordinary deep-wilds dangers, with one or two near the faint wrongness. Keep the cause oblique; the living faith still mostly holds. No mechanism.
**Objectives:** 1) Map where the pilgrims vanished (Tamsin's record). 2) Search the deep wilds (Sef Green). 3) Rescue the lost; learn what (faintly) draws them astray.
**Approaches:** Follow Tamsin's vanishing-map; search with Sef Green's guidance; treat the rescued's "green-sickness" (Onn); investigate the "aching" deep-grove; ward the Green Roads against both bandits and the wilds.
**Obstacles:** The deep wilds' ordinary dangers; bandits (Doss Sunward's edge); the faint wrongness near the aching grove; pilgrims' superstition.
**Important Scenes:** Tamsin's map of vanishings clustering near one deep-grove; finding a dazed pilgrim with "green-sickness"; the faint wrongness at the aching grove (oblique).
**Clues:** The vanishing-map (the crisis's faint southern *edge* — oblique, no theory); "green-sickness" in the deep-wilds-touched (M5-faint, living-faith); the living faith mostly holding (the hopeful contrast).
**Combat Options:** Deep-wilds beasts or grove-bandits (**Beast/Bandit-like**, scaled L8–11, telegraphed) — avoidable by route/stealth with Sef.
**Noncombat Options:** Tracking, healing, investigation, road-warding — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Sef Green's guidance (telegraph deep-wilds danger); Tamsin's wardens as backup; the rescued can be found alive; retreat is easy.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests on the Green Roads; a soft clock (more vanish if delayed).
**Rewards:** Tamsin Greenway's trust and safe-passage; the rescued pilgrims' gratitude; Onn's green-healing; the faint vanishing-map (an oblique edge-of-crisis clue); the green's welcome.
**Consequences:** Rescuing the lost and mapping the edge advances the player's faint understanding. Failing means more vanish and the Green Roads grow feared.
**Failure State:** Pilgrims keep vanishing → the Green Roads close to travel; reopen the search; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SUN_002 (the deep-grove ache); Q_SUN_001 (the bandits/cutters); the southern crisis-edge.
**Scaling Notes:** L8 — a few lost. L11 — a wave of vanishings near a strongly-aching grove.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (crisis southern edge), RELATIONSHIPS (Tamsin, Sef, Onn), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_SUN_004_THE_GREEN_WELCOME — The Green Welcome

**Quest Type:** Social / low-stakes / sanctuary. **Level Range:** 8–11; solo danger: low. **Secrecy:** player-safe.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 15 (Sun-Mother Mab / Grove-Elder Doss Sunhollow); Insight DC 15; Nature DC 14 (grove-custom).
**Starting Hook:** The Great Grove at Sunhollow is closed to outsiders by custom, and only by earning Sun-Mother Mab's welcome (and disarming Grove-Elder Doss Sunhollow's distrust) can the player gain the green's sanctuary, healing, and lore.
**Location:** The Great Grove at Sunhollow; the Grove-Camps.
**Primary NPCs:** Welcomer Sun-Mother Mab (the social key), Grove-Elder Doss Sunhollow (the distrustful gate), Grove-Keeper Sael Sunmark (the ultimate welcome), Grove-keeper's-aide Green-Tib (a friendly path, minor).
**Factions:** Independent (grove-tribes).
**Surface Problem:** Earn the grove-tribes' welcome to the sacred Great Grove.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The Sunmark's social on-ramp — establishing the grove-welcome as the gate to *everything* here (sanctuary, healing, lore, the hopeful contrast). A gentle, humane quest of respect and custom. Introduces recurring grove contacts. No central-mystery content.
**Objectives:** 1) Show respect for the green and its custom. 2) Win Sun-Mother Mab's welcome and ease Doss Sunhollow's distrust. 3) Gain the grove's sanctuary.
**Approaches:** Honor a grove-rite; help with a grove-camp's need; prove the player means no harm to the green; let Green-Tib vouch; demonstrate respect to Doss Sunhollow.
**Obstacles:** Doss Sunhollow's distrust of all settled-folk; grove-custom the player doesn't know; the tribes' wariness after the cutters' depredations.
**Important Scenes:** The Great Grove's hospitality offered (or withheld); Doss Sunhollow's hard questions; Sun-Mother Mab's warm welcome once earned.
**Clues:** None central (the green's welcome and the living faith, lived).
**Combat Options:** None expected (this is a hospitality quest).
**Noncombat Options:** Respect, service, persuasion, vouching — the whole quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat at all; the worst failure is a closed grove, not danger; multiple paths to welcome.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests once welcomed; no clock.
**Rewards:** The grove-tribes' welcome and the Great Grove's sanctuary, healing, and lore; recurring grove contacts; the gate to Sael and Doll; the green's friendship.
**Consequences:** Earning the welcome opens the whole region. Offending the custom closes it (and the hopeful contrast) for a while.
**Failure State:** The grove stays closed → the player must earn it another way (service, vouching); no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SUN_001 (defending the green that welcomed you); Q_SUN_002 (the deep lore); grove-faction goodwill.
**Scaling Notes:** Low-level throughout; no combat scaling.
**State Updates:** RELATIONSHIPS (Sun-Mother Mab, Doss Sunhollow, Sael), FACTION_STATE (grove-tribes), NPC_MEMORY.

# Q_SUN_005_THE_FAIR_TRADE — The Fair Trade

**Quest Type:** Faction (anti-Ledger) / trade / honest-broker. **Level Range:** 9–12; solo danger: low–moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 16 (the tribes / Honest Mab); Investigation DC 16 (Doss & Iss Sunward's scheme); Insight DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** Resin-Trader "Honest" Mab Sunward buys grove-resin *with* the tribes' consent and grove-custom — and Doss Sunward's greedy, Ledger-backed cutting is undercutting and threatening to destroy her honest trade. She needs an ally to keep fair trade alive in the south.
**Location:** The grove-edge; the Green Roads; the southern plains' edge.
**Primary NPCs:** Resin-Trader "Honest" Mab Sunward (giver/ally), Plains-Factor Doss Sunward (antagonist), Southern-Agent Iss Sunward (Ledger backer), Plains-Drover Doss Marrow (a decent settled route, secondary).
**Factions:** Cinder Ledger (edge-trade, antagonist), independent (honest trade & grove-tribes).
**Surface Problem:** Keep Honest Mab's fair grove-trade alive against Doss Sunward's Ledger-backed greed.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** A clean economic/moral quest showing the *right* way to engage the groves (consent and custom) vs. the Ledger's extractive way — the Sunmark face of Q_MAJOR_004. Protecting fair trade protects the living faith's autonomy. No central-mystery content.
**Objectives:** 1) Understand both trades (fair vs. extractive). 2) Strengthen Honest Mab / undercut Doss & the Ledger. 3) Keep the groves trading on their own terms.
**Approaches:** Open fair-trade routes (Doss Marrow); expose Doss & Iss Sunward's scheme; win the tribes to Honest Mab; out-bargain or shame the Ledger arm; broker grove-consent terms.
**Obstacles:** The Ledger's coin; Doss Sunward's plausibility; the tribes' suspicion of all traders; the price-undercutting.
**Important Scenes:** Honest Mab trading with grove-consent; Doss Sunward's extractive lowball; a tribe choosing whom to trust.
**Clues:** The Ledger's extractive southern edge-arm (Q_MAJOR_004); the contrast of consent vs. extraction (a clean moral frame).
**Combat Options:** Doss's toughs if pressured (**Guard/Bandit-like**) — avoidable.
**Noncombat Options:** Trade, exposure, brokering, route-opening — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; Honest Mab and the tribes as allies; multiple resolutions; the Ledger prefers profit to force.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at grove-edge rests; a trade-season soft-clock.
**Rewards:** Honest Mab as a fair-trade ally and southern route; the tribes' trust; leverage over the Ledger's southern arm; coin; a route to the settled south (Doss Marrow).
**Consequences:** Strengthening fair trade keeps the groves autonomous. Letting the Ledger win opens the groves to extraction (Q_MAJOR_004; feeds the cutting in Q_SUN_001).
**Failure State:** Honest Mab driven out → the Ledger's extractive trade dominates; reopen via the tribes; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SUN_001 (the cutting the Ledger funds); Q_MAJOR_004 (the continental Ledger); travel to the settled south.
**Scaling Notes:** L9 — a local trade-rivalry. L12 — a Ledger southern push.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Ledger southern arm, grove-tribes), RELATIONSHIPS (Honest Mab, Doss Sunward, Doss Marrow), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_SUN_006_THE_RECLAIMED_RUIN — The Reclaimed Ruin

**Rewards:** Concord Script lore; Wenn Green as a (respectful) Remnant contact; "Old Root's" deepest grove-lore; the hopeful contrast made vivid; the tribes' deeper trust if honored.
**Consequences:** A respectful study honors both faith and scholarship. Letting the Remnant defile the green offends the tribes (and the hopeful contrast). The grove-faith's success-where-Concord-failed is a key oblique hint.
**Failure State:** The ruin defiled / permission refused → the tribes harden or the lore stays closed; reopen respectfully; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SUN_002 (the deep-grove memory); Q_MAJOR_011/012 (the Remnant chain); the hopeful "just rite" thread.

# Q_SUN_007_THE_DEFILER_ANSWERED — The Defiler Answered

**Quest Type:** Climax / environmental reckoning / moral choice. **Level Range:** 11–13; solo danger: high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Nature DC 17 (the wilds' answer); Persuasion DC 17 (Doss Sunward, too late?); Insight DC 16; Survival DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** Doss Sunward has defiled a deep grove — and now the living wilds are *answering*, turning lethal against him, his cutters, and anyone caught in the green's wrath. The tribes ask the player to end it justly before the wilds' fury spreads.
**Location:** A defiled deep grove; the Sunward Wilds; the grove-edge.
**Primary NPCs:** Grove-Keeper Sael Sunmark (the only one who can calm the wilds), Plains-Factor Doss Sunward (the defiler, now imperiled), Grove-Warden Sela Green (the defense), Sun-Singer Doll (reads the wilds' grief).
**Factions:** Independent (grove-tribes & the living wilds); Cinder Ledger (Doss's backer, now fleeing).
**Surface Problem:** End the living wilds' lethal wrath after Doss Sunward's defilement — justly.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The campaign's vivid proof the grove-faith is *real and potent* (the living wilds answer a defiler) — the strongest demonstration of the hopeful contrast (a faith for the green/dead that *works*). The moral choice: save the defiler or let the wilds take him; calm the green or exploit the chaos. Sael's calming-rite is the true solution. No central-mystery content; the faith's potency is the point.
**Objectives:** 1) Survive/navigate the wilds' wrath. 2) Calm the living green (Sael's rite). 3) Decide Doss Sunward's fate (save, judge, or abandon him).
**Approaches:** Reach Sael and protect his calming-rite; defend survivors from the wilds; decide whether to save Doss (mercy/use) or let the green take him (justice); learn the faith's potency from Doll.
**Obstacles:** The living wilds' lethal fury (animate green, beasts, blight); Doss's panic; the cutters' deaths; the time pressure as the wrath spreads.
**Important Scenes:** The deep grove's living wrath made manifest; Doss Sunward begging amid the green's fury; Sael's calming-rite as the world hangs in balance.
**Clues:** The living faith's potency (the wilds *answer* — the hopeful contrast at full strength); the green grieving a defilement (Doll's reading).
**Combat Options:** The roused wilds (**Awakened-tree/Beast/Blight-like**, scaled L11–13, telegraphed by the grove's grief) — survivable by reaching the rite, not by fighting the forest.
**Noncombat Options:** The calming-rite, protection, judgment, mercy — fighting the wilds is the *wrong* tool.
**Solo Safety Valves:** The solution is Sael's rite (not a fight with a forest — telegraph this clearly); Sela Green and the tribes as allies; retreat from the deep grove; the wrath is targeted at the defilers first.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** No rest in the roused grove (a spreading-wrath clock); rest after at the camps.
**Rewards:** The tribes' deepest trust and the green's lasting alliance; a vivid demonstration of a faith that works (the hopeful contrast); Doss's fate as the player decides; Sael and Doll as committed allies.
**Consequences:** Calming the green justly cements the player as the groves' friend. Exploiting the chaos or letting the wrath spread burns the hopeful contrast and the tribes' trust. Doss's fate sets a lasting precedent.
**Failure State:** The wrath spreads → the wilds turn against settled-folk widely; the player loses the grove-alliance; reopen via Sael's mercy; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SUN_001/005 (the defilement's roots); Q_SUN_002 (the faith's deep power); the endgame "just rite" thread (very oblique).
**Scaling Notes:** L11 — one defiled grove. L13 — a spreading wrath across the deep wilds.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (grove-tribes, Ledger), RELATIONSHIPS (Sael, Doll, Sela, Doss's fate), CONSEQUENCES, HIDDEN_CLUES (hopeful-contrast).
