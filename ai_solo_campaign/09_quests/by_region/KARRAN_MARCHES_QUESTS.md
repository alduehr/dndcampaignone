## AI Use

Seven developed quests for the Karran Marches (a jagged outlaw-and-mining frontier walling the NE in the Karran Teeth; petty warlord holds and ungoverned wildland; Lvl 9–14). Cast from `08_npcs/by_region/KARRAN_MARCHES_NPCS.md`. A **second, harsher frontier mirroring the Sundering Reach** — cold stone, mines, feuds, rule of the strong. The Old Iron forts are **thin, peripheral Concord ruins**, NOT the keystone; their stirring dead corroborate the spread through a brutal mining-frontier lens. No NPC knows the apex truth. Anchors: Brask's Hold, Karran-Gate, the Deep Cuts, the Old Iron forts.

# Q_KM_001_THE_DEEP_THAT_WENT_WRONG — The Deep That Went Wrong

**Quest Type:** Dungeon (mine) / investigation / faction-patron. **Level Range:** 9–12; solo danger: high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Investigation DC 17 (the deep shaft); Survival DC 16 (the mine); Con saves DC 15 (thin-touch/old iron); Persuasion DC 16 ("Iron" Brask).
**Starting Hook:** Warlord "Iron" Brask's richest mine in the Deep Cuts has "gone wrong" — diggers come back changed, the deepest gallery has been sealed — and he'll pay a straight-dealing outsider well to clear it, because his own men won't go down.
**Location:** A sealed deep gallery in the Deep Cuts; Brask's Hold.
**Primary NPCs:** Warlord "Iron" Brask (patron/giver), Deep-Cuts Foreman Brann Karr (the mine, thin-touch witness), deep-mad "Cold" Coll (a changed digger), Hold-Steward Mab Stone (turnable insider).
**Factions:** Independent (Brask's hold).
**Surface Problem:** Clear the "gone-wrong" deep mine for Brask.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The deep gallery has cut into a **peripheral Old Iron (Concord) vein/ruin** — the "old iron" hums, the dead stir, diggers take the Karran's version of thin-touch. Corroborates the spread (M2/thin-touch at the far NE), NEVER the keystone. Brask frames it as "bad ground." Settle/seal it, not "master" it. Keep the mechanism unexplained.
**Objectives:** 1) Descend and find what went wrong. 2) Settle or seal the threat (the stirring dead / the humming vein). 3) Get Brask's diggers out safe.
**Approaches:** Descend with Brann Karr's guidance; seal the vein rather than mine it; settle the stirring dead (a Mourner token from "Grey" Tess); evacuate and wall it off; recover a changed digger.
**Obstacles:** The mine's dark and depth; the stirring deep dead; thin-touch exposure; Brask's expectation of a *fix* (not a retreat); the temptation of the "old iron."
**Important Scenes:** A sealed gallery the diggers won't pass; the "humming old iron" vein; "Cold" Coll's changed, dread-filled testimony.
**Clues:** Old Iron (Concord) reaching the far NE deep (M2 — the works are everywhere, thinly); thin-touch at the frontier's far edge; the dead stirring at a peripheral ruin (NOT the keystone).
**Combat Options:** Stirring deep dead (**Wight/Specter-like**, scaled L9–12) in a tight, dark mine — extremely dangerous in the deep; thinnable by token/light, avoidable by sealing.
**Noncombat Options:** Sealing, settling, evacuation, careful routing — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Brann Karr's guidance and miners' tools; sealing > fighting (telegraph that the deep is lethal); retreat up the shaft; the dead are slow in tight ground.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** No safe rest in the deep (air/dark clock); rest at the hold.
**Rewards:** Brask's straight-dealing patronage and a frontier base; mine-wealth/"old iron" salvage (with thin-touch risk); an M2 far-NE data-point; Brann Karr's trust.
**Consequences:** Sealing it earns Brask and proves the spread. "Mastering"/mining it (greed) spreads the wrongness (a Karran clock). Failing loses Brask's respect and the mine.
**Failure State:** The deep wrongness spreads → more changed diggers, a hold-wide dread; Brask cools but doesn't turn enemy; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_KM_002 (the Old Iron forts proper); Q_KM_004 (Mully Karr's "old iron" trade); Q_KM_006 (the Remnant's interest).
**Scaling Notes:** L9 — one gallery. L12 — a deep Old Iron ruin behind the mine and a worse stirring.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (M2 far-NE), FACTION_STATE (Brask), RELATIONSHIPS (Brask, Brann Karr, Mab Stone), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md`, `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/CLUE_INDEX.md`.

# Q_KM_002_THE_OLD_IRON_FORT — The Old Iron Fort

**Quest Type:** Dungeon (Concord ruin) / faction (Remnant) / escort. **Level Range:** 11–14; solo danger: high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** History/Arcana DC 18 (Script); Survival DC 17 (the high forts); Investigation DC 17; Con saves DC 16 (the stirring dead).
**Starting Hook:** Reclaimer-Captain Wenna Stone loses people every season in the Old Iron forts and is half-broken by the cost — she'll take the player into a fort to recover a specific relic, and (privately) hopes someone will tell her the works should be *feared*, not catalogued.
**Location:** An Old Iron fort high in the Karran Teeth; Karran-Gate.
**Primary NPCs:** Reclaimer-Captain Wenna Stone (giver, turnable conscience), Fort-Guide "Old Iron" Doss (the survivable route), Sub-Lector Wenn Karr (her fraying deputy), fort-mad prophet "Iron" (atmospheric witness).
**Factions:** Concord Remnant (Wenna's expedition), independent (Brask sells fort-access).
**Surface Problem:** Help the Remnant recover a relic from a deadly Old Iron fort.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The Old Iron forts are **peripheral Concord ruins** (the empire reached even here, thinly) — their stirring dead corroborate the spread, NOT the keystone. Wenna Stone is the **most disillusion-prone Remnant field officer** — ground down toward *fearing* the works. A frontier player can turn her. Script-fragments are M6-oblique, never the mechanism.
**Objectives:** 1) Reach and enter the fort (Old Iron Doss). 2) Recover the relic / read the Script (oblique). 3) Decide what to tell Wenna Stone about the works.
**Approaches:** Delve with Doss's guidance; help Wenna's team survive (lowering the cost that's breaking her); translate Script (gated, oblique); steer Wenna toward fear-of-the-works (turning her) or feed her order's ambition.
**Obstacles:** The fort's stirring dead; the high-peaks approach; the Remnant's reckless quotas; Wenn Karr's fraying nerve.
**Important Scenes:** A fort's dead rising as the team logs vaults; Wenna Stone counting her losses; a Script-fragment that hints (oblique) the works are something to fear.
**Clues:** Peripheral Concord ruins at the far NE (M2/M6-oblique — the works are continent-wide); the dead stirring worse each season (the spread); Wenna's growing dread (a turnable Remnant conscience).
**Combat Options:** Fort-dwelling stirring dead (**Wight/Specter/Animated-armor-like**, scaled L11–14) in a ruined fort — deadly; thin by route/token/light; retreat with Doss.
**Noncombat Options:** Guided delving, Script-scholarship, turning Wenna, careful retreat — the worst combat is avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Old Iron Doss is the survival-key (telegraph the forts kill); Wenna's expedition-hands; retreat routes; the relic can be reached without clearing the fort.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** A wary camp at the fort's edge (Doss's safe-spot); the forts themselves give no rest.
**Rewards:** Concord Script lore; the relic (for the Remnant or the player); Wenna Stone as a hard ally and a turnable conscience; an M2/M6-oblique far-NE clue.
**Consequences:** Turning Wenna toward fear seeds a future Remnant schism (good). Feeding the order's ambition deepens the dangerous reclaim-drive. Losing her people breaks her faster.
**Failure State:** A failed delve — losses mount, the relic stays; Wenna survives, closer to disillusion; reopen the lead; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_KM_001 (the deep mines); Q_MAJOR_011/012 (the Remnant chain to the Crown); Wenna's possible defection.
**Scaling Notes:** L11 — an outer fort. L14 — a deep fort and a season's worth of stirred dead.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (M2/M6-oblique), FACTION_STATE (Remnant NE expedition), RELATIONSHIPS (Wenna, Doss), KNOWN_CLUES (Script).
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_011/012), `../../07_factions/major_factions/CONCORD_REMNANT.md`.

# Q_KM_003_THE_STRONG_HAND — The Strong Hand

**Quest Type:** Faction-patron (warlord) / war / moral choice. **Level Range:** 10–14; solo danger: high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion/Intimidation DC 17 (the warlords); Insight DC 16 ("Red" Karr); Investigation DC 16 (the coalition); combat-tactics if it comes to war.
**Starting Hook:** Rival warlord "Red" Karr is building a coalition to break "Iron" Brask — and Brask, who deals straight and never lies, offers the player a place in the coming war, while "Red" Karr offers a richer, dirtier deal to switch sides.
**Location:** Brask's Hold; "Red" Karr's fortress; the contested holds.
**Primary NPCs:** Warlord "Iron" Brask (patron), Hold-Captain Doss Brask (his war-leader), Rival Warlord "Red" Karr (rival/temptation), Hold-Steward Mab Stone (turnable), Mercenary-Captain "Coin" Stone (a recruitable company).
**Factions:** Independent (warlord holds).
**Surface Problem:** Take a side (or broker) in the coming warlord war.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** A clean rule-of-the-strong moral quest mirroring the player's home frontier — straight-dealing Brask vs. treacherous-but-richer "Red" Karr. The choice is character-defining (honor vs. profit). No central-mystery content; the "deep going wrong" is a background pressure both warlords ignore.
**Objectives:** 1) Assess the coming war. 2) Choose a side, broker peace, or play both. 3) Live with the consequences for the player's Karran standing.
**Approaches:** Fight for Brask (straight, honorable); take "Red" Karr's richer/dirtier deal; broker a peace (hard — the holds respect strength, not talk); hire "Coin" Stone's company; turn an insider (Mab Stone).
**Obstacles:** Brask's deadly host; "Red" Karr's treachery; the holds' fickleness; the war's cost.
**Important Scenes:** Brask's blunt, honest offer; "Red" Karr's silky, richer temptation; a hold-skirmish or a parley.
**Clues:** None central (a character-test of honor vs. profit; the warlord-frontier in miniature).
**Combat Options:** Full warlord-host warfare (**Veteran/Berserker-like** companies; Brask is a **boss-class** Tier 1, AC 18, ~100 HP) — extremely dangerous; avoidable by brokering or choosing the winning side early.
**Noncombat Options:** Diplomacy, brokering, side-switching, mercenary-hire, insider-turning — war can be avoided or shaped.
**Solo Safety Valves:** The player need not fight the warlords personally; allies (a hired company, Brask's host); brokering avoids the war; clear retreat from the Marches.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at whichever hold backs the player; a war-mobilization clock.
**Rewards:** A warlord-patron and frontier base (Brask if backed honorably; "Red" Karr if not); war-spoils; a mercenary company's loyalty; a defining reputation.
**Consequences:** Backing Brask gains an honest, deadly ally. Backing "Red" Karr gains coin and a treacherous one. Brokering (if achieved) makes the player a respected power-broker.
**Failure State:** Picking the losing side — the player loses a patron and a hold, but survives; reposition with the winner or leave; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_KM_001 (the patron's mines); Q_KM_007 (the refugees the war creates); the Karran's power-balance.
**Scaling Notes:** L10 — a hold-skirmish. L14 — full warlord war with mercenary companies.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (warlord holds), RELATIONSHIPS (Brask, Red Karr, Mab Stone, Coin Stone), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_KM_004_THE_HUMMING_IRON — The Humming Iron

**Quest Type:** Faction (anti-Ledger) / smuggling / clue trail. **Level Range:** 10–13; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 16 (Mully Karr); Investigation DC 17 (the "old iron" trade); Insight DC 16; Stealth DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** Ore-Factor Mully Karr trades "old iron" — Concord salvage, some of it "humming and strange" — south for prices she doesn't understand, and the Cinder Ledger is muscling in on the lucrative, dangerous trade. She wants a guard and a partner who won't ask why the iron's strange.
**Location:** Karran-Gate; the trade-road south; the Ledger's northern post.
**Primary NPCs:** Ore-Factor Mully Karr (giver/ally), Northern-Factor Iss Tallow (Ledger antagonist), Ore-assayer Doune (the "strange iron" lead, minor), Gate-fence Vask (the relic-chain, minor).
**Factions:** Cinder Ledger (antagonist), independent (Mully Karr's trade).
**Surface Problem:** Help Mully Karr's "old iron" trade against the Ledger's encroachment.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The "humming old iron" is **Remembrance-relic / Concord salvage** seeping south from the Old Iron forts and Deep Cuts — proof the relic-phenomenon reaches the far NE (M2/M3-oblique). Mully Karr is an unwitting harvester-relic conduit; she understands nothing. Keep the "old iron" oblique; never explain Remembrance here.
**Objectives:** 1) Protect/partner Mully Karr's trade. 2) Trace where the "humming iron" comes from and goes. 3) Decide what to do about it (and the Ledger).
**Approaches:** Guard a metals-caravan; break the Ledger's squeeze (Iss Tallow); trace the "old iron" up to the Deep Cuts/forts or down to its southern buyers; partner Mully honestly; quietly pull the worst relics off the market.
**Obstacles:** The Ledger's gold; feud-raided routes; the relics' danger (thin-touch); Mully's mercenary "don't ask" stance.
**Important Scenes:** A "humming iron" piece on the assayer's bench; the Ledger's Iss Tallow muscling in; tracing a relic to a "gone-wrong" mine.
**Clues:** Remembrance-relics at the far NE ("humming old iron" — M2/M3-oblique); the relic-trade chain (where it goes); the Ledger's northern relic-arm (Q_MAJOR_004).
**Combat Options:** Feud-raiders (**Bandit/Scout-like**) on the trade-road; Ledger toughs — both avoidable by escort/negotiation.
**Noncombat Options:** Trade, guard-work, investigation, market-manipulation — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; Mully's caravan-guards; multiple resolutions; the Ledger prefers profit to force.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at Karran-Gate; a caravan/market soft-clock.
**Rewards:** Mully Karr as a fair trade-ally and relic-source; leverage over the Ledger's northern arm; coin; an M2/M3-oblique relic-trail toward the central mystery.
**Consequences:** Tracing the "old iron" links the far NE to the relic-mystery. Pulling the worst relics protects the south. Letting the Ledger win deepens the monopoly (Q_MAJOR_004).
**Failure State:** The Ledger takes the trade → Mully withdraws frontier-access; the "humming iron" flows unchecked; reopen via Mully or the assayer; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_KM_001/002 (the "old iron's" source); Q_MAJOR_004 (the continental Ledger).
**Scaling Notes:** L10 — a caravan. L13 — a Ledger trade-war and a worse relic-supply.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (M2/M3 far-NE relics), FACTION_STATE (Ledger northern arm), RELATIONSHIPS (Mully, Iss Tallow), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_004), `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/CLUE_INDEX.md`.

# Q_KM_005_THE_GATE_WITH_NO_LAW — The Gate With No Law

**Quest Type:** Regional / town-trouble / mercenary work. **Level Range:** 9–13; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** player-safe.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 16 (Gate-Boss Sera Gate); Insight DC 16; Investigation DC 15; Intimidation DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** Lawless Karran-Gate teeters on the edge of burning — feuds, raiders, refugees, and southern factions all press it — and Gate-Boss Sera Gate, who keeps the town from chaos by nerve alone, needs a capable, neutral hand.
**Location:** Karran-Gate (mining-and-mercenary town); the Broken Pick.
**Primary NPCs:** Gate-Boss Sera Gate (giver), Gate-innkeep Marrec (the rumor-mill, minor), Mercenary-Captain "Coin" Stone (a recruitable hand), Pass-Toll Warlord Hessa Teeth (the route out, secondary).
**Factions:** Independent (the Gate); brushes against all.
**Surface Problem:** Help Sera Gate keep lawless Karran-Gate from exploding.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The Karran's "neutral ground" hub — a frontier-town quest establishing Karran-Gate as a base, rumor-mill, and route between the Marches and the settled south (via Hessa Teeth). The refugees backing up are a quiet sign of the deep-wrongness driving folk out (oblique). No central-mystery gate.
**Objectives:** 1) Identify the Gate's worst immediate threat. 2) Defuse it (a feud spilling in, a raider-push, a faction power-grab, a refugee crisis). 3) Establish standing in the town.
**Approaches:** Mediate a feud spilling into the Gate; break a raider-push (hire "Coin" Stone); expose a faction's power-grab; manage the refugee influx with Hessa Teeth; back Sera Gate's authority.
**Obstacles:** The town's lawlessness; competing pressures; the refugee strain; the factions' meddling.
**Important Scenes:** The Broken Pick as a powder-keg of sell-swords and traders; Sera Gate holding the town by sheer nerve; a refugee column at the gate.
**Clues:** Refugees fleeing the "going-wrong" deep (a population-flow sign — oblique corroboration, no theory).
**Combat Options:** Bar-brawls, a raider-push, feud-spillover (**Bandit/Guard/Veteran-like**) — mostly avoidable by defusing the cause.
**Noncombat Options:** Mediation, exposure, refugee-management, hiring muscle — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; Sera Gate and "Coin" Stone's company as allies; multiple threats to address; clear retreat.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Lodging at the Broken Pick; a "powder-keg" soft-clock.
**Rewards:** Sera Gate's trust and Karran-Gate as a base/rumor-hub; a mercenary company's availability; Hessa Teeth's route south; coin and standing.
**Consequences:** Steadying the Gate gives the player a frontier base. Failing lets the town burn (losing the Marches' one neutral ground).
**Failure State:** The Gate erupts in violence → the town's a war-zone for a while; the player loses a base; reopen by helping rebuild; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_KM_003 (the feuds feeding the Gate); Q_KM_007 (the refugees); travel south via Hessa Teeth.
**Scaling Notes:** L9 — a single town-threat. L13 — multiple converging crises.
**State Updates:** RELATIONSHIPS (Sera Gate, Coin Stone, Hessa Teeth), FACTION_STATE (the Gate), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_KM_006_THE_DEEP_LISTENER — The Deep Listener

**Quest Type:** Faction (anti-Gravecaller) / mystery / moral dilemma. **Level Range:** 11–14; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Insight DC 17 (the "Deep-Listener"); Investigation DC 16; Persuasion DC 16 (the holds' fearful folk); Religion DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** A hollow-eyed stranger called "the Deep-Listener" has come to the Old Iron forts and Deep Cuts, "hearing the old iron's dead" — and the superstitious holds are about to hunt them as a witch, while the grieving quietly gather to listen.
**Location:** The Old Iron forts / Deep Cuts edges; Karran-Gate.
**Primary NPCs:** Wanderer "the Deep-Listener" (Gravecaller emissary, antagonist), Hold-Mourner "Grey" Tess (the humane counter), Reclaimer-Captain Wenna Stone (her forts are the draw), a frightened hold-folk (Mine-widow Sera, minor).
**Factions:** Gravecallers (DM-labeled), Mourners' Circle (thin presence), independent (the holds).
**Surface Problem:** Deal with the outsider "hearing the dead" before the holds hunt them — or before they spread.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The Deep-Listener is a **Gravecaller emissary** drawn to the far NE's worst stirring (the forts and deep mines) — the cult's far-region toehold. A moral dilemma: the holds' grief is real, the lynch-mob is unjust, the cult's "listening" is dangerous. They know no apex truth; keep fragments oblique. "Grey" Tess's true grief is the humane counter.
**Objectives:** 1) Understand the Deep-Listener's draw and danger. 2) Prevent both a lynching and a cult-rooting. 3) Drive off, expose, protect, or (hard) turn them.
**Approaches:** Counter with "Grey" Tess's true rites; stop the holds' witch-hunt (justice); expose the cult-fragments; reach the grieving first; confront or banish the emissary; investigate the cult's far-reach.
**Obstacles:** The holds' superstition and violence; the genuine grief; the forts' stirring dead that "prove" the listening; the emissary's eerie calm.
**Important Scenes:** A hold mob forming against the Deep-Listener; the grieving gathering to "hear the old iron's dead"; "Grey" Tess offering true mourning instead.
**Clues:** The Gravecaller cult reaching the far NE (M3/cult-spread — DM-oblique); the worst stirring drawing the cult; true-rite vs. "listening" (the campaign's core moral tension).
**Combat Options:** A lynch-mob (**Commoner/Guard-like**) to *prevent*; cult-touched grieving or a fort-dead "answer" (scaled L11–14) — best avoided by reaching people first.
**Noncombat Options:** Counter-rite, justice, exposure, persuasion, banishment — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** The true solution is social/spiritual, not a fight; "Grey" Tess as ally; the mob can be turned with words; retreat is easy.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at Karran-Gate; a soft clock (the mob and the cult both escalate).
**Rewards:** "Grey" Tess's trust and the far-NE old-songs thread; the holds protected; intelligence on the Gravecaller far-spread; a just resolution.
**Consequences:** A humane resolution protects the holds and the emissary. Failing means a lynching (an injustice and a martyr) or a cult-rooting (a Karran clock).
**Failure State:** A lynching or a cult-cell — either way a grim far-region thread opens; reopen as an anti-cult or justice arc; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_KM_002 (the forts that draw the cult); the Gravecaller far-spread; "Grey" Tess's network thread.
**Scaling Notes:** L11 — a lone emissary. L14 — a rooted cell exploiting the forts' worst dead.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Gravecaller far-toehold, Mourners), RELATIONSHIPS (Grey Tess, the Deep-Listener), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_KM_007_THE_PASS_OUT — The Pass Out

**Quest Type:** Travel / escort / humane choice. **Level Range:** 9–13; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 16 (Hessa Teeth); Survival DC 16 (the high pass); Insight DC 15; Investigation DC 15 (the refugee-flow).
**Starting Hook:** Pass-Toll Warlord Hessa Teeth holds the chief pass *out* of the Marches toward the settled south — and a flood of refugees fleeing the "going-wrong" deep is more than her gate can process, jamming the only way south.
**Location:** Hessa Teeth's pass-fort; the high Karran Teeth passes; the refugee column.
**Primary NPCs:** Pass-Toll Warlord Hessa Teeth (giver/gatekeeper), Settled-refugee "South" Mott (a fleeing witness, minor), Mountain-guide Brun (the high pass, minor), Caravan-guard Sef (escort, minor).
**Factions:** Independent (Hessa's gate); brushes the holds.
**Surface Problem:** Clear the jammed pass and deal with the refugee crisis (and the toll).
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The refugees backing up at the gate are a **quiet, concrete sign the deep-wrongness is driving folk out of the Marches** — corroboration with no theory attached. Establishes the player's route *out* of the Karran to the settled south, and a turnable lesser-warlord. Keep the cause oblique.
**Objectives:** 1) Understand the refugee-flow and the toll-jam. 2) Ease the crisis (process the refugees, fund or fight the toll, escort folk through). 3) Open the route south.
**Approaches:** Negotiate or reduce Hessa's toll (or pay it); organize the refugee processing; escort a column over the high pass (Brun); investigate why so many flee (oblique); turn Hessa from greed toward order.
**Obstacles:** Hessa's exact, merciless toll; the high-pass dangers; the refugees' desperation; rival warlords coveting the gate.
**Important Scenes:** A jammed pass-gate with desperate refugees; Hessa Teeth's fair-but-merciless tariff; a high-pass crossing with a refugee column.
**Clues:** Refugees fleeing the deep-wrongness (a population-flow corroboration — oblique, no theory); which holds are failing (Hessa's knowledge).
**Combat Options:** Pass-bandits or rival-warlord raiders (**Bandit/Scout-like**) on the high road — avoidable by escort/route.
**Noncombat Options:** Negotiation, organization, escort, investigation — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; Hessa's gate-guards and Brun's guidance; multiple resolutions incl. simply paying the toll; clear retreat.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Lodging at the pass-fort; a humanitarian soft-clock (the jam worsens).
**Rewards:** An open route south and Hessa Teeth as a turnable lesser-warlord ally; the refugees' goodwill; Hessa's knowledge of the failing holds; a humane reputation; coin.
**Consequences:** Easing the crisis opens the player's road south and a turnable ally. Failing jams the route and worsens the humanitarian crisis (a Karran clock).
**Failure State:** The pass stays jammed → the refugee crisis festers; the route south is harder; reopen later; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_KM_001/006 (the deep-wrongness the refugees flee); travel south to the Marrowdowns/settled regions.
**Scaling Notes:** L9 — a toll-negotiation. L13 — a full refugee crisis and rival-warlord pressure on the gate.
**State Updates:** RELATIONSHIPS (Hessa Teeth, refugees), FACTION_STATE (the pass, the holds), CONSEQUENCES, HIDDEN_CLUES (population-flow).
