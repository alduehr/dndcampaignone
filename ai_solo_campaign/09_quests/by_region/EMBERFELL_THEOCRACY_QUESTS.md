## AI Use

Eight developed quests for the Emberfell Theocracy / the Ashfast (volcanic fire-theocracy; buried works it was founded to contain; burns its dead so the crisis is *masked* here; Lvl 12–16). Cast from `08_npcs/by_region/EMBERFELL_THEOCRACY_NPCS.md`. **CRITICAL:** the Ashfast's buried works are a SEPARATE southern complex that *rhymes* with — but is NOT — the harvest/Hollow Court/keystone. The Pyre genuinely settles the dead (a real contrast). Never confirm the apex truth; never let the Forbidden Waste become "the real" endgame. Feeds Q_MAJOR_013 (The Fire That Mirrors Ours). Anchors: Ashfast, Cinderhold, the Ash Roads, the Forbidden Waste.

# Q_ET_001_THE_ASH_ROADS_PASSAGE — The Ash Roads Passage

**Quest Type:** Travel / social infiltration / negotiation. **Level Range:** 12–14; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion/Religion DC 17 (passing as no heretic); Insight DC 16 (the theocracy's law); Deception DC 18 if concealing northern faith; Survival DC 15 (volcanic roads).
**Starting Hook:** To enter the closed theocracy at all, the player must win safe-conduct on the Ash Roads from Pyre-Warden Sef Embren — who watches outsiders "the way one watches a spreading fire."
**Location:** The Ash Roads; the approach to Ashfast.
**Primary NPCs:** Pyre-Warden Sef Embren (gatekeeper), Pilgrim-Guide Doss Ash, Hidden Northern-Convert "Ash" Loy (secret aid), Pyre-Lieutenant Brann Embren.
**Factions:** Independent (Ashfast theocracy).
**Surface Problem:** Gain entry to the theocracy without being judged a heretic or "unburnt."
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The theocracy's fire-faith *genuinely keeps its dead from lingering* (burning severs lingering) — a real, important contrast a sharp player could later use. "Ash" Loy is a secret northerner who can witness the burial-vs-burning difference. No harvest content; the buried works stay sealed and distant here.
**Objectives:** 1) Win Embren's safe-conduct. 2) Travel the Ash Roads safely. 3) Avoid betraying any "heresy."
**Approaches:** Prove orthodoxy/respect to Embren; travel as a pilgrim with Doss Ash; quietly accept "Ash" Loy's coaching; earn a warrior's respect from Embren.
**Obstacles:** Embren's zealotry; Brann Embren's overreach; volcanic road hazards; the risk of revealing a northern faith.
**Important Scenes:** Embren's cold judgment at the road-gate; a pilgrim-road night under the Mountain's glow; "Ash" Loy's whispered guidance.
**Clues:** The fire-rite contrast (burning severs lingering — a real worldbuilding clue, kept practical not explained); the Ashfast's forbidding secrecy about the Waste.
**Combat Options:** A road-hazard (lava-vent, rockfall) or Brann Embren's overzealous patrol (**Veteran/Cultist-like**) — avoidable by compliance/guile.
**Noncombat Options:** Persuasion, pilgrimage, respect-earning, stealth — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Safe-conduct is winnable peacefully; "Ash" Loy and Doss Ash assist; the player can turn back; danger is telegraphed.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Pilgrim-waystations for rests; the Mountain's mood as soft pressure.
**Rewards:** Safe-conduct into the theocracy (gateway to all Emberfell quests); Embren's wary respect; the fire-rite contrast-knowledge; "Ash" Loy as a secret ally.
**Consequences:** Earning Embren's respect opens the Ashfast. Being judged a heretic bars the region (and marks the player for the Pyre). Ignored — the Emberfell stays closed.
**Failure State:** Judged a heretic — the player is expelled (not burned, if they flee); reopen via "Ash" Loy or a different pilgrimage; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_ET_002–008 (all gated behind entry); Q_MAJOR_013.
**Scaling Notes:** L12 — entry-trial. L14 — Embren's scrutiny intensifies; the Waste's edge grows dangerous.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Ashfast attitude), RELATIONSHIPS (Embren, Loy), NPC_MEMORY.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_013).

# Q_ET_002_THE_HERETICS_QUESTION — The Heretic's Question

**Quest Type:** Mystery clue trail / social infiltration. **Level Range:** 13–15; solo danger: low (political danger high). **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Insight DC 17 (reading Mira Cindra's double-layered speech); Persuasion DC 18; Stealth DC 17 (avoiding Pyre-Confessor Sera Cindra); Arcana/History DC 18 (forbidden lore).
**Starting Hook:** Ember-Sage Mira Cindra — orthodox in public, a heretic-scholar in private — watches the northern outsider "with the hunger of someone with forbidden questions" and asks, low: "what do your northern dead *do*?"
**Location:** Ashfast's lore-vaults; private corners of the fortress-temple.
**Primary NPCs:** Mira Cindra (giver/ally), Acolyte-Scribe Wenn Cindra (turnable), Pyre-Confessor Sera Cindra (danger — reads souls for heresy), Brother Vurr Cindra (watcher).
**Factions:** Independent (Ashfast); the Concord Remnant (forbidden correspondence).
**Surface Problem:** Trade knowledge with Mira Cindra without exposing her heresy.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Mira is the bridge between the Emberfell secret and the wider truth — but the Emberfell complex stays a *distinct southern thing*; she contributes a corroborating fragment, never the keystone or the Court. Protect her heresy (exposure means death). Keep her revelations gated and oblique.
**Objectives:** 1) Win Mira's trust. 2) Exchange frontier knowledge for Ashfast lore (gated). 3) Keep her secret from the Confessor.
**Approaches:** Honest exchange; smuggle her a forbidden text/relic from the Waste; carry her questions north; shield her from Sera Cindra.
**Obstacles:** Pyre-Confessor Sera's soul-reading; the Pyre's penalty for heresy; the difficulty of the forbidden lore.
**Important Scenes:** Mira's public-orthodox / private-probing double-speak; a near-discovery by the Confessor; a gated, oblique fragment about the buried works "changing."
**Clues:** The Ashfast's buried works are *changing* in ways its founding-texts never warned (oblique — a southern echo, NOT the keystone); the fire-rite's real mechanics (how burning severs lingering).
**Combat Options:** None by design (a fight exposes Mira); if cornered, flee — the Confessor's guards are **Veteran/Cultist-like**.
**Noncombat Options:** Scholarship, trust-building, secrecy, smuggling — the whole quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; Wenn Cindra and "Ash" Loy aid; the player controls how much they reveal; retreat preserves Mira.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Rests in guest-cells; discovery-risk rises with each meeting.
**Rewards:** Ashfast forbidden lore (gated); the fire-rite contrast; a brilliant endangered heretic-ally; a corroborating fragment for the player's understanding.
**Consequences:** Allying Mira gains a priceless, fragile ally. Exposing her (or being careless) gets her burned — a powerful indictment of the theocracy. Carrying her questions north links the Emberfell to the Remnant chain.
**Failure State:** Mira exposed → martyred; the player loses the ally but gains a moral lever against the theocracy; play continues.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_ET_005 (the Waste); Q_ET_007 (Remnant correspondence); Q_MAJOR_013.
**Scaling Notes:** L13 — guarded exchanges. L15 — the buried-works "changing" becomes an actionable lead.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (southern-echo, oblique), RELATIONSHIPS (Mira), FACTION_STATE (Ashfast danger), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_013), `../../03_canon/DM_ONLY_CANON.md`.

# Q_ET_003_THE_FORBIDDEN_SALVAGE — The Forbidden Salvage

**Quest Type:** Smuggling / faction (Ledger) / relic-trade. **Level Range:** 12–15; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Deception DC 17 (past the Pyre-Wardens); Stealth DC 16; Persuasion DC 16 (Doss Ashfast); Investigation DC 15 (the smuggling chain).
**Starting Hook:** Cinder-Master Doss Ashfast — a corrupt mining-lord beneath his ash-priest trappings — needs muscle to smuggle forbidden "old glass" out past the Pyre-Wardens for good coin.
**Location:** Cinderhold (the volcanic mining town); the Ash Roads; a smuggling rendezvous.
**Primary NPCs:** Doss Ashfast (giver), Mine-Foreman Sef Cinderhold ("ash-touched" crews), Smuggler-Factor "Vesk" (Ledger's Emberfell agent/buyer), Ash-Healer Mab Cinder.
**Factions:** Cinder Ledger (southern arm buyer), independent (Doss's corruption), Ashfast (the law being broken).
**Surface Problem:** Smuggle forbidden Remembrance-relic salvage out for profit.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** "Old glass" near the Waste = raw Remembrance relics (M2/M3, oblique); the deep-mine "ash-touched" sickness is the southern face of thin-touch. Proof the relic-phenomenon is continent-wide, here dug *beside* a related (distinct) southern complex. Doss knows nothing of the meaning. Never explain Remembrance.
**Objectives:** 1) Get the salvage past the Pyre-Wardens. 2) Deliver to the Ledger buyer (or divert it). 3) Avoid the wardens' notice.
**Approaches:** Smuggle by stealth/guile; bribe a warden; expose Doss's trade to the wardens (a lever); divert the relics to the Remnant or destroy them.
**Obstacles:** Pyre-Warden patrols; the relics' "humming"/thin-touch danger; the Ledger's pressure.
**Important Scenes:** A deep-mine where crews come back "ash-touched"; a tense warden-checkpoint; the Ledger buyer's cold transaction.
**Clues:** "Old glass" = raw relics near the buried works (M2 continental); thin-touch's southern face (M2/M10-oblique); the Ledger's continental relic-reach (M3).
**Combat Options:** A Pyre-Warden patrol (**Veteran/Cultist-like**) if the run is blown — avoidable by stealth/bribery; or mine-toughs if Doss is crossed.
**Noncombat Options:** Stealth, bribery, exposure, diversion — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Multiple paths; turnable foreman; the player can refuse/divert; retreat on the Ash Roads.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Rests in Cinderhold; a buyer-rendezvous deadline; thin-touch exposure as a soft attrition.
**Rewards:** Coin (Doss's cut); Ledger or Remnant goodwill by choice; a southern M2/M3 corroboration; possibly a relic [STAGE_14_FINALIZE].
**Consequences:** Smuggling feeds the Ledger's monopoly (Q_MAJOR_004). Exposing Doss ruins him (a lever or casualty). Diverting/destroying the relics is safest but earns no coin.
**Failure State:** Caught — the wardens seize the salvage and mark the player; reopen via a different mine or by turning on Doss; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_ET_002 (Mira); Q_ET_005 (the Waste); Q_MAJOR_004.
**Scaling Notes:** L12 — a simple run. L15 — heavy warden security and an "ash-touched" mine-incident.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (M2/M3 south), FACTION_STATE (Ledger/Ashfast), INVENTORY, NPC_MEMORY.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_004), `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/CLUE_INDEX.md`.

# Q_ET_004_THE_ASH_TOUCHED — The Ash-Touched

**Quest Type:** Investigation / rescue / haunted site (oblique). **Level Range:** 13–15; solo danger: moderate–high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Medicine DC 16 (Mab Cinder's craft); Investigation DC 17 (the deep-mine wrongness); Religion DC 16; Survival DC 16 (the deep cuts).
**Starting Hook:** Ash-Healer Mab Cinder is out of cures: Doss Ashfast's deep-mine crews come back "ash-touched and wrong," and a missing shift is trapped where the cuts edge the Forbidden Waste.
**Location:** Cinderhold's deep mines; the Waste's edge.
**Primary NPCs:** Mab Cinder (giver), Mine-Foreman Sef Cinderhold (frightened), the trapped/changed miners, Waste-Hermit "Old Ash" (oblique edge-lore).
**Factions:** Independent (Cinderhold), Ashfast (the Waste's law).
**Surface Problem:** Rescue the trapped shift; understand the "ash-touch" sickness.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The deep-mine wrongness is the southern thin-touch and the buried works' proximity (oblique). The miners' affliction echoes the frontier's thin-touch/Remembrance exposure. NEVER explain; the buried works are a distinct southern complex. "Old Ash" offers unreliable oblique edge-lore only.
**Objectives:** 1) Reach the trapped shift. 2) Bring them out (and treat the ash-touch). 3) Decide what to tell the wardens.
**Approaches:** A rescue delve; treat the sickness with Mab's craft + the player's frontier knowledge; consult "Old Ash" (oblique); avoid waking what's deeper.
**Obstacles:** The deadly deep cuts; the "ash-touched" changed miners (may be hostile/pitiable); the Waste's edge-danger; the wardens' suspicion.
**Important Scenes:** A deep cut where the rock "hums"; ash-touched miners, changed and frightening; "Old Ash" muttering of "the buried ones."
**Clues:** The southern thin-touch (M2/M10-oblique); the buried works' proximity (oblique, distinct); the contrast that fire-rite *can* settle even this (a hopeful practical clue).
**Combat Options:** Ash-touched miners (pitiable, scaled, AC 12) or a Waste-edge manifestation — settle-able/treatable rather than slain; telegraphed; retreat possible.
**Noncombat Options:** Rescue, medicine, rite, retreat — combat avoidable/regrettable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** The afflicted are help-able, not just enemies; Mab and Sef assist; clear retreat from the deep cuts; the Waste itself is avoidable.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Rests in Cinderhold; the trapped shift's air/time as a hard-ish clock.
**Rewards:** Cinderhold's gratitude; Mab Cinder as a healer-ally; a southern thin-touch data-point; the fire-rite contrast confirmed.
**Consequences:** Saving the shift earns deep local trust. Telling the wardens may seal the mine (and expose Doss). Ignored — the ash-touch spreads and Cinderhold dreads the deep.
**Failure State:** The shift lost → grief and a Cinderhold crisis-hook; the ash-touch thread persists; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_ET_003 (Doss); Q_ET_005 (the Waste proper); the fire-rite contrast (endgame-oblique).
**Scaling Notes:** L13 — a rescue. L15 — a deep-cut horror and a Waste-edge breach.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (thin-touch south), RELATIONSHIPS (Mab, Sef), FACTION_STATE (Ashfast/Cinderhold), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_ET_005_THE_WASTE_THAT_WAKES — The Waste That Wakes

**Quest Type:** Ruin / mystery (gated) / faction conflict. **Level Range:** 14–16; solo danger: high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Religion/Arcana DC 19 (the buried works — gated, oblique); Stealth DC 18 (past the wardens); Survival DC 17; Persuasion DC 18.
**Starting Hook:** The Forbidden Waste's buried works are stirring; the Hierophant fears the Ashfast's founding-charge is failing — and rival agents (Remnant, Ledger) are breaching the Waste. Someone must act before the wardens lose control.
**Location:** The Forbidden Waste's edge; a buried-works site.
**Primary NPCs:** Pyre-Warden Sef Embren (guardian/possible ally), Ash-Hierophant Vole Cindra (distant authority), Mira Cindra (forbidden knowledge), Sub-Lector Wenn Senn (reckless Remnant breacher), Wanderer "the Unburnt" (DM — Gravecaller seed).
**Factions:** Ashfast (guarding), Concord Remnant (breaching), Gravecallers (DM-watch).
**Surface Problem:** Stop the Waste's breach / settle the stirring works before catastrophe.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The buried works are a SEPARATE southern complex that *rhymes* with the harvest (an order founded to contain old works, now stirring) — NOT the keystone, NOT the Court's seat. NEVER let it become "the real" endgame. The fire-rite contrast and the stirring corroborate the theme. "the Unburnt" is a DM Gravecaller emissary — keep oblique.
**Objectives:** 1) Reach the breach. 2) Settle/seal the stirring works (with Embren or against the breachers). 3) Decide what to do with what's found (oblique).
**Approaches:** Side with Embren to seal it; help Mira understand it (gated); stop the Remnant breacher Wenn Senn; use the fire-rite to settle the stirring dead.
**Obstacles:** The Waste's deadly conditions; the stirring works; rival breachers; the wardens' lethal guard.
**Important Scenes:** The Waste's stirring under ash; Embren's grim stand; a Remnant breacher's reckless dig; an oblique, unexplained glimpse of the buried works.
**Clues:** The buried works "going wrong" like a distinct southern node (gated, oblique — never the mechanism); fire-rite settling even this (hopeful contrast).
**Combat Options:** Stirring guardians / a Waste-presence (scaled L14–16, telegraphed) and/or rival breachers — escapable; sealing > slaying.
**Noncombat Options:** Sealing-rite, persuasion (turn Embren or stop the breacher), retreat — combat is one path.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Sealing is non-violent; Embren can be an ally; clear retreat; the deepest works are avoidable; the fire-rite is a tool.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Limited safe ground; a breach-clock before the works wake further (hard pressure).
**Rewards:** The theocracy's wary alliance (if helped); the fire-rite contrast-mastery (endgame-relevant, oblique); a hard-won southern corroboration; Embren's respect.
**Consequences:** Sealing the Waste with Embren earns the theocracy's trust. Letting the Remnant breach it risks a southern catastrophe (a bad regional outcome). Ignored — the Mountain's "wrath" grows and the wardens are overwhelmed off-screen.
**Failure State:** The breach goes wrong → a regional disaster the player can later help contain; the apex truth is NOT revealed; no campaign-ending dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_ET_002/004; Q_MAJOR_013 (The Fire That Mirrors Ours); the Remnant chain (Q_MAJOR_012).
**Scaling Notes:** L14 — an edge-breach. L16 — the works fully stirring; a boss-tier Waste-presence.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Ashfast/Remnant/Gravecallers), HIDDEN_CLUES (southern-echo, gated), WORLD_CLOCKS (Emberfell breach-clock), CONSEQUENCES.
