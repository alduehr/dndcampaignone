## AI Use

Five developed quests for the Wender Steppe (a cold open grass-sea ridden by horse-clans the Concord never tamed; N-central, beyond the Highmark Spine; Lvl 8–13). Cast from `08_npcs/by_region/WENDER_STEPPE_NPCS.md`. The steppe is **ungoverned wildland** and a people **outside the Concord's whole history** — the campaign's clearest "outside view," to whom the crisis is a *foreign* affliction of the settled south. The steppe has **NO Concord ruins and NO harvest-history** — keep it clean of them; the Sky-Stones are an *independent* steppe holy site, never Concord. The seers' "thinning sky" is an extremely-oblique cosmic far-echo, never the mechanism. No NPC knows the apex truth. Guest-law governs everything. Anchors: the Winter-Camp at Cold Springs, the Summer-Riding, the Sky-Stones, the Spine-Foot trade-meet.

# Q_WS_001_THE_GUEST_LAW — The Guest-Law

**Quest Type:** Social / cultural on-ramp / sanctuary. **Level Range:** 8–11; solo danger: low–moderate. **Secrecy:** player-safe.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 16 (Welcomer Wind-Sai / the Speaker); Insight DC 15 (the guest-custom); Survival DC 16 (the open steppe); Animal Handling DC 15.
**Starting Hook:** The trackless steppe is deadly to outsiders, and only by earning guest-honor under the clans' sacred guest-law — through Welcomer Wind-Sai and the Clan-Speaker Tamur Wend-Khar — can the player cross it safely and ally with the free horse-clans.
**Location:** The Winter-Camp at Cold Springs; the open steppe.
**Primary NPCs:** Welcomer Wind-Sai (the social key), Clan-Speaker Tamur Wend-Khar (the welcome), Horse-Mother Wend-Sai (mounts/herds, secondary), Lone-Rider "Cold" Esha-Mor (the survival-guide, secondary).
**Factions:** Independent (steppe horse-clans).
**Surface Problem:** Earn the clans' guest-welcome and safe passage across the steppe.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The steppe's social on-ramp and "outside view" introduction — guest-law is the gate to the whole region (passage, mounts, riders, the Speaker's alliance, the seers' oblique read). A gentle cultural quest of respect and sacred hospitality. Establishes the steppe as a *foreign* world the crisis hasn't reached. No central-mystery content.
**Objectives:** 1) Observe and honor guest-law. 2) Win Wind-Sai's and the Speaker's welcome. 3) Gain safe passage and a steppe-guide.
**Approaches:** Honor the guest-custom; bring a worthy gift or service; prove the player comes as guest, not raider; aid a clan-need (a hard winter, a lost rider, a raider-threat); ride well (earn a rider's respect).
**Obstacles:** The clans' distrust of settled-folk's manners; the unfamiliar guest-law; the deadly open steppe; the difference between guest and raider.
**Important Scenes:** The guest-law explained at the Winter-Camp; the Speaker's frank appraisal; a first ride across the grass-sea with "Cold" Esha-Mor.
**Clues:** None central (the steppe as a world outside the Concord; the guest-law).
**Combat Options:** None expected if guest-law is honored; raiders or a misstep only if it's broken — avoidable.
**Noncombat Options:** Respect, service, gift, riding — the whole quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate if respectful; "Cold" Esha-Mor as the survival-key (telegraph the steppe kills the unguided); the clans protect a guest; clear retreat to Spine-Foot.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests once a guest; the steppe itself is the hazard (water/cold).
**Rewards:** Guest-honor and safe passage; mounts (Horse-Mother Wend-Sai); a steppe-guide; the Speaker's friendship and the clans' alliance-potential; the "outside view" of the crisis.
**Consequences:** Earning guest-honor opens the whole steppe. Breaking guest-law turns the entire grass-sea lethal (a hard, lasting consequence).
**Failure State:** Guest-honor denied → the player must earn it another way (service, a rider's respect) or risk the steppe alone; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_WS_002 (the Speaker's troubles); Q_WS_003 (the seer's omen); Q_WS_004 (the raiders).
**Scaling Notes:** Low–moderate; the steppe's environmental danger scales with how far the player rides unguided.
**State Updates:** RELATIONSHIPS (Wind-Sai, Tamur, Cold Esha-Mor), FACTION_STATE (steppe-clans), NPC_MEMORY.

# Q_WS_002_THE_KNOTTED_KIN — The Knotted Kin

**Quest Type:** Politics / feud-mediation / faction-binding. **Level Range:** 9–13; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 17 (the feuding clans / Kin-Mother Tamur-Sai); Insight DC 16 (the feud's root); History DC 15 (clan-lineage); Diplomacy across multiple scenes.
**Starting Hook:** The clan-feuds — Feud-Lord Kessa of the Grey Mare's grudge chief among them — are fracturing the Speaker's confederacy, and Kin-Mother Tamur-Sai, the true glue beneath the Speaker's word, asks a trusted guest to help knot the clans back together.
**Location:** The Winter-Camp; rival clan-camps; the Summer-Riding.
**Primary NPCs:** Kin-Mother Tamur-Sai (giver, the true glue), Clan-Speaker Tamur Wend-Khar (the public voice), Feud-Lord Kessa of the Grey Mare (the feud, secondary), War-Rider Captain Borr-Sai (the divided force, secondary).
**Factions:** Independent (steppe-clan confederacy).
**Surface Problem:** Heal the clan-feuds fracturing the confederacy.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Winning Kin-Mother Tamur-Sai does more to unite the steppe than winning the Speaker — she's the unglamorous human glue (marriages, memory, old debts) beneath the confederacy. A clean politics/mediation quest about *how a free people stays united.* The "thinning sky" dread is a background pressure the feuds ignore. No central-mystery gate; Kessa's grievance is a fair, resolvable false-lead.
**Objectives:** 1) Learn the feud's true root (Kessa's grudge). 2) Broker reconciliation (or justice). 3) Strengthen the confederacy through Tamur-Sai.
**Approaches:** Mediate Kessa's grudge (find its root); arrange a binding marriage/reconciliation (Tamur-Sai's craft); shame the clans with the seers' shared dread; win the war-captain (Borr-Sai) to unity; pick a just side.
**Obstacles:** Generations of clan-grudge; the clans' pride; the difficulty of a feud with a fair root; the Raid-Lord Borr's external pressure.
**Important Scenes:** Tamur-Sai mapping the human bonds beneath the Speaker's word; Kessa's grievance aired; a binding reconciliation (or its failure).
**Clues:** None central (the steppe's politics; how a free people holds together; Kessa's fair grievance).
**Combat Options:** Inter-clan skirmishers (**Scout/Veteran-like**, mounted) if mediation fails — avoidable.
**Noncombat Options:** Mediation, marriage-brokering, justice, persuasion — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; Tamur-Sai and the Speaker as allies; multiple resolutions incl. justly siding; the clans respect a fair guest.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests as a guest; a confederacy-fracturing soft-clock (Borr exploits division).
**Rewards:** Tamur-Sai's deep trust and the confederacy's unity; the Speaker's gratitude; a far stronger steppe alliance; the human map beneath the clans.
**Consequences:** Knotting the clans unites the steppe against the raiders and the settled-pressure. Failing fractures the confederacy (a steppe clock; Raid-Lord Borr profits).
**Failure State:** The feud splits the confederacy → the clans scatter, Borr rises; reopen via Tamur-Sai; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_WS_004 (Raid-Lord Borr exploiting division); Q_WS_001 (the guest-honor that enabled this); the steppe's unity-clock.
**Scaling Notes:** L9 — one feud. L13 — a confederacy-wide fracture with Borr pressing the seams.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (steppe confederacy, feuds), RELATIONSHIPS (Tamur-Sai, Tamur, Kessa, Borr-Sai), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `../../08_npcs/by_region/WENDER_STEPPE_NPCS.md`, `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md`.

# Q_WS_003_THE_THINNING_SKY — The Thinning Sky

**Quest Type:** Mystery (oblique/cosmic) / vigil / lore. **Level Range:** 9–13; solo danger: low–moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Insight DC 17 (Wind-Singer Esha's omen); Religion/Nature DC 17 (the Sky-Stones); Survival DC 16; Persuasion DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** Wind-Singer Esha reads a "thinning of the southern Sky" — something pulling at the dead far away — and the Sky-Stones "hum strange." She'll share her omen-lore only with a southern guest who tells her, truly, what the settled-folk's restless dead do.
**Location:** The Sky-Stones (an ancient steppe shrine-circle, NOT Concord); the open steppe.
**Primary NPCs:** Wind-Singer Esha (giver/oracle), Sky-Stones Keeper Old Wend-Mor (the Stones' lore, secondary), Seer-Apprentice Esha-Sai (the frightened young seer, secondary), Clan-Speaker Tamur Wend-Khar (wants the omen read).
**Factions:** Independent (steppe seers), oblique kin-thread to the Mourners (by parallel only).
**Surface Problem:** Help Wind-Singer Esha understand the "thinning sky" omen troubling the seers.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Esha *perceives the crisis* as "a thinning of the southern Sky, something pulling at the dead" — a deeply-oblique, deniable far-echo of the **Quiet Country thinning** (the harvest's deepest consequence), reached through steppe-cosmology with no framework to name it. The campaign's most *cosmic* far-region hint. Keep it EXTREMELY oblique. The Sky-Stones are an *independent* steppe holy site (NOT Concord) that "hum" by their own old nature — never let them become a Concord node. Never name the harvest, Quiet Country, or Court.
**Objectives:** 1) Earn Esha's trust (share the south's truth). 2) Keep a Sky-Stones vigil with her. 3) Carry the omen-warning to the settled-folk (or learn from the outside view).
**Approaches:** Tell Esha, truly, of the restless dead; vigil at the Sky-Stones; learn the Stones' independent lore (Wend-Mor); reassure the frightened apprentice; carry the omen south (an oblique parallel to the Mourners' warning).
**Obstacles:** Esha's careful obliqueness; the difficulty of naming what neither party understands; the deniability of an omen; the open-steppe distances.
**Important Scenes:** Esha singing the "thinning sky" at the humming Sky-Stones; the moment her omen rhymes with the player's frontier knowledge; the Stones humming "strange of late."
**Clues:** The crisis perceived from wholly outside the Concord (the "thinning sky" — extremely oblique cosmic echo); the Quiet Country thinning, sensed not named (handle with extreme care); a far-region independent perception with NO Concord framework.
**Combat Options:** Open-steppe hazards or raiders (**Scout/Bandit-like**, telegraphed) — avoidable with a guide.
**Noncombat Options:** Vigil, lore, persuasion, omen-carrying — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** A near-combatless lore quest; a steppe-guide for the open grass; the clans shelter a guest; retreat to camp.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests as a guest; no hard clock (a soft deepening of the omen).
**Rewards:** Esha's eerie trust and the steppe's omen-lore; the Sky-Stones' independent ancient lore; the most cosmic oblique perception of the crisis (from outside); the seers' counsel.
**Consequences:** Sharing the truth deepens Esha's (and the player's) oblique cosmic understanding. The "outside view" widens the player's sense of the world's stakes. Ignoring it lets the thinning deepen.
**Failure State:** If Esha isn't earned, her omen stays closed — reopen via the apprentice or the Stones' keeper; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_WS_001 (the guest-honor that opened the seers); Q_MAJOR_001 (the deep mystery); the Mourner old-songs parallel.
**Scaling Notes:** Lore/cosmic-weight scales with the player's central-mystery progress; minimal combat scaling.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (cosmic far-echo, Quiet-Country-thinning — extremely oblique), RELATIONSHIPS (Esha, Wend-Mor), FACTION_STATE (steppe-seers).
**Related:** `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/MYSTERY_WEB.md`, `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_001).

# Q_WS_004_THE_BLACK_HORSE — The Black Horse

**Quest Type:** Conflict / raider / wild-card / hard alliance. **Level Range:** 10–13; solo danger: high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion/Intimidation DC 18 (Raid-Lord Borr); Animal Handling/Athletics DC 16 (a contest of nerve); Insight DC 16 (splitting Borr from his lieutenant); combat-tactics if it comes to a charge.
**Starting Hook:** Raid-Lord Borr of the Black Horse rejects the Speaker's "tame" confederacy and rides to take by the old way — raiding the settled edge, the Ledger's caravans, and rival clans alike. The player will clash with him at the steppe's edge, and may fight, flee, or (hard) earn him.
**Location:** The far steppe; the Spine-Foot trade-meet's edges; an open-grass confrontation.
**Primary NPCs:** Raid-Lord Borr of the Black Horse (antagonist/possible ally), young raider "Hawk" Borr-Kin (a worshipful youth, minor), Raider-cook Kess-Tai (a turnable raider, minor), Caravan-Master Mab Wether (a raided victim, secondary).
**Factions:** Independent (steppe raiders), Cinder Ledger (his caravan-prey, oblique).
**Surface Problem:** Deal with the raid-lord Borr — fight, flee, turn, or out-ride him.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Borr is the steppe's **danger and dissent** — the "old way" foil to the Speaker, a wild-card a player can fight, flee, or (very hard) earn through a contest of nerve. Not a faction; a dissident clan-host. His raiding is a steppe-internal clock (it can fracture the confederacy). He's honor-bound to those who earn it. No central-mystery content; the "thinning sky" merely unsettles even him.
**Objectives:** 1) Survive the encounter with Borr's raid-host. 2) Resolve it (defeat, escape, tribute, or earn his respect). 3) Decide his place in the steppe's balance.
**Approaches:** Fight (extremely dangerous mounted); flee (a steppe-chase); pay tribute; win a contest of horse and nerve (earning his respect); split him from his brutal lieutenant; ally him against the Ledger's caravans (hard).
**Obstacles:** Borr's deadly mounted raid-host; his scorn for weakness; his honor-code; his brutal lieutenant straining it.
**Important Scenes:** Borr's theatrical, non-cruel challenge ("prey, or sport?"); a contest of nerve on horseback; splitting Borr from his lieutenant.
**Clues:** None central (the steppe's danger and the "old way"; the confederacy-fracturing pressure).
**Combat Options:** Borr's mounted raid-host (**Berserker/Scout-like**; Borr is **Tier 1**, AC 15, ~90 HP, mounted) — extreme open-grass danger; avoidable by tribute, a contest, or flight.
**Noncombat Options:** Tribute, a contest of nerve, splitting him from his lieutenant, alliance — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** The player need not fight the host (telegraph mounted lethality); flight, tribute, and the contest are real options; allies (the confederacy's riders) if guest-honored; Borr respects nerve over slaughter.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** No rest during a raid-chase; rest at the trade-meet or a clan-camp.
**Rewards:** Borr broken, paid, or (hard) earned as a fierce steppe ally; the settled edge and caravans protected; the "old way" path; standing among the riders.
**Consequences:** Earning Borr gains a wild, deadly ally and the old ways. Opposing him makes a deadly cavalry enemy. His fate shifts the steppe's balance (and the confederacy's unity).
**Failure State:** A lost clash — the player flees the open grass; Borr keeps raiding; reposition or earn him later; no cheap death (the chase has off-ramps).
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_WS_002 (the division Borr exploits); the Ledger's raided caravans; the steppe's power-balance.
**Scaling Notes:** L10 — a raid-skirmish. L13 — a full mounted raid-war across the open steppe.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (steppe raiders, confederacy), RELATIONSHIPS (Borr, the confederacy), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_WS_005_THE_EDGE_OF_TWO_WORLDS — The Edge of Two Worlds

**Quest Type:** Trade / escort / low-stakes bridge. **Level Range:** 8–12; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 16 (Trade-Meet Keeper Doss Spine / Edge-Factor Iss Karr); Insight DC 15; Survival DC 16 (the steppe-edge); Investigation DC 15.
**Starting Hook:** At the Spine-Foot trade-meet — the one neutral ground where steppe and settled trade — the Cinder Ledger's Edge-Factor Iss Karr is squeezing the clans, Borr's raiders bleed the caravans, and Trade-Meet Keeper Doss Spine struggles to keep the fragile bridge between two worlds open.
**Location:** The Spine-Foot trade-meet (steppe meets settled).
**Primary NPCs:** Trade-Meet Keeper Doss Spine (giver, the neutral broker), Edge-Factor Iss Karr (Ledger antagonist), Caravan-Master Mab Wether (the settled side, secondary), Steppe-Trader "Three-Furs" Borr-Tai (the steppe side, secondary).
**Factions:** Cinder Ledger (edge-trade, antagonist), independent (the trade-meet & clans).
**Surface Problem:** Keep the Spine-Foot trade-meet — the bridge between steppe and settled — open and fair.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The steppe's bridge to the settled world — establishing Spine-Foot as the player's gateway in/out of the steppe, and a neutral rumor-hub where the "outside view" meets the settled crisis. The Ledger's edge-squeeze is Q_MAJOR_004 at the world's northern margin. "Three-Furs" carries gossip of the south's troubles back to the uncomprehending clans (oblique). No central-mystery gate.
**Objectives:** 1) Understand the threats to the trade-meet (Ledger squeeze, raiders, distrust). 2) Keep the bridge open and fair. 3) Establish standing as a cross-world go-between.
**Approaches:** Broker fair trade between clans and settled (Doss Spine); break the Ledger's squeeze (Iss Karr); protect the caravans from Borr's raiders; build trust between "Three-Furs" and Mab Wether; carry word of the south's troubles to the clans.
**Obstacles:** The two worlds' mutual distrust; the Ledger's coin; Borr's raiders; the meet's fragility.
**Important Scenes:** Steppe and settled haggling under guest-truce; Iss Karr's cold squeeze; "Three-Furs" carrying southern rumor to the clans.
**Clues:** The Ledger reaching the world's northern margin (Q_MAJOR_004); southern crisis-rumor reaching the uncomprehending steppe (oblique, "outside view").
**Combat Options:** Raider-raids on caravans (**Scout/Bandit-like**) — avoidable by escort/route.
**Noncombat Options:** Brokering, trade, exposure, escort, rumor-carrying — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; Doss Spine's neutral-ground protection; caravan-guards; multiple resolutions; clear retreat.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Lodging at the trade-meet; a trade-season soft-clock.
**Rewards:** Doss Spine's trust and Spine-Foot as a cross-world gateway/rumor-hub; fair trade preserved; "Three-Furs" and Mab Wether as recurring contacts; leverage over the Ledger's edge-arm; coin.
**Consequences:** Keeping the bridge open gives the player a way in/out of the steppe and a cross-world hub. Letting it close isolates the steppe and hands the edge to the Ledger.
**Failure State:** The trade-meet collapses → steppe and settled drift apart; the player's route is harder; reopen via Doss Spine; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_WS_001 (entering the steppe proper); Q_WS_004 (Borr's caravan-raids); Q_MAJOR_004 (the Ledger); travel between steppe and settled.
**Scaling Notes:** L8 — a trade-squabble. L12 — a Ledger squeeze plus a raider-war on the caravans.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Ledger edge-arm, trade-meet), RELATIONSHIPS (Doss Spine, Iss Karr, Three-Furs, Mab Wether), CONSEQUENCES.
