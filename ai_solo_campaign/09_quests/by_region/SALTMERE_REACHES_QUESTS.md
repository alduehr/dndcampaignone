## AI Use

Seven developed quests for the Saltmere Reaches (a dying brackish inland-sea basin of salt-clans and drowned towns, ringed by the Bonepan Flats and the Ghostmark Range; Lvl 11–15). Cast from `08_npcs/by_region/SALTMERE_REACHES_NPCS.md`. The Saltmere's "going-down" is a **pre-Concord catastrophe-echo** — it *rhymes* with the campaign secret (the dead going down, an older fall) but is NEVER the keystone, the Hollow Court's seat, or the harvest mechanism. Drowned towns are pre-Concord ruins. No NPC knows the apex truth. Anchors: Brackhold, the Drowned Towns, the Bonepan Flats, Saltcairn.

# Q_SALT_001_THE_TOWN_THE_LAKE_GAVE_BACK — The Town the Lake Gave Back

**Quest Type:** Investigation / haunted ruin (oblique) / clan-politics. **Level Range:** 11–13; solo danger: moderate–high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Investigation DC 17 (the drowned town); Religion DC 16 (the salt-rites); Persuasion DC 17 (Matriarch Bryd Saltmere); Survival/Athletics(swim) DC 16 (the lake-floor).
**Starting Hook:** The receding lake has uncovered a drowned town whole — and its dead have begun to walk up out of the water onto the flats. Matriarch Bryd Saltmere wants it settled before it spreads, and before the delvers turn it into a circus.
**Location:** A newly-uncovered drowned town at the lake-edge; Brackhold.
**Primary NPCs:** Matriarch Bryd Saltmere (giver), Salt-Mother Tess Brackhold (the rites), Drowned-Town Delver Oss (access/witness), Lake-Pilot Brann Saltmere (the safe water-channels).
**Factions:** Independent (salt-clans), Mourners' Circle.
**Surface Problem:** Stop the drowned town's dead from walking onto the flats.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The drowned town is a **pre-Concord ruin** — its waking dead corroborate the "older, wider fall" theme, NOT the harvest or keystone. Settle it by the salt-and-going-down rite (Tess), not by a mechanism. The clearest signal that catastrophe and troubled dead are *older than the Concord.* Never let the town become the keystone.
**Objectives:** 1) Reach the drowned town safely. 2) Settle (or contain) its walking dead. 3) Keep the clans and delvers from making it worse.
**Approaches:** Bring Tess to perform the salt-going-down rite; investigate why *this* town woke; hold the lake-edge while the rite is sung; bargain Oss into helping rather than looting.
**Obstacles:** The walking drowned dead; the lake-floor's dangers; Oss's looting impulse; the clans' fatalism.
**Important Scenes:** Drowned dead rising from the receding water onto the white flats; Tess's salt-dirge "going-down" rite; Oss torn between greed and dread.
**Clues:** A pre-Concord town with walking dead (the "older fall" echo — M5-resonant); Tess's rite working where mechanism-talk fails; the drowned dead "stirring as in the going-down" (oblique).
**Combat Options:** Waking drowned dead (**Zombie/Wight-like**, scaled L11–13, water-slowed, telegraphed) — thinnable by rite; retreat to the flats.
**Noncombat Options:** Rite, containment, lake-craft — the worst fighting is avoidable by settling the dead.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Tess's rite is the true solution (not a fight); Brann's safe channels for retreat; the dead are slow and telegraphed; clan-warriors can hold the line.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at Brackhold; a soft clock (the longer it walks, the more dead rise).
**Rewards:** Bryd's clan-welcome and passage; the drowned-town "going-down" lore (oblique); Tess's trust and the old-songs thread; clan-salt and salvage.
**Consequences:** Settling it earns the clans; proving the "older fall" deepens the campaign theme. Failing spreads the walking dead onto the flats and to other drowned towns (a Saltmere clock).
**Failure State:** The town's dead spread → a basin-wide drowned-dead hook; the clans grow more fatalistic; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SALT_002 (Oss's deep dives); Q_SALT_004 (the old songs); the Saltmere drowned-dead clock.
**Scaling Notes:** L11 — one town. L15 — several drowned towns waking at once and a Gravecaller drawn to them.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (older-fall echo), FACTION_STATE (clans, Mourners), RELATIONSHIPS (Bryd, Tess, Oss), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md`, `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/CLUE_INDEX.md`.

# Q_SALT_002_WHAT_OSS_FOUND_BELOW — What Oss Found Below

**Quest Type:** Dungeon (drowned ruin) / escort / moral knot. **Level Range:** 12–15; solo danger: high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Athletics(swim)/Con saves DC 16 (the dive); Investigation DC 17; Insight DC 16 (Oss's haunting); Survival DC 16 (the lake-floor).
**Starting Hook:** Delver Oss has found something in a deep drowned town he won't describe — only that the dead down there "moved" — and he'll guide one person to it, for a price, because he can't face it alone again.
**Location:** A deep drowned town on the lake-floor; the Drowned Towns.
**Primary NPCs:** Drowned-Town Delver Oss (guide/giver), young diver Lell (his scared partner), Drowned-Relic Fence Doll Tallow (buyer), Lake-Pilot Brann Saltmere (surface support).
**Factions:** Independent (delvers), Cinder Ledger (relic-buyers, oblique).
**Surface Problem:** Help Oss reach and understand the deep drowned town.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** A pure pre-Concord delve — atmospheric drowned-ruin horror corroborating the "older fall," with NO keystone, Court, or mechanism. Oss's haunting is real and unexplained. The relics are pre-Concord going-down finds, NOT Remembrance-keystone relics. Keep it oblique; never resolve the mystery here.
**Objectives:** 1) Survive the dive into the deep drowned town. 2) Find what Oss found. 3) Decide what to do with it (and with Oss's sanity).
**Approaches:** Dive with Oss (Brann tends the surface); go slow and methodical; settle the deep dead with a Mourner token (Tess); recover the find or leave it lie.
**Obstacles:** Drowning hazards; the lake-floor's crush and dark; the deep walking dead; Oss's unraveling nerve; air/light limits.
**Important Scenes:** The descent into a whole town under water; the deep dead "moving" in the silt; Oss breaking down at what he found.
**Clues:** Deep pre-Concord drowned-ruin (the older fall, vivid — oblique); the dead waking even at the lake-floor (M5-resonant); a "wrong" going-down relic (M2-oblique, never the keystone).
**Combat Options:** Deep drowned dead (**Wight/Drowned-one-like**, scaled L12–15) in a flooded, light-starved ruin — extremely dangerous; avoid by stealth/route, settle by token, or retreat.
**Noncombat Options:** Stealth, careful routing, Mourner-token settling, simple retreat — combat is the worst option here.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Brann's surface-line and air-supply; Oss's guidance (losing him is the real danger — telegraph it); clear retreat to the surface; the deep dead are avoidable.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** No rest underwater (hard environmental clock — air/cold); rest before/after at Brackhold.
**Rewards:** The drowned town's deep loot and lore; Oss's lasting loyalty (or his rescue); an oblique "older fall" data-point; a "wrong" relic for the Remnant/Mourners to puzzle over.
**Consequences:** Helping Oss steadies the basin's only deep access. Pushing him too far breaks him (and loses the access). The deep find can be left, sold, or studied — each with consequences.
**Failure State:** A failed dive — Oss or Lell may be lost; the deep town stays unplumbed; the player escapes; no dead end (try again with more support).
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SALT_001 (the surface stirring); Q_SALT_006 (the Remnant's interest in the find); Doll Tallow's "wrong relic" trade.
**Scaling Notes:** L12 — a shallow drowned ruin. L15 — the deepest town, the worst dead, a Gravecaller racing for it.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (older-fall, deep), RELATIONSHIPS (Oss, Brann, Lell), INVENTORY (the find), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md`, `../../10_dungeons_and_ruins/` (drowned-ruin analogues).

# Q_SALT_003_THE_LEDGERS_SALT — The Ledger's Salt

**Quest Type:** Faction (anti-Ledger) / trade-war / clan-defense. **Level Range:** 11–14; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 17 (Factor Iss Tallow / Sera Brackhold); Investigation DC 16 (the lowball scheme); Insight DC 16; Intimidation DC 17.
**Starting Hook:** The Cinder Ledger is strangling the salt-trade the clans live on — lowballing salt, quietly buying drowned-town relics — and Salt-Master Sera Brackhold needs an outsider to break the squeeze before the dying realm starves.
**Location:** Brackhold's salt-works; the trade-track; the Ledger's basin post.
**Primary NPCs:** Salt-Master Sera Brackhold (giver), Factor Iss Tallow (Ledger antagonist), Salt-Caravan Master Marek Bonepan (the route out), Drowned-Relic Fence Doll Tallow (the relic-leg of the scheme).
**Factions:** Cinder Ledger (antagonist), independent (salt-clans).
**Surface Problem:** Break the Ledger's salt-squeeze and save the clans' one export.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** A clean continental-scale anti-Ledger quest (Q_MAJOR_004) dramatized in a dying realm — the Ledger bleeding the weakest region dry, with a quiet drowned-relic side-trade (M3). No central-mystery content beyond the oblique relic-trade.
**Objectives:** 1) Expose or break the Ledger's lowball. 2) Open an honest salt-route out (Marek Bonepan). 3) Optionally cut off the relic side-trade.
**Approaches:** Open a rival salt-market via Marek's caravans; expose Iss Tallow's collusion/relic-buying; out-bargain or pressure the Ledger; rally the clans behind Sera.
**Obstacles:** The Ledger's deep pockets; the flats-raiders (Mirage) on the route out; the clans' isolation; Iss Tallow's persistence.
**Important Scenes:** Iss Tallow's cold lowball at the salt-works; Marek Bonepan's flats-crossing; catching the quiet drowned-relic trade.
**Clues:** The Ledger bleeding the weakest region (Q_MAJOR_004, scale); the drowned-relic side-trade (M3-oblique).
**Combat Options:** Flats-raiders (**Bandit/Scout-like**) on the trade-route; Ledger toughs if pressured — both avoidable by escort/negotiation.
**Noncombat Options:** Trade, exposure, route-opening, rally — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; Marek's caravan-guards; multiple resolutions; the Ledger prefers profit to violence.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at Brackhold; a starvation/season soft-clock.
**Rewards:** The clans' lasting gratitude; an open salt-route and Marek as an ally; leverage over the Ledger's basin arm; an M3 relic-trade lead.
**Consequences:** Breaking the squeeze keeps the realm alive a while longer. Failing accelerates the Saltmere's death (and the Ledger's monopoly). Cutting the relic-trade hurts the Ledger's coastal/inland chain.
**Failure State:** The squeeze holds → the realm dwindles faster; the player gains a sympathetic-but-failing-realm thread; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_MAJOR_004 (the continental Ledger); Q_SALT_002 (the relics' source); Marek's route to the south.
**Scaling Notes:** L11 — local trade-war. L14 — a Ledger crackdown and raider-escalation on the route.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Ledger basin-arm, clans), RELATIONSHIPS (Sera, Iss Tallow, Marek), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_004).

# Q_SALT_004_THE_GOING_DOWN_SONG — The Going-Down Song

**Quest Type:** Faction (Mourners) / lore / old-songs network. **Level Range:** 11–14; solo danger: low. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Religion DC 16; Persuasion/Insight DC 16 (earning Tess); History DC 17 (the going-down lore).
**Starting Hook:** Salt-Mother Tess Brackhold keeps the "going-down" lament — a song older than the Concord that says how the drowned dead were lost — and she'll teach its truest verses only to someone who carries the old songs between the far elders.
**Location:** Brackhold's salt-shrines; the lake-edge; the wider network (by letter/token).
**Primary NPCs:** Salt-Mother Tess Brackhold (giver/anchor), Salt-dirge singer Tess (minor keeper), Sub-Lector Wenn Cairn (Remnant, the "older fall" distinction), Lake-boy Ferec (a failing-rite witness).
**Factions:** Mourners' Circle (old-songs network), Concord Remnant (edge interest).
**Surface Problem:** Earn and carry the Saltmere's "going-down" lament.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Tess is the **Saltmere node of the continental old-songs network**, where the network's M6-oblique fragment ("the grey hands took the dead") meets the local pre-Concord "going-down" myth. The campaign's strongest hint the troubled-dead pattern is *older and wider than the Concord* — carried in folk-memory. Keep it oblique; she conflates the older fall with the present crisis. Never the mechanism, Court, or keystone.
**Objectives:** 1) Earn Tess's trust. 2) Learn the going-down lament (M6-oblique). 3) Carry it to/from another elder, weaving the network.
**Approaches:** Help the basin's failing rites; honor the drowned dead; carry a song/token to a far elder (Senna Crale, Combe, Wend, etc.); let Wenn Cairn supply the scholarly "older fall" distinction.
**Obstacles:** Tess's caution; the basin's isolation; the danger of conflating two old dooms.
**Important Scenes:** Tess singing the going-down lament at the lake-edge; the moment the lament rhymes with what the player saw on the frontier; Wenn Cairn separating "older fall" from the present crisis.
**Clues:** The old songs' fragment fused with the going-down myth (M6-oblique — the truth survives in song; the pattern is older/wider); the "older fall" distinction (key — it is NOT the keystone).
**Combat Options:** None; this is a lore/social quest.
**Noncombat Options:** Religion, persuasion, lore-carrying, network-weaving — the whole quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat at all; the worst failure is lost trust, not danger.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at Brackhold; no hard clock.
**Rewards:** The going-down lament (a key oblique mystery-fragment); Tess's lasting trust; a node in the continental old-songs network; the Saltmere's deepest lore.
**Consequences:** Carrying the song advances the player's understanding (obliquely) and the Mourner-network. Misreading it (conflating the older fall with the keystone) is a fair false-lead the network itself can correct.
**Failure State:** If Tess isn't earned, the lament stays closed — reopen via another elder or by serving the basin; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_MD (Senna Crale), Q_MAJOR_001 (the deep mystery); the continental old-songs network.
**Scaling Notes:** Lore-weight scales with how far the player has progressed the central mystery; no combat scaling needed.
**State Updates:** KNOWN/HIDDEN_CLUES (M6-oblique, older-fall distinction), RELATIONSHIPS (Tess), FACTION_STATE (Mourner-network).
**Related:** `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/MYSTERY_WEB.md`, `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_001).

# Q_SALT_005_THE_WHITE_FLATS — The White Flats

**Quest Type:** Travel / survival / banditry. **Level Range:** 11–14; solo danger: moderate–high. **Secrecy:** player-safe.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Survival DC 17 (the Bonepan Flats); Con saves DC 15 (heat/salt); Insight DC 16 (Gurr, the turnable raider); Persuasion DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** Salt-Caravan Master Marek Bonepan's salt-trains are vanishing on the white flats — to the raider "Mirage" and to the flats themselves — and the basin's one land-route out is closing. He needs a guard who can cross the Bonepan and come back.
**Location:** The Bonepan Flats (white salt-badlands); the trade-track out of the basin.
**Primary NPCs:** Salt-Caravan Master Marek Bonepan (giver), Flats-Guide Sef Bonepan (the crossing), Flats-raider "Mirage" (antagonist, minor), Raider-cook Gurr (turnable).
**Factions:** Independent (caravans vs. flats-raiders).
**Surface Problem:** Save the salt-caravans crossing the deadly flats.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** A clean survival-and-banditry quest establishing the basin's land-route *out* (a practical exit for a high-level player) and a turnable bandit. The flats' mirages and salt-madness are natural hazards. No central-mystery content.
**Objectives:** 1) Survive/learn the Bonepan crossing. 2) Stop or turn "Mirage's" raiders. 3) Reopen the route out.
**Approaches:** Escort a caravan with Sef's guidance; ambush the ambushers; turn Gurr (a reluctant raider) against Mirage; negotiate a flats-toll; find the safer crossing.
**Obstacles:** The lethal flats (heat, salt, mirages, no water); the raiders' glare-ambushes; navigation.
**Important Scenes:** A salt-mirage ambush out of the white glare; Sef reading the trackless flats; Gurr's quiet willingness to turn.
**Clues:** None central (a practical route-out and a raider-network).
**Combat Options:** Mirage's flats-raiders (**Bandit/Scout-like**, glare-ambush) — avoidable by route/negotiation; survivable with Sef.
**Noncombat Options:** Survival, escort, turning a raider, toll-negotiation — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Sef's guidance is the survival-key (telegraph that crossing alone is lethal); Marek's caravan-guards; turnable enemy; retreat to the basin.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** No safe rest on the flats (environmental clock — water/heat); rest at basin-edge camps.
**Rewards:** An open land-route south and Marek's alliance; Sef as a flats-guide; the raiders broken or turned; coin/salt-fee.
**Consequences:** Reopening the route keeps the dying basin connected (and helps the player leave). Failing isolates the Saltmere further.
**Failure State:** The route stays closed → the basin's isolation deepens; reopen later with more support; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SALT_003 (the salt-trade the route serves); travel onward to the southern regions.
**Scaling Notes:** L11 — a single caravan. L14 — a raider war-band and a worse crossing.
**State Updates:** RELATIONSHIPS (Marek, Sef, Gurr), FACTION_STATE (flats-raiders), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_SALT_006_THE_LISTENER_AT_THE_WATER — The Listener at the Water

**Quest Type:** Faction (anti-Gravecaller) / mystery / moral dilemma. **Level Range:** 12–15; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Insight DC 17 (the "Drowned Sister"); Investigation DC 16 (her gatherings); Persuasion DC 17 (the bereaved); Religion DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** A hollow-eyed wanderer called "the Drowned Sister" has come to the lake-edge, drawing the grieving toward "listening" to the drowned dead — and Matriarch Bryd wants her gone before she turns the clans' grief into something forbidden.
**Location:** The lake-edge shrines; the drowned-town shore; Brackhold.
**Primary NPCs:** Wanderer "the Drowned Sister" (Gravecaller emissary, antagonist), Salt-Mother Tess Brackhold (the right way to grieve), Matriarch Bryd Saltmere (wants her gone), the bereaved Net-wife Cass (a tempted clanfolk).
**Factions:** Gravecallers (DM-labeled), Mourners' Circle, independent (clans).
**Surface Problem:** Deal with the outsider turning the clans' grief toward "listening" to the dead.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The Drowned Sister is a **Gravecaller emissary** drawn to the Saltmere's waking drowned dead — the cult's far-region toehold. The waking dead make the clans vulnerable. The quest is a moral dilemma (the grieving genuinely suffer; the cult offers false comfort). She knows no apex truth; keep her fragments oblique. Tess's true grief-rite is the humane counter.
**Objectives:** 1) Understand what the Drowned Sister offers (and why it's dangerous). 2) Protect the bereaved clans. 3) Drive off, expose, or (hard) turn her.
**Approaches:** Counter her with Tess's true rite; expose her cult-fragments to the clans; reach the bereaved (Cass) before she does; confront or banish her; investigate her network.
**Obstacles:** The clans' real grief (she offers comfort); her eerie persuasiveness; the waking drowned dead that prove her "right."
**Important Scenes:** The Drowned Sister "listening" with the grieving at the water; Cass torn between Tess's rite and the Sister's comfort; the confrontation.
**Clues:** The Gravecaller cult reaching the far Saltmere (M3/cult-spread — DM-oblique); the waking dead making the clans vulnerable; Tess's rite vs. the Sister's "listening" (the campaign's core moral tension, in miniature).
**Combat Options:** The Drowned Sister is no fighter; cult-touched bereaved or a drowned-dead "answer" to her listening (scaled L12–15) — avoidable by reaching people first.
**Noncombat Options:** Counter-rite, exposure, persuasion, banishment — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** The true solution is social/spiritual (Tess), not a fight; the clans back the player against an outsider; the bereaved can be reached; retreat is easy.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at Brackhold; a soft clock (the longer she stays, the more she gathers).
**Rewards:** Bryd's and Tess's deep trust; the clans protected; intelligence on the Gravecaller cult's far reach; a humane resolution.
**Consequences:** Driving her off protects the basin; turning her (hard) gains rare cult-insight. Failing lets a cult-seed take root in the dying realm (a Saltmere clock).
**Failure State:** The Sister stays → a far-region Gravecaller cell seeds; the clans fracture over grief; reopen as an anti-cult thread; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SALT_001 (the waking dead she exploits); Q_SALT_004 (Tess's true way); the Gravecaller far-spread.
**Scaling Notes:** L12 — a lone emissary. L15 — an established cell exploiting multiple drowned towns.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Gravecaller far-toehold, Mourners), RELATIONSHIPS (Bryd, Tess, the Sister), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_SALT_007_THE_DYING_CLAN — The Dying Clan

**Quest Type:** Low-stakes regional / clan-feud / humane choice. **Level Range:** 11–13; solo danger: low–moderate. **Secrecy:** player-safe.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 16 (the feuding holds); Insight DC 15; Investigation DC 15 (the feud's root).
**Starting Hook:** The Ghostmark-edge clan-town of Saltcairn is dying faster than Brackhold, and a bitter feud with a rival hold is bleeding both — Clan-Elder Tess Saltcairn begs help before her people scatter or kill each other.
**Location:** Saltcairn and a rival hold; the Ghostmark edge.
**Primary NPCs:** Clan-Elder Tess Saltcairn (giver), Welcomer Mab Saltcairn (the social key), Clan-feud matron Doll (the rival, minor), Feud-warrior Tam (the next generation, minor).
**Factions:** Independent (feuding salt-clans).
**Surface Problem:** Stop the feud killing two dying clans, and help a failing town.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Humane, character-driven clan-texture — the realm dying not just from the lake but from old grudges. Atmospheric Ghostmark "hum" is an oblique environmental color, never the mechanism. Introduces recurring clan contacts. No central-mystery content.
**Objectives:** 1) Learn the feud's root. 2) Broker peace (or pick a side justly). 3) Help Saltcairn survive a while longer.
**Approaches:** Mediate the feud (find its true root); shame both elders with their shared decline; aid Saltcairn practically (raiders, water, the failing flock); turn the next generation (Tam) from vengeance.
**Obstacles:** Generations of grudge; the clans' pride; the town's slow death; the Ghostmark's dread.
**Important Scenes:** Two dying towns trading blows over an old wrong; Tess Saltcairn watching her town empty; Tam burning for vengeance.
**Clues:** None central (the Ghostmark "hum" is oblique atmosphere; the feud is human).
**Combat Options:** Feud-skirmishers (**Guard/Bandit-like**) if mediation fails — avoidable.
**Noncombat Options:** Mediation, aid, persuasion, justice — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; multiple resolutions incl. justly picking a side; the clans respect a fair outsider.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests in Saltcairn; the town's decline as a soft clock.
**Rewards:** The clans' gratitude; Saltcairn as a recurring (dwindling) contact; Mab Saltcairn's friendship; a humane reputation in the basin.
**Consequences:** Brokering peace buys Saltcairn time. Failing lets the feud finish two clans. Picking a side has lasting basin-politics consequences.
**Failure State:** The feud burns out both clans → Saltcairn dies; the player gains a grim basin-thread; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SALT_005 (the flats-raiders bleeding the holds); the basin's slow death.
**Scaling Notes:** Low–moderate throughout; raise the feud-violence if the player escalates.
**State Updates:** RELATIONSHIPS (Tess Saltcairn, Mab, the rival), FACTION_STATE (clan-feud), NPC_MEMORY.
