## AI Use

Six developed quests for the Concord Heartlands / the Ruin'd Crown (the shattered SURFACE seat of the old Custodian Concord, astride the Greatspine; a vast contested ruin; Lvl 13–17, late-game). Cast from `08_npcs/by_region/CONCORD_HEARTLANDS_NPCS.md`.

**CRITICAL SECRECY:** This is the Concord's *surface* fallen-capital country. It is **NOT** the Concord Deep (the buried harvest-network) and **NOT** the Under-Shrine / Drowned Keystone beneath Hollowmere. The Heartlands **corroborate** the theme (the Concord's grandeur and fall) but are **NOT** the keystone and **NOT** the Hollow Court's seat. **No NPC here knows the apex truth.** The far-region grandeur must NEVER pull the climax away from Hollowmere. The Crown's deepest secret is a *record of what the Concord did*, NOT the living machine. Hollin Vane is apex-adjacent and a magnificent **dupe** — never let him confirm the Court or keystone. Anchors: Crownmouth, the Ruin'd Crown, the Greatspine passes, the Pilgrim Camps.

# Q_CH_001_THE_LAST_SAFE_TOWN — The Last Safe Town

**Quest Type:** Faction-patron (independent) / defense / on-ramp. **Level Range:** 13–16; solo danger: high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 17 (Warden Lyssa Crownmouth); Investigation DC 17 (the near-ruin threat); Survival DC 17 (the Crown's edge); combat-tactics for the defense.
**Starting Hook:** Crownmouth — the only safe town at the Ruin'd Crown — is pressed by ruin-horrors, its own rising dead, and great factions that want to *take* it; war-captain Warden Lyssa Crownmouth needs capable hands who'll fight for the town without trying to own it.
**Location:** Crownmouth; the near edge-ruins of the Crown.
**Primary NPCs:** Warden Lyssa Crownmouth (giver/patron), Militia-Sergeant Tace Ord (the defense, secondary), Grave-Sister Onn Crale (the rising dead, secondary), Innkeep Wenna Stair (the town's rumor-hub, secondary).
**Factions:** Independent (Crownmouth's free militia); brushes Remnant, Ledger, Emberfell.
**Surface Problem:** Help Lyssa keep Crownmouth — the last safe ground at the Crown — alive and free.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Crownmouth is the campaign's strongest *living* proof the crisis is **continent-wide and worst near the Concord's old heart** — but it's surface-history horror, NOT the keystone. Lyssa's dead-won't-rest problem is the **oldest and worst** of any region's; keep it severe but **unexplained.** Establishes Crownmouth as the player's late-game base at the deadliest ruin. The factions circling are the real political danger.
**Objectives:** 1) Earn Lyssa's trust by *helping the town* (not deals). 2) Defend Crownmouth from a pressing threat. 3) Establish a base at the Crown.
**Approaches:** Drive off a ruin-horror at the walls; settle the town's rising dead (Grave-Sister Onn); break a faction's attempt to seize the town; recover townsfolk lost in the near ruins; hold the walls with the militia.
**Obstacles:** The ruin-horrors; the town's own rising dead; the great factions' encroachment; too few defenders.
**Important Scenes:** A ruin-horror at the walls by night; Crownmouth's own dead walking; a faction agent trying to requisition the town.
**Clues:** The crisis worst near the Concord's heart (the oldest, worst dead — surface-history corroboration, NOT the keystone); the factions converging on the Crown.
**Combat Options:** Ruin-horrors and rising dead (**Wraith/Wight/Specter-like**, scaled L13–16) at the walls — deadly; thinnable by Onn's rites; the militia fights alongside; retreat into the town.
**Noncombat Options:** Settling the dead (rites), faction-diplomacy, rescue, wall-defense organization — combat is shared, not forced solo.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Lyssa's militia fights alongside (not a solo siege); Grave-Sister Onn's rites thin the dead; the town's walls for retreat; the threats are telegraphed by season.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests inside Crownmouth (the only safe ground); a worsening-ruin soft-clock.
**Rewards:** Crownmouth as a late-game base (lodging, supply, ruin-guides, salvage-market); Lyssa as a fierce ally; an honest read on every faction at the Crown; powerful thematic corroboration.
**Consequences:** Earning Lyssa secures the only safe ground at the Crown. Failing (or trying to own the town) bars the player from the deadliest ruin's only refuge. A faction seizing the town is a major loss.
**Failure State:** Crownmouth falls or bars the player → the Crown loses its safe ground; reopen by helping retake/rebuild; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_CH_002 (delving the Crown from this base); Q_CH_004 (the camps below); Q_CH_005 (the factions Lyssa warns of).
**Scaling Notes:** L13 — a single threat. L16 — converging horrors, rising dead, and a faction grab at once.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Crownmouth, the great factions), RELATIONSHIPS (Lyssa, Onn, Tace Ord), CONSEQUENCES, HIDDEN_CLUES (crisis-worst-at-the-heart).
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md`, `../../08_npcs/by_region/CONCORD_HEARTLANDS_NPCS.md`.

# Q_CH_002_INTO_THE_CROWN — Into the Crown

**Quest Type:** Dungeon (great ruin) / exploration / faction-race. **Level Range:** 14–17; solo danger: extreme. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Survival DC 18 (the deep Crown); History/Arcana DC 18 (Script/vaults); Investigation DC 18; Con saves DC 17 (deep-ruin dread/dead).
**Starting Hook:** Only "Old Crown" Mab — the legendary guide who always comes back — can take someone into the deep Ruin'd Crown and out alive, and she'll guide the player to a catalogued vault, because even she now fears the Crown is "waking" worse than in thirty years.
**Location:** The deep Ruin'd Crown (the fallen capital ruins).
**Primary NPCs:** "Old Crown" Mab (guide/giver), Ruin-Surveyor Onaia Tarn (the shifting layout, secondary), Field-Lector Wessel Ferrant (the Remnant racing the same vaults, secondary), Remnant-scribe Tace (an M6-oblique Script fragment, minor).
**Factions:** Concord Remnant (racing the player), independent (Mab).
**Surface Problem:** Delve a deep Crown vault and survive (before the Remnant strips it).
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The deep Crown is a *surface*-history ruin — its deepest secret is a **record of what the Concord did** (oblique, gated), NOT the living machine, NOT the keystone, NOT the Court's seat. Mab has **never** been to and does not know the buried Concord Deep or the Hollowmere keystone — keep it that way. The Crown "waking" corroborates the continent-wide reactivation (M2/M6-oblique). NEVER let a Crown vault confirm the apex truth; it records, it does not house.
**Objectives:** 1) Reach a deep vault with Mab's guidance. 2) Recover/read what it holds (oblique). 3) Get out (the toll of the Crown).
**Approaches:** Delve with Mab's craft (the survival-key); race or cooperate with Ferrant's Remnant team; map with Onaia Tarn against the shifting geometry; read Script (gated, oblique); recover a relic/record and retreat.
**Obstacles:** The Crown's lethal deep ruins; the *shifting* geometry; the stirring deep dead; the Remnant racing the same vault; the toll of getting out.
**Important Scenes:** The Crown letting them in "easy"; a passage that "went wrong" since Mab last walked it; a vault that *records* the Concord's grandeur and fall (oblique — never the mechanism).
**Clues:** The Crown reactivating worse than ever (M2/M6-oblique — continent-wide); a Concord *record* (surface history — the Concord was vast, fell deliberately-seeming, its inner circle "vanished rather than died" — read as tragedy, NEVER as the Hollow Court); Script lore.
**Combat Options:** Deep ruin-horrors and stirring dead (**Wraith/Wight/Animated-construct-like**, scaled L14–17) — extreme; thin by route/Mab's craft, avoid by stealth, retreat with Mab.
**Noncombat Options:** Guided delving, stealth, scholarship, the race-as-cooperation — the worst combat is avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** "Old Crown" Mab is the survival-key (telegraph the Crown KEEPS the careless); Onaia's maps; clear retreat (the toll is leaving); the vault can be reached without clearing the ruin.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** No rest in the deep Crown (a hard "the Crown keeps you" clock); rest at Crownmouth.
**Rewards:** A deep Crown vault's salvage/record (oblique); "Old Crown" Mab's loyalty; Concord Script lore; an M2/M6-oblique continent-wide clue; Onaia's true Crown-map.
**Consequences:** Recovering the record advances the player's surface understanding (the Concord's fall) WITHOUT confirming the apex truth. Letting the Remnant take it deepens their dangerous reclaim-drive (Q_MAJOR_011/012). Losing Mab makes the Crown far deadlier.
**Failure State:** A failed delve — the Crown "keeps" something/someone; the player escapes; the vault stays; reopen with more support; no dead end (but the deep is lethal — telegraph hard).
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_CH_003 (Hollin Vane wanting the record); Q_MAJOR_011/012 (the Remnant chain); Q_CH_001 (Crownmouth as base).
**Scaling Notes:** L14 — an outer vault. L17 — the deepest catalogued ruin and its worst dead.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (surface-history record, M2/M6-oblique), RELATIONSHIPS (Mab, Onaia, Ferrant), FACTION_STATE (Remnant race), KNOWN_CLUES (Script).
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_011/012), `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/MYSTERY_WEB.md`.

# Q_CH_003_THE_RECLAIMERS_AMBITION — The Reclaimer's Ambition

**Quest Type:** Faction (Remnant) / patron / late-game escalation. **Level Range:** 14–17; solo danger: high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion/Insight DC 18 (Hollin Vane); Investigation DC 17 (his true aims); Deception DC 17; History/Arcana DC 18.
**Starting Hook:** Crown-Reclaimer Magister Hollin Vane — the Remnant's grand expedition-lord, the end of the order's chain up-river — wants the player as a capable, deniable agent: secure a vault, eliminate a rival, recover a "keystone-fragment" relic, or bring him the frontier's live-node data.
**Location:** Crownmouth and the Ruin'd Crown; the Remnant's heartland expedition seat.
**Primary NPCs:** Crown-Reclaimer Magister Hollin Vane (patron/giver, apex-adjacent dupe), Field-Lector Wessel Ferrant (his turnable deputy, secondary), Factor Mira Crane (a turnable Ledger insider, secondary), "Old Crown" Mab (the guide he hires warily).
**Factions:** Concord Remnant (Vane's expedition), Cinder Ledger (rival salvagers).
**Surface Problem:** Take on (or assess) Hollin Vane's grand reclamation work.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Hollin Vane is the campaign's grandest **dupe** — a brilliant idealist who believes the Concord was benevolent and its knowledge can be *restored* for good. He does NOT know it was a harvest engine, does NOT know the Custodians survive as the Hollow Court, does NOT know the keystone is under Hollowmere. **The Remnant succeeding at the Crown is one of the campaign's bad endings.** He must NEVER confirm the Court or keystone in narration; his "keystone-fragment" is a *misnamed surface relic.* A late-game escalation lever. Vane reads the "vanished inner circle" as tragedy, never as the Court.
**Objectives:** 1) Take or assess Vane's work. 2) Learn his true ambition (restore the Concord, himself among the restorers). 3) Decide how to use this (serve, oppose, or seed his disillusion).
**Approaches:** Serve as his agent (deepening the danger); turn his deputy Ferrant's conscience; turn the Ledger insider Mira Crane; gather intelligence on the order's true reach; (very hard, later) confront Vane with the works' true nature — horrified ally or denying obstacle.
**Obstacles:** Vane's certainty and power; the deniability of his work; the Ledger's rival salvagers; the moral weight of aiding a dupe.
**Important Scenes:** Vane's visionary certainty ("the Concord was interrupted; we will finish the sentence"); a deniable errand with a hidden purpose; Ferrant's quiet doubt about Vane's pace.
**Clues:** The Remnant's true grand ambition (restore the Concord — a campaign-defining bad-ending lever); the "vanished inner circle" surface history (tragedy as Vane reads it — NEVER the Court); the order's chain leading up to Vane (Q_MAJOR_011/012).
**Combat Options:** A rival expedition's soldiers, ruin-horrors, or Vane's own guard (**Veteran/Knight-like**; Vane is **boss-class** Tier 1, AC 15, ~120 HP, archmage-like) — extreme; Vane is far more useful as a misguided patron than a fight.
**Noncombat Options:** Service, intelligence, conscience-turning, truth-revelation, faction-leverage — the real tools (combat with Vane is a last resort).
**Solo Safety Valves:** Vane is a patron, not a forced fight (telegraph his power); turnable insiders (Ferrant, Mira Crane); the player can decline the dirtiest work; retreat to Crownmouth.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at Crownmouth; a Remnant-expedition escalation-clock.
**Rewards:** The Remnant's institutional power as a (dangerous) patron — knowledge, Script-mastery, magic, money, a place in the "restoration"; the truest surface-history reading of the Concord; turnable insiders.
**Consequences:** Serving Vane straight advances a bad-ending lever. Seeding his disillusion (Ferrant, or a far-later truth-reveal) could turn the Crown expedition against the order's leadership. Opposing him makes a formidable late-game obstacle.
**Failure State:** A botched errand — Vane is coolly displeased; the intel may still leak; reopen via Ferrant or Mira Crane; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_CH_002 (the vaults Vane covets); Q_MAJOR_011/012 (the Remnant chain and its bad ending); the player's eventual reckoning with the Crown.
**Scaling Notes:** L14 — a deniable errand. L17 — a confrontation with the Remnant's grand ambition (and Vane himself).
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Remnant escalation-clock), RELATIONSHIPS (Vane, Ferrant, Mira Crane), KNOWN/HIDDEN_CLUES (surface history; NEVER apex), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_011/012), `../../07_factions/major_factions/CONCORD_REMNANT.md`, `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/MYSTERY_WEB.md`.

# Q_CH_004_THE_DOOR_THAT_STICKS — The Door That Sticks

**Quest Type:** Faction (anti-Gravecaller mirror) / moral tragedy / rescue. **Level Range:** 13–16; solo danger: moderate–high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Insight DC 17 (Father Casian Ord); Persuasion DC 17 (easing him back / the camps); Religion DC 16; Investigation DC 16 (the "freeing").
**Starting Hook:** In the Pilgrim Camps below Crownmouth, the gentle heretic-priest Father Casian Ord — breaking under the failing rites at the Concord's heart — has begun to wonder if the held dead should be *freed by force*, and a Gravecaller-adjacent mourner is drawing the desperate toward forbidden "listening."
**Location:** The Pilgrim Camps; the Crown's edge-ruins.
**Primary NPCs:** Father Casian Ord (the tragic priest), Pilgrim "Widow" Esh (Gravecaller-adjacent recruiter, secondary), Grave-Sister Onn Crale (the humane counter, secondary), Camp-Speaker Mother Sel Frome (the camps' order, secondary).
**Factions:** Three Thresholds (lapsed, Ord), Gravecallers (DM-adjacent), Mourners' Circle (Onn).
**Surface Problem:** Help the failing priest and the desperate camps before grief turns to catastrophe.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Ord is the **far-region mirror of the Gravecaller tragedy** — compassion sliding toward catastrophic "freeing." His fragment is the *folk-tragic half-truth* (the dead are *held*, not freed) — NEVER the mechanism, Court, or keystone. The Last Door fails worst here at the Concord's heart (corroboration). If a Gravecaller cell reaches the Crown, Ord is their natural convert. A late-game player can save him — or be pulled in. The core moral tension at its most acute.
**Objectives:** 1) Understand Ord's crisis of faith and the camps' danger. 2) Ease Ord back from the brink (or fail to). 3) Counter "Widow" Esh's forbidden "listening."
**Approaches:** Reach Ord with Onn's true grief-rite (the humane counter); show him a better way to ease the dead; counter "Widow" Esh among the camps; help the camps' material crisis (Mother Sel Frome); investigate (and stop) a "freeing" before it happens.
**Obstacles:** The genuine, overwhelming suffering; the worst-anywhere failing rites; Esh's eerie comfort; Ord's compassion leading him astray; the camps' desperation.
**Important Scenes:** Ord blessing the dead and the dying with failing rites; "Widow" Esh gathering the grieving to "listen"; the moment Ord voices the forbidden thought ("should we be the ones to open it?").
**Clues:** The Last Door failing worst at the Concord's heart (corroboration — surface-history, NOT mechanism); the folk-tragic half-truth (the dead are *held* — oblique, M6-tragic); the Gravecaller temptation at its purest.
**Combat Options:** Cult-touched grieving or a "freeing's" risen dead (scaled L13–16) — best PREVENTED by reaching people; not a forced fight.
**Noncombat Options:** Counter-rite, persuasion, material aid, prevention — combat is the failure-state, not the goal.
**Solo Safety Valves:** The true solution is moral/spiritual (Onn, persuasion), not a fight; the camps and Onn as allies; retreat to Crownmouth; a "freeing" can be prevented before it's dangerous.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at Crownmouth; a soft clock (Ord and Esh both slide further).
**Rewards:** Ord eased back (a saintly, knowledgeable ally) or the tragedy averted; the camps' trust and their fragmentary lore; Onn's deeper trust; intelligence on the Gravecaller far-reach; the core moral tension explored.
**Consequences:** Saving Ord gains a knowing ally and averts a catastrophe. Failing means a far-region "Breaking" at the Crown (a major late-game disaster). Countering Esh protects thousands.
**Failure State:** Ord falls / a "freeing" happens → a far-region cult-flashpoint and tragedy at the Crown; reopen as a containment arc; no dead end (but a real loss).
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_CH_001 (the camps Crownmouth shelters); the Gravecaller far-spread; the campaign's central moral question.
**Scaling Notes:** L13 — a priest's wavering. L16 — an imminent "freeing" with a cell present.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Gravecaller far-mirror, Mourners, Three Thresholds), RELATIONSHIPS (Ord, Esh, Onn, Sel Frome), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_CH_005_THE_CONTESTED_PASS — The Contested Pass

**Quest Type:** Travel / faction-intrigue / banditry. **Level Range:** 13–16; solo danger: high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 17 (Pass-Warden Brask Tarn); Investigation DC 16 (who moves armies); Stealth DC 16; Insight DC 16 (Bandit-Lord "Crow" Maddox).
**Starting Hook:** The Greatspine passes to the Crown are contested by bandits, ruin-horrors, and the great factions' moving forces — and Pass-Warden Captain Brask Tarn, who taxes everyone even-handedly, holds the key crossing the player (and every faction) must use.
**Location:** The Greatspine passes; Brask Tarn's toll-fort; the Crown Road.
**Primary NPCs:** Pass-Warden Captain Brask Tarn (the gatekeeper), Bandit-Lord "Crow" Maddox (the road-raider, secondary), Ash-Emissary Brother Vurr Cindra (the Emberfell rival, secondary), a Remnant/Ledger force on the move (faction agents).
**Factions:** Independent (Brask Tarn's fort), Emberfell Theocracy (Vurr), Concord Remnant & Cinder Ledger (moving forces), outlaw (Maddox).
**Surface Problem:** Secure passage through the contested Greatspine to the Crown.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The gateway to the Crown — establishing the contested passes as the chokepoint every faction (and the player) must cross, and where the great powers' convergence on the Crown becomes visible. Vurr Cindra ties the far-south Emberfell theocracy into the Crown rivalry (Q_MAJOR_013-adjacent). No central-mystery gate; the convergence is the strategic clue.
**Objectives:** 1) Win passage through the Greatspine (toll, deal, or force). 2) Deal with the road-bandits and rival forces. 3) Learn who's moving on the Crown.
**Approaches:** Pay/negotiate Brask Tarn's toll; break or turn "Crow" Maddox's bandits; out-maneuver the Emberfell emissary Vurr Cindra; learn from Brask Tarn who moves armies; escort a column over the pass.
**Obstacles:** Brask Tarn's exact toll; "Crow" Maddox's theatrical brigandage; the high-pass ruin-horrors; the great factions' moving forces; Vurr's hostility.
**Important Scenes:** Brask Tarn's even-handed toll at the fort; "Crow" Maddox's gilt-crowned ambush; the Emberfell emissary watching the pass with proprietary menace.
**Clues:** The great factions converging on the Crown (the strategic clue — everyone wants the ruin); the Emberfell theocracy's interest (Q_MAJOR_013-adjacent — the far south reaches the Crown).
**Combat Options:** Maddox's brigands (**Bandit/Scout-like**), pass-horrors, or a faction skirmish (**Veteran-like**; scaled L13–16) — avoidable by toll/route/negotiation.
**Noncombat Options:** Toll-negotiation, turning the bandits, faction-maneuvering, escort — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; Brask Tarn's even-handed passage (just pay); Maddox is negotiable; clear retreat down the pass; the horrors are telegraphed.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Lodging at the toll-fort; a faction-convergence soft-clock.
**Rewards:** Secured passage to/from the Crown; Brask Tarn's intelligence on who moves armies; "Crow" Maddox broken or turned; a read on the Emberfell rivalry; coin.
**Consequences:** Securing the pass opens the player's road to the Crown and reveals the convergence. Failing leaves the pass a deadly bottleneck. Turning Maddox gains a road-network; antagonizing Vurr opens a far-south enemy.
**Failure State:** The pass stays contested → the route to the Crown is perilous; reopen via Brask Tarn; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_CH_002/003 (the Crown the pass leads to); Q_MAJOR_013 (the Emberfell theocracy); the factions' convergence.
**Scaling Notes:** L13 — a toll and bandits. L16 — converging faction-armies and pass-horrors.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (the great factions' convergence, Emberfell, bandits), RELATIONSHIPS (Brask Tarn, Maddox, Vurr Cindra), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_013), `../../08_npcs/by_region/CONCORD_HEARTLANDS_NPCS.md`.

# Q_CH_006_THE_GRAVE_THAT_WONT_KEEP — The Grave That Won't Keep

**Quest Type:** Faction (Mourners) / lore / old-songs network (at the source). **Level Range:** 13–16; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Religion DC 17; Insight DC 17 (Grave-Sister Onn Crale); History DC 17 (the Crown's failing rites); Persuasion DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** Grave-Sister Onn Crale buries Crownmouth's dead — and re-buries them, because here at the Concord's heart the rites fail worst of anywhere — and she carries the old songs to a place where they were nearly born, asking the player to help her understand why the Door sticks worst *here.*
**Location:** Crownmouth's graveyard; the Crown's edge; the old-songs network (by token/letter).
**Primary NPCs:** Grave-Sister Onn Crale (giver/anchor, kin-in-faith to Senna Crale), Father Casian Ord (the failing official faith), Camp-Mourner's-aide Sel (a failing-rite witness, minor), Gate-priest Father Loy (whose blessings "stutter," minor).
**Factions:** Mourners' Circle (old-songs network at the source), Three Thresholds (failing official faith).
**Surface Problem:** Help Onn Crale understand and ease the worst-failing rites at the Concord's heart.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Onn Crale is the **old-songs network node at the Concord's old heart** — where the M6-oblique fragment ("the grey hands took the dead") is closest to its *source*, and the rites fail worst. The campaign's gravest far-region M5/M6 witness. Yet she knows no mechanism, Court, or keystone — the songs warn, they do not explain. The Crown is surface-history; the *cause* stays buried at Hollowmere. Keep her fragment oblique and grave; never let proximity-to-the-Crown become proximity-to-the-truth.
**Objectives:** 1) Help Onn's worst-failing rites (re-settle the re-rising dead). 2) Learn the old songs at their near-source (M6-oblique). 3) Carry them/the warning across the network.
**Approaches:** Help re-settle the rising dead by rite; learn the old songs from Onn (the near-source fragment); talk with Father Ord and Father Loy about the official faith's collapse; carry the song/warning to the far elders (Senna Crale, Combe, Wend); take the crisis seriously where others deny it.
**Obstacles:** The relentless re-rising dead; the worst-anywhere rite-failure; the grief; the danger of mistaking the Crown for the cause.
**Important Scenes:** Onn re-burying the same dead; the old songs sung at the Concord's heart, nearly at their source; the gravest proof the crisis is worst *here* — and still unexplained.
**Clues:** The old songs near their source ("the grey hands took the dead" — M6-oblique, gravest form); the rites failing worst at the Concord's heart (M5 extreme — surface corroboration, NOT the mechanism); the official faith's collapse (M1/M5).
**Combat Options:** Re-rising dead (**Wight/Specter-like**, scaled L13–16) in the graveyard/edge-ruins — thinnable by Onn's rites; the militia helps; retreat to town.
**Noncombat Options:** Rite, lore, network-weaving, persuasion — combat avoidable by settling the dead.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Onn's rites are the true solution; Crownmouth's militia as backup; the dead are slow; retreat to the town.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at Crownmouth; a re-rising soft-clock (the dead return).
**Rewards:** The old songs at their near-source (the gravest oblique mystery-fragment); Onn Crale's deep trust; the network node at the Concord's heart; the strongest thematic corroboration short of the truth.
**Consequences:** Carrying the near-source songs advances the player's oblique understanding profoundly (without confirming the apex truth). Easing Onn's rites helps the worst-afflicted town. Misreading the Crown as the cause is a fair false-lead the network and the buried truth ultimately correct.
**Failure State:** If Onn isn't helped, the re-rising worsens and the songs stay closed — reopen via the network or by serving Crownmouth; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_CH_004 (Father Ord's parallel crisis); Q_MD_004/Q_SALT_004 (the far elders); Q_MAJOR_001 (the deep mystery — whose answer is NOT here).
**Scaling Notes:** Lore/grief-weight scales with the player's central-mystery progress; combat scales with the re-rising dead.
**State Updates:** KNOWN/HIDDEN_CLUES (M6-oblique near-source, M5 extreme), RELATIONSHIPS (Onn, Ord), FACTION_STATE (Mourner-network), CONSEQUENCES.
