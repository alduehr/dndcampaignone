## AI Use

Eight developed quests for the Glassmere League (the Ledger's true heartland; a city-world of banks, guilds, civic intrigue; Lvl 9–13). Cast drawn from `08_npcs/by_region/GLASSMERE_LEAGUE_NPCS.md`. The League **denies the frontier crisis by prosperity** — keep failure-themes oblique here; the Ledger's continental scale is the live revelation, not the harvest. DM-Only sections never surface to the player. Feeds major quests Q_MAJOR_004 (Ledger), Q_MAJOR_011/019 (Remnant/League). Anchors: Glassmere, the Floor, the Three Bridges, the Reliquary, Lowwater.

# Q_GL_001_THE_LEAGUE_OVERTURE — The League's Overture

**Quest Type:** Negotiation / faction favor. **Level Range:** 9–11; solo danger: low (pure intrigue). **Secrecy:** player-safe.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion/Insight DC 15–18 reading Oren Glass's terms; Investigation DC 15 sourcing what the League really wants; Deception DC 17 if bluffing scale.
**Starting Hook:** League-Factor Oren Glass receives the frontier player warmly and asks them to carry a League trade-overture down-river to Caradril's council — "useful friends are always welcome."
**Location:** Glassmere city; the Floor; later the road/water down to Caradril.
**Primary NPCs:** Oren Glass (giver), Floor-Magister Doss Glass, Civic-Magistra Esh Cairn (reform contact), Cut-out "the Quiet Man."
**Factions:** Glassmere League (civic), Cinder Ledger (shadow-interest), Tidewater Council (recipient).
**Surface Problem:** Deliver the overture and broker a first League–Caradril contact.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The "civic" overture is partly a vehicle for the Ledger to fold Caradril's charter-trade into the League's orbit; Glass is fighting to keep his own government from Ledger capture. Demonstrates the Ledger's continental scale (Q_MAJOR_004) — NOT the harvest.
**Objectives:** 1) Learn what the overture truly carries. 2) Decide whether to carry it honestly, alter it, or refuse. 3) Deliver (or expose) it.
**Approaches:** Honest courier; read-and-renegotiate via Esh Cairn; sell the intel to a rival; refuse and keep League goodwill by other means.
**Obstacles:** Glass's flattery masking leverage; Ledger agents wanting the overture's contents; the reform faction wanting it stopped.
**Important Scenes:** Glass's terrace pitch on the Floor; a quiet warning from Esh Cairn; the Quiet Man offering to "carry it for you" (a trap).
**Clues:** League wealth flows on Concord-built canals (oblique world-texture, not a harvest clue); the Ledger's name in the overture's fine print (M3-adjacent scale).
**Combat Options:** None expected; if betrayed, 2–3 **Spy-like** League agents (AC 12, ~22 HP) intercept the courier on the road — avoidable by stealth or a safe escort (Mossa Drenn).
**Noncombat Options:** Entirely social — negotiation, courier-craft, alliance.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No forced fight; the player can decline at any stage; Esh Cairn and Drenn are allies; the road is scoutable.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Free rests at the Gilded Lock inn; soft pressure — Glass moves on without the player if ignored.
**Rewards:** League goodwill; introductions to the League's high circles; a League trade-charter (income); ~100–150 gp courier-fee.
**Consequences:** Success — a continental patron. Honest delivery quietly advances the Ledger's reach (Q_MAJOR_004). Exposing it earns reform-faction trust but makes Glass cool. Ignored — the League's reach grows off-screen.
**Failure State:** If the overture is lost/stolen, Glass is embarrassed but not hostile; the player can recover it or make amends — no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_GL_002 (the bank war); Q_GL_005 (reform); the Caradril correspondent thread.
**Scaling Notes:** L9 — pure social. L12+ — the interception becomes a real **Spy/Veteran** ambush; raise the political stakes (the overture carries a hostile clause).
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (League ±, Ledger ± by choice), RELATIONSHIPS (Glass, Cairn), NPC_MEMORY.

# Q_GL_002_THE_BANK_AND_THE_LEDGER — The Bank and the Ledger

**Quest Type:** City intrigue / faction-war leverage. **Level Range:** 10–12; solo danger: low–moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 17 (Sefwy Holt); Investigation DC 18 (Ledger paper-trail); Stealth DC 16 (the Three Bridges vault-house); Insight DC 15 (reading Under-Banker Sef Holt's nerves).
**Starting Hook:** Banker-Magistra Sefwy Holt summons the player: she wants proof of Cinder Ledger malfeasance — and the frontier player may already carry exactly that.
**Location:** The Three Bridges (banking quarter); the Floor; a Ledger counting-house.
**Primary NPCs:** Sefwy Holt (giver), Under-Banker Sef Holt (turnable heir), Heartland-Provost Iss Vyre (Ledger), Notary-Master Onaia Senn (paper-trail), Clerk Mavis Glass.
**Factions:** Glassmere League (Holt's bank), Cinder Ledger (target).
**Surface Problem:** Gather and deliver evidence that damages the Ledger's monopoly.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Holt is a clean continental lever against the Ledger (Q_MAJOR_004) — but allying with her trades one monopolist for another; she must never learn the harvest (she'd corner the market on it). Iss Vyre's books prove the Ledger's frontier squeeze is directed from here.
**Objectives:** 1) Secure proof of Ledger abuse. 2) Decide who receives it (Holt / civic court / no one). 3) Survive the Ledger's notice.
**Approaches:** Hand Holt frontier M3 proof the player already has; burgle/copy a Ledger counting-house; turn Under-Banker Sef Holt or Underfactor Coll Riis (if known from Caradril); legal route via Onaia Senn.
**Obstacles:** Iss Vyre's serene reach; the bank's over-leverage (Sef Holt's anxiety); notary forms that bind.
**Important Scenes:** Holt pricing the player's risk "in real time"; a tense counting-house infiltration; Iss Vyre's velvet warning.
**Clues:** Ledger directives flow down-river to Vyre/Caradril (M3 scale); the relic-monopoly rests on frontier "old glass" Holt doesn't understand (M2-oblique, do not explain).
**Combat Options:** If the infiltration is blown, 3–4 **Guard/Spy-like** Ledger bank-guards (AC 14–16) — avoidable by stealth, bribery, or going legal.
**Noncombat Options:** Proof-gathering, alliance, legal filing, turning an insider — no fight required.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Multiple proof-paths (the player may already hold the evidence); turnable insiders; legal off-ramp; the bank prefers paper to blood.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at the bank's guest-house; clock — the Ledger's monopoly clock advances if the player stalls.
**Rewards:** Holt's powerful (amoral) patronage; a line of League credit; coin; a real blow to the Ledger's monopoly clock if delivered.
**Consequences:** Allying Holt empowers a new monopolist (a genuine choice). Refusing keeps the player's hands clean but loses the lever. Crossing Holt withdraws the one continental counter to the Ledger.
**Failure State:** Caught with no proof — the Ledger marks the player (Q_MAJOR_004 enmity rises), but Holt still wants them; the quest reopens via another counting-house.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_GL_001; Q_MAJOR_004 escalation; a Ledger reprisal.
**Scaling Notes:** L10 — light security. L13 — Ledger fields a **Veteran** enforcer-captain (cf. Mauld Tallow's type) and a counter-investigation.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Ledger ↓ / Holt-League ↑), WORLD_CLOCKS (Monopoly clock), RELATIONSHIPS, INVENTORY (proof item).
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_004), `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/CLUE_INDEX.md`.

# Q_GL_003_THE_FACE_OF_THE_EAR — The Face of the Ear

**Quest Type:** Information-warfare / mystery. **Level Range:** 10–13; solo danger: low directly, high indirectly. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Investigation DC 18 tracing cut-outs; Insight DC 16 spotting a sold-out; Persuasion/Deception DC 17 trading secrets; Stealth DC 16 shadowing a go-between.
**Starting Hook:** The player needs something only the Glass Ear can sell — a person's location, a deal's contents, a faction's weakness — and the Ear wants frontier intelligence in trade.
**Location:** Glassmere citywide; ever-changing meeting-spots; never the same cut-out twice.
**Primary NPCs:** "the Glass Ear" (through cut-out "the Quiet Man"), the Bellman (if Caradril's Hush is known — a rival/trade-partner), a target NPC the player is buying on.
**Factions:** Independent (the Ear), the Hush (rival network).
**Surface Problem:** Buy a specific secret without being sold out.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The Ear knows everything *known* — which pointedly does NOT include the harvest, because no living source possesses it. Dramatizes that the apex truth is genuinely unsourceable. Never reveal a "true face" unless the campaign demands it.
**Objectives:** 1) Reach the Ear through the cut-out chain. 2) Pay a price (coin, a secret, a favor) without overpaying with one's own secrets. 3) Get clean information.
**Approaches:** Pay coin; trade frontier crisis-intelligence (cheap to the Ear, who shelved it as superstition); trade a rival's secret; map the cut-out chain to gain leverage (risky).
**Obstacles:** Every answer is a question; the Ear may sell the player out to the highest bidder; a rival network watching.
**Important Scenes:** A maddeningly indirect bargain in a back-room; realizing a "fact" was a plant; the Ear declining to sell the one thing the player most wants (the frontier truth — because no one can).
**Clues:** The Ear can confirm what factions *do*, never what the Concord *was* (a clean meta-clue about the mystery's nature).
**Combat Options:** None sane — fighting a cut-out tells you nothing; if the player tries, **Spy-like** cut-outs scatter.
**Noncombat Options:** Trade, deduction, network-mapping — the whole quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; the player sets the price they'll pay; walking away is always allowed.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** No hard clock; betrayal-risk rises the more the player reveals.
**Rewards:** Almost any *known* secret on the continent; a continental back-channel; the meta-knowledge that the central truth is unsourceable.
**Consequences:** Over-trading one's own secrets arms a future enemy (a delayed consequence). Cultivating the Ear yields a mercenary intelligence ally.
**Failure State:** Sold out — a secret of the player's surfaces in an enemy's hands later; play continues with a new complication, not a wall.
**Scaling Notes:** L10 — minor secrets. L13 — the secret bought becomes a major arc lever; the betrayal-stakes scale up.
**State Updates:** CONSEQUENCES (secrets traded), KNOWN_CLUES (info bought), RELATIONSHIPS (the Ear), NPC_MEMORY.
**Related:** `../../08_npcs/by_region/GLASSMERE_LEAGUE_NPCS.md`, `06_settlements/caradril_districts/THE_SILL.md` (the Hush parallel).

# Q_GL_004_THE_RELIQUARYS_JUDGEMENT — The Reliquary's Judgement

**Quest Type:** Faction (Remnant) / mystery clue trail. **Level Range:** 10–13; solo danger: low. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** History/Arcana DC 18 (Concord lore with Mareth Senn); Persuasion DC 17; Stealth DC 18 (sealed-vault gate via Tace Ferrant); Insight DC 16 reading the contain-vs-reclaim schism.
**Starting Hook:** Reliquary-Lector Mareth Senn requests the player deliver a frontier relic/rubbing to her grand chapter — and judge whether the frontier scholars (Briss, Quorrin) are "going too far."
**Location:** The Reliquary (Remnant grand chapter-house); the scholars' quarter.
**Primary NPCs:** Mareth Senn (giver), Sub-Lector Brann Senn (turnable), Translator-Adept Mira Vael (a frightening fragment), Archive-Warden Tace Ferrant (sealed-vault gate).
**Factions:** Concord Remnant (institutional faction-internal lever).
**Surface Problem:** Carry findings up the chain and report on the frontier chapters.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Mareth embodies the Remnant's "contain vs. reclaim" schism — she rightly fears the works but draws the wrong conclusion (contain to control, not destroy). Her knowledge is deep history/language, NOT the live mechanism; she must never confirm the harvest. Feeds Q_MAJOR_011/019.
**Objectives:** 1) Deliver the finding. 2) Decide what to report about the frontier chapters. 3) Optionally glimpse the sealed-vault fragment (gated).
**Approaches:** Honest report; shield the frontier scholars; play Mareth against field-commander Hollin Vane (Heartlands); pursue the sealed-vault text via Tace Ferrant or Mira Vael's translation.
**Obstacles:** The order's pride and self-serving idealism; the sealed-vault gate; Mira Vael's fear of her own translation.
**Important Scenes:** Mareth treating knowledge as treasure-and-weapon; Mira Vael's terror at a fragment she's read; a gated, oblique glimpse of a sealed text (M6-oblique — never the mechanism).
**Clues:** The Remnant's institution wants to *contain* (closer to right than Vane's *reclaim*) — a key M9/faction-direction clue; a frightening but inconclusive sealed fragment.
**Combat Options:** None by preference; the Reliquary is ward-and-guard defended — escalation is a losing fight (avoid).
**Noncombat Options:** Scholarship, interpretation, faction-leverage, persuasion — the entire quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; multiple report-choices; the sealed-vault is optional and gated; allies among the junior scholars.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at the chapter guest-cells; soft clock — the Remnant schism deepens if ignored.
**Rewards:** The deepest Concord scholarship short of the Crown; Script/Old-Custodial expertise; a lever inside the Remnant; honest (if filtered) interpretation of the player's findings.
**Consequences:** Reporting honestly may endanger Briss/Quorrin. Playing Mareth vs. Vane can split the order. Crossing the Reliquary turns the order's institutional weight against the player.
**Failure State:** A botched delivery or a blown vault-attempt loses Reliquary trust; reopen via a junior scholar — no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_MAJOR_011 (Sealed Archive chain), Q_MAJOR_012 (Heartlands/Hollin Vane), Q_CH_xxx (Crown).
**Scaling Notes:** L10 — surface scholarship. L13 — the sealed fragment becomes a real M6-gated lead; the schism becomes actionable.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Remnant internal), KNOWN_CLUES (M9 direction), RELATIONSHIPS, NPC_MEMORY.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_011/012), `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/MYSTERY_WEB.md`.

# Q_GL_005_THE_FLOOR_AND_THE_FORGOTTEN — The Floor and the Forgotten

**Quest Type:** Social / moral dilemma / city reform. **Level Range:** 9–12; solo danger: low. **Secrecy:** player-safe.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 16 (Civic-Magistra Esh Cairn's reform); Investigation DC 15 (Lowwater destitution); Intimidation/Persuasion DC 17 shaming a money-interest.
**Starting Hook:** Almoner Sera Frome (or Esh Cairn) begs help: Lowwater's poor are starving while the Floor counts fortunes, and a reform vote teeters.
**Location:** Lowwater (poor riverside district); the Floor; the Three Bridges.
**Primary NPCs:** Esh Cairn (reformer), Almoner Sera Frome, Guild-Master Holt Crucible (anti-banker ally), River-Boss "Carp" Maddox (underworld lever), Mother Wenna Lowwater (moral anchor).
**Factions:** Glassmere League (civic reform vs. money-interests).
**Surface Problem:** Tip a civic reform/relief vote and feed the forgotten poor.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** A low-stakes-but-humane civic quest that introduces Mother Wenna Lowwater (the League node of the old-songs network) as a *person* before any mystery use — and shows the League's prosperity-denial up close. No harvest content.
**Objectives:** 1) Build a reform coalition (guilds + reformers + the poor). 2) Counter the money-interests. 3) Win the vote or secure relief another way.
**Approaches:** Coalition-building; shaming the rich publicly (pamphleteer Doss); an underworld lever via Carp Maddox (morally grey); private charity if the vote fails.
**Obstacles:** Outnumbered reformers; bought councilors; the watch out-bribed.
**Important Scenes:** Mother Wenna tending the pauper-dead with dignity the rich forgot; a Floor confrontation; the vote.
**Clues:** None central — this is humane civic color that seeds future allies (Wenna, Cairn, Crucible).
**Combat Options:** None expected; a money-interest's hired toughs (2–3 **Thug/Guard-like**) may menace the player in Lowwater — avoidable.
**Noncombat Options:** Politics, charity, persuasion, coalition — the whole quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; multiple win-paths incl. failure-with-grace (private relief); strong allies.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests in Lowwater; vote-day is a soft deadline.
**Rewards:** Reform-faction and Lowwater goodwill; Mother Wenna as a trusted contact (humane ally, later network-key); Guild-Master Crucible's friendship.
**Consequences:** Winning shifts League civic balance toward reform. Failure leaves the poor worse but earns the player a beloved-among-the-poor reputation. Using Maddox owes the underworld a favor.
**Failure State:** Lost vote → relief still possible privately; the reform thread carries forward — no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Mother Wenna's old-songs network (gated, far later); a Maddox favor called in.
**Scaling Notes:** Stakes scale with how much of the League's politics the player has touched (Q_GL_001/002).
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (League civic balance), RELATIONSHIPS (Wenna, Cairn, Crucible, Maddox), NPC_MEMORY.

# Q_GL_006_THE_DRIFTING_GRAVES — The Drifting Graves of Lowwater

**Quest Type:** Investigation / death-rite failure (oblique). **Level Range:** 10–13; solo danger: low–moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Religion DC 16 (grave-rites); Investigation DC 17 (which graves won't keep); Insight DC 16 (Wenna's oblique fragment); Medicine DC 14.
**Starting Hook:** Grave-Singer Coll Lowwater is frightened: the pauper-dead are "drifting" — the League's first true failure-sign, in the city that denies the crisis exists.
**Location:** Lowwater's pauper-grounds; the riverside.
**Primary NPCs:** Coll Lowwater (witness), Mother Wenna Lowwater (oblique keeper), Hierarch Mother Ysolde Glass (denial — her grand rites "stutter," hidden), Temple-acolyte Ode (terrified witness).
**Factions:** Mourners' Circle (folk-faith), Three Thresholds (official denial).
**Surface Problem:** Settle the drifting pauper-dead; find out why the rites slip here.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The League's far-from-the-edge first failure — proof the crisis is reaching even the prosperous heartland. Wenna's "the grey hands took the dead" is M6-oblique folk-memory ONLY; she does not know the mechanism, the Court, or the keystone. Ysolde Glass is hiding that her official rites are stuttering (denial-by-prosperity).
**Objectives:** 1) Confirm the failures are real (against official denial). 2) Help settle the drifting dead by folk-rite. 3) Decide whether to force the temple to admit it.
**Approaches:** Folk-rite with Wenna/Coll; pressure or expose Ysolde Glass's hidden stutter; quiet investigation; bring frontier knowledge to bear.
**Obstacles:** Official denial; the city's contempt for "down-river superstition"; the cause unknowable here.
**Important Scenes:** A pauper-grave that won't keep; Wenna's grave, song-laced fragment; the acolyte Ode's terror at a "stuttered" rite; Ysolde's brittle denial.
**Clues:** Lowwater graves drifting (M5 inland); Wenna's oblique "grey hands" fragment (M6-oblique — keep oblique); the official rite stuttering (M1/M5 denied).
**Combat Options:** A single Restless-Remembrance-like figure (per ACT_1_THREATS scaling to tier; AC 12, scaled HP) may rise — settle-able by rite, not just force; telegraphed.
**Noncombat Options:** Rite, investigation, social pressure — combat is avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** The risen dead are settle-able non-violently; Wenna/Coll assist; retreat to the temple/inn always available.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests in Lowwater; soft clock — more graves drift the longer it's ignored.
**Rewards:** Mother Wenna's deeper trust (gateway to the old-songs network, far later); Mourner goodwill; a confirmed inland data-point for the player's own understanding.
**Consequences:** Forcing the temple to admit cracks the city's denial (a small civic earthquake). Settling the dead eases Lowwater. Ignored — the drift spreads and Wenna grieves alone.
**Failure State:** If the dead aren't settled, a Lowwater crisis hook opens and the Mourner thread persists — no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** The old-songs network (deeply gated); the temple's denial cracking; frontier-truth correlation.
**Scaling Notes:** L10 — one settle-able dead. L13 — multiple, and the temple's denial becomes a political flashpoint.
**State Updates:** KNOWN/HIDDEN_CLUES (M5 inland, M6-oblique), FACTION_STATE (Mourners ↑, Thresholds strain), RELATIONSHIPS (Wenna, Coll), NPC_MEMORY.

# Q_GL_007_THE_HUMMING_CABINET — The Humming Cabinet

**Quest Type:** Mystery / relic-trade / haunted object. **Level Range:** 10–13; solo danger: low–moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Investigation DC 17 (provenance); Arcana/Religion DC 18 (what the relic is — gated, oblique); Persuasion DC 16 (Antiquarian Doll Cairn); Stealth DC 16.
**Starting Hook:** Antiquarian Doll Cairn's prized Concord relic has "gone strange" — it hums, and her household is uneasy. She pays well for someone to make it stop (or take it).
**Location:** The scholars' quarter; Cairn's private cabinet; the relic-market.
**Primary NPCs:** Antiquarian Doll Cairn (giver), Reliquary-porter Tibb (won't touch the "humming" ones), Translator-Adept Mira Vael (interpretation), a Ledger relic-buyer (wants it).
**Factions:** Concord Remnant (interpretation), Cinder Ledger (buyer), independent collectors.
**Surface Problem:** Quiet or remove a "humming" relic.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The relic is a raw Remembrance relic (M2-oblique). It is reactivating like every node — but here it's a single object, far up the chain. NEVER explain Remembrance/the harvest to the player; keep it "old, strange, waking." A clean inland M2 corroboration that the relic-phenomenon is continent-wide.
**Objectives:** 1) Determine what's wrong with the relic. 2) Decide its fate (contain, destroy, sell, hand to Remnant). 3) Deal with who else wants it.
**Approaches:** Remnant interpretation (Mira Vael); contain it with a Mourner rite; sell it to the Ledger buyer (morally grey, feeds the monopoly); destroy it (loses its lore but is safest).
**Obstacles:** Competing buyers; the relic's growing "presence"; thin-touch-like sickness in Cairn's household (oblique, southern/inland face).
**Important Scenes:** The cabinet humming in the dark; Tibb refusing to carry it; Mira Vael's frightened, partial read; a Ledger buyer's pressure.
**Clues:** A raw relic "waking" far inland (M2 continental); thin-touch-like household sickness (M2/M10-oblique); the Ledger's hunger for such objects (M3).
**Combat Options:** If the relic's "presence" manifests, a minor Remembrance-manifestation (scaled, AC 12) — settle-able/containable, telegraphed; or buyers' toughs if a deal sours.
**Noncombat Options:** Investigation, rite, trade, scholarship — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** The manifestation is containable non-violently; multiple fates for the relic; allies (Mira, a Mourner) help; retreat available.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests; the relic worsens slowly if left.
**Rewards:** Coin (Cairn's fee or the sale); Remnant or Ledger goodwill by choice; an inland M2 data-point; possibly a minor relic [STAGE_14_FINALIZE].
**Consequences:** Selling to the Ledger advances the monopoly (Q_MAJOR_004). Handing it to the Remnant feeds their reclaim-ambition. Destroying it is safe but loses the lead.
**Failure State:** If the relic is lost/stolen, it surfaces later in worse hands — a follow-up complication, not a wall.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_GL_002 (Ledger), Q_GL_004 (Remnant), the relic resurfacing.
**Scaling Notes:** L10 — inert-but-eerie. L13 — an active manifestation and a multi-buyer bidding war.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (M2 inland), FACTION_STATE (by choice), INVENTORY, NPC_MEMORY.
