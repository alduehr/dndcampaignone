## AI Use

Five developed quests for the Marrowdowns (settled chalk-down sheep-country and barrow-fields of old Orrun; manorial holds and shire-moots; Lvl 6–10). Cast from `08_npcs/by_region/MARROWDOWNS_NPCS.md`. A **pastoral mid-continent** region — a gentler, more *ordinary* land than the haunted frontier, useful as a route south and a place of quiet old custom. The barrow-fields are **pre-Concord** (older than the Concord, like the Saltmere's drowned towns) — a resonant echo, NOT the keystone; the barrow-dead's restlessness reads as old down-country superstition. No NPC knows the apex truth. Anchors: Marrowmoot, Penmark Hold, the Barrow-Fields, Wether.

# Q_MD_001_THE_BARROWS_GONE_RESTLESS — The Barrows Gone Restless

**Quest Type:** Investigation / haunted site (oblique) / shire-politics. **Level Range:** 6–9; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Investigation DC 16 (the stirring barrows); Religion DC 15 (the barrow-custom); Persuasion DC 16 (Shire-Reeve Aldous Penmark); Insight DC 15.
**Starting Hook:** Shire-Reeve Aldous Penmark's ancient barrow-fields have "gone restless" — the dead won't lie quiet — and he blames grave-robbers, but Moot-Mother Senna Crale says the *songs* warn of something else. He wants it settled before the shire's long peace breaks.
**Location:** The Barrow-Fields; Marrowmoot; Penmark Hold.
**Primary NPCs:** Shire-Reeve Aldous Penmark (giver, blames robbers), Moot-Mother Senna Crale (knows it's more), Barrow-Master Doss Wether (the grey witness/aggravator), Barrow-Singer Coll Crale (which barrows stir, secondary).
**Factions:** Independent (shire-law), Mourners' Circle.
**Surface Problem:** Settle the restless barrows and keep the shire's peace.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The barrows are **pre-Concord ruins** — their waking dead corroborate the "older, wider fall" theme, NOT the keystone. Doss Wether's robbing *worsens* the local stirring (an unwitting aggravator). Senna Crale half-conflates the older fall with "the grey hands" (M6-oblique). Settle by barrow-custom (Senna), not mechanism. The crisis reaches even the orderly downs.
**Objectives:** 1) Find why the barrows stir (robbery vs. the deeper cause). 2) Settle the worst barrows (custom). 3) Decide how to handle Doss Wether's role.
**Approaches:** Investigate the stirring with Coll Crale; perform the barrow-custom with Senna Crale; stop Doss Wether's robbing (the local aggravator — a clean small win); persuade Penmark the cause is deeper than robbers.
**Obstacles:** The waking barrow-dead; Penmark's "it's only robbers" stance; Doss Wether's looting; the shire's complacency.
**Important Scenes:** A barrow whose dead won't stay down; Senna Crale singing the old custom against Penmark's robber-theory; catching Doss Wether mid-dig.
**Clues:** Pre-Concord barrows with waking dead (the "older fall" echo — M5-resonant); Doss Wether's robbing worsening it; Senna's old-songs fragment ("the grey hands" — M6-oblique).
**Combat Options:** Waking barrow-dead (**Skeleton/Zombie/Wight-like**, scaled L6–9, slow, telegraphed) — thinnable by custom; retreat across the downs.
**Noncombat Options:** Custom-rite, investigation, stopping the robber, persuasion — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Senna's custom-rite is the true solution; the dead are slow and telegraphed; Coll Crale assists; clear retreat on the open downs.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at Marrowmoot/Penmark Hold; a soft clock (more barrows stir if delayed).
**Rewards:** Penmark's gratitude and the shire's hospitality; Senna Crale's trust and the old-songs thread; the barrows settled; an "older fall" data-point.
**Consequences:** Settling them keeps the shire's peace and proves the spread reaches the orderly downs. Failing breaks the long peace (a Marrowdowns clock). Stopping Doss Wether eases the local stirring.
**Failure State:** The barrows spread their restlessness → the shire's peace cracks, gentry alarm; reopen as a barrow-crisis arc; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_MD_002 (Doss Wether's deep barrows); Q_MD_004 (the old songs); the Marrowdowns barrow-clock.
**Scaling Notes:** L6 — one stirring barrow. L9 — a barrow-field waking and a Remnant racing to claim it.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (older-fall echo, M6-oblique), FACTION_STATE (shire, Mourners), RELATIONSHIPS (Penmark, Senna, Doss Wether), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md`, `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/CLUE_INDEX.md`.

# Q_MD_002_THE_BARROW_THAT_BITES — The Barrow That Bites

**Quest Type:** Dungeon (barrow) / escort / moral knot. **Level Range:** 7–10; solo danger: moderate–high. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Investigation DC 16 (the deep barrow); Survival DC 15; Insight DC 16 (Doss Wether's nerve); Sleight/Thieves' tools DC 16 (barrow-traps).
**Starting Hook:** Barrow-Master Doss Wether has found a deep barrow whose dead "got up when he opened it," and he won't go back alone — he'll guide one person to its riches and its horror, because his reckless partner "Spade" Voss is about to crack it open anyway.
**Location:** A deep pre-Concord barrow; the Barrow-Fields.
**Primary NPCs:** Barrow-Master Doss Wether (guide/giver, aggravator), Barrow-Robber "Spade" Voss (the reckless partner, secondary), Moot-Mother Senna Crale (the right way), Sub-Lector Wenn Penmark (Remnant interest, secondary).
**Factions:** Independent (barrow-delvers), Concord Remnant (oblique), Mourners' Circle.
**Surface Problem:** Help Doss Wether reach (and survive) the deep barrow before "Spade" Voss wrecks it.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** A pre-Concord barrow-delve — atmospheric "older fall" horror with NO keystone, Court, or mechanism. The deep barrow is the oldest, most-stirring mound; "Spade" Voss's recklessness will make it catastrophically worse. The moral knot: looting *worsens* the local stirring. Keep it oblique; never resolve the deeper mystery here.
**Objectives:** 1) Reach the deep barrow safely. 2) Decide its fate (loot, settle, or seal). 3) Stop "Spade" Voss from making it worse.
**Approaches:** Delve with Doss Wether's barrow-craft; settle the deep dead with Senna's token; seal it rather than loot it; stop "Spade" Voss; let Wenn Penmark study (carefully) instead of plundering.
**Obstacles:** The deep barrow's traps and waking dead; "Spade" Voss's reckless greed; Doss Wether's looting impulse; the dark and the dread.
**Important Scenes:** The deep barrow's dead rising as it's opened; "Spade" Voss cracking a worse mound; the choice between loot and letting-lie.
**Clues:** The oldest barrow waking worst (the "older fall," vivid — oblique); looting *worsening* the stirring (the moral knot); a "wrong" pre-Concord relic (M2-oblique, never the keystone).
**Combat Options:** Deep barrow-dead and barrow-guardians (**Wight/Specter/Skeleton-like**, scaled L7–10) in a trap-laced mound — dangerous; thin by token/route, avoid by sealing.
**Noncombat Options:** Barrow-craft, settling, sealing, stopping the reckless partner — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Doss Wether's barrow-craft (losing him in the deep is the real danger — telegraph it); Senna's token; clear retreat; the dead are slow in tight ground.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** No rest in the barrow (a dread/air clock); rest before/after on the downs.
**Rewards:** The deep barrow's ancient loot and lore; Doss Wether's loyalty (or his redemption if he stops); an oblique "older fall" data-point; a "wrong" relic for the Remnant/Mourners.
**Consequences:** Settling/sealing it eases the local stirring. Looting it (or letting Voss) worsens it badly. Turning Doss Wether from robbing is a clean small win for the whole region.
**Failure State:** "Spade" Voss wrecks the barrow → the worst local stirring yet; Doss Wether may be lost; the player escapes; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_MD_001 (the restless barrows); Q_MD_004 (the old songs that warn of this); the Remnant's barrow-interest.
**Scaling Notes:** L7 — a shallow mound. L10 — the oldest, deepest barrow and its worst dead.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (older-fall, deep), RELATIONSHIPS (Doss Wether, Spade Voss, Senna), CONSEQUENCES, INVENTORY (the find).
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md`, `../../10_dungeons_and_ruins/THE_BARROW_OF_NINE_DOORS.md`.

# Q_MD_003_THE_WOOL_AND_THE_LEDGER — The Wool and the Ledger

**Quest Type:** Faction (anti-Ledger) / trade / shire-politics. **Level Range:** 6–9; solo danger: low. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 16 (the gentry / Sera Wether); Investigation DC 16 (the grey relic-trade); Insight DC 15.
**Starting Hook:** The Cinder Ledger's Wool-Factor Iss Penmark is edging into the orderly shire's wool-trade — and quietly buying barrow-relics on the side — and Wool-Master Sera Wether wants the Ledger's grip broken before the gentry's independence is bought out from under them.
**Location:** Marrowmoot; Wether (wool-village); Penmark Hold.
**Primary NPCs:** Wool-Master Sera Wether (giver), Wool-Factor Iss Penmark (Ledger antagonist), Shire-Reeve Aldous Penmark (the gentry's weight), Moot-Clerk Doll Crale (the paper-trail, secondary).
**Factions:** Cinder Ledger (antagonist), independent (shire-gentry).
**Surface Problem:** Break the Ledger's grip on the shire's wool-trade.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** A clean anti-Ledger trade quest dramatizing Q_MAJOR_004 in the orderly downs — with a quiet barrow-relic side-trade (M3-oblique) linking even the pastoral shire to the relic-mystery. No central-mystery gate; the relic-trade is the only oblique thread.
**Objectives:** 1) Understand the Ledger's wool-and-relic play. 2) Break or limit it (gentry weight, exposure, fair-trade). 3) Optionally cut the barrow-relic side-trade.
**Approaches:** Rally the gentry behind Sera Wether and Aldous Penmark; expose Iss Penmark's collusion and barrow-relic buying; out-bargain the Ledger; use the moot's old law against the factor; trace the relic side-trade.
**Obstacles:** The Ledger's coin and patience; the gentry's caution; the shire's reluctance to make a scene; Iss Penmark's smoothness.
**Important Scenes:** Iss Penmark edging into the wool-moot; Sera Wether rallying the wool-trade; catching the quiet barrow-relic buying.
**Clues:** The Ledger reaching the orderly downs (Q_MAJOR_004, scale); the barrow-relic side-trade (M3-oblique — even the shire touches the relic-mystery).
**Combat Options:** None expected; Ledger toughs only if badly pressured — avoidable.
**Noncombat Options:** Politics, exposure, trade, old-law — the whole quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; the gentry and Sera Wether as allies; multiple resolutions; the Ledger prefers profit to force.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests in the shire; a wool-season soft-clock.
**Rewards:** The gentry's gratitude and Aldous Penmark's deeper trust; Sera Wether as a trade-ally; leverage over the Ledger's downs-arm; an M3 relic-trade lead; coin.
**Consequences:** Breaking the grip keeps the shire independent. Letting the Ledger win buys out the downs (Q_MAJOR_004). Cutting the relic-trade hurts the Ledger's chain.
**Failure State:** The Ledger takes the wool-trade → the gentry's independence erodes; reopen via the moot; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_MD_002 (the relics' source); Q_MAJOR_004 (the continental Ledger); the shire's politics.
**Scaling Notes:** L6 — a trade-rivalry. L9 — a Ledger buyout-push on the whole shire.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Ledger downs-arm, gentry), RELATIONSHIPS (Sera Wether, Iss Penmark, Aldous Penmark), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_004), `../../08_npcs/by_region/MARROWDOWNS_NPCS.md`.

# Q_MD_004_THE_OLD_SONGS_OF_THE_DOWNS — The Old Songs of the Downs

**Quest Type:** Faction (Mourners) / lore / old-songs network. **Level Range:** 6–9; solo danger: low. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Religion DC 15; Persuasion/Insight DC 16 (earning Senna); History DC 16 (the barrow-custom).
**Starting Hook:** Moot-Mother Senna Crale keeps the downs' old songs — folk-memory of "the grey hands" that took the dead — and she'll share their truest verses only with someone who carries the old songs between the far elders and takes the barrow-warning seriously.
**Location:** Marrowmoot; the Barrow-Fields; the wider network (by letter/token).
**Primary NPCs:** Moot-Mother Senna Crale (giver/anchor), Barrow-Singer Coll Crale (her protégé, secondary), Shire-Parson Father Ode Marrow (the official faith's first failures, secondary), Hedge-Witch "Grandam" Tess (folk-omens, secondary).
**Factions:** Mourners' Circle (old-songs network), Three Thresholds (the parson's slipping rites).
**Surface Problem:** Earn and carry the downs' old songs and barrow-warning.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Senna Crale is the **southern-downs node of the continental old-songs network** — the M6-oblique fragment ("the grey hands took the dead") applied to the pre-Concord barrows (which she half-conflates with the Concord-age "grey hands"). A clean network-link to Combe, Wend, and the other elders. Father Ode's "stuttering" rites are the *official* faith's first downs-failures (M1/M5). Never the mechanism, Court, or keystone.
**Objectives:** 1) Earn Senna's trust. 2) Learn the old songs (M6-oblique). 3) Carry them to/from another elder; note the official faith's first failures.
**Approaches:** Help the barrow-troubles (Q_MD_001); honor the dead by custom; carry a song/token to a far elder; talk with Father Ode about his slipping rites; consult "Grandam" Tess's folk-omens.
**Obstacles:** Senna's caution; the danger of conflating the older fall with "the grey hands"; the shire's complacency.
**Important Scenes:** Senna singing "the grey hands" verse; Father Ode admitting his blessings "stutter" now; the moment the songs rhyme with the player's frontier knowledge.
**Clues:** The old songs' fragment ("the grey hands took the dead" — M6-oblique; the truth survives in song); the official faith's first downs-failures (M1/M5); the older-fall-vs-grey-hands distinction (a fair false-lead the network corrects).
**Combat Options:** None; a lore/social quest.
**Noncombat Options:** Religion, persuasion, lore-carrying, network-weaving — the whole quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat at all; the worst failure is lost trust, not danger.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests in the shire; no hard clock.
**Rewards:** The downs' old songs (a key oblique mystery-fragment); Senna Crale's trust; a node in the continental old-songs network; the downs' deepest lore.
**Consequences:** Carrying the songs advances the player's oblique understanding and the network. Misreading them (older fall vs. grey hands) is a fair, self-correcting false-lead.
**Failure State:** If Senna isn't earned, the songs stay closed — reopen via another elder or by serving the shire; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_SALT_004 (Salt-Mother Tess), the Vale/Reach Mourner-elders, Q_MAJOR_001 (the deep mystery).
**Scaling Notes:** Lore-weight scales with the player's central-mystery progress; no combat scaling.
**State Updates:** KNOWN/HIDDEN_CLUES (M6-oblique, M1/M5 official-failures), RELATIONSHIPS (Senna, Father Ode), FACTION_STATE (Mourner-network).
**Related:** `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/MYSTERY_WEB.md`, `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_001).

# Q_MD_005_THE_ROAD_SOUTH — The Road South

**Quest Type:** Travel / escort / banditry. **Level Range:** 6–9; solo danger: low–moderate. **Secrecy:** player-safe.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Survival DC 15 (the down-roads); Persuasion DC 15 (Horse-Reeve Edony Marrow); Investigation DC 15 (the rustlers); Insight DC 14.
**Starting Hook:** Barrow-robbers and the rustler "Crook" plague the down-roads, the wool-drives are being bled, and Horse-Reeve Edony Marrow — quicker than the slow shire-law — would deputize a capable outsider to clear the roads and (if they wish) ride south.
**Location:** The down-roads; Wether; the road toward the southern regions.
**Primary NPCs:** Horse-Reeve Edony Marrow (giver), Drover-Captain Doss Marrow (the wool-drives), rustler "Crook" (antagonist, minor), Constable-Sergeant Brann Penmark (the slow law, secondary).
**Factions:** Independent (shire-law & drovers).
**Surface Problem:** Clear the down-roads of robbers and rustlers; secure the route south.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The Marrowdowns' route-out quest — establishing the road south (the player's path onward to other southern regions) and Edony Marrow as a quick, recurring road-law ally. The barrow-robbers feeding the rustling tie obliquely to the barrow-trouble. No central-mystery gate.
**Objectives:** 1) Clear the down-roads (robbers, rustlers). 2) Protect the wool-drives. 3) Secure (and travel) the route south.
**Approaches:** Ride patrol with Edony Marrow's troop; catch the rustler "Crook"; escort a wool-drive (Doss Marrow); investigate the barrow-robbers feeding the rustling; deputize and clear the road.
**Obstacles:** The rustlers' speed; the barrow-robbers' cover; the shire-law's slowness; the open down-roads.
**Important Scenes:** A wool-drive ambushed by rustlers; Edony Marrow riding a problem down; the road south opening at last.
**Clues:** The barrow-robbers feeding the road-banditry (an oblique tie to the barrow-trouble); the route south (travel).
**Combat Options:** Rustlers and road-bandits (**Bandit/Scout-like**, scaled L6–9) — avoidable by patrol/ambush; Edony's mounted troop helps.
**Noncombat Options:** Patrol, investigation, escort, deputizing — combat manageable, not forced.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Edony Marrow's mounted troop as backup; Doss Marrow's drovers; multiple approaches; clear retreat on the open downs.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests in down-towns; a drive-schedule soft-clock.
**Rewards:** Edony Marrow's deputization and a quick road-law ally; the drovers' goodwill; the route south open; coin; a horse and road-mastery of the downs.
**Consequences:** Clearing the roads opens the player's way south and a recurring ally. Failing keeps the roads dangerous and the drives bled.
**Failure State:** The roads stay plagued → the wool-trade suffers, the route south is harder; reopen the patrol; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_MD_001/002 (the barrow-robbers); travel south to the Sallowmarch/Hollow Gulf/Sunmark; Q_MD_003 (the wool-trade the roads serve).
**Scaling Notes:** L6 — scattered rustlers. L9 — an organized barrow-robber-and-rustler ring.
**State Updates:** RELATIONSHIPS (Edony Marrow, Doss Marrow), FACTION_STATE (shire-law, rustlers), CONSEQUENCES.
