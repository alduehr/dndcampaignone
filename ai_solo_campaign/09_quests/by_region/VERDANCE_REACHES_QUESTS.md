## AI Use

Seven developed quests for the Verdance Reaches (the river-corridor up the Verdance, the player's first step into the wider continent; Lvl 8–12). Cast from `08_npcs/by_region/VERDANCE_REACHES_NPCS.md`. Death-rite failures are *just beginning to be noticed here* (fresher than the Reach, less denied) — the crisis is spreading up-river. Feeds Q_MAJOR_018 (River of Coin), Q_MAJOR_004 (Ledger scale). Anchors: Marrowfen Stair, Lord's Wend, Cresswater, the Nine Locks.

# Q_VR_001_THE_LOCKS_THAT_WONT_HOLD — The Locks That Won't Hold

**Quest Type:** Investigation / haunted site (oblique) / noble politics. **Level Range:** 8–10; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Investigation DC 16 (the lock-works' wrongness); Persuasion DC 16 (Lord Marrow); Arcana/History DC 17 (Concord works — oblique); Survival DC 15.
**Starting Hook:** Lord Ennis Marrow's ancestral Nine Locks are "going wrong" — barges stall, the works groan — and a ruined barge-season looms. He needs it investigated before his creditors take the locks.
**Location:** Lord's Wend; the Nine Locks (a minor Concord water-node).
**Primary NPCs:** Lord Ennis Marrow (giver), Lockmaster Dob Cress (knows *where* the wrongness centers), Steward Oons Marrow (hidden debt), Sub-Lector Hadwin Vael (Remnant interpretation).
**Factions:** Independent (Marrow), Concord Remnant, Cinder Ledger (creditor).
**Surface Problem:** Fix the failing locks and save the barge-season.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** The Nine Locks are a minor Concord water-node — the "wrongness" is the same waking the player saw at frontier nodes, now reaching up-river (M2/M5 inland). A clean corroboration that the crisis is SPREADING. Marrow dismisses it as decay; never explain the mechanism.
**Objectives:** 1) Find the source of the wrongness. 2) Slow/contain it (rite, repair, or Remnant aid). 3) Decide whom to tell.
**Approaches:** Investigate with Dob Cress; bring in Hadwin Vael (Remnant, gated-honest read); a Mourner rite (Odila Cress); handle Marrow's debt-politics to buy time.
**Obstacles:** The node's waking; Marrow's insolvency (Steward Oons hides it); creditor pressure; the works' danger.
**Important Scenes:** Dob Cress "talking to the locks like beasts"; a lock-chamber where the works "hum"; Hadwin Vael's excited, oblique read.
**Clues:** A minor Concord node waking up-river (M2 inland — the crisis spreading); Dob Cress pinpointing the center (M2); the works as Concord-built (oblique).
**Combat Options:** A node-manifestation in a flooded lock-chamber (scaled L8–10, telegraphed) — settle-able by rite; retreat possible.
**Noncombat Options:** Investigation, rite, repair, debt-politics — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Non-violent containment; Dob Cress and Hadwin assist; retreat from the chamber; the danger is telegraphed.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at Lord's Wend; the barge-season as a soft deadline.
**Rewards:** Marrow's grateful (unreliable) patronage; free passage up-river; the family's old pre-corridor records (Hadwin covets them); an inland M2 data-point.
**Consequences:** Containing it saves Marrow (and proves the spread). Failing ruins his season — and the Ledger/League takes the locks. Telling Hadwin links the Remnant chain.
**Failure State:** The locks fail → Marrow is bought out; the player loses a noble ally but gains a Ledger/League-controlled corridor; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_VR_002 (Marrow's debt); Q_VR_004 (the spreading "bad water"); the Remnant chain.
**Scaling Notes:** L8 — a contained node. L11 — an active manifestation and a corridor-wide barge crisis.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (M2/M5 inland), FACTION_STATE (Ledger/League corridor grip), RELATIONSHIPS (Marrow, Dob Cress, Hadwin), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_018), `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/CLUE_INDEX.md`.

# Q_VR_002_THE_LORDS_DEBT — The Lord's Debt

**Quest Type:** Noble politics / faction leverage / consequence cleanup. **Level Range:** 8–11; solo danger: low. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion/Deception DC 17 (creditors); Investigation DC 16 (the true books); Insight DC 16 (Steward Oons); Persuasion DC 15 (Marrow's guard).
**Starting Hook:** Lord Marrow is being quietly bought out by the Ledger (Wessel Crane) below and the League (Sefwy Holt) above; he begs a champion to break his creditors' grip without taking the locks himself.
**Location:** Lord's Wend; Marrowfen Stair (the creditors' agents).
**Primary NPCs:** Lord Marrow (giver), Steward Oons Marrow (hides the books, negotiates with Holt), Auditor-Agent Vesk Crane (Ledger enforcer), League-Agent Ondine Holt, Hall-Captain Brunn Sael (unpaid, turnable).
**Factions:** Cinder Ledger vs. Glassmere League (both hold Marrow's debt).
**Surface Problem:** Save Marrow's holding from his creditors.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** A clean political vise dramatizing the Ledger–League continental rivalry (Q_MAJOR_004/018) at a human scale. Steward Oons is secretly negotiating with the League behind his lord. No central-mystery content.
**Objectives:** 1) Uncover Marrow's true debt-position. 2) Play the creditors against each other (or find a third way). 3) Keep the locks out of any monopolist's hands.
**Approaches:** Expose Steward Oons's secret League dealings; play Crane against Holt; recover a debt owed *to* Marrow; turn the unpaid Hall-Captain; or broker a deal that costs Marrow his pride but keeps his hall.
**Obstacles:** Marrow's insolvency; Vesk Crane's polite menace; Ondine Holt's opportunism; Oons's betrayal.
**Important Scenes:** Marrow blustering about his ancestors while a clerk whispers debt; Vesk Crane's velvet pressure; catching Oons mid-betrayal.
**Clues:** The Ledger–League rivalry reaching down to a single lord (Q_MAJOR_004/018, scale).
**Combat Options:** None expected; Brunn Sael's household guard if a creditor turns to force — avoidable.
**Noncombat Options:** Politics, exposure, brokering, debt-play — the whole quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; multiple resolutions incl. graceful loss; turnable insiders.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at the hall; a foreclosure deadline.
**Rewards:** Marrow's gratitude and a free door up-river; leverage over both Ledger and League; coin; the Hall-Captain's loyalty if turned.
**Consequences:** Saving Marrow keeps the corridor's chokepoint independent. Letting it fall hands the corridor to a monopolist (Q_MAJOR_018). Exposing Oons fractures the hall.
**Failure State:** Marrow bought out → a vindictive minor enemy and a closed corridor; reopen the corridor via Mossa Drenn; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_VR_001; Q_VR_003 (Crane); Q_MAJOR_004/018.
**Scaling Notes:** L8 — debt-politics. L11 — a Ledger/League proxy-conflict over the locks.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Ledger/League corridor), RELATIONSHIPS (Marrow, Oons, Vesk Crane, Ondine Holt), CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_018).

# Q_VR_003_THE_FACTOR_GENERALS_ERRAND — The Factor-General's Errand

**Quest Type:** Faction (Ledger) / negotiation / moral dilemma. **Level Range:** 9–12; solo danger: low–moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion/Insight DC 17 (Crane); Investigation DC 17 (a League leak); Deception DC 16; Stealth DC 15.
**Starting Hook:** Factor-General Wessel Crane — the Ledger's authority *above* Caradril — hires the player for deniable corridor work: lean on a lord, intercept a League agent, or "assess whether the down-river failures are a market risk worth pulling out over."
**Location:** Marrowfen Stair; the corridor; a League rendezvous.
**Primary NPCs:** Wessel Crane (giver), Toll-Reeve Halsa Wend (building a case Crane is divesting), Auditor-Agent Vesk Crane (ambitious nephew), League-Agent Ondine Holt (target/rival).
**Factions:** Cinder Ledger (Crane), Glassmere League (Holt's bank).
**Surface Problem:** Do Crane's deniable corridor errand.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Crane is quietly DIVESTING the Ledger's deepest down-river relic positions — he smells something wrong in the "failures" and may *abandon the Reach* to save the inland trade. He does NOT know the harvest exists; he reads a bad omen as a bad market. A player who grasps this can warn the frontier or profit from his retreat (Q_MAJOR_004/018).
**Objectives:** 1) Complete (or appear to complete) Crane's errand. 2) Learn what Crane truly intends (the divestment). 3) Decide how to use that knowledge.
**Approaches:** Carry out the errand straight; turn Halsa Wend or Vesk Crane for the divestment intel; warn the frontier; sell the intel to the League/Holt; play both.
**Obstacles:** Crane's surgical patience; the deniability; the League's counter-moves.
**Important Scenes:** Crane pricing the player "exactly"; Halsa Wend hinting at the divestment; a corridor errand with a hidden second purpose.
**Clues:** Crane divesting the frontier (a major strategic clue — the Ledger may abandon the Reach; Q_MAJOR_004); the Ledger's true inland scale.
**Combat Options:** None expected; a League agent's guards if an interception turns hot — avoidable.
**Noncombat Options:** Negotiation, intelligence, betrayal, brokering — the whole quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; multiple uses of the intel; turnable sources; the player can decline the dirtier work.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests at the Stair; a Ledger-quarter deadline.
**Rewards:** Crane's (mercenary) patronage and continental credit; the divestment intel (a strategic lever); coin; a turned insider.
**Consequences:** Serving Crane straight deepens the Ledger's inland grip. Using the divestment intel can warn or profit (a major choice). Crossing Crane freezes the player's credit across the river.
**Failure State:** A botched errand — Crane is coolly displeased but the divestment intel may still leak; reopen via Halsa Wend; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_VR_002; Q_MAJOR_004 (the Ledger abandoning the Reach); Q_GL_002 (Holt).
**Scaling Notes:** L9 — a corridor errand. L12 — a continental Ledger–League intelligence game.
**State Updates:** FACTION_STATE (Ledger divestment-clock, League), KNOWN_CLUES (Crane's divestment), RELATIONSHIPS, CONSEQUENCES.
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_004/018).

# Q_VR_004_THE_BAD_WATER — The Bad Water

**Quest Type:** Investigation / death-rite failure (fresh) / clue trail. **Level Range:** 8–11; solo danger: low–moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Religion DC 16 (failing rites); Insight DC 16 (river-omens); Investigation DC 16 (the spread); Survival DC 15 (the river).
**Starting Hook:** River-Captain Mossa Drenn keeps a private chart of stretches where the rites have failed and the dead "come up wrong" — and she'll only share it with someone who helps her understand the spreading "bad water."
**Location:** The Verdance; Cresswater; the corridor's "bad-water" stretches.
**Primary NPCs:** Mossa Drenn (giver/ally), Grief-Mother Odila Cress (fresh inland M5), Net-Wife Senna Drove (river-omen), Hedge-Mourner Tace Wend (Lord's Wend rites slipping).
**Factions:** Mourners' Circle (inland), independent (bargefolk).
**Surface Problem:** Help the corridor's failing rites and map the spreading "bad water."
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Drenn's chart is the cleanest single artifact proving the crisis is MOVING UP-RIVER (M5 inland). Odila Cress's rites have "begun to slip" — fresh, less-denied than the Reach. The Mourners' oblique fragment is M6-oblique only; no mechanism. The crisis is spreading from the frontier.
**Objectives:** 1) Win Drenn's chart (by helping her understand). 2) Settle a slipping inland rite. 3) Decide who else should see the chart.
**Approaches:** Help Odila Cress's rites; investigate a "bad-water" stretch; correlate the chart with the player's frontier knowledge; warn the corridor or a faction.
**Obstacles:** The fresh, frightening failures; river-danger; the corridor's reluctance to believe.
**Important Scenes:** Drenn's chart of failed stretches; Odila Cress's rite "not taking"; Net-Wife Senna's river-omen ("the fish are leaving the bad water").
**Clues:** Drenn's spread-chart (M5 inland — the crisis moving up-river); fresh inland rite-failures (M5); the Mourners' oblique fragment (M6-oblique).
**Combat Options:** A river-drowned manifestation at a "bad-water" stretch (scaled L8–11, telegraphed) — settle-able by rite; retreat by barge.
**Noncombat Options:** Rite, investigation, correlation — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Non-violent settling; Drenn's barge for retreat; allies (Odila, Drenn); the threat is telegraphed.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Rests aboard Drenn's barge; no hard clock (soft spread-clock).
**Rewards:** Drenn's spread-chart (a key inland mystery artifact); Drenn as a trusted cross-continent ferry; Mourner goodwill; an inland M5 data-point.
**Consequences:** The chart proves the spread (advances the player's understanding and the campaign's stakes). Sharing it with a faction has consequences (the Ledger would profit; the Remnant would investigate). Ignored — the bad water spreads up-river.
**Failure State:** If a rite isn't settled, an inland Mourner-crisis hook opens; Drenn still respects the effort; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_VR_001 (the Nine Locks node); Q_VR_006 (the Remnant); the continental old-songs network (Odila links the elders).
**Scaling Notes:** L8 — one fresh failure. L11 — the bad water spreading corridor-wide.
**State Updates:** KNOWN/HIDDEN_CLUES (M5 inland spread), RELATIONSHIPS (Drenn, Odila), FACTION_STATE (Mourners), INVENTORY (the chart).
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_001/004), `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/CLUE_INDEX.md`.

# Q_VR_005_THE_EEL_OF_THE_VERDANCE — The Eel of the Verdance

**Quest Type:** Escort / outlaw / smuggling. **Level Range:** 8–11; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 16 (turning "Eel" Maddox); Stealth DC 16; Insight DC 15; Survival/Vehicles(water) DC 15.
**Starting Hook:** River-Pirate "Eel" Maddox robs the rich barges and spares the poor — and either menaces a cargo the player guards, or offers a turnable alliance against the Ledger's river-trade.
**Location:** A wild stretch of the Verdance; hidden channels; a barge.
**Primary NPCs:** "Eel" Maddox (outlaw/possible ally), Mossa Drenn (whose barges he spares), Wharf-Boss Garn Drenn (anti-faction), a Ledger cargo-master (target/victim).
**Factions:** Independent (Eel's crew), Cinder Ledger (his prey).
**Surface Problem:** Deal with Eel Maddox — guard against him, or turn him.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** A turnable grey-outlaw quest (the Verdance's Cady Renn-equivalent). The "bad water" wrecks his ambushes (a grey-network witness to the spread). No central-mystery content beyond an oblique "bad water" note.
**Objectives:** 1) Resolve the Eel encounter (fight, pay, or turn). 2) Optionally ally with him against the Ledger. 3) Protect the cargo/poor he claims to defend.
**Approaches:** Guard the cargo (negotiate or fight); turn Eel against a richer Ledger target; pay his toll; expose his hideout to the law (betrayal).
**Obstacles:** His hidden channels; his Robin-Hood loyalty (he spares the poor); the "bad water" disrupting both sides.
**Important Scenes:** Eel's theatrical, non-cruel robbery; a hidden-channel ambush; turning him with a better game.
**Clues:** The "bad water" wrecking his ambushes (M5-oblique grey-network witness).
**Combat Options:** Eel's crew (**Bandit/Scout-like**, AC 13) in a river-ambush — avoidable by parley/payment; he's not gratuitously cruel.
**Noncombat Options:** Negotiation, alliance, payment, betrayal — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Eel prefers toll to murder; turnable; Drenn's barge for retreat; multiple resolutions.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Rests aboard ship; a cargo-deadline if escorting.
**Rewards:** Safe passage; a roguish river-ally (if turned); the outlaw-network's intelligence; coin or a cargo-fee.
**Consequences:** Turning Eel gains a river-ally and hurts the Ledger. Betraying him to the law is profitable but burns a grey-network. Ignored — he keeps robbing the corridor.
**Failure State:** A lost cargo or a failed turn — Eel becomes a recurring nuisance, not an enemy-for-life; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_VR_003 (the Ledger); Q_VR_004 (the bad water); a smuggling network.
**Scaling Notes:** L8 — a toll-ambush. L11 — a running river-battle and a Ledger bounty on Eel.
**State Updates:** RELATIONSHIPS (Eel, Drenn), FACTION_STATE (Ledger river-trade), CONSEQUENCES.

# Q_VR_006_THE_SCHOLARS_RUBBING — The Scholar's Rubbing

**Quest Type:** Faction (Remnant) / escort / clue trail. **Level Range:** 9–12; solo danger: moderate. **Secrecy:** mixed.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** History/Arcana DC 17 (Script); Survival DC 16 (corridor ruins); Persuasion DC 16; Stealth DC 15.
**Starting Hook:** Sub-Lector Hadwin Vael — happier in a ruin than a hall — wants the player to escort him to a corridor Concord ruin, or to bring him a rubbing/relic from a frontier node the player has actually entered.
**Location:** A corridor Concord ruin; Marrowfen Stair; up-river toward Glassmere.
**Primary NPCs:** Hadwin Vael (giver), Copyist Wenna Drove (his scribe), Boatwright Coll Tarr (built a "strange watertight case" once), a rival Ledger salvager.
**Factions:** Concord Remnant, Cinder Ledger (rival salvage).
**Surface Problem:** Help Hadwin study a corridor ruin before the Ledger strips it.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Hadwin has independently deduced the corridor's nodes are a CONNECTED NETWORK "switching on" up-river from the frontier — but reads it as restoration, not harvest (his idealism is a dupe's idealism). A clean conduit toward the Heartlands Remnant chain (Hollin Vane) and a man who could be horrified into an ally. Keep his deduction oblique; never confirm the mechanism.
**Objectives:** 1) Get Hadwin to/from a ruin (or bring him a rubbing). 2) Help him read it (gated, oblique). 3) Decide what to do with his "network" theory.
**Approaches:** Escort him safely; bring him a frontier rubbing; help translate (Script); steer his theory (warn or feed his idealism); race the Ledger salvager.
**Obstacles:** Corridor-ruin dangers; the Ledger racing to strip it; Hadwin's reckless curiosity.
**Important Scenes:** Hadwin's delight at a "live-node" account; a Script-translation that hints at a network (oblique); a Ledger salvager's rivalry.
**Clues:** The nodes as a connected network switching on (M6-oblique — gated; never the mechanism); Concord Script lore; the Ledger stripping ruins (M3).
**Combat Options:** Ruin-hazards or a Ledger salvage-crew (**Guard/Veteran-like**) — avoidable by stealth/negotiation.
**Noncombat Options:** Escort, scholarship, persuasion, racing — combat avoidable.
**Solo Safety Valves:** Hadwin is a noncombatant ally (utility magic); retreat from ruins; the Ledger crew is avoidable; the theory is optional.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Rests at the Stair; a salvage-race deadline.
**Rewards:** Concord Script translation; Hadwin as a scholar-ally with continental contacts; an introduction to the Glassmere/Heartlands Remnant; an M6-oblique network clue.
**Consequences:** Feeding Hadwin's idealism advances the Remnant's reclaim-ambition (dangerous). Disillusioning him (if shown the truth, far later) could turn the inland chapter. The Ledger stripping the ruin is a loss.
**Failure State:** The ruin stripped / Hadwin endangered → he survives, the lead persists; reopen via the Glassmere Remnant; no dead end.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_GL_004 (the Reliquary); Q_MAJOR_011/012 (the Remnant chain to the Crown).
**Scaling Notes:** L9 — a corridor ruin. L12 — a deeper node and a serious Ledger race.
**State Updates:** HIDDEN_CLUES (M6-oblique network), FACTION_STATE (Remnant/Ledger), RELATIONSHIPS (Hadwin), KNOWN_CLUES (Script).
**Related:** `MAJOR_CAMPAIGN_QUESTS.md` (Q_MAJOR_011/012), `../../11_mysteries_and_secrets/MYSTERY_WEB.md`.

# Q_VR_007_THE_FREE_FERRY — The Free Ferry

**Quest Type:** Low-stakes local color / protection / social. **Level Range:** 8–10; solo danger: low. **Secrecy:** player-safe.
**Likely Checks/DCs:** Persuasion DC 14–15; Insight DC 14; Investigation DC 14.
**Starting Hook:** Ferry-Wife Mab Cress runs a small free ferry the poor depend on — and the toll-lords want to tax it out of existence. She (and the corridor's poor) need a champion.
**Location:** Cresswater; a river-crossing; Lord's Wend.
**Primary NPCs:** Ferry-Wife Mab Cress (giver), Lord Marrow / a toll-lord (the pressure), Net-Wife Senna Drove, Caravan-Master Doss Frome (toll-hating ally).
**Factions:** Independent (the corridor poor vs. toll-lords).
**Surface Problem:** Save the free ferry from the toll-lords.
**Hidden Truth (DM):** Humane corridor-texture introducing recurring everyday contacts (Mab Cress, Senna Drove, Doss Frome) and the corridor's daily life and rumor. No central-mystery content.
**Objectives:** 1) Understand the toll-lords' move. 2) Protect or restore the free crossing. 3) Keep Mab Cress safe.
**Approaches:** Negotiate with the toll-lord; rally the corridor's poor and toll-haters (Doss Frome); expose a toll-lord's overreach; pay the tax for her.
**Obstacles:** The toll-lords' legal pressure; the poor's lack of power; a petty enforcer.
**Important Scenes:** Mab Cress carrying the poor "for coppers"; a toll-lord's enforcer at the crossing; a corridor-poor rally.
**Clues:** None central (rich corridor rumor-source via Mab).
**Combat Options:** A toll-lord's 2–3 **Guard-like** enforcers if it turns to force — avoidable.
**Noncombat Options:** Negotiation, rallying, exposure, charity — the whole quest.
**Solo Safety Valves:** No combat gate; multiple resolutions incl. simply paying; allies among the poor.
**Rest/Time Pressure:** Safe rests in Cresswater; a tax-deadline.
**Rewards:** The corridor poor's goodwill; Mab Cress as a corridor rumor-hub; Doss Frome and Senna Drove as recurring contacts; a free crossing.
**Consequences:** Saving the ferry endears the player to the corridor's poor. Failing loses a beloved crossing and a rumor-source.
**Failure State:** The ferry taxed shut → a grievance-thread and a sympathetic-poor reputation; play continues.
**Follow-Up Hooks:** Q_VR_004 (Senna Drove's river-omens); corridor rumor-mongering.
**Scaling Notes:** Low-level throughout; raise the enforcer threat if the player escalates.
**State Updates:** RELATIONSHIPS (Mab Cress, Doss Frome, Senna Drove), FACTION_STATE (toll-lords), NPC_MEMORY.
