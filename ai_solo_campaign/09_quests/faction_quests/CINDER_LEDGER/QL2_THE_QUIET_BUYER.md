## AI Use
The trust-building link. The Ledger entrusts the player with a relic-buying run into the Heights — and the player gets their first real look at what the Ledger is hoarding ("old glass" / raw Remembrance relics). Delivers a direct M3 demonstration (relics "hold" the dead) while keeping the *why* hidden. Reveals capped at R2-R3 (the relics are a substance; the Ledger collects them).

## Quest Type
Faction (Cinder Ledger) — trust-building task

## Level Range
3-5 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate. A thin-touch hazard near "old glass"; toughs over salvage; an optional Remnant rival.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Appraise relics (real vs. worthless): Investigation/Arcana DC 14.
- Negotiate a buy from wary miners: Persuasion DC 14.
- Resist/recover from thin-touch exposure: CON save DC 13 (custom thin-touch mechanic).
- Spot a Remnant agent shadowing the buy: Perception/Insight DC 14.

## Starting Hook
Domic, trusting the player now, hands them a purse and a route: "Harrowgast's miners are pulling up old glass — Concord salvage. Buy all of it, quietly, before the scholars or the Mourners get wind. The Ledger pays for discretion as much as the goods."

## Location
Harrowgast (the mining ruin-town) and the Deep Adit's upper edges where "old glass" is dug.

## Involved NPCs
Domic Sael (the brief), Nessa Coyle (Ledger buyer already in Harrowgast; a rival or partner), Dig-boss Hennet (the miners' foreman), "thin" miners (M10 background), an optional Concord Remnant agent (Veska Dunn's people) racing for the same glass.

## Involved Factions
Cinder Ledger (primary). Concord Remnant (rival buyer). Mourners' Circle (would call this grave-robbery if they learned of it).

## Surface Problem
Buy up the Heights' relic-glass quietly and bring it back — beating rivals and managing wary, half-sick miners.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
"Old glass" is **raw Remembrance relics** that demonstrably store the dead (a direct M3 demonstration the player can witness). The "thin" miners are early evidence of the thinning (M10) from working near a leaking node (the Deep Adit). The Remnant racing the player is the Reclamation clock (#7) made visible. **The harvest's authors stay hidden; the player learns the relics are a substance the Ledger is hoarding (R2-R3).**

## Objectives
1. Appraise and buy the relic-glass.
2. Beat or manage the Remnant rival.
3. Return the goods to Domic (or make a different choice — see consequences).

## Possible Approaches
- **Economic:** out-bid and out-charm the Remnant; secure the glass.
- **Investigative:** examine a relic closely and *witness* it holding a dead voice (M3) — a quiet revelation.
- **Social:** win the miners' trust (they're scared and sick); learn what the glass does to them.
- **Stealth/sabotage:** spoil the Remnant's buy without a fight.

## Obstacles
Thin-touch hazard near concentrated glass; the Remnant rival; the miners' fear; the temptation to keep or sell a relic elsewhere; the dawning unease of what the glass *is*.

## Important Scenes
- A relic in the player's hand, faintly speaking with a dead voice (M3 demonstration).
- A "thin" miner — hollow-eyed, fading — explaining how the deep digs leave men "less."
- A tense bidding standoff with a Remnant scholar who wants the same glass for very different reasons.

## Clues
- **C-M3 (direct):** the glass holds the dead — relics are a storable substance.
- **M10 background:** the "thin" miners (do not over-explain).
- **#7 visible:** the Remnant is racing for the same relics.

## Combat Options
Salvage toughs or a cornered Remnant agent — **Thug/Scout/Spy-like, AC 13, ~22-30 HP**, telegraphed, escapable. Thin-touch is a hazard, not an enemy. See `../../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md` and `../../../10_dungeons_and_ruins/THE_DEEP_ADIT.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Buying, charming, investigating, and out-maneuvering resolve the run. Combat is a fallback.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Thin-touch is reversible and telegraphed; the miners warn of the deep digs; the player can buy a smaller, safe lot rather than risk the worst glass; retreat is open.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Rest at the Cold Drum (Harrowgast). Time pressure: delay lets the Remnant win the glass (#7 advances).

## Rewards
- Major Ledger trust (gate to QL3 and the Caradril/vault thread).
- A direct M3 revelation (the relics hold the dead).
- Coin and a cut; optionally, a single kept relic (with thin-touch risk and Mourner stigma).
- Domic's growing reliance on the player.

## Consequences
- **Deliver to Ledger:** the Ledger's hoard grows (#6 advances); Domic trusts the player with the vault next (QL3).
- **Witness M3 and keep a relic:** the player gains a clue and a moral weight; the Ledger doesn't mind.
- **Sell elsewhere / sabotage Ledger:** Ledger standing dips; the chain can pivot to opposition.
- **Let the Remnant win:** #7 advances; the Ledger is annoyed; the player loses a buy.
- **Excessive force / a miner harmed:** Harrowgast turns wary; a watch/Mourner problem.

## Failure State
A failed buy means the Remnant or another rival takes the glass (#7 advances) and the Ledger is mildly disappointed — not a block; QL3 still opens through Domic.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QL3 What the Vault Remembers; the Remnant chain (the rival agent remembers the player); the Deep Adit thread (#7); the kept relic as a recurring clue.

## Scaling Notes
- **L3:** light rivalry; one toughs-knot; soften thin-touch DC by 1.
- **L5:** an organized Remnant crew; heavier thin-touch concentration; raise DCs by 1.
- Cap reveals at R2-R3; never name the harvest's authors.
