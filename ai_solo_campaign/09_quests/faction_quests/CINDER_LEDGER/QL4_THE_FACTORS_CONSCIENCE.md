## AI Use
The internal-conflict + decision-point capstone. The Ledger's split (Domic Sael's conscience vs. Calla Vyre's monopoly ambition) breaks open around the Vyre-Quorrin deal. The player tips it: help Vyre close the monopoly, break the deal by exposing it, or hijack the Ledger by backing Domic against her. Sets the Ledger's posture for the "sell it / seize it" endgames and the Monopoly (#6) and Deal (C1) clocks. Reveals capped at R4 (the harvest is real and being commodified; the Court still unnamed).

## Quest Type
Faction (Cinder Ledger) — internal conflict + decision point

## Level Range
7-11 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate (mostly social/intrigue; one optional intercept). The danger is leverage and choice, not damage.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Turn Domic into an open witness against Vyre: Persuasion/Insight DC 16.
- Expose the deal to the right audience (council, idealists, rivals): Persuasion/Deception DC 16.
- Out-negotiate Vyre directly: Persuasion DC 18 (very hard; she is the best in the region).
- Detect/counter Vyre's countermove (debt-call, blacklist, framing): Insight/Investigation DC 16.

## Starting Hook
The deal is nearly closed. Domic Sael — who has watched the Ledger become something he can't stomach — comes to the player privately: "She's going to sell the dead. I have the books that prove it. I can't stop her alone, and I'm a coward. But you... you could." Meanwhile Vyre, sensing the player's weight, makes her own offer: a place at the top of the new order, if the player helps close the monopoly.

## Location
The Hollowmere counting-house and Caradril (the Ledger Keep, the Counting-Quays; the deal lives in the city — C1).

## Involved NPCs
Factor Domic Sael (the conscience; the witness), Mother Calla Vyre (the ambition; the matriarch), Tallytooth Ren (turnable muscle), Underfactor Coll Riis (turnable Caradril clerk with proof), Provost Anneth Vell (Vyre's loyal deputy), optionally Archivist Quorrin Vane (the other half of the deal).

## Involved Factions
Cinder Ledger (the split is internal). Concord Remnant (the deal's other party — exposing it hits both). Whoever the player brings proof to (Compact, Wardens, Mourners, Tidewater Council).

## Surface Problem
The Ledger is about to seal a deal to commodify the dead. Domic wants to stop it; Vyre wants to close it; the player decides.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The deal is the **Vyre-Quorrin Remembrance monopoly** (C1; feeds #6 and #7). Domic's books are real proof (a C-M3/M6 corroboration; the dead as commodity). Vyre genuinely doesn't grasp the full cosmic horror — she sees the deal of the millennium; that makes her *recruitable* later if the player can show her the true cost. **The Court is never named; cap at R4.**

## Objectives
1. Choose a side in the Ledger's split (Vyre's ambition or Domic's conscience) — or a third path.
2. Resolve the deal: close it, break it, or hijack it.
3. Manage the fallout (Vyre's economic war, or the Ledger's new direction under Domic).

## Possible Approaches
- **Back Vyre (close the monopoly):** the deal seals; #6/C1 advance toward the "sell it" endgame; the player gains wealth, Ledger power, and complicity. Domic breaks (quits, flees, or is silenced — telegraphed, never cheap-killed on-screen by the player's hand).
- **Back Domic (break/hijack the Ledger):** expose the deal with his books to the right audience; #6/C1 setback; Vyre is weakened or deposed; Domic (or the player) reshapes the Ledger toward honest trade. Vyre becomes an enemy or, if shown the true cost, a shocked potential ally.
- **Expose to a rival faction:** hand the proof to the Mourners/Wardens/Compact/Council — shatters the deal publicly and aligns the player with the "seal it" or anti-monopoly camp.
- **Play both / hijack:** turn Coll Riis and Ren, gut the deal, and seize the Ledger's relic pipeline for the player's own endgame aims (the "seize it" path).

## Obstacles
Vyre's brilliance and resources; Domic's cowardice (he needs courage); Provost Vell's loyalty; the deal's momentum; Vyre's countermeasures (debt-calls, blacklisting, framing) if crossed.

## Important Scenes
- Domic, books in hand, terrified and resolved, asking the player to be braver than he is.
- Vyre's velvet offer: a seat at the top of a world where money buys immortality.
- The deal-table (Caradril): Vyre and Quorrin, each smiling, each planning to betray the other — and the player able to detonate it.
- The aftermath: a Ledger remade, broken, or triumphant.

## Clues
- **C-M3/M6 corroboration:** Domic's books prove the dead are being commodified (no Court name).
- **C1/#6/#7:** the full shape of the monopoly deal confirmed.

## Combat Options
Optional and avoidable: if the player exposes the deal, Vyre may send deniable pressure — **Spy/Thug/Veteran-like, AC 13-16, ~30-45 HP** — to recover the books, telegraphed and escapable. Vyre herself never fights (Tier 2 noncombat). See `../../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/CARADRIL_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
This is an intrigue capstone — resolvable entirely through persuasion, evidence, and turning insiders.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
No forced combat; Vyre's reprisals are economic/social/legal first (never assassination as a default); Domic, Riis, and Ren are turnable allies; the player can walk away from the deal at the cost of Ledger standing.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest throughout. Time pressure: the deal (C1) closes if the player dithers — back Domic *before* it seals to break it cleanly; after, it must be unwound the hard way.

## Rewards
- **The Ledger's posture** for the endgame (monopoly-closed / broken / hijacked).
- Wealth and Ledger power (if Vyre), or an honest Ledger ally and the deal's proof (if Domic), or the relic pipeline (if hijacked).
- A standing relationship with Vyre (ally, enemy, or shocked potential convert) for Act 4-5.
- Domic Sael resolved (a free man, a martyr, or a new Ledger head).

## Consequences
- **Back Vyre:** the monopoly closes (#6/C1 to Stage 3-4); the "sell it" endgame strengthens; the player is rich and complicit; the dead become inventory unless undone later.
- **Back Domic / expose:** the deal breaks (#6/C1 setback); Vyre wars on the player economically; the anti-monopoly/"seal it" camp gains a powerful proof.
- **Hijack:** the player controls the Ledger's relic supply — strong for a "seize it" endgame and a double-edged power.
- **Ignore:** the deal closes by default (#6/C1 advance) and Domic breaks alone — a quiet tragedy and a stronger "sell it" default.
- **Excessive force:** open violence against the Ledger brings the Tide-Watch and Vyre's full war.

## Failure State
If the player botches the exposure or backs the wrong insider, Vyre closes the deal and neutralizes the witnesses (Domic flees or is ruined) — the monopoly stands, and the player has made a dangerous enemy. Recoverable in the endgame, but the "sell it" outcome is now the strong default. Not a campaign block.

## Follow-Up Hooks
Toward Act 4-5: Vyre as an endgame claimant or convert; the deal's proof as a power-card; `Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE.md` (the Remnant half); Domic's new Ledger (if hijacked/reformed) as an ally bloc.

## Scaling Notes
- **L7-8:** the deal is forming; breaking it is cleaner.
- **L9-11:** the deal is nearly sealed; unwinding it is harder and Vyre's reprisals fiercer.
- Cap reveals at R4; the Court stays unnamed.
