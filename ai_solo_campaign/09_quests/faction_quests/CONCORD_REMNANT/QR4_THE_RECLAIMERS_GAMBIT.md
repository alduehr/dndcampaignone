## AI Use
The internal-conflict + decision-point capstone. The Remnant's split (Briss's idealists vs. Quorrin's Reclaimers) breaks open as the Reclaimers move to seize the harvest. The player tips it: expose Quorrin to the rank-and-file and shatter the Reclaimers, help them seize the harvest (the "seize it" path), or turn the Remnant against its rival the Hollow Court. Sets the Remnant's endgame posture and drives the Reclamation (#7) and Deal (C1) clocks. This is the latest-tier faction chain and may brush the threshold of the Court reveal — but the **Court is still not confirmed here**; that is the main-arc Act 4 beat. Reveals capped at R5-R6.

## Quest Type
Faction (Concord Remnant) — internal conflict + decision point

## Level Range
10-13 (scales). **Solo danger:** high (Quorrin is a boss-class rival; Reclaimer agents; the Archive's wards). The danger is real but always telegraphed and resolvable by guile or alliance.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Expose Quorrin to the idealist rank-and-file convincingly: Persuasion DC 18 (needs QR3 proof).
- Outmaneuver Quorrin's countermove: Insight/Investigation DC 17.
- Deny the Reclaimers the relics/knowledge they need: varies; DC 16-18.
- Turn Tamsin Orr / disillusion the rank-and-file: Persuasion/Insight DC 16.

## Starting Hook
The Reclaimers move. With the Ledger's backing (the Vyre-Quorrin deal) and the shrine's location deciphered, Quorrin Vane prepares to send agents to seize the harvest's keystone and crown the Reclaimers as the new Custodians. Briss and the idealists still don't know. The player holds the proof from the Archive — and the choice of what the Remnant becomes.

## Location
Caradril (the Lantern Reach; the Reclaimers' mobilization) and the road toward the Reach (the agents' march toward the shrine).

## Involved NPCs
Archivist Quorrin Vane (the Reclaimer leader; boss-class rival), Lector Briss (the idealists' soul; the lever), Sub-Lector Tamsin Orr (turnable idealist), Keeper-Archivist Wessel Dree (Reclaimer enforcer), Veska Dunn (Reach field-agent leading the keystone push).

## Involved Factions
Concord Remnant (the schism). Cinder Ledger (the deal — C1; ties to QL4). The Hollow Court (DM-only: the Reclaimers are racing the Court to the same keystone — a rivalry the player can exploit without yet knowing the Court's full nature).

## Surface Problem
The Reclaimers move to seize the harvest. The player decides whether to shatter them, join them, or aim them at their hidden rival.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The Reclaimers intend to seize the drowned shrine's keystone and restart the harvest under a new Concord (the "seize it" ending — a living tyranny on the dead). They are **racing the Hollow Court**, whom Quorrin only suspects exists. The player can: shatter the Reclaimers (idealist exposure), help them win (seize it), or **turn the Remnant against the Court** — but doing the last requires the player to know the Court exists, which is the main-arc Act 4 reveal, NOT something this quest grants. **If the player has not yet reached the Court reveal, the "turn them on the Court" option appears only as "aim them at whatever truly controls the shrine" — unnamed.** Cap at R5-R6; the Court is never confirmed by this quest.

## Objectives
1. Choose the Remnant's fate: shatter the Reclaimers, join them, or redirect them.
2. Resolve the idealist-vs-Reclaimer schism (expose Quorrin or protect his secret).
3. Affect the race for the keystone (#7) and the Deal (C1).

## Possible Approaches
- **Shatter the Reclaimers (expose Quorrin):** bring the Archive proof to Briss and the rank-and-file — the faction fractures; the idealists may reform the Remnant honestly; #7 set back. The strongest anti-"seize it" outcome.
- **Join the Reclaimers (seize it):** help Quorrin secure the keystone — the "seize it" endgame trajectory; power and complicity; #7/C1 advance sharply.
- **Redirect them:** aim the Reclaimers at "whatever truly controls the shrine" (their hidden rival) — a wedge that sets the Remnant against the apex power (named only once the main arc reveals it).
- **Quiet sabotage:** deny relics, sabotage the agents' march, slow #7 without open war.

## Obstacles
Quorrin's brilliance, resources, and boss-class capability; the Reclaimers' head start; the Ledger's backing; the danger of moving against the inner circle openly; protecting Briss and the idealists from Quorrin's reprisal.

## Important Scenes
- The Reclaimers mobilizing — agents, relics, and a deciphered map to the keystone.
- The confrontation with Quorrin: certain, eloquent, offering the player the Concord restored — or warning them off.
- Briss learning the truth (if exposed): the idealists' anguish, then their resolve.
- The race toward the shrine, the Remnant now a rival the player has shaped.

## Combat Options
Quorrin Vane — **Mage/Archmage-like Tier 1 boss, AC 12-15, ~60-110 HP** (scaled to tier), *Counterspell, Wall of Force, Sending, Scrying*, Remembrance-relic one-offs; a late-game rival who prefers to win by knowledge and never a cheap-death ambush. Reclaimer agents and Dree (**Spy/Veteran-like**). Telegraphed; the quest is winnable by exposure, alliance, and sabotage without defeating Quorrin in battle. See `../../../07_factions/major_factions/CONCORD_REMNANT.md`.

## Noncombat Options
The capstone is fully resolvable through exposure, persuasion, alliance, and sabotage — Quorrin is beaten more surely by splitting his faction than by combat.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Quorrin prefers debate and recruitment to murder; Briss, Tamsin, and the idealists are powerful allies if turned; the Reclaimers' reprisals are deniable and escalating, not instant-lethal; the player can shatter the faction without ever fighting Quorrin directly; losing here is a setback (the Reclaimers gain ground), not a campaign block.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Rest before the confrontation. Sharp time pressure: the Reclaimers march for the keystone — the player must act with the proof and allies they've gathered; delay lets #7/C1 advance toward the "seize it" outcome.

## Rewards
- **The Remnant's endgame posture** (shattered/reformed idealist ally / Reclaimer "seize it" force / a rival aimed at the apex power).
- A decisive lever on the Reclamation (#7) and Deal (C1) clocks.
- Briss and the idealists as allies (if exposed); or Reclaimer power (if joined).
- The deep-history apex fully in the player's hands (R5-R6) for the endgame choice.

## Consequences
- **Shatter/expose:** #7 set back; the Reclaimers fracture; the idealists may reform the Remnant; the "seize it" threat recedes; a strong anti-tyranny outcome.
- **Join:** #7/C1 advance; the "seize it" endgame strengthens; the player is complicit in a new Concord on the dead.
- **Redirect:** the Remnant becomes a wedge against the apex power (powerful, double-edged; named only at the main-arc reveal).
- **Quiet sabotage:** #7 slowed; the Reclaimers suspicious; the player keeps options open.
- **Ignore:** the Reclaimers reach the keystone as a third racer (#7 to Stage 3-4); the "seize it" outcome becomes a strong endgame default.
- **Excessive force:** open war with the Remnant in Caradril brings the Tide-Watch and Quorrin's full power.

## Failure State
If the player loses the gambit (the Reclaimers reach the keystone, or Quorrin neutralizes the player), the "seize it" trajectory dominates the endgame and the Remnant is a powerful enemy — a major setback, recoverable through the other factions and the endgame choice, not a campaign-ending block.

## Follow-Up Hooks
Toward Act 4-5: the Remnant as a "seize it" claimant or a reformed idealist ally; the race for the keystone; the main-arc Court reveal (which recontextualizes the Reclaimers as the Court's rivals); the deep-history apex as the player's endgame argument.

## Scaling Notes
- **L10:** the Reclaimers are mobilizing; exposing Quorrin is hard but doable with QR3 proof.
- **L13:** the Reclaimers are nearly at the keystone; Quorrin is at full power; stopping them needs every ally and proof.
- Cap at R5-R6; never confirm the Court (that is the main arc's job).
