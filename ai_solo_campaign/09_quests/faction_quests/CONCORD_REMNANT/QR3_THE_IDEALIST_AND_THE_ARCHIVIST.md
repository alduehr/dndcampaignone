## AI Use
The moral-complication link. The player reaches the Sealed Archive's truths and discovers the inner circle's real aim — the Reclaimers want to seize and restart the harvest to rebuild the Concord's empire. The player must decide what to do with that knowledge: keep serving the Remnant (knowingly), warn the idealists, or quietly oppose the inner circle. Delivers C-M6/M9 (the harvest and the deliberate Quietfall, written and confirmed) and the Reclaimers' secret. Reveals capped at R5 (the harvest is confirmed; the Hollow Court is still never named — the Reclaimers are *rivals* who don't fully know the Court either).

## Quest Type
Faction (Concord Remnant) — moral complication

## Level Range
8-11 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate-to-high. The Archive's wards, Reclaimer agents protecting their secret, city intrigue — telegraphed; heavy noncombat options.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Read the Sealed Archive's deepest texts: Intelligence (Concord Script) DC 17.
- Confirm the Reclaimers' true aim: Investigation/Insight DC 17.
- Get the truth past Wessel Dree (or around him): Deception/Stealth DC 16.
- Decide whether to tell Briss (and survive the inner circle's reaction): Persuasion/Insight DC 16.

## Starting Hook
The player finally reaches what the Lantern Reach truly guards: the Sealed Archive's account of the Concord — and a set of inner-circle papers that don't belong to scholars. They reveal that Archivist Quorrin Vane and his "Reclaimers" know the Concord harvested the dead, know the Quietfall was deliberate, and intend to **do it all again** — with themselves as the new Custodians. Briss, the kindest man the player knows, has no idea his life's work serves this.

## Location
Caradril — the Sealed Archive within the Lantern Reach.

## Involved NPCs
Archivist Quorrin Vane (inner-circle leader; the secret embodied), Lector Briss (the idealist who must not be allowed to find out wrong, or who must be told), Keeper-Archivist Wessel Dree (Reclaimer gatekeeper; now an active obstacle), Sub-Lector Tamsin Orr (idealist; a potential ally if disillusioned).

## Involved Factions
Concord Remnant (the split is internal). Cinder Ledger (the Vyre-Quorrin deal — C1; the Reclaimers' funding). The Hollow Court (DM-only: the Reclaimers are the Court's *rivals* for the same prize; neither yet fully knows the other).

## Surface Problem
The player learns the Remnant's inner circle wants to seize and restart the harvest. What do they do with the most dangerous knowledge in Caradril?

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The Sealed Archive confirms **the Concord harvested the dead and the Quietfall was deliberate** (C-M6/M9; R5 — the deep-history apex, *written and certain*). The Reclaimers (Quorrin) intend to seize and restart the harvest to rebuild the Concord with themselves as Custodians. **Crucially: the Reclaimers are RIVALS of the Hollow Court, not its agents, and they do NOT yet fully know the Court survives** — Quorrin only *suspects*. The player must never be told here that the Court exists; they learn the Concord's crime and the Reclaimers' ambition, not the apex power. Cap at R5; the Court stays unnamed.

## Objectives
1. Reach and read the Sealed Archive's deepest truth (C-M6/M9).
2. Uncover the Reclaimers' true aim (seize/restart the harvest).
3. Decide what to do with the knowledge (serve, warn Briss, oppose, or expose).

## Possible Approaches
- **Knowing service:** keep working for the Remnant, now aware of the Reclaimers' aim (a grey/dark path; the "seize it" trajectory).
- **Warn the idealists:** bring the truth to Briss and Tamsin Orr — the lever that can shatter the Reclaimers (seeds QR4).
- **Quiet opposition:** copy the proof, deny the Reclaimers relics/knowledge, slow #7 without open conflict.
- **Stealth/scholarship:** extract the C-M6/M9 truth for the player's own ends (the deep history is reachable here for *any* aligned player).

## Obstacles
The Archive's wards; Dree's active obstruction; Quorrin's evaluating menace; the agony of Briss's innocence; the temptation of the Reclaimers' offer (knowledge and power); the danger of crossing the inner circle in their own house.

## Important Scenes
- The Sealed Archive: the Concord's crime written plainly, in the player's own newly-learned script.
- The Reclaimer papers: Quorrin's design to become the new Custodians.
- Quorrin himself, calm and certain, offering the player a place in the restored Concord — or a warning.
- Briss, if told: the kindest man's world breaking.

## Clues
- **C-M6/M9 (confirmed, R5):** the Concord harvested the dead; the Quietfall was deliberate — written and certain.
- **The Reclaimers' aim:** seize/restart the harvest to rebuild the Concord (the chain's secret payload).

## Combat Options
Optional and avoidable: Reclaimer agents or Dree protecting the secret (**Spy/Veteran/Mage-lite-like, AC 13-16, ~30-60 HP**); Quorrin himself is a late-game rival who prefers to win by knowledge (Tier 1 boss-class — never a cheap-death ambush here; he would rather recruit or warn). The Archive's wards are a hazard. Telegraphed; winnable by stealth and scholarship. See `../../../07_factions/major_factions/CONCORD_REMNANT.md` (Quorrin's mechanics) and `Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE.md`.

## Noncombat Options
The truth can be reached, and the choice made, entirely through scholarship, stealth, and persuasion.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The Archive's wards and Quorrin's menace are telegraphed; Quorrin prefers recruitment to murder; Briss and Tamsin Orr are allies; the player can extract the proof and withdraw; crossing the Reclaimers brings escalating, deniable pressure first, not assassination.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Caradril (warily). Time pressure: the Vyre-Quorrin deal (C1) and the Reclamation clock (#7) advance; reaching the Archive's truth before the deal closes matters.

## Rewards
- **The deep-history apex (C-M6/M9, R5)** — the Concord's crime and the deliberate Quietfall, confirmed in writing.
- The Reclaimers' secret (a power-card for QR4).
- Briss and Tamsin Orr as potential disillusioned allies; a foothold to shatter the inner circle.
- (If served) the Reclaimers' trust and the "seize it" path.

## Consequences
- **Warn the idealists:** sets up QR4's faction-shattering; the Remnant's rank-and-file may turn on Quorrin.
- **Knowing service:** the player advances the Reclaimers' aim (#7/C1); the "seize it" endgame strengthens; a dark complicity.
- **Quiet opposition:** #7 slowed; the Reclaimers grow suspicious; the player keeps the proof.
- **Ignore:** the Reclaimers advance unopposed (#7/C1); the player still gained the deep history (it's reachable here regardless).
- **Excessive force:** open violence in the Archive brings the Tide-Watch and Quorrin's full enmity.

## Failure State
If the player is caught and the proof seized, the Reclaimers tighten the Archive and discredit the player academically — the deep history remains reachable (the Reach, the Mourner songs, a second Archive attempt), so it is not a block. The Reclaimers are simply forewarned for QR4.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QR4 The Reclaimers' Gambit; `Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE.md`; the Ledger chain (the Vyre-Quorrin deal; C1); Briss/Tamsin Orr as disillusioned levers.

## Scaling Notes
- **L8:** lighter wards, fewer agents; Quorrin distant.
- **L11:** full Archive security, active Reclaimer agents, Quorrin personally attentive.
- Cap at R5; the Court is never named (the Reclaimers don't fully know it either).
