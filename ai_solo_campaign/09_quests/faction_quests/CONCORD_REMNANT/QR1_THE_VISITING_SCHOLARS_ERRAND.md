## AI Use
First link of the Remnant chain. The sincere Lector Briss recruits the player for honest fieldwork at a Concord ruin and, as a reward, begins teaching Concord Script — the campaign's key clue-gate. Introduces the Remnant as harmless-seeming idealists. No reveal of the inner circle; the player simply gains a scholarly ally and a vital skill.

## Quest Type
Faction (Concord Remnant) — intro hook

## Level Range
3-5 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate. A Concord ruin's hazards, an optional rival or Remembrance — telegraphed; exploration/investigation-primary.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Copy or rubbing a Concord inscription accurately: Investigation/Dexterity DC 13.
- Begin learning Concord Script (Briss teaches): Intelligence DC 13 (cumulative; not one-roll).
- Navigate a ruin's hazards: Investigation/Survival DC 14.
- Tell Briss's sincerity from politeness: Insight DC 13.

## Starting Hook
Lector Briss — bright-eyed, over-eager, genuinely kind — needs a capable hand: "I'm no adventurer, and the ruin past the moor has an inscription that could rewrite what we know of the Concord. Help me reach it and copy it, and I'll teach you to read the old script yourself. Few living can. It's a gift, truly."

## Location
A Concord ruin in the Reach (e.g., the Whispering Cairn or the Roofless Hall) and Hollowmere (Briss's lodgings/lessons).

## Involved NPCs
Lector Briss (sincere idealist; tutor; clue source), Sub-Lector Tamsin Orr (his student; Script-tutor; appears later in Caradril), an optional rival relic-hunter (Ledger or independent), the ruin's restless dead.

## Involved Factions
Concord Remnant (primary). Cinder Ledger (a rival relic-hunter may compete). Ashen Wardens / Mourners' Circle (uneasy that scholars are poking the dead's ruins).

## Surface Problem
Help a scholar reach and record a Concord inscription — and earn the gift of reading the old script.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
Briss is exactly the sincere idealist he seems — he knows *nothing* of the Reclaimers' true aim and is a long-term clue-source and sympathetic dupe. The inscription is genuine Concord history (a seed toward M6/M9, oblique). Learning Concord Script here is the **key clue-gate** for the deep-history mysteries. **No inner-circle reveal; the Remnant looks harmless. Cap at R2.**

## Objectives
1. Help Briss reach and accurately copy the inscription.
2. Begin learning Concord Script.
3. Manage the ruin's hazards and any rival.

## Possible Approaches
- **Exploration:** navigate the ruin; protect Briss (a non-combatant).
- **Investigation:** copy the inscription precisely; begin translating it.
- **Social:** out-talk a rival relic-hunter for access.
- **Protective:** lay or avoid the ruin's restless dead (fetch a Warden if needed).

## Obstacles
The ruin's hazards (traps, unstable stone, restless dead); Briss's helplessness in danger; a rival relic-hunter; the slow work of learning a dead language.

## Important Scenes
- Briss's delighted, tumbling enthusiasm at the inscription.
- The first Concord Script lesson — the player beginning to read the old world.
- A rival's appearance, or a Remembrance stirring among the carvings.

## Clues
- **Concord Script (the gate):** the player begins to read it — unlocking deep-history clues across the campaign.
- M6/M9 *seed:* the inscription hints at the Concord's true scale (oblique; no harvest-naming yet).

## Combat Options
Optional: a rival relic-hunter (**Scout/Thug-like, AC 13, ~22 HP**) or a ruin Remembrance (**specter-like, AC 12, ~30 HP**). Telegraphed; Briss cannot fight (Tier 2 noncombat). See `../../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md` and `../../../10_dungeons_and_ruins/THE_WHISPERING_CAIRN.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Exploration, investigation, and persuasion resolve the errand. Combat is a ruin/rival fallback.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The ruin's hazards are telegraphed; Briss is a liability to protect, not a fighter; the player can fetch a Warden for the dead; rivals can be out-talked; retreat is open.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Rest in Hollowmere; Script lessons take downtime. Light time pressure: a rival may reach the inscription first if the player delays.

## Rewards
- **Concord Script** (the key clue-gate — begins here, deepens across the chain).
- Lector Briss as a sincere scholarly ally (and a future lever against the inner circle).
- An M6/M9 seed; access to the Remnant's fieldwork.

## Consequences
- **Help Briss:** the player gains Script and a devoted ally; the Remnant courts them (QR2 opens).
- **Out-compete a rival:** Briss is grateful; the inscription is secured.
- **Ignore:** the player misses the Script-gate for now (recoverable via Tamsin Orr in Caradril).
- **Excessive force / disturb the dead:** the Wardens and Mourners disapprove; Briss is shaken.

## Failure State
If the inscription is lost (rival, ruin collapse) or Briss is alienated, the Script-gate stays closed for now — reachable later via Tamsin Orr or the Lantern Reach. Not a block.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QR2 The Reader of Ruins; the Caradril Lantern Reach (Briss's seat; Tamsin Orr); Concord Script as a recurring key.

## Scaling Notes
- **L3:** a simple ruin; a gentle first lesson.
- **L5:** a hazardous ruin, a rival crew, a tougher Remembrance.
