## AI Use
First link of the Mourners' chain. The player proves they respect the dead by helping with a hard, unglamorous funeral — earning the Circle's first warmth (the gate to everything else they offer). No deep reveal; pure trust-test and moral texture.

## Quest Type
Faction (Mourners' Circle) — intro hook

## Level Range
1-3 (scales). **Solo danger:** low. Social/labor-primary; an optional single Remembrance.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Help wash, dress, and sing a passing properly: Religion/Medicine DC 12 (the Circle teaches; respect matters more than the roll).
- Comfort a grieving relative: Persuasion/Insight DC 12.
- Recover a body lost in the fen-edge: Survival DC 13.
- Notice the Circle testing you: Insight DC 13.

## Starting Hook
A pauper's death no one will tend — a drowned fen-cutter with no kin and no coin. Grandmother Wend, watching from the Mourner's Green, says only: "Everyone deserves washing. Will you help, or are you like the rest?" It is a test, and she does not hide it.

## Location
Hollowmere's Mourner's Green and the fen-edge where the body lies.

## Involved NPCs
Grandmother Wend (the eldest; watching), Iola Wend (her apprentice; helps and assesses), Bann Oester (grave-digger; gruff, protective), the dead fen-cutter (a possible loose Remembrance).

## Involved Factions
Mourners' Circle (primary). The Ashen Wardens may pass through (allies).

## Surface Problem
An unwanted dead needs proper rites. Help do the work no one else will — and prove your character to the Circle.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
This is the Circle's standard trust-test for outsiders; passing it opens the chain (and eventually the old songs). The fen-cutter drowned near a leaking node (faint M5 backdrop). Wend is quietly deciding whether the player is someone she could ever trust with the Circle's deeper inheritance.

## Objectives
1. Recover the body (if lost in the fen).
2. Help wash, dress, and sing the passing the Circle's way.
3. Treat the dead and the grieving with respect.

## Possible Approaches
- **Labor/respect:** simply do the hard, humble work well (the intended path).
- **Ritual:** learn and join the Circle's washing-and-singing.
- **Social:** comfort any mourners; show genuine care.
- **Protective:** lay the cutter gently if he stirs as a Remembrance.

## Obstacles
The grim, unpleasant work; the fen hazard; Bann's suspicion of strangers; an optional loose Remembrance; the temptation to do it carelessly or for show.

## Important Scenes
- Wend's flat, testing question on the Green.
- The washing: cold water, old words, the Circle watching how the player's hands move.
- Wend's verdict — a single nod, or a closed door.

## Clues
- Faint M5 backdrop (drowned near a node) — flavor only.
- The Circle's character test (the real "clue" is social — passing opens QM2).

## Combat Options
Optional single Remembrance — **specter-like, AC 12, ~17 HP**, sorrowful, layable by the Circle's rite. Telegraphed; combat is a last resort. See `../../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/ACT_1_THREATS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
The whole quest is respect, labor, and ritual. Combat is avoidable.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
No real threat; the Circle is present; fetching a Warden is allowed; the dead is sorrowful, not hostile.

## Rest And Time Pressure
No pressure; safe rest in Hollowmere. The dead can wait a day, but not many.

## Rewards
- The Circle's first warmth (the gate to QM2 and all their lore/aid).
- A rural/Reach-wide network of grieving families who'll shelter the player.
- Wend's willingness to be approached again.

## Consequences
- **Respectful:** Wend opens the door; Bann thaws; the chain proceeds.
- **Careless/for-show:** Wend closes the door; the chain stalls until the player proves themselves elsewhere (a redirect, not a block).
- **Ignore:** the cutter is laid by an exhausted Circle; the player simply isn't trusted yet.
- **Excessive force / disrespect:** the Circle turns cold Reach-wide; recovering trust is slow.

## Failure State
Failing the test isn't a block — it closes the Mourners' chain for now and can be reopened by later acts of respect (honoring the dead elsewhere, helping at a funeral). The Circle remembers both slights and kindnesses.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QM2 The Old Songs; the Ashen Wardens chain (the Wardens love a respectful helper); Iola's hidden Gravecaller bridge (seeded for later).

## Scaling Notes
- **L1:** pure social/labor; no combat.
- **L3:** add the fen-recovery and an optional Remembrance for a fuller scene.
