## AI Use
The trust-building link. Wend begins to teach the player the old songs — the Circle's folk-memory that encodes the Concord's harvest. The player must earn each verse by deed, not coin. Delivers a major clue path (M5/M6 moral truth, **oblique** — the songs say the Concord "ate the dead" but don't name the Court). Reveals capped at R3 in Act 1-2.

## Quest Type
Faction (Mourners' Circle) — trust-building task

## Level Range
3-5 (scales). **Solo danger:** low-to-moderate. Mostly social; an optional fen-recovery or a confrontation with a relic-buyer.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Earn a verse by deed (help a family, recover a lost dead, defend a grave): roleplay-gated, no single roll.
- Memorize/interpret a song's meaning: History/Religion/Insight DC 14.
- Recognize an old song matches a Concord ruin's carvings (M5/M6 bridge): Investigation DC 15.
- Win a wary elder's trust in another town: Persuasion/Insight DC 14.

## Starting Hook
Wend, having judged the player worthy, makes an offer no coin could buy: "There are songs we sing only over the deepest dead. They carry what the world forgot. I'll teach you — verse by verse, if you keep earning them. Some of what they say will frighten you." The first verse is given freely; the rest must be earned.

## Location
Hollowmere's Green and graves; other Circle towns (Greywater Holm, Candlewick) where elders hold further verses; a Concord ruin whose carvings echo a song.

## Involved NPCs
Grandmother Wend (the teacher), Iola Wend (helps, conflicted), Elder Mabb Tay (Greywater; holds a verse), Mother Ezrith Combe (Vale Mourner elder; another verse), Bann Oester (escort).

## Involved Factions
Mourners' Circle (primary). Cinder Ledger (relic-buyers the player may have to keep from a grave). Concord Remnant (would *kill* to hear these songs — keep them from the Reclaimers).

## Surface Problem
Earn the old songs from the Circle's elders by serving the dead across the Reach — and begin to understand what the songs are really saying.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The old songs **encode the Concord's harvest** — the moral truth that the Concord consumed the dead (M5/M6, oblique). A song matched to a Concord ruin's carvings is a three-clue bridge toward R3 and (later) R4-R5. **The songs never name the Custodians/Court; they are folk-memory, not a roster.** The Remnant inner circle would love to acquire them; keeping them from the Remnant matters (#7).

## Objectives
1. Earn verses by serving the dead (multiple small deeds).
2. Interpret the songs' meaning (the Concord "ate the dead").
3. Optionally bridge a song to a ruin's carvings (M5/M6).
4. Keep the songs from the Remnant.

## Possible Approaches
- **Service:** earn verses by helping grieving families and defending graves (the intended path).
- **Scholarship:** interpret the songs; cross-reference with a Concord ruin (Investigation/History).
- **Protective:** keep relic-buyers and Remnant agents away from the elders and the songs.
- **Social:** win distant elders' trust to gather the full set.

## Obstacles
The Circle's caution (verses are earned, not given); the temptation to write the songs down and sell them; Remnant interest; relic-buyers desecrating graves the songs guard; Iola's growing conflict.

## Important Scenes
- Wend, at a graveside, singing the first verse — and the player feeling the weight of a hidden history.
- A distant elder testing the player before yielding a verse.
- The moment a song's words line up with a ruin's carved Concord Script: the oblique dawning that the songs are *true*.

## Clues
- **C-M5/M6 (oblique):** the songs say the Concord ate the dead (moral truth; no Court name).
- **Three-clue bridge:** a song + a ruin's carvings + an elder's gloss converge on R3.

## Combat Options
Optional: relic-buyer toughs or a Remnant agent at a grave — **Thug/Spy/Scout-like, AC 13, ~22-30 HP**, telegraphed, escapable. The songs are won socially, not in battle. See `../../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
The entire quest is service, scholarship, and trust. Combat is avoidable.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
No forced combat; the Circle escorts the player (Bann); verses can be earned slowly over many sessions; the songs are a clue path, not a trap.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Circle towns. Light time pressure: if relic-buyers or the Remnant reach the elders first, a verse may be lost or corrupted (a complication, not a block).

## Rewards
- **The old songs** — a major M5/M6 clue path toward the campaign's moral truth.
- Deep Circle trust (gate to QM3 and the Circle as an endgame "seal it / let-them-rest" ally).
- A network of trusting elders Reach-wide; Wend's confidence.
- Standing to keep the songs out of Remnant hands (#7 lever).

## Consequences
- **Earn the songs respectfully:** the player holds the moral truth; the Circle becomes a committed ally; #7 hindered (the Remnant can't get the songs).
- **Sell/write down carelessly:** a verse leaks to the Remnant or Ledger (#7 advances); the Circle's trust cracks.
- **Ignore:** the songs stay closed; the player loses a key clue path (recoverable later but harder).
- **Excessive force:** violence at a grave appalls the Circle; trust collapses.

## Failure State
If trust breaks or the songs leak, the player loses easy access to this clue path — but M5/M6 remain reachable via Warden, Gravecaller, and ruin routes (three-clue rule preserved). Not a block.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QM3 The Buyer at the Graveside; the songs as an Act 3-4 truth-bridge; keeping them from the Remnant (#7); Iola's hidden conflict deepening toward QM4.

## Scaling Notes
- **L3:** a few verses, simple deeds, light interpretation.
- **L5:** the full song-cycle, a ruin cross-reference, Remnant competition.
- Cap reveals at R3; the songs never name the Court.
