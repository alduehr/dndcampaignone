## AI Use
The internal-conflict + decision-point capstone. The Circle's schism breaks open: Wend's elders counsel patient grief; a radical faction (with Iola as the secret bridge) has stockpiled salt, oil, and old Concord breaking-tools to destroy a shrine — aligning, dangerously, with the Gravecallers. The player tips the Circle toward patience or destruction. Sets the Circle's endgame posture and drives clocks #4 and #5. Reveals capped at R4 (the Circle now knows the moral truth; the Court is still unnamed).

## Quest Type
Faction (Mourners' Circle) — internal conflict + decision point

## Level Range
7-12 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate (social/intrigue with one optional confrontation). The danger is the *choice* and its catastrophic potential.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Mediate Wend vs. the radicals: Persuasion/Insight DC 17.
- Expose or confront Iola's Gravecaller bridge: Insight/Investigation DC 16.
- Talk the radicals down from a shrine-strike: Persuasion DC 17 (very hard).
- Secure or neutralize the breaking-tools cache: Stealth/Investigation DC 16.

## Starting Hook
After the graveside, the Circle is openly splitting. Bann and a knot of grieving radicals — guided in secret by Iola and the Gravecallers — have gathered salt, oil, and stolen Concord breaking-tools. They mean to destroy a shrine and "free the dead for good." Wend, frightened of what that would unleash, begs the player: "I taught you the songs. You know what the Concord did. Now help me stop my own people from becoming the thing the songs warn against."

## Location
Hollowmere (the schism) and a Greyfens hideout where the breaking-tools and the radical-Gravecaller alliance meet.

## Involved NPCs
Grandmother Wend (patience; the songs' true reading), Iola Wend (the secret bridge; torn between grandmother and the Gravecallers), Bann Oester (the radicals' fist), Sister Knell or Cole Ashby (the Gravecaller partners), Elder Mabb Tay (a swing elder).

## Involved Factions
Mourners' Circle (the schism). Gravecallers (the radicals' dangerous allies). The Ashen Wardens (would be horrified by a shrine-strike; potential help). The Hollow Court (DM-only: a shrine-strike is one of the *worst* outcomes the Court fears — but also catastrophic for everyone).

## Surface Problem
The Circle is about to ally with the Gravecallers to destroy a shrine. The player decides whether the Circle stays a faith of patient grief or becomes an instrument of catastrophic liberation.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The radicals are *right* that the harvest is real and monstrous — but destroying a shrine outright would end resurrection and **destabilize the living/dead boundary** (the Gravecaller catastrophe). The old songs, fully read, actually *warn against* this very act (Wend's deepest reading). Iola's bridge to the Gravecallers is the secret that, exposed, splits the Circle. **The Court is never named; cap at R4. A successful radical strike here can advance #5 toward its Final Consequence — telegraph the catastrophe heavily.**

## Objectives
1. Uncover/confront the radical cache and the Iola-Gravecaller bridge.
2. Choose the Circle's path: patient grief (stop the strike) or destruction (enable it).
3. Resolve the schism and set the Circle's endgame posture.

## Possible Approaches
- **Back Wend (patience):** expose and dismantle the cache; talk the radicals down with the songs' true warning; hold the Circle together as a "let-them-rest / seal it" ally. (#4 and #5 eased; the Seal Bloc strengthens.)
- **Back the radicals (destruction):** help arm and aim the strike; ally the Circle with the Gravecallers toward shrine-breaking — the "destroy it" path, with catastrophic potential. (#4 and #5 advance sharply.)
- **Broker a third way:** redirect the radicals' fury into informed, non-catastrophic action (protecting graves, exposing the Ledger/Court agents) — the hardest, best outcome, requiring the songs and high trust.
- **Expose Iola:** split the Circle by revealing her bridge (ends the alliance but may shatter the Circle).

## Obstacles
The radicals' righteous grief (they're not wrong about the harvest); Iola's divided heart; Bann's loyalty to the cause; the genuine moral weight of "the dead deserve freedom"; the catastrophic stakes the radicals deny.

## Important Scenes
- The cache in the fens: salt, oil, iron breaking-tools, and Gravecallers shoulder-to-shoulder with grieving Mourners.
- Wend singing the song's *final* verse — the one that warns that breaking the seal wrong "drowns the living with the dead."
- Iola's confession or exposure; the Circle choosing, with the player's word tipping it.

## Clues
- **The songs' true warning (R4-adjacent):** breaking a shrine wrong is catastrophe (a key argument against the Gravecaller path).
- **#5 link:** the Circle's radicals are now bridged to the Gravecallers (the alliance the relationship map flags).

## Combat Options
Optional and avoidable: a standoff at the cache may turn to blows — radical Mourners and Gravecallers (**Cultist/Berserker/Thug-like, AC 12-14, ~30-45 HP**), with Cole Ashby (Tier 1 lite) the most dangerous. Telegraphed; the player can resolve by persuasion or by neutralizing the cache nonviolently. See `../../../07_factions/major_factions/GRAVECALLERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
The capstone is winnable entirely through the songs, persuasion, exposure, and dismantling the cache by stealth.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
No forced combat; Wend and (if willing) the Wardens back the player; the radicals can be talked down; the cache can be neutralized quietly; a botched fight still resolves into a redirect, not a campaign block.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Rest before the confrontation. Sharp time pressure: the strike is *imminent* — delay risks the radicals acting alone (advancing #5 toward catastrophe).

## Rewards
- **The Circle's endgame posture** (patient "seal it" ally / catastrophic "destroy it" force / informed third-way bloc).
- The songs' full reading (a complete M5/M6 moral-truth clue path; R4-adjacent).
- Wend's total trust or the radicals' loyalty, depending on the path.
- A major lever on the "destroy it" and "seal it" endgames.

## Consequences
- **Patience:** #4/#5 eased; the Circle becomes a coherent "let-them-rest" ally; the Seal Bloc is strong for the endgame.
- **Destruction:** #4/#5 advance sharply; the Circle + Gravecallers move toward shrine-breaking — powerful, catastrophic, and a strong "destroy it" trajectory (telegraph the looming disaster).
- **Third way:** the radicals' fury becomes informed opposition to the *real* enemy; the rare best outcome.
- **Expose Iola:** the alliance ends but the Circle may fracture (a weakened faction).
- **Ignore:** the radicals act alone — #5 lurches toward its Final Consequence off the player's watch (but only after telegraphed warnings).

## Failure State
If the player can't hold the Circle and the radicals strike a shrine, the catastrophe begins to unfold (a major world consequence, heavily telegraphed beforehand) — the "destroy it" outcome arrives messily and early. This reshapes the endgame; it is not a campaign-ending block, and the player can still act on the aftermath.

## Follow-Up Hooks
Toward Act 4-5: the Circle as a "seal it" or "destroy it" bloc; the Gravecaller chain (the alliance); the songs' full reading as a truth-bridge; the catastrophe (if triggered) as a new world-state.

## Scaling Notes
- **L7-8:** the schism is heated; the cache is small; the catastrophe is distant.
- **L11-12:** the radicals are organized and armed; a strike is genuinely imminent and world-shaking.
- Cap reveals at R4; never name the Court.
