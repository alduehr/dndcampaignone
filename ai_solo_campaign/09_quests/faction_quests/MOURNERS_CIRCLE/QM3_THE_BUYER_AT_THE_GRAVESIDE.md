## AI Use
The moral-complication link. Relic-buyers (the Ledger) are desecrating Circle graves for "old Concord salvage," and the Circle's grief is curdling into mob fury. The player must choose how far to let it go: protect the dead within the law, lead the mob, broker a truce, or let the Circle's rage off the leash. Drives the Circle-Turns-Militant clock (#4). Reveals capped at R3.

## Quest Type
Faction (Mourners' Circle) — moral complication

## Level Range
5-7 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate. A mob, hired Ledger muscle, and a possible riot — all telegraphed; many noncombat outs.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Calm or steer a forming mob: Persuasion/Intimidation DC 15.
- Catch grave-robbers in the act: Perception/Stealth DC 14.
- Broker a truce between Wend and a Ledger factor: Persuasion DC 16.
- Prove the Ledger ordered the desecration: Investigation DC 15.

## Starting Hook
A Circle grave has been dug up overnight and a relic taken; the dead man's daughter is screaming for blood, and Bann Oester is already gathering men. Wend asks the player to handle it "before grief becomes something we can't take back." A Ledger buyer is in town, all alibis and smiles.

## Location
A Circle cemetery (Hollowmere or Saltmargin) and the streets where the mob and the buyers collide.

## Involved NPCs
Grandmother Wend (restraint), Bann Oester (the mob's fist), Iola Wend (drawn toward harder answers), a Ledger relic-buyer (Iss Marlow or Nessa Coyle), Tallytooth Ren (Ledger muscle; turnable), the bereaved daughter.

## Involved Factions
Mourners' Circle (primary). Cinder Ledger (the desecrators). The Ashen Wardens (would help keep the peace).

## Surface Problem
Grave-robbers have struck the Circle's dead; the Circle wants vengeance; the player must resolve it without a massacre.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The Ledger ordered the grave-robbing as part of its relic-monopoly (#6); the stolen relic is raw Remembrance (M3). This incident is the campaign's first big test of the Circle-Turns-Militant clock (#4): how the player handles it decides whether the Circle's grief stays mournful or hardens toward shrine-breaking. Iola is quietly noting that the Gravecallers "at least do something." **No Court reveal; cap at R3.**

## Objectives
1. Stop the immediate desecration / recover the stolen relic.
2. Manage the mob (restrain, lead, or redirect it).
3. Decide how to deal with the Ledger (lawful proof, truce, or reprisal).

## Possible Approaches
- **Lawful:** catch the robbers, prove the Ledger's order, take it to the Compact (justice without a riot; #4 eased).
- **Restraint:** talk the mob down; broker a truce that keeps buyers from Circle graves (best for the Seal Bloc; #4 eased).
- **Lead the mob:** let the Circle's fury loose on the buyers (#4 advances; Ledger becomes an enemy; satisfying and dangerous).
- **Investigation:** turn Tallytooth Ren (who hates this work) into a witness against the Ledger.

## Obstacles
The mob's momentum; the daughter's grief; the Ledger's alibis; Iola pushing for harder measures; the genuine pull to just let the Circle have its vengeance.

## Important Scenes
- The opened grave at dawn; the daughter's wail; Bann's men with torches.
- The Ledger buyer's smooth denial — and the relic hidden in his cart.
- Wend and Iola, openly disagreeing for the first time, both looking to the player.

## Clues
- **C-M3:** the stolen relic holds the dead (witnessed if recovered).
- **#6 confirmed:** the Ledger orchestrated the desecration.
- **#4 lever:** how this resolves sets the Circle's militancy trajectory.

## Combat Options
Hired Ledger muscle (**Thug/Veteran-like, AC 13-16, ~22-45 HP**), or a riot the player must contain (a social/crowd hazard more than a kill-box). Telegraphed; the relic can be recovered by stealth or law. See `../../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Persuasion, investigation, law, and turning Ren resolve the quest without a massacre.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The mob is an ally (if steered) more than a threat; the Wardens will help keep the peace; Ren is turnable; the player can always choose the lawful or truce path to avoid violence.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Rest before/after. Sharp time pressure: the mob forms *now* — dithering lets it riot (#4 advances; people hurt).

## Rewards
- The Circle's deepening trust (toward QM4 and the endgame).
- M3 confirmation; #6 proof against the Ledger (a power-card).
- The mob's loyalty (a telegraphed force the player can call later); the daughter's gratitude.
- Standing to influence the Circle's militancy either way.

## Consequences
- **Lawful/restraint:** #4 eased; the Circle stays mournful; the Ledger is checked; the Seal Bloc strengthens.
- **Lead the mob:** #4 advances; the Circle hardens; the Ledger becomes an enemy; a buyer hurt or killed brings the watch.
- **Ignore:** the mob riots without guidance (#4 to Stage 2); deaths on both sides; the Circle's grief turns to rage anyway.
- **Turn Ren:** a witness against the Ledger and a lasting ally.
- **Excessive force:** a massacre the Circle didn't want appalls even Wend.

## Failure State
A botched handling leaves a town scarred by a riot (people dead, the Ledger vengeful, the Circle radicalized) — a heavy standing consequence that pushes #4 and the Circle toward the militant path. Not a block; it simply makes QM4's schism sharper.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QM4 Salt, Oil, and Iron; the #6 proof against the Ledger (feeds the Ledger chain QL4); Iola's drift toward the Gravecallers; Ren as a turned ally.

## Scaling Notes
- **L5:** a small mob, light muscle.
- **L7:** a near-riot, organized Ledger muscle, higher stakes.
- Cap reveals at R3.
