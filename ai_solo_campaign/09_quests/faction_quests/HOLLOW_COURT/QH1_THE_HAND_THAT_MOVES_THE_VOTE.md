## AI Use
**DM-ONLY.** The Court's opposition-intro: the player, pulling on the basin scheme (Compact chain) or the failing rites, senses that *someone hidden* is steering events toward the shrine — but they oppose an **agent and an effect, never the Court itself**. This quest names a corrupt councilor (Reke, via the Compact chain) and establishes that a hidden hand exists, capped hard at R3. The Court is never confirmed.

## Quest Type
Faction (Hollow Court — opposition) — intro hook

## Level Range
6-9 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate. Deniable agents, intrigue, optional shrine-approach hazards — telegraphed; investigation-primary.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Trace who really benefits from the basin scheme: Investigation/Insight DC 16.
- Follow Reke's nighttime basin-shore walks (C-M4-3): Stealth/Survival DC 15.
- Resist deniable intimidation: Insight/Investigation DC 15.
- Sense a pattern behind "unconnected" pushes toward the shrine: Investigation DC 16 (cumulative).

## Starting Hook
Whether through the Compact, the Wardens, or the Mourners, the player keeps hitting the same wall: every road leads to the basin, and someone is *clearing that road* — doctored surveys, a steered vote, a councilor who walks the shore alone at night. It isn't chance. Someone wants the shrine reopened, and they're very good at not being seen.

## Location
Hollowmere (the Compact; Reke), the basin shore (Reke's walks; the Surfacing Ruin), and the drainage works.

## Involved NPCs
Councilor Aldous Reke (the Court's knowing agent — exposed here as *corrupt*, never as a Court agent), Reeve Coalmont (ally), Clerk Pevin Oss (proof), Sefra Quick (peripheral; her unease seeded for QH2).

## Involved Factions
Hollow Court (DM-only; the hidden hand). Reachward Compact (the infiltrated vehicle). Cinder Ledger (unwitting funder).

## Surface Problem
Someone is steering the Reach toward reopening the drowned shrine. Find out who — and stop them.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
Reke is the Court's knowing agent; the basin scheme is the Court's plan executed through procedure. The player can expose Reke as **corrupt** (Secret 9) and even glimpse his strangeness (he is partly Remembrance-touched) — but **the Court is never named or confirmed here.** The "hidden hand" the player senses is real; its identity stays gated to R7. Cap at R3.

## Objectives
1. Identify the hidden hand behind the basin scheme (Reke).
2. Gather proof (doctored surveys; the shore walks).
3. Oppose or expose the agent — and register that *something larger* moves behind him.

## Possible Approaches
- **Investigation:** trace the money, the surveys, and the shore walks to Reke.
- **Stealth:** observe Reke at the basin (C-M4-3) and notice his unnatural calm/strangeness.
- **Political:** expose Reke as corrupt via the Compact chain (QC4).
- **Social:** press Sefra Quick, who is uneasy and knows more than she says (seeds QH2).

## Obstacles
Reke's perfect cover and charm; deniable agents protecting the scheme; the difficulty of proving a conspiracy; the eerie sense that exposing Reke doesn't reach the *real* hand.

## Important Scenes
- Reke at the basin shore at night, speaking softly to the water (C-M4-3) — wrong in a way the player can't name.
- The doctored surveys laid bare (ties to QC2/QC4).
- The unsettling realization, even after exposing Reke, that *someone else* set this in motion.

## Clues
- **Secret 9:** Reke is corrupt (and strange).
- **R3 cap:** a hidden hand exists behind the basin scheme — unnamed.

## Combat Options
Optional and avoidable: Reke's deniable agents (**Spy/Veteran-like, AC 13-16, ~30-45 HP**); Reke himself flees rather than fights cheaply (**Mage/Spy-like if cornered** — see his profile). Shrine-approach hazards are telegraphed and lethal-by-design (drowning, cold, dread) to deter early descent. See `../../../07_factions/major_factions/REACHWARD_COMPACT.md`.

## Noncombat Options
The quest is investigation, stealth, and political exposure — winnable without combat.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Reke prefers deflection and deniable pressure to violence; the shrine itself is telegraphed as lethal to keep the player from descending early; the player can withdraw with proof; exposing Reke is a political act, not a fight.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Hollowmere. Time pressure: the basin clock (#2) and master clock (#1) advance; the longer the hidden hand works unopposed, the closer the shrine to exposure.

## Rewards
- Exposure (or knowledge) of Reke as the corrupt hand (Secret 9).
- A confirmed sense that a *larger* hidden power moves behind the scheme (the Court thread, unnamed).
- A lever on the basin/master clocks (#2/#1).
- Sefra Quick's unease surfaced (gate to QH2).

## Consequences
- **Expose Reke:** #2/#1 slowed; the Court loses its key agent (but works other angles); the hidden hand thread deepens.
- **Oppose without exposing:** the scheme slows; Reke grows wary; the player keeps the thread alive.
- **Ignore:** the hidden hand works unopposed (#1/#2 advance); the shrine nears exposure.
- **Descend the shrine early:** heavily telegraphed lethal hazards turn the player back (a warning, not a cheap death).

## Failure State
If the player can't expose or oppose the hand, the basin scheme advances and the shrine nears exposure (#1/#2 advance) — a setback, not a block. The hidden-hand thread remains, and QH2 still follows.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QH2 The Probe's Own Strings (Sefra's secret; the steering); the Compact chain QC4 (the vote); the basin/master clocks; the still-unnamed power behind Reke.

## Scaling Notes
- **L6-7:** Reke is a subtle obstacle; the larger hand is only sensed.
- **L9:** Reke's strangeness is more apparent; the shrine's pull stronger; the hidden hand thread sharper.
- **Hard cap R3:** never name or confirm the Court here.
