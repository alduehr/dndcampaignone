## AI Use
**DM-ONLY.** The "moral complication" beat: the player descends to the under-shrine and makes **first contact with the Hollow Court** — the apex reveal (R7). The Court (Veyl) makes its genuinely persuasive case. This is the campaign's central moral engine: the Court is not a cartoon villain, and its argument is hard to refute. Paced to the main-arc Act 4 (`MAIN_ARC_OVERVIEW.md`). Reveals R7 (the Court is named, met, and understood). The endgame *choice* itself is QH4.

## Quest Type
Faction (Hollow Court — opposition) — moral complication (first contact)

## Level Range
13-16 (scales). **Solo danger:** high (the under-shrine; Court manifestations; lethal environment) — but the encounter is built around *choice and dialogue*, not a forced damage race. Heavily telegraphed.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Survive the under-shrine's environment (drowning, cold, dread): CON/WIS saves DC 16-18; Survival DC 17.
- Read Concord Script / the Court's true history at the source: Intelligence (Concord Script) DC 18.
- Resist the Court's persuasion / hold one's own counsel: WIS/CHA saves DC 17.
- Reach Custodian Maire (the doubter) past Orre (the hardliner): Insight/Persuasion DC 17.

## Starting Hook
Everything has led here: the basin drained or breached, the drowned shrine open, the under-shrine waiting straight down beneath Hollowmere. The player descends — and the Hollow Court is real. Preserved memory-beings, grieving and certain, the surviving Custodians who designed the Quietfall. Veyl speaks: "You have come a long way to judge us. Sit. Hear why we did what we did — and what we must do again. Then decide whether you can refuse us."

## Location
The drowned shrine and the deepest under-shrine beneath Hollowmere (the Court's seat; the harvest's keystone).

## Involved NPCs
Custodian Veyl (the leader; the final voice; the Court's reasoned case), Custodian Maire (the doubter; secretly believes the Court should let itself end — a synthesis ally), Custodian Orre (the hardliner; would harvest faster and broader), Reke (if still active; the Court's surface agent).

## Involved Factions
Hollow Court (the reveal). Every other faction's stake converges here (Wardens/Mourners would seal it; Remnant/Ledger would seize it; Gravecallers would destroy it).

## Surface Problem
The player reaches the Hollow Court and learns the full truth. The Court makes its case. The player must decide what they believe — before deciding what to do (QH4).

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
**This is the apex reveal (R7):** the Court are the surviving Custodians, persisting as Remembrance, who caused the Quietfall deliberately to pause the harvest and are now restarting it "responsibly" to save civilization from the slow thinning. **Their case is genuinely hard to refute** — they are utilitarian, grieving, and certain. Maire's heresy (the Court should let itself end) is the hidden lever for the synthesis ending. Orre wants a faster, crueler harvest. The player does NOT decide the fate of the Remembrance here — that is QH4; here they *understand* and choose what to believe. Reveals R7.

## Objectives
1. Reach and survive the under-shrine.
2. Make contact with the Court; hear Veyl's case.
3. Find Maire (the doubter) and gauge the Court's internal divide.
4. Decide what the player believes — and prepare for the endgame choice (QH4).

## Possible Approaches
- **Diplomatic:** hear Veyl out; engage the Court's argument; seek Maire.
- **Investigative:** confirm the full truth at the source (Concord Script; the keystone).
- **Defiant:** refuse the Court's case (and survive its disappointment — it prefers persuasion to destruction).
- **Allied:** bring faction allies (Wardens/Mourners/Gravecallers/Remnant) to the threshold to shape the confrontation.

## Obstacles
The under-shrine's lethal environment; the Court's manifestations; the *force* of Veyl's argument; Orre's hostility; the temptation of the Court's offer (immortality, knowledge, "mercy"); the weight of finally knowing.

## Important Scenes
- The descent: cold, drowning dread, the keystone humming with a century of stored dead.
- Veyl's case: grief, certainty, and an argument the player cannot simply dismiss.
- Maire, apart from the others, whispering doubt — the seed of another way.
- Orre's contempt; the Court's internal fracture made visible.

## Combat Options
The Court manifests as **Remembrance-beings (ghost/wraith/lich-like, scaled to Tier 4)** with built-in weaknesses and non-combat win conditions (severing the keystone, persuading Maire, the player's chosen artifact/ally). **Veyl is never a "you must out-damage me" wall** — combat here is one option among many, always with terrain, allies, and alternatives. Orre is the most combat-prone; Veyl prefers to convince. See `../../../07_factions/major_factions/HOLLOW_COURT.md` (Mechanical Notes) and (Stage 13/15) boss design.

## Noncombat Options
First contact is built for dialogue, investigation, and persuasion. The player can leave the under-shrine without a fight to prepare the endgame.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The environment is telegraphed as lethal at every prior level (so the player arrives prepared, not ambushed); the Court prefers to persuade, not kill; the player can withdraw to choose their endgame approach (QH4); Maire offers a non-combat lever; allies brought to the threshold can cover a retreat.

## Rest And Time Pressure
No safe rest in the under-shrine (a constraint, telegraphed). The master clock (#1) is at or near its climax; the player should arrive prepared. The player can retreat to rest and return for QH4.

## Rewards
- **The full truth (R7):** the Court is named, met, and understood — the campaign's apex revelation.
- Knowledge of the Court's internal divide (Veyl/Maire/Orre) — the lever for the endgame.
- Maire as a potential synthesis ally.
- Everything the player needs to make the endgame choice (QH4).

## Consequences
- **Hear the case / find Maire:** the player is equipped for every endgame branch, including the synthesis (transform).
- **Defy the Court:** the lines are drawn for an oppositional endgame (seal/destroy/seize).
- **Bring allies:** the endgame confrontation is shaped by who stands with the player.
- **Refuse to descend (delay):** the master clock advances toward the Court's default restart (the worst outcome looms) — a telegraphed cost, not a block.

## Failure State
There is no "fail" that ends the campaign here — even a disastrous first contact (a fight, a forced retreat) leaves the player alive, informed, and able to return for QH4. A botched descent costs resources and may advance the master clock, but the apex truth, once glimpsed, cannot be un-learned. The endgame remains open.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QH4 The Fate of the Remembrance (the endgame choice); the Court's internal divide (Veyl/Maire/Orre); every faction's endgame stake converging on the keystone; the master clock's climax.

## Scaling Notes
- **L13-14:** the Court's manifestations are dangerous but the encounter is dialogue-first.
- **L15-16:** the under-shrine is at full lethality; Orre is a real threat; Veyl's case is at its most compelling.
- **R7 cap:** this quest *is* the reveal — but the *choice* (and its consequences) is QH4. Do not pre-resolve the endgame here.
