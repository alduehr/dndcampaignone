## AI Use
**DM-ONLY.** The "trust-building" beat reskinned for the Court: the player discovers that *they themselves* were steered to Hollowmere as the Court's unwitting probe (M8 / Secret 16). This is a trust-shock, not a recruitment. Delivers R4-R5 (the steering is real; a hidden patron arranged the player's involvement) while still **not naming the Court** — the player traces the steering to a cutout, not to the Court itself. Reveals capped at R5.

## Quest Type
Faction (Hollow Court — opposition) — trust-building (reskinned: self-discovery)

## Level Range
8-12 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate. Intrigue, a vanishing cutout, optional deniable pressure — telegraphed; investigation/social-primary.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Press Sefra Quick for the truth of who hired her: Persuasion/Insight DC 16.
- Trace the cutout patron's money/messages: Investigation DC 17.
- Detect that the trail is *meant* to dead-end (the cutout is insulated): Insight DC 16.
- Protect Sefra from being "tidied up": varies; Investigation/Stealth DC 16.

## Starting Hook
Sefra Quick — the fixer who first pointed the player at the failed rite, at the basin, at every "opportunity" — is unravelling. She drinks more, looks over her shoulder, and finally breaks: "I was paid to make sure a capable newcomer poked at the shrine. You. Someone *wanted* you here, doing exactly this. I don't know who. I'm scared of what happens to me now that I've told you."

## Location
Hollowmere (Sefra; the Drowned Lantern; the cutout's trail) and wherever the money leads (a dead-end by design).

## Involved NPCs
Sefra Quick (the cutout's unwitting tool; the player's first contact; now a frightened confessor), the cutout patron (insulated, faceless — the trail dead-ends here by design), Reke (peripheral; another instrument of the same hand), Halla Coalmont (the inn; a witness to Sefra's fear).

## Involved Factions
Hollow Court (DM-only; the steering hand). No other faction knows of the steering.

## Surface Problem
The player learns they were steered to Hollowmere by a hidden patron. Who arranged it — and why?

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
A **Hollow Court cutout** hired Sefra to position the player as the Court's probe (M8 / Secret 16). The Court wanted a capable, deniable agent to "poke at" the shrine and do reconnaissance the dormant Court could not. **The trail is insulated: it ends at the cutout, NOT at the Court.** The player learns the steering is real and that a singular hidden power arranged it — but **the Court is still never named** (that is QH3 / R7). The Court may move to "tidy up" Sefra if neglected (clock #8 Final). Cap at R5.

## Objectives
1. Get the full truth from Sefra (the steering; M8).
2. Trace the cutout patron as far as the trail allows (it dead-ends by design).
3. Decide what to do with the knowledge — and whether to protect Sefra.

## Possible Approaches
- **Social:** earn Sefra's full confession; protect her.
- **Investigation:** follow the cutout's money/messages to the (insulated) dead-end — confirming a singular hidden hand.
- **Protective:** shield Sefra from being silenced (the Court may move on her; #8).
- **Confrontational:** use the steering knowledge to recalibrate trust with every faction (the player can no longer assume their path was their own).

## Obstacles
Sefra's fear and self-preservation; the cutout's perfect insulation; the trust-shock (the player's whole journey reframed); the Court's quiet move to tidy up loose ends.

## Important Scenes
- Sefra's confession at the Drowned Lantern, drunk and terrified.
- The money trail dead-ending at a faceless cutout — proof of a hidden hand, no face attached.
- The chilling reframe: every "lucky" opportunity the player took may have been laid for them.

## Clues
- **M8 / Secret 16 (R5):** the player was steered to Hollowmere by a hidden patron — confirmed.
- A singular hidden power arranged it (the Court, *unnamed*).

## Combat Options
Optional and avoidable: the Court's deniable agents may move to silence Sefra or warn the player off — **Spy/Assassin-lite-like, AC 14, ~40 HP**, telegraphed, escapable; they prefer to vanish rather than fight to the death. No cheap death. See `../../../07_factions/major_factions/HOLLOW_COURT.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Confession, investigation, and protection resolve the quest — winnable without combat.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The trail's dead-end is by design, not a player failure; Sefra can be protected (or her loss is telegraphed); the agents prefer disappearance to lethal confrontation; the trust-shock is a story beat, not a mechanical penalty.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Hollowmere. Time pressure: clock #8 (The Steering Unravels) — if the player neglects Sefra, the Court moves to tidy her up (she vanishes; the confession is lost but the steering remains discoverable via the cutout trail).

## Rewards
- **The steering truth (M8 / R5)** — a personal, campaign-reframing revelation.
- Sefra Quick as a protected ally and witness (if saved).
- Confirmation of a singular hidden power behind everything (the Court, unnamed) — the direct setup for QH3.
- A recalibration of trust with every faction.

## Consequences
- **Get the truth + protect Sefra:** the player knows they were steered and keeps the witness; the Court thread sharpens toward QH3.
- **Truth but Sefra lost:** the Court tidies her up (#8 Final); the steering is known but the human cost is paid.
- **Ignore:** Sefra vanishes; the steering stays hidden longer; the player's reframe is delayed (recoverable via the cutout trail).
- **Confront a faction wrongly:** mis-aiming the steering blame at an innocent faction is a fair red-herring the player can correct.

## Failure State
If the player ignores Sefra, she is silenced and the confession is lost — but the steering remains discoverable through the cutout's money trail and Reke's parallel instrumentation (multiple paths preserved). Not a block; the personal reframe simply arrives later and colder.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QH3 The Court Beneath (first contact); the personal trust-crisis with every faction; protecting Sefra (or avenging her); the singular hidden power now demanding a name.

## Scaling Notes
- **L8-9:** the steering is a shock; the agents are subtle.
- **L11-12:** the Court moves more openly to tidy up; the dead-end is guarded; the reframe lands hardest as Act 4 nears.
- **Hard cap R5:** never name the Court here.
