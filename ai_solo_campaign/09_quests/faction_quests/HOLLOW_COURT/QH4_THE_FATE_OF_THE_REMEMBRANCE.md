## AI Use
**DM-ONLY.** The campaign's capstone: the player decides the fate of the Remembrance at the keystone. This is the "internal conflict + decision point" for the Court — and the whole campaign. The Court's internal divide (Veyl's restart vs. Maire's heresy vs. Orre's hardline) is the lever. This quest is the faction-chain face of the main-arc Act 5; full branching detail lives in `MAIN_ARC_OVERVIEW.md` and (Stage 15) `ENDGAME_STATES.md`. Reveals R8 (full truth; the choice and its consequences).

## Quest Type
Faction (Hollow Court) — internal conflict + decision point (campaign endgame)

## Level Range
17-20. **Solo danger:** apex — but the climax is built around **choice and clever play**, never an unwinnable damage race. Every branch has non-combat resolutions, allies, terrain, and built-in weaknesses.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Enact the chosen endgame (sever/restart/seize/destroy/transform the keystone): varies by branch; key checks DC 18-22 with multiple paths (never a single gate).
- Turn Maire against Veyl (the synthesis lever): Persuasion/Insight DC 19.
- Withstand Orre (the hardliner): combat or counter-manifestation; or bypass him via Maire/Veyl.
- Unite the player's gathered allies for the chosen branch: roleplay-gated on accumulated faction standing.

## Starting Hook
The master clock is at its climax. The basin is open, the keystone exposed, the Court awake and resolved to restart the harvest. Every faction the player has shaped stands at the threshold — Wardens, Mourners, Ledger, Remnant, Gravecallers — each demanding (or pleading for) a different fate. Veyl waits at the keystone. The player must choose what becomes of every soul that has ever died.

## Location
The keystone at the deepest under-shrine beneath Hollowmere.

## Involved NPCs
Custodian Veyl (the restart; the final voice), Custodian Maire (the heresy; the synthesis lever — believes the Court should let itself end), Custodian Orre (the hardline; would harvest faster), and the player's accumulated faction allies/enemies (Othetha, Wend, Vyre, Quorrin, the Tallow Man, etc., per the player's chains).

## Involved Factions
Hollow Court (the apex). All six other factions converge here, each aligned to an ending by the player's faction chains:
- **Wardens + Mourners → Seal it.**
- **Ledger / Remnant inner circle → Seize it / Sell it.**
- **Gravecallers (+ radical Mourners) → Destroy it.**
- **Hollow Court (Veyl) → Restart it controlled.**
- **The synthesis (Maire + united unlikely allies) → Transform it.**

## Surface Problem
Decide and enact the fate of the Remembrance. The campaign ends on this choice.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The five endgame branches (`MAIN_ARC_OVERVIEW.md`):
1. **Seal it** — re-seal the shrine; the dead rest; resurrection mostly ends. (Wardens/Mourners.)
2. **Restart it controlled** — the Court resumes a "gentler" harvest; stability and immortality for some, the dead consumed forever. (Veyl.)
3. **Seize it** — hand the machine to a living faction; empire or monopoly; new tyranny. (Remnant/Ledger.)
4. **Destroy it** — shatter the harvest; free the dead; resurrection ends and the boundary destabilizes. (Gravecallers.)
5. **Transform it** — the hardest synthesis: a *consensual* system where the dead choose; requires Maire and united unlikely allies. The "best," not guaranteed.

**No ending is labeled correct.** Each has real costs. Maire's heresy is the lever to the synthesis. Orre is the obstacle to mercy. The player's faction chains determine which allies, levers, and branches are reachable. Reveals R8.

## Objectives
1. Choose the fate of the Remembrance.
2. Gather the allies and levers the chosen branch requires.
3. Enact the choice at the keystone (with the Court's internal divide as the pivot).
4. Live with the consequences (epilogues per `ENDGAME_STATES.md`).

## Possible Approaches
- **Seal:** with the Wardens (Othetha) and Mourners (Wend), re-seal the keystone — sever the harvest, let the dead rest.
- **Restart (join Veyl):** side with the Court — the dark "mercy"; immortality for stewards, the dead consumed.
- **Seize:** deliver the keystone to the Ledger/Remnant — a living tyranny on the dead.
- **Destroy:** with the Gravecallers, shatter the keystone — free the dead, accept the catastrophe.
- **Transform (synthesis):** turn Maire against Veyl, unite unlikely allies, and remake the harvest into a consensual system — the hardest, best path.

## Obstacles
Orre's hardline resistance; Veyl's final, compelling case; the cost of *every* choice; the difficulty of the synthesis (requires Maire and broad alliances built over the whole campaign); the player's accumulated enemies arriving to contest the keystone.

## Important Scenes
- The threshold: every faction the player shaped, arriving to demand a fate.
- Veyl's last argument; Maire's whispered alternative; Orre's defiance.
- The keystone, the choice made flesh, the world changing on the player's word.
- The epilogues: the world the player's century-spanning choice leaves behind.

## Combat Options
The climax can be combat (Orre; manifestations; contesting factions), but **never a forced, unwinnable damage race.** Veyl is a Tier-4 solo-boss with built-in weaknesses and non-combat win conditions (severing the keystone, persuading Maire, the player's chosen artifact/ally). Every branch has a non-combat path to enact it. See `../../../07_factions/major_factions/HOLLOW_COURT.md` and (Stage 13/15) boss/endgame design.

## Noncombat Options
Every ending is reachable without defeating Veyl in battle — by sealing, persuading, allying, sabotaging, or transforming. The synthesis ending is *primarily* non-combat.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The climax is telegraphed across the entire campaign; the player arrives prepared, with allies and levers earned through their chains; Maire offers a non-combat pivot; every branch has multiple enactment paths; the player can retreat to gather more allies if a branch isn't yet reachable. No cheap death — the campaign's climax rewards accumulated clever play.

## Rest And Time Pressure
The master clock is at climax; this is the final scene. The player should arrive prepared (rest and ally-gathering happen *before* the final descent). Once enacted, the choice is the campaign's ending.

## Rewards
- **The campaign's resolution** — the fate of every soul, decided by the player.
- The endgame state and epilogues (`ENDGAME_STATES.md`): faction outcomes, NPC fates, the world after.
- The culmination of every faction chain the player ran.

## Consequences
Per `MAIN_ARC_OVERVIEW.md` and `ENDGAME_STATES.md` — each branch reshapes the world:
- **Seal:** the dead rest; resurrection mostly ends; a quiet, costly, humane world.
- **Restart:** the Court rules; immortality for some; the dead consumed forever.
- **Seize:** a living faction's tyranny on the dead.
- **Destroy:** the dead freed; resurrection ends; the boundary destabilized; chaotic liberation.
- **Transform:** a consensual system where the dead choose; the hard-won best outcome.

## Failure State
There is no "campaign-block" failure here — only different endings. A player who arrives under-prepared may be pushed toward a less-favorable branch (the Court's default restart, or a costly seal/destroy), but every outcome is a *real ending*, not a dead end. The synthesis is the only branch that can be "missed" (it requires the deepest play); its absence is a poorer ending, not a failure screen.

## Follow-Up Hooks
The epilogues (`ENDGAME_STATES.md`); unresolved threads; optional sequel hooks (Stage 19). This is the campaign's end.

## Scaling Notes

- The synthesis branch scales in difficulty with how many unlikely allies the player united across all faction chains.
