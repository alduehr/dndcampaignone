## AI Use

**DM-ONLY. The Hollow Court is a ghost story to the player until a late-arc earned revelation.** Unlike the six open factions, the Court has **NO friendly recruit/intro quest** by design — there is no "join the Court" on-ramp a low-level player can stumble into. Instead this chain is an **opposition-and-revelation arc**: the player *uncovers, races, and finally confronts* the Court, and only at the very end can choose to ally with, oppose, seal, destroy, or transform it. The chain is paced to the main-arc revelation schedule (`REVELATION_MAP.md`) and must never name the Court before its earned point.

This chain is the faction-quest companion to the main arc; the master clock (#1) and the full endgame branching live in `MAIN_ARC_OVERVIEW.md` and (Stage 15) `ACT_4`/`ACT_5`/`ENDGAME_STATES.md`. This file supplies the **Court-specific quest beats** that thread through those acts.

## Chain Structure

| # | Quest | Chain Role | Level | Reveal Cap | File |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Hand That Moves the Vote | Intro hook (opposition; agent, not Court) | 6-9 | R3 (a hidden hand exists) | `QH1_THE_HAND_THAT_MOVES_THE_VOTE.md` |
| 2 | The Probe's Own Strings | Trust-building (the player learns *they* are an instrument) | 8-12 | R4-R5 (the steering; M8) | `QH2_THE_PROBES_OWN_STRINGS.md` |
| 3 | The Court Beneath | Moral complication (first contact; the Court's case) | 13-16 | R7 (the Court named/met) | `QH3_THE_COURT_BENEATH.md` |
| 4 | The Fate of the Remembrance | Internal conflict + decision point (the endgame choice) | 17-20 | R8 (full truth; endgame) | `QH4_THE_FATE_OF_THE_REMEMBRANCE.md` |

> **Note on "chain role" framing:** because the Court has no recruitable membership, the standard chain roles are reskinned: the "intro hook" is the player first *opposing* the Court's hidden hand (an agent, not the Court); "trust-building" is reskinned as the player discovering *their own* entanglement (the steering); the "moral complication" is first contact and the Court's genuinely persuasive case; the "decision point" is the campaign's central endgame choice. The Court's *internal* conflict (Veyl vs. Maire vs. Orre) is the lever at the decision point.

## Chain Throughline
The player never "joins" the Court early. They first fight its *effects* (a steered vote, a corrupt councilor), then discover they themselves were steered to Hollowmere as the Court's probe, then finally descend to meet the Court and hear its terrible, reasonable case — and at the end decide the fate of the Remembrance: seal it, restart it (the Court's plan), seize it, destroy it, or transform it.

## Key NPCs (DM-Only)
Custodian Veyl (leader; final voice), Custodian Maire (doubter; synthesis ally), Custodian Orre (hardliner), Councilor Aldous Reke (the Court's knowing agent), Sefra Quick (the cutout's unwitting tool who steered the player).
