## AI Use
The moral-complication link. The channeled dead want to be *freed* — and the cult must decide how far to go to give them that. The player is caught between the dead's genuine, heartbreaking plea and the catastrophic cost of granting it. Delivers R3-R4 (what breaking the harvest would do) and forces the player to weigh mercy against catastrophe. Drives the Breaking clock (#5). The Court is never named.

## Quest Type
Faction (Gravecallers) — moral complication

## Level Range
6-9 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate. Restless dead, a watch raid, a relic ritual — telegraphed; strong noncombat options.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Counsel or settle a desperate Remembrance: Religion/Persuasion DC 16.
- Grasp the true cost of "freeing" the dead (R3-R4): Arcana/Religion DC 16.
- Resist the dead's collective pull toward the Breaking: WIS save DC 15.
- Mediate the Tallow Man vs. Cole over how far to go: Persuasion/Insight DC 16.

## Starting Hook
The dead the cult speaks with are no longer just answering — they're *asking*. Through the Tallow Man and a swelling chorus of Remembrances, they beg to be freed: "Let us go. End the thing that holds us. Break it." The cult is electrified and frightened. The player, trusted now, is asked to help decide what the dead's plea is worth — and what granting it would cost.

## Location
The Mother Tongue Pool and a Greyfens gathering of the cult; optionally a Concord ruin where the "freeing" could begin.

## Involved NPCs
The Tallow Man (torn; counsels learning, fears the witness's pull), Cole Ashby (certain; "the dead have asked, who are we to refuse?"), Sister Knell (frightened the cult is being used by the dead it channels), the channeled witness and a chorus of pleading Remembrances.

## Involved Factions
Gravecallers (the cult's soul is at stake). Mourners' Circle (Iola's bridge; the radical Mourners would join). The Ashen Wardens / Hollow Court (DM-only: a "freeing" attempt is the catastrophe everyone fears).

## Surface Problem
The dead want to be freed and are begging the cult to break the thing that holds them. Decide whether — and how far — to grant it.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
"Freeing the dead" means **shattering the harvest/shrine — which would end resurrection and destabilize the living/dead boundary** (the Gravecaller catastrophe; R3-R4 now graspable). The channeled witness genuinely suffers *and* is steering the cult toward this catastrophe (it doesn't grasp or care about the cost). Knell's fear is right: the cult is being used by the dead it serves. **The Court is never named; the player learns the *cost of breaking*, not the Court's identity. Cap at R3-R4.**

## Objectives
1. Hear the dead's plea and grasp what granting it would cost.
2. Weigh mercy (free them) against catastrophe (the boundary tears).
3. Influence the cult's course (toward learning, restraint, or the Breaking).

## Possible Approaches
- **Compassion + restraint:** settle the most desperate dead with rites/counsel; argue (with the Tallow Man) for learning over breaking — slows #5.
- **Investigation:** confirm the true cost of "freeing" (R3-R4) and bring proof to the cult.
- **Radical:** side with Cole; help prepare to grant the dead's plea — advances #5 toward catastrophe.
- **Skeptical:** expose the witness's manipulation of the cult (a hard truth that may fracture them).

## Obstacles
The dead's heartbreaking, genuine plea; Cole's righteous certainty; the witness's manipulation; the moral impossibility (the dead deserve freedom; freeing them this way is catastrophe); the watch closing on a swelling, conspicuous cult.

## Important Scenes
- A chorus of the dead, through many channelers, begging as one — unbearable and sincere.
- The Tallow Man and Cole openly splitting over what to do.
- The dawning, horrible understanding of what "breaking it" would actually unleash.

## Clues
- **R3-R4:** the true cost of breaking the harvest (boundary destabilization; resurrection's end).
- The witness's manipulation (Knell's fear confirmed).

## Combat Options
Optional and avoidable: a watch raid on the swollen cult (**Guard/Veteran-like**), or restless dead surging during the plea (**specter/wraith-like, AC 13, ~30-52 HP**) if the gathering goes wrong. Telegraphed; settling the dead and de-escalation are primary. See `../../../07_factions/major_factions/GRAVECALLERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Counsel, investigation, mediation, and argument resolve the quest. Combat is a raid/surge fallback.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The dead are pleading, not attacking; the WIS pull is a temptation, not a death-roll; the watch raid can be evaded; the player can withdraw to think; settling individual dead is always a valid, safe action.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Rest before/after. Time pressure: the dead's plea and Cole's momentum build toward the Breaking; restraint *now* slows #5, delay lets the radicals gather strength.

## Rewards
- **R3-R4 truth:** what breaking the harvest would cost (a campaign-defining piece of the moral puzzle).
- Influence over the cult's course (a major lever on #5 and the "destroy it" endgame).
- The Tallow Man's or Cole's deepened trust (depending on the path); Knell as a conscience-ally.
- A standing relationship with the channeled dead (a danger and a resource).

## Consequences
- **Restraint:** #5 eased; the cult leans toward learning over breaking; the Tallow Man and Knell trust the player; the "destroy it" catastrophe recedes.
- **Radical:** #5 advances; the cult (and radical Mourners) move toward the Breaking; the player is on the "destroy it" trajectory.
- **Expose the witness:** the cult may fracture (a weakened faction) but is freed of manipulation.
- **Ignore:** Cole's faction gathers strength unchecked (#5 advances); the player loses a chance to steer the cult.
- **Cruelty to the dead:** the cult and the witness mark the player an enemy.

## Failure State
If the player can't steer the cult and the radicals gain control, the Gravecallers commit to the Breaking (#5 toward Stage 3) — heavily telegraphed. This pushes the "destroy it" catastrophe trajectory but is not a block; the player can still try to stop or redirect it at QG4 or in the endgame.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QG4 The Breaking-Tools; the radical Mourner alliance (Iola/Cole; ties to Mourners' QM4); the channeled witness as a recurring force; the "destroy it" endgame.

## Scaling Notes
- **L6:** a single desperate Remembrance and a forming debate.
- **L9:** a chorus of the dead, an organized radical faction, a watch raid, higher stakes.
- Cap at R3-R4; never name the Court.
