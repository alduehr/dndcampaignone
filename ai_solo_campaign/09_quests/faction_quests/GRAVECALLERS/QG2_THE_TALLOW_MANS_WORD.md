## AI Use
The trust-building link. The player is brought before the Tallow Man and, by completing a task for the cult (recover a relic, carry a message from the dead, or shield a cell), earns the right to hear the dead's testimony — the chain's truth-payload. Delivers a major M5/M6 clue path (a Quietfall-era witness describing the Concord's harvest, **obliquely**). Reveals capped at R3; the Court is never named.

## Quest Type
Faction (Gravecallers) — trust-building task

## Level Range
4-6 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate. A fen ruin, a watch threat, optional Remembrances — telegraphed; many noncombat outs.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Complete the cult's task (recover a relic / carry a message): varies; Investigation/Stealth/Persuasion DC 14-15.
- Endure/interpret the Tallow Man's channeling: Religion/Insight DC 15.
- Resist the channeled Remembrance's pull (it has its own agenda): WIS save DC 14.
- Cross the deep fen to the sacred pool: Survival DC 15.

## Starting Hook
Knell brings the player deeper: the Tallow Man will speak for them — but trust is earned. "Do this for the dead, and the dead will speak to you. The Tallow Man's voice isn't entirely his own, and what it says... you'll never unhear it." The task: recover a stolen relic, carry a dead person's message to the living, or shield a cell from a watch sweep.

## Location
A Greyfens cell, the Mother Tongue Pool (the cult's sacred channeling site), and wherever the task leads.

## Involved NPCs
The Tallow Man (the speaker; channels a Quietfall-era Remembrance), Sister Knell (sponsor), Cole Ashby (present, intense, sizing up the player as a potential radical), the channeled dead (a real witness with its own agenda).

## Involved Factions
Gravecallers (primary). Reachward Compact (the watch hazard). Mourners' Circle (would be horrified; Iola may appear secretly).

## Surface Problem
Earn the Tallow Man's word by serving the cult — and hear what the dead have to say.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The Tallow Man channels a **genuine Quietfall-era witness** to the Concord's harvest (a major M5/M6 truth-source). The testimony reveals — obliquely — that the Concord *consumed the dead* and that "something was paused" (R3). **It does NOT name the Custodians/Court** and the witness has **its own agenda** (it wants to be freed, and may steer the player toward the catastrophic Breaking). Cole Ashby marks the player as possible radical material (seeds QG4). Cap at R3.

## Objectives
1. Complete the cult's task to earn trust.
2. Attend the channeling and hear the dead's testimony.
3. Interpret the truth (and the witness's agenda) without being captured by it.

## Possible Approaches
- **Service:** complete the relic-recovery / message-carrying task cleanly.
- **Ritual/insight:** attend the channeling; interpret the witness's words and motives.
- **Stealth/protective:** shield the cell from the watch (an alternative trust-task).
- **Skeptical:** verify the testimony against other clues (Mourner songs, ruins) before believing.

## Obstacles
The task's hazards (a fen ruin, a watch sweep); the channeled witness's eerie pull and hidden agenda; Cole Ashby's radicalizing pressure; the watch closing in; the moral weight of trafficking with the dead.

## Important Scenes
- The Mother Tongue Pool at night; the Tallow Man's voice changing as the dead speak through him.
- The witness's testimony: grief, fury, and a fragment of the truth — "they took us, and then they stopped, and now they've begun again."
- Cole Ashby afterward: "Now you've heard it. Now you know why we have to break it. Are you with us?"

## Clues
- **C-M5/M6 (oblique, major):** a Quietfall-era witness describing the harvest and "the pause" (R3; no Court name).
- The witness's agenda (it wants freeing — a danger flag, seeds QG3/QG4).

## Combat Options
Optional and avoidable: a watch sweep (**Guard/Veteran-like, AC 16, ~22-45 HP**) or a hostile Remembrance if the channeling goes wrong (**specter/wraith-like, AC 13, ~30-45 HP**). Telegraphed; the task and channeling are primarily noncombat. See `../../regional_quests/Q_THE_SECOND_MARK.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Service, ritual attendance, and interpretation resolve the quest. Combat is a fen/watch fallback.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The cult protects a trusted player; the channeling is unsettling but not a damage-trap (the WIS save is a pull, not a death-roll); the watch can be evaded; the player can leave the channeling at any point.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Rest at a cell or withdraw to Hollowmere. Time pressure: the watch hunts the cells; lingering risks a sweep (and the cult's persecution worsens, advancing #5's recruitment pressure).

## Rewards
- **The dead's testimony** — the truest, most dangerous M5/M6 clue path (R3).
- The Tallow Man's word (deep cult trust; gate to QG3).
- Access to the cult's hidden knowledge and fen routes.
- A standing (and a warning) about the channeled witness's agenda.

## Consequences
- **Earn the word:** the cult opens fully; the player holds a major truth-fragment; Compact/Mourner suspicion deepens if seen.
- **Refuse the radical pull:** the player keeps the truth but resists Cole's recruitment (sets up a moderate QG4).
- **Embrace Cole's framing:** the player leans toward the Breaking (sets up a radical QG4; #5 nudged).
- **Ignore/botch the task:** the cult stays guarded; the channeling is withheld; the clue path narrows (recoverable via Mourner songs/ruins).
- **Betray the cult:** the witness and the cult mark the player an enemy of the dead (telegraphed reprisals).

## Failure State
If the player botches the task or is captured by the witness's pull, the testimony is incomplete or warped — a partial clue and a complication (the witness may haunt the player), not a block. M5/M6 remain reachable elsewhere.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QG3 What the Dead Want; the channeled witness's agenda (a recurring danger); Cole Ashby's radical recruitment (toward QG4); the Mourners' chain (Iola's bridge).

## Scaling Notes
- **L4:** a simple task; a gentle channeling.
- **L6:** a hazardous task, a watch sweep, a stronger and more demanding witness.
- Cap at R3; never name the Court.
