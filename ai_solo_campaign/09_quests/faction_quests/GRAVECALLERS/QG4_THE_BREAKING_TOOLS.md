## AI Use
The internal-conflict + decision-point capstone. The cult's split breaks open: the Tallow Man counsels patience and learning; Cole Ashby has gathered Concord breaking-tools and a shrine map and is ready to shatter the harvest now. The player decides whether the Gravecallers become careful truth-keepers or the instruments of catastrophic liberation. Sets the cult's endgame posture and drives the Breaking clock (#5) — potentially toward its Final Consequence. The Court is never named; reveals capped at R4.

## Quest Type
Faction (Gravecallers) — internal conflict + decision point

## Level Range
8-13 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate-to-high (a possible raid on the cache; Cole as a Tier 1 threat). The deeper danger is the *catastrophe* a wrong choice unleashes.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Talk Cole down from an immediate shrine-strike: Persuasion DC 18 (very hard; needs the R3-R4 cost-of-breaking proof).
- Secure or neutralize the breaking-tools cache: Stealth/Investigation DC 16.
- Rally the Tallow Man's moderates: Persuasion/Insight DC 16.
- Disrupt or aid the shrine-strike preparation: varies; Investigation/Arcana DC 17.

## Starting Hook
Cole Ashby has what the radicals always wanted: stolen Concord breaking-tools and a map of the drowned shrine. He means to use them — soon — with or without the cult's blessing, and the radical Mourners (via Iola) are ready to join. The Tallow Man, afraid of what the player learned in QG3, begs them to stop it: "The dead asked us to free them. But the boy will drown the world to do it. Help me hold us back — or help me make sure, if it's done, it's done right."

## Location
A Greyfens stronghold (the cache and the cult's reckoning) and the approaches to the drowned shrine.

## Involved NPCs
The Tallow Man (patience; the cult's wavering soul), Cole Ashby (the radical with the tools; Tier 1 zealot), Sister Knell (terrified; a conscience-ally), Iola Wend (the Mourner bridge; her radicals stand with Cole), the channeled witness (urging the Breaking).

## Involved Factions
Gravecallers (the schism). Mourners' Circle (radical wing allied via Iola — ties to `../MOURNERS_CIRCLE/QM4_SALT_OIL_AND_IRON.md`). The Ashen Wardens / Reachward Compact (would stop a shrine-strike if warned). The Hollow Court (DM-only: a successful strike is both the Court's nightmare *and* a world catastrophe).

## Surface Problem
Cole has the means to shatter the harvest now. The player decides whether the Gravecallers do it, refrain, or find a third way — and lives with the consequences.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
Cole's breaking-tools and shrine map are real (#5 at Stage 2-3). A successful, *uncontrolled* strike triggers the **catastrophe** — resurrection ends, the living/dead boundary tears (R4; the "destroy it" ending arrived violently). The R3-R4 cost-of-breaking from QG3 is the key argument to stop it. **The Court is never named here** — even at the shrine, the player confronts tools and consequences, not the Custodians (that is Act 4-5). The "done right" path (controlled, with allies) is the seed of the *synthesis/transform* ending, only reachable with deep cross-faction play. Cap at R4.

## Objectives
1. Confront the cache and the cult's split (Tallow Man vs. Cole).
2. Choose: stop the Breaking, enable it, or steer it toward a controlled "done right."
3. Resolve the schism and set the cult's endgame posture.

## Possible Approaches
- **Stop it (patience):** secure/neutralize the cache; talk Cole down with the QG3 cost-proof; rally the moderates — #5 eased; the cult becomes a careful truth-keeper (and a "seal it"-compatible ally).
- **Enable it (the Breaking):** help Cole strike — the "destroy it" trajectory, catastrophic and heavily telegraphed; #5 toward Final Consequence.
- **Steer it ("done right"):** redirect the cult toward a *controlled*, allied unmaking of the harvest (with Wardens/Mourners/others) — the hard synthesis seed; requires deep play and broad alliances.
- **Expose/disrupt:** warn the Wardens/Compact to stop the strike (saves the world, ends the cult as an ally; the dead's plea goes unanswered).

## Obstacles
Cole's zeal and his tools; the channeled witness urging the Breaking; the radical Mourners' resolve; the moral agony (the dead truly want freedom; granting it wrong is catastrophe); the watch and Wardens if the strike goes loud.

## Important Scenes
- The cache: Concord breaking-tools, a shrine map, and grieving zealots ready to drown the world for mercy.
- The Tallow Man and Cole's final confrontation, the cult choosing its soul.
- (If it proceeds) the approach to the drowned shrine, the catastrophe looming, the player's last chance to stop, steer, or commit.

## Combat Options
Cole Ashby — **Acolyte/Berserker-like Tier 1 lite, AC 13, ~30-45 HP** (scaling), dangerous when committed to the Breaking; radical Mourners and Gravecallers (**Cultist/Thug-like, AC 12-14, ~30-45 HP**); optional watch/Warden intervention. All telegraphed; the cache can be neutralized by stealth and the strike stopped by persuasion. Cole is never a cheap-death ambush; he can be talked down, outmaneuvered, or subdued. See `../../../07_factions/major_factions/GRAVECALLERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
The capstone is winnable by securing the cache, presenting the cost-of-breaking proof, rallying moderates, and persuasion — no kill required.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Cole and the radicals can be talked down or outmaneuvered; the cache can be neutralized nonviolently; the Tallow Man, Knell, and (if allied) the Wardens back a restraining player; a botched confrontation still resolves into a redirect (the strike delayed, the catastrophe telegraphed), not a campaign-ending block.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Rest before. Sharp time pressure: Cole means to strike *soon* — the player must act with the proof and allies they've gathered; delay risks the radicals striking alone (advancing #5 toward catastrophe).

## Rewards
- **The Gravecallers' endgame posture** (careful truth-keeper / catastrophic "destroy it" force / synthesis-seed ally).
- A decisive lever on the Breaking clock (#5) and the "destroy it" / "transform it" endgames.
- The Tallow Man's or Cole's loyalty; Knell as an ally; the cult's hidden knowledge in full.
- The cost-of-breaking truth (R4) as an endgame argument.

## Consequences
- **Stop it:** #5 eased; the cult becomes a careful ally; the catastrophe recedes; the "seal it" and "transform it" paths stay open.
- **Enable it:** #5 toward Final Consequence; the catastrophe begins (heavily telegraphed); the "destroy it" ending arrives violently and early — a transformed, harsher world the player must then navigate.
- **Steer it "done right":** the rare synthesis seed; requires broad alliances and deep play; the best and hardest outcome.
- **Expose/disrupt:** the strike is stopped from outside; the world is safe but the cult is broken and the dead's plea unanswered.
- **Ignore:** Cole strikes alone (after telegraphed warnings) — the catastrophe unfolds off the player's watch.

## Failure State
If the player can't hold the cult and Cole strikes uncontrolled, the catastrophe begins — a major, heavily-foreshadowed world consequence (the "destroy it" ending arriving messily). The player can still act on the aftermath (a transformed world, the boundary torn) in the endgame. Not a campaign-ending block — a dramatic, earned turn.

## Follow-Up Hooks
Toward Act 4-5: the Gravecallers as a "destroy it" / "transform it" bloc; the radical-Mourner alliance (QM4); the catastrophe (if triggered) as a new world-state; the drowned shrine endgame.

## Scaling Notes
- **L8-9:** the cache is small; Cole is a manageable threat; the catastrophe is distant.
- **L12-13:** Cole is armed, allied, and imminent; a strike is genuinely world-shaking; stopping it needs every ally and proof.
- Cap at R4; never name the Court (even at the shrine approach).
