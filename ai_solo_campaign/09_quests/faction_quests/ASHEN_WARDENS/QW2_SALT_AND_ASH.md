## AI Use
The trust-building link. The Wardens are running out of rite-salt as failures multiply; the player secures their lifeline and is brought into the order's confidence. Builds toward QW3's moral complication. Cap reveals at R1.

## Quest Type
Faction (Ashen Wardens) — trust-building task

## Level Range
3-5 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate. Toll-bandits, a smuggler, a fen hazard, optional Remembrance. Multiple noncombat paths.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Negotiate a salt price/escort with a Saltmargin pan-master: Persuasion DC 13.
- Spot a tampered or diverted shipment: Investigation DC 14.
- Talk past or evade toll-bandits: Persuasion/Deception/Stealth DC 13-15.
- Cross a flooded stretch of the Pale Road safely: Survival DC 13.

## Starting Hook
Hale (or Sirrin) is grim: "We bless the salt for every rite. The Coast's pans are short, the road's dear, and the buyers are hoarding. Without salt we can't lay anyone." The Wardens need a reliable rite-salt run secured — and they're trusting the player to do it.

## Location
The road from Hollowmere through Saltmargin to the Pale Coast salt-pans; the Ashwalk Rest as the delivery point.

## Involved NPCs
Hale/Sirrin (the ask), a Saltmargin pan-master or salt-warden (the source; see `Q_THE_SALT_RUN.md`), a Ledger buyer hoarding salt (obstacle), optional toll-bandit or smuggler ("Gull" Heddwyn).

## Involved Factions
Ashen Wardens (primary). Cinder Ledger (hoarding/diverting salt for profit — a friction point). Mourners' Circle (also needs salt; a potential ally on the road).

## Surface Problem
Secure and escort rite-salt to the Wardens before the next wave of failures outpaces them.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The salt shortage is partly the Ledger quietly cornering rite-salt as another "Concord-adjacent" commodity (a small M3/Monopoly tell). The Wardens' dependence on blessed salt is also a faint structural clue — the rites are a *deliberately incomplete* Concord technique (Secret, **not** revealed here).

## Objectives
1. Source rite-salt at the Coast/Saltmargin.
2. Get it past the obstacles (bandits, hoarders, the road).
3. Deliver it to the Wardens at the Ashwalk Rest.

## Possible Approaches
- **Social/economic:** out-talk or out-bid the Ledger buyer; broker a fair price.
- **Stealth:** move the salt quietly past the toll.
- **Investigation:** discover *why* salt is short (the Ledger's hoarding) and break it.
- **Combat (fallback):** fight off a bandit ambush — telegraphed, escapable.

## Obstacles
A Ledger buyer hoarding salt; toll-bandits on the road; a flooded road stretch; the temptation to skim the valuable salt for coin.

## Important Scenes
- Sirrin showing the player the order's near-empty salt store.
- A standoff with a Ledger buyer who "got there first."
- The grateful, exhausted Wardens receiving the salt — and Othetha, briefly present, taking the player's measure for the first time.

## Clues
- A small M3 tell: the Ledger is hoarding *rite-salt too*, not just relics (the buyer lets it slip).
- C-M5 continues: the towns nearest the basin are burning through salt fastest.

## Combat Options
Toll-bandits (the Tollmen or local toughs) — **Bandit/Thug-like, AC 12-13, ~16-22 HP**, morale breaks when outmatched. A smuggler — **Spy-like**. All telegraphed and avoidable. See `../../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Negotiation, stealth, and investigation resolve the run without a fight in nearly every case.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The road has safe waystations; bandits prefer toll to murder; the player can hire a guard or travel with a Mourner salt-party; retreat is always open.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest at Saltmargin, the Ashwalk Rest. Time pressure: each day's delay, a town's rites go un-salted (#3 advances; a Mourner plea fires).

## Rewards
- Deepened Warden trust (chain progress; eases #3 meaningfully).
- Othetha's first real notice of the player (gates later confession access).
- Coin/supplies; rite-salt for the player's own use; a Warden token granting safe-house access region-wide.

## Consequences
- **Success:** the Wardens are re-supplied; failures contained for now; Othetha begins to consider the player trustworthy.
- **Partial/skim:** the player pockets salt for coin — the Wardens notice the short delivery; trust dips (a redirect, not a break).
- **Ignore:** the salt shortage bites; a town's rites collapse (#3 and #4 advance; a grim follow-up scene).
- **Nonviolent:** ideal; the Ledger is out-maneuvered without bloodshed.
- **Excessive force:** killing the Ledger buyer brings Ledger pressure and a watch problem.

## Failure State
If the run fails, the Wardens ration salt and start refusing the worst sites (#3 to Stage 2). The chain continues; the player can try again or make it up in QW3.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QW3 The Rite That Won't Take; the Ledger's salt-hoarding folds into the Monopoly thread (#6); Othetha access opens.

## Scaling Notes
- **L3:** one bandit knot (~16 HP each); soften DCs by 1.
- **L5:** a coordinated ambush or a tougher Ledger blockade; raise DCs by 1-2.
- Keep the salt's structural hint oblique; no rite-secret reveal here.
