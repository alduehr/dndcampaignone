## AI Use
The internal-conflict + decision-point capstone. The Wardens fracture: young Wardens (Hale's generation) want to break silence and fight the crisis openly; the elders (Othetha's oath) counsel patience and secrecy. The player is trusted enough to tip it. This is the gate at which **Othetha may share a fragment of the true secret (Secret 8 seed)** — but only the *seed*, and only if trust is high; the full truth remains Act 3-4. Sets the Wardens' posture toward the "seal it" endgame and clock #3.

## Quest Type
Faction (Ashen Wardens) — internal conflict + decision point

## Level Range
7-10 (scales). **Solo danger:** low (mostly social) with one optional protective fight. The danger here is *choice*, not damage.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Mediate the young-vs-elder schism: Persuasion/Insight DC 16.
- Earn Othetha's fragment: Persuasion/Insight DC 16 **and** demonstrated chain-long respect for the dead — never a one-roll unlock.
- Talk Pell (or another doubter) back from leaving: Insight/Persuasion DC 15.
- Detect Reke's hand pressuring the Wardens (Compact underfunding as leverage): Investigation DC 16.

## Starting Hook
The order is breaking. After Greywater, Pell and the younger Wardens demand the elders "stop hiding" and petition the Compact openly for authority and aid. Othetha forbids it. Both sides ask the player — the one outsider both trust — to back them. Othetha sends a quiet word: "Come to the Rest. Before this tears us, there is something I can finally tell you. Some of it."

## Location
The Ashwalk Rest (the schism comes to a head) and Hollowmere (the Compact angle).

## Involved NPCs
Warden Sister Othetha (the oath; the fragment-giver), Warden Brother Hale (the young, torn between his elder-respect and his doubt), Warden Pell (the doubter ready to leave or rebel), Hospitaller Warden Sirrin (the practical middle).

## Involved Factions
Ashen Wardens (the schism is internal). Reachward Compact (Reke quietly worsens the rift by starving the Wardens of funds — a hidden pressure).

## Surface Problem
The Wardens are fracturing over secrecy versus open action. The player must help decide which way the order goes.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
Othetha's silence guards the seal: the order exists to keep the harvest paused, and going "open" risks exposing/reopening the shrine. **If trust is high, she shares the SEED of Secret 8** — that the shrine must never reopen and the order was founded to keep it sealed — *without* the full harvest/Custodian truth (that is Act 3-4 / R5-R6). Reke is covertly starving the Wardens of Compact funds to push them toward collapse (clock #3 toward the Court's benefit).

## Objectives
1. Hear out both factions of the schism (Othetha's oath vs. the young Wardens' demand).
2. Help resolve it: back patience, back openness, or broker a third way.
3. (Trust-gated) Receive Othetha's fragment of the truth.

## Possible Approaches
- **Back the elders (patience/secrecy):** the order holds, stays the seal's quiet guardian; #3 stabilizes; Othetha's fragment is most likely shared.
- **Back the young (open action):** the Wardens petition the Compact, gain resources and visibility — but risk drawing the Court's and Remnant's eyes to the shrine (#3 eased near-term, the basin made more contested).
- **Broker a third way:** the player carries Othetha's *reasons* (even partly) to the young, holding the order together without full exposure — the hardest, best outcome, requiring the fragment first.
- **Investigation:** uncover Reke's funding sabotage and remove that pressure (helps any resolution).

## Obstacles
Othetha's binding oath; Pell's bitterness; Hale's divided loyalty; Reke's invisible hand; the genuine merit of *both* sides (open action could help; secrecy protects the seal).

## Important Scenes
- The Rest at night: young Wardens and elders facing off; the player asked to speak.
- Othetha alone, choosing — for the first time in the campaign — to *almost* tell the truth, and giving the player the fragment with a warning to tell no one.
- Pell's fate: steadied, departed, or turned (toward the Gravecallers, if QW3 went their way).

## Clues
- **Secret 8 (SEED only):** the shrine must stay sealed; the order was founded to keep it so. **Not** the harvest/Custodian truth.
- Reke's funding sabotage (a fresh M2/Basin-clock lever pointing at the Compact infiltration).

## Combat Options
Optional and avoidable: if Reke's sabotage is exposed, a deniable thug or two may try to intimidate the player off it — **Thug/Spy-like, AC 13, ~30 HP**, breaks at resistance, never lethal-by-ambush. See `../../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
The entire quest can be resolved through dialogue, mediation, and investigation. This is a social/decision capstone.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
No mandatory combat; every NPC here is non-hostile to a trusted player; the fragment is gated on accumulated trust, not a single roll; if the player lacks trust, Othetha withholds the fragment and the chain still resolves (just without the reveal).

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest at the Rest. Time pressure: if left unresolved, the schism resolves *against* the order (Pell and others quit; #3 to Stage 3) — a worse default, not a block.

## Rewards
- **The Wardens' chosen posture** for the rest of the campaign (held-together-and-sealing, or open-and-contesting).
- **Othetha's fragment** (Secret 8 seed) — a major, earned step toward the "seal it" endgame and the Act 3 confession.
- Othetha as a committed ally; Hale's deepened arc; Pell resolved.
- Standing to call on the Wardens as a faction in the endgame.

## Consequences
- **Back elders / broker third way:** the order holds; the seal's guardians stay coherent; #3 stabilizes; strongest path to the "seal it" ending and Othetha's full Act 3 confession.
- **Back the young:** the Wardens gain resources and visibility but the shrine becomes more contested (Court and Remnant take note); a louder, riskier order.
- **Ignore:** the schism breaks the order (#3 to Stage 3-4); the seal's guardians scatter; the Master clock accelerates — a real, telegraphed cost.
- **Expose Reke's sabotage:** removes a hidden pressure on #3 and feeds the Basin-clock investigation (#2).

## Failure State
If the player has too little trust or ignores the schism, Othetha withholds the truth and the order fractures — the Wardens become a weakened, scattered presence rather than a coherent endgame ally. Recoverable later only with great effort. Not a campaign block; the "seal it" ending simply becomes harder.

## Follow-Up Hooks
Toward Act 3: Othetha's full confession (Secret 8 / R5-R6 — **gated**); the Wardens as the spine of the "seal it" endgame; Reke's exposure thread feeds the Compact chain and the Basin clock.

## Scaling Notes
- **L7-8:** the schism is heated but bloodless; the optional thug-scene is minor.
- **L9-10:** Reke's pressure is heavier; the thug-scene (if triggered) is a real fight; the fragment carries more weight as Act 3 nears.
- The fragment is the SEED only — never the full harvest/Custodian truth here.
