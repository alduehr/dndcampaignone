## AI Use
The moral-complication link. In a town where rites simply *fail*, the player must choose between the Wardens' patient way (lay them slowly, repeatedly, at terrible cost) and faster, darker options offered by desperate people. Delivers R2-adjacent insight (Remembrance behaves like a *substance being pulled*) without naming the harvest's authors. Reveals capped at R2.

## Quest Type
Faction (Ashen Wardens) — moral complication

## Level Range
5-7 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate. Multiple Remembrances over time; a possible mob; an optional Gravecaller confrontation. Strongly noncombat-favoring.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Perform repeated rites against the pull: Religion DC 15 (cumulative; the closer to the basin-edge, the harder).
- Hold a frightened town together: Persuasion/Insight DC 15.
- Resist or refute a Gravecaller's "faster way": Insight/Religion DC 14.
- Map why Greywater fails worst (M5 confirmation): Investigation DC 14.

## Starting Hook
Greywater Holm — the village where rites fail worst — sends for the Wardens, and the Wardens send the player (now trusted). Three of the Holm's dead will not stay laid. The Wardens' way works, but slowly, and each one fights to return. The town is exhausted and terrified, and a gentle Gravecaller recruiter is already among them, offering "a kinder way."

## Location
Greywater Holm and its fen-edge graveyard; the Drowned Blind nearby (Gravecaller presence).

## Involved NPCs
Hale (assists, fraying), Warden Pell (here, openly doubting — see `Q_PELLS_DOUBT.md`), Elder Mabb Tay (Mourner; the Holm's grief-keeper), Sister Knell (Gravecaller, gentle recruiter; the temptation), a bereaved family.

## Involved Factions
Ashen Wardens (primary). Mourners' Circle (the Holm's grief). Gravecallers (the alternative the player must weigh).

## Surface Problem
Three dead won't rest in a dying village. Lay them — but the Warden way is slow and costly, and faster ways are on offer.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
Greywater fails worst because it sits in the basin's pull (M5, now confirmable). The dead "fight to return" because the harvest draws them. The Gravecaller "kinder way" — letting the dead *speak* before release — genuinely soothes them but tightens the cult's hold and inches toward the catastrophic Breaking. **Do not reveal the harvest's authors; the player may now grasp that Remembrance behaves like something being pulled (R2-adjacent).**

## Objectives
1. Lay the three resistant dead (the Warden way) — or weigh the alternatives.
2. Keep the terrified town from panic or a mob.
3. Decide how to handle the Gravecaller recruiter (refuse, learn from, expose, or quietly allow).

## Possible Approaches
- **The Warden way (intended):** repeated patient rites; costly but clean.
- **Learn from the Gravecaller:** let the dead speak first (eases them, gains truth — and Gravecaller favor; Warden unease).
- **Social:** hold the town together; talk the bereaved through it.
- **Investigation:** confirm the basin-pull pattern and report it to Othetha.

## Obstacles
The pull (rites resist); the town's terror and a brewing mob; Pell's open doubt undermining morale; Knell's gentle, genuinely-helpful temptation; Hale's exhaustion.

## Important Scenes
- A rite that takes three attempts, each one harder, the dead weeping to stay.
- Knell, kind and sad, offering the family "one last word" — and it working.
- Pell, bitter: "We lay them and lay them and they come back. Nobody will tell me *why*. Do you know?"
- A choice at a graveside under a failing sky.

## Clues
- **C-M5 (confirmed):** Greywater fails worst because it is nearest the basin's pull.
- **R2-adjacent:** the dead behave like something *drawn*, not random; relics/songs/Gravecaller testimony converge.

## Combat Options
Resistant Remembrances — **specter/wraith-like, AC 13, ~38-52 HP**, sorrowful but capable of harm if a rite is botched and they break loose. Telegraphed; layable. A panicked mob is a social hazard, not a kill-box. See `../../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Rites, the Gravecaller method, and social work resolve every beat. Combat is a failure-branch fallback.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Hale and Pell assist; the dead are telegraphed as sorrowful; the player can fetch Othetha or more Wardens; retreat from a botched rite is allowed; the Gravecaller option is a genuine (if costly) safety valve, not a trap.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Rest at the Holm (tense) or withdraw to the Ashwalk Rest. Time pressure: each unlaid night, fear rises toward a mob and the Mourners' Circle clock (#4) ticks.

## Rewards
- Major Warden trust (the gate to QW4 and, eventually, Othetha's confession).
- Deep M5 confirmation; R2-adjacent insight.
- The Holm's gratitude (a rural safe harbor); Mourner goodwill (Mabb Tay).
- If the Gravecaller path is taken: a forbidden truth-fragment (and Gravecaller favor, Warden unease) — a real trade-off.

## Consequences
- **Warden way:** the dead rest clean; Warden trust peaks; Knell leaves disappointed; clock #5 (Gravecallers) eased locally.
- **Gravecaller way:** the dead are eased and *heard*; the player gains truth and Gravecaller favor but the cult's hold on the Holm tightens (#5 advances); Pell is fascinated, Othetha troubled.
- **Ignore:** the Holm's dead overwhelm it; a family flees or a mob forms; #4/#5 advance; the village slides toward abandonment.
- **Nonviolent:** expected and rewarded.
- **Excessive force:** fighting the dead horrifies the Mourners and Wardens; trust stalls.

## Failure State
A failed handling leaves Greywater haunted and half-emptied — a standing consequence and a vivid M5 monument, not a block. The chain continues; the player's choice here colors QW4.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QW4 The Oath and the Doubt; `Q_PELLS_DOUBT.md` resolves toward Pell leaving, breaking, or being steadied; Knell may seek the player again (Gravecaller chain).

## Scaling Notes
- **L5:** two resistant dead (~38 HP); Hale/Pell carry a rite.
- **L7:** three dead (~52 HP), a near-mob, a relic complication; the pull is stronger.
- Cap reveals at R2; never name the harvest's authors.
