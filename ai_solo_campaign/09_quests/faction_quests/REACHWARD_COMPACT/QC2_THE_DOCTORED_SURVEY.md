## AI Use
The trust-building link. Coalmont (or the player's own suspicion) tasks the player with checking the basin-drainage survey — and the records don't add up. The player earns Coalmont's deep trust and finds the first hard proof the basin scheme is built on doctored numbers (Secret 9 seed; C-M2/M4). Reke's name attaches to the doctoring, but his *true* nature stays hidden. Reveals capped at R2.

## Quest Type
Faction (Reachward Compact) — trust-building task

## Level Range
3-6 (scales). **Solo danger:** low-to-moderate. Intrigue-primary; an optional deniable-thug intimidation.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Audit the survey records: Investigation DC 14.
- Coax the truth from frightened Clerk Pevin Oss: Persuasion/Insight DC 14.
- Survey the basin yourself to compare: Survival/Investigation DC 15.
- Detect you're being watched: Perception/Insight DC 14.

## Starting Hook
Coalmont, uneasy about the basin project's costs, asks the player to quietly verify the survey behind it: "The water-rights numbers smell wrong, and I can't ask the watch — half of them answer to the project. Check the records. Be discreet." (Or the player, suspicious from QC1, initiates this themselves.)

## Location
The Hollowmere council records office (Pevin Oss's domain) and the basin shore for comparison.

## Involved NPCs
Reeve Marda Coalmont (the cautious patron), Clerk Pevin Oss (the frightened keeper of the doctored filings; the proof), Councilor Aldous Reke (whose surveys are doctored; appears, smooth and watchful), Bailiff Aldwin Cray (a grey figure who may help or hinder).

## Involved Factions
Reachward Compact (primary, internal). Cinder Ledger (funds the project; a background interest).

## Surface Problem
Verify whether the basin survey is honest. It isn't — and proving it is dangerous.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The basin "water-rights" justification is **partly fabricated** and Reke's surveys are **doctored** to disguise the scheme's real purpose (reopening the shrine). Pevin Oss holds the proof (C-M2-3 / C-M4-2) but is terrified of Reke. **This exposes Reke as corrupt (Secret 9 seed), NOT as a Hollow Court agent** — that deeper truth is far later. The basin is not actually needed for resettlement (the player can confirm by surveying it). Cap at R2.

## Objectives
1. Audit the survey and find the discrepancies.
2. Get Pevin Oss to talk (he needs protection/courage).
3. Confirm the basin isn't really needed for resettlement.
4. Decide what to do with the proof.

## Possible Approaches
- **Investigation:** audit the records; cross-check against the real basin survey.
- **Social:** win Pevin's trust; promise him cover from Reke.
- **Exploration:** survey the basin to disprove the "water-rights" claim.
- **Stealth:** copy the doctored filings without tipping off Reke.

## Obstacles
Pevin's terror of Reke; the records' deliberate obfuscation; the dawning sense of being watched; the temptation to confront Reke too early (he's untouchable for now).

## Important Scenes
- Pevin, white-faced, admitting "the numbers were changed — and I filed them, God help me."
- The basin survey vs. the filed one: the lie made plain.
- Reke, all warmth, "just checking how the audit's going" — a velvet warning.

## Clues
- **C-M2-3 / C-M4-2:** the doctored basin filings (Secret 9 seed — Reke is corrupt).
- The basin isn't needed for resettlement (the "water rights" pretext is false).

## Combat Options
Optional and avoidable: if the player gets too loud, a deniable thug or two may try to "encourage discretion" — **Thug/Spy-like, AC 13, ~22-30 HP**, breaks at resistance, never lethal-by-ambush; the trail leads back toward the project, not (yet) Reke directly. See `../../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
The quest is an audit and a social rescue of a frightened witness — winnable with no combat.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
The danger is intrigue, not damage; Pevin is a non-combatant who needs protecting, not fighting; the player can copy proof and withdraw; Coalmont offers political cover.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Hollowmere. Time pressure: the basin vote (QC4 / clock #2) creeps closer; the longer the audit takes, the more momentum the scheme gains.

## Rewards
- **Hard proof the basin scheme is corrupt** (Secret 9 seed; a power-card for QC4 and the Basin clock #2).
- Coalmont's deep trust; Pevin Oss as a protected ally/witness.
- Standing to oppose the basin scheme politically.

## Consequences
- **Get the proof + protect Pevin:** the player can challenge the scheme (QC4 strengthened); #2 can be slowed; Coalmont is a firm ally.
- **Proof but Pevin exposed:** the witness is silenced or flees (Reke's deniable pressure); the proof is weaker; #2 harder to slow.
- **Ignore:** the scheme rolls on unchallenged (#2 advances); the player misses the clearest early handle on the master clock.
- **Confront Reke too early:** he deflects with perfect charm and tightens security; the player tips their hand (a setback, not a block).
- **Excessive force:** violence draws the watch and Reke's political reprisal.

## Failure State
If the proof is lost or Pevin is silenced, the basin scheme proceeds with less resistance (#2 advances) — but the doctored-survey truth remains reachable via the basin survey itself and Cray's grey knowledge (three-clue rule). Not a block.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QC3 The Reed Holms Eviction; QC4 The Basin Vote; `Q_THE_BAILIFFS_LADDER.md` (Compact politics); the proof feeds the Basin clock (#2) and, much later, Reke's full exposure.

## Scaling Notes
- **L3:** a straightforward audit; light intimidation.
- **L6:** layered obfuscation, a watched witness, real deniable muscle.
- Cap at R2; Reke is exposed as *corrupt*, never as a Court agent here.
