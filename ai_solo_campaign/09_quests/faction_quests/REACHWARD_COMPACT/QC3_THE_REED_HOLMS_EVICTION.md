## AI Use
The moral-complication link. The basin works require evicting the fen-families of the Reed Holms; the Compact orders the player to enforce it. The player must choose between Compact loyalty (carry out a cruel, legally-valid eviction that serves the scheme) and conscience (resist, delay, or sabotage it). Drives the Basin clock (#2) and the player's standing with the Mourners/Wardens. Reveals capped at R2-R3.

## Quest Type
Faction (Reachward Compact) — moral complication

## Level Range
5-8 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate. A resistant community, fen hazards, optional violence — all telegraphed; heavy noncombat options.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Persuade the Holm families to leave / negotiate terms: Persuasion DC 15.
- Find a legal flaw to delay the eviction: Investigation DC 16.
- Cross/secure the flooded Holms: Survival DC 14.
- Resist Reke's pressure to "just get it done": Insight DC 15.

## Starting Hook
The basin works can't proceed until the Reed Holms — fen-island families who've lived there for generations — are cleared. The Compact (pushed by Reke) hands the player the eviction order: "It's legal, it's funded, and frankly it's kinder coming from you than from the watch." The families have nowhere to go, and the order serves a scheme the player may now suspect is rotten.

## Location
The Reed Holms (basin-shore fen islands) and Hollowmere (the order's source).

## Involved NPCs
Councilor Aldous Reke (pushes the eviction; the scheme's hidden hand), Reeve Marda Coalmont (uneasy, looking for a way out), the Holm families (proud, frightened, rooted), Old Sashe (fen-guide; knows the Holms and the basin), Sergeant Hesk (would rather not do this).

## Involved Factions
Reachward Compact (primary). Mourners' Circle (the Holms are theirs; furious). Cinder Ledger (funds the works; impatient).

## Surface Problem
Evict the Reed Holms families to clear the basin works. Legal, ordered — and morally wrenching.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The eviction exists only because the basin scheme (Reke/Court) needs the shore cleared to reopen the shrine — there's no honest resettlement need (confirmed in QC2). Carrying it out advances the Basin clock (#2) and the master harvest clock (#1). Sabotaging or delaying it *slows the master clock*. **The player still doesn't know about the Court; they're choosing between a corrupt scheme and the families. Cap at R2-R3.**

## Objectives
1. Decide whether to enforce, negotiate, delay, or sabotage the eviction.
2. Handle the families (and the Mourners' fury) accordingly.
3. Manage the consequences with the Compact, Reke, and the basin clock.

## Possible Approaches
- **Enforce (Compact loyalty):** clear the Holms by law/persuasion; #2 advances; the player serves the scheme (and gains Reke's favor, the Mourners' hatred).
- **Negotiate:** win the families better terms and a real place to go (humane; slows the works a little; Coalmont grateful).
- **Delay (legal/bureaucratic):** find a flaw, stall the eviction (slows #2; frustrates Reke).
- **Sabotage/resist:** refuse or actively obstruct (slows #2 sharply; makes Reke a quiet enemy; wins the Mourners and the families).

## Obstacles
The eviction's legal validity; Reke's pressure and favor; the families' rootedness; the Mourners' mob potential; the fen's hazards; the genuine bind (the player's job vs. the families' lives).

## Important Scenes
- The Holms at dusk: old people, children, a way of life — and an order in the player's hand.
- Reke, all reason: "Sentiment is expensive. The Reach needs this done."
- Coalmont, quietly: "If you found a *reason* to delay this... I wouldn't look too hard at it."

## Clues
- **R2-R3 confirmation:** the eviction serves the basin scheme, not resettlement (the lie made personal).
- **#2 lever:** enforcing advances the master infrastructure clock; resisting slows it.

## Combat Options
Optional and avoidable: if the player resists and Reke escalates, deniable enforcers or impatient Ledger-funded toughs appear — **Thug/Guard/Veteran-like, AC 13-16, ~22-45 HP**, telegraphed, escapable. The families are non-combatants. See `../../regional_quests/Q_THE_REED_HOLMS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Negotiation, legal delay, and quiet sabotage resolve the quest without harming anyone.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
No forced combat against the families; the Mourners and Wardens will back a player who protects the Holms; legal/bureaucratic delay is a fully valid path; the player can refuse the job at the cost of Compact/Reke favor.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Rest before. Time pressure: the works press forward; each delay the player wins slows #2, each day's enforcement speeds it.

## Rewards
- A defining moral choice that sets the player's standing with the Compact, the Mourners, and the basin clock.
- If humane/resisting: the Holm families and the Mourners as devoted allies; #2 slowed (a direct brake on the master clock).
- If enforcing: Reke's favor and Compact standing (useful, compromising).
- Old Sashe's deepened trust (fen access) if the player protects the Holms.

## Consequences
- **Enforce:** #2 advances toward Stage 2; the Mourners hate the player; Reke trusts them more (and steers harder); the master clock ticks.
- **Negotiate/delay:** #2 slowed; Coalmont and the families grateful; Reke frustrated.
- **Sabotage/resist:** #2 slowed sharply; the Mourners and Holms devoted; Reke becomes a quiet enemy (deniable reprisals later).
- **Ignore:** the watch does the eviction brutally (#2 advances; worse for the families) — the player loses the chance to soften or stop it.
- **Excessive force:** harming the families is a moral catastrophe; the Mourners turn militant (#4) and the Wardens recoil.

## Failure State
If the eviction goes ahead brutally (whether by the player or by default), the Holms are scattered and the basin works gain momentum (#2 advances) — a heavy, lasting consequence that strengthens Reke's hand for QC4. Not a block; the player can still fight the scheme at the vote.

## Follow-Up Hooks
QC4 The Basin Vote; the Mourners' Circle chain (their fury at the basin scheme); Reke's hidden enmity (if resisted); Old Sashe and the fen-families as allies.

## Scaling Notes
- **L5:** a small, sympathetic community; light pressure.
- **L8:** organized enforcement, heavy Reke/Ledger pressure, higher stakes.
- Cap at R2-R3; the Court stays hidden.
