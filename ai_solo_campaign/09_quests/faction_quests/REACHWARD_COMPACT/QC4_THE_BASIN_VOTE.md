## AI Use
The internal-conflict + decision-point capstone, and the player's single most powerful brake on the master clock. The Compact's split (Coalmont's cautious bloc vs. Reke's "progress" bloc) comes to a head in a council vote on whether to fund and complete the basin-drainage project. The player can swing the vote, expose Reke as corrupt (Secret 9), or even help the scheme. Directly sets the Basin clock (#2) and thus the Harvest clock (#1). Reveals capped at R3-R4 (Reke as corrupt/dangerous; his Court allegiance only hinted, never confirmed here).

## Quest Type
Faction (Reachward Compact) — internal conflict + decision point

## Level Range
7-12 (scales). **Solo danger:** moderate (political intrigue with optional reprisal violence). The danger is the *vote's consequence* — it can accelerate the master clock.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Sway undecided councilors: Persuasion DC 16 (modified by accumulated proof/allies).
- Expose Reke's corruption to the council: Persuasion/Investigation DC 17 (Secret 9; needs QC2 proof).
- Counter Reke's rhetoric in open debate: Persuasion/Insight DC 17.
- Survive/deflect Reke's reprisal if exposed: Insight/Investigation DC 16.

## Starting Hook
The basin-drainage project comes to a binding council vote: fund it to completion, or halt it. Coalmont's cautious bloc and Reke's "progress" bloc are deadlocked, and both sides court the player — now a known Compact hand with proof, allies, and reputation. Reke is smooth and certain; Coalmont is honest and outmatched. The vote will decide how fast the basin drains — and the player holds the swing.

## Location
The Hollowmere council hall (the vote) and its corridors (the lobbying).

## Involved NPCs
Reeve Marda Coalmont (the cautious bloc; ally), Councilor Aldous Reke (the "progress" bloc; the infiltrator), Clerk Pevin Oss (the witness, if protected in QC2), undecided councilors (improv/minor; the swing votes), Cinder Ledger factor (lobbying for the works).

## Involved Factions
Reachward Compact (the vote is internal). Cinder Ledger (funds and lobbies for the works). Mourners' Circle (mobilizes against the scheme). The Hollow Court (DM-only: Reke's vote, and thus the master clock, hinge here).

## Surface Problem
A council vote on the basin project. The player swings it — and the outcome speeds or slows the campaign's central threat.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
This vote is the cleanest, earliest *lever on the master clock*: halting the project sets back #2 (and #1); funding it advances both toward shrine-exposure. Reke can be exposed as **corrupt** (Secret 9) using QC2's proof — discrediting him and his bloc — but his **Hollow Court allegiance stays hidden** (that's Act 4 / R7). If exposed, Reke retreats but is not destroyed; he works other angles (the basin can still be pushed by the Ledger or a successor). **Cap at R3-R4; never confirm the Court here.**

## Objectives
1. Choose a side: halt the project, fund it, or use the vote for another aim.
2. Sway the undecided councilors (with proof, allies, rhetoric, or pressure).
3. Optionally expose Reke's corruption.
4. Manage the fallout (Reke's reprisal; the Mourners; the basin clock).

## Possible Approaches
- **Halt the project (slow the master clock):** rally Coalmont's bloc, present QC2 proof, mobilize the Mourners, win the vote — #2 set back, the campaign's central threat slowed. The strongest "seal it"-aligned outcome.
- **Expose Reke:** use the doctored-survey proof to discredit him and his bloc (Secret 9); halts the project *and* removes a key Court instrument (without naming the Court).
- **Fund the project (advance the master clock):** back Reke's bloc — #2 advances, the shrine nears exposure; a dark path (serving the scheme, knowingly or not).
- **Leverage the vote:** trade the player's swing for a different prize (Compact authority, a charter, an ally) — a self-interested third path.

## Obstacles
Reke's brilliance and standing; the undecided councilors' self-interest; the Ledger's lobbying money; the difficulty of proving corruption against a respected man; Reke's reprisal if cornered.

## Important Scenes
- The council hall, deadlocked, every eye turning to the player's word.
- Reke's masterful speech for "progress and water and the future of the Reach."
- The reveal (if the player has the proof): Pevin's doctored filings laid on the table; Reke's composure cracking — just slightly.
- The count, and the basin's fate hanging on it.

## Clues
- **Secret 9 (Reke is corrupt):** provable with QC2's doctored-survey proof.
- **#2/#1 lever:** the vote's outcome directly sets the master infrastructure clock.

## Combat Options
Optional and avoidable: if Reke is exposed and cornered, his deniable agents may move against the player or the witness afterward — **Spy/Veteran/Mage-lite-like, AC 13-16, ~30-45 HP** (Reke himself flees rather than fights cheaply; see his profile). Telegraphed; escapable. See `../../../07_factions/major_factions/REACHWARD_COMPACT.md` (Reke's mechanics).

## Noncombat Options
This is a political capstone — winnable entirely through lobbying, evidence, rhetoric, and alliances.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
No forced combat; the vote is decided by persuasion and proof, not arms; Coalmont, the Mourners, and (if protected) Pevin back the player; Reke's reprisals are deniable, telegraphed, and never cheap-lethal; losing the vote is a setback, not a block.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Rest before the vote. The vote itself is the time-pressure climax: once called, the player must act with whatever proof and allies they've gathered across the chain.

## Rewards
- **Direct control of the Basin clock (#2)** — the player's clearest brake (or accelerator) on the master harvest clock (#1).
- If Reke is exposed: a discredited infiltrator and a Compact freed of his steering (a major endgame asset).
- Coalmont's total trust; the Compact as a "seal it"-aligned ally; the Mourners' gratitude.
- Standing as the Reach's political power-broker.

## Consequences
- **Halt / expose Reke:** #2 set back (and #1 slowed); Reke retreats and works other angles; the Seal Bloc and the "seal it" endgame strengthen; the player is the Compact's hero.
- **Fund the project:** #2 advances toward Stage 2-3; the shrine nears exposure; the master clock accelerates; the player has served the scheme (the dark/"restart" trajectory).
- **Leverage:** the player gains a personal prize but the basin question stays live (the clock unset).
- **Ignore / lose the vote:** Reke's bloc wins by default; #2 advances; the player missed their best handle on the master clock.
- **Excessive force:** open violence in the council shatters the player's political standing and brings the watch.

## Failure State
If the player loses the vote or fails to expose Reke, the basin project is funded and the works press toward the shrine (#2/#1 advance) — a major setback that makes the "restart" trajectory the strong default. Recoverable later (sabotage the works directly, ally the Wardens/Mourners, expose Reke another way), but the campaign's central threat has gained ground. Not a campaign-ending block; the player has simply lost a battle, not the war.

## Follow-Up Hooks
Toward Act 3-4: Reke's full exposure (R7 / Court allegiance — **gated**); the basin works as a recurring sabotage target; the Compact as a "seal it" ally bloc; whoever the player traded their swing to.

## Scaling Notes
- **L7-8:** a tight vote; exposing Reke is hard but possible with QC2 proof.
- **L11-12:** the basin is nearly funded to completion; halting it requires every ally and proof the player has gathered; Reke's reprisal is fiercer.
- Cap at R3-R4; never confirm the Court.
