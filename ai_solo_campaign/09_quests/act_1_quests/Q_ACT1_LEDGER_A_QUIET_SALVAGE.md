## AI Use

The **Cinder Ledger Act 1 alignment quest** — how a player who takes the bank's coin experiences levels 1–4. Factor Domic Sael hires the player to fetch Concord salvage (the Peat Chapel relic), then to run further "quiet jobs," building a comfortable, compromising relationship with the region's money. Delivers the **M3 *fragment*** (a relic is uncanny — it shows a dead face) **without** the Act 2 reveal that it *stores a person.* Domic's unease is seeded as a future lever (Secret 4).

## Quest Type
Faction (Cinder Ledger) — exploration / fetch-and-deliver with moral weight

## Level Range
1–4 (scales). **Solo danger:** low (the first delve) rising to moderate. Telegraphed single threats; retreat routes; the first dungeon is built as a confidence-builder.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Find/navigate the Peat Chapel: Survival DC 12 (or a local points the way).
- Deal with the chapel's lurking threat: see Combat Options (avoidable).
- Recognize the relic is *uncanny* (M3 fragment): Arcana/Religion DC 13 (a feeling at DC 10; details at 15).
- Haggle Domic up / read his unease: Persuasion or Insight DC 13.
- (Later jobs) Spot the Ledger's thumb on the scale (`Q_LIGHT_ON_THE_SCALE.md`): Investigation DC 14.

## Starting Hook
Factor Domic Sael, all ink-stained warmth: "Between us — there's a piece of old Concord stone out at the sunk chapel in the fen. The Ledger wants it. Fetch it, bring it here, no questions, and there's good coin and more work where that came from. Watch the damp, and maybe a beast."

## Location
Hollowmere counting-house (Domic); **The Peat Chapel** (`THE_PEAT_CHAPEL.md`) — a small, telegraphed, half-sunk Concord wayside chapel a few hours into the fen, built as the player's **first dungeon** at level 1.

## Involved NPCs
Factor Domic Sael (patron; secretly uneasy — Secret 4), Tallytooth Ren (Ledger enforcer, looms in later/harder jobs), Mother Calla Vyre (distant matriarch; a name, not a presence, in Act 1), Old Sashe (can guide to the chapel), Mourner-keepers (who resent relic-buying).

## Involved Factions
Cinder Ledger (patron); Mourners' Circle (morally opposed to selling grave-relics); Gravecallers (also want such relics — a possible rival at the chapel).

## Surface Problem
Retrieve a specific piece of Concord salvage and deliver it for pay; then do more quiet, profitable jobs for the bank.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The "salvage" is a genuine **Remembrance relic** — it *stores a dead person* (M3). The Ledger is cornering the market on these to sell immortality (Monopoly clock #6; Secrets 3/12). **Act 1 reveals only that the relic is *uncanny*** — cold, wrong, showing a dead face. The substance-truth (R2/M3) is Act 2. Domic disapproves of Vyre's relic obsession and is quietly documenting it (Secret 4 — a future turn-lever, dormant in Act 1).

## Objectives
1. Reach the Peat Chapel and recover the relic.
2. Decide what to do with it: deliver it, study it, sell it elsewhere, give it to the Mourners, or keep it.
3. (If continuing) take further Ledger jobs and read where the work is leading.

## Possible Approaches
- **Exploration:** delve the chapel; deal with its single threat; take the relic.
- **Stealth:** slip past the lair; grab and go.
- **Social:** hire/persuade someone else to fetch it; or buy/trick it from a scavenger who beat you there.
- **Moral pivot:** hand the relic to the Mourners or Gravecallers instead of the Ledger (a faction-defining choice with consequences).

## Obstacles
The fen-damp and the chapel's lurking scavenger/beast; the relic's wrongness (an unsettling moment, possible Wisdom save vs. dread); a rival (a Gravecaller or scavenger) who also wants it; the moral weight of selling the dead's leavings (Mourner disapproval).

## Important Scenes
- The tilted star-spire across the fen; the canted nave; the flooded vestry and its lair.
- The altar recess: lifting a smooth grey stone that, held, shows the **face of a long-dead traveler** — uncanny, cold, *almost* a person. The first real touch of the campaign's central horror.
- Domic taking delivery with a too-quick smile and a flicker of something like guilt ("the matriarch will be pleased… don't think too hard about it").

## Clues
- **C-M3-1 (fragment)** — the relic is uncanny; it shows a dead face (full M3 = Act 2). See `ACT_1_CLUE_TRAILS.md`.
- **Seed of Secret 4** — Domic's unease (a future lever; do not resolve in Act 1).

## Combat Options
The Peat Chapel's threat: a lone **fen scavenger-beast (giant frog/constrictor-like, CR 1/4–1, AC 11–13, ~10–20 HP)** OR, if a rival contests it, a **scavenger/Gravecaller scout (Bandit/Cultist-like, AC 12, ~11 HP)** — telegraphed, avoidable, breaks morale at half. If the player provokes the relic or lingers, a weak **chapel Remembrance (specter-like, AC 12, ~17 HP)** may stir. See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/ACT_1_THREATS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Stealth past the lair; social-fetch via a third party; buy the relic off a scavenger; or avoid combat entirely with care. The relic itself is taken, not fought.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
A tiny, scoutable, escapable first dungeon with one telegraphed threat and clear retreat; Old Sashe can guide; Domic's job-brief warns of the danger; no time-lock on the delve.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Hollowmere before/after; a short rest is feasible in the chapel if cleared. Mild time pressure: a rival may reach the relic first if the player delays (reroutes the clue, doesn't block it). Continuing Ledger work feeds the Monopoly clock (#6).

## Rewards
- **Good coin** (the Ledger pays well — the lure) and a standing offer of more work.
- **Domic's goodwill** and, over time, access to gossip and (eventually, if turned) the counting-house vault relic.
- The **relic itself** if the player keeps it (an Act 2 M3 study-object; uncanny, possibly hazardous — thin-touch risk if mishandled).
- A loan line / financial cushion (with the hook of debt).

## Consequences
- **If the player delivers to the Ledger:** paid and trusted; the Monopoly clock (#6) ticks; the Mourners cool toward the player.
- **If the player keeps/studies the relic:** banks an Act 2 M3 lead; Domic is mildly put out; thin-touch risk if reckless.
- **If the player gives it to the Mourners/Gravecallers:** earns those factions; burns Ledger trust (Domic disappointed, Vyre noting the loss) — a redirect, not a block.
- **If the player ignores the job:** Domic hires someone else; the relic still surfaces in the world (Act 2); no loss of truth.
- **If the player uses excessive force** (e.g., robs a rival, kills a scavenger needlessly): a watch matter or a feud seed; the Ledger doesn't mind, but the town notices.

## Failure State
If the delve fails (driven off, relic lost to a rival), the player retreats with the chapel's *image* and a sense of wrongness — a clue, not a defeat. The relic resurfaces via the Ledger's wider buying or a rival faction; M3 remains reachable by three other paths. The Ledger offers an easier follow-up job to keep the relationship warm.

## Follow-Up Hooks
`Q_LIGHT_ON_THE_SCALE.md` (the Ledger's crooked weights — Domic's unease deepens); Hook 10 "Debts and the Drowned" (the human cost of Ledger debt); toward Act 2, studying the relic (R2/M3) and turning Domic (Secret 4).

## Scaling Notes
- **L1:** a single weak beast; soften DCs by 1–2; the relic moment is the centerpiece, not a fight.
- **L3–4:** add a contesting rival (Gravecaller/Remnant scout) or a stirred Remembrance; a tougher chapel; raise the relic's thin-touch risk if mishandled.
- Cap M3 at the *fragment* (uncanny relic) in Act 1.
