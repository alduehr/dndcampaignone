## AI Use

The **Mourners' Circle Act 1 alignment quest** — how a player who honors the dead earns the grief-keepers' trust over levels 1–4. Grandmother Wend tests the player through service (the salt shortage, tending a rite, refusing to sell the dead), opening the door to the Circle's deepest asset: the old songs (a major late clue, **gated**). Delivers R1 via the folk/M5 angle without revealing the songs' true content (M6 — Act 3).

## Quest Type
Faction (Mourners' Circle) — social / service / trust-building

## Level Range
1–4 (scales). **Solo danger:** low. Service, social, and short travel; any danger is a telegraphed road/fen hazard with Sashe available.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Earn a first measure of Wend's trust (show you honor the dead): roleplay; no single roll — judged by *deeds*.
- Secure or recover the rite-salt (travel/social): Persuasion DC 13 or Investigation DC 13; smuggler haggle DC 13.
- Sing/assist a passing rite correctly: Performance or Religion DC 13 (Wend coaches).
- Coax a *fragment* of an old song from Wend: Persuasion/Insight DC 14 **plus** earned trust — never a one-roll unlock.
- Notice the salt runs short *because rites keep failing* (M5): Insight/Investigation DC 13.

## Starting Hook
Grandmother Wend, dirt under her nails, eyes too sharp for comfort: "The rite-salt's run thin, child, and the dead don't wait on shortages. There's a load promised off the Pale Coast — fetch it, or find who's holding it back. Do it kindly. I'll be watching how you treat the dead along the way." (Or: the player meets Iola first, restless and grieving, and is drawn in through her.)

## Location
Hollowmere (Mourner's Green; Wend's cottage); the salt road toward Saltmargin / the Pale Coast; possibly a Greyfens detour with Sashe. Tie-in: `Q_THE_SALT_RUN.md`.

## Involved NPCs
Grandmother Wend (eldest; the gatekeeper to the songs), Iola Wend (restless apprentice; secret Gravecaller bridge — dormant in Act 1), Bann Oester (grave-digger; grave-drift witness), Old Sashe (road/fen guide), Saltmargin salt-folk and smugglers (improv/secondary).

## Involved Factions
Mourners' Circle (patron); Cinder Ledger (whose relic-buying the Circle despises — a tension the player navigates).

## Surface Problem
The rite-salt is running out as failures spike. Fetch or recover a fresh load, tend the dead well along the way, and prove yourself to the grief-keepers.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The salt shortage is downstream of the failing rites (M5). The Circle is the campaign's moral conscience; Wend carries the folk-memory that "the Concord ate the dead," encoded in old songs (M6, Secret 5). **Act 1 reveals none of the songs' content** — only that Wend *has* something she shares solely with the worthy, and that rites fail worse near the basin (R1/M5). Some elders quietly stockpile shrine-breaking tools (Clock #4 — hidden).

## Objectives
1. Earn a first measure of Wend's trust through deeds.
2. Resolve the salt shortage (fetch, recover, or arrange a supply).
3. Tend at least one rite well along the way.
4. (Optional, late Act 1) Receive a *fragment* of an old song — beautiful, sad, not yet decoded.

## Possible Approaches
- **Social:** persuade a Pale Coast trader; mediate with a smuggler (Gull) holding the shipment.
- **Investigation:** find why the salt is short / who's hoarding it (the Ledger's hand, or simple scarcity).
- **Travel/Exploration:** run the salt road (with Sashe if the fens are involved); handle a telegraphed hazard.
- **Service:** simply *be present* and reverent at rites — the surest path to Wend's trust.

## Obstacles
Wend's scrutiny (trust is earned slowly, by behavior); a smuggler or trader holding the salt for leverage; the road's hazards; the Ledger's relic-buying souring the player's standing if they've taken Ledger coin; Iola's restlessness pulling at the Circle's patience.

## Important Scenes
- Wend washing a body and singing low, watching to see if the player looks away or helps.
- A rite the player can assist or fumble; the family's gratitude or grief either way.
- The salt secured — and, if trust is earned, Wend by candlelight humming the *first lines* of an old song, then stopping: "The rest is for when you've earned it, and not before."
- Iola, frustrated, asking the player whether *just mourning* is really enough (the Gravecaller seed — do not resolve).

## Clues
- **C-M5** (folk angle) — rites/salt fail worse near the basin; Wend and Bann Oester have noticed the dead "leaning waterward." See `ACT_1_CLUE_TRAILS.md`.
- **C-M1-3 / C-M6-4 (seed only)** — the old songs *exist* and matter; their meaning is Act-3 gated.

## Combat Options
**None required.** Road/fen hazards are checks, not fights. If a smuggler standoff turns hot, **Spy/Bandit-like (AC 12–13, ~11–22 HP)**, telegraphed, morale-prone — but negotiation is the intended path. See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/ACT_1_THREATS.md` / Reach encounter tables.

## Noncombat Options
The whole quest is social, investigative, and service-based. This is a near-pure noncombat alignment path.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
A safe base and a patient patron; Old Sashe for any fen leg; the salt road is scoutable; trust is earned by behavior, so no single failed roll locks the player out of the Circle.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Hollowmere/Saltmargin. Time pressure: the **Circle Turns Militant clock (#4)** — prolonged neglect of the dead (or visible Ledger alignment) hardens the Circle's grief into anger. Helping eases it.

## Rewards
- **Wend's trust** (the gate to the old songs — the campaign's most important Mourner clue, *banked* for Act 3).
- Deep **community goodwill** in Hollowmere (the Mourners' word carries weight).
- Rite-salt, blessings, and folk protections (minor, flavorful boons; possibly advantage on a rite, or a charm vs. the restless dead — light, level-appropriate).
- Iola as a future contact (a door to the Gravecallers later, if the player wants it).

## Consequences
- **If the player succeeds:** the Circle embraces the player; clock #4 eased; the songs become reachable (Act 3); Hollowmere warms.
- **If the player partially succeeds:** the salt comes late or short; rites suffer; Wend remains guarded.
- **If the player ignores it / sides with the Ledger:** the Circle cools or turns hostile; #4 advances toward militancy; Iola drifts toward the Gravecallers — a redirect (the Gravecaller door opens) not a dead end.
- **If the player solves it nonviolently and reverently:** ideal; maximal trust.
- **If the player profanes the dead** (robs graves, sells relics, mocks a rite): the Circle marks the player; Wend's door closes (other M5/M6 paths remain).

## Failure State
If the salt fails or the player alienates the Circle, rites across town worsen and the Circle hardens — a visible, world-changing consequence and an M5 data-point, not a block. Wend's songs stay out of reach, but M5 (Sashe, graves, Wardens) and M6 (Act 3, archives/Tallow Man) remain reachable by other paths. The player can still re-earn the Circle slowly through later reverence.

## Follow-Up Hooks
`Q_THE_SALT_RUN.md` (the wider salt crisis); the Mourners-vs-Ledger relic conflict (Hook 10); Iola's bridge to the Gravecallers (Hook 7); toward Act 3, the fully-decoded songs (M6 — **gated**).

## Scaling Notes
- **L1–2:** soften DCs by 1; the test is mostly behavioral; a simple fetch.
- **L3–4:** add a smuggler standoff or a road haunting; the salt shortage is acute (#4 nearer Stage 2); Iola's restlessness sharper.
- Never decode the songs in Act 1.
