## AI Use

The **Concord Remnant Act 1 alignment quest** — how a player drawn to scholarship and the Concord's lost gentleness experiences levels 1–4. Lector Briss (the sincere, beloved public face) visits the Reach and recruits the player to escort and protect him while he translates a Concord inscription, beginning the player's path to **learning Concord Script** — the key clue-gate that unlocks Act 3's archives. Delivers an M2/M6 *fragment* (the Concord built something here) without the sin (M6 full = Act 3) or any hint of the inner circle's true aim (Secret 12 — Act 3).

## Quest Type
Faction (Concord Remnant) — escort / exploration / research

## Level Range
3–4 (scales down to 2 with care). **Solo danger:** moderate (a cairn delve with telegraphed guardians/scavengers and a weak-caster ally). Retreat routes throughout.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Protect Briss / handle cairn threats: see Combat Options (telegraphed, avoidable).
- Navigate the cairn / spot hazards: Perception DC 13, Investigation DC 13.
- Begin learning Concord Script (with Briss's tutoring): Intelligence-based; no single gate — it's a *track* the player starts here and continues over sessions.
- Read a simple inscription with Briss's help: Investigation/Arcana DC 14 (auto with Briss for the gist).
- Read Briss himself / sense the Remnant's depths: Insight DC 14 (he is sincere; there is nothing false to catch in *him*).

## Starting Hook
Lector Briss, bright and generous, lecturing in the Reach: "There's a cairn out east with Concord script no one's read in a century — and I'm told the roads aren't safe for a scholar alone. Walk with me? Keep me breathing while I read, and I'll teach you the old letters as we go. The Concord wasn't what the frighten-tales say. It was *kind*. Let me show you."

## Location
Hollowmere (Briss's lectures); a Concord archive-cairn east of town — use **The Whispering Cairn** (`THE_WHISPERING_CAIRN.md`) or a roadside cairn. Tie-in: `Q_THE_SCHOLARS_REQUEST.md` (Hook 8).

## Involved NPCs
Lector Briss (patron; sincere dupe — Secret 13; the Script teacher), Archivist Quorrin Vane (distant; a revered name Briss admires, unaware of his true aim — Act 3 rival), Veska Dunn (Remnant reclaimer-agent who may contest the cairn — a rival, not an ally), scavengers/cairn guardians (improv/bestiary).

## Involved Factions
Concord Remnant (patron). Indirectly: the Cinder Ledger (a possible rival buyer at the cairn).

## Surface Problem
Escort a kind, vulnerable scholar to a Concord cairn, protect him while he translates an old inscription, and start learning the old letters.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
Briss is a **sincere dupe** (Secret 13): he has no idea the Remnant's inner circle (Quorrin) means to seize and restart the harvest (Secret 12). The Script he teaches is the **key clue-gate** for Act 3's archives (M6/M9). The cairn inscription seeds M6 (the Concord "kept the dead's pattern") as an *innocent-sounding fragment* — the sinister reading waits for Act 3. **Never reveal the inner circle's aim or the harvest in Act 1.**

## Objectives
1. Escort and protect Briss to the cairn and back.
2. Help him translate the inscription (gain an M2/M6 fragment).
3. Begin learning Concord Script (start the gate-track).
4. Decide how to relate to the Remnant: a curious student, a paid escort, or a skeptic keeping distance.

## Possible Approaches
- **Exploration/Combat:** delve the cairn; handle telegraphed guardians/scavengers; protect Briss.
- **Stealth:** slip Briss in and out unseen if rivals (Veska Dunn) are about.
- **Research/Social:** lean into the tutoring; build a real bond with Briss (his disillusionment later can shatter the Remnant — an Act 3 lever the player banks now).
- **Rival management:** out-talk or out-pace Veska Dunn's crew for the inscription.

## Obstacles
Cairn guardians/scavengers; a rival Remnant or Ledger crew also wanting the inscription; Briss's helplessness in danger (a protect-the-NPC dynamic); the slow difficulty of the Script (a track, not a one-session unlock); the temptation to dismiss the Concord as the frighten-tales do.

## Important Scenes
- Briss lighting up over a worn glyph, reading aloud a line about "keeping the dead's pattern gentle" — to him, proof of the Concord's kindness; to the player (later), something colder.
- A telegraphed cairn threat the player must handle while Briss cowers and then thanks them too profusely.
- Veska Dunn's crew arriving to claim the same stone — a tense rival scene (negotiation or a race, rarely a fight).
- Briss teaching the first Concord letters by firelight, utterly trusting, utterly unaware.

## Clues
- **C-M2 / C-M6 fragment** — the cairn inscription: the Concord "kept the dead's pattern" (innocent-sounding; the sin is Act 3). See `ACT_1_CLUE_TRAILS.md`.
- **The Script gate (begun)** — the player starts learning Concord Script, the key to Act 3 archives. (Track, not a single clue.)

## Combat Options
Cairn threats: **scavengers/claim-jumpers (Bandit/Scout/Thug-like, CR 1/8–1, AC 12–13, ~11–22 HP)** or **cairn guardian-construct/restless dead (specter or animated-guardian-like, AC 12–13, ~22–30 HP)** — telegraphed, with retreat routes; morale-prone. Veska Dunn (Scout/Mage-lite) is a *recurring rival*, never killed cheaply. Briss is a weak caster ally (**Scholar/Acolyte-like, AC 11, ~18 HP, a cantrip and Mage Armor**) — protect, don't rely on. See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/ACT_1_THREATS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
Stealth the cairn; negotiate with rivals; or simply guard Briss through a non-hostile read. The Script-learning and bond-building are pure noncombat reward.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
A scoutable, escapable cairn; Briss can cast a defensive spell; rivals prefer to talk/race; the player can withdraw and try again; nothing here is time-locked into a death.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in Hollowmere; a short rest in a cleared cairn. Mild time pressure: a rival crew may reach the inscription first (reroutes, doesn't block). The **Reclamation clock (#7)** is low in Act 1 but the player can glimpse the Remnant's reach starting to extend into the Reach.

## Rewards
- **The start of Concord Script** — the single most valuable Act 1 *capability* for the late game (unlocks Act 3 archives, M6/M9).
- **Briss's friendship** — a sincere ally and a future Act 3 lever (disillusioning him can shatter the Remnant).
- Remnant goodwill and archive access (Caradril's Lamplighters' Hall, later).
- A copied inscription (an M2/M6 fragment to revisit); modest scholar's pay; possibly a minor Concord trinket.

## Consequences
- **If the player succeeds:** begins the Script gate; Briss is devoted; the Remnant sees a promising agent; an Act 3 truth-path opens early.
- **If the player partially succeeds:** the inscription is half-read or lost to a rival; the Script-track still begins.
- **If the player ignores it:** the Remnant uses another agent; the Script can still be learned later (Briss in Caradril); no truth permanently lost — but the player enters Act 3 *without* the early gate, making the archives slower.
- **If the player alienates Briss** (mocks the Concord, robs the cairn): the Script teacher closes; Veska Dunn or Caradril become the slower alternate gate.
- **If the player solves it nonviolently:** ideal; Briss is unharmed and devoted.

## Failure State
If the escort fails (cairn lost, Briss alienated, rivals win), the player loses the *easiest* early Script path — but the Script remains learnable in Caradril (Act 2+) and the M6/M9 truth has multiple other paths. Briss survives by design (a cheap death of the sincere dupe would waste a key Act 3 lever); at worst he's frightened off, recoverable later.

## Scaling Notes
- **L2–3:** soften cairn DCs by 1–2; fewer/weaker guardians; Briss does more of the reading.
- **L4:** add Veska Dunn's crew as a live rival; a tougher guardian (~30 HP); the inscription harder to reach.
- Keep the inscription's meaning *innocent* in Act 1; never reveal the inner circle's aim.
