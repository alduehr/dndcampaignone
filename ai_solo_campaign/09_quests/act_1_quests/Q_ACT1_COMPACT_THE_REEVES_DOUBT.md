## AI Use

The **Reachward Compact Act 1 alignment quest** — how a player who sides with the honest town council experiences levels 1–4. Reeve Coalmont, uneasy about the basin-drainage scheme but unable to act openly, recruits the player to *quietly look into it.* Delivers the **M4 shape** (someone is pushing the basin for a hidden reason) without exposing Reke's allegiance (that's Act 2 / R3). A lawful, investigation-and-social path with no forced combat.

## Quest Type
Faction (Reachward Compact) — investigation / political

## Level Range
1–4 (scales). **Solo danger:** low. Primarily social, legal, and investigative; any violence is avoidable and enforcer-level, never lethal-by-ambush.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Read the council room / spot who really drives the vote: Insight DC 13.
- Find the holes in the "water rights" justification: Investigation DC 14 (records office).
- Get Clerk Pevin Oss to talk / hand over filings: Persuasion or Insight DC 13 (he's nervous; multiple angles).
- Tail Reke to the basin shore unseen: Stealth DC 14 (Perception DC 13 to learn anything).
- Sway a cautious councilor or win a procedural point: Persuasion DC 15 (with evidence: DC 12).

## Starting Hook
Reeve Coalmont, after watching the player act honestly, takes them aside: "I can't say this where the council hears. The basin scheme stinks to me, and I can't prove it, and if I push without proof I look like I'm holding the town back. You're new. Nobody owns you yet. Would you look — quietly?"

## Location
Hollowmere: the council hall, the Compact records office (Pevin Oss), the Drowned Lantern (Halla's ear), the basin shore at night (tailing Reke). Kettle Bridge for the filings/toll-trail.

## Involved NPCs
Reeve Marda Coalmont (patron), Clerk Pevin Oss (nervous records-keeper), Councilor Aldous Reke (the subject — charming, unrevealed), Halla Coalmont (rumor conduit; the reeve's sister), Factor Domic Sael (Ledger funding grumbles).

## Involved Factions
Reachward Compact (the player's lawful patron); Cinder Ledger (funds the scheme); Hollow Court (covert, via Reke — never named in Act 1).

## Surface Problem
Is the basin-drainage project really about water and resettlement, or is something else going on? Find out, lawfully and quietly.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The scheme is a Hollow Court effort (via Reke) to expose and reopen the drowned shrine (M4, Secret 9; Clock #2). **Act 1 delivers only the *shape*:** the justification is fabricated and someone influential wants the basin opened. Reke's allegiance, the Court, and the shrine's true nature stay hidden until Act 2+.

## Objectives
1. Assess who really drives the basin vote (Insight; follow the influence to Reke).
2. Find the cracks in the "water rights" story (the filings don't support it).
3. Gather quiet evidence (records, a tailing, funding grumbles) Coalmont can use.
4. Decide what to do with it: arm Coalmont, confront Reke, or hold the line on the vote.

## Possible Approaches
- **Investigation:** the records office (Pevin Oss's filings; the fabricated water-rights basis).
- **Social:** read the council; turn Pevin Oss; mine Halla's gossip; press Domic on funding.
- **Surveillance/Stealth:** tail Reke to the basin shore at night (C-M4-3) — eerie, telegraphed, no combat by default.
- **Political:** help the cautious bloc win a delay or a vote (`Q_THE_BAILIFFS_LADDER.md`).

## Obstacles
Reke's charm and procedural skill; Pevin Oss's fear; the appearance of obstructionism (the town *wants* prosperity); the Ledger's money greasing the project; the risk of tipping off Reke too early.

## Important Scenes
- A tense council session where Reke makes draining the basin sound modest and obvious, and the player watches *who* he persuades.
- The records office: a "water rights" survey that cites land and depths that don't match what's actually there.
- The basin shore at night: a lone silver-haired figure standing at the water's edge, listening — Reke, doing something a water-rights champion has no reason to do. (Atmosphere; **do not** let him be cheaply caught or killed.)
- Coalmont weighing the evidence, torn between duty and the fear of being wrong.

## Clues
- **C-M4-1** (the reeve's suspicions + the votes), **C-M4-2** (the fabricated filings), **C-M4-3** (tailing Reke to the shore), **C-M4-4** (the Ledger funding grumble). See `ACT_1_CLUE_TRAILS.md` — only ~2 are needed to grasp the M4 *shape*.

## Combat Options
**None required.** If the player is reckless (e.g., breaks into the records office and is caught, or confronts Reke physically), the watch responds (**Guard-like, AC 16, ~11 HP**) — telegraphed, arrest-first, not lethal. Reke never fights in Act 1; he deflects and reports (a relationship cost, not a death).

## Noncombat Options
The entire quest is solvable by investigation, social play, and stealth. This is the campaign's clearest "no combat" Act 1 path.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
A lawful patron (Coalmont) and a safe base (Hollowmere); the watch is an ally, not a threat, while the player stays lawful; the night-shore tail is escapable and never an ambush; Halla provides a low-stakes rumor backstop if the player stalls.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Full safe rest in Hollowmere. Time pressure: the **Basin Drains clock (#2)** — if the player dawdles, the vote advances and drainage is approved, then begins. Failure here doesn't block the truth; it raises the stakes.

## Rewards
- **Coalmont's trust and patronage** — the staunchest lawful ally in Act 1; access to Compact resources and the watch.
- Evidence the player can spend in Act 2 to expose Reke (banked R3 material).
- Standing in Hollowmere; legal cover for future jobs.
- Modest coin/retainer from the reeve; a writ or token of the Compact's favor.

## Consequences
- **If the player succeeds:** Coalmont is armed; the vote can be delayed or contested; the Basin clock (#2) slows; the player is positioned as the honest faction's agent.
- **If the player partially succeeds:** suspicion without proof; Coalmont can stall but not win; Reke grows wary.
- **If the player ignores it:** the vote goes Reke's way; drainage is approved (#2 advances; raises master #1); a redirect, not a dead end — the surfacing ruins become a new Act 2 entry.
- **If the player tips off Reke** (clumsy investigation): Reke quietly moves against Coalmont and watches the player — an Act 2 antagonism seed, not an Act 1 death.
- **If the player solves it nonviolently:** ideal and intended.

## Failure State
If the investigation collapses (caught, stonewalled, or ignored), the basin scheme proceeds and Coalmont is sidelined — but the *evidence still exists* and other M4 paths remain (the filings, the shore, Domic's grumbles). The player can pick the thread back up in Act 2 when the surfacing shrine makes the stakes obvious. No truth is permanently lost.

## Follow-Up Hooks
`Q_THE_BAILIFFS_LADDER.md` (the basin vote); `Q_THE_REED_HOLMS.md` (the scheme's human cost); Hook 10 "Debts and the Drowned" (Ledger pressure); toward Act 2, exposing Reke (R3).

## Scaling Notes
- **L1–2:** soften DCs by 1; Coalmont spells out the next step if the player stalls.
- **L3–4:** as written; add a wary Reke who notices clumsy moves; the watch is more alert if the player has a record.
- Cap reveals at the M4 *shape*; never expose Reke's true master in Act 1.
