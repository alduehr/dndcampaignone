## AI Use

The **Gravecallers Act 1 alignment quest** — how a player drawn to forbidden comfort and dangerous truth experiences levels 1–4. A grieving contact (often Tomas) or the gentle recruiter Sister Knell opens a door to the outlawed cult, culminating in a guarded, eerie audience that delivers a **fragment** of forbidden truth. Carries real legal risk (the Compact outlaws them). Caps reveals at R1: the dead "answer," and they speak of the water — **not** the harvest's history (M6 is Act 3) and never the Court.

## Quest Type
Faction (Gravecallers) — social / forbidden / investigation

## Level Range
2–4 (scales). **Solo danger:** low-to-moderate. Mostly social and stealth; danger comes from the *law* and from radicals (Cole Ashby), not from the cult's gentle face. Telegraphed throughout.

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Find who is contacting the bereaved (read the Knock / follow a lead): Investigation DC 13.
- Convince the Gravecallers you're no informer: Persuasion or Insight DC 14 (deeds and grief matter more than the roll).
- Stay unseen by the watch while meeting the cult: Stealth DC 14.
- Bear the Tallow Man's audience without panic: Wisdom save DC 13 (the doubled voice; the cold).
- Tell the gentle wing (Knell) from the radical wing (Ashby): Insight DC 13.

## Starting Hook
Someone is knocking softly at the doors of the grieving, promising "a last word with the one you lost." Tomas Brevin — desperate to keep Wren — has sought them out (Secret 19), or a grieving family begs the player to find out who, before the Compact's purge falls on them. (Or: Sister Knell finds the player when their *own* grief or curiosity is loudest.)

## Location
Hollowmere's poorer Boards and Mourner's Green (the Knock); the Greyfens edge and a hidden cell (the Drowned Blind / Mother Tongue Pool) for the audience. Tie-in: `Q_THE_SECOND_MARK.md`.

## Involved NPCs
Sister Knell (gentle recruiter; the humane door; secretly wavering — Secret 10), The Tallow Man (the speaker; channels a real Quietfall-era witness — the truth-source), Cole Ashby (radical; the danger; Secret 11), Tomas Brevin (the grieving man who opened the door; Secret 19), Iola Wend (the Mourner who secretly bridges to the cult).

## Involved Factions
Gravecallers (the door); Reachward Compact (outlaws them — the legal risk; a purge looms); Mourners' Circle (overlapping grief, official enmity — Iola's secret tie).

## Surface Problem
Find who is contacting the bereaved with promises of a last word — and decide what to do about it: expose them, protect them, or join them.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
The Gravecallers genuinely channel Remembrances and have learned *fragments* of the truth (M3/M6). The Tallow Man's doubled voice is a real Quietfall-era witness with its own agenda. **Act 1 delivers only an R1-level fragment:** the dead *answer*, they speak of "the door under the water," and they are not at peace. Do **not** surface Remembrance-as-substance (M3, Act 2), the Concord's sin (M6, Act 3), or the Court (M7, Act 4). Cole Ashby is gathering breaking-tools toward catastrophe (Clock #5 — hidden).

## Objectives
1. Discover who is contacting the bereaved (the Gravecallers).
2. Make contact safely — and choose: expose, protect, or learn from them.
3. (If learning) sit the Tallow Man's audience and carry away a fragment of forbidden truth.
4. Avoid (or weather) the Compact's purge.

## Possible Approaches
- **Social:** follow Knell's gentle thread; let grief be your introduction.
- **Investigation:** trace the Knock; read the marks; find the cell (the Second Mark).
- **Stealth:** shadow a contact to the cell unseen; observe before committing.
- **Protective:** warn or shelter the cult from the watch (earns deep cult trust); or, conversely, expose them to the Compact (earns the Compact, makes a cult enemy).

## Obstacles
The Compact's law and an imminent purge (legal danger); the cult's wariness of informers; the Tallow Man's unnerving audience; Cole Ashby's radicalism (he may see the player as an obstacle); the moral weight of forbidden comfort and Tomas's desperation.

## Important Scenes
- A soft knock at a grieving door at dusk; a chalked mark the player can learn to read.
- Knell, finding the player at their lowest, offering comfort with no demand — only an open door.
- The audience: a wax-stained figure whose voice *doubles*, the air gone cold, a dead voice speaking *through* him of "the door under the water" and the dead who "walk the wrong way." (Wisdom save vs. dread; never a cheap attack.)
- Cole Ashby, vibrating with conviction, talking about "the Breaking" — a chilling foreshadow the player can bank or try to defuse.

## Clues
- **C-M2-4 (fragment)** — the channeled dead name "the door under the water" (points at the shrine; R1/M2 shape). See `ACT_1_CLUE_TRAILS.md`.
- **C-M5-3 (fragment)** — cult lore: the dead drift toward the basin.
- **Seed of M3/M6** — the dead *know things*; full content is Act 2–3 gated.

## Combat Options
**Avoidable by design — the Gravecallers prefer to talk.** Threats, if any: the **watch during a purge (Guard-like, AC 16, ~11 HP)** — flee or talk, don't brawl; or **Cole Ashby (Acolyte/Berserker-like, AC 13, ~30 HP) + 1–2 recruits** if the player crosses the radicals. Telegraphed; morale-prone; the Tallow Man flees into the fens rather than die cheaply. See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/ACT_1_THREATS.md`.

## Noncombat Options
The intended path is entirely social/investigative/stealth. The cult wants a *listener*, not a fight.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Knell is a safe, gentle first contact; the cult avoids violence; the fens are navigable with Sashe; the player can withdraw from the audience at any time; the purge is telegraphed (the player chooses whether to be present).

## Rest And Time Pressure
Rest safely in Hollowmere between beats; the cell offers uneasy shelter if the player is trusted. Time pressure: the **purge** (Compact) and **The Breaking clock (#5)** — delay risks a purge (cult radicalizes) or Ashby moving on the basin.

## Rewards
- A **door to the Gravecallers** — the richest *truth-source* of Acts 1–2 (banked toward M3/M6).
- The **Gravecaller Knock** (a secret signaling code; access to hidden fen routes and cells).
- Sister Knell as a wavering insider/ally; the Tallow Man as a dangerous mentor-figure.
- A fragment of forbidden knowledge (the "door under the water" — an early shrine pointer).
- (If the player protected them) deep cult loyalty; (if exposed them) Compact favor instead.

## Consequences
- **If the player learns from them:** gains a forbidden truth-line and cult trust; risks the Compact's suspicion; #5 may ease (if radicals are calmed) or hold.
- **If the player protects them from a purge:** strong cult loyalty; Compact disfavor; Knell warms.
- **If the player exposes them:** Compact favor; a dangerous cult enemy (Ashby especially); the truth-line closes here (other M3/M6 paths remain).
- **If the player ignores it:** a purge happens or the cult radicalizes; #5 advances; Tomas may fall deeper in (Wren's Fate clock #9 worsens) — a redirect with new hooks.
- **If the player uses excessive force:** the gentle wing scatters; Ashby's radicals harden into enemies; the watch takes interest.

## Failure State
If contact fails or the cult is alienated, the forbidden truth-line here closes — but the dead's testimony remains reachable later (the Tallow Man can be sought again; Knell may defect; M3/M6 have multiple other paths). A botched approach that triggers a purge radicalizes the cult (Ashby ascendant) — a world-changing consequence and a new threat, not a dead end.

## Follow-Up Hooks
`Q_THE_SECOND_MARK.md` (mapping the cult / the Knock); Tomas's deepening desperation (Wren's Fate); Cole Ashby and "the Breaking" (Clock #5); toward Act 2–3, the Tallow Man's full testimony (M3/M6 — **gated**).

## Scaling Notes
- **L2:** keep it social and eerie; one telegraphed legal risk; soften DCs by 1.
- **L3–4:** add a live purge to navigate or a radical confrontation (Ashby); the audience hits harder (a second dread save; a stronger summoned Remembrance, ~30 HP).
- Cap all reveals at R1; never surface M3-as-substance, M6, or the Court.
