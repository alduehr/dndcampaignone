## AI Use

The **Ashen Wardens Act 1 alignment quest** — how a player drawn to the rite-keepers experiences levels 1–4. Begins with Wren or a failing funeral and grows into a working relationship with Brother Hale (and, conditionally, Othetha). Delivers R1 (the failures have a source/pattern; M1/M5) and builds Warden trust toward the late-arc confession (Secret 8) **without revealing it in Act 1**. Pairs with the repeatable `Q_THE_FAILING_FUNERAL.md`.

## Quest Type
Faction (Ashen Wardens) — multi-beat Act 1 alignment

## Level Range
1–4 (scales). **Solo danger:** low-to-moderate. Single, telegraphed Remembrances; worse near the basin. Noncombat-primary (the true rite).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Assist/perform the true rite: Religion DC 13 (DC 11 with Hale assisting; DC 15 fully alone).
- Calm a grieving family / prevent a mob: Persuasion or Insight DC 13.
- Notice rites fail *worse* near the basin (M5): Religion/Medicine/Investigation DC 13, cumulative.
- Win Hale's trust enough to be brought to a Warden safe-house: roleplay, no single gate roll.
- (Late Act 1) Earn a fragment from Othetha: Persuasion/Insight DC 15 **and** demonstrated respect for the dead — never a one-roll unlock.

## Starting Hook
A funeral is failing and a lone, overstretched Warden — usually **Brother Hale** — needs a steady hand. (Or: the player met Hale during Wren's crisis and he asks for help with the next one.) "I can't be everywhere. Will you stand with me while I send this one home?"

## Location
Hollowmere's Mourner's Green first; then any Reach settlement or roadside as failures spread. The Warden Rest (Hollowmere) and the Ashwalk Rest waystation are the faction's safe nodes.

## Involved NPCs
Brother Hale (companion-class Warden; first contact), Warden Sister Othetha (senior, rare, trust-gated), grieving families (improv/minor), Warden Pell (doubt thread; see `Q_PELLS_DOUBT.md`).

## Involved Factions
Ashen Wardens (the rite); Mourners' Circle (the grief — often present, sometimes prickly).

## Surface Problem
The dead won't rest. Help the Wardens lay them, ease the families, and keep the towns from panic.

## Hidden Truth (DM-Only)
Rites fail because the harvest is reawakening and *pulls* at the dead (M5); failures worsen toward the basin. The Wardens unknowingly guard the seal; Othetha is the sole local keeper of why. **None of this surfaces in Act 1** — the player earns trust and the *pattern* (R1), not the secret (R5/M6 is Act 3, Secret 8 confession is Act 3–4).

## Objectives
1. Help complete failing rites (one or several).
2. Build a working relationship with Hale (and the order).
3. Begin to notice the geographic pattern (the basin connection).
4. (Optional, late Act 1) Earn enough trust that Othetha shares a *fragment* of worry — not the secret.

## Possible Approaches
- **Ritual:** learn and perform the true rite (the core path; Hale teaches).
- **Social:** de-escalate families and mobs; comfort the grieving.
- **Investigation:** map *where* failures cluster (M5); compare notes with Hale.
- **Protective:** guard a Warden or a rite from interference (Gravecaller radicals, a fearful mob).

## Obstacles
The rite's resistance (the pull); frightened crowds; Hale's exhaustion and self-doubt; the Mourners' suspicion of outsiders; a Remembrance that slips loose.

## Important Scenes
- A casket-lid shifting; Hale's hands shaking as he tries the rite again, grateful for help.
- The dawning, several-funerals realization that the basin is the cause.
- Hale, late at night, confessing he thinks "the elders aren't telling us everything" — his doubt (not the secret).
- (Trust-gated) Othetha, ash-cloaked and weary, watching the player and almost saying something — then giving only a blessing and a warning to "keep the water at your back."

## Clues
- **C-M5 (cumulative)** — failures cluster and worsen toward the basin (`ACT_1_CLUE_TRAILS.md`).
- **C-M1-2** — a Warden examining the dead: "something holds them here" (Secret 8 *seed*, not the secret).

## Combat Options
A single failed-rite Remembrance — **specter-like, AC 12, ~17 HP (L1) scaling to ~30 HP (L4)**; non-hostile by default, layable by rite. Combat only if it escapes and threatens, and even then telegraphed; Hale assists (**Acolyte/Priest-like, AC 13, ~22 HP, Cure Wounds/Bless/Sacred Flame**). See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/ACT_1_THREATS.md` (Threat 1).

## Noncombat Options
The true rite (Religion) and social de-escalation resolve nearly every beat. Combat is the fallback.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Hale is usually present as an ally; Remembrances are sorrowful, not ambushers; retreat-and-fetch-help is always allowed; basin-adjacent failures are flagged as harder *before* engagement.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest at the Warden Rest, the Ashwalk Rest, or any settlement. Time pressure: an unresolved failure seeds a loose Remembrance (new minor threat) and advances the Wardens Break clock (#3).

## Rewards
- **Warden trust** (toward the Act 3–4 confession; eases clock #3).
- The **true rite technique** (Hale teaches assisting/performing — a reusable noncombat tool vs. the dead).
- A Warden safe-house and Hale as a recurring **companion ally**.
- Cumulative **M5 insight** (the pattern — crucial, quiet).
- Modest coin/supplies from grateful families and Mourners; rite-salt; minor healing draughts (level-appropriate).

## Consequences
- **If the player succeeds:** the dead rest; communities steady; the Wardens gain a trusted helper; clock #3 eased; Hale's loyalty deepens.
- **If the player partially succeeds:** rites half-take; "quieted" dead return later (a follow-up scene); Hale frays.
- **If the player ignores it:** failures go uncontained; loose Remembrances seed in the fens/roads; the Wardens strain and start refusing the worst sites (#3 advances).
- **If the player solves it nonviolently:** ideal; maximal Warden and Mourner goodwill.
- **If the player uses excessive force** (fights the dead instead of laying them): the Wardens are disturbed; Mourners turn cold; trust stalls — a redirect, not a block (`ACT_1_FAILURE_STATES.md`).

## Failure State
A failed or abandoned rite leaves a loose Remembrance and a frightened settlement — a **complication and an M5 data-point, not a dead end**. The quest recurs as the crisis worsens. If Hale is alienated, Othetha (or Pell) can still be reached later by other means.

## Follow-Up Hooks
`Q_PELLS_DOUBT.md` (a Warden questioning the order); `Q_THE_HOLM_THAT_WONT_BURY_ITS_DEAD.md` (the worst failures, Greywater); the basin scheme (the cause); Hale's companion arc; toward Act 3, Othetha's confession (Secret 8 — **gated**).

## Scaling Notes
- **L1–2:** weak Remembrance (~17 HP); Hale does most of the rite; soften DCs by 1.
- **L3–4:** basin-adjacent failure (~30 HP), a near-mob, a clearer M5 pattern; a rite may need two successes or a relic complication.
- Never let the player learn the order's secret in Act 1; cap reveals at R1.

## State Updates

- **Failure/ignore:** `WORLD_CLOCKS.md` (#3 advances), `CONSEQUENCES.md` (loose Remembrance), `OPEN_THREADS.md` (uncontained failures).
