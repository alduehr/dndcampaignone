## AI Use

The **portable M3 path in the city**: trace, buy, or shut down a black-market relic that "holds the dead." A commercial, hands-on way to learn what Remembrance *is* (M3) without the Reach delves or the Archive, plus a moral/criminal tangle with the Salt Syndicate. Ashmarket centerpiece; pairs with the Crucible and the clergy's protest.

## Quest Type
City / Mystery / Investigation

## Level Range
4–7. Solo danger: low-moderate (criminal/social; thin-touch hazard from the relic; Syndicate muscle if pushed).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Find the quiet-coin market: Streetwise/Persuasion DC 13 (or a contact: the Bellman, a fence).
- Get an honest appraisal: via Mooren DC 14, or read it yourself Arcana/Religion DC 15.
- Confirm it stores a person (M3): study DC 15 (Arcana/Religion) — or hear it speak a dead name.
- Bargain with Sorrel: Persuasion/Insight DC 14.
- Handle the raw relic safely: DC 14 or risk **thin-touch** (reversible).

## Starting Hook
Rumor at the Ashmarket: a relic changing hands in the back rooms "talks" — and the buyer went strange. A frightened appraiser (Mooren), a grieving customer, or the Bellman points the player at the quiet-coin trade and the relic at its center.

## Location
Caradril, the Ashmarket (the Curtain Market, Sorrel's counting-room); threads to the Crucible and Highmourn.

## Involved NPCs
"Tidewife" Sorrel (Syndicate boss), Appraiser Mooren (honest, unnerved), Fence Greel-of-the-Ash, a desperate buyer, Mother Ysarra/clergy (the protest).

## Involved Factions
Salt Syndicate (runs the trade), Cinder Ledger (skims the best relics; the deal upstream), Mourners' Circle (protests grave-robbery).

## Surface Problem
What is the relic that holds the dead — and what to do about the trade in it?

## Hidden Truth (DM)
Quiet-coin relics are **raw Remembrance relics** (old glass); a few genuinely store the dead (C-M3 demonstration). The Syndicate sells them without understanding what they are — horror inside a hustle. The specific relic at the quest's center "remembers" a recent Reach death, a chilling, portable M3 clue. The Ledger feeds and skims this trade (links to `Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER`).

## Objectives
1. Trace the quiet-coin relic.
2. Confirm what it is (M3).
3. Decide its fate — recover it, destroy it, return it to the dead's kin, expose the trade, or seize it.

## Possible Approaches
- **Buy it:** coin solves the acquisition; then study it.
- **Investigate:** appraisal + study to prove what it is.
- **Pressure the Syndicate:** bargain with or lean on Sorrel.
- **Expose the trade:** arm the clergy/reformers with proof (a Magisterium vote, a moral win).
- **Steal it:** a low-stakes back-room theft (Stealth/Sleight DC 14).

## Obstacles
The Syndicate's control; the relic's thin-touch danger; the Ledger's interest; the moral weight (a dead person in your hands).

## Important Scenes
- A back-room sale where the relic murmurs a dead man's name.
- Mooren's lens trembling over a relic that "reads wrong."
- Sorrel, grandmotherly and dangerous, sizing up the player's intentions.
- The choice: a person reduced to a coin — what do you do with them?

## Combat Options
None required. Crossing the Syndicate brings Thug/Bandit-like muscle (telegraphed) — a flee/negotiate beat. The relic's danger is thin-touch, not violence.

## Noncombat Options
Buying, investigating, bargaining, exposing — all noncombat. The "fight" is moral and social.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Multiple routes to the relic and to M3 (it's reachable elsewhere too); thin-touch is reversible and telegraphed; Sorrel prefers leverage to blades; safe rest nearby.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in the district. Time pressure: the relic may be sold on (and the buyer harmed) if the player dawdles; a short clock.

## Rewards
- **M3 demonstration in hand** (proof Remembrance stores a person).
- The relic itself (a usable, eerie clue-object; handle with care) — or the gratitude of returning it to the dead's kin.
- Coin/standing from the Syndicate or the reformers (by choice).
- A lead to the Ledger's relic-skim (links `Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER`).

## Consequences
- **Recover/return it:** a humane M3 confirmation; the dead honored; the player marked as one who "meddles."
- **Expose the trade:** the clergy/reformers gain; the Syndicate and Ledger lose; a Magisterium vote shifts.
- **Sell/seize it:** coin and complicity; the trade continues.

## Failure State
If the relic is lost or sold on, M3 is still reachable (the Crucible, the Sunken Wards, the Reach delves, the Gravecallers). No dead-end; a buyer comes to harm, deepening the city's quiet crisis.

## Follow-Up Hooks
`Q_THE_SMELTING` (relics destroyed), `Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER` (the Ledger's skim), `Q_THE_FUNERAL_THAT_WOULDNT_TAKE` (the city's failures), arming the reformers.

## Scaling Notes
- L4: one relic, a simple trace; thin-touch as a gentle warning.
- L7: a wider trade, the Ledger's hand visible, a Syndicate confrontation; richer rewards and clearer M3 implications.
