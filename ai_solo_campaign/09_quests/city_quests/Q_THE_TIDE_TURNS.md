## AI Use

The city's **underworld faction-conflict quest**, standalone from the main arc: Captain Kade wants to break the Salt Syndicate's grip on the Ashmarket and Sill, but the law favors the rich and the Hush holds everyone's secrets. The player becomes the swing factor in a three-way struggle (Watch / Syndicate / Hush) with the Ledger profiting on the edges. Pure intrigue; teaches the city's criminal ecosystem; no main-arc dependency, so it's safe to run any time.

## Quest Type
City / Faction / Social / Investigation

## Level Range
5–9. Solo danger: moderate (criminal reprisal, telegraphed; optional combat, never forced).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Win Kade's confidence: Persuasion DC 13 (he respects competence and honesty).
- Gather evidence on the Syndicate's quiet-coin trade: Investigation DC 15.
- Read the Hush's true game: Insight DC 15 (the Bellman plays all sides).
- Turn a Syndicate runner informant: Persuasion/Intimidation DC 14.
- Avoid being framed by the losing side: Insight DC 14 + caution.

## Starting Hook
Captain Kade — disgusted that the Watch protects the Quays and squeezes the Sill — quietly asks the player to help him break the Salt Syndicate's hold "where the law won't let me." But the Hush knows Kade's every move and sells it; the Syndicate has the Sill's loyalty (and fear); and the Ledger would rather the underworld stay profitable. Whose side is the player really on?

## Location
Caradril, the Sill and the Ashmarket (the Syndicate's turf); the Magisterium (Kade's barracks); the Hush, everywhere.

## Involved NPCs
Captain Yorell Kade (the honest lawman), "Tidewife" Sorrel (the Syndicate boss), the Bellman (the Hush, playing all sides), a turnable Syndicate runner, Renna Sill (the Sill's conscience).

## Involved Factions
Tide-Watch (wants to break the Syndicate), Salt Syndicate (entrenched), the Hush (sells everyone out), Cinder Ledger (profits from the status quo).

## Surface Problem
Help Kade break the Syndicate — or decide the underworld is better left as it is.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
This is a *clean* faction conflict with no main-arc gate — but it intersects the conspiracy at the edges: the Syndicate's quiet-coin trade is the relic black market (links `Q_QUIET_COIN`), and the Hush brokers the deal-fragments (links `Q_THE_BELLMANS_PRICE`). Breaking the Syndicate disrupts the relic trade; empowering the Hush makes the Bellman more dangerous; the Ledger quietly prefers the player fail. There's no "right" side: the Syndicate also *shelters* the Sill the Watch neglects, so breaking it has a human cost (Renna's stake).

## Objectives
1. Pick a side (or play them against each other).
2. Gather what your side needs (evidence, informants, leverage).
3. Tip the balance — and live with what the new order does to the Sill.

## Possible Approaches
- **Back the Watch:** evidence and informants to break the Syndicate (and harm the relic trade).
- **Back the Syndicate:** protect the Sill's rough order against the Watch's class-bias.
- **Use the Hush:** play all sides; profit; empower the Bellman (dangerous).
- **Broker peace:** force a truce that protects the Sill *and* curbs quiet-coin (the hard, best path).

## Obstacles
The Hush's omniscience; the Watch's class-bias; the Syndicate's grip and the Sill's loyalty; the Ledger's quiet sabotage; the risk of being framed.

## Important Scenes
- Kade, off-record, admitting the law is rigged and asking for help anyway.
- Sorrel, grandmotherly, reminding the player who actually feeds the Sill.
- The Bellman selling the player's own plan back to them.
- Renna, caught between protection and exploitation, asking the player to do right by her people.

## Combat Options
None required. Reprisal comes as Thug/Bandit/Spy-like crews (telegraphed), framing, and leverage — flee/talk/outwit beats. Kade's Watch can back a lawful player; the Syndicate prefers not to spill blood in its own market.

## Noncombat Options
The whole quest: investigation, social maneuvering, informant-turning, brokering.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Multiple sides and a peace path (no single failure dead-ends it); Kade or Renna as protectors depending on side; reprisal is social/criminal, not assassination; safe rest in the upper city.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in the upper city; risky in the Sill. Time pressure: each side moves while the player decides; dawdling lets the Hush or Ledger steer the outcome.

## Rewards
- A standing ally in the city's law or underworld (Kade, Sorrel, or the Hush — by choice) and a favor.
- Coin/leverage (100–300 gp by tier or equivalent influence).
- Side-effects on `Q_QUIET_COIN` (relic trade) and `Q_THE_BELLMANS_PRICE` (the Hush's reach).
- The Sill's gratitude or grievance (Renna).

## Consequences
- **Watch wins:** the relic trade disrupted; the Sill loses its rough protector (a human cost).
- **Syndicate wins:** the Sill protected; quiet-coin (and the conspiracy's relic flow) continues.
- **Hush wins:** the Bellman's reach grows; everyone owes him; a dangerous city.
- **Peace:** the hardest, best outcome — the Sill safe *and* quiet-coin curbed.

## Failure State
If the player botches their side, the balance tips against them — a setback and possibly a city enemy, not a dead-end. The underworld reconfigures; future Sill/Ashmarket quests shift but remain open.

## Follow-Up Hooks
`Q_QUIET_COIN` (the relic trade), `Q_THE_BELLMANS_PRICE` (the Hush's debts), Kade as a lawful ally for later city work, the Sill's fate (Renna).

## Scaling Notes
- L5: a two-sided struggle, clear stakes.
- L9: a three-way knot with the Ledger meddling; higher DCs; the outcome bends the city's criminal ecosystem and brushes the conspiracy's relic flow.
