## AI Use

The **Ledger-work quest** that turns into the player's first hard look at the Vyre–Quorrin monopoly deal. Begins as paid odd-jobs at the Ledger Keep, escalates into the conspiracy via Coll Riis. The Archive-free path to M3/M6 fragments and the Monopoly/Reclamation intersection. Patron and clue centerpiece for the Counting-Quays.

## Quest Type
City / Faction / Investigation

## Level Range
5–9. Solo danger: low-moderate (economic/social pressure; optional stealth; Ledger muscle if pushed, telegraphed).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Earn a Vyre/Vell audience and trust: Persuasion DC 13 (Vyre wants a useful hand).
- Turn Coll Riis (the frightened clerk): Persuasion/Insight DC 14 + a credible promise of protection.
- Tail a Remnant courier to/from the Keep: Stealth DC 13 / Perception DC 14.
- Read the deal-pages once obtained: Investigation DC 14 (or Concord Script for the Remnant half).
- (Optional) Pilfer a ledger from an office: Sleight of Hand/Stealth DC 15.

## Starting Hook
Provost Vell (or, by letter, Mother Vyre herself) offers the capable newcomer Ledger work at the Keep: a debt to collect, salvage to appraise, a discreet errand. Good pay, clear strings. The first job is clean; the second brushes against something the Ledger would rather the player not see.

## Location
Caradril, the Counting-Quays (the Ledger Keep, the Last Tally); threads to the Lantern Reach (the Remnant half of the deal).

## Involved NPCs
Calla Vyre (matriarch; the offer), Anneth Vell (runs the Keep), Coll Riis (the turnable clerk), a Remnant courier (lead to Quorrin), "Tidewife" Sorrel (smuggling overlap).

## Involved Factions
Cinder Ledger (patron), Concord Remnant (secret partner), Salt Syndicate (skims the trade).

## Surface Problem
Do Ledger work, get paid, stay useful.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The Ledger is negotiating the **Remembrance monopoly with Quorrin Vane** here (M3/M6 access; the Monopoly + Reclamation clocks intersect). **Coll Riis** has copied incriminating deal-pages and is terrified — turning him is a clean, Archive-free clue path proving the Ledger and Remnant plan to *sell and seize the dead*. The deep vault holds a refined relic (M3 demonstration; gated, L8+). Vyre means to betray Quorrin and vice-versa — a fracture the player can exploit.

## Objectives
1. Do enough Ledger work to gain access and trust.
2. Notice the deal (the courier, Riis's fear, the odd accounts).
3. Obtain proof — turn Riis, tail the courier, or pilfer the pages.
4. (Optional, L8+) reach the deep-vault relic.

## Possible Approaches
- **Loyal insider:** serve the Ledger, rise, and learn the deal from the inside (then choose to use it or not).
- **Turn the clerk:** befriend and protect Coll Riis for the deal-pages (cleanest M3/M6 path).
- **Surveillance:** tail the Remnant courier and connect Vyre to Quorrin.
- **Heist (L8+):** infiltrate the deep vault for the refined relic (multi-path; see the Ledger Vault's Hollowmere cousin for the template).

## Obstacles
The Ledger's leverage (debt, blacklist); Vell's suspicion; Riis's fear (and the clock to silence him); the vault's wards; the temptation of Ledger money to look away.

## Important Scenes
- Vyre's audience: warm, total, an offer with a hidden clause.
- Riis in the Last Tally, almost confessing, terrified of being overheard.
- A Remnant courier slipping into the Keep after dark.
- The deal-pages, read at last: the Ledger and Remnant planning to sell and seize the dead (M3/M6, the conspiracy made concrete).
- (L8+) the deep-vault relic "remembering" a name in a dead woman's voice.

## Combat Options
None required. Crossing the Ledger brings hired muscle (Thug/Spy-like, AC 13–14, ~30–40 HP; intimidation over killing) and economic war — telegraphed, escalating, no cheap death. A botched heist triggers capture/flee, not slaughter.

## Noncombat Options
The core paths are social, investigative, and stealth. The conspiracy can be uncovered without a single fight.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Multiple proof paths (Riis, courier, pages, vault) so no single failure blocks M3/M6; the Last Tally and city inns for safe rest; the heist is optional and retreat-rich; the Ledger prefers leverage to violence.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in the district. Time pressure: Riis can be silenced if the player dawdles (a short clock); the deal matures on the Monopoly clock; the deep-vault relic may be moved if the player is slow.

## Rewards
- **M3/M6 deal-proof** (the Vyre–Quorrin monopoly — a major mid-arc clue).
- Ledger pay and standing (50–250 gp by tier; loans, gear access) — or, if the player turns on the Ledger, an enemy and the moral high ground.
- (L8+) a refined Remembrance relic (M3 demonstration; handle with thin-touch care).
- Coll Riis as a protected informant (or a casualty if abandoned).

## Consequences
- **Stay loyal:** rich patron, deepening debt, complicity.
- **Expose the deal:** the player can shatter or exploit the Vyre–Quorrin alliance (Monopoly/Reclamation clocks slow or fracture); the Ledger becomes an enemy.
- **Save Riis:** a loyal informant and a clean clue path; the Ledger hunts a leak.
- **Heist the vault:** an M3 relic and the Ledger's lasting enmity.

## Failure State
If Riis is silenced and the player gets no proof here, M3/M6 remain reachable (the Sealed Archive, the Ashmarket's quiet-coin, the Crucible's smelting, the Reach delves, the Gravecallers). No dead-end; a clean path lost and the Ledger emboldened.

## Follow-Up Hooks
`Q_QUIET_COIN` (the relics the deal trades), `Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE` (the Remnant half), `Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER` (the Ledger's political reach), exposing the deal to Tolm or the council.

## Scaling Notes
- L5: clean odd-jobs + Riis path; no vault; modest pay.
- L9: full deal investigation, courier tails, optional deep-vault heist; richer rewards; the clocks visibly move on success.
