## AI Use

The **Act-3 truth-cache quest**: reach the Concord Remnant's Sealed Archive, where the clearest written proof of the harvest and the deliberate Quietfall is kept (C-M6-1, C-M9). A major-revelation gate, deliberately level-9–12 and Script-gated. Multiple entry paths (trust, theft, disillusioned-Briss, the Sunken Wards back-way) so it never single-points-of-failure. **Guardrail:** delivers M6/M9, NOT the apex Hollow Court reveal (M7) — give the *Concord's sin and the deliberate pause*, and let M7 converge later.

## Quest Type
City / Mystery / Investigation / (optional) Heist

## Level Range
9–12. Solo danger: moderate (Reclaimer response; the Sunken Wards if used; never a cheap-death gauntlet).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Gain inner-circle trust for legitimate access: Persuasion DC 17 (or stack favors/Remnant service to lower).
- Break in (front): Stealth/Thieves' Tools DC 18–20 (multi-step; Dree's keys, a forged warrant, the wards).
- Back-way via the Sunken Wards: Survival/Stealth DC 14 + the Wards' own hazards (see that file).
- Disillusion Briss into letting you in: Persuasion DC 15 + hard prior proof (e.g. the deal-pages from `Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER`).
- Read the Archive's proof: requires **Concord Script** (the gate; learned via `Q_THE_LANTERN_AND_THE_LAMP`).

## Starting Hook
The player, now reading some Concord Script and suspecting the truth, learns the Remnant keeps its most precious texts behind a sealed door no outsider passes. Wessel Dree bars the way with a courteous, immovable smile. The proof the player needs is *right there* — and getting to it means trust, theft, or turning the Remnant against itself.

## Location
Caradril, the Lantern Reach (the Sealed Archive); optionally via the Sunken Wards back-way (the Sill → the Sealgate).

## Involved NPCs
Wessel Dree (the gatekeeper Reclaimer), Quorrin Vane (the inner-circle head, racing the player), Lector Briss (the disillusionment key), Tamsin Orr (an innocent who might help unknowing), Old Pater Dunk (the Wards back-way guide).

## Involved Factions
Concord Remnant (guards it; the Reclaimers respond), Hollow Court (the truth here *points toward* them — but does not name them yet; DM).

## Surface Problem
Get into the Sealed Archive and read what's inside.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The Archive holds the **clearest written proof that the Concord harvested the dead (C-M6-1) and that the Quietfall was deliberate (C-M9)** — the Act-3 revelation cache. Reaching it confirms M6 and M9 and *foreshadows* M7 (surviving Custodians) without naming the Hollow Court — keep the apex for convergence. The Reclaimers (Dree, Quorrin) will lie, delay, and — if pushed — send agents to *turn the player back*, not to murder them (telegraphed, escalating). Disillusioning Briss with prior proof can both open the Archive *and* begin shattering the Remnant (Secret 13).

## Objectives
1. Become able to read Concord Script (prerequisite; `Q_THE_LANTERN_AND_THE_LAMP`).
2. Reach the Sealed Archive (trust, theft, Briss, or the Wards back-way).
3. Read and extract the proof (M6/M9).
4. Get out — and decide who learns what.

## Possible Approaches
- **Trust:** serve the Remnant until granted access (slow, ties you to Quorrin).
- **Theft:** a multi-path break-in (Dree's keys, a forged warrant, the wards) — the riskiest, cleanest-handed route.
- **Disillusion Briss:** show him prior proof of the Reclaimers' aim; he opens the door and the Remnant fractures.
- **The back-way:** descend the Sunken Wards (`Q_BELOW_THE_STILLING`) to the Archive's lower connection — avoids the front entirely.

## Obstacles
The Script gate; Dree's wards and lies; Quorrin's race and watchfulness; the Reclaimers' turn-back response; the Wards' hazards if used.

## Important Scenes
- Dree barring the stair with a polite, total refusal.
- The Archive itself: a Concord page that says, in the player's now-readable Script, what the Concord *did* — the floor dropping out (M6).
- A second text: the Quietfall, planned, signed (M9).
- Quorrin arriving too late, or catching the player — a tense, non-violent confrontation of equals (he'd rather recruit than kill).
- Briss, if disillusioned, reading the proof and breaking — then resolving (the Remnant's fracture begins).

## Combat Options
None required. Reclaimer agents (Spy/Mage-like, AC 13–14, ~40–60 HP; Hold Person, Counterspell, Remembrance one-offs) try to *turn the player back* — telegraphed, escapable, never a death-trap. Quorrin himself is a Tier-1 mind, not an ambusher. The Wards route has its own (avoidable) hazards.

## Noncombat Options
Trust, theft, disillusionment, and the back-way are all primarily social/stealth/exploration. The truth can be reached without a fight.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Four distinct entry paths (no single failure blocks M6/M9); retreat always available (the Stacks, the Sill, the city); Reclaimers turn-back rather than kill; the back-way is a graded dungeon with retreat. Per the three-clue rule, M6/M9 are *also* reachable outside Caradril (Othetha, the Tallow Man, the Reach delves) — the Archive is the clearest, not the only, path.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest in the city between attempts. Time pressure: Quorrin's Reclamation clock races; the longer the player circles, the closer the Remnant comes to the keystone — and the tighter Archive security gets.

## Rewards
- **M6 and M9 confirmed** — the Concord's sin and the deliberate Quietfall, in writing (major Act-3 revelations).
- Portable proof (copies/rubbings) usable to disillusion Briss, sway the council, or arm other factions.
- (If Briss turns) a powerful ally and the start of the Remnant's fracture.
- Concord lore and minor relics; standing as one who *knows*.

## Consequences
- **Read it:** the player carries the truth; the Remnant knows they know (a dangerous escalation); choices about who to tell reshape the faction war.
- **Disillusion Briss:** the Remnant fractures (Secret 13); a major ally gained; Quorrin endangered or enraged.
- **Caught:** Quorrin tries to recruit or contain the player (not kill); the Reclamation accelerates.
- **Back-way:** the Wards exposed; the Remnant's secret route compromised.

## Failure State
If every Archive path fails, M6/M9 remain reachable through Othetha's confession, the Tallow Man's channeled witness, and the Reach sites (Barrow, Deep Adit, Candlewick stone) — no dead-end. The Archive simply stays the *unread clearest* proof, and the Remnant keeps its advantage.

## Follow-Up Hooks
M7 convergence (the truth here + M4 + the under-shrine), the Remnant's fracture (Briss), confronting Quorrin, carrying the proof to the council/Mourners/Wardens, the endgame choice (M10).

## Scaling Notes
- L9: one viable path emphasized; Reclaimer response light; the proof delivered cleanly.
- L12: tighter security, a Quorrin confrontation, the Wards' deeper flood if the back-way is used; richer relics; the revelation lands amid an accelerating faction war.
