## AI Use

The **clue-shortcut-with-strings quest**: the Hush's broker, the Bellman, will sell the player a fragment of nearly any city secret — the Vyre–Quorrin deal, the Magisterium correspondent thread, the Sunken Wards route — for a hidden price (a favor, a debt, a name). A fair, dangerous accelerant and an introduction to the Sill and its humane stakes. Teaches that information has a cost. The Sill centerpiece.

## Quest Type
City / Social / Investigation

## Level Range
4–9. Solo danger: low (social/criminal; the "danger" is the price, not violence).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Find/contact the Bellman: Streetwise/Persuasion DC 14 (or via the Brass Ferry, Renna, a Hush-runner).
- Bargain the price down: Persuasion/Insight DC 14 (Insight reveals the *true* cost behind the offer).
- Verify the secret you bought is real (not a plant): Investigation DC 13.
- (If the price is a job) the job's own checks vary by what's asked.

## Starting Hook
Stuck on a thread — who Reke writes to, what the Ledger and Remnant are really doing, how to get into the Sunken Wards — the player hears that in the Sill, "the Bellman knows." For a price. Or Renna Sill, needing help for the refugees, points the player toward the only person who can shortcut what they need.

## Location
Caradril, the Sill (the Brass Ferry, the Hush's shifting "Belfry").

## Involved NPCs
The Bellman (the broker), Renna Sill (a humane alternative source/ally), a Hush-runner (the contact), the subject of whatever secret is bought.

## Involved Factions
The Hush (sells the secrets), and — depending on what's bought — the Cinder Ledger, Concord Remnant, or the Magisterium correspondent thread (the secrets point at these).

## Surface Problem
Buy a secret the player needs.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The Hush genuinely brokers fragments of the campaign's mid-arc clues — the Vyre–Quorrin deal (M3/M6), the Magisterium correspondent thread (the Reke lead), and the Sealgate route into the Sunken Wards. These are *real, fair* shortcut-clues — but **every secret carries a hidden price**: a favor owed (a future job), a debt (leverage the Hush keeps), or a name (the player must give up a secret of their own or another's). The Bellman is a dangerous convenience; the price is the quest. Renna Sill is the humane counter-path: help the Sill, and information comes free, slower.

## Objectives
1. Decide what secret to buy.
2. Negotiate the price (and understand its true cost).
3. Pay it — or find Renna's slower, cleaner alternative.
4. Use the secret (and live with the debt).

## Possible Approaches
- **Buy:** pay the Bellman's price for a fast clue-fragment.
- **Bargain:** read the true cost (Insight) and haggle it down.
- **Earn:** help Renna and the refugees; gain the same info slower, without a debt.
- **Refuse and investigate:** walk away and find the clue the hard way (the long, safe road).

## Obstacles
The hidden price; the Hush's leverage; the temptation of the shortcut; the moral cost if the "price" is betraying someone.

## Important Scenes
- The Brass Ferry at night; a Hush-runner sliding into a booth.
- The Bellman — never quite seen plainly — naming a price that is never only coin.
- The dawning of the *true* cost behind a "simple" favor.
- Renna, offering the harder, cleaner way: "Help my people, and I'll tell you for nothing."

## Combat Options
None. The Hush never fights; it threatens with knowledge. A player who crosses the Bellman risks *exposure*, not assassination — a social consequence.

## Noncombat Options
The entire quest. Negotiation, investigation, and choice.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
No combat; the clue is reachable without the Bellman (Renna, or the long road); the price is always disclosed if the player reads it (Insight); refusing is always allowed.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest nearby. Time pressure: light — the Bellman's offer may expire or sour, but the underlying clue persists.

## Rewards
- **A real shortcut-clue** (the deal, the correspondent thread, or the Wards route) — a major accelerant.
- (Via Renna) the refugees' trust and the Sill as an ally (a humane reward).
- Knowledge of how the city's underworld works.

## Consequences
- **Pay the price:** the clue gained, a debt owed — the Hush *will* collect (a future hook).
- **Bargain well:** a smaller price, the Bellman's respect.
- **Earn via Renna:** the clue and a clean ally; the Sill remembers.
- **Betray someone for a clue:** the secret gained at a moral cost the world remembers.

## Failure State
If the player can't or won't pay, they simply lose the shortcut — the underlying clue (deal/correspondent/Wards) remains reachable by the longer authored paths. No dead-end; the Hush keeps its secrets and its leverage.

## Follow-Up Hooks
Whatever the bought secret points to (`Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER`, `Q_BELOW_THE_STILLING`, the correspondent thread); the Hush calling in its debt (a future job); helping the Sill (Renna).

## Scaling Notes
- L4: a small secret, a modest price.
- L9: a major mid-arc clue, a steep price (a name, a betrayal), the Hush's debt a lasting thread.
