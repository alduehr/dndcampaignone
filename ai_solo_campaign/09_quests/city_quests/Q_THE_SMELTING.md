## AI Use

The **hands-on, hazardous M3 path**: investigate, stop, or exploit the relic-smelting at Harrow's Foundry, where Concord salvage is melted for profit — destroying evidence and unleashing "incidents." Brings M3 alive through industrial accident, sets up the Ledger-vs-Remnant relic proxy-war, and ties to the Sill's thin-touched workers. Crucible centerpiece.

## Quest Type
City / Mystery / Investigation

## Level Range
5–8. Solo danger: low-moderate (foundry hazards — heat, slag, thin-touch; no required combat).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Learn what's being smelted: Investigation/Insight DC 14.
- Turn Foundress Harrow into a witness: Persuasion DC 14.
- Spot Ordo's relic-diversion to the Remnant: Perception/Investigation DC 13.
- Navigate the foundry hazards: Dex/Con DC 13 (heat/slag; 2d6 fire if careless).
- Handle a raw relic / "screaming" cast safely: Arcana/Religion DC 14 or risk **thin-touch**.

## Starting Hook
A night-shift smelt at Harrow's Foundry "screamed" in a human voice; a worker's gone cold and strange (thin-touched); the Sill is muttering. Beck Harrow, frightened of what she's caused, asks the player to "find out what we've been melting" — or a Remnant scholar, or the clergy, wants the smelting stopped for opposite reasons.

## Location
Caradril, the Crucible (Harrow's Foundry, the slag-yards); threads to the Sill (the workers) and the Lantern Reach/Quays (the relic proxy-war).

## Involved NPCs
Beck Harrow (turnable witness), Smelt-master Ordo (bribed by the Remnant), Soot the apprentice (thin-touched, hiding it), Renna Sill (the workers' advocate).

## Involved Factions
Craft guilds (run the smelting), Cinder Ledger (pays to destroy embarrassing relics), Concord Remnant (bribes to divert the best ones), Salt Syndicate (supplies grey-market salvage).

## Surface Problem
Why is the foundry having "incidents," and what to do about the relic-smelting?

## Hidden Truth (DM)
Smelting raw Remembrance relics is not inert: it destroys the harvest-evidence they carry (which suits the Ledger) *and* causes incidents — a smelt that weeps a voice, a casting that holds a dead man's last word, workers gone thin-touched (C-M3 by industrial accident; a vivid M3 demonstration). The Ledger pays to smelt the embarrassing relics; the Remnant bribes Ordo to divert the best to the Sealed Archive — a small proxy-war the player can exploit.

## Objectives
1. Investigate the incidents (what's being melted; the screaming cast).
2. Confirm M3 (the relics hold the dead).
3. Address it — stop the smelting, save the workers, expose the Ledger/Remnant, or exploit the proxy-war.

## Possible Approaches
- **Investigate:** the foundry, the slag-yards, the records of what's been smelted.
- **Turn Harrow:** make the frightened foundress a witness.
- **Follow the relics:** catch Ordo's diversion; connect Ledger and Remnant.
- **Protect the workers:** help the thin-touched (links Renna Sill); rally the Sill.
- **Exploit:** play the Ledger and Remnant against each other over the relics.

## Obstacles
Foundry hazards (heat, slag, thin-touch); guild secrecy; the Ledger's and Remnant's competing interests; Harrow's fear and greed.

## Important Scenes
- A night-shift smelt that wails in a human voice as the metal runs.
- A slag-yard cast that, picked up, murmurs (thin-touch warning).
- Soot the apprentice hiding his growing thin-touch, terrified.
- Ordo quietly diverting a crate to a Remnant courier (a tail-able lead).
- Harrow, soot-streaked and scared, confronting what her foundry has done.

## Combat Options
None required. Guild toughs (Guard/Thug-like) are a last resort; the real danger is the foundry hazard-zone (heat, slag, thin-touch — reversible), not a brawl. Retreat is easy.

## Noncombat Options
Investigation, social, and careful handling. The "fight" is against a hazard and a moral problem.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Hazards telegraphed; thin-touch reversible; multiple M3 sources elsewhere; retreat from the foundry always available; safe rest nearby.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest adjoining. Time pressure: each smelt destroys evidence and risks another worker; Soot's thin-touch worsens; the Remnant's diversions deplete the relics — short, escalating clocks.

## Rewards
- **M3 demonstration** (relics hold the dead — proved by accident).
- A "casting that holds a voice" (an eerie, portable clue-object).
- Harrow as a witness; the Sill workers' gratitude (Renna ally).
- Leverage in the Ledger–Remnant relic proxy-war (coin or alliance).

## Consequences
- **Stop the smelting:** evidence preserved; the Ledger's cover blown; the guild's profit hit; the Remnant or Ledger resentful.
- **Save the workers:** thin-touched folk healed/sheltered; the Sill grateful; Harrow turned.
- **Exploit it:** coin and chaos as Ledger and Remnant clash over the relics.
- **Ignore it:** evidence destroyed, workers harmed, the proxy-war won by default.

## Failure State
If the smelting continues, relics (and evidence) are lost — but M3 is reachable elsewhere. Soot or another worker may be permanently harmed (a tragic cost, not a dead-end). The conspiracy's cover-up succeeds locally.

## Follow-Up Hooks
`Q_QUIET_COIN` (the relic trade upstream), `Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER`/`Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE` (the Ledger/Remnant ends of the proxy-war), helping the Sill (Renna), arming the clergy/reformers against the relic-trade.

## Scaling Notes
- L5: one incident, a clear M3 cast, gentle hazards.
- L8: repeated incidents, a deeper foundry hazard-zone, the proxy-war active; richer clues and a thin-touch-heavy slag-yard climax (still escapable).
