## AI Use

The city's **political fulcrum quest**: a vacant trade-charter pits Sefa Dann's reform bloc, Esren Tolm's old Houses, and the Cinder Ledger against each other, and the player's evidence and favors can tip the council. Teaches Caradril's politics, introduces every Magisterium NPC, and sets up the player as a power-broker. Multi-path, no combat required. Faction-conflict centerpiece.

## Quest Type
City / Faction / Social / Investigation

## Level Range
5–9. Solo danger: low-moderate (political; possible Ledger/House intimidation, never default combat).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Win a magister's vote: Persuasion DC 17 (lower per evidence/favor stacked; e.g. a debt called in, a secret exposed, a public win each give advantage or −2).
- Uncover a candidate's leverage/dirt: Investigation DC 15 (registry, the Hush, the Last Tally).
- Sway the Wardmoot to pressure the council: Persuasion DC 14.
- Resist a bribe/threat with poise: Insight DC 13 to read the offer's true cost.

## Starting Hook
A trade-charter has come vacant (a House failing, a scandal, a death). Three want it: the Ledger (Vell, for Vyre), an old House (Tolm's client), and a reform-backed newcomer Dann champions. All three court the player — because a frontier outsider's word, evidence, or muscle could tip a knife's-edge vote.

## Location
Caradril, the Magisterium (Magisters' Hall, the Wardmoot, the Pale Star); threads into the Quays and Lantern Reach.

## Involved NPCs
Sefa Dann (reform), Esren Tolm (old money), Anneth Vell (Ledger), Halloran Voss (the swing chair), Captain Kade (keeps order), Steward Maell / the Bellman (dirt for sale).

## Involved Factions
Tidewater Council & Charter Houses (the contest), Cinder Ledger (a contender), Concord Remnant (quietly backs whoever serves its access).

## Surface Problem
Decide who gets the charter — and help make it happen.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The charter contest is a proxy for the city's deeper alignment: a Ledger win strengthens the Monopoly clock and Vyre's hand; a Tolm win entrenches complacency but checks the Ledger; a reform win (Dann) makes the council *capable of heeding the Reach crisis later* — the only outcome that helps the main arc, at the cost of powerful enemies. The Ledger and the Remnant both prefer the council stay pliable; Dann's win is the "hard right thing."

## Objectives
1. Investigate the three candidates (their leverage, their lies, their backers).
2. Choose a side (or play them against each other).
3. Tip the vote — by evidence, persuasion, favor-trading, or public pressure.

## Possible Approaches
- **Reform:** back Dann; gather proof against the Ledger/Tolm; rally the Wardmoot.
- **Power:** back the Ledger (Vell rewards it richly) or Tolm (a treacherous ally vs. the Ledger).
- **Broker:** sell your tipping vote to the highest bidder; bank favors with all sides.
- **Expose:** reveal a candidate's dirt and collapse the contest into scandal.

## Obstacles
A near-even council; bribes and threats; the player's outsider status (DC to be heard); each faction's leverage on the magisters; the temptation to take the easy money.

## Important Scenes
- Three courting offers in one evening at the Pale Star.
- A registry dig that turns up a candidate's hidden debt (or crime).
- The charter-hearing in Magisters' Hall — the player's word or evidence swinging Voss.
- Dann, after, either triumphant and grateful or quietly ruined.

## Combat Options
None required. Crossing the Ledger or Tolm risks hired intimidation (Thug/Spy-like, telegraphed) — a flee/talk/bribe beat, not a brawl. Kade keeps the hall safe.

## Noncombat Options
The entire quest. Persuasion, investigation, favor-trading, and exposure are the tools.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Multiple winning paths (no single roll decides it — evidence/favors stack); the Pale Star and Kade as safety; the player can walk away and the vote still resolves (just not their way).

## Rest And Time Pressure
Safe rest throughout. Time pressure: the vote is on a set day; favors and dirt must be gathered before it; dawdling lets the Ledger or Tolm lock it up.

## Rewards
- **A faction ally seated in power** (Dann, the Ledger, or Tolm — by choice) and a standing favor.
- Coin (a broker's fee or a backer's gift, 100–300 gp by tier).
- City reputation (power-broker); a charter-token contact for future access.
- If Dann wins: the council can later be moved on the Reach crisis (main-arc payoff).

## Consequences
- **Reform wins:** Dann gains power, the Ledger and Tolm become enemies; the city can later act on the crisis.
- **Ledger wins:** the Monopoly clock advances; Vyre owes the player (or owns them).
- **Tolm wins:** complacency entrenched, but the Ledger checked; a treacherous patron gained.
- **Broker/expose:** coin and chaos; the council distrusts the player; opportunities and enemies both.

## Failure State
If the player botches their chosen side, the charter falls to a rival — a setback, not a dead-end. The city's politics simply tilt away from the player; future city quests get harder, not impossible.

## Follow-Up Hooks
The correspondent thread (a magister's odd ally), `Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER` (Ledger entanglement), carrying the Reach warning to a now-reformed council, Tolm's or the Ledger's next scheme.

## Scaling Notes
- L5: a single clear swing vote; lower DCs; modest stakes.
- L9: a tangled multi-magister contest; the Ledger and Remnant both maneuvering; higher DCs and richer rewards; the outcome visibly bends a world clock.
