## AI Use

The clean **on-ramp to Caradril**: a Compact errand to deliver a sealed message to the Tidewater Council gives the player a reason to make the long journey and a first tour of the city's politics. Low-stakes, high-utility; introduces the Magisterium, Sefa Dann, and the unidentified "Magisterium correspondent" thread (lead only). Good for a first city visit at the end of Act 1 / start of Act 2.

## Quest Type
City / Travel / Social

## Level Range
3–6. Solo danger: low (a long road, a political errand; optional road trouble en route).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Secure a council audience to deliver the letter: Persuasion/Etiquette DC 13 (or a sponsor waves it through).
- Notice the letter's reply is odd / who it's really for: Insight DC 14.
- (Optional, intrusive) Read the sealed reply without breaking trust: Sleight of Hand/Investigation DC 15; failure has social cost.
- Road events en route: Survival/Travel DCs per `../../01_runner_protocol/TRAVEL_PROTOCOL.md` and the Reach encounter tables (the journey crosses the Tollwood edge).

## Starting Hook
Reeve Coalmont (or Clerk Pevin Oss) needs a trustworthy hand to carry a sealed Compact petition to Caradril's council — the Reach's plea for aid with the failing-rites crisis. The thin watch can't be spared for a three-week round trip; the player can.

## Location
Hollowmere → the road southeast (Tollwood edge) → Caradril, the Magisterium.

## Involved NPCs
Reeve Coalmont / Pevin Oss (sender), First Magister Voss (recipient), Sefa Dann (the one magister who *cares*), Steward Maell (the Pale Star). Optionally Reke (the sender's letter passes near him — DM seed).

## Involved Factions
Reachward Compact (sends), Tidewater Council (receives, mostly ignores), Cinder Ledger (lobbies the council), Hollow Court (the correspondent thread — DM).

## Surface Problem
Carry the letter to Caradril, deliver it to the council, bring back a reply.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
The council will *shelve* the petition (complacency). But the errand quietly exposes the player to the correspondent thread: the reply, or a second sealed note, is addressed oddly — a lead that **Reke corresponds with someone seated in the Magisterium** (NOT a named second agent; a thread to investigate later, never auto-resolved). Sefa Dann's frustration with the council's inaction is the real payoff: she becomes the player's reform lever.

## Objectives
1. Travel to Caradril (the campaign's first long journey; introduces the city).
2. Deliver the letter to the council.
3. Observe how Caradril treats the Reach's crisis (complacency made concrete).
4. (Optional) Catch the correspondent-thread lead; bring a reply home.

## Possible Approaches
- **Straightforward:** deliver, observe, return — and gain the city as a known place.
- **Political:** use the errand to meet Sefa Dann and start the reform thread.
- **Investigative:** notice the odd reply / sealed note and bank the correspondent lead.
- **Mercantile:** travel with a Ledger caravan (cheaper, safer, indebting).

## Obstacles
The long road (travel events); council gatekeeping; the city's indifference; the temptation to read what isn't yours.

## Important Scenes
- First sight of Caradril after the Reach — stone, bells, no fear of the dead.
- A council clerk taking the petition with a bored "it will be reviewed."
- Sefa Dann, afterward, telling the player the truth: no one here will act.
- (Optional) A sealed note with a wrong name — the first thread of the correspondent mystery.

## Combat Options
None required. Optional road encounters en route (Tollwood-edge bandits or a Remembrance on the old road) — telegraphed, avoidable, per the encounter tables.

## Noncombat Options
The whole quest is travel + social. No fight is needed at any point.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
Travel with a caravan or guide; the Pale Star is a safe city base; the council errand carries no personal danger; the correspondent lead is optional and never gated behind a single roll.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Ample rest on the road (waystations, the Ashwalk Rest at the start) and in Caradril. Mild time pressure: the Reach crisis worsens while the player is away (clocks tick), making the city's indifference sting more on return.

## Rewards
- **Caradril unlocked** as a known hub (the quest's main reward).
- Compact pay (modest; ~25–50 gp + favor with Coalmont).
- Introduction to Sefa Dann (reform lever).
- (Optional) the correspondent-thread lead.

## Consequences
- **Delivered:** the Reach's plea is "on record" and ignored; the player has seen the city's denial.
- **Befriend Dann:** the reform thread opens (`Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER`).
- **Catch the lead:** the correspondent mystery begins (a long-fuse thread toward the Magisterium).
- **Pry too hard:** social cost; the council marks the player as a frontier troublemaker.

## Failure State
If the player never delivers it (lost, sold, ignored), the Compact's plea simply goes unsent — Coalmont is disappointed, the city stays ignorant a while longer. No dead-end; the city is reachable by other hooks.

## Follow-Up Hooks
`Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER` (with Dann), `Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER` (Ledger work), `Q_THE_LANTERN_AND_THE_LAMP` (Briss/Script), the correspondent thread (long-fuse).

## Scaling Notes
- L3: pure travel/social; gentle road events; the city as wonder.
- L6: heavier road events; the correspondent lead more legible; council politics already in motion (the open charter live on arrival).
