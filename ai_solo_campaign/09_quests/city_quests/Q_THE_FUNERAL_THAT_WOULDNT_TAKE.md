## AI Use

The **city-internal M1/M5/M6 thread**: prove that the crisis has reached "untouched" Caradril by uncovering Mother Ysarra's cover-up of failed raise-dead rites (Secret 7). Brings the campaign's horror into the safe city, gives a humane Wren-echo, and links the ignored Wardens to the main arc. Highmourn centerpiece.

## Quest Type
City / Mystery / Investigation / Social

## Level Range
4–8. Solo danger: low (social/investigative; the only "monster" is an optional, tragic wrong-come-back, telegraphed and non-default).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Notice a civic funeral "didn't take": Insight/Religion DC 13.
- Get Grave-Magister Quayle to talk: Persuasion/Insight DC 14 + a promise of safety.
- Find the hushed-up records (re-dug graves): Investigation DC 15 (Bone-clerk Vass's ledger).
- Get Ysarra to admit anything: DC 18 (better to turn Quayle/Vass than confront her).
- Examine a "wrong-come-back" humanely: Medicine/Religion DC 13 (a true rite resolves it).

## Starting Hook
A grand civic funeral falters before the player's eyes — and the clergy smoothly, chillingly, cover it. Or Grave-Magister Quayle, conscience cracking, seeks out the capable newcomer: "Some of our dead won't keep. The Mother says not to speak of it." Or the ignored Warden chapter begs the player to *make someone listen*.

## Location
Caradril, Highmourn (the Threshold Cathedral, the Quiet Houses, the Warden chapter-house).

## Involved NPCs
Mother Ysarra (covers it up), Grave-Magister Quayle (turnable witness), Bone-clerk Vass (the damning ledger), Sister Onae (the ignored Warden), a grieving charter family.

## Involved Factions
Three Thresholds clergy (the cover-up), Ashen Wardens (the ignored truth-tellers), Tidewater Council (pressures the clergy to keep calm), Mourners' Circle (institutional, uneasy).

## Surface Problem
Why are Caradril's funerals — and raisings — quietly failing, and who's hiding it?

## Hidden Truth (DM)
Caradril's raise-dead rites are failing too, and **Ysarra is covering it up** (Secret 7) to prevent panic and protect the Cathedral's authority — hushing away "wrong-come-backs" (a local Wren-echo; M1/M5 in the city). The institutional Mourners and the ignored Wardens both half-know the harvest-truth (a soft M6 echo). This is the city's *own evidence* that the crisis is real, continental, and accelerating — the thing the city most denies.

## Objectives
1. Confirm the failures are real (witness one; find the records).
2. Identify the cover-up (Ysarra) and turn a witness (Quayle/Vass).
3. Decide what to do — expose it, force the council/clergy to act, or quietly help a wrong-come-back rest.

## Possible Approaches
- **Investigate:** the records, the re-dug graves, the pattern.
- **Turn a witness:** Quayle or Vass, with safety, become the player's proof.
- **Ally the Wardens:** Sister Onae confirms the truth and gains a voice.
- **Confront/expose:** force Ysarra or the council to acknowledge the crisis (a civic shock).
- **Humane:** find and lay to rest the hushed-up wrong-come-back (a Wren-echo; a true rite).

## Obstacles
Ysarra's authority and denial; the council's pressure to keep calm; the clergy's institutional weight; the grief of the families involved.

## Important Scenes
- A civic funeral that falters — and the clergy's smooth, cold covering of it.
- Quayle, by lamplight, showing the player a grave that "won't keep."
- Vass's ledger: graves quietly re-dug, names erased.
- Ysarra, gracious and immovable, insisting "Caradril's dead rest well."
- A wrong-come-back found in a sealed tomb — a person, not a monster — and the choice to release them.

## Combat Options
None required. A wrong-come-back is a roleplay/clue scene (flavor a wrong-come-back revenant, AC 12, ~30 HP) resolved by a true rite, a Warden's help, or compassion — combat only if forced, telegraphed, with a non-combat out.

## Noncombat Options
The whole quest is investigation, social, and ritual. The horror is uncovered, not fought.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
A safe district (healing, rest); multiple witness paths; the wrong-come-back is non-default combat with a peaceful resolution; the Wardens as allies.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Abundant safe rest (Highmourn's defining service). Time pressure: each hushed failure makes the next harder to hide; Ysarra may move to silence Quayle if the player dawdles (a short clock).

## Rewards
- **City-internal M1/M5/M6 proof** (the crisis has reached Caradril — usable to wake the council/clergy).
- The ignored Wardens as allies (Sister Onae; a link to Othetha's secret later).
- Quayle/Vass as protected witnesses; the gratitude of a grieving family.
- The catharsis (and clue) of laying a wrong-come-back to rest.

## Consequences
- **Expose it:** Caradril is forced to see the crisis (reformers gain; the conspiracy scrambles; Ysarra disgraced or chastened).
- **Quiet help:** a person laid to rest; the Wardens strengthened; the cover-up persists but the player knows.
- **Confront Ysarra:** she resists or breaks; a major civic scene either way.

## Failure State
If the cover-up holds and the player gets no proof, the city stays in denial a while longer — but the same truths are reachable elsewhere (the Reach, the Archive, the Tallow Man). No dead-end; a missed early warning and a wrong-come-back left to suffer.

## Follow-Up Hooks
Carrying the proof to the council (links `Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER`/Sefa Dann), allying the Wardens (a path to Othetha's confession, C-M6-2), `Q_QUIET_COIN` (the relic trade behind the failures).

## Scaling Notes
- L4: one clear failure, a turnable witness, a gentle wrong-come-back.
- L8: a pattern of failures, a tense Ysarra confrontation, a more anguished revenant; the civic stakes (and the council's reaction) larger.
