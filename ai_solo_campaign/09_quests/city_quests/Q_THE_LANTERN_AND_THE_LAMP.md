## AI Use

The **Concord Script clue-gate quest** at the city scale: learn to read Concord Script from Briss or Tamsin Orr, unlocking nearly every deep clue in the campaign, and get a first, *fragmentary* taste of the Lantern Reach's deeper secrets. The on-ramp to `Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE`. Mostly safe, scholarly, relationship-building; sets up the Briss-disillusionment lever.

## Quest Type
City / Faction / Investigation (downtime-heavy)

## Level Range
3–7. Solo danger: very low (a college; the only risk is intellectual and social).

## Likely Checks And DCs
- Befriend Briss (or Tamsin Orr): Persuasion DC 12 (Briss is warm; Tamsin warmer).
- Learn Concord Script: a **downtime activity** over several sessions (no single roll; track progress in `PLAYER_CHARACTER.md`). Each study-block advances fluency; a relevant Int (History/Arcana) check DC 13 can accelerate it.
- Read a first gated inscription: Investigation DC 13 once basic fluency is reached.
- (Optional) Sense the Remnant's deeper game: Insight DC 15 (Briss lets something slip; Quorrin tests you).

## Starting Hook
Lector Briss — met in the Reach or here — offers to teach the curious player Concord Script "so the ruins can speak." Or Tamsin Orr, his student, takes the player on for free, no strings. Either way, the door to reading the Concord opens.

## Location
Caradril, the Lantern Reach (Lamplighters' Hall, the Stacks). Briss also teaches in the Reach.

## Involved NPCs
Lector Briss (warm idealist tutor), Tamsin Orr (strings-free tutor), Quorrin Vane (the watching Archivist), Binder Oslo (the Stacks).

## Involved Factions
Concord Remnant (the only fluent readers of Script).

## Surface Problem
Learn to read Concord Script.

## Hidden Truth (DM)
Concord Script is the **key clue-gate** for M2, M3, M6, M9, and the shrine inscriptions. Learning it from the Remnant means entering Quorrin's orbit — the inner circle evaluates the player as a useful tool. Briss is a sincere dupe; Tamsin is innocent. The Stacks yield *fragments* (M2/M3-level), but the apex truth is locked in the Sealed Archive (`Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE`) and act-paced — never surface the harvest/Quietfall apex here in Act 1–2. The relationship built here is the foundation of the **Briss-disillusionment lever** (Secret 13) that can shatter the Remnant later.

## Objectives
1. Befriend a tutor (Briss or Tamsin).
2. Learn Concord Script (the campaign's master clue-key; a downtime reward).
3. Read a first gated inscription (a Stacks fragment, or a Reach text the player carries).
4. (Optional) Begin to sense the Remnant's deeper game.

## Possible Approaches
- **Through Briss:** warm, fast, but ties the player to the Remnant's public face (and the disillusionment lever).
- **Through Tamsin:** slower, strings-free, the "clean" path.
- **Self-study + a relic/rubbing:** with a text in hand and a tutor's notes, learn the basics independently.

## Obstacles
The time the learning takes (downtime; the world clocks tick); Quorrin's quiet evaluation; the temptation to trust the Remnant too far.

## Important Scenes
- Briss lighting up explaining the Concord's lost roads, then troubled when the player asks the wrong question.
- Tamsin teaching Script late by green lamp, generous and unaware.
- The first inscription the player reads *themselves* — the ruins beginning to speak.
- Quorrin "happening by," courteous, reading the player like a text.

## Combat Options
None. This is a peaceful, scholarly quest by design — a deliberate low-danger beat amid the city's intrigue.

## Noncombat Options
Entirely social and intellectual.

## Solo-Play Safety Valves
No danger; the college is safe; learning Script has no failure state (only a time cost); two tutors so losing one doesn't block the gate.

## Rest And Time Pressure
Fully safe rest. Time pressure only at the world-clock level: time spent studying is time the harvest advances — a meaningful choice, not a threat.

## Rewards
- **Concord Script fluency** — the single most useful clue-key in the campaign (a permanent, downtime-earned capability).
- A tutor-ally (Briss or Tamsin) and Stacks access.
- A first gated clue (M2/M3 fragment).
- Groundwork for the Briss-disillusionment lever (Secret 13).

## Consequences
- **Learn it:** every gated clue in the campaign opens; the deep history becomes reachable.
- **Tie to Briss:** the disillusionment lever is set up (a future faction-shatter).
- **Draw Quorrin's eye:** the Remnant marks the player as useful — opportunity and danger both.

## Failure State
If the player never learns Script here, it's still learnable via the Reach (Candlewick's `Q_THE_SOLD_STONE`, Briss in the field) — no dead-end. Without *any* Script, gated clues remain reachable through non-textual paths (the Tallow Man's witness, Othetha's confession, relic study) per the three-clue rule.

## Follow-Up Hooks
`Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE` (the apex cache), reading the shrine inscriptions, translating Reach finds, disillusioning Briss.

## Scaling Notes
- L3: a gentle on-ramp; basic fluency; safe fragments.
- L7: faster fluency; the player reads harder texts; Quorrin's evaluation sharpens; the Archive becomes the obvious next step.
