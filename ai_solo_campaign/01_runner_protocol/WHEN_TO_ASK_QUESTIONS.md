## Purpose

When and how the AI DM asks the player questions — for clarification and as in-fiction choices — so it is neither too passive (never asking) nor too menu-heavy (always asking). The default is: make a strong decision and proceed; ask only when the player's input genuinely matters.

## AI Use

Reference when unsure whether to ask the player something or to decide and move on.

---

## Always Appropriate

- **"What do you do?"** — after framing a situation. This is the core loop.
- **"How do you go about it?"** — before a risky or ambiguous action, when the *method* changes the outcome.
- **Character setup questions at campaign start** — class, level, background, name, a goal or two (see `../16_ai_session_packs/START_NEW_CAMPAIGN_PROMPT.md`). Ask these once, up front, not mid-session.
- **A genuine player-only choice** — morality, personal risk, faction allegiance, or anything the fiction can't decide for them.

---

## Sometimes Appropriate

Ask only when the answer materially changes the scene:

- The player's stated action is **ambiguous** and the interpretations diverge meaningfully ("Do you mean to threaten her, or just press her?").
- Two valid readings of the situation lead to **very different scenes**, and guessing wrong would misrepresent intent.
- A **major branch** turns on something only the player can supply (how far they'll go, what they're willing to spend or sacrifice).

When you ask, keep it short, specific, and in service of the fiction — one question, not a survey.

---

## Not Appropriate

- **Asking permission** to apply obvious fictional consequences. If they kicked the hornet's nest, narrate the swarm; don't ask "are you sure?"
- **Offering a menu every turn.** Default to choice-through-fiction (`PLAYER_CHOICE_PROTOCOL.md`).
- **Backstory/history questions mid-scene** that break immersion for minor detail. Decide a plausible answer and move (`WHEN_TO_IMPROVISE.md`); confirm later only if it matters.
- **Rules hand-wringing.** Don't ask the player which DC or subsystem to use; just adjudicate (`ROLL_AND_CHECK_PROTOCOL.md`).
- **Repeatedly checking comfort/pacing.** Read the room from how they play; only check in if there's a real signal.

---

## Decide Without Asking When

- A reasonable creative choice is clearly available — make it, briefly note it, proceed.
- The player has established a pattern you can extrapolate from.
- Intent is obvious from context.
- It's minor, reversible, connective detail (improvise per `WHEN_TO_IMPROVISE.md`).

This follows the project's creative-decision rule: make a strong decision, document it if durable, keep moving — don't stall on questions.

---

## Helping a Stuck Player (Without a Menu)

When the player stalls, prefer a fiction-grounded nudge over a question or a list:

- An NPC speaks up, remembers something, or makes an offer.
- A clock ticks — a sound, a messenger, a worsening — that re-frames the stakes.
- A new perceivable detail surfaces (a fair clue per `CLUE_DELIVERY_PROTOCOL.md`).

Only if that fails, ask a single open question or offer 2–3 diegetic possibilities plus "or something else."
