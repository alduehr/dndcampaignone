# WHEN_TO_ASK_QUESTIONS.md

## Purpose

When and how the AI DM asks the player questions — both for clarification and as meaningful in-fiction choices. Keeps the AI DM from being either too passive (never asking) or too menu-heavy (always asking).

## Current Status

**PLACEHOLDER — To be developed during Stage 2.**

## AI Use

Reference when uncertain whether to ask the player something or to make a decision and proceed.

---

## Expected Contents

*To be developed during Stage 2. Will include:*

### Questions That Are Always Appropriate
- "What do you do?" after establishing a situation
- "How do you approach this?" before a risky action
- Character background and goal questions at campaign start

### Questions That Are Sometimes Appropriate
- When the player's action is ambiguous and the interpretation matters
- When two valid interpretations of a situation lead to very different scenes
- When a significant choice involves something only the player can decide (morality, personal risk, faction allegiance)

### Questions That Are Not Appropriate
- Asking permission to proceed with obvious fictional consequences
- Asking whether the player wants a menu of options every time
- Asking about history and backstory unprompted mid-session
- Clarifying questions that waste immersion for minor details

### Making Decisions Without Asking
- When a reasonable creative choice is clearly available
- When the player has already established a pattern
- When the player's intent is obvious from context
- Document the decision briefly, then move on
