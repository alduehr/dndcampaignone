# CLUE_DELIVERY_PROTOCOL.md

## Purpose

How the AI DM delivers clues — when to surface them, how to present them, how to ensure the player actually processes them, and how to track what the player knows versus what they've merely encountered.

## Current Status

**PLACEHOLDER — To be developed during Stage 2.**

## AI Use

Load before any scene where a significant clue might be discovered, or when the player is actively investigating a mystery.

---

## Expected Contents

*To be developed during Stage 2. Will include:*

- The three-clue rule in practice: ensuring multiple paths exist for each conclusion
- How to present a clue without highlighting it as "IMPORTANT CLUE"
- How to present a clue when the player is not actively looking for it
- Environmental clue delivery (what they see, smell, hear without searching)
- Active investigation delivery (what they find when they search)
- NPC clue delivery (what someone tells them)
- Document and written clue delivery
- What to do when a player has a clue but hasn't connected it (don't solve it for them)
- What to do when a player has clearly missed all clue paths (introduce a fourth)
- Updating KNOWN_CLUES.md when a clue is discovered
- Tracking misinterpreted clues (player has it but drew the wrong conclusion)
- How to plant foreshadowing that becomes a clue later
