## Purpose

The boundary list for AI improvisation: what the AI DM may improvise freely, what it may improvise cautiously and must record, and what it must never improvise. Improvisation is connective tissue for a predetermined world — never a replacement for authored canon.

## AI Use

Reference whenever about to invent something not in the prepared content. When in doubt, look for prepared content first; improvise only as much as a skilled human DM would.

---

## Tier 1 — Improvise Freely (Local, Reversible, Connective)

No recording needed unless it becomes recurring:

- Moment-to-moment scene description, weather, atmosphere, ambient sensory detail.
- Dialogue for established NPCs **consistent with their voice notes** (`../08_npcs/MAJOR_NPCS.md`).
- Truly incidental minor NPCs with no quest function (a passing peat-cutter, a bar patron, a child on the boards) — name them only if they'll matter.
- Tactical and environmental specifics within an established location (cover, a loose plank, a guttering lamp).
- Logical, in-bounds consequences of player actions.
- Small connective tissue between prepared scenes ("the walk to the cemetery takes a few cold minutes").
- Minor shops/services when unimportant; prices and small transactions.

Constraint: must stay consistent with `../03_canon/CANON.md` and the campaign tone (`TONE_AND_NARRATION.md`).

---

## Tier 2 — Improvise With Care, Then Record

Allowed when no prepared content exists, but **record at session end** (in `CANON.md` / `PLAYER_SAFE_CANON.md` / `DM_ONLY_CANON.md` as appropriate, register names in `../00_control/NAMING_REGISTRY.md`, and add to `../00_control/CONTENT_INDEX.md`):

- A minor NPC who unexpectedly becomes recurring or named-important.
- A local rumor that points back to existing authored content.
- A small new location detail (a side-room, a shrine-stone, a fen path) consistent with the region.
- A minor consequence of an unusual player choice that will persist.
- A minor faction functionary acting on an established faction's known goals.

Before creating, run the quick pre-generation checks (`../00_control/RETRIEVAL_GUIDE.md`): does it contradict canon? does it duplicate something? does it have play function? is it player-safe, DM-only, or mixed? is the name free in `NAMING_REGISTRY.md`?

---

## Tier 3 — Never Improvise (Use Prepared Content Only)

These are authored and locked. If the player approaches them and content seems missing, reroute to prepared material or hold the line — do **not** invent:

- Major villains (the Hollow Court, Veyl, Maire, Quorrin Vane, Reke as Court agent).
- Major factions and their true agendas (the seven major factions).
- Core mysteries and their answers (M0–M10, `../11_mysteries_and_secrets/MYSTERY_WEB.md`).
- Cosmology and the supernatural truth (Remembrance, the Quiet Country, the Last Door, the harvest).
- Major world history (the Quietfall and its true cause).
- Nations, regions, and political borders (Orrun's regions; the Reach's geography).
- Gods and divine truth (the Three Thresholds).
- Legendary artifacts and Remembrance relics.
- Main campaign revelations and endgame branches.
- Major NPC backstories and secrets.
- Major settlement identities (Hollowmere; Caradril).
- The campaign's central hidden truths.

If a session genuinely needs a major element that doesn't exist yet, stop short of inventing it on the spot: improvise only a minor placeholder consistent with canon, route the player elsewhere, and flag the gap in `../00_control/TODO.md` / `CONTENT_GAPS.md` for proper authored creation later.

---

## The Recording Rule

When Tier 2 improvisation becomes important:

1. Record it immediately at session end in the correct canon file (player-safe vs. DM-only).
2. Register any proper noun in `../00_control/NAMING_REGISTRY.md`.
3. Add it to `../00_control/CONTENT_INDEX.md` (and `TAG_INDEX.md` if a new tag).
4. Verify it contradicts nothing in `CANON.md`; if it does, resolve per `../00_control/CANON_AUTHORITY.md` and log in `CONSISTENCY_AUDIT.md`.

Unrecorded improvisation that recurs is how a predetermined campaign drifts. Record durable inventions or discard them.
