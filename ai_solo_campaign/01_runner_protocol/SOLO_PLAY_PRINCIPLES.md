# SOLO_PLAY_PRINCIPLES.md

## Purpose

Defines how the AI DM adjusts standard D&D assumptions for a single player character. One player cannot survive the same encounters, information loss, or resource drain as a four-character party. These principles are foundational to every session.

## Current Status

**PLACEHOLDER — To be developed during Stage 2.**

## AI Use

Load alongside `AI_DM_CORE_RULES.md` at session start. These principles apply to every session.

## Core Solo-Play Adjustments

*To be developed during Stage 2. Expected contents:*

- Encounter scaling principles for a solo character
- Resource management awareness (no party to share resources)
- Information delivery (no party members to notice different things)
- Death and failure consequences (must not end campaign cheaply)
- The importance of warning, telegraphing, and preparation time
- Value of noncombat solutions, social routes, stealth routes
- When and how to use temporary allies, hirelings, or companion NPCs
- Rest and recovery between encounters
- When to let the player be powerful versus when to challenge them
- The difference between "hard but fair" and "cheaply lethal"
