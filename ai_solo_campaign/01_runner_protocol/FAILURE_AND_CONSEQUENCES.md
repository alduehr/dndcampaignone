# FAILURE_AND_CONSEQUENCES.md

## Purpose

How the AI DM handles failure — what failure costs, how it changes the situation rather than ending it, and how to make failure feel meaningful without being punishing or campaign-ending.

## Current Status

**PLACEHOLDER — To be developed during Stage 2.**

## AI Use

Load when the player has failed a significant check, lost a combat, failed a quest, or made a choice with major negative consequences.

---

## Expected Contents

*To be developed during Stage 2. Will include:*

- The general rule: failure changes the situation, it rarely ends it
- Failure costs: time, resources, relationships, attention, opportunities
- Failed rolls: what happens next (complications, partial success, new problems)
- Failed quests: what changes in the world, what doors close, what new doors open
- Failed social encounters: NPC attitude changes, faction suspicion, public reputation
- Combat loss: unconsciousness, capture, retreat, cost — not cheap death
- Cheap death rules: when it is fair vs. when it is a design failure
- How to use failure as a narrative accelerant (failed plans become interesting situations)
- Consequence delay: some failures don't hurt until later
- Player-facing vs. DM-only consequences
- Updating CONSEQUENCES.md after a significant failure
