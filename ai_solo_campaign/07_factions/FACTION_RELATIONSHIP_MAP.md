## AI Use

Load when the player's actions affect more than one faction, when running a faction turn (`FACTION_TURN_RULES.md`), when a faction reacts to the player helping its rival, or when narrating how an event ripples across the Reach. This is the authoritative pairwise map of every major faction relationship — **public reason is player-safe; hidden reason is DM-only and must never be narrated as fact.**

A compact version of this map lives inside `FACTION_INDEX.md`; this file is the full, every-pair expansion required for faction turns and consequence tracking.

## How To Read This File

- **Relationship** — the structural stance (Allied / Cautious allies / Strained / Rivals / Enemies / Infiltration / Unaware).
- **Public Reason** — what people in the Reach believe the relationship is (safe to surface).
- **Hidden Reason (DM)** — the true driver (never narrate as fact; reveal only through earned play).
- **Current Tension** — the live friction at campaign start (Act 1), and what raises or lowers it.
- **Player Lever** — how the player can shift this specific relationship.

## Player-Facing Faction Pairs (Six Open Factions)

The six factions the player can openly meet, join, or oppose: Ashen Wardens, Cinder Ledger, Mourners' Circle, Reachward Compact, Gravecallers, Concord Remnant. The Hollow Court's relationships are in their own DM-only section below.

### Ashen Wardens ↔ Cinder Ledger

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Strained / wary |
| Public Reason | The Wardens serve the dead for free; the Ledger profits from everything, including grave-salvage near the sites Wardens tend. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | The Ledger's relic-buying quietly undermines the seal the Wardens unknowingly guard; Othetha senses (without knowing why) that the relic trade is dangerous. |
| Current Tension | Low but rising — Wardens disapprove of salvage crews; the Ledger finds Wardens "unprofitable" and inconvenient. |
| Player Lever | Bring the Wardens proof the Ledger is buying grave-relics (raises Warden hostility to the Ledger); or broker a truce by routing salvage away from tended sites. |

### Ashen Wardens ↔ Mourners' Circle

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Allied (closest natural alliance in the Reach) |
| Public Reason | Both revere letting the dead rest; the Circle grieves, the Wardens lay — they share every funeral. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | The Wardens secretly guard the seal; the Circle secretly carries the folk-memory of the harvest. Neither knows the other's secret, but their instincts align. |
| Current Tension | Almost none; both are overwhelmed by the failures and lean on each other. The one fracture: militant Mourners want to *destroy* shrines, which would horrify the Wardens. |
| Player Lever | Strengthen the alliance by serving both at funerals; or split them by pushing the Circle toward militancy (the Wardens recoil). |

### Ashen Wardens ↔ Reachward Compact

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Cautious allies / dependent |
| Public Reason | The Compact funds and licenses the Wardens; the Wardens keep the towns from panic. A working partnership. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | Reke (Court agent) steers the Compact toward the basin scheme the Wardens would oppose if they understood it; Othetha is quietly alarmed but oath-bound to silence. |
| Current Tension | Rising under the surface — Othetha distrusts the basin push but cannot say why; the Compact underfunds the Wardens as failures mount. |
| Player Lever | Get the Compact to fund the Wardens (eases the Wardens-Break clock); or expose that the basin scheme endangers what the Wardens protect. |

### Ashen Wardens ↔ Gravecallers

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Enemies |
| Public Reason | The Gravecallers *call* the dead the Wardens lay; the Wardens see them as the desecrators who undo their work. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | The Wardens unknowingly guard the seal the Gravecallers want to break; this is the campaign's deep structural conflict played out by people who don't grasp it. |
| Current Tension | High and rising — Gravecallers gain ground among the grieving as rites fail; Wardens grow harsher toward suspected cultists. |
| Player Lever | A player serving one side is treated as an enemy by the other; mediating is nearly impossible but a synthesis-path player may try. |

### Ashen Wardens ↔ Concord Remnant

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Wary |
| Public Reason | The Wardens are humble rite-keepers; the Remnant are ambitious scholars who "want the old order back" — the Wardens distrust grand ambitions about the dead. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | The Wardens were founded to keep the harvest paused; the Remnant inner circle wants to restart it. They are unknowing opposites. Othetha would fight the Reclaimers if she understood them. |
| Current Tension | Low at campaign start (the Remnant is mostly in Caradril); rises in Acts 2–3 as Remnant agents reach the Reach ruins. |
| Player Lever | Warn Othetha about the Remnant's relic-hunger; or use Warden rite-knowledge to beat the Remnant to a ruin. |

### Cinder Ledger ↔ Mourners' Circle

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Enemies |
| Public Reason | The Ledger buys "old Concord salvage" — which the Circle calls grave-robbery and desecration of the dead. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | The Ledger's monopoly plan would make the dead into inventory — the exact horror the Circle's folk-memory warns against. |
| Current Tension | High; mob action is brewing. The Circle blocks salvage caravans; a relic-buyer has already been roughed up in some towns. |
| Player Lever | Side with the Circle to drive Ledger buyers out of a town; or protect a Ledger caravan and earn the Circle's lasting cold. |

### Cinder Ledger ↔ Reachward Compact

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Allied |
| Public Reason | The Ledger funds Compact works (roads, ferries, and the basin-drainage project); the Compact gives the Ledger contracts and standing. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | The Ledger profits from the basin scheme without knowing it serves Reke's plan to reopen the shrine; an alliance of convenience steering toward catastrophe. |
| Current Tension | None public; this is the comfortable money-and-power alliance the cautious Compact bloc (Coalmont) distrusts on principle. |
| Player Lever | Cut the Ledger's funding of the basin works to slow the Basin clock; or expose the Ledger-Compact coziness to turn the public against both. |

### Cinder Ledger ↔ Gravecallers

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Enemies (uneven) |
| Public Reason | The Ledger backs the Compact that outlaws the Gravecallers; the Gravecallers see relic-buyers as thieves of the dead. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | The Ledger covets the very relics the Gravecallers steal to break the harvest; both want Concord relics, for opposite ends — a quiet bidding war over the same graves. |
| Current Tension | Low-visibility but real; relic theft and relic-buying compete in the same ruins. |
| Player Lever | Play relic-supplier to one and informant to the other; or expose a Gravecaller cell to the Ledger's hired hands. |

### Cinder Ledger ↔ Concord Remnant

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Secret allies (mutual mistrust) |
| Public Reason | None public — to outsiders, a bank and a scholars' society have no obvious tie. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | Vyre and Quorrin are secretly negotiating a **Remembrance monopoly**: the Ledger's money and reach plus the Remnant's knowledge of what the relics are. Each plans to betray the other. The deal is sealed in Caradril (city clock C1). |
| Current Tension | Hidden; both circle each other, each believing they hold the better hand. |
| Player Lever | Expose the deal (shatters both factions' standing); turn Coll Riis or Domic Sael; play Vyre and Quorrin against each other (`Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER`, `Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE`). |

### Mourners' Circle ↔ Reachward Compact

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Strained |
| Public Reason | The Compact's basin-drainage scheme would disturb the dead and drown graves; the Circle is furious and the Compact is dismissive. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | Reke (Court agent) drives the scheme; the Circle's fury is morally right but aimed at the wrong target — they blame the Compact, not the hidden Court. |
| Current Tension | High; this is one of the campaign's loudest open conflicts at the start. The Circle petitions, protests, and threatens non-cooperation. |
| Player Lever | Champion the Circle in a Compact vote to slow the Basin clock; or broker between Coalmont and Wend; or let the conflict boil into the Circle's militancy. |

### Mourners' Circle ↔ Gravecallers

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Enemies — but radicals secretly overlap |
| Public Reason | The Gravecallers call the dead the Circle wants released; the Circle publicly despises them as defilers. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | Both ultimately want the harvest *ended*. The Circle's radical elders would ally with the Gravecallers to destroy a shrine; Iola Wend already bridges them secretly. |
| Current Tension | Publicly hostile, privately converging — the most volatile hidden tie in the Reach. |
| Player Lever | Expose Iola's contact (splits the Circle); or quietly help the radical-Mourner / Gravecaller alliance toward (dangerous) shrine-breaking; or talk both back from the brink. |

### Mourners' Circle ↔ Concord Remnant

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Enemies (latent) |
| Public Reason | The Remnant wants to restore the Concord — and the Circle's folk-songs (dismissed as superstition) say the Concord *ate the dead*. The Circle distrusts anything that glorifies the old order. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | The Circle's folk-memory is the moral truth; the Remnant inner circle wants to repeat the Concord's sin. They are moral opposites who barely know each other yet. |
| Current Tension | Low at start (little contact); rises sharply if Remnant agents come buying relics in Circle towns. |
| Player Lever | Bring Wend's old songs to a sympathetic Remnant scholar (Briss) as a clue-bridge; or set the Circle against arriving Remnant agents. |

### Reachward Compact ↔ Gravecallers

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Enemies (law vs. outlaw) |
| Public Reason | The Compact has outlawed the Gravecallers by decree; the cult evades the watch. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | Reke (Court agent) is happy to see the Gravecallers persecuted — they are the faction most likely to break the harvest he wants restarted. The purge serves the Court. |
| Current Tension | Persistent; periodic watch sweeps drive the cult deeper into the fens and harden its radicals. |
| Player Lever | Hunt cells for the Compact (legal reward, Gravecaller enmity); or shield the cult from a purge (Gravecaller trust, Compact suspicion). |

### Reachward Compact ↔ Concord Remnant

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Cautious / transactional |
| Public Reason | The Remnant offers the only readers of Concord Script the Compact needs for surveys and old land-claims; the Compact tolerates the scholars. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | Remnant agents quietly use Compact survey access to locate Concord sites (and the shrine); Reke subtly aids them, content to let a rival racer expose the basin for him. |
| Current Tension | Low and bureaucratic; deepens in Act 3 as the Remnant's Reach presence grows. |
| Player Lever | Use Compact records plus Remnant translation to beat both to a truth; or expose Remnant agents abusing Compact access. |

### Gravecallers ↔ Concord Remnant

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Enemies (deep) |
| Public Reason | Little public contact; one is an outlawed fen-cult, the other a city scholars' society. |
| Hidden Reason (DM) | They are mirror opposites who both know fragments of the truth: the Gravecallers want to **destroy** the harvest, the Remnant inner circle wants to **seize and restart** it. If they ever fully understood each other, it would be war. |
| Current Tension | Hidden; both are "right" about the harvest and want opposite outcomes. |
| Player Lever | Carry truth between them to provoke the conflict; or learn from the Gravecallers to undercut the Remnant's monopoly on Concord knowledge. |

## The Hollow Court's Hidden Relationships (DM-ONLY)

**DM-ONLY. Never narrate any of this as fact. The Court is a ghost story to the player until a late-arc earned revelation.** The Court has no public relationships — every tie below is invisible, mediated by cutouts, or structural.

### Hollow Court → Ashen Wardens

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Natural enemies (invisible) |
| Hidden Reason | The Wardens were founded by Quietfall survivors to keep the harvest *paused*; the Court is restarting it. The order is the seal's guardian and thus the Court's deepest structural opponent — though almost no Warden knows it, and the Court does not regard the humble Wardens as a serious threat. |
| Live tension | The Court (via Reke) maneuvers to undercut Warden authority and reopen the shrine the Wardens unknowingly protect. |
| Player Lever | Strengthening/awakening the Wardens (protecting Othetha, reaching her confession) is the single most direct counter to the Court's plan. |

### Hollow Court → Reachward Compact

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Infiltration |
| Hidden Reason | Councilor Aldous Reke is a **knowing Court agent** steering the Compact to drain the basin and reopen the shrine, disguised as a water-rights/resettlement project. |
| Live tension | Reke works through procedure; the cautious bloc (Coalmont) resists without knowing why. |
| Player Lever | Expose Reke (Secret 9); sway the Compact vote; sabotage the works — all slow the Master clock. |

### Hollow Court → Cinder Ledger

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Unwitting instrument |
| Hidden Reason | The Ledger funds the basin works for profit, not knowing the works serve the Court. The Court is content to let greed do its labor. |
| Live tension | None felt by the Ledger; the Court simply benefits from the Ledger-Compact alliance. |
| Player Lever | Cut Ledger funding to slow the basin works; the Court has no direct hold on the Ledger to replace it quickly. |

### Hollow Court → Concord Remnant

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Rivals (mutual partial awareness, late) |
| Hidden Reason | Both want the harvest; the Remnant inner circle would *replace* the Court as the new Custodians. Quorrin has deciphered enough to suspect the Court exists and is racing it to the keystone. The Court regards the Remnant as upstart pretenders. |
| Live tension | A hidden race for the shrine; neither yet holds the other's full hand. |
| Player Lever | Set the two against each other in the endgame; expose each to the other; or exploit the race to reach the keystone first. |

### Hollow Court → Gravecallers

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Hidden enemies (the Court's worst threat) |
| Hidden Reason | The Gravecallers want to **shatter** the harvest the Court is restarting. They are the faction whose success would most catastrophically thwart the Court — and they are the only faction speaking to the dead who *witnessed* the truth. The Court quietly benefits from the Compact's persecution of them. |
| Live tension | The Gravecaller radicals (Cole Ashby) inch toward the shrine; the Court works to see them broken or beaten there. |
| Player Lever | Protect or empower the Gravecallers to threaten the Court's plan; or (fearing catastrophe) stop them — either way the player is acting on the Court's deepest conflict. |

### Hollow Court → Mourners' Circle

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Relationship | Hidden enemies (moral) |
| Hidden Reason | The Circle's folk-memory carries the moral truth — that the Concord ate the dead — and the radical Mourners would destroy the shrine. The Court sees them as superstitious folk who happen to be dangerously close to the truth. |
| Live tension | The Circle's fury at the basin scheme indirectly opposes the Court; the Court relies on the Circle blaming the Compact, not the hidden hand behind it. |
| Player Lever | Decode Wend's old songs to name the Court's crime; turn the Circle's moral force from protest into informed opposition. |

## Triangle and Bloc Notes (for faction turns)

- **The Seal Bloc (Wardens + Mourners):** the campaign's "let the dead rest / keep it sealed" coalition. Strengthens toward the *Seal it* ending. Fractures only if the Mourners turn militant (shrine-breaking), which the Wardens cannot abide.
- **The Profit-and-Power Bloc (Ledger + Remnant inner circle):** the secret monopoly deal; trends toward the *Seize it / Sell it* endings. Held together by greed and ambition; shatters if the deal is exposed or one betrays the other (they will).
- **The Liberation Edge (Gravecallers + radical Mourners):** the "destroy it / free the dead" tendency; trends toward the *Destroy it* ending. Bridged secretly by Iola Wend; catastrophic if it reaches the keystone alone.
- **The Hidden Hand (Hollow Court via Reke + the steering of the player):** drives the Master clock through the Compact and the basin works; trends toward *Restart it controlled* by default if the player never intervenes.
- **The Swing Pieces:** the **Compact** (governed by whoever wins its votes — Coalmont vs. Reke) and the **player** (the true swing vote of the endgame). The synthesis (*Transform it*) ending requires uniting pieces from across these blocs.
