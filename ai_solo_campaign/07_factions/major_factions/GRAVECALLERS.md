## AI Use

Load when the player seeks forbidden knowledge about the dead, encounters the outlawed cult, or pursues the "what is Remembrance / free the dead" thread. The Gravecallers are feared, persecuted, partly right, and dangerously extreme.

## One-Sentence Identity

An outlawed, persecuted circle that genuinely speaks with the dead — and, having learned a fragment of the truth, wants to free them by breaking the harvest, whatever it costs.

## Public Face

Boogeymen. Said to creep into graveyards and "call the dead back," to traffic with Remembrances, to lure the grieving with promises of a last word with a lost loved one. Outlawed by the Reachward Compact. Most people fear and revile them. The desperate sometimes seek them out — and a few never come home.

## True Agenda (DM-Only)

The Gravecallers **really can speak with Remembrances** — and from the dead themselves they have learned fragments of the truth: that the Concord *ate* the dead, that a machine is waking, that the dead are not free. Their goal is liberation: **break the harvest, shatter the shrines, free every soul.** They are the campaign's tragic mirror — closer to the truth than anyone, but their method would **end resurrection entirely and destabilize the boundary between living and dead**, a catastrophe they refuse to admit. They are not evil; they are desperate, persecuted, and right enough to be dangerous.

## Founding / History

Formed ~AQ 80 from grief-mad folk who discovered, in the worsening leakage, that the dead would *answer*. They organized as the rites began failing, gathering forbidden knowledge the rest of the world fears to hear.

## Leader

**The Tallow Man**, the cult's "speaker," whose voice (it is said) is not entirely his own. See `MAJOR_NPCS.md`.

## Important Members

- **The Tallow Man** — speaker; channels a real Remembrance that witnessed the Concord's sin.
- **Sister Knell** — recruiter; gentle, persuasive, finds the grieving.
- **Cole Ashby** — young zealot; would break a shrine tomorrow.

## Resources

Forbidden knowledge (real history, from the dead themselves), the ability to speak with Remembrances, hidden meeting-places in the fens and ruins, the loyalty of the bereaved, the Gravecaller Knock (secret signaling code), a few stolen Concord "breaking-tools."

## Methods

Secret rites, recruitment of the grieving, theft of relics and breaking-tools, and — increasingly — direct action against the shrine and its protectors.

## Territory / Influence

No territory; cells hidden across the Reach, densest near the Greyfens and old ruins.

## Internal Conflict

The Tallow Man counsels patience and *learning* from the dead; Cole Ashby and the radicals want to break the shrine now, consequences be damned. Sister Knell fears they are being used by the very Remembrances they channel.

## Allies

Secretly, the radical wing of the Mourners' Circle (Iola Wend). Otherwise universally opposed.

## Enemies

The Ashen Wardens (who lay the dead the Gravecallers call). The Reachward Compact (which outlaws them). The Hollow Court (whose harvest they want to destroy — though they don't yet know the Court exists). Frightened townsfolk.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- They are *right* about the harvest — a primary truth-source for the player.
- The Tallow Man's channeled Remembrance is an actual Quietfall-era witness; speaking with it is a major clue path (and a danger — it has its own agenda).
- Breaking the shrine would cause catastrophe; the radicals hide or deny this even from themselves.

## Current Activity

Recruiting frightened, grief-struck Reach folk as the rites fail. Probing the drowned shrine. Watching the basin-drainage project with grim interest — it may hand them the access they crave.

## Escalation Clock — "The Breaking"

### What Advances The Clock
Persecution; failed rites; the shrine being exposed (basin clock); the radicals gaining tools.

- **Stage 1:** Gravecaller cells swell with the desperate; whispered truths spread.
- **Stage 2:** They acquire Concord breaking-tools and a map of the shrine.
- **Stage 3:** A radical cell attempts to breach the shrine to "free the dead."
- **Stage 4:** They attempt to shatter the harvest's keystone.
- **Final Consequence:** If they succeed alone, the dead are freed violently and the living/dead boundary tears — the chaotic-liberation ending, with terrible side effects.

### How The Player Can Slow/Stop/Redirect/Exploit It
Learn the truth from them; protect them from a purge; talk the radicals down; help them break the harvest *safely* (the hard synthesis path); or expose/stop them if the player fears the catastrophe.

## Quests Offered By This Faction
Steal a Concord relic; protect a cell from the watch; carry a message from the dead to the living; help breach a ruin; learn the Gravecaller Knock.

## Quests Against This Faction
Hunt a cell for the Compact; rescue a grieving person being recruited; stop the radicals from breaking the shrine.

## How They React To The Player
Cautious, then eager if the player shows grief, curiosity, or hatred of the harvest. Hostile if the player serves the Compact or Wardens against them.

## What They Do If Ignored
Grow, radicalize, and move on the shrine per the clock — potentially triggering catastrophe without the player.

## How They Can Help A Solo Player
The richest *truth* source in Act 1–2; access to the dead's own testimony; forbidden lore; hidden routes through the fens; desperate but real allies.

## How They Can Threaten A Solo Player Without Cheap Death
They can mark the player as an enemy of the dead, send Remembrances to harass, lure the player into the fens, or (radicals) treat the player as an obstacle to "the Breaking." Cole Ashby is the most dangerous.

## Mechanical Notes
- **The Tallow Man:** Tier 1 — **Cult Fanatic/Warlock-like** (AC 12, ~45 HP, *Speak with Dead* at will, *Hold Person, Spare the Dying, Phantasmal Force*; can briefly call a spectral Remembrance as backup). Avoids cheap death; flees into the fens.
- **Cole Ashby:** Tier 1 lite — **Acolyte/Berserker-like** zealot (AC 13, ~30 HP). Dangerous when committed to "the Breaking."
- Summoned Remembrances: flavor **specters/ghosts**; telegraph and allow social resolution where possible.

## Related NPCs
The Tallow Man, Sister Knell, Cole Ashby; secretly Iola Wend.

## Related Locations
Greyfens hideouts, old ruins, the drowned shrine's approaches.

## Related Mysteries
What Remembrance is; the Concord's harvest; the cost of breaking a shrine.
