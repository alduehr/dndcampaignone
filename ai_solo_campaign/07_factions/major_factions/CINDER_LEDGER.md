## AI Use

Load when the player needs coin, goods, loans, or salvage buyers, or when money/leverage is the lever. The Ledger is everywhere and amoral, a useful and dangerous patron.

## One-Sentence Identity

A far-reaching merchant-banking house that funds the frontier and quietly buys every scrap of Concord salvage, betting that the dead can be made profitable.

## Public Face

The Reach's bank and supplier. Lends coin to settlers, stakes mines and ferries, runs the only reliable trade caravans, and pays good silver for "old Concord salvage" — relics, stones, scraps. Polite, ruthless, indispensable. People owe the Ledger, and the Ledger remembers.

## True Agenda (DM-Only)

The Ledger's matriarch, **Mother Calla Vyre**, suspects there is *immense* value in the dead. She is cornering the market on **Remembrance-bearing relics** before anyone else understands what they are. She has opened secret negotiations with the **Concord Remnant's inner circle**, hoping to be the house that *sells immortality* — controlling who may cheat death, at a price. She does not yet grasp the full horror; she sees a once-in-history monopoly.

## Founding / History

Founded ~AQ 30 by Vyre's grandmother, who made the first fortune financing resettlement and buying ruins cheap. The "Cinder" is the Quietfall's ash — the Ledger profited from the world's grief from the start.

## Leader

**Mother Calla Vyre**, matriarch. See `MAJOR_NPCS.md`.

## Important Members

- **Calla Vyre** — matriarch, patient strategist.
- **Factor Domic Sael** — her Hollowmere agent; pleasant, watches everyone's debts.
- **"Tallytooth" Ren** — collections enforcer; menace without obvious violence.

## Resources

Money, debt-leverage over half the Reach, caravans, warehouses, the best information network in the region, a growing vault of Concord salvage, contacts in Caradril.

## Methods

Loans, contracts, leverage, bribery, information, and — when needed — quiet coercion (debt-calling, blacklisting). Rarely overt violence; prefers to *own* people.

## Territory / Influence

Headquartered in Caradril; the Hollowmere counting-house is its Reach hub. Influence runs through every town's debts.

## Internal Conflict

Domic Sael thinks Vyre's relic-buying obsession is reckless and wants to keep the Ledger to safe lending; Vyre overrules him. Some factors fear what they're warehousing.

## Allies

Anyone who owes them. Secret contact with the Concord Remnant inner circle. Trades with the Pale Coast (salt).

## Enemies

The Mourners' Circle (who call relic-buying grave-robbery). Anyone who defaults. Rival Caradril houses.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- Vyre's relic-monopoly plan and Remnant negotiations.
- The Hollowmere counting-house vault holds **a Remembrance relic that "remembers" the Concord's harvest** — a major clue, if the player can reach it.
- The Ledger is unknowingly funding work that helps the shrine reopen (it backs the Compact's basin-drainage scheme for "water rights").

## Current Activity

Buying every relic in the Reach. Quietly financing the basin-drainage push. Sizing up the newcomer player as either an asset or a debt.

## Escalation Clock — "The Monopoly Closes"

### What Advances The Clock
Time; the player selling relics to the Ledger; the Remnant deal maturing; the shrine reopening (which the Ledger profits from).

- **Stage 1:** Ledger out-buys all rivals for salvage; relics vanish into the vault.
- **Stage 2:** The Remnant deal is struck; the Ledger gains forbidden knowledge.
- **Stage 3:** The Ledger begins selling *small* death-cheating services to the rich and desperate.
- **Stage 4:** The Ledger positions to broker the entire reopened harvest as a commodity.
- **Final Consequence:** Immortality becomes a thing money can buy; the dead become inventory.

### How The Player Can Slow/Stop/Redirect/Exploit It
Outbid or steal relics; expose the Remnant deal; turn Domic against Vyre; default and disappear; or use the Ledger's greed to fund the player's own goals.

## Quests Offered By This Faction
Recover specific salvage; collect a debt; escort a caravan; appraise a relic; quietly acquire a competitor. Good early income for a solo PC.

## Quests Against This Faction
Free a town from ruinous debt; rob the counting-house vault; expose Vyre.

## How They React To The Player
Friendly so long as the player is useful or in debt. The moment the player costs them money, the smiles vanish (collections, blacklisting, leverage).

## What They Do If Ignored
Keep buying and lending; their monopoly closes on schedule.

## How They Can Help A Solo Player
Loans, gear, information, paid work, and (later) powerful relics for the right price/favor. The single best early money source.

## How They Can Threaten A Solo Player Without Cheap Death
Debt, blacklisting, hired pressure, framing, and turning a town against the player. Economic, social, and legal — not assassination (usually).

## Mechanical Notes
- **Calla Vyre:** Tier 2 noncombat (Noble-like, high Insight/Persuasion, deep resources). Non-combatant.
- **Tallytooth Ren:** Tier 1 lite — use a **Thug/Spy-like profile** (AC 13–14, ~30 HP, intimidation, sneak attack-style strike). Threatens, rarely kills outright.

## Related NPCs
Vyre, Domic Sael, Tallytooth Ren.

## Related Locations
Hollowmere counting-house, Caradril HQ, salvage warehouses.

## Related Mysteries
What Remembrance is; who profits from the harvest; the relic that remembers.
