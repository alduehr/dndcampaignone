## AI Use

Load when the player researches the Concord, reads Concord Script, visits Caradril, or pursues the deep history. The Remnant are the main *rival claimant* to the harvest — idealistic dupes led by a knowing, ambitious inner circle.

## One-Sentence Identity

Scholars and zealots who revere the lost Concord and want to rebuild it — most dreaming of restored order, a hidden few scheming to seize the harvest and repeat the original sin.

## Public Face

A learned society, mostly seated in Caradril, that collects Concord relics, deciphers Concord Script, and preaches that the world's salvation lies in *restoring the Concord* — its roads, its archives, its peace with the dead. To most they are harmless antiquarians and dreamers. They are the only people who can reliably read Concord Script, which makes them indispensable to anyone investigating the ruins.

## True Agenda (DM-Only)

Two layers:
- **Rank-and-file:** sincere idealists who believe the Concord was humanity's golden age and want it back. They do not know what the Concord truly did.
- **Inner circle (the "Reclaimers"):** know the truth — that the Concord harvested the dead — and **want to seize and restart the harvest themselves**, to rebuild the Concord's empire with the Reclaimers as the new Custodians. They are not in league with the Hollow Court; they are *rivals* for the same prize, and would replace the Court if they could. Their leader is **Archivist Quorrin Vane**.

## Founding / History

Formed ~AQ 85 among Caradril scholars who recovered intact Concord archives. The inner circle coalesced when those archives revealed the truth — and the Reclaimers decided the answer was not horror but *ambition*.

## Leader

**Archivist Quorrin Vane**, head of the inner circle (publicly a revered scholar). See `MAJOR_NPCS.md`.

## Important Members

- **Archivist Quorrin Vane** — inner-circle leader; brilliant, certain, dangerous.
- **Lector Briss** — public face; sincere idealist, unaware of the inner circle's true aim (a sympathetic dupe and clue source).
- **Quorrin's agents in the Reach** — antiquarians and relic-buyers working with the Cinder Ledger.

## Resources

The only fluent readers of Concord Script; intact archives in Caradril; scholarly legitimacy; relic-collecting funds (partly via the Ledger deal); a network of antiquarians; fragments of genuine Concord technique.

## Methods

Research, persuasion, relic-acquisition, infiltration of academic and political circles, and (inner circle) quiet maneuvering toward the shrine. They prefer knowledge and influence to force — but the Reclaimers will kill to protect their secret.

## Territory / Influence

Headquartered in Caradril; growing reach into the Sundering Reach via relic-buying and the Ledger deal.

## Internal Conflict

The idealist rank-and-file (Lector Briss) versus the secret Reclaimers (Quorrin). If the rank-and-file learned the inner circle's plan, the faction would shatter — a lever for the player.

## Allies

Secret negotiations with the Cinder Ledger (Calla Vyre). Otherwise courted by everyone who needs Concord Script read.

## Enemies

The Hollow Court (rival claimant — though neither yet fully knows the other's hand). The Mourners' Circle and Gravecallers (who oppose any restart). Anyone who would seal or destroy the harvest.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The inner circle knows the truth and wants to seize the harvest.
- Quorrin has deciphered enough to suspect the Hollow Court exists and is racing to reach the shrine first.
- The Caradril archives contain the clearest written proof of the harvest and the deliberate Quietfall — a major Act 3 clue cache.

## Current Activity

Buying relics in the Reach; deciphering the shrine's location; cultivating the Ledger; sizing up the player as a possible tool or threat. Lector Briss may openly recruit the player as a "fellow seeker of the Concord's wisdom."

## Escalation Clock — "The Reclamation"

### What Advances The Clock
Time; the player feeding them relics/knowledge; the Ledger deal; the shrine being exposed.

- **Stage 1:** The Reclaimers locate the drowned shrine via archives and survey records.
- **Stage 2:** They secure the Ledger's backing and relic supply.
- **Stage 3:** They send agents to the Reach to prepare to seize the shrine.
- **Stage 4:** They race the Hollow Court and the player to control the keystone.
- **Final Consequence:** If they win, the harvest restarts under a *new* Concord — a living tyranny built on the dead.

### How The Player Can Slow/Stop/Redirect/Exploit It
Expose Quorrin to the idealist rank-and-file; deny them relics; learn Concord Script from Briss and beat them to the truth; turn them against the Hollow Court; or join them (a dark path).

## Quests Offered By This Faction
Recover a specific Concord relic or text; escort a scholar into a ruin; translate a found inscription; deliver an archive to Caradril (with hidden strings).

## Quests Against This Faction
Expose the inner circle; intercept a relic shipment; protect a ruin from their agents; rescue a duped scholar.

## How They React To The Player
Briss is warm and recruiting; Quorrin is coolly evaluating. The Remnant becomes more dangerous the closer the player gets to the truth they guard.

## What They Do If Ignored
Advance the Reclamation clock; reach the shrine as a third racer.

## How They Can Help A Solo Player
The key to **reading Concord Script** (a major clue gate), access to archives, scholarly allies (Briss), and translation of ruins. Indispensable for the deep history.

## How They Can Threaten A Solo Player Without Cheap Death
Withhold translation; discredit the player academically; sabotage the player's standing in Caradril; and (Reclaimers) send agents to silence the player — telegraphed, escalating.

## Mechanical Notes
- **Quorrin Vane:** Tier 1 — **Mage/Archmage-like** scaled to tier (AC 12–15, ~60–110 HP across tiers, *Counterspell, Wall of Force, Sending, Scrying*; uses Remembrance relics for one-off effects). A late-game rival; never a cheap-death ambush. Prefers to win by knowledge.
- **Lector Briss:** Tier 2 noncombat (Scholar/Acolyte-like). Non-combatant; an ally if disillusioned.

## Related NPCs
Quorrin, Briss, Reach agents.

## Related Locations
**Caradril — the Lantern Reach** (the Lamplighters' Hall public college + the **Sealed Archive**, the clearest written proof of the harvest/Quietfall; Quorrin's seat; Wessel Dree; Tamsin Orr); the **Sunken Wards** (a secretly-mapped relic-cousin level and an Archive back-way); Reach ruins; the drowned shrine. See `../../06_settlements/caradril_districts/THE_LANTERN_REACH.md` and the city clock **C1 The Deal Closes**.

## Related Mysteries
The Concord's harvest; the deliberate Quietfall; the race for the keystone.
