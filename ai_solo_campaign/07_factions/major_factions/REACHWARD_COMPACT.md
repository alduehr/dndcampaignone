## AI Use

Load for law, governance, permits, disputes, and politics in the Reach. The Compact is the closest thing to government — and it is being quietly steered by a Hollow Court agent toward reopening the drowned shrine.

## One-Sentence Identity

The loose council of frontier settlements that governs the Sundering Reach by negotiation and necessity, now riddled with a hidden agenda it cannot see.

## Public Face

A council of town reeves, village headmen, and elected councilors who meet in Hollowmere (the Reach's de facto seat) to settle disputes, fund roads and wardens, and keep the frontier from fracturing. Underfunded, slow, and earnest. In the crisis, it is the body everyone petitions and blames.

## True Agenda (DM-Only)

The Compact has no single hidden agenda — but it is **infiltrated**. **Councilor Aldous Reke**, a respected senior councilor, is a **knowing Hollow Court agent**. He is steering Compact policy to **drain and reopen the Hollowmere basin** — sold publicly as a resettlement and water-rights project, secretly intended to expose and reactivate the drowned shrine. He has allies of convenience (the Cinder Ledger, which profits from the works) who do not know his true purpose.

## Founding / History

Formed ~AQ 40 when scattered Frontier towns agreed they needed *some* way to settle water, road, and grave disputes without war. Named for the "reachward" oath to defend the frontier together.

## Leader

No single leader; the council is chaired in rotation. The most influential voices: **Reeve Marda Coalmont** (Hollowmere's honest reeve) and **Councilor Aldous Reke** (the infiltrator).

## Important Members

- **Reeve Marda Coalmont** — Hollowmere's reeve; pragmatic, overworked, honest. A potential player ally.
- **Councilor Aldous Reke** — senior councilor; secret Hollow Court agent.
- **Clerk Pevin Oss** — the Compact's records-keeper; knows where every permit and body is buried (clue source).

## Resources

Legal authority (such as it is), the town watch, the treasury (thin), permits and licenses, the basin-drainage project's labor and funds, public legitimacy.

## Methods

Debate, permits, taxes, the watch, and slow consensus. Reke works through *procedure* — burying the shrine reopening inside dull infrastructure votes.

## Territory / Influence

All settled parts of the Reach; strongest in Hollowmere.

## Internal Conflict

Coalmont and the cautious bloc resist the costly basin scheme; Reke and the "progress" bloc push it. The council is split and slow, which Reke exploits.

## Allies

The Cinder Ledger (funds the works). Nominally the Ashen Wardens and Mourners' Circle.

## Enemies

The Mourners' Circle (furious about the basin scheme). Anyone who flouts Compact law. The Gravecallers (outlawed by Compact decree).

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- Reke is a Hollow Court agent; the basin scheme is a cover to reopen the shrine.
- Clerk Pevin Oss has, without understanding it, filed Concord-era survey records that reveal the shrine's true extent — a clue.
- The "water rights" justification is partly fabricated; the basin is not actually needed for resettlement.

## Current Activity

Debating and (slowly) approving the basin-drainage project. Fielding panic petitions about failed rites. Hiring capable newcomers (the player) for jobs the thin watch can't handle.

## Escalation Clock — "The Basin Drains"

### What Advances The Clock
Time; Reke's maneuvering; Ledger funding; the player ignoring Compact politics.

- **Stage 1:** The drainage project is approved over Coalmont's objection.
- **Stage 2:** Work begins; the basin's water level drops; ruins surface.
- **Stage 3:** The drowned shrine's upper structure is exposed; leakage worsens sharply.
- **Stage 4:** The shrine is breachable; the Hollow Court gains direct access to the living world.
- **Final Consequence:** The harvest's keystone is reopened. This is the master clock the whole campaign races against.

### How The Player Can Slow/Stop/Redirect/Exploit It
Expose Reke; sway Coalmont and the cautious bloc; sabotage the works; rally the Mourners' Circle; win a council vote; or (darkly) help the drainage to reach the shrine first.

## Quests Offered By This Faction
Investigate failed rites "officially"; hunt a fen predator menacing the works; deliver a sealed message to Caradril; mediate a water dispute; guard the drainage site (with hidden consequences).

## Quests Against This Faction
Stop the basin scheme; expose a corrupt councilor; defy an unjust decree; protect the Gravecallers from a Compact purge.

## How They React To The Player
Eager to hire a competent newcomer (cheap labor for hard jobs). Coalmont trusts the player if honest; Reke flatters and steers the player toward the shrine.

## What They Do If Ignored
The basin drains on the master clock — this is the campaign's central ticking threat.

## How They Can Help A Solo Player
Legitimate work and pay, legal cover, the town watch as backup, permits and access, and Coalmont as an honest ally.

## How They Can Threaten A Solo Player Without Cheap Death
Arrest, fines, outlawry, withdrawing the watch's protection, and legal entanglement. Reke can frame or politically isolate the player.

## Mechanical Notes
- **Marda Coalmont:** Tier 2 noncombat (Veteran/Noble hybrid — can fight if cornered, AC 15, ~40 HP, but prefers law).
- **Aldous Reke:** Tier 2 noncombat, secretly Tier 1-capable — **Mage/Spy-like** if exposed (he is partly Remembrance-touched; AC 12, ~45 HP, *Counterspell, Hold Person, Dimension Door* at higher tiers). Telegraph before any fight; he flees rather than dies cheaply.

## Related NPCs
Coalmont, Reke, Pevin Oss.

## Related Locations
Hollowmere council hall, the basin/drowned shrine, the drainage works.

## Related Mysteries
Who is pushing the basin scheme and why; the Hollow Court; the shrine.
