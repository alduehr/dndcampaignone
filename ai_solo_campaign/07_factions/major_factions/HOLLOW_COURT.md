## AI Use

**DM-ONLY FACTION FILE.** The campaign's apex power. To the player, the Hollow Court is a ghost story until very late. Never confirm its reality to the player without an earned revelation. Use this file to drive the master clock and to play Veyl in the endgame.

## One-Sentence Identity

The surviving inner Custodians of the Concord, persisting beneath Hollowmere as preserved Remembrance, now restarting the harvest they once paused.

## Public Face (What Players May Hear)

A legend: a "court of the dead" said to rule beneath the Reach, to judge the living, to wait in the drowned places. Treated as superstition. Most clues the player gathers will *seem* to point at other factions long before the Court is named.

## True Nature (DM-Only)

The Hollow Court are the **original Custodians who refused to die in the Quietfall**. They stored themselves as Remembrance in the deepest under-shrine beneath the Hollowmere basin and went dormant for a century to let the Quiet Country refill. They are neither alive nor dead — beings of preserved memory and will. Now awake, they judge the afterlife "full enough" and have resolved to **restart the harvest**, gently and "responsibly," ruling as immortal stewards who believe they are the only ones who can prevent the slow thinning of all souls.

They are **not cartoon villains.** They are grieving, certain, utilitarian, and — from their own century-long vantage — tragically reasonable. Their case is genuinely hard to refute, which is the campaign's central moral engine.

## History

See `../../03_canon/WORLD_HISTORY.md` (True Timeline). Key beats: harvest founded → afterlife drained → Quietfall as deliberate pause → Custodians self-store as the Court → century of dormancy → reawakening (AQ ~95) → restart (now).

## Leader

**Custodian Veyl** — first among the Court, the campaign's final voice. Believed long dead; in truth a preserved Remembrance of the Custodian who designed the Quietfall. See `MAJOR_NPCS.md`.

## Important Members (DM-Only)

- **Custodian Veyl** — leader, designer of the pause, advocate of the "merciful harvest."
- **Custodian Orre** — the Court's hardliner; would harvest faster and broader.
- **Custodian Maire** — the Court's doubter; secretly believes the Court should let itself end (a potential player ally for the synthesis ending).

## How They Act in the Living World

The Court can barely touch the living world directly while the shrine is sealed. So they work through **agents and cutouts** who mostly don't know whom they serve:
- **Councilor Aldous Reke** (knowing agent) steers the Compact to reopen the basin.
- A **cutout patron** hired **Sefra Quick** to steer the player toward investigating the shrine — the Court wants a capable probe.
- Leaked Remembrances are their unwitting "scouts," drawn toward the reawakening shrine.

As the basin drains and the shrine opens, the Court's direct reach grows.

## Resources

A century of refilled Remembrance; deep Concord knowledge and technique; the drowned under-shrine (the harvest's keystone); hidden agents; the ability to manifest as compelling Remembrance-apparitions once the shrine is breached; near-immortality.

## Methods

Patience, proxies, persuasion, and — in the endgame — direct manifestation. They prefer to *convince* rather than destroy; they would much rather the player agree with them than fight.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- They caused the Quietfall on purpose.
- They are the surviving Custodians.
- The harvest's restart is run from the drowned shrine; reopening the basin is *their* plan, executed via Reke.
- The player was steered to Hollowmere as their probe (via Sefra's cutout patron).
- Custodian Maire's heresy — that the Court should let itself end — is a hidden lever for the player.

## Escalation Clock — "The Harvest Restarts" (MASTER CLOCK)

### What Advances The Clock
The basin draining (Reke/Compact clock); the shrine being exposed/breached; relics returning to the shrine; the player advancing toward it for *any* reason.

- **Stage 1:** Leakage worsens; rites fail across the Reach (campaign start).
- **Stage 2:** The basin begins to drain; the upper shrine surfaces; the Court's reach grows.
- **Stage 3:** The shrine is breached; the Court manifests to those who enter; first deliberate harvest of a local death.
- **Stage 4:** The Court can harvest at scale; the Quiet Country begins draining again; thin-born births rise.
- **Final Consequence:** The harvest fully restarts under the Court's rule — immortality for stewards, the dead consumed forever. This is the campaign's worst default outcome if the player never intervenes.

### How The Player Can Slow/Stop/Redirect/Exploit It
Slow the basin clock (oppose Reke/Compact); keep the shrine sealed (ally with Wardens/Mourners); turn Maire against Veyl; race rival factions to the keystone; choose an endgame branch (`MAIN_ARC_OVERVIEW.md`). The Court can be sealed, destroyed, seized from, joined, or *transformed*.

## Faction Quest Chain (Stage 8 — DM-ONLY)
The Court has **no friendly recruit quest by design.** Its arc is an opposition-and-revelation chain in `../../09_quests/faction_quests/HOLLOW_COURT/` (DM-only), paced to the main-arc revelation schedule: QH1 The Hand That Moves the Vote (oppose an agent, R3) → QH2 The Probe's Own Strings (the steering; M8, R5) → QH3 The Court Beneath (first contact; R7) → QH4 The Fate of the Remembrance (the endgame choice; R8). Never name the Court before its earned point.

## How They React To The Player
Early: indirectly, as a steering hand. Mid: as a useful probe to be guided. Late: as Veyl, making the Court's case directly — recruiting, bargaining, or, if all else fails, opposing.

## How They Can Help A Solo Player
If joined (a dark path): immortality, knowledge, power, and the "mercy" of the controlled harvest. Veyl is a genuine, persuasive patron in the endgame.

## How They Can Threaten A Solo Player Without Cheap Death
Through agents (Reke), Remembrance-apparitions, and moral/existential pressure. In the under-shrine they are dangerous, but the campaign's climax is built around *choice and clever play*, not an unwinnable damage race. Telegraph everything; offer non-combat resolutions.

## Combat Capability And Stat References (DM-Only)

The Court has **no army and no surface territory** — it cannot field soldiers. Its "forces" are itself (preserved Remembrance-beings, reachable only late, in the under-shrine), its **deniable human agents** (Reke; cutout-hired hands), and **leaked Remembrances** drawn to the reawakening shrine. Solo-PC danger: **negligible until the endgame, then apex** — but the apex is built around *choice and clever play*, never a forced damage race. Telegraph everything; offer non-combat resolution always.

## Mechanical Notes
- The Court members are **Remembrance-beings**: flavor as **ghost/wraith/lich-like** without copying stat blocks. For the endgame, scale Veyl to a Tier-4 solo-boss profile **with built-in weaknesses and non-combat win conditions** (severing the shrine, persuading Maire, the player's chosen artifact/ally). Detailed boss design = Stage 13/15.
- **Solo rule:** Veyl must never be a "you must out-damage me" wall. The under-shrine provides terrain, allies, and alternatives.
