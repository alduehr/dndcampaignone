## AI Use

Load for funerals, grief, folk-faith scenes, grave customs, and any conflict over "letting the dead rest." The Circle holds the campaign's *moral* truth and will violently resist any restart of the harvest.

## One-Sentence Identity

A grief-keeping folk faith that tends the dead and the grieving — and remembers, in old songs, that the Concord "ate the dead."

## Public Face

Not a church but a community: the women and men who wash the dead, dig the graves, sing the passings, and sit with the bereaved. Every Reach town has a Circle. They distrust outsiders meddling with the dead and despise the Gravecallers. In the failing-rites crisis they are exhausted, angry, and increasingly militant about *who gets to touch the dead*.

## True Agenda (DM-Only)

The Circle is closest to the campaign's *moral* truth. Their elders preserve a folk-memory — half-song, half-warning — that **the Concord did not protect the dead; it consumed them.** They want the dead **let go**, permanently. The most radical elders would **destroy the shrines outright** to end the harvest forever, overlapping dangerously with the Gravecallers' catastrophic method even as they hate the Gravecallers. Grandmother Wend carries the clearest fragment of the truth.

## Founding / History

As old as the Frontier Age — grief is the one institution that never needs founding. The folk-memory of the Concord's sin survives only here, disguised as superstition ("salt at the sill, the Concord's hunger").

## Leader

**Grandmother Wend**, eldest of the Hollowmere Circle. See `MAJOR_NPCS.md`. The Circle has no hierarchy; elders lead by respect.

## Important Members

- **Grandmother Wend** — eldest, keeper of the old songs.
- **Bann Oester** — grave-digger and unofficial muscle; protective, hot-tempered.
- **Iola Wend** — Grandmother's granddaughter; torn between tradition and the Gravecallers' answers.

## Resources

Total community trust; control of graves, funerals, and rite-customs; folk knowledge; the moral high ground; numbers (every grieving family is a potential ally); old songs that encode real history.

## Methods

Custom, shame, refusal, vigil, and — when pushed — mobs and sabotage. They can shut a town's cooperation against anyone who "wrongs the dead."

## Territory / Influence

Every cemetery and funeral in the Reach. Strongest in Hollowmere and the rural villages.

## Internal Conflict

Elders split between **patient grief** (let the dead rest, oppose meddling) and **militant action** (destroy the shrines, even ally with anyone who'll help). Iola is drawn toward the Gravecallers, who *seem* to be doing something.

## Allies

The Ashen Wardens (shared reverence). Grieving families everywhere.

## Enemies

The Gravecallers (who call the dead the Circle wants released) — yet the radicals share their goal. The Cinder Ledger (grave-robbers). Anyone reopening the shrine.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The old songs encode the Concord's harvest. Wend can decode them for the worthy.
- A faction of elders is secretly stockpiling salt, oil, and old Concord "breaking-tools" to destroy a shrine if it reopens.
- Iola has already made quiet contact with the Gravecallers.

## Current Activity

Tending an unprecedented backlog of failed rites and frightened families. Watching the Compact's basin-drainage push with fury. Wend is deciding whether to trust the player with the old songs.

## Escalation Clock — "The Circle Turns Militant"

### What Advances The Clock
Failed rites; the shrine reopening; the Ledger buying grave-relics; the Compact ignoring the Circle.

- **Stage 1:** Grief turns to anger; the Circle refuses cooperation with relic-buyers.
- **Stage 2:** Mobs block salvage caravans; a relic-buyer is beaten.
- **Stage 3:** Radical elders ally with the Gravecallers in secret.
- **Stage 4:** The Circle moves to destroy a shrine — risking the catastrophe of a broken harvest.
- **Final Consequence:** A shrine is shattered; the dead are "freed" violently and the living/dead boundary destabilizes locally.

### How The Player Can Slow/Stop/Redirect/Exploit It
Honor the dead; help with funerals; earn Wend's trust and the old songs; keep relic-buyers out of town; mediate between elders; or *use* their fervor against the harvest (carefully).

## Quests Offered By This Faction
Recover a body lost in the fens; stop a grave-robber; help a family whose rite failed; learn (and protect) the old songs; investigate the basin-drainage scheme.

## Quests Against This Faction
Acquire grave-relics for the Ledger; suppress a mob; talk the radicals down from breaking a shrine.

## How They React To The Player
Cold to strangers; warm to anyone who treats the dead with respect; hostile to grave-meddlers and Ledger agents. Earning the Circle is one of the campaign's key moral milestones.

## What They Do If Ignored
Anger curdles into militancy on the clock above.

## How They Can Help A Solo Player
Safe harbor in any town, folk-lore and the old songs (major clues), warning of dangers in the fens, mob support against the player's enemies, and Bann Oester as a tough short-term ally.

## How They Can Threaten A Solo Player Without Cheap Death
Social exile, refusal of burial/aid, mob violence (telegraphed), and turning whole towns against the player.

## Mechanical Notes
- **Grandmother Wend:** Tier 2 noncombat (Priest/Acolyte-like, high Insight/Religion). Non-combatant.
- **Bann Oester:** Tier 1 lite — **Guard/Thug-like** (AC 14, ~30 HP, heavy spade as a maul). A protective ally or a telegraphed brawler.

## Related NPCs
Wend, Bann Oester, Iola Wend.

## Related Locations
Hollowmere cemetery, village graveyards, the Greyfens.

## Related Mysteries
The Concord's harvest; what the old songs mean; the cost of breaking a shrine.
