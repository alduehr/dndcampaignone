## AI Use

Load when the player seeks help with a restless dead, meets a wandering rite-keeper, or pursues the seal/Concord mystery. The Wardens are the friendliest early faction and a solo-safety patron — but their highest secret is load-bearing for the main arc.

## One-Sentence Identity

Wandering rite-keepers who lay the restless dead to rest, beloved and a little feared, and — unknowingly — the guardians of the seal that keeps the harvest paused.

## Public Face

Successors of the Concord's "kindest work." Travel the Reach in ash-grey cloaks, performing the rites that send a Remembrance through the Last Door. When a funeral fails, you send for a Warden. They take no fee but accept food and shelter. Respected, sometimes feared (they handle the dead), and stretched terribly thin as failures multiply.

## True Agenda (DM-Only)

The order was **secretly founded by Quietfall survivors** to keep the harvest paused — to keep the great shrines quiet and the dead truly resting. Almost no living Warden knows this. Their rites *genuinely work* and genuinely oppose the reawakening. The single local keeper of the truth is **Warden Sister Othetha**, who knows the drowned shrine must never reopen and is forbidden by her oath from explaining why. The Wardens are unknowing protectors of the seal — and thus the Hollow Court's natural enemy.

## Founding / History

Publicly: formed in the early Frontier Age (~AQ 20) by pious survivors. Truly: seeded by a handful of Custodians who *did not* join the Hollow Court and chose instead to guard the pause, hiding their purpose inside an order of humble rite-keepers.

## Leader

**Warden Sister Othetha** (Reach circle). See `08_npcs/MAJOR_NPCS.md`. There is no continent-wide head anymore; the order is a network of small circles.

## Important Members

- **Warden Sister Othetha** — senior, secret-keeper.
- **Warden Brother Hale** — young, earnest, road-worn; the player's likely first Warden contact.
- **Warden Pell** — bitter veteran who suspects something is wrong but lacks the truth.

## Resources

Rite knowledge (the only reliable method to lay the dead), public goodwill, safe-houses in most towns, salt and rite-materials, mobility, moral authority.

## Methods

Travel, perform rites, train apprentices, document failures. Quiet, patient, non-political — which is why factions underestimate them.

## Territory / Influence

Everywhere in the Reach, owning nothing. Strongest moral influence in Hollowmere and the Mourners' Circle's towns.

## Internal Conflict

Younger Wardens want to demand resources and authority from the Compact to fight the crisis; elders (and Othetha's secret oath) counsel patience and secrecy. Some Wardens are quietly breaking under the workload and questioning the order's "do not ask why" tradition.

## Allies

Mourners' Circle (shared reverence for letting the dead rest). Cautious cooperation with the Reachward Compact.

## Enemies

Gravecallers (who *call* the dead the Wardens lay). The Hollow Court and its agents (without most Wardens knowing it). Wary of the Concord Remnant's ambitions.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The founding purpose: guard the pause. (Othetha only.)
- The drowned shrine must never reopen. (Othetha only.)
- The rites are a *deliberately incomplete* fragment of Concord technique — designed to release, never to harvest.

## Current Activity

Overwhelmed. Triaging failed rites across the Reach. Othetha is quietly alarmed by the Compact's push to drain/reopen the Hollowmere basin.

## Escalation Clock — "The Wardens Break"

### What Advances The Clock
Failures outpacing Wardens; Wardens dying or quitting; the Compact ignoring Othetha; the shrine reopening.

- **Stage 1:** Wardens stretched thin; response times grow.
- **Stage 2:** A town's rite goes unanswered; a Remembrance harms someone.
- **Stage 3:** Wardens begin refusing to enter the Greyfens; whole districts go un-tended.
- **Stage 4:** Othetha breaks her oath and reveals the truth to a trusted few (possibly the player) in desperation.
- **Final Consequence:** The order fractures; the seal's guardians scatter; the harvest accelerates unopposed.

### How The Player Can Slow/Stop/Redirect/Exploit It
Help with rites; recruit aid for the Wardens; protect Othetha; pressure the Compact to fund them; or (darkly) exploit their collapse to access the shrine.

## Quests Offered By This Faction
Lay a specific restless dead; escort a Warden through the Greyfens; recover rite-salt from the Pale Coast; investigate why a town's rites keep failing. (Hooks in `09_quests/HOOKS_TABLE.md`.)

## Quests Against This Faction
Rarely targets of player hostility, but a Gravecaller- or Remnant-aligned player may need to evade or oppose them.

## How They React To The Player
Warmly if respectful of the dead; coldly if the player traffics with Gravecallers or disturbs graves. Othetha watches any newcomer near the shrine closely.

## What They Do If Ignored
Keep working until they break (see clock). The seal weakens without their notice.

## How They Can Help A Solo Player
A Warden ally is the campaign's gentlest **solo-safety patron**: rite knowledge, healing/protective folk magic (Priest-like profile), safe shelter, and warnings before dangerous dead. Brother Hale can travel with the player short-term as a low-power ally (see Companion mechanics in `DND_MECHANICS_REQUIREMENTS.md`).

## How They Can Threaten A Solo Player Without Cheap Death
Social/moral pressure, refusal of aid, exposure of the player's grave-meddling to a town. Not assassins.

## Combat Capability And Stat References

The Wardens are a **non-militant order** — their power is ritual, mobility, and moral authority, not force. They are the least dangerous faction in a fight and the safest patron for a solo PC. Solo-PC danger of a confrontation: **low** (the player almost never has cause to fight Wardens; if they do, Wardens defend and flee rather than kill).

| Member / type | Profile (reference, not copied) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Warden Sister Othetha** (leader) | Tier 2 noncombat — **Priest-like**, AC 13, ~33 HP, high Insight/Religion, *Cure Wounds, Lesser Restoration, Gentle Repose, Spirit Guardians* | Non-combatant unless cornered; flees/defends |
| **Warden Brother Hale** (companion) | Companion — **Priest/Acolyte-like**, AC 13, ~22-27 HP, *Cure Wounds, Bless, Sacred Flame*; *Spirit Guardians* at higher tiers | Supports, never solves fights; scales modestly |
| **Warden Pell** (veteran, secondary) | **Acolyte/Veteran hybrid**, AC 14, ~30 HP, can fight defensively if pushed | The order's rare martial-capable member |
| **Hospitaller Warden Sirrin** (quartermaster) | Tier 2 noncombat — **Acolyte-like**, AC 13, ~22 HP | Logistics, not combat |
| **Warden Initiate** (rank-and-file) | **Acolyte-like baseline**, AC 12, ~13 HP, *Sacred Flame, Cure Wounds (1/day)*, the true rite | The order's foot-soldiers; ritualists, not warriors; morale breaks fast in violence |

Scaling: Wardens do not "level up" as combatants — at higher tiers they gain stronger *rite* and *protective* magic (Spirit Guardians, Death Ward, Gentle Repose), never offensive power. A Warden ally's value is solo-safety (healing, rites, warnings), not damage. Their "weapons" against the player are social and moral (see Threat Profile), not blades.

## Mechanical Notes
- **Brother Hale (companion/ally):** use a **Priest-like profile** (AC 13, ~27 HP, *Bless, Cure Wounds, Sacred Flame, Spirit Guardians* at higher tiers), noncombatant-leaning; scales modestly. Avoid letting him solve fights.
- **Othetha:** Tier 2 noncombat (Priest-like, stronger Insight/Religion); dangerous only if cornered.
