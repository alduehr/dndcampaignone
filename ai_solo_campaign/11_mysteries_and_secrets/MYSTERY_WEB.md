## Purpose

**DM-ONLY.** The full network of campaign mysteries, how they connect, and the **three+ clue paths** for each. Use to deliver clues fairly and to avoid revealing a connected mystery too early. Never summarize to the player.

## Central Mystery

> **Why won't the dead rest — and who is doing it?**

Every other mystery is a layer peeling toward the answer in `../03_canon/DM_ONLY_CANON.md`: the Concord harvested the dead; the Quietfall was a deliberate pause; the Hollow Court is restarting the harvest beneath Hollowmere.

## Mysteries (with Three+ Clue Paths Each)

### M0 — Central: Why won't the dead rest?
- **True answer:** The reawakening harvest; Remembrances are leakage. (Full chain below.)
- This is solved by assembling M2/M3/M6/M7. Not a single discovery.

### M1 — What is wrong with Wren? (Act 1 opener)
- **True answer:** She is a partial Remembrance-revenant — the shrine's leakage given form; her rite "failed" because the machine pulled at her.
- **Clue paths (need ~2 to grasp the *shape*):**
  1. **Social:** Speak with Wren carefully (Insight/Persuasion DC 12); she "remembers" dying and clings to an unfinished thing. (Roleplay scene, no roll needed to start.)
  2. **Ritual/Religion:** A Warden (Hale/Othetha) examines her; the rite "won't take" — *something is holding her here* (Religion/Medicine DC 13).
  3. **Folk:** Grandmother Wend recognizes the signs from the old songs ("the water won't let her go").
  4. **Physical:** Her grave on Mourner's Green is subtly *pulled* toward the basin; soil and salt drift waterward (Investigation/Survival DC 12).

### M2 — What is the drowned shrine? (Act 1–2)
- **True answer:** The central node of the Concord's harvest; the Hollow Court's seat.
- **Clue paths:**
  1. **Exploration:** Reach the basin shore / a surfacing ruin; Concord Script markings (needs Comprehend Languages or a reader — M-gate via Briss).
  2. **Testimony:** Old Sashe's map of the dead drifting toward the water points straight at it.
  3. **History:** Compact clerk Pevin Oss's filed Concord survey records reveal its true extent (Investigation DC 14 in the records).
  4. **Forbidden:** The Tallow Man's channeled dead name "the door under the water."

### M3 — What is Remembrance, really? (Act 2)
- **True answer:** A harvestable substance of identity, drawn from the dead.
- **Clue paths:**
  1. **Magic:** Study a Remembrance relic (Arcana/Religion DC 15) — it *stores a person*.
  2. **Teaching:** The Gravecallers (Tallow Man) explain what they've learned from the dead.
  3. **Mercantile:** The Ledger vault relic (via Domic Sael) demonstrably "remembers" a dead person.
  4. **Scholarly:** Concord Script texts (Briss/Remnant) describe "the keeping of the dead's pattern."

### M4 — Who is pushing the basin scheme, and why? (Act 1–2)
- **True answer:** Councilor Reke, a Hollow Court agent, to reopen the shrine.
- **Clue paths:**
  1. **Political:** Reeve Coalmont's suspicions + following the votes (Investigation/Insight DC 13).
  2. **Paper trail:** The "water rights" justification is fabricated; Pevin Oss's records don't support it (Investigation DC 14).
  3. **Surveillance:** Tail Reke; he visits the basin shore alone at night (Stealth/Perception).
  4. **Money:** The Ledger funds the works; Domic Sael grumbles it's "not really about water."

### M5 — Why do the dead drift toward the water? (Act 1–2)
- **True answer:** The reawakening shrine is *pulling* leaked Remembrances toward itself.
- **Clue paths:** Old Sashe's observations; Wren's grave-drift; Gravecaller lore; a Warden noticing rites fail *worse* nearer the basin.

### M6 — What did the Concord actually do? (Act 3)
- **True answer:** It secretly harvested the dead to power its golden age.
- **Clue paths:**
  1. **Archives:** Caradril Concord archives (Remnant access; the clearest written proof).
  2. **Confession:** Warden Othetha breaks her oath (late, conditional on trust).
  3. **The dead:** A Quietfall-era Remembrance (via the Tallow Man) testifies firsthand.
  4. **Song:** Wend's fully-decoded old songs ("the Concord ate the dead").

### M7 — Who/what is the Hollow Court? (Act 4 — apex)
- **True answer:** Surviving Custodians persisting as Remembrance, restarting the harvest.
- **Clue paths:**
  1. **Physical:** Enter the under-shrine and meet them (late-arc).
  2. **Convergence:** M4 (Reke's master) + M6 (surviving Custodians) + M9 (deliberate Quietfall).
  3. **Heretic:** Custodian Maire reaches out, or the Tallow Man's dead name "the ones who would not pass."
- **Guard rail:** Never name the Court before Act 3–4; earlier clues should point at Reke/Remnant/Ledger first.

### M8 — Why is the player really here? (flexible, Act 2–3)
- **True answer:** Steered by a Hollow Court cutout via Sefra Quick, as an unwitting probe.
- **Clue paths:**
  1. **The fixer:** Press Sefra (Insight/Persuasion); her "patron's" money is wrong; she confesses.
  2. **Follow the coin:** Trace the patron's payments back to Reke/a cutout (Investigation).
  3. **Pattern:** Notice how conveniently the player keeps being pointed at the shrine (player-driven realization the DM can confirm).

### M9 — What was the Quietfall really? (Act 3)
- **True answer:** A deliberate, controlled self-destruction to pause the harvest.
- **Clue paths:** Caradril archives; Othetha's confession; the Tallow Man's dead witness; Concord Script inscriptions at the shrine.

### M10 — What should be done about the Remembrance? (Act 4–5)
- **Not a "true answer" — a choice.** See `../12_campaign_arc/MAIN_ARC_OVERVIEW.md` endgame branches: seal, restart-controlled, seize, destroy, transform.
- **"Clue" paths = consequence knowledge:** Gravecaller warning (destroying it = catastrophe), Warden warning (sealing = end of resurrection), Veyl's case (controlled = stability + horror), Maire's heresy (the synthesis is possible).

## Stage 4 Additions — Caradril City Clue Access

**No new central mystery, faction, god, cosmology, or artifact was created in Stage 4.** Caradril adds *city-scale access points* to the existing M2/M3/M4/M6/M8/M9 chains, deepening the three-clue rule without changing any true answer. The city is the campaign's **library, bank, and battlefield** for the mid-game. Act-pacing guardrail: Caradril delivers M6/M9 (the Concord's sin and the deliberate Quietfall) at Act 3 / level ~9–12; it must **never name the Hollow Court (M7)** — the apex stays for convergence elsewhere.

| Mystery | Caradril access point(s) | Method | Gate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **M2** (drowned shrine / the network) | The **Sunken Wards** warming + in-situ Concord Script | Exploration + Script | the Wards (L6–10); a *fragment* proving the network is continental |

## Anti-Railroad Note

Every mystery has 3+ paths spanning social, exploration, ritual, scholarly, mercantile, and forbidden approaches, so no single failed roll, dead NPC, or skipped faction blocks the truth. If the player avoids one faction entirely, another path still exists. **Caradril deepens but never gates this:** M3, M6, and M9 are reachable in the Reach as well as the city, so a player who never travels to Caradril is never blocked — the city is the *clearest* path to the deep history, not the only one.
