## AI Use

**DM-ONLY. Never summarize to the player.** For each of the seven major factions: what they say publicly, what members vs. leaders know, what they misunderstand, what they hide/suppress/destroy, what they falsely blame, what they are right and wrong about, which clues (REV/clue IDs) they can provide or block, and what changes when the player earns trust or becomes an enemy. Use this to route clue access through faction relationships and to keep each faction's knowledge phase-appropriate.

**THE HOLLOW COURT SECTION IS APEX DM-ONLY** — never surface any of it, and never let the player gain Court "trust" that unlocks the apex before Act 4 (there is no friendly Court quest by design).

## Ashen Wardens

- **Publicly say:** "We lay the restless dead to rest; we are the Concord's heirs." Stretched thin by the failures.
- **Most members believe:** their rites are a sacred craft; the elders know best; the crisis is decay/wrong-doing to be fought rite by rite.
- **Leaders know:** Othetha (sole local keeper) knows the order was **founded to keep the harvest paused** and the seal must hold; Warden-Confessor Voss is *near* this truth and defying the silence.
- **Leaders misunderstand:** most Wardens do NOT know what they guard; they think they fight a curse, not a machine.
- **Hide / suppress:** the order's true founding purpose; that their rites are a *deliberately limited fragment*; that the seal exists.
- **Would destroy:** nothing material — but Othetha would suppress a confession that breaks the order's faith prematurely.
- **Falsely blame:** sometimes the Gravecallers (for "stirring the dead") — a fair partial-misread.
- **Right about:** the dead must rest; sealing works; meddling is dangerous (REV_008 seal-cost).
- **Wrong about:** that the seal is a permanent solution — it only pauses the harvest; the Court is restarting it.
- **Clues they provide:** REV_001 (rites fail worse near water, C_SR_010), REV_005/REV_006 via Othetha's confession (C_SR_022/C_SR_025, late/trust-gated), REV_008 seal-cost warning (C_SR_031), node-seal fragments (Adwen/Verge/Anselm Verge, conditional).
- **Clues they block:** the order's silence delays REV_005/REV_006 unless trust is earned or Voss cracks.
- **Trust unlocks:** Othetha's confession; Warden node-knowledge; the "seal it" ending path; Hale as a companion.
- **Enmity costs:** Wardens refuse rites and confession; the order closes ranks; the player loses the cleanest seal-ending allies (but Mourners/Remnant/dead-witness paths still reach REV_005/REV_006).
- **Phase limit:** Wardens give REV_001 in Act 1, fragments in Act 2, REV_005/REV_006 in Act 3, REV_008 in Act 4-5. They never name the Court (most don't know it).

## Cinder Ledger

- **Publicly say:** "We fund resettlement and buy old Concord salvage." Wealthy, everywhere, useful.
- **Most members believe:** it's lucrative salvage and lending; the "relic" trade is just antiquities.
- **Leaders know:** Vyre (matriarch) knows the relics **hold the dead** and plans to **monopolize and sell** the Remembrance; she is courting the Remnant (Quorrin). Vell/Domic/Riis hold pieces of the monopoly proof.
- **Leaders misunderstand:** Vyre does NOT know the harvest is a century-old machine or that the Court exists; she thinks she's first to a new commodity.
- **Hide / suppress:** the monopoly design; the Vyre-Quorrin deal; which salvage "holds the dead"; the basin-scheme funding link.
- **Would destroy:** ledger-pages proving the deal (Coll Riis's copies); witnesses who could expose the relic trade.
- **Falsely blame:** market rivals; "frontier superstition" (to dismiss the crisis and keep buying cheap).
- **Right about:** the relics store the dead (REV_002); there is profit and power in the Remembrance.
- **Wrong about:** that it can be safely commodified; that they control what they're selling; the harvest's true nature.
- **Clues they provide:** REV_002 (the vault relic C_SR_013; quiet-coin C_CAR_005; smelting C_CAR_006), REV_003 funding link (C_SR_017), REV_004 payment trail (C_CAR_009), REV_005 fragments via the deal-pages (C_CAR_011 feeder).
- **Clues they block:** the monopoly stays hidden unless a factor is turned (Domic/Riis/Tallytooth Ren) or a heist runs.
- **Trust unlocks:** vault access; the deal-proof; the "seize/sell" ending path; Ledger muscle and money.
- **Enmity costs:** the Ledger sics enforcers (Mauld Tallow), calls in debts, and freezes salvage access (but relics are also reachable via the cult, the Crucible, and far "old glass").
- **Phase limit:** REV_002 from Act 2; the deal-pages feed REV_005 only at Act 3. The Ledger never knows/names the Court.

## Concord Remnant

- **Publicly say:** "We are rebuilding the lost Concord; we read the old Script." Seen as harmless dreamers.
- **Most members believe (rank-and-file):** idealistic restoration; the Concord was a noble fallen order. They are **dupes.**
- **Leaders know (inner circle / Reclaimers):** Quorrin Vane, Sabine Ferrant, Wessel Dree, Veska Dunn know the **harvest is real** and want to **seize and restart it** as a new Concord; Quorrin *suspects* the Court.
- **Leaders misunderstand:** they believe they can control the harvest where the Concord could not; they underestimate the Court (Quorrin only suspects it).
- **Hide / suppress:** the inner circle's true aim; the Sealed Archive's damning contents from the rank-and-file; the Reclamation's real purpose.
- **Would destroy:** evidence that would turn the idealistic rank-and-file against the inner circle (a key player lever).
- **Falsely blame:** "ignorant frontier fear" for resisting the Reclamation.
- **Right about:** the Concord's true scale and sin (REV_005); the deliberate Quietfall (REV_006); that the Script holds the answers.
- **Wrong about:** that restoring the harvest is salvation; that they can master it; treating the Court as a tool, not a rival.
- **Clues they provide:** REV_002 (Script texts C_CAR_007), REV_005 (the Sealed Archive C_CAR_011 — the *clearest* proof), REV_006 (signed Quietfall C_CAR_012), the Concord Script gate itself (Briss/Tamsin Orr), the far Heartlands record (Hollin Vane, C_FC_001).
- **Clues they block:** Wessel Dree is the human lock on the Archive; the inner circle gatekeeps the Script and the truth.
- **Trust unlocks:** Script literacy; Archive access; the "seize/new Concord" or "restore" ending paths; but trusting the *inner circle* risks being used.
- **Enmity costs:** the Remnant closes the Archive and Script access (but Othetha, the dead-witness, the old songs, and the far Crown record still reach REV_005/REV_006).
- **Phase limit:** Script fragments from Act 1-2; the Archive's M6/M9 truth at Act 3. **The Remnant points *toward* the Court (Quorrin suspects) but must never confirm/name it — the apex stays for convergence.**

## Gravecallers

- **Publicly say:** (outlawed) "We speak with the dead; we give a last word." Feared as a dangerous cult.
- **Most members believe:** they serve the dead and free them; the rites are sacred; the world wrongs the dead.
- **Leaders know:** the Tallow Man channels a **real Quietfall-era witness** and has learned **fragments of the truth** (the Concord took the dead; the dead are angry). Cole Ashby has Concord **breaking-tools** + a partial shrine map and would **shatter the keystone**. Knell fears the cult is *used by* the dead.
- **Leaders misunderstand:** they underestimate the catastrophe total destruction would cause (resurrection ends, the living/dead boundary destabilizes); the channeled dead have their own agenda.
- **Hide / suppress:** their cells, contacts, and the breaking-tools; cutouts inside other groups (e.g., a bargeman).
- **Would destroy:** the shrines themselves (the destroy-ending) — the gravest danger.
- **Falsely blame:** the Concord and "all who keep the dead" for the world's wrong — broadly right in spirit, dangerously simplistic in method.
- **Right about:** Remembrance is harvestable identity (REV_002); the Concord wronged the dead (REV_005); the dead should be *let go* (closest to the moral truth).
- **Wrong about:** that shattering the shrines is safe; that they grasp the full mechanism; that the dead they channel are trustworthy.
- **Clues they provide:** REV_002 (teaching C_SR_012), REV_005/REV_006 via the channeled witness (C_SR_023), REV_007 fragment ("the ones who would not pass" C_SR_029), REV_008 destroy-cost (C_SR_030), REV_010 synthesis fragments (C_SR_038), M2/M5 ("the door under the water").
- **Clues they block:** they are outlawed and secretive — contact requires a bridge (Iola, Tomas Brevin, Mabb Tay, Den Hask).
- **Trust unlocks:** the channeled-dead truth (a *distorted-but-sincere* source); the "destroy" ending path; cult guides through dangerous fen sites.
- **Enmity costs:** the cult marks the player as a destroyer-of-the-dead; loses the forbidden truth-source (but relics, archives, and Othetha still reach REV_002/REV_005).
- **Phase limit:** REV_002 from Act 2; the channeled witness gives REV_005/REV_006 atmosphere in Act 2 but the *full* truth only at Act 3; REV_007 fragment only at Act 4. Handle channeled reveals as sincere-but-possibly-distorted.

## Mourners' Circle

- **Publicly say:** "We tend the dead and the grieving; outsiders must not meddle with the dead."
- **Most members believe:** the old ways must be kept; the dead deserve mourning and rest; the Circle's songs hold wisdom.
- **Leaders know:** Wend (and the **old-songs network** — Combe, both Sennets, Bryd, Mam Tace, far anchors) carry the **folk-memory that "the Concord ate the dead."** Some elders/radicals stockpile shrine-breaking tools and would destroy the shrines (overlapping the Gravecallers).
- **Leaders misunderstand:** the *mechanism* (they hold the moral truth, not the documentary/technical one); some radicals underestimate the destroy-catastrophe.
- **Hide / suppress:** the radical faction's breaking-tools; the bridge to the Gravecallers (Iola); the fullest old-song verses.
- **Would destroy:** the shrines (the radicals) — a danger shared with the Gravecallers.
- **Falsely blame:** "outsiders meddling" for worsening the dead — a fair partial-misread that gates their trust.
- **Right about:** the dead should be let go (REV_008 moral compass); the Concord ate the dead (REV_005, folk-form); the failures are real and worsening (REV_001).
- **Wrong about:** that keeping the old ways alone can stop it; that destruction (radicals) is safe.
- **Clues they provide:** REV_001 (old songs treat it as known grief, C-M1-3), REV_005 (decoded old songs C_SR_024 + the network C_FC_011), REV_008/REV_010 (the moral/synthesis compass), the Gravecaller door (Iola).
- **Clues they block:** outsiders get nothing without proving they honor the dead; the deepest verses are gated.
- **Trust unlocks:** Wend's songs; the network's far anchors; the Gravecaller bridge; the "seal/destroy/transform" moral framing; protection of Wren-like NPCs.
- **Enmity costs:** the Circle shuns the player as a meddler; the old-songs path closes (but the Remnant archives, Othetha, and the dead-witness still reach REV_005).
- **Phase limit:** REV_001 from Act 1; the decoded songs (REV_005, folk-form) at Act 3. The Mourners never know the Court as such; they hold the *moral* truth, not the apex identity.

## Reachward Compact

- **Publicly say:** "We govern the Reach and keep order." A loose frontier council.
- **Most members believe:** they manage a frontier crisis with thin resources; the basin scheme is about water/resettlement.
- **Leaders know:** honest leaders (Reeve Coalmont, Magister-Reeve Sela Coalmont, Sgt. Hesk) **suspect the basin scheme is wrong** but can't prove it. One senior councilor — **Reke** — is a **knowing Hollow Court agent** steering the reopening; cover-up officials (Tindle, Frost, Cray) bury unnatural reports for their careers.
- **Leaders misunderstand:** the honest Compact doesn't know *who* steers them or *why* (REV_003 territory); they can't see the Court behind Reke.
- **Hide / suppress (cover-up officials):** unnatural drowning reports, failed-rite counts, the doctored water-rights surveys.
- **Would destroy:** the doctored survey originals; whistleblower evidence (Pevin Oss is too scared to act).
- **Falsely blame:** "frontier panic," the Mourners, or the Gravecallers for the unrest.
- **Right about:** the Reach is in crisis and needs governance; the basin scheme smells wrong (honest leaders).
- **Wrong about:** that the scheme is about water; that the Compact is acting freely (it is infiltrated).
- **Clues they provide:** REV_003 (Coalmont's suspicions C_SR_014; doctored filings C_SR_015; Sela Coalmont's "steered against the Reach" C_SR_018), REV_001 (suppressed failure-reports, regional), REV_004 (the correspondent lead via Caradril council).
- **Clues they block:** the cover-up officials suppress the failure-record and the survey proof; Reke actively misdirects.
- **Trust unlocks (honest Compact):** the votes, the survey records, watch support against Reke (Hesk); the lawful basin-scheme lever.
- **Enmity costs:** the Compact (or Reke) brands the player a troublemaker; watch pressure rises (but the paper trail, surveillance, and Sela Coalmont still reach REV_003).
- **Phase limit:** REV_003 *suspicion* in Act 1, *confirmation* in Act 2. **Reke is exposed as a traitor in Act 2 (REV_003) but his Court master only at Act 4 (REV_007).** The honest Compact never knows the Court.

## The Hollow Court — APEX DM-ONLY (never surface; no friendly trust path before Act 4)

> **This section is the campaign's most protected knowledge.** The Court is a *legend* to the player until Act 4. There is no friendly Court quest; the player cannot "earn Court trust" to unlock the apex early. Use this only to drive the Court's hidden moves and to play the apex.

- **Publicly (to the player):** a ghost story — "a court of the dead beneath the Reach." Treated as superstition. **Most clues the player gathers seem to point at other factions long before the Court is named.**
- **What the Court knows:** *everything* — they ARE the surviving Custodians who designed the Quietfall and run the harvest. They know the keystone, the Concord Deep, the afterlife's finitude, and every other faction's blind spots.
- **What the Court misunderstands:** their own certainty — they believe the merciful harvest is the only salvation (their tragic blind spot is moral, not factual). Veyl believes; Maire doubts; Orre would harvest by force.
- **Hide / suppress:** their existence, location (under-shrine, straight down beneath Hollowmere), membership, and methods. They work through cutouts who don't know whom they serve (Reke; the Sefra patron; leaked Remembrances as scouts).
- **Would destroy:** anyone close to confirming their survival prematurely; they prefer concealment to violence while the shrine is sealed.
- **Right about:** the afterlife is thinning and souls are failing (REV_009); the easy alternatives all have terrible costs (REV_008).
- **Wrong about:** that consuming the dead forever is mercy; that they have the right to decide for all souls.
- **Clues they "provide" (only at the apex, Act 4-5):** REV_007 (their existence, C_SR_027/028/029), REV_008 (Veyl's case C_SR_032), REV_010 (Maire's heresy C_SR_036/037).
- **Clues they block (all along):** their existence is suppressed by design; Reke and the Sefra patron are firewalls; the legend status is the cover.
- **Trust path:** **none friendly before Act 4.** At the apex, Maire may ally for the synthesis ending; Veyl may persuade toward the controlled-restart ending; the player chooses.
- **Enmity costs:** the Court accelerates the master clock, deploys Reke and Remnant/Ledger proxies, and (once the shrine breaches) manifests as compelling Remembrance-apparitions; the final confrontation can be combat, debate, sabotage, or persuasion.
- **PROTECTION:** never name, confirm, locate, or "trust-unlock" the Court before Act 4 (REV_007 gate). Earlier, every Court tell must read as pointing at Reke / the Remnant / the Ledger. See `../SECRET_PROTECTION_MATRIX.md`.
