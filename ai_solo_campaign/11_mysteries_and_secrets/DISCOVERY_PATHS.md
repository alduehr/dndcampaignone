## AI Use

**DM-ONLY. Never summarize to the player.** Shows how **any playstyle** reaches the campaign's revelations — so no preferred approach, skipped region, dead NPC, failed roll, ignored quest, or faction focus locks the player out. For each playstyle: likely first clues, NPCs, quests; how to progress without the "intended" route; how to redirect *without railroading*; what's available early/mid/late; what stays hidden. **The mystery must remain playable even if the player skips a region, kills an NPC, fails a check, ignores a quest, travels far, plays social-only, or explores-only.**

**Core guarantee (from `REVELATION_MAP.md`):** every REV has 3+ independent sources spanning approaches. This file maps those sources onto playstyles so the AI DM can always offer the *next* in-fiction door that fits how the player plays.

## 1. Investigator / clue-focused play
- **First clues:** the fabricated water-rights filings (C_SR_015), the survey records (C-M2-3), the grave-drift (C_SR_004).
- **NPCs:** Pevin Oss, Reeve Coalmont, Halla Coalmont, the Bellman (Caradril).
- **Quests:** Q_ACT1_COMPACT_THE_REEVES_DOUBT, Q_THE_SEALED_LETTER, Q_MAJOR_002/007.
- **Progress without the intended route:** paper trails reach REV_003/REV_004 without ever fighting or befriending a faction; the Archive (documentary) reaches REV_005/REV_006.
- **Redirect (no railroad):** when a trail goes cold, surface a *new document* via a clock advance (a leaked survey, a misfiled record) — same truth, new door.
- **Early/mid/late:** REV_001 (Act 1) -> REV_003/REV_004 (Act 2) -> REV_005/REV_006 documentary (Act 3) -> convergence REV_007 (Act 4).
- **Hidden:** the Court until convergence; the keystone location until the under-shrine.

## 2. Faction-aligned play
- **First clues:** whatever the joined faction provides (see `by_faction/FACTION_KNOWLEDGE_MAP.md`).
- **NPCs:** the faction's part-truth holders.
- **Quests:** the faction's 4-quest chain + Stage 10 faction quests.
- **Progress:** each faction is a *different* path to the web — Wardens->seal/REV_005/006; Ledger->REV_002/seize; Remnant->REV_005/006 documentary; Mourners->REV_001/005 folk; Gravecallers->REV_002/destroy; Compact->REV_003.
- **Redirect:** if the player commits to one faction, deliver that faction's clues *plus* cross-faction friction that surfaces a rival's piece (e.g., a Ledger job stumbles on a Remnant Script fragment).
- **Early/mid/late:** faction-dependent; all converge on REV_007 at Act 4.
- **Hidden:** no faction (but the Court) confirms the apex; trusting the inner Remnant/Ledger risks being *used* (a consequence, not a block).
- **Note:** there is **no friendly Hollow Court alignment** before Act 4 (by design).

## 3. Social play
- **First clues:** Wren (C_SR_001), Wend's songs (C-M1-3), Sashe's testimony (C_SR_005), the reeve's suspicion (C_SR_014).
- **NPCs:** the surface-knowledge and old-songs NPCs; Sefra (REV_004 confession).
- **Quests:** Q_MAJOR_001, the Mourner/Warden chains.
- **Progress:** confession and folk-knowledge reach REV_001/REV_004/REV_005(folk) without documents or dungeons; Othetha's confession reaches REV_005/REV_006; Veyl/Maire reach the apex by *argument*.
- **Redirect:** if the player avoids reading/exploring, lean on NPC confession and the old-songs network; an NPC seeks the player out when a clock advances.
- **Early/mid/late:** REV_001 (Act 1) -> REV_004 + REV_002(teaching) (Act 2) -> REV_005/006 (confession/songs, Act 3) -> REV_007/008/010 (apex argument, Act 4-5).
- **Hidden:** the documentary specifics aren't needed; the folk/confession path is *complete on its own*.

## 4. Combat-heavy play
- **First clues:** combat aftermath — a laid Remembrance's last memory (R1-capped flavor); relics looted from adversaries (C_SR_011); a defeated cutout's papers.
- **NPCs:** enforcers, the Tallow Man (if fought-then-parleyed), Mauld Tallow.
- **Quests:** the toll-war, the wreckers, Q_MAJOR_028 (faction war).
- **Progress:** clearing a node yields relics/inscriptions (REV_002/REV_005 fragments); the campaign **never requires winning a fight to advance** — but combat *aftermath* is a valid clue channel.
- **Redirect:** after a fight, the *scene* yields evidence (a body's relic, a captured order, an inscription) so a combat player still gathers clues; telegraph apex threats so the player doesn't suicide into the under-shrine.
- **Early/mid/late:** fragments throughout; the apex still requires REV_003+005+006 convergence (a combat player gets these from looted documents, captured NPCs, and forced confessions).
- **Hidden:** the Court can be *reached* by fighting to the under-shrine (Act 4) but Veyl is a debate/persuasion/sabotage option too, not only a damage check.

## 5. Exploration-heavy play
- **First clues:** the surfacing ruin (C_SR/C-M2-1 shape), the Drift-Line, the basin shore, node cousin-sites.
- **NPCs:** Old Sashe, fen/wood/coast guides; Remnant node-mappers (Maren Voll).
- **Quests:** Q_THE_REED_HOLMS, the Deep Adit, the Drowned Lamp, the Whispering Cairn.
- **Progress:** physically reaching sites yields the M2 network shape and (Script-gated) REV_005/REV_006 inscriptions; the geography-of-failure reaches REV_001 directly.
- **Redirect:** an explorer who skips towns still finds the pattern in the *land* (the drift, the warming nodes, the inscriptions); pair sites with a portable Script reader (Tamsin Orr/Briss) or a non-reader testimony path.
- **Early/mid/late:** REV_001 (Act 1 sites) -> M2 network + REV_002 relics (Act 2) -> REV_005/006 inscriptions (Act 3, Script-gated) -> the under-shrine REV_007 (Act 4).
- **Hidden:** Script-gating keeps damning inscriptions Act-3; lethal-telegraphing keeps the under-shrine Act-4.

## 6. City-intrigue play (Caradril)
- **First clues:** the Ashmarket quiet-coin (REV_002), the misfiled charter record (C_CAR_008), the correspondent thread (C_CAR_010).
- **NPCs:** Vyre, Quorrin, Briss, Tamsin Orr, the Bellman, Coll Riis, Mother Ysarra.
- **Quests:** the 11 city quests; Q_MAJOR_008 (The Stilling Cracks).
- **Progress:** the city is the clearest documentary path to REV_005/REV_006 (the Sealed Archive) and the easiest commercial REV_002.
- **Redirect:** city politics naturally surface the deal-pages, the Archive, and the correspondent lead through intrigue, not just investigation.
- **Early/mid/late:** REV_002 (Act 2) -> REV_005/006 Archive (Act 3). **Never REV_007 in the city.**
- **Hidden:** the Court (Caradril points at Reke/Remnant/Ledger); the keystone (the Wards are a cousin, not the keystone).

## 7. Wilderness-travel play
- **First clues:** travel events; the drift along the Concord roads; Star-Stones warming; Ring 1 honest M5 windows.
- **NPCs:** guides (Sashe, Brann, Mabon Crale, Wenna Roke); wandering Wardens (Sef, Ošric).
- **Quests:** the Ring 1 regional quests; the drowned roads (Q_MAJOR_009).
- **Progress:** travelling the network *is* the M2/M5 lesson; Ring 1 confirms the failures radiate from the Reach (REV_001 corroboration); far travel adds REV_002/REV_005 echoes.
- **Redirect:** a constant traveler meets the truth *en route* (warming nodes, drifting dead, road-side rumors that point to real clues).
- **Early/mid/late:** REV_001 + regional corroboration (Acts 1-2) -> far echoes REV_002/REV_005/REV_009 (Act 3) -> home for the apex (Act 4).
- **Hidden:** the keystone and the Court (far echoes never deliver them).

## 8. Religious / rite-focused play
- **First clues:** the rite "won't take" (Warden exam, C-M1-2); rites fail worse near water (C_SR_010); the salt-and-tide/old-songs rites.
- **NPCs:** Othetha, Hale, Mother Ysarra, the old-songs network, Anchorite Sef.
- **Quests:** the Warden chain; Q_MAJOR_006 (the seal and the doubt); the Mourner chain.
- **Progress:** rite-craft reaches REV_001 (the dead are *held*), then Othetha's seal-knowledge reaches REV_005/REV_006, then REV_008 seal-cost.
- **Redirect:** a rite-focused player learns through *failed rites* and a Warden's growing doubt; Voss and Othetha are the on-ramps.
- **Early/mid/late:** REV_001 (Act 1) -> REV_005/006 via Othetha (Act 3) -> REV_008 seal-ending (Act 4-5).
- **Hidden:** the Court (most Wardens don't know it); the mechanism (rite-craft gives the *seal*, not the *machine's blueprint*).
- **Note:** REV_009 (finite afterlife) is especially resonant here — "the Last Door is partly a mechanism" lands hardest for a rite-focused PC.

## 9. Criminal / underworld play
- **First clues:** the relic black market (quiet-coin, "old glass"); the Salt Syndicate; the Hush; smugglers (Gull Heddwyn).
- **NPCs:** Tallytooth Ren, Tidewife Sorrel, the Bellman, "Mother" Silque, Cassia Mourn.
- **Quests:** Q_QUIET_COIN, Q_THE_BELLMANS_PRICE, the Ledger Vault heist.
- **Progress:** the black market *is* the REV_002 path (someone collects the dead on purpose); the Hush sells fragments of REV_004 and the correspondent thread.
- **Redirect:** an underworld player buys/steals the clues other playstyles earn socially — relics, ledger-pages, the Sealgate route into the Wards.
- **Early/mid/late:** REV_002 (Act 2) -> deal-pages -> REV_005 fragments (Act 3) -> the apex by heist/sabotage (Act 4).
- **Hidden:** the Court (the market knows relics, not the keystone).

## 10. Far-continent wandering
- **First clues:** far relic trade (C_FC_004/010), the old-songs network fragment (C_FC_011), the Ruin'd Crown record (C_FC_001).
- **NPCs:** Hollin Vane, Mira Cindra, Wenna Stone, the far old-songs anchors, Wind-Singer Esha, Sun-Singer Doll.
- **Quests:** the 82 far-continent developed quests; Q_MAJOR_012/019/020/024/026.
- **Progress:** far play **corroborates** REV_002/REV_005/REV_009 and finds the synthesis hope (REV_008/REV_010 via Sunmark) — but **never** the keystone or the Court.
- **Redirect:** a far-wandering player's *current* failure-trail keeps pointing home; the apex requires returning to the NW (the endgame is vertical, under Hollowmere).
- **Early/mid/late:** far echoes (Acts 3-4) -> home for REV_007/008 (Act 4-5).
- **Hidden (always):** the Hollow Court, the Concord Deep, the Under-Shrine/Drowned Keystone, the keystone's location.

## Universal Redirect Rules (anti-railroad)

1. **No required NPC.** If a key NPC is dead/avoided, route the same REV through one of its 3+ alternate sources (see `REVELATION_MAP.md`).
2. **No required roll.** If a check fails, the clue is *delayed or costlier*, never gone — another path or a clock surfaces it.
3. **No required region.** Every REV is reachable in the NW home regions; far/Ring 1 only add corroboration.
4. **No required quest.** Quests are doors, not gates; the world-clocks deliver the truth to a passive player slowly.
5. **Clocks as a backstop.** If the player chases nothing, `WORLD_CLOCKS.md` advances the harvest and *forces* the pattern (REV_001) and later truths into view — slower, never blocked.
6. **Apex stays gated regardless of playstyle.** No playstyle reaches REV_007 before Act 4; the protections in `SECRET_PROTECTION_MATRIX.md` apply to all paths.
