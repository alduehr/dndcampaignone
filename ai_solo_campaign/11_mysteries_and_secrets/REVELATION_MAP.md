## Purpose

**DM-ONLY.** The sequence and timing of revelations. Use to pace secrets and avoid revealing the central truth too early. Never quote to the player.

## AI Use

Before delivering a major clue, check the act/level here, what it unlocks, and what must *not* leak with it.

## Revelation Timeline

### Act 1 (Levels 1–4) — Surface
Player learns: the failures have a **source and a pattern**, centered on Hollowmere and the basin. Reveal: M1 (Wren's nature, partial), M5 (the dead drift toward the water), the *existence* of the drowned shrine (M2 shape). **Do not reveal:** Remembrance-as-substance, the Concord's sin, the Court, or the steering. Reke seems a reasonable politician; the shrine seems a hazard.

### Act 2 (Levels 5–8) — Conspiracy
Player learns: M3 (**Remembrance is a substance someone collects**), M2 (the shrine is the center), M4 (**Reke is pushing the basin scheme for a hidden reason**), possibly M8 (the player was steered). Reveal a *human* conspiracy around the dead. **Do not reveal:** that the conspiracy is a century-old machine, or the Court.

### Act 3 (Levels 9–12) — The Concord's Sin
Player learns: M6 (**the Concord harvested the dead**) and M9 (**the Quietfall was deliberate**). The noble-fallen-order myth dies. The player realizes the crisis is the *re-running of an ancient system*. **Do not reveal yet:** that the original Custodians survive as the Court (hint only).

### Act 4 (Levels 13–16) — The Hollow Court
Player learns: M7 (**the Court survives and is restarting the harvest**), meets Veyl, hears the Court's case, learns of Maire's heresy. The apex truth lands. The endgame branches (M10) become explicit choices.

### Act 5 (Levels 17–20) — The Choice
Player learns: M10 — the full consequence-set of each ending. All secrets resolved. The epilogue reflects what the player knew and chose.

## Revelation Entries

### R1 — The Failures Have a Source (Act 1)
- **What is revealed:** The dead aren't lingering randomly; they drift toward the basin; Wren's rite failed because *something held her.*
- **Before:** The player thinks it's a curse, decay, or bad luck.
- **How discovered:** Wren scene; Old Sashe; Wend; grave-drift. (M1, M5)
- **Changes:** Turns horror into a solvable mystery; points at the basin.
- **Must not leak:** The Concord, the Court, the substance.
- **Danger if premature:** None major; this is the intended Act 1 frame.

### R2 — Someone Is Collecting the Dead (Act 2)
- **What is revealed:** Remembrance is a substance; a relic *stores a person*; someone wants it.
- **Before:** The player thinks the dead just "won't pass."
- **How discovered:** Relic study; Gravecallers; Ledger vault; Concord Script. (M3)
- **Changes:** Reframes the crisis as a conspiracy with profit and power motives; implicates the Ledger and Remnant.
- **Must not leak:** The machine's age; the Court.
- **Danger if premature:** Skipping to "ancient harvest" robs Act 3 of impact — keep it *contemporary* in Act 2.

### R3 — Reke Is a Traitor (Act 2)
- **What is revealed:** The basin scheme is a cover to reopen the shrine; Reke serves a hidden master.
- **How discovered:** Coalmont's suspicions; fabricated water-rights; tailing Reke; Ledger funding. (M4)
- **Changes:** Names a human antagonist; raises the stakes of the basin clock.
- **Must not leak:** Reke's master is the Court (reveal "someone above him" only).
- **Danger if premature:** Don't let the player learn "the Hollow Court" from Reke directly until Act 4.

### R4 — You Were Steered Here (Act 2–3)
- **What is revealed:** Sefra was paid to point the player at the shrine; the player is a probe.
- **How discovered:** Pressing Sefra; following the money; pattern recognition. (M8)
- **Changes:** A trust crisis; personal stakes; the player questions every "lucky" lead.
- **Must not leak:** The cutout traces to the Court — keep it murky (Reke-adjacent).
- **Danger if premature:** Fine to land early as a shock; just keep the *ultimate* employer hidden.

### R5 — The Concord Ate the Dead (Act 3)
- **What is revealed:** The Concord's "keeping the dead at peace" was a harvest. The golden age was powered by consuming souls.
- **How discovered:** Caradril archives; Othetha's confession; the Tallow Man's dead witness; Wend's songs. (M6)
- **Changes:** Destroys the noble-Concord myth; recontextualizes all history; implicates the Wardens (as unwitting guardians) and the Remnant (as would-be heirs).
- **Must not leak:** The Court's survival (still only hinted).
- **Danger if premature:** This is Act 3's centerpiece; landing it in Act 1–2 collapses the mystery's build.

### R6 — The Quietfall Was On Purpose (Act 3)
- **What is revealed:** The Custodians destroyed their own order to pause the harvest and refill the afterlife.
- **How discovered:** Same sources as R5, plus shrine inscriptions. (M9)
- **Changes:** Reframes the world's founding trauma; explains the "age of healing"; sets up the Court.
- **Danger if premature:** Pairs with R5; don't separate them by more than an act.

### R7 — The Hollow Court Lives (Act 4 — apex)
- **What is revealed:** The original Custodians survive as Remembrance beneath the basin and are restarting the harvest "to save everyone."
- **How discovered:** Under-shrine; convergence of R3+R5+R6; Maire's contact. (M7)
- **Changes:** Names the apex power; makes the antagonist a *person with an argument* (Veyl).
- **Must not leak earlier:** This is the campaign's keystone reveal. Earlier, keep the Court a "legend."

### R8 — The Choice and Its Costs (Act 4–5)
- **What is revealed:** Every ending has a terrible price; the synthesis is possible but hard (Maire).
- **How discovered:** Veyl's case; Gravecaller/Warden warnings; experimentation. (M10)
- **Changes:** Turns the climax into an informed moral decision, not a reflex.

## Pacing Guard Rails

- Keep Act 1 *small and personal* (Wren), Act 2 *conspiratorial and contemporary* (collectors, Reke), Act 3 *historical and shattering* (the Concord), Act 4 *apex* (the Court).
- If the player rushes (e.g., dives the shrine at level 4), don't dump R7 — gate it: the upper shrine is dangerous, the Court is dormant/sealed, and they get *fragments* (more clues), not the apex truth. Telegraph lethal danger to discourage premature dives without railroading.
- The world clocks advance the truth toward the player even if the player is passive, so no revelation strictly *requires* the player to chase it — but chasing is always faster and safer.
