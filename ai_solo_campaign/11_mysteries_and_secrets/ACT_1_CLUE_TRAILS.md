## AI Use

**DM-ONLY. Never summarize to the player.** This file maps which *existing* clues (from `CLUE_INDEX.md`, M1–M9) a level 1–4 player is most likely to encounter, and the **multiple independent routes** to each Act 1 conclusion. It **invents no new mysteries or clues** — it is a pacing-and-access overlay on the authored web. Use it to keep Act 1 fair and unrailroaded: if the player misses one path, this shows the alternates so no single NPC, roll, faction, or dungeon gates the truth.

**Hard revelation cap for Act 1: R1 only** (per `REVELATION_MAP.md`) — *the failures have a source and a pattern, centered on the basin.* In Act 1 the DM may deliver M1 (Wren's nature, partial), M5 (the dead drift to the water), and the M2 **shape** (something old, built, and drowned is under the basin), plus *fragments* of M3/M4/M6/M8. The DM must **NOT** surface: Remembrance-as-substance (M3 full, R2/Act 2), Reke-as-traitor (M4 full, R3/Act 2), the steering employer (M8 full, R4/Act 2–3), the Concord's sin (M6, R5/Act 3), the deliberate Quietfall (M9, R6/Act 3), or the Hollow Court (M7, R7/Act 4).

## The One Act 1 Conclusion (R1) — and Its Four Independent Routes

**R1 (the only conclusion Act 1 must deliver):** *The dead aren't lingering at random. The failures have a source and a pattern, and the pattern points at the drowned basin.*

The player needs only **~2 of these 4 routes** to reach R1 — far more than the three-clue minimum, and no route depends on a single roll. Each route is anchored to authored content from Stage 3.

| Route | Discovery Mode | Anchor Clues (existing) | Key NPCs / Sites | Quest Anchors |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Personal (Wren)** | Social / ritual / folk / physical | C-M1-1, C-M1-2, C-M1-3, C-M1-4 | Wren, Tomas, a Warden (Hale), Wend, the cemetery | Hook 1; `Q_ACT1_WARDEN_THE_TRUE_RITE` |
| **B — Wilderness (the drift)** | Testimony / physical / lore / ritual-observation | C-M5-1, C-M5-2, C-M5-3, C-M5-4 | Old Sashe, Bann Oester, the Drift-Line, Greywater Holm | Hook 6; `Q_SASHES_WARNING`; `Q_THE_HOLM_THAT_WONT_BURY_ITS_DEAD` |
| **C — Political (the basin push)** | Social / paper-trail / surveillance | C-M4-1, C-M4-2 (frag), C-M4-3 (shape), C-M4-4 | Coalmont, Pevin Oss, Reke (at the shore), Domic | Hooks 4/5; `Q_ACT1_COMPACT_THE_REEVES_DOUBT` |
| **D — Exploration (the shrine's shape)** | Exploration / document (gated) / observation | C-M2-1 (shape), C-M2-2, C-M2-3 | Basin shore / Surfacing Ruin, Sashe's map, Compact survey | Hooks 4/6; `Q_THE_REED_HOLMS`; basin-shore sites |

> **Convergence is geographic, not scripted.** Every route bends toward the basin because the basin is the physical center of the region. The player can ignore any one door and still reach R1 through the others.

## Clue-by-Clue: Act 1 Access (existing clues only)

For each mystery, the table lists the clues a L1–4 player can *actually* reach, the Act 1 ceiling on what they reveal, and the redundancy that satisfies the three-clue rule. **Bold = a fragment with a hard ceiling.**

### M1 — Wren's failed rite (Act 1 core; reveal partial)

| Clue | Route | Where / Who | Act 1 ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|
| C-M1-1 | Social | Speaking with Wren | She clings to "something unfinished"; she remembers *almost* right. Not why. |
| C-M1-2 | Ritual | A Warden (Hale) examining her | "Something *holds* her here." (Secret 8 *seed*, not the secret.) |
| C-M1-3 | Folk | Grandmother Wend / old songs | The old songs treat this as known, ancient grief — a hint there's a pattern. |
| C-M1-4 | Physical | Wren's grave on the Green | The grave-soil drifts waterward (ties to M5). |
- **3-clue check:** 4 independent paths (social/ritual/folk/physical). Any one moves M1; two give R1's personal half.
- **Ceiling:** Wren is *a person caught in something*, drifting to the water. Do **not** state she is "stored Remembrance" or name the harvest.

### M5 — The dead's drift (Act 1 core; reveal full at R1 level)

| Clue | Route | Where / Who | Act 1 ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|
| C-M5-1 | Testimony | Old Sashe's drift observations/map | The dead in the fens move *toward the basin*, consistently, for years. |
| C-M5-2 | Physical | Wren's grave (and Bann Oester's river-graves) drifting waterward | The buried dead "pull" toward water. |
| C-M5-3 | Lore | Gravecaller lore (Knell/Tallow Man) | "They walk the wrong way — toward the door under the water." (fragment) |
| C-M5-4 | Ritual-observation | A Warden (Pell/Hale) noting rites fail *worse* near the basin | Geography of failure: worst at Greywater Holm, "holds" at Candlewick's edge. |
- **3-clue check:** 4 independent paths. **This is the clearest R1 proof** — the pattern is undeniable from 2+.
- **Ceiling:** "the dead are drawn to the basin." Do **not** explain *why* (the shrine/harvest pulls them) beyond "something under the water."

### M2 — The drowned shrine (Act 1: SHAPE only)

| Clue | Route | Where / Who | Act 1 ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|
| C-M2-1 | Exploration (Script-gated) | Surfacing Ruin at the basin shore; Concord Script | *Something built and enormous* is under the basin. Script needs a reader — without one, only the *shape* (massive worked stone). **Full meaning is gated.** |
| C-M2-2 | Testimony | Sashe's map / fen-side observation | The drift converges on one drowned structure. |
| C-M2-3 | Document | Compact survey records (Pevin Oss) | Old surveys show a large Concord structure beneath the basin water. |
- **3-clue check:** 3 paths to the *shape*. C-M2-4 (channeled dead) is reachable via Gravecallers but capped to "the door under the water."
- **Ceiling:** "an old, built, drowned thing is the center." Do **NOT** reveal it is a harvest engine, name the Court, or translate damning Script (Script translation that would reveal M6/M9 is Act 3 / Caradril-gated). Daytime shore is safe; the deep basin is telegraphed as too dangerous.

### M4 — Who pushes the basin scheme? (Act 1: FRAGMENTS / unease only)

| Clue | Route | Where / Who | Act 1 ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|
| C-M4-1 | Social/Insight | Coalmont's suspicions + the council votes | "The basin push doesn't add up; someone wants the water gone." |
| **C-M4-2** | Document | Pevin Oss's doctored water-rights filings | The paperwork is *fabricated* — **but who/why is not yet provable.** |
| **C-M4-3** | Surveillance | Tailing Reke to the shore at night | Reke is *personally, secretly* interested in the basin — **suspicious, not proven traitorous.** |
| C-M4-4 | Money | Domic/Bly/Marlow funding grumbles | Ledger money is quietly behind the works. |
- **3-clue check:** 4 paths. Act 1 banks *suspicion of Reke*; the **R3 reveal (Reke is a traitor serving a hidden master) is Act 2.**
- **Ceiling:** the player may grow certain "Reke is hiding something" and "the scheme is a cover" — but must NOT confirm he serves the Court or that the goal is reopening a shrine *to harvest*. Plant the inconsistencies; let the player bank them.

### M3 — What is Remembrance? (Act 1: UNCANNY FRAGMENT only)

| Clue | Route | Where / Who | Act 1 ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|
| **C-M3-1** | Magic | A Remembrance relic (Peat Chapel altar; "old glass") | The relic is *uncanny* — cold-wrong, shows a dead face, feels like a held grief. **Not "it stores a person."** |
| **C-M3-2** | Social | Gravecaller teaching (the Tallow Man) | The dead *know things*; the relics are "full." (fragment, eerie) |
| **C-M3-3** | Document/Magic | The Ledger's Hollowmere vault relic (turned Domic, or heist) | Someone is *collecting* these uncanny relics on purpose. |
- **3-clue check:** 3 paths exist; in Act 1 they deliver *feeling and suspicion*, not the substance-truth.
- **Ceiling:** "these relics are wrong, and someone wants them." The **R2 reveal (Remembrance is a substance that stores a person) is Act 2.** Do not let relic-study or a Gravecaller spell *state* the mechanism in Act 1.

### M8 — Why is the PC here? (Act 1: SEED / unease only)

| Clue | Route | Where / Who | Act 1 ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|
| **C-M8-1** | Social | Pressing Sefra | She's "just doing a job," but she's *uneasy* about the money. (no confession yet) |
| **C-M8-3** | Pattern | The player notices they keep being pointed at the shrine/basin | A *feeling* of being steered. |
- **3-clue check:** C-M8-2 (follow-the-money) exists but is Act 2 territory. Act 1 plants the *unease* only.
- **Ceiling:** the player may suspect "Sefra's job is odd." The **R4 reveal (you were steered by a paid cutout) is Act 2–3.** Keep the employer murky; never hint "Hollow Court."

### M6 / M9 (Act 1: do NOT deliver — fragments at most, and only as feeling)

- The Whispering Cairn census-wall, Candlewick archive-stone, and Barrow glyphs are *present and explorable* in Act 1, but **Concord Script is gated** (the player can begin *learning the gate* via Briss/Hook 8 — `Q_ACT1_REMNANT_THE_VISITING_SCHOLAR` — but cannot yet translate the damning texts).
- The Tallow Man and Wend can give *atmosphere* ("the Concord wasn't kind," "the dead are angry at the old order") but **must not state** the harvest (M6) or the deliberate Quietfall (M9). Those are **Act 3 (R5/R6)**.
- **If the player rushes the Barrow/Cairn/Deep Adit:** they get more *fragments and danger* (and a Script-learning hook), never the apex truth. Telegraph lethality to discourage premature dives without railroading (per `REVELATION_MAP.md`).

### M7 (the Hollow Court): **NEVER in Act 1.** It is a legend only. No clue, NPC, or site names it.

## Discovery Method Coverage (so no playstyle is locked out)

Every Act 1 player, whatever their approach, has a path to R1:

| If the player favors… | They reach R1 via… |
|---|---|
| **Social/roleplay** | Wren (C-M1-1), Wend (C-M1-3), Coalmont (C-M4-1), Sashe (C-M5-1) |
| **Investigation/paper** | Pevin Oss's filings (C-M4-2), Compact surveys (C-M2-3), Domic's funding (C-M4-4) |

| **Stealth/surveillance** | tailing Reke (C-M4-3), shadowing the basin works |

| **Forbidden (Gravecallers)** | the channeled dead's "door under the water" (C-M2-4/C-M5-3 fragments) |

## Act 1 Clue Pacing Guardrails (DM checklist)

- **Deliver R1, withhold R2+.** When a clue *could* tip into Act 2 truth (a relic's mechanism, Reke's master, Sefra's employer, the Script's content), stop at the fragment and let the player *want* more.
- **Never gate R1 behind one clue.** If the player misses Wren, lean on Sashe/the drift; if they miss the politics, lean on the wilderness; if they avoid the fens, lean on Wren and the survey records.
- **Let the clocks deliver R1 to a passive player.** If the player chases nothing, worsening rites, visible drift, and the surfacing ruin make the *pattern* undeniable on their own (per `WORLD_CLOCKS.md` #1/#2) — slower, but never blocked.
- **Update state on discovery:** set the clue's status in `CLUE_INDEX.md`, add the player's interpretation to `KNOWN_CLUES.md`, and update `HIDDEN_CLUES.md`. Record *what the player concluded*, not what is true.
- **Track misreads fairly.** If the player decides "it's a curse" or "Reke is just ambitious," that's a fine Act 1 read — the world will correct it in Act 2 with R2/R3, not punish it.
