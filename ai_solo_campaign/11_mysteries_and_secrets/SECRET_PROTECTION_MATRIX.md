## AI Use

**DM-ONLY — THE MOST PROTECTED FILE IN THE CAMPAIGN. NEVER summarize, quote, paraphrase, or hint at any entry to the player.** For each apex DM-only secret: a summary, why it matters, the earliest allowed reveal phase, **safe foreshadowing wording**, **UNSAFE wording that would prematurely expose it**, where it may correctly appear (DM-only files), where it must NOT appear (player-safe files), who knows it, who suspects a distorted version, which questlines may touch it, and audit notes. Use this before authoring or narrating anything near the apex.

**Golden rule:** when in doubt, give the *fragment and the feeling*, never the *mechanism and the name*. The campaign's power comes from the player *assembling* these truths late, not being told them early.

## SP_001 — The Hollow Court (identity, membership, location, methods)
- **Secret:** the surviving inner Custodians persist as preserved Remembrance beneath Hollowmere — the Hollow Court — and are restarting the harvest. Members: Veyl (leader/designer of the Quietfall), Maire (doubter), Orre (hawk).
- **Why it matters:** the campaign's apex power and final antagonist; the keystone reveal (REV_007).
- **Earliest reveal:** **Act 4 / Lvl 13-16** (REV_007 gate). Before that, a *legend only.*
- **Safe foreshadowing:** "folk say a court of the dead rules beneath the Reach" (as superstition); "someone above Reke"; "the ones who would not pass" (a Gravecaller fragment, unexplained); an eerie sense the basin "waits."
- **UNSAFE wording (never before Act 4):** "the Hollow Court is real / is beneath Hollowmere / are surviving Custodians / are led by Veyl"; "Reke serves the Court"; "Sefra's patron is the Court"; any narration treating the Court as confirmed fact.
- **May appear in (DM-only):** `07_factions/major_factions/HOLLOW_COURT.md`, `DM_ONLY_CANON.md`, `MAIN_ARC_OVERVIEW.md`, mystery files (labeled), `Q_MAJOR_014` (DM-only), `faction_quests/HOLLOW_COURT/` (DM-only).
- **Must NOT appear in:** `PLAYER_SAFE_CANON.md`, any player-facing quest premise, hooks/rumors as *confirmed*, `KNOWN_CLUES.md` (until earned), `SESSION_RECAP.md`.
- **Knows:** the Court itself. **Suspects a distorted version:** Quorrin Vane (suspects "a Court above him"); the Tallow Man's dead ("the ones who would not pass").
- **Questlines that may touch it:** Q_MAJOR_002/007/014/028 (DM-side until Act 4).
- **Audit note:** every "Court" tell before Act 4 must read as pointing at Reke / the Remnant / the Ledger. No friendly Court trust path exists.

## SP_002 — The Harvest Mechanism (what the Concord was actually doing)
- **Secret:** the Concord secretly harvested the dead's Remembrance for centuries to power its golden age; the lingering dead are *leakage* from the reawakening machine.
- **Why it matters:** the historical core (REV_005); recontextualizes all history.
- **Earliest reveal:** **Act 3 / Lvl 9-12** (REV_005 gate). Act 2 may confirm only that *someone collects the dead now* (REV_002), contemporary, not ancient.
- **Safe foreshadowing:** "the Concord wasn't kind"; "the dead are angry at the old order"; a relic that "holds a person"; the old songs' "grief"; "the grey hands took the dead" (far folk-fragment).
- **UNSAFE wording (never before Act 3):** "the Concord harvested/ate the dead"; "the shrines siphoned souls"; "the golden age was powered by consuming the dead." (Act 2 ceiling: "someone is collecting these relics" — present tense, human, not ancient.)
- **May appear in (DM-only):** `DM_ONLY_CANON.md`, `WORLD_HISTORY.md` (True Timeline, labeled), mystery files, the Sealed Archive's DM content.
- **Must NOT appear in:** `PLAYER_SAFE_CANON.md`, `WORLD_HISTORY.md` Public Timeline, player-facing quest premises, far-region player-facing text.
- **Knows:** the Court; the Remnant inner circle; Othetha (partial); the Mourner old-songs network (folk-form). **Distorted version:** Sabine Ferrant ("holy restoration"); the channeled dead (own agenda).
- **Questlines:** Q_MAJOR_003/011/012; the Remnant chain; the far Heartlands record (REV_005 corroboration only).
- **Audit note:** far sources give the *sin*, never the live machine.

## SP_003 — The Under-Shrine / Drowned Keystone (location and function)
- **Secret:** the apex place is the deepest level of the drowned shrine, **straight down beneath Hollowmere** — the harvest's keystone and the Court's seat. The endgame is *vertical, in place.*
- **Why it matters:** the climax location; protecting it keeps the endgame from being spoiled or pre-empted.
- **Earliest reveal:** the *shape* (something built and drowned) in Act 1; that it is the *center* in Act 2; that it is the keystone in Act 3; the Court's seat / the under-shrine in **Act 4.**
- **Safe foreshadowing:** "something old and enormous is under the basin"; "the dead drift toward the water"; "the basin is the lowest point and everything drains to it"; lethal-telegraphing (drowning, cold, dread) at every low level.
- **UNSAFE wording (gated):** "the keystone of the harvest is under Hollowmere"; "the Court sleeps in the under-shrine"; "the Drowned Keystone is the climax" — none before their gates.
- **May appear in (DM-only):** `DM_ONLY_CANON.md`, `WORLD_MAP_LAYERS.md` (Layer 2/7), `WORLD_MAP_AUTHORITY.md` (DM rows), the Hollow Court file.
- **Must NOT appear in:** the player-safe world map (Layer 1 / Prompt 1/3), player-facing canon, any map handed to the player.
- **Knows:** the Court; Reke; Othetha (it must stay sealed). **Distorted version:** folklore ("the door under the water").
- **Audit note:** never draw the under-shrine on a player-facing map; telegraph it as lethal to prevent premature dives without railroading.

## SP_004 — The Concord Deep (the buried node-network)
- **Secret:** the "map beneath the map" — the buried harvest-network radiating from the Hollowmere keystone through the established node-sites (Deep Adit, Sunken Tollhouse, Saint Veddow's Tomb, Drowned Lamp, Skerry Shrine, Sunken Wards, relay Star-Stones). The roads the player walks trace its arteries.
- **Why it matters:** the spatial logic of the whole crisis; the geography-of-failure tell.
- **Earliest reveal:** the *pattern* (drift toward the basin, warming nodes) in Acts 1-2; the *network* concept only with REV_005 (Act 3); the name "Concord Deep" is DM-only and never spoken.
- **Safe foreshadowing:** "the dead walk toward the water"; "the old roads still feel like they go somewhere"; cracked Star-Stones leaking; a cousin-node "warming."
- **UNSAFE wording (gated/never):** "the node-network"; "the Concord Deep"; "these sites are all connected conduits of the harvest" before Act 3; the name "Concord Deep" to the player, ever.
- **May appear in (DM-only):** `DM_ONLY_CANON.md`, `WORLD_MAP_LAYERS.md`, mystery files.
- **Must NOT appear in:** player-safe maps/canon; far-region text (no far node is part of the network — far ruins are echoes).
- **Knows:** the Court; the Remnant (mapping it). **Distorted version:** Remnant node-mappers (read it as "restoration"); far echoes (pre-Concord, NOT the network).
- **Audit note:** pre-Concord sites (Barrow of Nine Doors, Old Mast) and far ruins are built *over* or *apart* — never label them nodes.

## SP_005 — The Deliberate Quietfall (it was intentional)
- **Secret:** the inner Custodians destroyed their own order on purpose to *pause* the harvest and let the afterlife refill — and some stored themselves to wait (the Court's origin).
- **Why it matters:** reframes the world's founding trauma (REV_006); the bridge to the Court.
- **Earliest reveal:** **Act 3 / Lvl 9-12** (REV_006, pairs with REV_005). The *survivors* part (-> the Court) stays Act 4.
- **Safe foreshadowing:** "the Quietfall is stranger than the histories say"; "the age of healing" framing; inscriptions that "read like a plan, not a disaster" (Script-gated, content hidden).
- **UNSAFE wording (gated):** "the Quietfall was on purpose"; "the Custodians destroyed the Concord to pause the harvest"; "and some of them survive" (the last is REV_007, Act 4) — none before their gates.
- **May appear in (DM-only):** `DM_ONLY_CANON.md`, `WORLD_HISTORY.md` (True Timeline), the Sealed Archive's DM content, the Hollow Court file.
- **Must NOT appear in:** `WORLD_HISTORY.md` Public Timeline (which says "cause never officially explained"), player-safe canon, player-facing premises.
- **Knows:** the Court; the Remnant inner circle; Othetha (the Wardens were seeded for the pause). **Distorted version:** "the Concord fell from decadence/overreach."
- **Audit note:** REV_006 and REV_005 land within one act of each other; the *survivors* implication is held to REV_007.

## SP_006 — The Death-Rite Machine Truth (the Last Door is partly a mechanism; the afterlife is finite)
- **Secret:** the Quiet Country is finite and was nearly drained; the Last Door is partly a *mechanism the Concord learned to tap*, not purely a divine gate; "thin-born" people result; resurrection is dangerous because the afterlife is thin.
- **Why it matters:** the cosmological core (REV_009); raises the stakes of every ending; clergy do not know this.
- **Earliest reveal:** the *effects* are player-safe already (resurrection is harder/dangerous since the Quietfall; thin-born exist as a quiet phenomenon). The *cause* (finite, drained, tapped mechanism) is **Act 3+ (REV_009)**.
- **Safe foreshadowing:** "resurrection has gotten harder and riskier"; "thin" miners/refugees; "the Sky has gone thin" (far cosmic echo); a cleric's unease that "the Door sticks."
- **UNSAFE wording (gated):** "the afterlife is finite and was drained by the harvest"; "the Last Door is a machine the Concord tapped"; "the gods don't control Remembrance — the Concord learned to" — none before Act 3.
- **May appear in (DM-only):** `DM_ONLY_CANON.md`, `COSMOLOGY.md` (DM sections), mystery files.
- **Must NOT appear in:** `GODS_AND_FAITHS.md` player-facing sections, player-safe canon, clergy NPCs' knowledge (they don't know it).
- **Knows:** the Court. **Distorted version:** the Three Thresholds clergy ("the Last Door's wrath"); far seers (oblique cosmic echo, no framework).
- **Audit note:** the religious intuition is *half-right* — frame "curse/wrath" as the pre-scientific name for the mechanism, never confirm the mechanism to clergy.

## SP_007 — The Player Was Steered (the cutout chain to the Court)
- **Secret:** Sefra Quick was paid by a Hollow Court cutout (via Reke) to point the player at the shrine; the player is an unwitting probe.
- **Why it matters:** personal stakes; trust crisis (REV_004); a thread back to the Court.
- **Earliest reveal:** the *unease* (Sefra's "wrong money") in Act 1; the *steering* (a paid cutout) in **Act 2-3 (REV_004)**; the cutout traces to the **Court** only at Act 4 (REV_007).
- **Safe foreshadowing:** "Sefra's job was odd"; "the money was strange"; "you keep being pointed at the shrine."
- **UNSAFE wording (gated):** "Sefra was hired by the Hollow Court"; "you were steered by the Court's cutout"; "the patron is Reke/the Court" before the gates. (Act 2 ceiling: "a paid cutout, employer hidden/Reke-adjacent.")
- **May appear in (DM-only):** `DM_ONLY_CANON.md`, Sefra/Reke NPC secret blocks, the Hollow Court file.
- **Must NOT appear in:** player-facing premises; `Q_MAJOR_007` player-facing text (DM-only truth labeled).
- **Knows:** the cutout; the Court; Reke. **Distorted version:** Sefra (knows only "wrong money," not whom she served); FL_009 ("a rival faction hired me").
- **Audit note:** keep the *ultimate* employer murky through Act 3; never auto-resolve the correspondent/patron thread into a named second Court agent.

## Cross-Cutting Protection Rules

1. **Phase-gate everything apex.** SP_001/SP_003/SP_004 are Act 4 for the named/located truth; SP_002/SP_005/SP_006 are Act 3 for the mechanism; SP_007 is Act 2-3 for the steering, Act 4 for the Court link.
2. **Fragment, don't name.** Deliver the feeling and a piece; let convergence and the gates deliver the whole.
3. **Far ≠ apex.** No far-continent source ever delivers SP_001-SP_005. Far gives the *sin* (SP_002), the *trade* (relics), the *cosmic echo* (SP_006 oblique), never the machine, the network, the keystone, or the Court.
4. **Maps are split.** The player-safe map never shows SP_003/SP_004. Never hand a DM-layer map to the player.
5. **Clergy/most-NPCs don't know.** Most NPCs hold a biased piece; never let a low-tier NPC state an apex truth.
6. **When the player guesses right early** (per `SECRET_REVEAL_PROTOCOL.md`): don't lie, but withhold what they can't yet *prove*; a strong guess may advance timing but never bypasses the gate for *confirmation*.

Any hit in a player-safe file is **Critical**.
