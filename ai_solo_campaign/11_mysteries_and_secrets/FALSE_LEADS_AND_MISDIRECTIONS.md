## AI Use

**DM-ONLY. Never summarize to the player.** The campaign's authored false leads — each **fair** per `00_control/MYSTERY_STANDARDS.md`: it has a reason to exist, can be disproven, teaches something useful, points toward real content, and never punishes reasonable thinking or blocks progress. **Every false lead here does at least one of:** reveals a smaller truth, exposes a faction motive, points to a real clue, creates an interesting consequence, or helps the player understand the world. **No pure dead ends.**

**How to run a false lead:** deliver it sincerely (as the in-world source believes it), let the player invest, and let *later clues* — never an authorial "gotcha" — correct it. If the player adopts a false belief, track it as a **misinterpreted** clue in `KNOWN_CLUES.md` and let the world respond honestly. A false lead that has done its job should be retired (below) so it doesn't loop.

## FL_001 — "A grave-robber is causing the restless dead"
- **Appears to suggest:** the failures are caused by Drane the Reedwarden robbing the failed-rite graves.
- **Plausible because:** Drane really does rob graves and "feeds the fen"; disturbed graves correlate with restless dead.
- **Who believes it:** frightened fen-folk; some Compact officials who want a simple culprit.
- **Who benefits:** the real cause (the Court) — a human scapegoat deflects suspicion; cover-up officials who want it solved cheaply.
- **Supporting evidence:** robbed graves; Drane's reputation; restless dead near his sites.
- **Contradicting evidence:** Old Sashe's drift-map shows the dead drifting basin-ward for *years* before Drane; rites fail worse near water regardless of robbery; failures occur where Drane never went.
- **Recovery:** the player compares timelines/geography (Sashe, Bann Oester) and sees Drane is a predator *exploiting* the crisis, not causing it.
- **Useful truth it still points toward:** REV_001 (the real pattern); the relic trade (Drane sells what he robs -> REV_002 thread).
- **Retire when:** the player confirms REV_001's geographic pattern, or deals with Drane and the failures continue.
- **Related:** Drane (major NPC 21); `by_region/SUNDERING_REACH_CLUES.md`.

## FL_002 — "The Gravecallers are stirring the dead"
- **Appears to suggest:** the cult's rites are what's making the dead linger.
- **Plausible because:** the Gravecallers openly contact the dead and are feared; their activity rose with the crisis.
- **Who believes it:** most townsfolk; the Ashen Wardens sometimes blame them; the Compact (outlawed cult = easy enemy).
- **Who benefits:** the Court (deflection); anyone wanting the cult crushed.
- **Supporting evidence:** cult sightings near restless-dead sites; the Knock; channeled dead.
- **Contradicting evidence:** the drift and failures predate cult activity (Sashe's years-long map); the cult is *reacting* to the dead, and the Tallow Man's witness blames the Concord, not the cult.
- **Recovery:** contact the cult (via Iola/Tomas Brevin) and learn they're symptom-chasers; the timeline disproves causation.
- **Useful truth it still points toward:** REV_002 (the cult teaches the substance); REV_005 fragments (the channeled witness).
- **Retire when:** the player makes cult contact and grasps they're a tragic-mirror, not the cause.
- **Related:** the Tallow Man, Knell (NPCs 9-10); `by_faction/FACTION_KNOWLEDGE_MAP.md`.

## FL_003 — "The basin scheme is just water rights / ordinary corruption"
- **Appears to suggest:** Reke's basin drain is a mundane land/water grab; he's just an ambitious or bribed politician.
- **Plausible because:** the cover story is professionally built (doctored surveys; real Ledger money; resettlement framing).
- **Who believes it:** most of the Compact; the player, early.
- **Who benefits:** Reke and the Court — the mundane reading is the whole point of the cover.
- **Supporting evidence:** the water-rights filings; Ledger funding; the resettlement rhetoric.
- **Contradicting evidence:** the filings are *fabricated* (Pevin Oss); Reke visits the basin shore *alone at night*; the scheme targets the drowned shrine specifically; Sela Coalmont senses the Compact is "steered."
- **Recovery:** the paper trail + surveillance + the reeve's suspicion converge on "this is a cover."
- **Useful truth it still points toward:** REV_003 (Reke is a traitor serving a hidden master) — the false lead is the *gateway* to the real one.
- **Retire when:** the player confirms REV_003. (Note: even after, his *master* stays hidden until Act 4 — a deliberate deeper layer, not a false lead.)
- **Related:** Reke (NPC 8); `Q_MAJOR_002`.

## FL_004 — "The Ledger / the Remnant is the apex enemy"
- **Appears to suggest:** the top of the conspiracy is Vyre's Ledger (monopoly) or Quorrin's Remnant (seize the harvest).
- **Plausible because:** both DO know the harvest is real and DO want to control it; in Act 3 they are the most visible powerful villains.
- **Who believes it:** the player, in Act 3 — this is the *intended* mid-game misread.
- **Who benefits:** the Court (the perfect decoys — real, ambitious, knowable enemies who draw fire).
- **Supporting evidence:** the Vyre-Quorrin deal; the Reclamation; the Sealed Archive in Remnant hands; Veska Dunn's "seize is preservation."
- **Contradicting evidence:** both answer to *no one above them yet act like pieces*; Quorrin *suspects a Court above him*; Reke's master is neither; leaked Remembrances and the basin's pull predate and exceed any living faction's reach.
- **Recovery:** convergence (REV_003 + REV_005 + REV_006) reveals a power *older and beneath* the living factions — REV_007.
- **Useful truth it still points toward:** REV_005/REV_006 (the Ledger/Remnant ARE the path to the historical truth); the "seize/sell" endings.
- **Retire when:** REV_007 lands (Act 4). Until then, let the player believe the Remnant/Ledger is the top — it's a fair, productive read.
- **Related:** Vyre, Quorrin (NPCs 3, 12); `by_faction/FACTION_KNOWLEDGE_MAP.md`.

## FL_005 — "It's a curse / divine punishment / the gods are angry"
- **Appears to suggest:** the failures are supernatural punishment, a curse on Hollowmere, or the Last Door's wrath.
- **Plausible because:** the Three Thresholds faith frames death as divine; the eerie tone invites it; clergy may say so.
- **Who believes it:** devout folk; some Threshold clergy; Act 1 players.
- **Who benefits:** no one deliberately — but it comforts those who'd rather not investigate, and it shields the real cause by mystifying it.
- **Supporting evidence:** the uncanny; clergy interpretation; the "Last Door sticks" framing.
- **Contradicting evidence:** the failures have a *physical pattern* (geography, the drift, the relics) inconsistent with random divine wrath; the substance behaves like an industrial process, not a curse.
- **Recovery:** REV_001 (pattern) then REV_002 (substance) reframe it as mechanism, not wrath.
- **Useful truth it still points toward:** REV_009 (the Last Door IS partly a tapped mechanism — the religious intuition is *half right*).
- **Retire when:** REV_002 lands; thereafter "curse" reads as the pre-scientific name for the machine.
- **Related:** `03_canon/GODS_AND_FAITHS.md`; Mother Ysarra.

## FL_006 — "The Hollow Court is just a ghost story"
- **Appears to suggest:** the "court of the dead beneath the Reach" is pure superstition.
- **Plausible because:** it IS treated as a legend; no credible evidence surfaces until very late; every clue seems to point elsewhere.
- **Who believes it:** essentially everyone, including the player, until Act 4. **This is the protective default and is *correct enough* to act on until the apex.**
- **Who benefits:** the Court (its legend status is its camouflage).
- **Supporting evidence:** folklore; the absence of proof; the visible factions filling the "villain" role.
- **Contradicting evidence:** only assembled very late (REV_003 + REV_005 + REV_006 convergence; the under-shrine).
- **Recovery:** this is the ONE false lead that should NOT be corrected early. Let it stand until REV_007's gate. Correcting it prematurely collapses the campaign.
- **Useful truth it still points toward:** itself, eventually (the legend turns out true).
- **Retire when:** Act 4 / REV_007 only.
- **Related:** `SECRET_PROTECTION_MATRIX.md` (REV_007 protection); `07_factions/major_factions/HOLLOW_COURT.md`.

## FL_007 — "Destroying the shrines will fix everything"
- **Appears to suggest:** shattering the keystone/shrines frees the dead and ends the crisis cleanly (the Gravecaller/Mourner-radical pitch).
- **Plausible because:** it's morally appealing (free the dead) and the radicals are sincere and sometimes right.
- **Who believes it:** Cole Ashby; Mourner radicals; a player drawn to the "liberation" framing.
- **Who benefits:** no one cynically — but acting on it risks the destroy-ending catastrophe.
- **Supporting evidence:** the dead's suffering; the cult's fragment-truth; the breaking-tools exist.
- **Contradicting evidence:** the Warden warning (sealing/destruction ends resurrection) and deeper lore: total destruction destabilizes the living/dead boundary (REV_008 destroy-cost).
- **Recovery:** the player learns the *cost* (REV_008) before committing — the lead isn't *false* so much as *dangerously incomplete*; it points at a real (if catastrophic) ending.
- **Useful truth it still points toward:** REV_008 (the destroy-ending and its price); the synthesis (REV_010) as the harder, better answer.
- **Retire when:** REV_008 lands; then it's an informed choice, not a misread.
- **Related:** Cole Ashby (NPC 11); `MAIN_ARC_OVERVIEW.md` endgame branches.

## FL_008 — "The far ruins / the Ruin'd Crown are where the real power is"
- **Appears to suggest:** the harvest-heart, or the Court, is at the great Concord ruins far away (the Heartlands/Ruin'd Crown), not under a frontier town.
- **Plausible because:** the Crown is the Concord's vast fallen *capital*; Hollin Vane's expedition treats it as the prize; it's grander than Hollowmere.
- **Who believes it:** Hollin Vane; the Remnant's reclaimers; a far-wandering player.
- **Who benefits:** the Court (misdirection toward a distant decoy keeps the keystone hidden).
- **Supporting evidence:** the Crown's scale; its harvest-*record*; the factions' covetousness.
- **Contradicting evidence:** the Crown holds a *record* of the Concord's deeds, never the live machine; the drift, the worsening, and the keystone's pull all center on Hollowmere; the endgame is vertical, under the starting town.
- **Recovery:** the player who chases the Crown finds REV_005 corroboration (the Concord's sin, written) but NOT the Court — and the trail of *current* failures keeps pointing home.
- **Useful truth it still points toward:** REV_005 (the Concord's true scale/sin); the futility of "seize the Crown" as an ending.
- **Retire when:** the player grasps the keystone is under Hollowmere (latest at REV_007).
- **Related:** `by_region/FAR_CONTINENT_ECHO_CLUES.md`; Hollin Vane; `SECRET_PROTECTION_MATRIX.md`.

## FL_009 — "Sefra set me up for a rival faction"
- **Appears to suggest:** the player's steering traces to an ordinary faction (a Ledger or Remnant recruiter), not something deeper.
- **Plausible because:** factions DO recruit; Sefra is a known fixer; "wrong money" reads as a faction's coin.
- **Who believes it:** the player, on first learning of the steering (REV_004).
- **Who benefits:** the Court (the cutout chain is designed to look like ordinary faction work).
- **Supporting evidence:** Sefra's fixer reputation; faction interest in the player.
- **Contradicting evidence:** the payment trail dead-ends at a cutout that matches *no* faction's books; it traces Reke-adjacent.
- **Recovery:** following the money far enough shows it isn't a normal faction — it's a firewall (the deeper truth waits for Act 4).
- **Useful truth it still points toward:** REV_004 (you were steered) and, eventually, the Court link.
- **Retire when:** the player accepts the employer is hidden/Reke-adjacent (REV_004); fully resolved only at REV_007.
- **Related:** Sefra Quick (NPC 16); `Q_MAJOR_007`.
