## AI Use

Load only when the player braves the offshore **Skerries** — the sea-stacks where wrecks pile, the worst drowned gather, and a Concord sea-shrine stands guarded by the dead. The Pale Coast's gated apex: a higher-tier (level 6–9), boat-and-tide-locked site that delivers the Coast's deepest M2/M5/M6 oblique fragment. **Strictly capped:** fragments, the wreckers' worst secret, and danger — never the apex truth, never the Hollow Court. Storm, tide, and the sea are the gate and the danger.

## One-Sentence Identity

A Concord sea-shrine on a drowned skerry, ringed by wrecks and guarded by the sea's worst drowned, where the Coast's maritime harvest shows its oldest face — for any crew brave or foolish enough to make the crossing.

## History

Publicly: cursed sea-stacks where ships die and the drowned gather; the wreckers' worst haunt; folk won't go near. **Truly (DM):** the largest of the coastal harvest-nodes — a sea-shrine that "kept" the deep-sea drowned, now half-submerged on a skerry. The wreckers use the Skerries' false-light reefs and stash their worst secrets here; the Gravecallers' boldest come for the drowned that "remember the taking." As the keystone wakes, the Skerry Shrine warms and the worst drowned gather — making the Skerries the Coast's deadliest reach.

## Entrance

A boat-crossing from Wrackmouth or the wreckers' strand, only in fair weather and the right tide, to a skerry where a drowned Concord shrine stands half-awash. **Telegraphed hard:** every skipper warns against it; storms and tides lock it; false lights lure boats onto the reefs; the worst drowned ring it. A low-level crossing is signposted as drowning.

## Solo Danger Rating

**High and gated (level 6–9).** The crossing itself (storm, tide, false lights, the worst drowned) is the chief danger; the shrine holds older drowned and a climactic keeper. Telegraphed throughout; the deepest submerged level is off-limits. Requires a boat, a skipper or seamanship, and weather-judgment — self-limiting for a low-level party.

## Zone / Room List

1. **The Reef Approach** — the false-light reefs and wrecks; the crossing and the wreckers' lures (the gate).
2. **The Skerry Strand** — the stack's shingle; wrecks, the wreckers' worst stash, and the gathered drowned.
3. **The Drowned Porch** — the shrine's awash entrance; Script and the first fragment (M2).
4. **The Keeping-Deep (awash at high tide)** — the shrine's main chamber; Script-graven, drowned, warming; the worst drowned and the oldest sea-relics (M3); the climax.
5. **The Sea-Throat (off-limits)** — the node proper, descending into the deep sea; **capped, drowned, and lethal** — telegraphed as a drowning death.

## Encounter List

- **Reef Approach / Skerry Strand:** environmental (false lights, wrecks, tide); **the worst drowned** gather here — flavor **wraith/drowned-spirit-like** (AC 13–14, ~45–60 HP); telegraphed; resist easy laying (offering, salt-lore, the right rite matter; some cannot be laid, only honored or escaped). Possibly **wreckers** guarding their stash (Bandit/Veteran-like).
- **Drowned Porch / Keeping-Deep (climax):** the **Skerry-Keeper** — the Remembrance of the shrine's original keeper, the eldest of the coastal keepers — flavor a **powerful wraith/ghost-like guardian** (AC 14, ~70 HP at L7; *drowning grip, keening wail, frightful presence, summon a drowned-one 1/short*). **Not mindless:** it keeps the deep drowned and tests intruders; reverence/rite/parley resolve it better than force.

## Traps / Hazards (DCs)

- **The crossing (storm & tide):** Survival/seamanship DC 16 (DC 18 in worsening weather); failure swamps/wrecks the boat (cold-sea drowning; recoverable only by a skipper/the right tide). The chief, telegraphed danger.
- **False lights / wreckers' lures:** Wis DC 14 to see through them; failure drives the boat onto the reefs.
- **Tide-lock:** the Keeping-Deep floods at high tide; Survival DC 15 to time it; a missed tide strands the player on the awash skerry (telegraphed; survivable by waiting if alive).
- **Cold-water immersion:** Con DC 14 or exhaustion/worse.
- **Thin-touch (sea-relics, the node):** Con DC 14; reversible.
- The sea is the danger; no lethal mechanical traps beyond it.

## Puzzle / Clue Checks

- **The Drowned Porch Script:** Concord Script (reader required) — the shrine "kept the drowned of the deep for the Keepers" (M2/M5/M6 oblique **fragment**: the largest coastal node; act-paced; never the Court).
- **The Skerry-Keeper's testimony (gated, Act 2–3+):** with reverence/rite/parley (Insight/Persuasion DC 16, or the right salt-and-tide rite Religion DC 16), the eldest keeper testifies — "we kept them all; the sea never runs dry of the dead" — the Coast's deepest oblique M6 fragment. **Cap: a fragment and a dread, never the harvest's mechanism, never the Court.**
- **The wreckers' worst stash:** Investigation DC 13 — what the wreckers hide here (a campaign-flavor secret: e.g., a high-value sea-relic, or evidence implicating Cole/the Ledger deeper).

## Resting Constraints

**No safe rest on the Skerries** (tide, the worst drowned, the wreckers). Wrackmouth or Cobble Strand, a sea-crossing away, are the havens. The tide and weather force the player off — a built-in safety valve.

## Treasure

- The oldest **sea-relics** (high-value Remembrance relics; M3; the gravest moral choice yet on the Coast).
- The wreckers' worst stash (significant plunder + a campaign-flavor secret/leverage).
- A **keeper's deep-token** (marks the bearer to coastal Mourners/Wardens; possible rite-aid boon).
- **No node-treasure** — the Sea-Throat is off-limits.

## Boss / Climax Mechanics

The **Skerry-Keeper** is the eldest coastal guardian — a tougher Drowned-Lamp-Keeper:
- **Combat:** AC 14, ~70 HP (L7); resistant to nonmagical damage; *keening wail* (Con DC 15, 4d8 cold/necrotic, recharge), *drowning grip*, *frightful presence*, *summon a drowned-one*. Hard; **fades/breaks off if the player retreats to the boat ahead of the tide** (bound to the node and the sea).
- **Ritual/parley (best):** the right salt-and-tide rite or reverent parley (DC 16) settles it and yields the deepest oblique M6 fragment; the intended resolution.
- Force alone is the worst path; the sea and the keeper punish it.

Scaling: L6 → ~56 HP, fewer worst-drowned; L8–9 → ~84 HP, the Skerries fully roused; the Sea-Throat stays gated regardless.

## Retreat Options

The boat and the tide are the retreat — and the gate; the Keeper and the drowned cannot leave the node/sea, and the tide drives the player off. A skipper (Wenna Roke) is the lifeline. Diving the Sea-Throat is telegraphed drowning.

## Scaling Notes

- **L6:** the crossing turns the player back early; thin fragment; "the Skerries aren't for you yet."
- **L7 (intended):** as written.
- **L8–9:** the full oblique fragment and the wreckers' worst secret; the Skerries fully dangerous; the node loud.
- **Always gated:** never the Court, never the harvest mechanism, never the Sea-Throat. The Coast's deepest truth points at the harvest from the sea; it is not a shortcut to the apex (the main M2/M3/M5 truth lives at the Drowned Lamp, the Reach, and Caradril).

## Consequences If Ignored

If left alone, the Skerry Shrine warms unwatched, the worst drowned gather, the wreckers keep it as a redoubt, and the Coast's deepest maritime truth stays with the dead and the cult. If the Skerries fully rouse (the Coast clock's end), the outer coast becomes a killing sea and the drowned-tide reaches the harbor-towns — a Coast-scale disaster the player could have eased.
