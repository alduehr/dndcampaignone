## AI Use

Load for the Tollwood's mid-wood dark heart — an ancient oak-grove where the Gravecaller cell meets and the old forest dead gather. A site dungeon that is part old-growth haunting, part Gravecaller parley/conflict, and the clearest place to hear the deep wood's **oblique M5/M6 corroboration** ("the grey lords took the dead before the roads fell") short of the gated Old Mast. Pre-Concord and strange; not a simple combat crawl.

## One-Sentence Identity

A grove of vast, old oaks the wood "hung offerings" in since before the Concord, where the Gravecallers come to speak with dead older than the harvest — and where the deep wood's restlessness is loudest.

## History

Publicly: a haunted grove the woodfolk avoid except to leave offerings; the Gravecallers are rumored to meet here. **Truly (DM):** a pre-Concord sacred grove — an outlying expression of the Old Mast's power, where the land's oldest dead linger and *answer.* The Gravecallers shelter here because the deep dead remember the harvest's beginning and will testify (an oblique M5/M6 fragment-source). As the keystone reawakens, the grove's dead grow restless and the cell grows bolder — and more reckless, edging toward the Old Mast itself.

## Entrance

A deer-path off the Coldhearth wood into a clearing of immense, gnarled oaks hung with old offering-cords, bone-charms, and rags. **Telegraphed:** the woodfolk warn against it; offering-marks thicken on the approach; the dread builds (Wis DC 13); birds fall silent. A guide (Brann) brings the player to the edge and balks at the grove itself.

## Solo Danger Rating

**Moderate (level 4–6).** Old, hard-to-lay dead; a Gravecaller cell that is dangerous if made hostile but is primarily a *parley/knowledge* encounter; and the dread of a place the wood guards. Telegraphed throughout; combat is avoidable and usually the wrong move.

## Zone / Room List

1. **The Offering-Edge** — the grove's threshold, hung with woodfolk offerings; the dread begins; the choice to enter respectfully or not.
2. **The Old Oaks** — the grove proper: vast trees, lingering old dead, the cell's hidden camp.
3. **The Cell's Hollow** — where the Gravecaller cell meets and speaks with the deep dead; the social/knowledge core (the cell, led by a Sister-Knell-style speaker, plus Wren-of-the-Wood, drawn here).
4. **The Hung Tree** — the grove's heart, a single ancient oak where the oldest dead "answers"; the M5/M6 oblique fragment (and a danger — the dead here have their own ancient agenda).

## Encounter List

- **The Old Oaks:** **old forest Remembrances** (Specter/ghost-like, older/stranger, AC 13, ~30 HP); lingering, mostly non-hostile to the respectful; resist easy laying (true rite Religion DC 15; some cannot be laid, only honored). Telegraphed.
- **The Cell's Hollow (social core):** the **Gravecaller cell** — a speaker (**Cult Fanatic/Warlock-like**, AC 12, ~45 HP, *Speak with Dead* at will), 2–4 recruits (**Cultist/Commoner-like**), and **Wren-of-the-Wood** (a Coldhearth guide, **Scout-like**, drawn in too deep). They prefer to *talk* — they want a witness, not a fight — and turn dangerous only if attacked or betrayed.
- **The Hung Tree (climax):** the **oldest answering dead** — flavor a powerful **ghost/fey-bound-undead** (AC 13, ~52 HP); not a slayer but an ancient, alien presence that *tests and testifies*; resolved by reverence, offering, or careful parley far better than by combat.

## Traps / Hazards (DCs)

- **Grove-dread:** Wis DC 13 on entry (DC 14 at the Hung Tree); failure = frightened until a rest or until the player makes a proper offering.
- **Taboo-breaking:** defiling the offerings or attacking the lingering dead turns the *whole grove* hostile (old dead rise; the cell defends itself) — a telegraphed, avoidable escalation.
- **Snaring roots / deadfall:** Athletics/Acrobatics DC 13 in the deep grove; 1d6 on a fall.
- **The answering dead's "price":** speaking with the Hung Tree's dead may demand a memory, an offering, or a true service (a roleplay cost, never a cheap kill).
- No mechanical lethal traps; the danger is dread, the old dead, and giving offense.

## Puzzle / Clue Checks

- **The grove's nature:** Religion/Nature DC 14 — this is *older than the Concord*; the offerings are a bargain the woodfolk keep (M5/M6 oblique shape).
- **The cell's teaching:** the Gravecallers explain what the deep dead have told them (no roll if the player is respectful) — "the grey lords took the dead before the roads fell" (**oblique M5/M6 fragment**; corroborates the harvest from a pre-Concord vantage; **never the harvest's mechanism, never the Court**).
- **The Hung Tree's testimony (gated, Act 2–3):** with reverence/parley (Insight/Persuasion DC 15, or the cell's mediation), the oldest dead testifies to "the taking" beginning long ago — the richest oblique M6 fragment short of the Old Mast. **Cap: a fragment and a dread, not the full truth.**

## Resting Constraints

No safe rest in the grove (dread, old dead) unless the player has made a proper offering and the cell vouches for them (then a tense, guarded rest is possible). Coldhearth is the recovery point.

## Treasure

- Old offering-charms and pre-Concord tokens (minor; selling them is a grave taboo).
- The Gravecaller cell's forbidden knowledge (the real "treasure" — the M5/M6 oblique fragment).
- Rare deep-wood herbs at the grove's edge (Survival DC 13).
- The Hung Tree's "gift" (DM's option: a one-time boon — a true-rite blessing, or an old-dead's guidance — earned by reverence, never looted).

## Boss / Climax Mechanics

The "boss" is the **encounter with the oldest answering dead and the choice it poses** — not a fight. It can be:
- **Honored/parleyed:** offering and reverence earn the M5/M6 fragment and safe passage; the best outcome.
- **Fought:** only if provoked — AC 13, ~52 HP, *frightful presence*, *withering touch* (3d6 necrotic, recharge); rouses the whole grove; a hard, avoidable fight that yields less.
- **Exploited (Gravecaller way):** let the cell push deeper for more — which advances the regional clock toward waking the Old Mast (a tempting, dangerous path).

Scaling: L4 → ~40 HP, fewer old dead; L6 → ~64 HP, the grove more awake, the cell more committed; the Old Mast remains gated regardless.

## Retreat Options

Open retreat back along the deer-path to the Coldhearth wood; the old dead and the grove do not pursue beyond the Offering-Edge. Making an offering on the way out atones for minor offense. The cell will let a respectful party leave.

## Scaling Notes

- **L4:** lean on dread, parley, and the cell; soften the old dead.
- **L5 (intended):** as written.
- **L6:** the grove is more awake; the cell edges toward the Old Mast; the fragment comes with a clearer warning.

## Consequences If Ignored

If ignored, the Gravecaller cell grows bolder and pushes toward the Old Mast (regional clock advances), the grove's restless dead spread toward the Coldhearth wood and the road, and the deep wood's oblique testimony (M5/M6) passes only to the reckless cult. The woodfolk's bargain frays further.
