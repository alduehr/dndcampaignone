## AI Use

Load when the player descends below Saint Veddow's shrine into the inner tomb — the Vale's keystone site and the **largest Concord installation outside the Reach.** This is the clearest Ring 1 place to learn *what the Concord built* (M2/M6 fragments). **Strictly act-gated:** it yields fragments and danger at level 4–6, never the apex truth. Never name the Hollow Court here. The seal-guarding resident Wardens (Sister Adwen) are the main obstacle and the main safety valve.

## One-Sentence Identity

A "saint's tomb" that descends into a dormant Concord shrine — a secondary node of the harvest, capped and sanctified for a century, now beginning, very faintly, to warm.

## History

Publicly: the holy resting-place of Saint Veddow, who "settled the dead with a touch"; the inner tomb is sacred and forbidden. **Truly (DM):** a major Concord shrine — the harvest network's largest southern node — was capped after the Quietfall and disguised as a saint's tomb to keep it sealed and revered. The "saint" legend grew over the cap. The resident Ashen Wardens were stationed here, like the Reach Wardens, to guard a seal they don't fully understand. As the keystone reawakens far to the north, this node is **faintly warming** — the source of Saint Veddow's failing rests.

## Entrance

A sealed bronze-bound door at the back of the pilgrim hall, beneath the flower-altar, kept by the resident Wardens. **Getting in is the real "lock":** persuade Sister Adwen (very hard — she guards on instinct), gain Mother Sennet's sacred permission, find the old service-stair the Remnant has been quietly excavating, or (worst) force the door as sacrilege. **Telegraphed:** the air from the door is wrong-cold; pilgrims won't stand near it; the Wardens visibly dread it.

## Solo Danger Rating

**Moderate-to-high (level 4–6).** Cold, dread, a warming Concord node, and a climactic warden-Remembrance. Telegraphed throughout; multiple retreat points; the deepest chamber is sealed (the node proper is endgame-tier and explicitly off-limits). Not a low-level dive — the approach itself warns the player off.

## Zone / Room List

1. **The Saint's Vestibule** — a beautiful upper tomb-chamber; the saint's flower-draped sarcophagus (empty — there is no Veddow). Atmospheric; first Script-traces (M2/M6 fragment).
2. **The Keeping Cells** — a ring of niche-chambers where, the Script says, "travelers' patterns were kept." Minor Remembrance relics; the clearest M3 demonstration in the Vale.
3. **The Warden's Watch** — a guard-cell where generations of resident Wardens kept vigil; old Warden records (a Warden truth-fragment, if Adwen or her records are consulted; M6 conditional).
4. **The Cold Stair** — a long descending stair into genuine Concord shrine-works; the cold and dread intensify (the warning to turn back).
5. **The Antechamber of the Node** — the deepest reachable chamber: a great Script-graven hall around a **sealed shaft** humming faintly with reawakening (M2 fragment — proof the network is continental and the Vale held a real node). The climax happens here.
6. **The Sealed Shaft (off-limits)** — the node proper, descending toward the harvest-works. **Capped; explicitly endgame-tier and lethal.** A low-level attempt to open it is telegraphed as suicidal.

## Encounter List

- **Vestibule/Keeping Cells:** none by default; touching a Keeping-Cell relic stirs a faint, sorrowful Remembrance of a "kept" traveler (Specter-like, AC 12, ~17 HP; non-hostile; M3 experiential).
- **Cold Stair:** environmental dread (Wis DC 13 fear-check on the unprepared); a single drifting Remembrance descending, layable.
- **Antechamber (climax):** the **Tomb-Warden** — the Remembrance of the original Concord keeper bound to the node, flavor a **wraith/ghost-like guardian** (AC 13, ~52 HP at L5; *chill touch, frightful presence, withering wail 1/short*). **It is not a mindless monster:** it challenges intruders and can be **reasoned or ritualed past** (see Boss Mechanics). Telegraphed by failing torches and a voice that is many voices.

## Traps / Hazards (DCs)

- **Sacrilege backlash (if the door is forced):** forcing the seal cracks it — a burst of leaked Remembrance (Con DC 14 or gain **thin-touch**, reversible) and the Tomb-Warden rouses hostile rather than wary.
- **Cold/dread:** descending the Cold Stair unprepared = Wis DC 13 or frightened (disadvantage) until a rest; cumulative.
- **Collapsing Script-floor (Antechamber):** DC 14 Perception to spot; DC 13 Dex save or fall into rubble (1d6).
- **Thin-touch (Keeping Cells / Antechamber):** Con DC 14 near raw relics; reversible.
- No lethal mechanical traps; the danger is the Warden and the dread.

## Puzzle / Clue Checks

- **The empty sarcophagus (Vestibule):** Investigation/Religion DC 13 — *there is no saint here; this is older and stranger* (M2 shape; the legend is a lid).
- **The Keeping-Cell Script (Cells):** Concord Script (Comprehend Languages or a reader — Briss/Oneth Vael/Tamsin Orr) — "here the patterns of the dead were kept" (M2/M3/M6 **fragment**; act-paced — give the *what*, not the *why* of the harvest below Act 3).
- **The Warden records (Watch):** Investigation DC 14, or earned from Sister Adwen — the Wardens were *placed here to keep something sealed* (M6 fragment; conditional, R6-gated to Act 3 — early access yields "we guard, we don't ask").
- **The Antechamber shaft:** Arcana/Religion DC 15 — the shaft is *active machinery, faintly waking* (M2 fragment proving the continental network; never the Court).

## Resting Constraints

No safe rest below the Vestibule (dread, cold, the Warden). Safe long rest in the pilgrim hall / Warden House above. The Warden House is the recovery point throughout — another reason to befriend, not force, the Wardens.

## Treasure

- **Keeping-Cell Remembrance relics** (2–4 minor; M3 — and a hard moral choice: leave them, give them to the Mourners to release, sell to the Ledger/Remnant, or study).
- A **Warden's seal-token** (in the Watch) — minor, but it marks the bearer to Ashen Wardens elsewhere as someone who has seen the truth (roleplay weight; possible attunement-light boon at the DM's option for rite-aid).
- Old Concord salvage and a Script-graven plate (the Remnant pays well; the Mourners want it destroyed).
- **No node-treasure** — the shaft is sealed and off-limits.

## Boss / Climax Mechanics

The **Tomb-Warden** is a guardian, not a slayer. It demands to know why the intruder disturbs the kept dead, and resolves three ways:
- **Combat:** AC 13, ~52 HP (L5); resistant to nonmagical damage; *frightful presence* (Wis DC 13), *withering wail* (Con DC 14, 3d8 necrotic, recharge). Tough but beatable; **breaks off / fades if the intruder retreats up the Cold Stair** (it cannot leave the node).
- **Ritual (best):** a true rite acknowledging the kept dead (Religion DC 15, or DC 13 with a Warden's aid) settles it — it gives a final fragment of testimony ("we kept them... we were told it was mercy") and fades. The richest M6 fragment available pre-Act-3 (carefully capped — no Court, no full harvest).
- **Parley:** Insight/Persuasion DC 15 to convince it the player means the dead no harm; it lets them pass and answers one question (DM gives a fragment, not the apex).

Scaling: L4 → ~40 HP, drop *withering wail* to 2d8; L6 → ~64 HP, add a summoned drifting Remembrance ally; L7+ → it bars the Sealed Shaft as a true mini-boss but the shaft stays gated regardless.

## Retreat Options

Open retreat up the Cold Stair at any point; the Tomb-Warden cannot pursue beyond the Antechamber. Sister Adwen will physically help a respectful party withdraw. Forcing deeper is telegraphed as lethal.

## Scaling Notes

- **L4:** soften the Warden; emphasize dread and the moral weight of the Keeping Cells.
- **L5 (intended):** as written.
- **L6+:** the node hums louder (more leakage, more drifting dead on the stair); the Remnant has gotten further in (a rival-presence complication).

## Consequences If Ignored

If the tomb is never entered, Saint Veddow's failing rests worsen and the secret stays capped — until the Remnant forces access (advancing the Reclamation clock at a second site) and begins working the node, or the Gravecallers arrive to break it. The Vale loses its clearest M2/M6 fragment-source to whoever gets in first.
