## AI Use

Load when the player explores the ruined Concord lighthouse on the headland above Cobble Strand — the Pale Coast's keystone site and a **coastal Concord harvest-node** (M2/M5/M6 oblique fragments). The clearest place on the Coast to learn the lighthouses were *not* lighthouses. **Act-gated:** fragments and danger at level 4–6, never the apex, never the Hollow Court. The tide is both the dungeon's clock and its safety valve.

## One-Sentence Identity

A "lighthouse" that was never a lighthouse — a Concord sea-shrine that kept the drowned, drowned at its own cliff-foot, now waking with the tide and calling the sea's dead home.

## History

Publicly: an old Concord lighthouse, broken in the Quietfall; the songs say its light "called the drowned home." **Truly (DM):** a maritime harvest-node — it settled and "kept" the sea's endless drowned for the Concord. Its lower levels drowned when the cliff-foot gave way. As the keystone reawakens, it warms and "lights" faintly in the fog, pulling the drowned tide-ward — the source of Cobble Strand's worsening dead. The songs remember its true function as superstition.

## Entrance

The Lamp Stair, a cliff-path up from Cobble Strand to the ruined tower on the headland; the upper tower is open ruin, the lower levels reached by a tide-cut stair that floods at high water. **Telegraphed:** the Strand reveres and fears it; the tide-table governs access; the lower stair is wet-cold and wrong; the Lamp "gleams" in fog.

## Solo Danger Rating

**Moderate-to-high (level 4–6).** Tide-timing is the core challenge and the safety valve — the lower node floods, so the player must read the tide. A warming node, drowned dead, and a climactic keeper-Remembrance. Telegraphed; the deepest drowned level is tide-locked and gated.

## Zone / Room List

1. **The Broken Tower** — the open upper ruin; the "lamp-room" (no lamp — a Script-graven shrine-focus); first fragments (M2).
2. **The Keeper's Round** — the lighthouse-keeper's quarters; old records (a keeper's truth-fragment; M6 oblique, conditional).
3. **The Tide-Cut Stair** — the descending stair into the drowned lower node; floods at high water (the tide-clock).
4. **The Settling Hall (flooded at high tide)** — the node's main chamber; Script-graven, drowned, faintly warming; "sea-relics" (M3); the drowned gather here.
5. **The Sea-Shaft (off-limits)** — the node proper, descending below the waterline toward the harvest-works; **capped, tide-locked, and lethal** — telegraphed as a drowning death for a low-level dive.

## Encounter List

- **Broken Tower / Keeper's Round:** none by default; touching a relic stirs a faint drowned Remembrance (Specter-like, AC 12, ~22 HP; sorrowful, non-hostile; M3 experiential).
- **Tide-Cut Stair / Settling Hall:** **drowned Remembrances** — the sea's dead, drawn here (Specter/"drowned-one"-like, AC 13, ~30 HP); rise with the tide; layable by a salt-and-tide rite (Religion DC 14) or parley. The climax keeper waits in the Settling Hall.
- **The Lamp-Keeper (climax):** the Remembrance of the original Concord keeper, bound to the node — flavor a **wraith/ghost-like guardian** (AC 13, ~52 HP at L5; *chill touch, drowning grip, frightful presence, keening wail 1/short*). **Not mindless:** it keeps its drowned and challenges intruders; reasoned, ritualed, or fought (see Boss Mechanics). Telegraphed by the faint cold light and a voice like the surf.

## Traps / Hazards (DCs)

- **The tide (core mechanic):** the lower levels flood at high water — Survival DC 14 to time the descent; misjudging it floods the Settling Hall (Athletics DC 13 to reach the stair; cold-water immersion Con DC 13; a stranded player must wait out the tide or risk drowning — telegraphed by the tide-table and the rising water).
- **Cliff & wet stone:** Athletics DC 13 on the Lamp Stair and the wet stair; falls serious.
- **Cold-damp / leakage:** Con DC 12; **thin-touch** near the node's raw relics (Con DC 14, reversible).
- **Collapsing ruin (Broken Tower):** Perception/Dex DC 13; 1d6 from falling stone.
- The tide is the danger and the clock; no lethal mechanical traps beyond it.

## Puzzle / Clue Checks

- **The lamp-room shrine-focus:** Investigation/Religion DC 13 — *there was never a lamp here; this is a shrine* (M2 shape; the light is the node, not a beacon).
- **The keeper's records (Keeper's Round):** Investigation DC 14, or earned — the keeper "kept the drowned for the Keepers" (M6 oblique **fragment**; conditional, act-paced — early access yields "we kept the sea's dead," not the harvest's full purpose).
- **The Settling Hall Script:** Concord Script (reader required) — the node "keeps the drowned and gives them onward" (M2/M3/M5 **fragment**: the maritime node; never the Court).
- **A sea-relic:** Arcana/Religion DC 13, or experiential — it stores a drowned person (M3).

## Resting Constraints

No safe rest below the Broken Tower (tide, cold, drowned). Safe long rest in Cobble Strand below or the upper tower at low tide between attempts. The tide forces the player out — a built-in safety valve.

## Treasure

- **Sea-relics** (2–4 minor Remembrance relics; M3 — and the recurring moral choice: leave, give to Bryd to release, sell to Cole/the Ledger, or study).
- A **keeper's tide-token** (in the Keeper's Round) — minor, marks the bearer to coastal Mourners/Wardens as one who has seen the truth (roleplay weight; possible rite-aid boon at DM's option).
- Old Concord salvage and a Script-graven plate (the Remnant and Cole both want it; Bryd wants it destroyed).
- **No node-treasure** — the Sea-Shaft is tide-locked and off-limits.

## Boss / Climax Mechanics

The **Lamp-Keeper** is a guardian, not a slayer. It demands why the intruder disturbs the kept drowned, and resolves three ways:
- **Combat:** AC 13, ~52 HP (L5); resistant to nonmagical damage; *frightful presence* (Wis DC 13), *keening wail* (Con DC 14, 3d8 necrotic/cold, recharge), *drowning grip* (grapple + suffocation flavor). Tough; **breaks off / fades if the intruder retreats up the stair ahead of the tide** (it is bound to the node and the water).
- **Ritual (best):** a salt-and-tide rite acknowledging the kept drowned (Religion DC 15, or DC 13 with Bryd's aid/teaching) settles it — it gives a final fragment ("we kept them... the sea always gives more") and fades. The richest M6 fragment available pre-Act-3 on the Coast (carefully capped — no Court, no full harvest).
- **Parley:** Insight/Persuasion DC 15 to convince it the player means the drowned no harm; it lets them pass and answers one question (a fragment, not the apex).

Scaling: L4 → ~40 HP, drop *keening wail* to 2d8; L6 → ~64 HP, more drowned rise with the tide; L7+ → it bars the Sea-Shaft as a mini-boss, but the shaft stays gated regardless.

## Retreat Options

Open retreat up the Lamp Stair; the Lamp-Keeper and the drowned cannot leave the node/water; **the tide itself drives the player out** if they linger. Bryd will aid a respectful withdrawal. Diving the Sea-Shaft is telegraphed as drowning.

## Scaling Notes

- **L4:** soften the Keeper; emphasize the tide and the drowned-tide's tragedy.
- **L5 (intended):** as written.
- **L6+:** the node hums and "lights" stronger; more drowned; the Remnant or Cole's salvagers already at work (a rival complication).

## Consequences If Ignored

If the Lamp is never explored, the drowned-tide worsens and the node warms unchecked — until the Remnant forces access (a second Reclamation site) or Cole strips its sea-relics for the Monopoly clock, or the Gravecallers reach it. Cobble Strand's clearest M2/M5/M6 fragment-source (and Bryd's vindication) is lost to whoever gets there first; the Coast clock advances toward the drowned overwhelming the rites.
