## AI Use

Load for the Coast's **human-villainy / tide-puzzle** site — the sea-caves under the Drowned Lamp headland, used by the wreckers and a Gravecaller current. A stealth/social/tide dungeon against living criminals (and a moral undead core): break the wreckers' hold, trace the sea-relic smuggling (M3), and reach (or rescue) the Gravecaller current and young Ferec. Tide-timing is the central hazard.

## One-Sentence Identity

A maze of tidal sea-caves where wreckers stash their plunder, smugglers move the drowned's stolen relics, and the grieving slip in to hear the sea's dead speak.

## History

Publicly: dangerous sea-caves the village avoids — wreckers, smugglers, the tide. **Truly (DM):** the wreckers lure or salvage ships and strip the drowned (breaking the Coast's salt-rites, to the Mourners' fury); they fence Concord "sea-relics" (Remembrance relics; M3) through Cole's chain and the Wrack. A Gravecaller current uses the deepest cave (where the drowned answer readily) to recruit the grieving, like young Ferec. The caves connect, at their deepest, toward the Drowned Lamp's leakage — why the drowned gather here.

## Entrance

A shingle cove and a low sea-cave mouth under the headland, accessible only at low-to-mid tide. **Telegraphed:** the village warns against it ("the wreckers, the tide, or the drowned'll have you"); the tide-table governs entry; the wreckers post a lookout.

## Solo Danger Rating

**Moderate (level 3–5), tide- and social-weighted.** The tide is the core hazard (caves flood); the wreckers prefer threats but will fight; the Gravecaller current is a parley, not a slaughter. Storming the caves at the wrong tide is the failure path.

## Zone / Room List

1. **The Cove Mouth** — the lookout and the tide-gate; the stealth/social entry (sneak, talk, or time the tide).
2. **The Plunder Cave** — the wreckers' stash: ship-goods, and crated "sea-relics" labeled for the Wrack (M3, damning).
3. **The Wreckers' Hall** — the gang's camp; the boss (Wreck-Master) and the salvage-ledger (M3 proof).
4. **The Tide-Maze** — flooding side-caves; the tide-puzzle and a drowned-haunting.
5. **The Deep Cave (the Listening Pool)** — where the Gravecaller current meets and the drowned answer; the moral/clue core (the current, young Ferec, the answering drowned).

## Encounter List

- **Cove Mouth / Plunder Cave:** a **lookout** and **2–4 wreckers** (**Bandit/Thug-like**, AC 12–13, ~16–32 HP); human; prefer to threaten/ransom; break on morale.
- **Wreckers' Hall:** the **Wreck-Master** (**Veteran/Spy-like**, AC 15, ~45 HP) and 2–3 wreckers; the boss-fight if forced; surrenders/flees on morale.
- **Tide-Maze:** a **drowned Remembrance** (Specter/"drowned-one"-like, AC 12, ~26 HP); sorrowful, layable by a salt-and-tide rite (Religion DC 13); the haunting that draws the Gravecallers.
- **Deep Cave (moral core):** the **Gravecaller current** — a speaker (**Acolyte/Cult Fanatic-like**, AC 11–12), 1–2 recruits, and **young Ferec** (a grieving fisher-lad). They want a witness, not a fight; parley-first.

## Traps / Hazards (DCs)

- **The tide (core mechanic):** the caves flood — Survival DC 14 to time entry/exit; a missed tide traps the player in a flooding cave (Athletics DC 13 to reach high ground; cold-water immersion Con DC 13; wait out the tide or risk drowning). Telegraphed by the tide-table and the wet walls.
- **Slick rock / deep pools:** Acrobatics/Athletics DC 13; falls into cold water.
- **Wreckers' alarm:** a tripline/lookout (Perception DC 13 / Stealth DC 13).
- **Thin-touch (sea-relics):** Con DC 14 near raw relics; reversible.
- No lethal mechanical traps beyond the tide.

## Puzzle / Clue Checks

- **The salvage-ledger (Wreckers' Hall):** Investigation DC 12 — "sea-relics" logged by wreck, bound for Cole/the Wrack (M3 documentary; the Monopoly clock's coastal arm; mirrors the Pellow dig-ledger).
- **The tide-maze:** Survival DC 14 to navigate the flooding side-caves to the Deep Cave (or wait for the right tide).
- **The Listening Pool:** the Gravecaller current explains what the drowned tell them (no roll if respectful) — "the drowned are called home" (M5/M6 oblique **fragment**; capped; never the Court).

## Resting Constraints

No safe rest in the caves (tide, wreckers, the haunting). Cobble Strand is a short, safe climb at low tide. Infiltrate-and-leave is the intended loop.

## Treasure

- The **salvage-ledger** (the real prize — leverage on Cole and the smuggling chain).
- Crated sea-relics (M3; sell to Cole = complicity, give to Bryd = release, keep = study — the choice).
- The wreckers' plunder (~120 gp in ship-goods and coin; some belongs to wreck-victims' kin — a moral hook).
- A drowned-one's keepsake (Mourners' goodwill if returned).

## Boss / Climax Mechanics

Two "bosses," neither a simple kill:
- **The Wreck-Master** (social/stealth/combat obstacle): break, expose, or co-opt the wrecking trade. Fights only if cornered (AC 15, ~45 HP); surrenders/flees on morale; can be turned (the wrecking pays poorly and the tide is turning against them).
- **The Listening Pool choice** (moral core): expose/stop the Gravecaller current, learn from it, or save young Ferec from it. The current is a parley; the drowned haunting is laid by rite, not blade.

Scaling: L3 → fewer wreckers; L5 → add a Ledger enforcer protecting the relic-trade, a wrathful drowned in the maze, and a harder tide-window.

## Retreat Options

Open at the right tide; the cove and cliff back to Cobble Strand. The tide itself limits pursuit. If caught by the wreckers, capture/ransom is the default, not death; if caught by the tide, wait it out on high ground.

## Scaling Notes

- **L3:** stealth/tide-weighted; the wreckers prefer threats.
- **L4 (intended):** as written.
- **L5:** a Ledger enforcer, a wrathful drowned, a tighter tide; the relic-haul and stakes rise.

## Consequences If Ignored

If ignored, the wreckers keep luring/stripping ships and feeding Cole's sea-relic trade (the Monopoly clock advances), the Gravecaller current recruits the grieving (young Ferec lost to it), and the cove stays closed to honest fishing. The Coast's cleanest M3 documentary clue and a Gravecaller-current contact pass to the criminals.
