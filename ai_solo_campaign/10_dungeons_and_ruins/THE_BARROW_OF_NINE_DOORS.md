## AI Use

Load when the player goes deep into the Greyfens after the dead's drift, the Gravecallers' truth, or a tougher fen delve. A pre-Concord barrow the harvest-network was built *over*, now a knot in the drift where the oldest Reach dead gather. **Moderate-high solo danger (level 3–5); a true dungeon with puzzle-doors, layable dead, and a meaningful M5/M6 payoff.** Old Sashe can guide the player here.

## One-Sentence Identity

An ancient nine-doored barrow in the heart of the Greyfens, older than the Concord, where the drifting dead pile up against a threshold that will not open — and the Gravecallers come to listen.

## History

Publicly: a haunted old barrow-mound the fen-folk shun. **Truly (DM):** a pre-Concord burial-barrow that the Concord's harvest-network was built around and *tapped*. Its "nine doors" are ritual thresholds. Now the basin's pull drags drifting Remembrances here and they jam against the doors, unable to pass — a vivid demonstration of M5 (the dead pulled the wrong way) and a place the Tallow Man's dead speak more freely (M6 path, gated).

## Entrance

A grass-grown mound rising from the fen, its single open door (the others sealed) breathing cold. **Telegraphed:** Sashe will guide a respectful player; the drift of dead converging on the mound is visible from far off; Greywater Holm folk warn it's "where the old dead go and can't get through."

## Solo Danger Rating

**Moderate-to-high (level 3–5).** More and tougher dead than other fen sites, real puzzles, and a possible Gravecaller presence. Mitigated by Sashe's guidance, true-rite resolutions, and clear retreat. **Not a level-1 site — telegraph it.**

## Zone / Room List

1. **The Open Door (antechamber)** — the one unsealed threshold; drifting Remembrances crowd here, jammed against the inner doors. Atmospheric and crowded; a true-rite mass scene possible.
2. **The Ring of Nine** — a circular chamber with nine carved door-thresholds (eight sealed, one to the deep). Concord Script *and* older pre-Concord glyphs (a layered **C-M5/M6 clue**). The puzzle hub.
3. **The Listening Hollow** — a side-chamber where the dead's voices carry; the Gravecallers come here to channel (forbidden-knowledge clue; the Tallow Man may be present).
4. **The True Threshold** — the ninth, deepest door: a genuine pre-Concord burial-cell, where the barrow-lord's Remembrance waits — the dungeon's climax and its clearest M5/M6 testimony.

## Encounter List

- **The Open Door:** **3–5 weak drifting Remembrances** (specter-like, AC 12, ~17 HP each) jammed at the threshold — **non-hostile, mournful**; a mass true-rite (Religion DC 14) lays them and *eases the local drift* (a visible win). Provoking them triggers a dangerous swarm (telegraphed).
- **The Ring of Nine:** the puzzle (below); a sealed door, if forced wrong, releases **1 angry Remembrance** (wraith-like, AC 13, ~36 HP) as a warning.
- **The Listening Hollow:** possible **Gravecaller presence** — the Tallow Man (T1, see MAJOR_NPCS) or 2–3 cult recruits; **social/forbidden-knowledge first**, not an ambush.
- **The True Threshold (climax):** the **barrow-lord Remembrance** — a powerful, ancient, *non-evil* dead (wraith/ghost-boss-like, AC 14, ~60 HP) who has watched the harvest for an age. **Primary resolution = speak, not slay.**

## Traps / Hazards (DCs)

- **Cold dread aura (whole barrow):** Wisdom save DC 13 on entry or be shaken (disadvantage on first fear save).
- **The sealed doors (Ring of Nine):** forcing a wrong door (Strength DC 16) releases an angry Remembrance and "wrongs" the room (advantage to hostile dead for 1 min). Solving the puzzle opens the True Threshold cleanly.
- **Drift-crush (Open Door):** pushing through the jammed dead without laying or calming them = Wisdom DC 14 or 1 level of fear-exhaustion (a custom, short-rest-recoverable strain).
- **Sinking approach:** the mound sits in soft fen (mire rules on the way in; Sashe negates this).

## Puzzle / Clue Checks

- **The Ring of Nine door-puzzle:** the nine thresholds must be "named in the old order" to open the True Threshold without violence. Solutions: read the layered glyphs (Concord Script + Religion/History DC 14); **or** a Mourner's knowledge of the old star-order (Grandmother Wend or Elder Mabb can teach it — a faction reward); **or** let the barrow-lord's voice guide you (if approached respectfully). **Three valid paths — three-clue rule honored.**
- **The layered glyphs (C-M5/M6):** Concord Script over older carving shows the Concord *built on and tapped* the old barrow — a fair M6 seed and a striking M5 confirmation (the drift converges here because the network does).

## Resting Constraints

No safe long rest inside (the dread aura, the drifting dead). Short rests possible in the Listening Hollow if no Gravecallers. Greywater Holm (a few hours with Sashe) is the recovery point.

## Treasure

- Pre-Concord grave-goods in the True Threshold (real value: ~120 gp in old gold and a *non-cursed* minor magic item, e.g. a cloak or amulet themed to the dead — see `REWARDS_BY_LEVEL.md` when built; tier-2 appropriate).
- A clean Remembrance relic and a Script rubbing (M3 + M6 clues, portable).
- The barrow-lord's **testimony** (the real prize: an ancient dead's account of watching the network feed — a strong, gated M5/M6 clue).

## Boss / Climax Mechanics

**The barrow-lord Remembrance.** **Weaknesses / non-combat win:** it *wants* to be understood and released; opening the True Threshold "in the old order" (puzzle) lets the player parley. It will give its testimony (M5/M6) and, if offered true release (a Mourner mass-rite or the player's sincere word, Persuasion/Religion DC 15), pass peacefully — ending the encounter and easing the whole Greyfens drift. **In combat (if provoked):** AC 14, ~60 HP, life-drain + a fear-aura (Wisdom DC 14 or frightened), incorporeal, summons 1d4 jammed dead as reinforcements — genuinely dangerous to a solo PC. **But fully telegraphed, escapable (it cannot leave the barrow), and avoidable** via the parley path. Morale: it does not pursue; it grieves.

## Retreat Options

The Open Door is always open; the player can withdraw to the antechamber and out at any time. The barrow-lord is bound within. Sashe waits outside to lead the player home.

## Scaling Notes

- **Level 3:** barrow-lord ~45 HP, no summons; fewer jammed dead.
- **Level 5–6:** barrow-lord ~75 HP, summons each round if fought, dread aura Reach-wide-feeling; add an active Gravecaller standoff at the Listening Hollow (three-way scene).

## Consequences If Ignored

The drift jams ever thicker at the barrow; the Gravecallers deepen their use of the Listening Hollow toward "the Breaking"; and the barrow-lord, un-parleyed, may eventually be *forced* through by the basin's pull — a grim sign the harvest is winning. A potent, gated M5/M6 clue and a memorable boss go unmet.
