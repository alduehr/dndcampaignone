## AI Use

Load when the player descends the Harrowgast mines toward the deep Concord delving the miners fear. A secondary Concord harvest node — not the keystone, but the same machine in miniature, and the clearest place to *understand* the harvest before the apex (M6/M7 foreshadow). **Mid-tier solo danger (level 3–5); the Remnant may be racing the player here.** Telegraph hard.

## One-Sentence Identity

A deep Concord harvest-adit beneath the Sunder Heights mines, leaking the dead into the rock, that the Concord Remnant is racing to claim.

## History

Publicly: the deepest, oldest mine-tunnel under Harrowgast, abandoned for being "bad rock." **Truly (DM):** a Concord harvest node — a smaller cousin of the Hollowmere shrine — that drew Remembrance from Heights burials into the network. The Quietfall severed it; it sat dormant; now it leaks, saturating the rock so it "remembers" and turning deep-digging miners "thin." The **Concord Remnant** (Veska Dunn's crew) has identified it and is digging to reach its core for relics and proof.

## Entrance

The barred **Deep Mouth** below Harrowgast, past the fear-line the miners won't cross. Reached through the worked upper adits. **Telegraphed:** "thin" Coll, dig-boss Hennet's terror, the coin left at the Mouth, the cold air flowing out. The Remnant crew may already hold the entry (a social or stealth obstacle before the dungeon proper).

## Solo Danger Rating

**Moderate (level 3–5).** Tougher dead than the lowlands, real environmental danger (cave-ins, the thinning aura), and a possible faction confrontation. Strong telegraphing and retreat routes keep it solo-fair.

## Zone / Room List

1. **The Fear-Line** — where the miners stop. Cold, "talking" rock (Whisper hazard). The Remnant's forward camp may be here (social/stealth gate; Veska Dunn).
2. **The Worked Drift** — the last dug tunnel; tool-marks, abandoned gear, a cave-in hazard. A "thin" miner's body or a lost crewman (grief-clue).
3. **The Glass Seam** — where "old glass" (raw Remembrance relics) is embedded in the rock. **C-M3 (direct):** the relics demonstrably *store the dead*. Harvesting them is valuable and risky (thinning aura).
4. **The Relay-Vault** — the node's chamber: a Concord relay-structure, leaking, dense with Concord Script (**C-M6/M9 fragments**, language-gated; the harvest's purpose stated plainly enough to chill). The thinning aura is strongest here.
5. **The Drowned Sump** (optional deep) — a flooded shaft going further down toward the node's root; **explicitly telegraphed as beyond this tier** (the way to deeper truth, saved for later levels) — a gate, not a trap.

## Encounter List

- **The Fear-Line:** Remnant forward camp — **Veska Dunn** (secondary; **Scout/Mage-lite**, AC 13, ~30 HP) + **2 hired bravos** (**Thug/Scout-like**, AC 13–14, ~16–30 HP). **Social/stealth first** — they'd rather hire or warn off the player than fight; combat is a choice.
- **The Worked Drift:** cave-in hazard; possibly **1 highland Remembrance** (cold miner-ghost, **specter/wraith-like**, AC 13, ~30 HP), telegraphed.
- **The Glass Seam:** the thinning aura (hazard, below); a Remembrance may manifest if relics are pried loose (telegraphed).
- **The Relay-Vault (climax):** **1–2 wrathful Heights Remembrances** bound to the node (**wraith-like**, AC 13, ~36 HP) — the node's "keepers." **Telegraphed** (the dread, the cold light). Non-combat options: a true rite (Religion DC 15) to lay them; Concord Script to "speak the closing word" (DC 15) and quiet the node.

## Traps / Hazards (DCs)

- **Whisper / talking rock:** Wisdom save DC 13 on entry and at the Relay-Vault or be unnerved (disadvantage on next save vs. fear).
- **Thinning aura (Glass Seam, Relay-Vault):** prolonged exposure or prying relics = Con save DC 14 or gain a point of "thin-touch" (a custom, reversible condition: disadvantage on Death saves until a true rite or long rest at a shrine restores you). **Strongly telegraphed** ("thin" Coll is the living warning). A hard cap of meaningful danger without cheap death.
- **Cave-in (Worked Drift):** DC 14 Perception to spot stress-cracks; DC 13 Dex save or 3d6 bludgeoning and a blocked passage (retreat via an alternate drift; never fully sealed).
- **Cold / dark:** standard cave hazards; light required.

## Puzzle / Clue Checks

- **Concord Script (Relay-Vault):** language-gated; Investigation DC 14 yields an **M6/M9 fragment** — the harvest's purpose stated in the old order's own words. In Act 1–2, give a chilling *fragment*, not the apex truth.
- **The Glass Seam (Arcana/Religion DC 15):** confirms "old glass" stores a dead person's pattern (**C-M3 direct**).
- **The "closing word" (Concord Script DC 15):** an alternate, non-combat way to quiet the node's keepers and briefly stop its leak.

## Resting Constraints

No safe rest in the Glass Seam or Relay-Vault (thinning aura). Short rests possible in the Worked Drift if no Remnant pursuit. Long rest only back at Harrowgast (and no magical healing there — supply matters).

## Treasure

- "Old glass" Remembrance relics (M3; the Ledger and Remnant pay heavily — a moral/economic choice, and a thin-touch risk to harvest).
- Concord tools and metal (salvage value).
- The **Relay-Vault Script rubbing** (a portable M6/M9 clue the player can carry to a reader — a key, non-combat prize).

## Boss / Climax Mechanics

The Relay-Vault keepers (1–2 Heights Remembrances). **Weaknesses/alternatives:** vulnerable to a true rite (auto-lay on Religion DC 15) and to the "closing word" (Concord Script DC 15, ends the encounter non-violently and quiets the node). In combat: AC 13, ~36 HP, life-drain touch (~2d6 necrotic, recharge), incorporeal (move through rock), but **bound to the vault** — the player can always retreat out of range. Morale: they cannot pursue beyond the node; reducing or out-rituals them releases them gratefully. **No cheap death:** telegraphed, escapable, with two non-combat outs.

## Retreat Options

Open tunnels back to the Worked Drift and the upper adits throughout (except a cave-in, which has an alternate route). The Remnant camp is the only thing that might block the *entrance* — and that's a social/stealth problem, not a lock.

## Scaling Notes

- **Level 2–3:** drop the Relay-Vault to 1 keeper (~22 HP); soften thinning DC to 12; remove the bravos (Veska alone).
- **Level 5–6:** add a second keeper, make the thinning aura pulse Reach-wide-feeling, and have the Remnant crew actively contest the Relay-Vault (a three-way scene: player vs. keepers vs. Remnant).

## Consequences If Ignored

The Remnant reaches the Relay-Vault, harvests the Glass Seam, and carries proof and relics to Quorrin (advancing the Reclamation clock toward Act 3). The thinning spreads from Harrowgast. The node leaks unchecked, and the Heights dead grow and descend.

## State Update Triggers

- If the node is quieted ("closing word" or mass-laying): log a slowed Reclamation clock and eased Heights leakage (`WORLD_CLOCKS.md`, `FACTION_STATE.md`).
- If the Remnant is beaten to it / fought: update `FACTION_STATE.md` (Remnant attitude → hostile/wary) and `RELATIONSHIPS.md` (Veska Dunn).
- Mark C-M3 / C-M6 / C-M9 fragments in `KNOWN_CLUES.md`; note any thin-touch in `PLAYER_CHARACTER.md`.
