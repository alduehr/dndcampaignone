## AI Use

Load only when the player presses into the deepest Tollwood — the ancient stand the Concord's roads bend around, where a **pre-Concord landmark-power** sleeps. This is a **gated, higher-tier site (level 6–10)** and the Tollwood's apex. It is **not** the Hollow Court, **not** the harvest keystone, and **not** a new god or cosmology — it is a regional primal/fey-flavored presence that points *obliquely* at M5/M6 and resents the harvest's reawakening. **Cap reveals: the deep presence gives dread, a fragment, and a warning — never the campaign's apex truth. Telegraph lethal danger to discourage premature dives without railroading.**

## One-Sentence Identity

The oldest, deepest heart of the Tollwood, where something older than the Concord sleeps — a power the Concord could not master and chose to build *around*, now stirring in resentment as the harvest wakes far away.

## History

Publicly: the deep wood the woodfolk forbid and the Concord roads avoid; a place of dread and old stories. **Truly (DM):** the seat of a **pre-Concord power** bound to the land and its oldest dead — neutral, vast, and slow, present before the harvest existed. The Concord, building its continental machine, **could not subsume the Old Mast** and routed its roads around it (the deliberate bends; a parallel to the Reach's Barrow of Nine Doors, where the network was built *over* an older thing it could not fully tap). The Old Mast has slept through the Concord age and the Quietfall. Now, as the keystone reawakens, **it stirs and resents the renewed taking** — its restlessness rippling out as the Tollwood's danger. It is a *witness*, not a player in the harvest: the deepest, most alien corroboration that the dead were taken long ago, available to a player who survives reaching it.

## Entrance

There is no door — the player walks in, past the Old Mast edge (the gated boundary in `TOLLWOOD_SITES.md`), into trees that grow older, darker, and more *aware* with each mile. **Telegraphed hard:** the woodfolk and even the Gravecallers will not come here; the offering-marks end; the dread mounts (Wis DC 14, then 16, cumulative); the wood "watches" and turns the unworthy back. A guide brings the player to the edge and no further. **A low-level intrusion is explicitly suicidal and signposted as such.**

## Solo Danger Rating

**High and gated (level 6–10).** The danger is the wood itself awake — disorientation, the watchers, the presence's regard, and the real risk of being *lost* rather than killed. The presence is never a low-level fight; it is an encounter to *survive and learn from*, not defeat. Multiple, heavily telegraphed turn-back points.

## Zone / Room List

1. **The Failing Path** — past the edge; the last offering-marks; the dread becomes constant; the wood actively misleads (Survival DC 16 to keep direction).
2. **The Watcher-Stands** — stands of awakened/ancient trees and the presence's watchers, who *test and turn back* intruders (warning, not slaughter).
3. **The Still Heart** — the deepest grove, unnaturally silent and timeless, where the presence is *aware* of the intruder; the encounter and the oblique fragment.
4. **The Root-Dark (off-limits)** — a sense of vast depth below the Still Heart where the presence truly sleeps; **explicitly unreachable / not built** — the wood does not allow it, and a player who insists is lost (a telegraphed, non-cheap removal: stranded, marked, and turned out, not killed outright at low level).

## Encounter List

- **The Failing Path:** environmental — getting lost (the chief danger), dread, the wood misleading.
- **The Watcher-Stands:** the presence's **watchers** — flavor **awakened tree / dryad / ancient fey-bound dead** (AC 14, ~60 HP; *entangle, frightful presence, slam*); **they warn, test, and turn back, fighting only the violent or the foolish.** Telegraphed; retreat ends the encounter.
- **The Still Heart (climax):** the **presence's regard** — not a stat-block to kill but an ancient awareness that *tests the intruder's purpose* and may grant or deny passage and testimony. Resolved by reverence, woodcraft, and parley; force fails and gets the player lost.

## Traps / Hazards (DCs)

- **The misleading wood:** Survival DC 16 (DC 18 at the Still Heart) each leg; failure strands the player deeper (cumulative; a guide cannot help past the edge). The signature danger.
- **Cumulative dread:** Wis DC 14 → 16; failure = frightened and, on repeated failure, compelled to flee (a built-in safety valve that *pushes the unworthy out alive*).
- **The watchers' tests:** non-lethal by default — entangle, fear, displacement — meant to turn intruders back.
- **The presence's price:** earning the fragment may cost a memory, a true offering, or a binding promise (roleplay cost; never a cheap death).
- **The Root-Dark:** insisting on going deeper than allowed = lost in the deep wood (DM: a telegraphed removal — stranded and marked, recoverable via Coldhearth/woodcraft, not instant death at low level).

## Puzzle / Clue Checks

- **The presence's nature:** Nature/Religion/History DC 16 — this is *older than the Concord*, and the roads bend because the Concord *could not take it* (the deep-wood truth; parallels the Barrow).
- **The oblique fragment (gated, Act 2–3+):** if the presence grants testimony (reverence + Insight/Persuasion DC 16, or earned via the Gravecallers/Sennet), it confirms, in ancient and alien terms, that **"the dead were taken, long before the roads fell, and are taken again"** — the deepest M5/M6 corroboration in Ring 1, **from outside the Concord entirely.** **Strict cap: a fragment and a warning — it does NOT name the harvest's mechanism, the Custodians, or the Hollow Court, which it does not know and would not call by those names.**

## Resting Constraints

**No safe rest anywhere in the Old Mast.** The deepest haven is Coldhearth, days back. This alone makes the site self-limiting for a low-level party.

## Treasure

- Nothing safely *takeable* — the Old Mast does not give loot, and taking from it is the gravest taboo.
- The only "reward" is the oblique fragment and, possibly, the presence's *favor*: a one-time boon at the DM's discretion (a true-rite blessing, safe passage through the wood thereafter, or an old-dead's guidance) — earned, never seized.

## Boss / Climax Mechanics

There is **no boss to defeat.** The climax is **surviving the presence's regard and earning (or being denied) its testimony.**
- **Reverent/earned:** the player receives the oblique M5/M6 fragment and the presence's favor; the wood quiets toward them.
- **Forced/violent:** the watchers turn the player back (non-lethally at first; lethally only if the player escalates against a vastly superior power — a clearly telegraphed bad choice), and the player leaves marked and empty-handed.
- **Reckless (Gravecaller way):** pushing to wake the presence advances the regional clock catastrophically (the Old Mast fully roused — a Tollwood-scale disaster), a consequence the cult courts and the player can prevent.

Scaling: this site stays gated regardless of level; at higher tiers the watchers are tougher and the fragment richer, but the presence is never a kill-target and the Root-Dark is never opened.

## Retreat Options

The dread and the misleading wood are designed to *push the player out*; following the dread back, or accepting the watchers' turn-back, leads out alive. A respectful exit (an offering) atones. The Old Mast's whole design is a survivable warning, not a death-trap — but pressing past every warning has real, non-cheap consequences (lost, marked, the presence roused).

## Scaling Notes

- **L6:** the watchers turn the player back early; deliver dread and a thin fragment; emphasize "you should not be here yet."
- **L8–10:** the player can reach the Still Heart and earn the full oblique fragment and the presence's favor.
- **Always gated:** never the Court, never the harvest mechanism, never the Root-Dark. The Old Mast points at the truth from outside; it is not a shortcut to the apex.

## Consequences If Ignored

If left alone, the Old Mast sleeps — unless the Ledger's deep-cutting or the Gravecallers' recklessness wakes it (the regional clock). A fully roused Old Mast is a Tollwood-scale disaster: the deep wood spills its old dead and its dread toward the road and the settlements, sealing the region and threatening the East Road to Caradril. The player's choices in Hartfell, Coldhearth, and the Hanging Oaks decide whether it stays asleep.
