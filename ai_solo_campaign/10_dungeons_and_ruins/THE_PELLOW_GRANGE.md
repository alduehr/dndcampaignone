## AI Use

Load for the Vale's **human-villainy / social-infiltration** site — a Ledger-foreclosed farm-estate over a field-shrine, now a quiet relic-quarry Pell's men work after dark. This is the Vale's "dungeon" that is really a stealth/social challenge against living people (not undead): expose or stop the Ledger's relic-quarrying (M3), with a haunting as the moral core. Built so combat is the *worst* option.

## One-Sentence Identity

A foreclosed farmstead where the Cinder Ledger digs the dead out of a field-shrine by lamplight — and the player must decide whether to expose it, stop it, rob it, or join it.

## History

Publicly: the Pellow family lost their grange to Ledger debt; now it stands empty but for a caretaker crew. **Truly (DM):** Factor Wymar Pell foreclosed it *because* it sits over an old Concord field-shrine, and the Ledger is quietly quarrying its Remembrance relics (M3) to ship south — a direct, commercial demonstration of the relic-greed driving the Monopoly clock. The disturbed shrine has begun to leak; the grange is mildly haunted, which the crew hides because panic would draw attention.

## Entrance

A walled farmstead a few miles from Tilbrook, gates barred, a Ledger pennant at the door. By day: a sleepy caretaker and a foreman. By night: lamplight in the field behind, and the clink of digging. **Telegraphed:** Tilbrook folk know "Pell's men work the Pellow place after dark, and nobody's let near." The player can scout, talk their way in, sneak, or (badly) storm it.

## Solo Danger Rating

**Low-to-moderate (level 2–4), social/stealth-weighted.** The danger is being caught and made a Ledger enemy, not death. The dig-crew are toughs who prefer threats to violence; the haunting is gentle. Storming the grange is the *failure* path, not the intended one.

## Zone / Room List

1. **The Farmyard** — barred gate, caretaker's lodge, barn; the social/stealth gate (talk past the caretaker, climb the wall, or wait for the night-shift).
2. **The Foreman's Office (old farmhouse)** — Drust the foreman's room; the **dig-ledger** (the proof: relics logged by grave; M3) and a strongbox.
3. **The Cellar** — relic-store: crates of "saint's relics" labeled for shipment south (M3, damning).
4. **The Back Field (the shrine-dig)** — the open quarry over the field-shrine; the crew digging by lamplight; the leak and the haunting.
5. **The Shrine-Pit** — the exposed Concord field-shrine itself at the dig's bottom; minor relics, Script-traces (M2/M3 fragment), and the bound dead the digging disturbed.

## Encounter List

- **Farmyard:** the **caretaker** (Commoner-like) and a **dog**; by night, **2–3 dig-crew toughs** (**Thug-like**, AC 11, ~32 HP) and **Foreman Drust** (**Spy/Thug-like**, AC 13, ~40 HP). All human; all prefer to intimidate, ransom, or run rather than kill.
- **Back Field / Shrine-Pit (moral core):** a **sorrowful Remembrance** the digging woke (Specter-like, AC 12, ~22 HP), non-hostile, grieving over the disturbed grave; layable by a true rite (Religion DC 13). Telegraphed; never an ambush.

## Traps / Hazards (DCs)

- **The wall / the dog:** Stealth DC 13 to cross unseen; Animal Handling DC 12 to quiet the dog.
- **Dig-pit collapse (Back Field):** loose spoil; DC 13 Dex save or slide into the pit (1d6, climb Athletics DC 11).
- **Foul cellar air / cave-in (Shrine-Pit):** Con DC 12; DC 13 Perception to spot an unsafe shore.
- **Thin-touch (Shrine-Pit):** Con DC 14 near raw relics; reversible.
- No lethal traps; the threat is human and reputational.

## Puzzle / Clue Checks

- **The dig-ledger (Foreman's Office):** Investigation DC 12 to find, read, or steal — relics logged *by which grave they came from* (M3: incontrovertible proof the "saint's relics" are the dead, and that the Ledger knows it). The Vale's cleanest M3 documentary clue.
- **The shipping-labels (Cellar):** the relics are bound for Pell → the Ledger south (ties the Vale relic-quarrying to the Monopoly clock; a thread toward Caradril).
- **The Shrine-Pit Script:** Concord Script (reader) — a field-shrine of the network (M2/M3 fragment; act-paced).

## Resting Constraints

No rest on-site (active crew / haunting). Tilbrook is a short, safe walk for a long rest. The intended loop is infiltrate-and-leave, not hold-and-rest.

## Treasure

- The **dig-ledger** (the real prize — leverage, not loot).
- Crated Remembrance relics (M3; sell to the Ledger = complicity, return to the Mourners = release, keep = study — the choice that defines the player's stance).
- Drust's strongbox (~80 gp in wages-coin and a Ledger writ).
- Minor field-shrine salvage and a relic in the Shrine-Pit.

## Boss / Climax Mechanics

No monster boss. The "boss" is **Foreman Drust** as a *social/stealth* obstacle and the **choice** about the relics and the evidence:
- **Expose:** carry the dig-ledger to Miller Brunt, Mother Combe, the Mourners, or the Compact — checks the Ledger's Vale relic-supply and earns powerful allies (and a Ledger enemy).
- **Stop:** lay the disturbed dead, fill the pit, run the crew off (Intimidation/Persuasion DC 14, or a clean fight the crew loses on morale) — a quieter win.
- **Rob:** take the relics for yourself — easy coin, but you become what Pell is (Mourners turn cold; M3 the cynical way).
- **Join:** sell the silence to Pell — coin and a Ledger favor, at a moral cost the campaign remembers.

Drust fights only if cornered (AC 13, ~40 HP), and surrenders or flees at half HP — he's a wage-man, not a fanatic.

## Retreat Options

Open walls, fields all around; easy withdrawal to Tilbrook. The crew won't pursue far from the grange. If caught, capture/ransom is the default, not death.

## Scaling Notes

- **L2:** fewer toughs (2); emphasize stealth and the ledger.
- **L3 (intended):** as written.
- **L4:** add a Ledger enforcer (Tallytooth-Ren-class, or a Veteran-like guard) and a wrathful element to the disturbed dead; raise the relic-haul's value and the stakes of the choice.

## Consequences If Ignored

If ignored, the Ledger finishes quarrying the Pellow field-shrine and ships its relics south (a clean advance on the Monopoly clock), the Pellow dead are left disturbed and leaking (worsening the Vale clock), and Pell foreclosing a further shrine-grange goes unopposed. Tilbrook's suspicion curdles into helpless resentment — fertile ground for the Vale's first Gravecaller cell.
