## AI Use

Load for the Tollwood's accessible Concord-ruin delve — the drowned vault below the ruined toll-station at Tollstone Cross. A minor Concord road-node, leaking faintly (M2/M5 fragment; a Tollwood cousin of the Reach's Sunken Tollhouse). Good level 2–4 dungeon: water-hazard exploration, a layable drowned dead, and the cause of the local dead-drift the Tollmen fear.

## One-Sentence Identity

A flooded Concord toll-vault under the East Road's broken arch, where the road's old machinery still faintly hums and the drowned dead drift up the stair the Tollmen won't descend.

## History

Publicly: an old flooded cellar under the ruined toll-station; the Tollmen avoid it. **Truly (DM):** a minor Concord road-node — part of the continental network's road-conduits — drowned when the causeway broke. It leaks faintly as the keystone reawakens (M2/M5 fragment), drawing the dead to this stretch (the Green Mile drift) and frightening the Tollmen. A direct, low-level demonstration that the road *is* the machine, mirroring the Reach.

## Entrance

A choked stair down from the Broken Arch at Tollstone Cross, behind the Tollmen's roost; or a flooded culvert from the Green Mile. **Telegraphed:** the Tollmen won't go down ("the cold stair"); the dead drift near the arch at dusk; the air off the stair is wrong-cold.

## Solo Danger Rating

**Low-to-moderate (level 2–4).** Water hazards, faint leakage, and a single layable drowned Remembrance. No lethal gauntlet; built as a comfortable Tollwood node-delve. The thin-touch risk near the leaking node is the main wrinkle.

## Zone / Room List

1. **The Choked Stair** — the descent; rubble and rising damp; the Tollmen's dread made literal.
2. **The Toll-Hall (flooded)** — the main vault, knee-to-waist deep; the old toll-machinery and a drowned cart's salvage; the drowned dead drift here.
3. **The Conduit Room** — the node proper: a Script-graven channel humming faintly (M2/M5 fragment — the road is a conduit). Thin-touch risk.
4. **The Sunk Strongroom** — an optional flooded side-vault with the toll-station's old strongbox (treasure) and a second, broken relic.

## Encounter List

- **Choked Stair / Toll-Hall:** environmental (water, cold); a **drowned Remembrance** — a toll-keeper or traveler lost when the road broke (Specter-like, AC 12, ~22 HP); sorrowful, non-hostile, layable by a true rite (Religion DC 13) or parley (Insight DC 12).
- **Conduit Room:** faint leakage (thin-touch risk); no combat by default.
- **Sunk Strongroom:** a flooded-cellar scavenger possible (giant frog/constrictor/rat-swarm-like, CR 1/4–1), telegraphed.

## Traps / Hazards (DCs)

- **Flooded footing / deep water:** Athletics DC 13 in the deep stretches; failure = swept toward a sump (Athletics DC 13 or an ally/rope to recover). Telegraphed by the water's pull.
- **Cold-damp:** lingering = Con DC 12 or minor fatigue.
- **Collapsing stair/vault:** DC 13 Perception to spot; DC 13 Dex save or 1d6 from falling stone.
- **Thin-touch (Conduit Room):** Con DC 14 near the leaking node; reversible (disadvantage on Death saves until a true rite / shrine rest).
- No lethal traps.

## Puzzle / Clue Checks

- **The Conduit Script:** Concord Script (reader required) — the toll-road is a *conduit* that "carries the settled dead onward" (M2/M5 **fragment**: the road is the machine; mirrors the Reach's Sunken Tollhouse — never the harvest's full purpose, never the Court).
- **The drowned dead's testimony:** speaking with the toll-keeper Remembrance (no roll to start) — the dead drift up the stair because something "calls them west" (M5 fragment, oblique).
- **The salvage relic (Sunk Strongroom):** Arcana/Religion DC 13, or experiential — it stores a dead person (M3).

## Resting Constraints

Short rest possible in the dry upper stair; no safe long rest below (damp, cold, the Tollmen above). Tollstone Cross (uneasy) or, better, Hartfell/Kettle Bridge for a long rest.

## Treasure

- The toll-station strongbox (~70 gp in old toll-coin and a Concord road-token).
- A minor Remembrance relic (M3) and a broken second relic (study).
- Concord road-salvage (~30 gp; the Ledger and the Remnant both want it).
- The Tollmen will reward (or ransom) the player for clearing their feared stair.

## Boss / Climax Mechanics

No combat boss. The **drowned toll-keeper Remembrance** is the emotional climax — a choice to lay him (Religion DC 13), hear him (M5/M2 fragment), or ignore him. If attacked, he defends weakly (AC 12, ~22 HP, chill touch ~1d6) and is far better settled. Clearing the drift here briefly eases the local dead and may flip the Tollmen's gratitude (a lever in the toll-war). Scaling: L2 → ~16 HP; L4 → ~30 HP + add a wrathful element if the node is disturbed roughly.

## Retreat Options

Open stair back to the surface; the Green Mile culvert as an alternate exit. No pursuit beyond the vault. The leakage is faint and survivable.

## Scaling Notes

- **L2:** a soft water-and-haunting node-delve.
- **L3 (intended):** as written.
- **L4:** add a scavenger fight, a wrathful drowned dead, and a deeper thin-touch risk; raise the relic stakes.

## Consequences If Ignored

If ignored, the node keeps leaking, the dead keep drifting to this stretch (the Green Mile worsens, the East Road grows eerier), and the Tollmen's fear festers — which Skell can exploit to seize control. A clean M2/M5 fragment-site (and a lever in the toll-war) goes unused.
