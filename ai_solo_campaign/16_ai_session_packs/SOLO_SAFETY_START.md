## AI Use

The solo-play safety net for a single level-1 PC. Load at campaign start. These structures keep a lone character alive and supported without solving the campaign for them. Use whichever fit the player's choices; do not force all of them.

## Why This Exists

One PC at level 1 is fragile. The campaign uses **layered, fictionally-grounded safety valves** — not plot armor — so that danger is real but death is fair and telegraphed. Per `AI_DM_CORE_RULES.md`: telegraph danger, allow retreat/preparation/allies, make non-combat options valuable.

## The Four Starting Safety Layers

### 1. A Home Base — The Drowned Lantern
Halla Coalmont's inn is a guaranteed safe haven: rest, food, information, and a motherly protector who notices when the player is hurt or in trouble. The player always has somewhere to retreat to in Hollowmere. (Major NPC #19.)

### 2. A Low-Power Companion — Warden Brother Hale
Hale can accompany the player on early, death-rite-related outings as a **support companion** (Priest-like: healing, *Bless*, light protection). He is deliberately weak enough not to overshadow the PC and reluctant to enter true danger, but he keeps a level-1 character from dying to a single bad fight. He is optional and earned by being decent to the Wardens. (Major NPC #2; companion mechanics in `DND_MECHANICS_REQUIREMENTS.md`.)

### 3. A Wilderness Guardian — Old Sashe
Any trip into the Greyfens or toward the basin can be made survivable by hiring/earning Old Sashe as a guide. He provides safe routes, hazard warnings, and the ability to *retreat* from the fens. The fens are lethal without a guide and navigable with one — a clear, fair gate. (Major NPC #20.)

### 4. A Patron Safety Net — Sefra Quick / the Compact / the Ledger
The player begins with easy access to paid, low-danger work (Sefra's jobs, the Compact's errands, the Ledger's odd jobs), guaranteeing income, direction, and a reason for NPCs to help. This prevents the "broke, aimless, alone" failure spiral common to solo starts. (Note: Sefra's patron is a DM-only thread — see M8 — but the *practical* effect early is a steady job supply.)

## Combat-Safety Rules at Levels 1–4 (DM)

- **No cheap death.** Every dangerous encounter gets warning signs, telegraphed before commitment.
- **The opening (Wren) is not a forced fight.** It resolves through ritual/social/investigation by default.
- **Single-revenant rule:** Early "restless dead" come one at a time and can usually be *laid to rest* (a non-combat true rite) rather than fought. If fought, use a single, telegraphed, fleeing-or-fading creature, not a swarm.
- **Retreat is always available.** The fens, ruins, and town all have escape routes; enemies have goals and morale, not death-wishes.
- **Healing access:** Hale, the Mourners, and a Hollowmere hedge-healer provide affordable healing so a solo PC isn't one bad roll from death.
- **Telegraph the shrine:** If the player tries to dive the drowned shrine early, make its lethal danger obvious (cold, dread, Concord wards, Sashe's refusal). Do not block with a wall; warn, then let consequences be real.

## What This Is NOT

- Not plot armor: the PC can be hurt, captured, robbed, or (with warning and bad choices) killed.
- Not a crutch that solves mysteries: companions support and warn; they do not hand over the truth.
- Not permanent: by Act 2 the player should stand more on their own; these props can be removed, killed, or outgrown to raise stakes.
