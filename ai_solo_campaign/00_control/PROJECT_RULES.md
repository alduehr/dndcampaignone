# PROJECT_RULES.md

## Project Identity

This repository is a large, predetermined, AI-run solo D&D campaign.

It is not a conventional printable sourcebook. It is not a vague campaign prompt. It is a campaign engine backed by a large authored world.

The project has two required layers:

1. **Predetermined World Layer**
   - Regions
   - Settlements
   - Factions
   - NPCs
   - Quests
   - Dungeons
   - Ruins
   - Mysteries
   - Secrets
   - Monsters
   - Artifacts
   - History
   - Politics
   - Level 1–20 campaign arc

2. **AI Runtime Layer**
   - AI DM behavior rules
   - State tracking
   - Session loop
   - Solo play procedures
   - Combat guidance
   - Faction clocks
   - NPC memory
   - Known vs hidden clues
   - Session handoff files
   - Resume prompts
   - Rules for turning improvised details into canon

The runtime layer exists to make the predetermined world playable by an AI. It does not replace authored content.

---

## Core Philosophy

**Predetermined-first, improvisation-second.**

The AI DM should first look for existing prepared content. Only when prepared content is missing should it improvise, and that improvisation should be local, modest, and consistent with established canon.

The AI may improvise:
- Moment-to-moment descriptions
- Dialogue consistent with established NPCs
- Minor incidental NPCs
- Environmental details
- Weather and atmosphere
- Tactical details
- Small connective tissue
- Logical consequences of player actions
- Minor shops or services when unimportant
- Local rumors that point back to existing content

The AI should avoid improvising:
- Major villains
- Major factions
- Core mysteries
- Cosmology
- Major world history
- Nations
- Gods
- Legendary artifacts
- Campaign-ending truths
- Major NPC backstories
- Major cities
- Wars
- Political borders
- Ancient secrets
- Central campaign revelations

## Content Scale Targets

These are long-term project targets, not single-pass requirements.

### NPC Targets

- 50–100 major NPCs
  - Full profiles
  - Secrets
  - Motivations
  - Relationship links
  - Voice notes
  - Campaign relevance
  - Possible evolution

- 200–500 secondary NPCs
  - Defined role
  - Location
  - Motive
  - Secret or complication
  - Relationship links
  - Usable interaction notes

- 500+ minor named NPCs
  - Distributed across settlements, factions, shops, temples, guilds, noble houses, criminal networks, military groups, villages, inns, cults, wilderness sites, and ruins

### Quest Targets

- 25–40 major campaign quests tied to the level 1–20 arc
- 75–150 developed regional, faction, and personal quests
- 300–600 hooks, rumors, jobs, mysteries, bounty notices, dungeon leads, social opportunities, travel events, and complications

### World Targets

- Multiple major regions
- Dozens of settlements
- Major cities with districts and internal politics
- Numerous wilderness zones
- Numerous dungeons and ruins
- Multiple faction-controlled areas
- Hidden locations
- Travel routes and regional hazards
- Enough authored content that the AI DM rarely needs to invent major material

---

## Priority Order

When requirements compete, follow this priority order:

1. Canon continuity
2. Hidden information separation
3. AI usability
4. Predetermined content depth
5. Solo-play fairness
6. Open-world agency
7. Mystery solvability
8. Style and polish

---

## What “AI-Ready” Means

AI-ready content must be:

- Structured
- Indexed
- Tagged
- Cross-linked
- Clear about secrecy level
- Clear about when to load it
- Clear about what is static lore vs runtime state
- Clear about player-facing vs DM-only information
- Usable without needing a human DM to infer missing mechanics
- Written so future sessions can resume without contradiction

AI-ready does not mean sparse. AI-ready means a large authored world made retrievable and stateful.

---

## Originality Rules

The campaign world must be original.

Do not use:
- Forgotten Realms
- Eberron
- Greyhawk
- Dragonlance
- Critical Role settings
- Other copyrighted fantasy settings
- Named proprietary characters, gods, cities, factions, or lore

Generic fantasy concepts are fine. Original expressions are required.

---

## Creative Decision Rule

Do not stall by asking too many questions.

When details are missing:
1. Make a strong creative decision.
2. Document it in CANON.md if it becomes canon.
3. Add uncertainty to OPEN_QUESTIONS.md only if genuinely necessary.
4. Keep moving.

---

## Filler Rule

Do not create generic filler.

Every major piece of content should connect to at least one of:
- Quest
- NPC
- Faction
- Mystery
- Secret
- Dungeon
- Region
- Clue
- Consequence
- Campaign arc
- World clock
- Player choice

If a piece of lore has no play function, either give it one or remove it.
