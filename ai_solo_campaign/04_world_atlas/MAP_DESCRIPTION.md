## AI Use

Load for travel questions, to orient the player, and to place new locations relative to known ones. Use the travel table for journey pacing.

## Map Scale and Assumptions

- **Scope:** The campaign uses the continent of **Orrun**, focusing on its northwestern quarter for Acts 1–2.
- **Travel scale:** Point-to-point between named sites. Within the Sundering Reach, sites are **half a day to three days** apart on foot. The nearest city (Caradril) is **~10–12 days** overland from Hollowmere.
- **Map style:** Region-based, point-to-point. No hex grid required.
- **Terrain difficulty:** The Reach is hard country — fens, moors, and old roads. Off-road travel doubles time and adds hazard.

## Continental Shape (Orrun, Northwest Quarter)

Imagine Orrun's northwest as a cold, wet shelf of land between mountains and sea:

- **North/northeast:** the **Sunder Heights**, broken highlands and the old mountains the Concord mined; the Reach's name comes from these sundered ridges.
- **Center (starting region):** the **Sundering Reach** — a basin of fens, peat moors, and scattered ruins, drained by the slow **Mirewend River**. At its heart sits the flooded **Hollowmere basin** and the town of **Hollowmere**.
- **West:** the land falls to the **Pale Coast** and the cold sea; salt marshes and fishing villages.
- **South:** the ground rises and warms into the **Ashgarden Vale**, Orrun's nearest breadbasket.
- **East:** the **Tollwood**, deep forest threaded by overgrown Concord toll-roads, leading eventually toward **Caradril**.
- **Southeast (beyond the Vale and Tollwood):** **Caradril**, the inland city-state, nearest seat of real power.

## Major Geographic Features

- **The Mirewend River** — slow, peat-dark; the Reach's main artery; floods in autumn.
- **The Hollowmere basin** — a wide flooded hollow over the great drowned Concord shrine; Hollowmere town rings its shore.
- **The Sunder Heights** — cold highland ruins and mines to the north.
- **Old Concord roads** — raised stone causeways crossing the fens; the safest routes, though haunted and crumbling.
- **The Greyfens** — the most dangerous wetland, fog-bound, where Remembrances gather.

## Travel Time Reference (from Hollowmere)

| Destination | Mode | Time | Hazards |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nearby Reach villages (Stage 3) | Foot, Concord road | 0.5–2 days | Fen fog, Remembrances, weather |
| The Greyfens edge | Foot | ~1 day | High Remembrance activity; getting lost |
| Sunder Heights ruins | Foot | 2–3 days | Cold, broken ground, highland predators |
| Pale Coast (nearest village) | Foot/cart | ~5 days | Salt marsh, smugglers |
| Ashgarden Vale (edge) | Foot/cart | ~6 days | Bandits on the road south |
| Tollwood (edge) | Foot | ~4 days | Forest, toll-bandits |
| Caradril (city) | Cart/road | ~10–12 days | Long road; multiple region crossings |
