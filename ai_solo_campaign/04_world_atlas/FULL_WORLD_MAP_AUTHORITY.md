## AI Use

This is the **master cartographic reference for the whole continent of Orrun** (and for Vael's other landmasses at placeholder level). Load it when:
- Drawing or commissioning a **full-world / full-continent map** (use with `FULL_WORLD_MAP_COORDINATES.md`, `FULL_WORLD_MAP_LAYERS.md`, `FULL_WORLD_MAP_PROMPTS.md`).
- Answering a player's "what lies beyond the frontier?" / "what is the rest of the world like?" question at the broadest scale.
- Placing a far-off, not-yet-built region so the AI DM never invents major continental geography on the fly.

**Authority relationship (important):**
- This file is the authority for **whole-continent and whole-world** geography.
- `WORLD_MAP_AUTHORITY.md` and `WORLD_MAP_COORDINATES.md` remain the authority for the **current campaign area** (the NW-quarter cluster: Sundering Reach + Ring 1 + Caradril). They are **not superseded** for that area — this file *embeds* them, it does not replace them.
- **Established travel times and region descriptions are never overridden.** This file records the larger spatial frame the campaign area sits inside and adds *placeholders* for far regions. Most placeholders are **non-campaign or far-future**; do not deep-detail them.

**Secrecy:** Keep DM-only geography (forbidden zones, the buried network, the endgame placement, the deep truth about the world's shape) out of player-facing narration. See §11 and `FULL_WORLD_MAP_LAYERS.md` Layers 2/4/7.

## 1. World Scope Decision

**Decision (recorded): the primary map is the full continent of Orrun.** Vael's other landmasses are placed only at **placeholder level** (one line each), because the authored campaign never leaves Orrun and the arc's climax is *under Hollowmere*, not overseas.

- **World:** **Vael.**
- **Continent of play (mapped in full here):** **Orrun.**
- **Other landmasses of Vael (placeholder only — not campaign content):**
  - **Surren** — a large continent **across the ocean to the south/southwest**, beyond the Pale Sea's far reaches; warmer, older, the half-legendary "first lands" most Orrun folk only know from broken sailors' tales. One-sentence placeholder. Non-campaign.
  - **The Iron Skards** — a chain of **cold volcanic islands far to the north**, past the Highmark Spine's seaward end; storm-locked, barely known. Placeholder. Non-campaign.
  - **The Sundered Isles (the Far Wrack)** — scattered **far-western mid-ocean islands** in the Pale Sea between Orrun and Surren; way-stops and wreck-grounds for the boldest traders. Placeholder. Non-campaign.

These exist so the world feels finite-but-large; none is required, and none should be developed in this pass.

## 2. Full Continent Shape of Orrun

- **Size feel:** Orrun is a **large continent** — the mapped campaign NW quarter (a ~12-day corner-to-corner span) is roughly **one-quarter to one-fifth of the continent's breadth**. The full continent is on the order of a **two-to-three-month** journey corner to corner by road, far more by foot through wild country. Most people live and die within a few regions of their birth.
- **Dominant geographic axis:** **northwest-to-southeast.** The cold, broken, ruin-strewn **frontier sits in the northwest**; the land warms, lowers, and grows richer and more settled toward the **southeast and south**, where the great rivers run to a warm southern sea. The continent "tilts" from cold-poor-NW to warm-rich-SE.
- **Coastline character:** **highly varied** — a rugged, fjorded, storm-battered **northwest and west coast** (the Pale Coast and beyond); a long **mountain-walled northern edge** with few harbors; a **broad, deltaic, river-mouthed south coast** with great ports; and a **drier, island-fringed southeast** opening to a warm inland sea.
- **Overall feel:** a continent of **many small powers in the shadow of one fallen great one.** The Concord once spanned much of it; its ruins and roads thread the whole land. No single state has replaced it. The world is a patchwork — leagues, city-states, theocracies, tribal lands, fallen realms — connected by old roads and rivers, divided by mountains, marsh, forest, and distrust.

## 3. Placement of the Existing Campaign Area

The authored campaign cluster (Sundering Reach center; Ashgarden Vale S, Tollwood E, Pale Coast W; Caradril SE) occupies the **far northwestern corner** of Orrun.

- In the full-continent grid (`FULL_WORLD_MAP_COORDINATES.md`, 0–100, NW = (0,0)), the campaign cluster sits in roughly **X 8–40, Y 8–42** — the upper-left quarter.
- **Hollowmere**, the dramatic keystone, sits at about **(24, 23)** — deep in the cold NW.
- **Caradril**, the campaign's SE corner and only true city, sits at about **(34, 35)** — and is, at continental scale, a **modest frontier-edge city-state**, not a continental capital. The "richer, older Orrun" the player hears lies *southeast of Caradril, off the campaign cluster, deeper into the continent.*
- **Proportional scale:** one full-continent grid unit ≈ **8–12 miles** (vs. ~2–2.6 miles per unit on the campaign-area grid). The campaign-area grid is therefore a **~3× zoom-in** on the NW corner. See the rescaling note in `FULL_WORLD_MAP_COORDINATES.md`.

## 9. Scale Assumptions

- **Full-continent travel:** corner-to-corner ≈ **2–3 months** by road in fair season; the NW campaign cluster is a **~12-day** span — roughly **one grid-quarter**.
- **Grid:** `FULL_WORLD_MAP_COORDINATES.md` uses a normalized **0–100** grid for all Orrun; NW = (0,0), SE = (100,100). One unit ≈ **8–12 miles**.
- **Campaign-area zoom:** the campaign-area grid (`WORLD_MAP_COORDINATES.md`) is a **~3× zoom** on the NW corner (full-grid X 8–40, Y 8–42). The rescaling transform is documented in `FULL_WORLD_MAP_COORDINATES.md`.
- **Canonical units stay days, not miles.** All established day-counts (Hollowmere→Caradril ~10–12 d, etc.) are unchanged and authoritative; miles are a drawing convenience only.

## 11. Secrecy Section

### Player-safe geography (a well-traveled person could know)
- Orrun is a large continent; the campaign frontier is its cold NW corner. The land warms and grows richer toward the south and southeast.
- Past Caradril, the Verdance runs up into the settled interior (the Verdance Reaches), toward great river-cities (the Glassmere League) on the continent's main river (the Glasswater), which runs south to warm ports on a southern sea (the Hollow Gulf / the Calm Reach).
- A great mountain range (the Greatspine) splits the continent; the old Concord's fallen capital country lies somewhere astride it (ruined, contested).
- There are tribal steppes in the north (the Wender Steppe), an outlaw mountain frontier in the northeast (the Karran Marches), a fire-mountain land in the southeast (the Emberfells), a dying inland salt sea (the Saltmere), and warm southern forests and deltas.
- Overseas lands exist (Surren far south; cold isles far north) but are the stuff of sailors' tales.

### Rumored / half-known
- That the Concord once spanned nearly the whole continent (true; ruins everywhere prove it).
- That the Concord's heart-country is a forbidden ruin everyone covets.
- That something old "went down" in the Saltmere and the Drowned Steps (echoes of pre-Concord falls; oblique, not the campaign secret).

### DM-only (never narrate as map fact)
- That the harvest network (the **Concord Deep**) and the endgame (the **Under-Shrine / Drowned Keystone**) are **beneath the NW campaign cluster**, not out on the continent — the endgame is **vertical (down beneath Hollowmere)**, never a distant land.
- That the far surface ruins (Concord Heartlands, Cindern Waste works, Saltmere drowned towns) are surface-Concord history and pre-Concord echoes — they **corroborate** the theme but are **not** the keystone and **not** the Hollow Court's seat. Do not let far-region grandeur pull the climax away from Hollowmere.
- Other surviving Custodians, if any, remain off-map and undefined; do not invent them in narration.
