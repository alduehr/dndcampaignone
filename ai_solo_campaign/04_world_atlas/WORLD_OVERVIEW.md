## One-Sentence Identity

Vael is a world where the dead do not leave cleanly, and Orrun is a continent picking itself up a century after the order that "kept the dead at peace" destroyed itself.

## Player-Facing Overview

**Vael** is the world; **Orrun** is the continent where the campaign takes place. A hundred years ago, the **Custodian Concord** — a continent-spanning order of rite-keepers — collapsed in a single catastrophic season, the **Quietfall**. With it went roads, archives, long lives, and the great shrines that "kept the dead at peace."

Today Orrun is a patchwork of independent regions, towns, and councils, living among Concord ruins. The defining fear of the age is simple and terrible: **death-rites are failing.** Some of the dead will not rest. Pale, grieving figures — **Remembrances** — are seen near old shrines and graves, and it is getting worse. Resurrection, once solemn but possible, has become rare and dangerous.

The campaign begins on Orrun's cold northwestern frontier, the **Sundering Reach**, where the failures are worst.

## World-Level Threats (Player-Facing)

- The spreading failure of death-rites.
- The rise of feared groups like the Gravecallers.
- Frontier instability: no central authority since the Quietfall.

## DM-Only Truth

The death-rite failures are not decay or divine anger. They are the **reawakening of the Concord's hidden death-harvesting machine**, centered on the drowned shrine beneath Hollowmere, driven by surviving Custodians (the **Hollow Court**). The Quietfall was a deliberate, controlled pause of that machine. The whole continent's "age of healing" was the afterlife refilling after near-total drain. See `../03_canon/DM_ONLY_CANON.md` and `../03_canon/WORLD_HISTORY.md`. **Never narrate this to the player.**
