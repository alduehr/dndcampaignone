## Purpose

All active escalating threats and faction timelines. Clocks advance when time passes, when the player ignores threats, or on specific triggers. The DM uses this to make the world consequential. **Mixed secrecy:** some clock *causes* are DM-only (the harvest); never narrate hidden causes to the player.

## Clock Details

### 1. The Harvest Restarts (MASTER CLOCK)
- **Type:** world / DM-only cause. **Driver:** Hollow Court.
- **What advances it:** the Basin clock (#2) advancing; the shrine being breached; relics returning to the shrine; long stretches of player inaction.
- **Stage 1:** Leakage worsens; rites fail across the Reach (now).
- **Stage 2:** Basin begins to drain; upper shrine surfaces; the Court's reach grows.
- **Stage 3:** Shrine breached; the Court manifests to those who enter; first deliberate harvest of a local death.
- **Stage 4:** Harvest at scale; Quiet Country drains again; thin-born births rise.
- **Final:** Harvest fully restarts under the Court's rule (worst default outcome).
- **Player levers:** slow #2; keep the shrine sealed (Wardens/Mourners); turn Maire; choose an endgame branch.
- **Last advanced:** session 0 (start). **File:** `../07_factions/major_factions/HOLLOW_COURT.md`.

### 2. The Basin Drains
- **Type:** political. **Driver:** Reke, Compact, Ledger funding.
- **Advances:** time; Compact votes going Reke's way; player ignoring Compact politics; Ledger funding.
- **Stage 1:** Drainage project debated/approved over Coalmont's objection.
- **Stage 2:** Work begins; water drops; ruins surface.
- **Stage 3:** Upper drowned shrine exposed; leakage spikes.
- **Stage 4:** Shrine breachable; Court gains direct access (drives #1 to Stage 3+).
- **Final:** Keystone reopened.
- **Player levers:** expose Reke (Secret 9); sway Coalmont/cautious bloc; sabotage works; rally the Circle; win a vote.
- **File:** `../07_factions/major_factions/REACHWARD_COMPACT.md`.

### 3. The Wardens Break
- **Driver:** Ashen Wardens under crisis load.
- **Stages:** thin → a rite goes unanswered/someone harmed → Wardens refuse the Greyfens → Othetha breaks her oath and reveals the truth to a trusted few.
- **Final:** order fractures; the seal's guardians scatter (accelerates #1).
- **Player levers:** help with rites; recruit aid; protect Othetha; fund the Wardens.
- **File:** `../07_factions/major_factions/ASHEN_WARDENS.md`.

### 4. The Circle Turns Militant
- **Driver:** Mourners' Circle.
- **Stages:** grief→anger; mobs block relic caravans; radicals ally with Gravecallers; the Circle moves to destroy a shrine.
- **Final:** a shrine shattered violently (destabilization).
- **Player levers:** honor the dead; earn Wend's trust; keep relic-buyers out; mediate elders.
- **File:** `../07_factions/major_factions/MOURNERS_CIRCLE.md`.

### 5. The Breaking (Gravecallers)
- **Driver:** Gravecaller radicals (Cole Ashby).
- **Stages:** cells swell; acquire breaking-tools + shrine map; a cell attempts a breach; attempt to shatter the keystone.
- **Final:** if they succeed alone, the dead are freed catastrophically.
- **Player levers:** learn from them; protect them from purge; talk radicals down; help them break it *safely* (synthesis); or stop them.
- **File:** `../07_factions/major_factions/GRAVECALLERS.md`.

### 6. The Monopoly Closes
- **Driver:** Cinder Ledger (Vyre).
- **Stages:** out-buy all salvage; Remnant deal struck; sell small death-cheating to the rich; broker the reopened harvest.
- **Final:** immortality becomes purchasable; the dead become inventory.
- **Player levers:** outbid/steal relics; expose the Vyre–Quorrin deal; turn Domic; default.
- **File:** `../07_factions/major_factions/CINDER_LEDGER.md`.

### 7. The Reclamation
- **Driver:** Concord Remnant inner circle (Quorrin).
- **Stages:** locate the shrine via archives; secure Ledger backing; send agents to the Reach; race for the keystone.
- **Final:** harvest restarts under a new living Concord (a tyranny).
- **Player levers:** expose Quorrin to the idealists; deny relics; beat them to the truth; turn them on the Court.
- **File:** `../07_factions/major_factions/CONCORD_REMNANT.md`.

### 8. The Steering Unravels
- **Type:** personal / DM-only. **Driver:** Court cutout via Sefra.
- **Stages:** Sefra keeps feeding the player toward the shrine; her unease grows; she nearly confesses; the patron moves to "tidy up."
- **Final:** the player learns they were steered (M8) — a trust shock; or Sefra vanishes if neglected.
- **Player levers:** press Sefra; follow the money; protect her.
- **File:** `../08_npcs/MAJOR_NPCS.md` (#16); M8 in `MYSTERY_WEB.md`.

### 9. Wren's Fate
- **Type:** personal. **Driver:** Wren, Tomas, the town (Act 1 immediate).
- **Stages:** the town pities Wren → fear grows / a mob forms / Tomas turns to the Gravecallers → the Wardens are forced to act → resolution forced one way or another.
- **Final:** Wren released, hidden, fought, or "taken back" by the shrine — each with consequences.
- **Player levers:** Hook 1 — resolve peacefully, protect her, or use her.
- **File:** `../09_quests/HOOKS_TABLE.md` (Hook 1); `MAJOR_NPCS.md` (#17/#18).

### 10. The Thinning Spreads
- **Type:** world / DM-only background consequence of #1.
- **Stages:** isolated thin-born births and failed resurrections → noticeable pattern → regional dread → (Act 4+) demographic alarm.
- **Final:** the slow horror that makes even the Court's argument tempting.
- **Player levers:** indirect — only stopping #1 stops this.
- **File:** `../03_canon/COSMOLOGY.md` / `DM_ONLY_CANON.md`.

## Stage 4 Caradril City Clocks (City-Internal)

Three city-internal clocks for Caradril (the first major city), added in Stage 4. They are **city-scale faction conflicts that feed the existing master clocks** (chiefly #6 Monopoly and #7 Reclamation) — they do not replace or duplicate them. All start at **Stage 1 (campaign start)**; advance when the player visits/ignores Caradril or as the master clocks turn. **DM-only causes (the deal, the correspondent) must never be narrated as such.**

### C1. The Deal Closes (Caradril)
- **Type:** faction/city (feeds #6 Monopoly + #7 Reclamation). **Driver:** Vyre + Quorrin (the monopoly deal).
- **Advances:** time; the player feeding either faction relics/knowledge; ignoring the Counting-Quays/Lantern Reach.
- **Stage 1:** Vyre and Quorrin negotiate quietly in Caradril.
- **Stage 2:** terms struck; the Ledger funds the Remnant's relic-supply; Coll Riis copies the proof in fear.
- **Stage 3:** the Ledger begins selling small death-cheating to the rich; the Remnant secures its relic-pipeline.
- **Stage 4:** the alliance positions to broker/seize the reopened harvest as a commodity *and* an empire.
- **Final:** the two richest factions co-own the dead — unless the player exposed or shattered the deal first.
- **Player levers:** turn Coll Riis; reach the Sealed Archive; expose the deal to Tolm/the council/the idealists; play Vyre and Quorrin against each other.
- **Files:** `Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER.md`, `Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE.md`, `CINDER_LEDGER.md`, `CONCORD_REMNANT.md`.

### C2. The Council Sleeps (Caradril)
- **Type:** political/city. **Driver:** the Tidewater Council's complacency (Voss; the Charter Houses; the Reke correspondent thread).
- **Advances:** time; the player ignoring Magisterium politics; the open charter falling to the Ledger/old Houses.
- **Stage 1:** the council shelves the Reach's warnings; the open charter contested.
- **Stage 2:** the Ledger or an old House wins the charter; reform (Dann) is sidelined.
- **Stage 3:** Caradril stays in denial as the crisis spreads; the relic-trade swells unchecked; the correspondent thread deepens.
- **Stage 4:** the city is among the last to believe — a well-resourced, dangerous endgame player if the player never engaged it.
- **Final:** Caradril's wealth and complacency become an obstacle (or, if reformed, an asset) at the endgame.
- **Player levers:** `Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER` (back Dann); carry the Reach warning to the council; ally the ignored Wardens; develop the correspondent lead.
- **Files:** `Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER.md`, `Q_THE_SEALED_LETTER.md`, `THE_MAGISTERIUM.md`.

### C3. The City's Denial Cracks (Caradril)
- **Type:** world/city (feeds #10 The Thinning Spreads). **Driver:** the crisis arriving in "untouched" Caradril (Highmourn failures; the Sill refugees; the smelting/quiet-coin).
- **Advances:** time; the master harvest clock (#1) turning; the player exposing or ignoring the city's own evidence.
- **Stage 1:** isolated covered-up raise-dead failures (Ysarra); thin-born refugees trickle into the Sill.
- **Stage 2:** the cover-up strains; quiet-coin and smelting "incidents" spread; the refugee tide grows.
- **Stage 3:** the failures become hard to hide; the Sill restless; a wrong-come-back or two surface; the latent Gravecaller possibility quickens.
- **Stage 4:** Caradril can no longer deny the crisis — panic, or reckoning, depending on whether the player prepared the city.
- **Final:** the city faces the truth — too late to be calm, in time (if the player acted) to matter.
- **Player levers:** `Q_THE_FUNERAL_THAT_WOULDNT_TAKE` (expose the cover-up); protect the refugees (Renna); curb quiet-coin/smelting; ally the Wardens.
- **Files:** `Q_THE_FUNERAL_THAT_WOULDNT_TAKE.md`, `HIGHMOURN.md`, `THE_SILL.md`.
