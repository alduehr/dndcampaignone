## Opening Scene Description (player-safe, deliver or adapt)

> You came to Hollowmere for your own reasons — work, a fresh start, a name you'd rather leave behind. The grey town rings a hollow of black, still water; salt sits at every sill and a small star is carved above every door. You arrived in time for a funeral.
>
> On the cemetery green, a midwife named Wren Brevin is being laid to rest. The grief-keeper sings the passing; salt is poured; the star is set. And then — wrong. The song falters. The cold deepens. Mourners step back. The grief-keeper's voice cracks as she begins the rite again, and again, and the dead woman in the open grave does not lie quiet.
>
> Her husband is on his knees. Someone near you whispers, "Not another one." A sharp-smiling woman at your elbow — Sefra, she'll say her name is — murmurs, "You look capable. There's coin in being capable, in a town like this." She's watching you, not the grave.

End on the implicit "what do you do?" — no menu. Alternative openers (B: the inn / mercenary; C: the Concord road / eerie) are in `../16_ai_session_packs/OPENING_SCENES.md` if the player wants a different mood.

- Sefra was paid by a Hollow Court cutout (via Reke) to steer a capable newcomer toward the Brevins and the shrine (Secrets 10/16; M8). She doesn't know whom she serves and is uneasy. Let her unease flicker; reveal nothing.
- The broken grey shapes under the basin are the drowned shrine (M2) — atmosphere now, a clue later.
- Solo safety is on: Wren is not a forced fight; Hale/Othetha can assist; the Drowned Lantern is a safe base; retreat is always available.
- Session-1 goals: establish Hollowmere as home base and the failing rites as the world's heartbeat; introduce 2–3 major NPCs; land Hook 1; offer a safe base and an ally; end on a clear next step.
