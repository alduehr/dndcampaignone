## AI Use

Load when the player ventures toward the Greyfens, follows the failing-rites or Gravecaller threads, or needs a deep-fen village where the dead-don't-rest crisis is at its worst outside Hollowmere. Greywater Holm is the last firm ground before the Greyfens; ~1 day southeast of Hollowmere, near Old Sashe's hut.

## One-Sentence Identity

A shrinking fen-village on the edge of the Greyfens where rites fail almost every time now, and where the desperate are quietly turning to the Gravecallers.

## First Impression

The road gives out into a corduroy of half-sunk logs, and the village rises on a low holm barely above the black water — a huddle of turf-roofed cottages, a single crooked longhall, and a burial-ground that has crept up onto the only dry rise because the lower graves "won't stay." Mist hangs even at midday. Every door has salt and a star, and many have a second mark scratched beneath the star that the villagers won't explain. It is quiet in a way that feels like held breath.

## Public Overview

Greywater Holm was a peat-cutting and fowling village of perhaps two hundred souls. It is now perhaps half that and falling — families leave for Hollowmere or Kettle Bridge because here, on the Greyfens' lip, the rites fail more than they hold. The **Mourners' Circle** is the only real authority, led by an exhausted elder. **Ashen Wardens** come when they can, which is not often enough. There is no Compact watch and no Ledger house. The village is poor, frightened, grieving, and increasingly fertile ground for the one group that promises the grieving a *last word*.

## DM-Only Truth

Greywater Holm is the **closest inhabited place to the Greyfens' drift** (M5) and to the drowned shrine's outer pull — which is exactly why its rites fail worst. The second mark beneath the star on many doors is the **Gravecaller Knock**: Sister Knell has been working this village for a season, and a hidden cell now meets in a sunk fowler's blind out in the fen. The village is the campaign's clearest demonstration that the failures get worse nearer the water, and a low-stakes door to the Gravecallers (M3/M6) gentler than the Tallow Man's. The elder here secretly tolerates the Gravecallers because they are the only ones who can make a failed rite *stop screaming*.

## Local Leadership

**Elder Mabb Tay** of the Mourners' Circle — old, grief-worn, and quietly heretical: she has stopped believing the rites can be saved and tolerates the Gravecallers' comfort even as she dreads them. She speaks for the village because no one else will.

## Factions Present

Mourners' Circle (governs morally), Gravecallers (hidden cell, growing), Ashen Wardens (intermittent). No Compact, no Ledger. This is the only settlement where the Gravecallers are functionally winning.

## Shops and Services

- No inn; travelers shelter in the longhall by the elder's leave (a meal and floor-space for a copper or a kind deed).
- A single peat-and-fowl trader; no smith, no temple healer.
- **Old Sashe** sells fen-guiding from his hut — the village's most valuable service and a key solo-safety resource for the Greyfens.
- Folk healing only (Widow Sarn knows herbs).

## Laws and Customs

- No formal law; the elder mediates. Theft and violence are punished by exile into the fen — effectively a death sentence.
- Custom: the second mark beneath the star (publicly "an old fen-charm," secretly the Knock); the dead are now buried higher each year.
- Outsiders are watched warily but a respectful hand is welcomed — the village badly needs help.

## Current Tensions

- **Rites failing almost every time** — the village is losing its faith in the Wardens and the Circle both.
- **The Gravecallers' quiet spread** — half the grieving have taken the second mark; the elder looks away.
- **Depopulation** — every family that leaves makes the holm less defensible against the fen and its dead.
- **Sashe's warnings** — he says the dead are drifting *through* the village toward the water and no one wants to hear it.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The hidden Gravecaller cell in the Sunk Blind (M3/M6 door; Knell).
- The Knock-mark on the doors.
- Rites fail worst here because of the shrine's pull (M5 — the clearest field proof in Act 1).
- Elder Mabb tolerates the cult and is ashamed.

## Quest Hooks

- **"The Holm That Won't Bury Its Dead"** — help a family whose rite keeps failing (direct M5 evidence; Warden-trust path).
- **"The Second Mark"** — investigate the Knock-marks (gentle Gravecaller door via Knell).
- **"Sashe's Warning"** — Old Sashe will guide the player to *see* the drift if they earn his respect (M5; Secret 20).

See the regional hooks and quest files.

## Possible Scenes

- A rite fails in the longhall before the player's eyes; the dead won't go down; the village turns to whoever can help.
- Sister Knell, gentle and grieving herself, offering the player "a kinder way."
- Old Sashe at his hut, refusing to guide a disrespectful traveler, opening up to a kind one.
- Widow Sarn asking the player, simply, to make her husband rest.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch response:** none. The danger here is the fen and the failing dead, not authority.
- **Failed-rite encounter:** a single **wrong-come-back Remembrance** (flavor **specter/ghost-like**, AC 12, ~22 HP, telegraphed, resolvable by a true rite without combat). Solo-safe if the player fetches a Warden or works with the elder.
- **Gravecaller cell:** noncombatant by preference (Knell). If the player threatens them, **2–3 grief-mad commoners** (**Commoner/Cultist-like**, AC 11–12, ~10–22 HP) defend the blind; they would rather flee or talk.
- **Social DCs:** earn the elder's trust DC 13; get Sashe to guide you DC 13 (DC 8 if respectful of the dead); be invited to the cell DC 12 (via Knell, lower if grieving).
- **Rest/recovery:** the longhall (safe but spare long rest); folk healing only.
- **Solo danger:** low in the holm; the adjacent Greyfens are moderate-to-high (see region/wilderness files).

## Consequences If Ignored

If the player never comes here, Greywater Holm continues to empty and the Gravecaller cell matures into a launch point for "the Breaking" (advancing the Gravecaller clock); Sashe's drift-evidence (a key M5 clue) goes unwitnessed unless the player meets him elsewhere; and the holm becomes, by mid-Act 2, a half-abandoned Gravecaller stronghold on the Greyfens' edge.
