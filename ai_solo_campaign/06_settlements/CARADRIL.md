## AI Use

The campaign's first major city and the mid-game hub (Acts 2–4, levels ~5–12, with a few level-3–5 on-ramps and level-12+ endgame threads). Load when the player travels southeast to Caradril, researches the Concord's deep history, pursues the Cinder Ledger or Concord Remnant to their seats, or needs a true city's services, politics, and intrigue. **Player-Facing** sections are safe; **DM-Only** sections are not. Districts live in `caradril_districts/`; city NPCs in `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `MINOR_NPCS.md`; city quests in `../09_quests/city_quests/`; hooks/rumors in `../09_quests/hooks_and_rumors/CARADRIL_HOOKS.md` and `CARADRIL_RUMORS.md`; encounters/scenes in `../13_encounters_and_bestiary/CARADRIL_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## One-Sentence Identity

A proud, prosperous inland city-state that survived the Quietfall by going quiet and getting rich — and now hosts the two factions racing hardest to own the dead, beneath a council too comfortable to notice.

## First Impression

After ten days of fen, forest, and broken road, Caradril is a shock of stone and noise: a tiered city climbing the banks of a slow green river to a still inland lake crowded with masts. Bells, not funeral-bells but trade-bells, mark the hours. The streets are paved, the lamps are lit at dusk by a guild that takes itself seriously, and the people are *not* afraid of the dead — here, death is tidy, expensive, and handled by professionals. Coin moves everywhere. Above the counting-houses fly the banners of the Charter Houses; above the river-college burn the green lamps of the scholars. It feels, after the Reach, almost untouched — which is exactly what should make a careful traveler uneasy.

## Public Overview

Caradril is the nearest true city to the Sundering Reach: an independent city-state on the **Verdance** river where it widens into the harbor-lake locals call the **Stillwater**. It was a modest Concord river-town before the Quietfall and, alone among the great settlements of the northwest, came through the collapse mostly intact. The century since — what Caradrese call **the Stilling** — made it rich as a trade-clearing house and refuge. It is governed by the **Tidewater Council** of magisters, drawn from the chartered merchant families (the **Charter Houses**) and headed by a **First Magister**, currently **Halloran Voss**. Its law is kept by the **Tide-Watch**. It is home to the headquarters of the **Cinder Ledger** (the merchant-bank that funds the frontier) and the seat of the **Concord Remnant** (the scholars who can read Concord Script). Most of its people are merchants, clerks, artisans, dockhands, scholars, and the poor of the riverside undercity called **the Sill**. Caradril honors the **Three Thresholds** with grand civic funerals on **Highmourn** hill. It thinks of itself as the safe, civilized future the rest of Orrun should imitate.

## DM-Only Truth

Caradril is where the campaign's conspiracy lives in daylight. **The Cinder Ledger's HQ (the Ledger Keep) and the Concord Remnant's true vault (the Sealed Archive) are both here, and Mother Calla Vyre and Archivist Quorrin Vane are negotiating their secret Remembrance-monopoly deal in this city.** The city's clearest *written proof* of the harvest and the deliberate Quietfall is locked in the Sealed Archive (C-M6-1 / C-M9). Caradril's century of "blessed calm" has a buried reason the city does not know: **the Quietfall's pause is why Caradril prospered — the harvest stopped draining the region, and the relief read as good fortune.** The city is complacent because it is, unknowingly, living *inside the success of the very crime the player is uncovering.* The Hollow Court has no troops here, but it has interests: the Remnant's Reclamation and the Ledger's Monopoly both run through Caradril, and **Reke corresponds with someone in the Magisterium** (a thread, not yet a named second agent — keep it a lead, not a reveal). Beneath the city lie the **Sunken Wards**, a flooded lower Concord level that is a *cousin* of the Reach's nodes — a minor, long-dormant relic-site the Remnant has quietly mapped and the Court's network faintly touches. Caradril is not the keystone; it is the **library, bank, and battlefield** of the mid-game. See `../03_canon/DM_ONLY_CANON.md`. Never narrate this.

## Scale and Layout

- **Population:** roughly 18,000–22,000 — by far the largest place the player has seen. Treat as a true city with districts, not a town.
- **Shape:** tiered on the north and south banks of the Verdance where it meets the Stillwater harbor-lake. The river runs roughly west-to-east through the city; the lake is at the east end.
- **Upper city (north bank, higher ground):** the **Magisterium** (government), **Highmourn** (temples/cemetery hill), and the **Lantern Reach** (scholars).
- **River and harbor (the working heart):** the **Counting-Quays** (Ledger banking and docks on the Stillwater), the **Ashmarket** (relic and salvage trade), and the **Crucible** (foundries, guildhalls).
- **Low city (south bank, riverside):** the **Sill** (the poor undercity), built over and into older flooded structure.
- **Underneath everything:** the **Sunken Wards**, a partly flooded older Concord level reached by a few sealed and forgotten stairs (the **Sealgate** is the known one).

## Local Leadership

The **Tidewater Council**: a body of ~nine **magisters** chartered from the merchant houses, chaired by **First Magister Halloran Voss** (comfortable, conflict-averse, secretly out of his depth). The reform-minded **Magister Sefa Dann** and the proud old-money **Charter-Lord Esren Tolm** are the two poles the player can play between. Law is enforced by the **Tide-Watch** under **Captain Yorell Kade**. See `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` (Caradril section) and `caradril_districts/THE_MAGISTERIUM.md`.

## Government and Law Structure

- **The Tidewater Council** holds all real power: it grants **charters** (trade licenses), sets tolls and taxes, judges high disputes, and commands the Tide-Watch. Seats are effectively hereditary among the Charter Houses; a charter can be bought, granted, or revoked, which makes the council a marketplace as much as a government.
- **The Wardmoot** is a powerless commoners' assembly of district representatives — it petitions, it does not rule. It is a pressure valve and a recruiting ground for reformers (Sefa Dann's base).
- **The Tide-Watch** keeps order: competent, bribable at the edges, deferential to charter-holders, and harder on the Sill than on the Quays. Crossing a Charter House is far more dangerous than crossing the law.
- **Caradril is NOT part of the Reachward Compact.** It is an independent city-state. It treats the Reach as a poor, useful, dangerous hinterland — a source of salvage, salt, debt, and unsettling news. The Compact and Caradril deal as near-equals by necessity; Caradril is richer, the Reach is closer to the crisis.

## Factions Present

- **Cinder Ledger** — *headquartered here* (the Ledger Keep, Counting-Quays). Vyre's seat of power; the Quay Charter is its guild-arm. See `../07_factions/major_factions/CINDER_LEDGER.md`.
- **Concord Remnant** — *seated here* (Lamplighters' Hall + the Sealed Archive, Lantern Reach). Quorrin's inner circle and the idealist Collegium. See `../07_factions/major_factions/CONCORD_REMNANT.md`.
- **Tidewater Council / Charter Houses** — the city government (city-internal power bloc; full treatment in the Magisterium district file).
- **Tide-Watch** — the city watch (combat-capable city faction; stat references in the encounter file).
- **Salt Syndicate** — smuggling + relic black market (the Ashmarket and the Sill); city-internal criminal network.
- **The Hush** — information and blackmail brokers (citywide; based in the Sill); city-internal criminal network.
- **Mourners' Circle** — present as Caradril's funeral-clergy on Highmourn; a wealthier, more institutional cousin of the Reach Circle, with the same buried unease.
- **Ashen Wardens** — a thin presence; a single chapter-house, overstretched and politically ignored (the city "doesn't need them" — it thinks).
- **Hollow Court / Gravecallers** — not openly present. The Court works only through correspondence and interests (DM). The Gravecallers have no cell here, but desperate Sill folk and the Sunken Wards are where they *would* take root if the city's calm cracks.

## Shops and Services

Caradril offers everything a town cannot: full magic-item commerce (modest at low tiers, real at higher), spellcasting services (identify, remove curse, lesser restoration, even raise dead — at steep, regulated price and risk), master craftsmen, moneylending and letters of credit (Ledger), scholarly research and translation (Remnant/Collegium), every mundane good, hirelings and guides, ships and caravans onward, and discreet criminal services (Syndicate, Hush). Specific shops and their NPCs live in each district file. **Resurrection note:** raise dead is *available* here, expensive, council-licensed, and quietly unreliable — a player-facing way to feel the campaign's core wrongness (per `../03_canon/MAGIC_RULES.md`).

## Laws and Customs

- **Charter law:** trade without a charter is the city's signature crime; the Tide-Watch enforces it for the Houses. Relic-trading is *licensed* (the Ashmarket) — unlicensed relic-dealing ("quiet-coin") is a black-market offense the Syndicate controls.
- **Funeral law:** the dead are handled by the Quiet Houses on Highmourn for a fee; private or "frontier-style" rites in the city are frowned upon. Gravecaller practice is illegal here too, but unheard-of enough that the law is rarely invoked.
- **Customs:** Caradrese cover the eyes (not the mirrors) of the dead; the lamps are lit at dusk "so the dead can find the Door and not the living"; charter-holders wear a house-token; debt is no shame here, it is *business*.
- **Outsiders:** frontier folk are tolerated, condescended to, and watched. A capable newcomer is a tool or a threat; a newcomer asking about the Concord is *interesting* to exactly the wrong people.

## Current Tensions

- **The Vyre–Quorrin deal** (DM): the Ledger and Remnant are negotiating their Remembrance monopoly *in this city* — and each is trying to cheat the other. The player can detect, exploit, or shatter it here.
- **Charter politics:** Sefa Dann's reform bloc vs. Esren Tolm's old Houses; the Wardmoot agitates; Voss waffles. A charter is about to come open (a city-quest fulcrum).
- **The Sill vs. the Quays:** the poor riverside resents the bankers; thin-born refugees drift in from the failing Reach (a quiet alarm the city denies); the Hush profits from both.
- **The relic boom:** Reach salvage floods the Ashmarket; prices and rumors rise; the clergy and reformers call it grave-robbery; the Crucible smelts what it shouldn't.
- **The Wardens ignored:** the lone Warden chapter-house warns of the Reach crisis and is dismissed as frontier superstition.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

1. The **Sealed Archive** holds the clearest written proof of the harvest and the deliberate Quietfall (C-M6-1, C-M9 sources).
2. **Vyre and Quorrin** are negotiating the monopoly here, each planning to betray the other (M3/M6 access; the Monopoly + Reclamation clocks intersect).
3. **Caradril's "Stilling" prosperity is a side-effect of the harvest's pause** — the city is living inside the crime (M6/M9 thematic key; a late, earned realization, never an early reveal).
4. **Reke corresponds with someone in the Magisterium** — an unrevealed thread toward a possible second Court contact (lead only).
5. The **Sunken Wards** are a dormant minor relic-level beneath the city; the Remnant has secretly mapped them and Wessel Dree guards the way (M2/M3 cousin-site; M7 faint touch — never names the Court).
6. **Underfactor Coll Riis** has copied ledger-pages proving the Vyre–Quorrin deal and can be turned (a clean clue path that doesn't require the Archive).
7. **The Quiet Houses' raise-dead failures** are being quietly covered up by the clergy — Caradril's own evidence the boundary is failing, which Mother Ysarra hides to keep the city calm.

## Possible Scenes

- The Pale Star at dusk: the lamplighters making their rounds, a Ledger clerk and a Remnant scholar pretending not to watch each other across the common room.
- A charter-hearing in Magisters' Hall where the player's word can tip a vote.
- Mother Vyre receiving the player in the Ledger Keep, warm and total, an offer with a hidden clause.
- Quorrin Vane in the Lamplighters' Hall, courteous and reading the player like a text.
- A civic funeral on Highmourn — grand, paid-for, hollow — that "doesn't take," and the clergy hurrying to hide it.
- A quiet-coin deal in an Ashmarket back-room that turns out to be selling the dead.
- The Sill at night: thin-born refugees, the Brass Ferry, and the Bellman offering to *know* something for a price.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Tide-Watch response:** patrols in the upper city and Quays are prompt and well-equipped; the Sill and Sunken Wards are under-policed. Use **Guard-like** rank-and-file (AC 16, ~11 HP), **Veteran-like** sergeants (AC 17, ~58 HP), and a **Knight/Veteran-like** captain (Kade) for serious incidents. Solo danger: a *single* crime is survivable; provoking the Watch in force is a "flee/talk/bribe," not a stand-and-fight, situation.
- **Social DCs:** bribe a watchman to look away DC 13; secure a charter audience DC 15 (or a sponsor); persuade a magister mid-vote DC 17 (or stack evidence/favors to lower it); buy silence from the Hush — coin, not a roll. Crossing a Charter House triggers economic and legal retaliation (debt-calls, charter-blocks, framed charges), not assassination by default — telegraphed, escalating, per the Ledger's methods.
- **Rest/recovery:** abundant. Inns (the Pale Star is the recommended base), the Threshold Cathedral (paid healing, lesser restoration), the Quiet Houses (rites), Ledger letters of credit, and hireling protection. Caradril is a safe long-rest hub — the danger here is social, political, and underground, not random violence.
- **Level framing:** the city is safe to *enter* at levels 3–5 (deliveries, errands, learning Script). Its faction intrigue and the Sunken Wards target levels 5–12. A few threads (the Archive's deepest truths, the Vyre–Quorrin endgame, the Sunken Wards' lower flood) reach into levels 12+.

## Consequences If Ignored

If the player never comes to Caradril, the deep history can still be reached by other paths (Othetha's confession, the Tallow Man's channeled witness, the Barrow, the Deep Adit) — **no mystery dead-ends on the city.** But the factions here *act*: the **Monopoly** and **Reclamation** clocks advance from Caradril; the Vyre–Quorrin deal matures into the Ledger selling death-cheating and the Remnant securing relic-supply to race for the keystone. The city's complacency means it will be among the *last* places to believe the crisis — and a dangerous, well-resourced player of the endgame if the player has not engaged it. Ignoring Caradril cedes the conspiracy's two richest factions a free hand. See `../02_runtime_state/WORLD_CLOCKS.md` (clocks 6, 7) and the city clocks in this district set.
