## AI Use

Load when the player travels the central Concord road between Hollowmere and Kettle Bridge, needs a small waypoint hamlet, or wants a low-stakes failed-rite or travel scene. Reedford is a reed-cutting hamlet on a Mirewend ford; ~half a day east of Hollowmere — the first stop on the road to the bridge.

## One-Sentence Identity

A tiny reed-cutting hamlet at a shallow Mirewend ford, the kind of close-knit nowhere where everyone is related and one failed funeral can break the whole place.

## First Impression

Half a day out of Hollowmere the road dips to a reedy ford where the Mirewend runs wide and shallow over gravel. A dozen cottages, a reed-thatcher's yard, drying-racks of cut reed, and a single shrine-stone on the bank. Children mind geese. Everyone knows everyone. A rope-and-plank ferry crosses when the ford floods. It is peaceful, poor, and so small that strangers are an event.

## Public Overview

Reedford cuts and dries the reed that thatches half the Reach and ferries travelers across the ford. Perhaps eighty people, most of them three or four families. There is no resident authority — a Hollowmere wardman rides through weekly, and a **Mourners' Circle** widow tends the single shrine-stone. **Ashen Wardens** stop on their way to and from Kettle Bridge. It is a waypoint, a meal, and a bed of straw for travelers, and otherwise keeps to itself.

## DM-Only Truth

Reedford's shrine-stone is a minor genuine Concord marker, and the hamlet's recent unease — a rite that *almost* failed, a child's nightmares of "the grey woman at the ford" — is early, mild leakage carried *down the river* from Kettle Bridge's Sunken Tollhouse (M5; ties Reedford to Kettle Bridge). It is a deliberately low-stakes place to first show the player that the crisis is in the water and moving, before they reach a town where it's worse.

## Local Leadership

No formal leader; **Thatcher Goodwife Nan** speaks for the hamlet by force of personality and being related to most of it.

## Important NPCs

- **Goodwife Nan** (minor) — the thatcher; unofficial voice of Reedford; sharp and kind.
- **Ferryman Dob** (minor) — runs the ford-ferry; saw "the grey woman"; rattled.
- **Widow-mourner Sel** (minor) — tends the shrine-stone; worried the rites are slipping here too.

See `../08_npcs/MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Mourners' Circle (one widow), Reachward Compact (a passing wardman), Ashen Wardens (passing). No Ledger, no Gravecallers — too small to matter to them yet.

## Shops and Services

- No inn; a meal and straw-bed from Goodwife Nan for a few coppers or a kindness.
- Reed, thatch, ferry-crossing (1 cp), and river news.
- Folk healing only.

## Laws and Customs

- No law but custom and kinship; disputes go to Nan, then to the Hollowmere wardman.
- Custom: drop a reed in the river for the dead; the ford is never crossed after dark "when the water's wrong."

## Current Tensions

- **The near-failed rite** — the first time Reedford's dead didn't go down easy; the hamlet is frightened.
- **The "grey woman at the ford"** — Dob and two children have seen her; no one wants to talk about it.
- **Flood season** — the autumn Mirewend is rising; the ford may close, isolating the hamlet.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The leakage reaching Reedford comes down-river from the Sunken Tollhouse at Kettle Bridge (M5; links the two).
- The "grey woman" is a drifting Remembrance following the current toward... the basin (the wrong way; Sashe's drift, seen small).

## Possible Scenes

- Dob refusing to ferry the player at dusk, then pointing, white-faced, at the empty water.
- Widow Sel's hands shaking through a rite that doesn't quite take.
- The whole hamlet sharing a meal and asking the player, hopefully, for news from Hollowmere.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch response:** none resident; a Hollowmere wardman within a day.
- **The "grey woman":** a single, sorrowful drifting **Remembrance** (flavor **specter/ghost-like**, AC 12, ~17 HP) — by default a non-combat, resolvable haunting (a true rite, or guiding her with words; Religion/Persuasion DC 12). Combat only if forced; she does not ambush.
- **Social DCs:** earn the hamlet's trust DC 8 (they are desperate for kindness); get Dob to describe the sighting DC 10.
- **Rest/recovery:** safe long rest at Nan's hearth; folk healing only.
- **Solo danger:** very low. Reedford is a gentle waypoint and a soft on-ramp for the M5 mystery.

## Consequences If Ignored

If unvisited, Reedford's haunting worsens quietly; the grey woman is eventually joined by others drifting down-river, and by mid-Act 2 the hamlet may abandon the ford entirely, breaking the central road and forcing Hollowmere–Kettle Bridge traffic onto the dangerous fen routes. A small loss that compounds.
