## AI Use

Load when the player goes deeper into the Tollwood — a charcoal-burners' clearing-village that keeps the oldest forest-customs and is the player's true window into the wood's rules and its deep secret. The home of the wood's best guides and the place that knows what sleeps in the Old Mast. ~2 days off the East Road from Hartfell, deep in the wood (guide strongly advised).

## One-Sentence Identity

A half-feral charcoal-village deep in the Tollwood that has kept the forest's old bargains for longer than the Concord existed — and knows exactly why the roads bend around the deep wood.

## First Impression

A day and more off the road, in a clearing of stumps and smoking charcoal-mounds, sits Coldhearth: low turf-roofed huts, racks of drying wood, and the constant blue haze of the burns. The people are lean, watchful, and quiet, marked with charcoal and old scars, and they speak a slow forest-dialect thick with words for things outsiders don't have. Every threshold, every tool, every grave has an offering-mark. They watch a stranger the way the wood watches — patiently, and from all sides.

## Public Overview

Coldhearth burns the Tollwood's charcoal — the trade that feeds Hartfell's smiths and the city's foundries — and has done so, family by family, since before anyone's reckoning. They keep the **old forest-customs** the road-towns only half-remember: the toll-shrines, the marked trees, the bargains with the wood. They distrust the Ledger, fear the deep wood, and shelter (uneasily) the Gravecallers, who alone treat the deep dead with the respect Coldhearth thinks they're owed. Outsiders come here for one thing: a guide who can keep them alive in the deep wood.

## DM-Only Truth

Coldhearth is the **Tollwood's keeper of the deep secret.** Its forest-elder, **Goodwife Sennet** (a Mourners' forest-elder; not the Saint Veddow's shrine-keeper of the same family-name — see naming note), holds the old bargain: that something *older than the Concord* sleeps in the Old Mast, that the roads bend around it because even the Concord could not master it, and that the woodfolk's whole way of life is the price of keeping it asleep. She knows, in the old songs, that "the grey lords took the dead long before the roads fell" — an **oblique M5/M6 corroboration from a pre-Concord vantage** (the deep dead remember the harvest beginning). The Gravecallers shelter here because the deep dead *answer*; Coldhearth tolerates them and fears where they'll lead. **The deep presence is gated to the Old Mast dungeon and is a regional landmark-power, not the Court or a new god. Cap reveals; deliver fragments and dread.**

## Local Leadership

**Goodwife Sennet**, the forest-elder, leads by the authority of the old customs and the bargain she keeps. There is no other authority — the Ledger and Compact have no reach here. The guides defer to her; even the Gravecallers tread carefully.

## Important NPCs

- **Goodwife Sennet** (Mourners forest-elder; secondary) — keeper of the deep secret and the old bargain; the wood's wisest voice.
- **Brann the Guide** (secondary) — the wood's best guide; will keep an outsider alive if respected (the Tollwood's Sashe).
- **Old Gethin** (minor) — charcoal-foreman; gruff, practical, knows the near wood.
- **Wren-of-the-Wood** (minor) — young guide; reckless, has been deeper than she should (naming note: not Wren Hollowmere — disambiguated by epithet).

See `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `../08_npcs/MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Mourners' Circle (the forest-custom; Sennet), Gravecallers (sheltered cell in the Sheltered Hollow), Ashen Wardens (rare visitors — the deep dead resist their rites). No Ledger or Compact presence; outsiders are tolerated, not welcomed.

## Shops and Services

- No inn; a respected guest is given hearth-room and charcoal-stew (the village's hospitality is real but earned).
- Guides into the deep wood (the village's true export to outsiders; essential and not cheap — coin, a service, or respect).
- Folk healing (Sennet and the wood-women; herb-lore beyond the road-towns'); the Mark-Stone for rites and offerings.
- No banking, no fine goods; charcoal, herb-lore, and woodcraft to trade.

## Laws and Customs

- The old customs are law: offer at the Mark-Stone, keep the bargains, never lead anything out of the deep wood. Breaking the bargain is the one unforgivable crime, judged by Sennet and the wood.
- Hospitality is sacred — a guest who respects the customs is protected; one who scoffs is turned out into the dark.
- The Gravecallers are sheltered but watched; Coldhearth fears their recklessness even as it agrees with their reverence.

## Current Tensions

- **The logging nears** — Dorr's cutting creeping toward Coldhearth's wood and the Old Mast; the village's whole bargain at risk.
- **The Gravecallers' recklessness** — the cell wants to go deeper, to *speak with the grey lords*; Sennet fears they'll wake what sleeps.
- **The young guides** — Wren-of-the-Wood and others going deeper than the bargain allows, drawn by the deep dead.
- **Outsiders** — every stranger is a risk; the player must earn the village's trust.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The deep secret: a pre-Concord power sleeps in the Old Mast (gated; M5/M6 oblique).
- Sennet's old songs hold a pre-Concord witness to "the taking" (M5/M6 fragment-source).
- The Gravecaller cell is learning from the deep dead and may wake the presence (regional clock).
- The forest-bargain is the price of the presence's sleep; the logging breaks it.

## Possible Scenes

- Earning a place at the Guides' Fire by respecting the customs — or being turned into the dark for scoffing.
- Sennet, by firelight, deciding whether the player is worthy of the old songs.
- The Gravecaller cell quietly inviting the player deeper, against Sennet's warning.
- Wren-of-the-Wood, returned from the deep wood changed, unable to say what she saw.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **"Watch" response:** none formal — the village and the wood are the watch. Breaking the customs gets the player guided *out*, or abandoned in the deep wood (a real, telegraphed threat). No militia; the danger is the wood and lost trust.
- **Social DCs:** earn hospitality/trust DC 13 (respect the customs, do a service); win Brann as a guide DC 13; earn Sennet's old songs DC 16 (DC 13 with deep earned trust); learn the forest-rules DC 10 (freely taught to the respectful).
- **Rest/recovery:** safe hearth-rest for an honored guest; folk healing; the Mark-Stone for rites. **The deepest safe haven before the Old Mast.**
- **Solo danger:** low *in the village*; getting here and going further is the danger. Respect is the key to safety.

## Consequences If Ignored

If Coldhearth and its warnings are ignored, the Ledger's logging breaks the forest-bargain, the Gravecaller cell goes deeper and wakes the presence (regional clock advances toward the Old Mast stirring), the young guides are lost, and the deep wood turns actively hostile — sealing the deep Tollwood and endangering the East Road. The wood's oldest knowledge (and its oblique M5/M6 corroboration) passes to the reckless Gravecallers instead of the player.
