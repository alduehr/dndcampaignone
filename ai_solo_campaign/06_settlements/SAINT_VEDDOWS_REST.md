## AI Use

Load when the player visits the Vale's famous pilgrimage shrine-town, follows the relic / older-Concord thread, or approaches the **Saint Veddow's Tomb** dungeon (the Vale's keystone site; M2/M6 fragments). A small devout town built around a hill-tomb; the clearest place in Ring 1 to see what the Concord *built*. Half a day's climb above Orchardmere.

## One-Sentence Identity

A devout little pilgrim-town wrapped around the oldest standing Threshold shrine in the region — a "saint's tomb" that is really a capped Concord shrine, and the deepest secret outside the Reach.

## First Impression

The pilgrim-track switchbacks up a green hill crowned by an old, beautiful shrine of pale stone — far older and finer than anything in the Reach. Bells, incense, and the murmur of crowds. The town below is small but rich on pilgrim coin: hostels, candle-sellers, relic-stalls hawking "saint's bones" and blessed earth, and lines of the faithful climbing to lay flowers at Saint Veddow's Rest. The faithful come here because, they say, *the dead always rest easy at Saint Veddow's.* Lately the keepers have grown tight-lipped, and the lines a little shorter.

## Public Overview

Saint Veddow's Rest venerates a Frontier-Age holy woman said to have "settled the restless dead with a touch" — the most beloved death-saint of the Vale. Her hill-tomb is the oldest intact Threshold shrine for a hundred miles and the Vale's great pilgrimage. The **Mourners' Circle** keeps the shrine; a resident **Ashen Warden** chapter (unusual — Wardens usually wander) guards the inner tomb and aids the pilgrims; relic-sellers and hostels live off the trade. It is a place of real comfort and real money, both now under strain.

## DM-Only Truth

**Saint Veddow's Rest is a major Concord shrine — the largest harvest installation outside the Reach — capped, sanctified, and disguised as a saint's tomb after the Quietfall.** "Saint Veddow" is a composite legend grown over the cap. The resident Ashen Wardens are here for the same reason the Reach Wardens exist: **they unknowingly guard a seal** (the resident chapter's senior keeper, like Othetha, holds a fragment of the truth). The inner tomb (the dungeon) descends into genuine Concord shrine-works — a *secondary node*, dormant but beginning to warm as the keystone reawakens. The "saint's relics" sold below are Remembrance relics quarried from the shrine's outer chambers (M3). The Concord Remnant's scholar-pilgrims are here to get *into* the inner tomb (M2/M6). **This is the clearest Ring 1 place to learn what the Concord built — but it must yield M2/M6 fragments only, R5/R6 gated to Act 3, and must never name the Hollow Court.** See `../10_dungeons_and_ruins/SAINT_VEDDOWS_TOMB.md`.

## Local Leadership

**Mother Sennet**, the shrine-keeper (a Mourners' elder of great public warmth and private dread), governs the shrine and town by sacred authority. The resident Warden **Sister Adwen** holds independent authority over the inner tomb's seal and answers to the Ashen Wardens, not the town.

## Important NPCs

- **Mother Sennet** (Mourners; secondary) — shrine-keeper; publicly serene, privately knows the rests are failing and the relics are wrong.
- **Warden Sister Adwen** (Ashen Wardens; secondary) — resident seal-keeper; holds a fragment of the Wardens' true purpose (Othetha-parallel).
- **Sub-Lector Oneth Vael** (Concord Remnant; secondary) — scholar-pilgrim angling for inner-tomb access; Briss-linked.
- **Relic-monger Coyle Tamm** (minor) — sells "saint's relics"; sources them from Pell and grave-diggers.
- **Hosteler Wenna Frey** (minor) — hostel-keeper; the town's confessor-by-accident.

See `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `../08_npcs/MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Mourners' Circle (shrine-keepers), Ashen Wardens (resident chapter — the only one in Ring 1), Concord Remnant (scholar-pilgrims pressing for tomb access), Cinder Ledger (relic-buying through Pell from Orchardmere). Gravecallers are not here yet but covet the place above all others in the Vale.

## Shops and Services

- **Pilgrim hostels** — lodging from 2 sp (dormitory) to 8 sp (fine); ample, safe.
- **Relic Row** — candles, blessed earth, "saint's relics" (mostly fakes; some real Remembrance relics — M3); pilgrim supplies.
- The **Warden House** offers true rite-aid and minor healing magic (the Vale's best non-Caradril healing) and sanctuary.
- The shrine offers funeral rites and grief-blessing; no resurrection (and lately, no guarantee the rite will hold).

## Laws and Customs

- Sacred law: the shrine-keeper's word is final on the hill; the inner tomb is forbidden to all but the Wardens (trespass is sacrilege).
- Custom: pilgrims climb barefoot the last furlong; lay a flower, not coin, at the saint's altar; speak no ill of the dead on the hill.
- Relic-selling is *technically* frowned on but enormously profitable and quietly tolerated — a hypocrisy a sharp player will notice.

## Current Tensions

- **The failing rests** — pilgrims whose dead "rested easy at Saint Veddow's" are coming back wrong; the keepers hide it; the town's whole promise is collapsing.
- **Tomb access** — the Remnant's Oneth Vael pressing Mother Sennet and Sister Adwen for the inner tomb; Adwen refusing on instinct she can't fully explain.
- **Relic-fraud / blasphemy** — real Remembrance relics sold as saint's-bones; Mother Combe in Orchardmere suspects; the Mourners may turn on their own.
- **Crowd-fear** — a public failure on the hill could panic a pilgrim crowd (a real hazard).

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The inner tomb is a capped Concord shrine / secondary node (M2/M6; dungeon).
- Sister Adwen unknowingly guards a seal and holds a Warden truth-fragment (M6 path; conditional).
- The "saint's relics" are Remembrance relics (M3 — direct, commercial).
- Mother Sennet keeps a lower cell of pilgrims whose rest failed (M1/M5 Vale echo).

## Quest Hooks

- **"The Saint Who Weeps"** (AV-H8) — investigate the shrine's failing rests for a frightened pilgrim or Mother Sennet (M1/M5/M2 fragment).
- **"The Sealed Door"** (AV-H9) — Oneth Vael (or the player's own curiosity) seeks the inner tomb; Adwen resists (leads to the dungeon; M2/M6 fragments).
- **"Saint's Bones"** (AV-H3 continuation) — trace the relic-fraud to Relic Row (M3).

See `../09_quests/regional_quests/Q_THE_SEALED_DOOR.md` and `Q_SAINTS_BONES.md`.

## Possible Scenes

- A pilgrim's "rested" father rising from the lower cells; a keeper begging the player's silence and help.
- Oneth Vael and Sister Adwen, courteous and immovable, in a quiet war over a sealed door — and the player's choice whose side to take.
- A relic-stall sale where the "saint's bone" in the player's hand shows them a stranger's dying face (M3, experiential).
- A pilgrim crowd on the hill, one failure away from a stampede.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch response:** the shrine has no real guard but the resident Wardens; sacrilege (forcing the tomb) draws the Wardens (**Priest/Veteran-like**, AC 13–17, ~22–58 HP) and the wrath of a pilgrim crowd, not a militia. Adwen prefers to *talk a trespasser out* — she's guarding, not hunting.
- **Social DCs:** get Mother Sennet to admit the rests are failing DC 15; persuade Adwen to discuss the tomb DC 16 (DC 12 if the player has earned deep trust / shown the seal matters); expose the relic-fraud DC 13; calm a panicking pilgrim crowd DC 15.
- **Rest/recovery:** the Warden House (safe rest + minor healing + true rite-aid; the Vale's best); pilgrim hostels (safe rest).
- **Solo danger:** low in town, **moderate-to-high in the inner tomb** (the dungeon) — telegraphed and gated.

## Consequences If Ignored

If ignored, the failing rests at Saint Veddow's worsen and can no longer be hidden; the pilgrim trade collapses, beggaring the Vale and radicalizing it; the Remnant eventually buys, bribes, or forces inner-tomb access and begins quietly working the secondary node (advancing the Reclamation clock at a Vale site); Gravecaller recruiters arrive to "free the saint." Sister Adwen, unsupported, may break her silence — or be removed.
