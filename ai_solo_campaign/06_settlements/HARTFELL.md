## AI Use

Load when the player reaches the Tollwood's hub — the stockaded road-town on the East Road, the only true town in the forest and the staging point for everything deeper. Services, the Ledger's logging operation, the road-watch, and guides into the wood. ~4 days east of Kettle Bridge on the East Road; ~5–6 days west of Caradril.

## One-Sentence Identity

A stockaded timber road-town that lives off the Caradril trade and the toll, keeps its back to the deep wood, and never quite admits how afraid of the trees it is.

## First Impression

The East Road runs out of the trees into a clearing, and there's Hartfell: a sprawl of timber halls and sawpits inside a log stockade, woodsmoke and the scream of saws, hides and charcoal and stacked timber bound for the city. Hounds, hard men, and laden carts. It feels almost safe after the road — until you notice the stockade faces *inward*, toward the trees, and the east gate is barred and offering-marked, and nobody goes out it after dark.

## Public Overview

Hartfell is the Tollwood's only town — the place the East Road trade pools, where timber and charcoal are weighed and the city's goods are sold on to the wood. The **Cinder Ledger** runs the big sawmill, the timber-weigh, and the credit; the **Reachward Compact** keeps a road-watch that mostly guards the trade; a **Mourners' Circle** chapter keeps the old forest-customs alongside the road-faith. It's prosperous, rough, and pragmatic — and it survives by respecting the wood's rules even as the Ledger profits by breaking them.

## DM-Only Truth

Hartfell sits on the **fault-line of the Tollwood's master tension**: the Ledger's logging pushes deeper toward the Old Mast every season, and Hartfell's prosperity depends on it — but the deeper they cut, the more the deep wood stirs, and Hartfell's leaders quietly know it. Factor **Hesk Dorr** drives the cutting and suppresses the woodfolk's warnings (a Reke-light figure of *commercial*, not conspiratorial, denial — he's greedy, not a Court agent). The town's barred, offering-marked east gate is the tell: Hartfell *keeps the old rules in private while selling them out in public.* A Gravecaller contact passes through Hartfell to the deep cells. **Cap reveals at R1; the deep wood's secret is gated to the Old Mast.**

## Local Leadership

No single ruler; Hartfell is run by a rough partnership of the **Ledger's Factor Hesk Dorr** (who holds the purse) and the **Compact's Road-Warden Captain Brannoch Vey** (who holds the gate and the watch). The Mourners' forest-elder commands moral authority on all matters of the wood. The three do not entirely trust one another.

## Important NPCs

- **Factor Hesk Dorr** (Ledger; secondary) — drives the logging; suppresses the warnings; greedy, not malign.
- **Road-Warden Captain Brannoch Vey** (Compact; secondary) — keeps the road and the gate; secretly heeds the wood's rules.
- **Innkeep Marrec Tull** (minor) — the Antlers; hires out the wood's best guides.
- **Goodwife Edda Sorrel** (Mourners; secondary) — forest-custom keeper; the town's link to Coldhearth and the old ways.
- **Brann the Guide** (minor) — a Coldhearth woodsman who guides outsiders for coin (a Sashe-equivalent).

See `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `../08_npcs/MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Cinder Ledger (dominant; sawmill and credit), Reachward Compact (road-watch), Mourners' Circle (forest-custom; Coppice Shrine), Ashen Wardens (rare; the forest dead are hard to lay). A Gravecaller courier passes through. Concord Remnant survey-scholars stop here, curious why the toll-roads bend around the deep wood.

## Shops and Services

- **The Antlers** — lodging (5 sp), food, forest gossip, guides; the town's hub.
- **The Timber-Weigh** — Ledger banking, loans, timber/charcoal trade, road-supplies, carts and credit east to Caradril or west to Kettle Bridge.
- A smith (good axes and woodcraft gear), a fletcher, a tanner, a healer's house (folk + one minor-spell hedge-priest), guides into the wood (essential — see Coldhearth).
- The Coppice Shrine for rites and offerings; no resurrection.

## Laws and Customs

- A rough partnership-law: the Ledger fines, the road-watch (Guard/Veteran-like) jails; serious wood-crimes (breaking a toll-shrine, leading the deep dead to the road) are judged by the Mourners and dreaded more than the Compact's.
- **Forest-rules, kept in private:** offer at the toll-shrine before traveling; never cut a marked tree; never go out the east gate after dark; carry salt and an offering. Outsiders who scoff are quietly shunned.
- The east gate is barred at dusk and not opened till dawn for any price.

## Current Tensions

- **The logging push** — Dorr cutting deeper toward the Old Mast; the woodfolk and Brann's guides warning it's waking something; Dorr suppressing them.
- **The toll-war** — the Tollstone Cross bandits squeezing the road-trade; the watch too thin to clear them.
- **Old rules vs. profit** — Hartfell keeping the forest-customs in private while the Ledger sells them out.
- **The deep dead drawing nearer** — woodcutters seeing the old dead closer to the road each season.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- Dorr's logging is waking the Old Mast (regional clock; M5/M6 oblique).
- Brannoch Vey privately keeps the forest-rules he publicly enforces against (a turnable ally).
- A Gravecaller courier uses the Antlers (a thread to the deep cells; M3/M6 oblique).
- The barred east gate and offering-marks are the town admitting, in deed, what it denies in words.

- **"Toll-War"** (TW-H2) — Brannoch Vey hires the player to deal with the Tollstone Cross bandits (toll-war).
- **"A Guide into the Wood"** (TW-H3) — hire Brann and learn the forest-rules before going deep (the safety on-ramp).

## Possible Scenes

- The barred east gate at dusk, the watch refusing to open it, and something in the trees beyond.
- Dorr, all bluff cheer, dismissing a woodsman's warning while the woodsman's hands shake.
- Brann laying out the forest-rules over ale at the Antlers, deadly serious about every one.
- A logging crew that went deep coming back short a man, and saying nothing.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch response:** the road-watch (8–10, **Guard-like** AC 16 ~11 HP; Captain Vey **Veteran-like** AC 17 ~58 HP) — competent on the road, useless in the deep wood (they won't go). Crossing the Ledger brings fines and blacklist; crossing the wood-customs brings shunning and worse.
- **Ledger enforcement:** Dorr can field **3–4 logger-toughs** (**Thug/Scout-like**, AC 12–13, ~32 HP) — telegraphed, avoidable.
- **Social DCs:** hire Brann / win a guide's trust DC 13; get Vey to admit he keeps the old rules DC 14 (and turn him against Dorr DC 15 with evidence); haggle the Timber-Weigh DC 13; learn the forest-rules DC 10 (the woodfolk *want* outsiders to know).
- **Rest/recovery:** the Antlers (safe long rest); the healer's house (folk + minor magic); the Coppice Shrine for rites. Hartfell is the wood's main safe haven.
- **Solo danger:** low *in town*; the danger is everything past the east gate.

## Consequences If Ignored

If the logging push is ignored, Dorr cuts deeper, the Old Mast stirs further (regional clock advances), the deep dead spread toward the road, and Brann's guides stop working the east wood entirely — making the deep Tollwood and the Old Mast more dangerous and the East Road to Caradril less safe. The toll-war, unaddressed, chokes the road-trade and impoverishes Hartfell, strengthening the bandits and the sheltered Gravecaller cells.
