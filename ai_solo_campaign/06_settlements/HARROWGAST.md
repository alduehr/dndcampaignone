## AI Use

Load when the player travels north into the Sunder Heights, follows the Concord-mine / salvage / Remnant threads, or needs the region's roughest, highest, most dangerous settlement. Harrowgast is a half-ruined Concord mining town clinging to the Heights; ~2.5 days north of Hollowmere, the staging point for the Heights ruins. Level range trends higher (2–5).

## One-Sentence Identity

A cold, half-abandoned mining town built into the bones of a Concord delving in the Sunder Heights, where men dig the old order's tunnels for salvage and pretend not to hear what's deeper down.

## First Impression

The Concord road climbs out of the fens into bare grey highland, and Harrowgast appears clinging to a scree slope — slate roofs, spoil-heaps, and the black square mouths of old adits cut into the mountain by hands long dead. Half the town is ruins the living have re-roofed; the other half is ruins no one will touch. Wind never stops here. The folk are lean, soot-marked, and superstitious about the deep tunnels, where they say the rock "remembers." It is the only place in the Reach where the Concord's old works are not drowned but *open*.

## Public Overview

Harrowgast was a Concord mining-delving before the Quietfall and a salvage-town after. Its people dig the abandoned upper tunnels for Concord stone, metal, and "old glass" — which the **Cinder Ledger** buys eagerly at a Heights weigh-post. The **Reachward Compact** barely reaches here; a single overseer keeps nominal order. **Concord Remnant** salvage-crews have begun arriving, paying better than the Ledger and asking strange questions about how deep the tunnels go. It is poor, hard, and lawless at the edges, and the cold makes everything worse.

## DM-Only Truth

Harrowgast sits atop a **secondary Concord node** — not a great shrine like Hollowmere's, but a deep harvest-adit (the **Deep Adit**; see dungeon) that the miners have unknowingly been digging *toward* for years. The deepest tunnels are where the "old glass" (raw Remembrance relics) comes from, and where the rock "remembers" because leaked Remembrance saturates it. The **Concord Remnant** has worked this out and is racing to reach the Deep Adit before the Ledger or anyone else — this is the clearest place in the Reach to witness the Reclamation clock in motion (M6/M7 foreshadow; Quorrin's agents). Miners who go too deep come back "thin."

## Local Leadership

**Overseer Brack** of the Reachward Compact — a hard, pragmatic former miner who keeps order with his fists and a short rope, and who cares more about the next salvage-quota than about ghost-stories. He is not corrupt, just overwhelmed and out of his depth.

## Important NPCs

- **Overseer Brack** (Compact; secondary) — pragmatic, fist-first town boss.
- **Ledger buyer Nessa Coyle** (Cinder Ledger; secondary) — runs the Heights weigh-post; competing with the Remnant for "old glass."
- **Dig-boss Hennet** (secondary) — leads the digging crews; terrified of the deep tunnels and hiding it.
- **A Remnant salvage-master** (secondary, see SECONDARY_NPCS — **Reclaimer-agent Veska Dunn**) — a Quorrin agent racing for the Deep Adit.
- **Old "Thin" Coll** (minor) — a miner who went too deep and came back wrong; living warning.

See `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Cinder Ledger (weigh-post), Reachward Compact (thin overseer rule), Concord Remnant (salvage-crews, growing — the live presence here), Ashen Wardens (rarely; the Heights dead are bad). No Mourners' house (too remote); no Gravecaller cell yet, though the "thin" returnees are drawing whispers.

## Shops and Services

- **The Cold Drum** — rough tavern and bunkhouse (4 sp/night, watered ale, hot stew, knife-fights).
- The Ledger weigh-post (buys salvage; sells tools, rope, lamp-oil, cold-weather gear at a markup).
- A blacksmith-toolwright; no temple, no healer beyond a bonesetter; mine-tools and climbing gear (the best in the Reach).
- Guides into the upper adits for hire (dangerous, mercenary).

## Laws and Customs

- Compact law in name; in practice Brack's word and the miners' code. Theft from a dig is punished by the rope.
- Custom: never whistle in the deep tunnels; leave a coin at the Deep Mouth; never speak the name of a miner who went "thin."
- Outsiders are tolerated if they can work or pay; despised if they ask too many questions about the deep tunnels.

## Current Tensions

- **Ledger vs. Remnant** — a bidding and shoving war over "old glass" and dig-access (the live faction conflict here).
- **The deepening dread** — miners refusing to dig past a certain level; Hennet losing his crews.
- **The "thin" returnees** — men who came back wrong from the deep; the town's denial cracking.
- **Brack's overreach** — the overseer ruling harder as fear rises, risking a revolt.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The Deep Adit (secondary Concord harvest node; dungeon; M6/M7 foreshadow).
- "Old glass" is raw Remembrance relics (M3 — a hard, direct demonstration).
- The Remnant is racing to reach the Deep Adit (Reclamation clock made visible).
- The deep rock "remembers" because it's Remembrance-saturated; the "thin" miners are early thinning evidence (M10 background).

- **"Old Glass"** — recover or investigate a salvage relic for the Ledger or Remnant (M3; a relic in hand).
- **"The Crew That Won't Dig"** — find out what Hennet's miners saw in the deep (leads to the Deep Adit).
- **"The Bidding War"** — broker, sabotage, or exploit the Ledger–Remnant salvage conflict.

## Possible Scenes

- A shoving match at the weigh-post between Coyle's and the Remnant's men over a crate of "old glass."
- "Thin" Coll in the Cold Drum, repeating the same half-sentence, cold to the touch.
- Dig-boss Hennet begging the player to go where his crews won't.
- A Remnant salvage-master quietly offering the player good coin to map the deep tunnels.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch response:** Brack and **3–4 miner-toughs** (**Thug-like**, AC 11, ~32 HP) enforce order brutally but not lethally unless pushed.
- **Faction muscle:** the Remnant crews include **1–2 hired bravos** (**Scout/Thug-like**, AC 13–14, ~16–30 HP) protecting their dig-claim.
- **Social DCs:** get dig-access past the fear-line DC 15; broker the Ledger–Remnant dispute DC 18; pry the truth from Hennet DC 13; learn what "thin" means from Coll DC 12 (Insight — Coll can barely speak).
- **Rest/recovery:** the Cold Drum (safe but rough long rest); no magical healing — wounds carry north of here, so the player should arrive supplied.
- **Solo danger:** moderate (level 2–5). The town is rough; the Deep Adit and the Heights wilds are genuinely dangerous and telegraphed as such. This is the "go north when you're ready" frontier.

## Consequences If Ignored

If the player never comes north, the Remnant reaches the Deep Adit first (advancing the Reclamation clock and giving Quorrin direct relic-supply), the "old glass" trade quietly arms multiple factions with Remembrance relics, and Harrowgast either revolts against Brack or empties as the deep dread spreads. The Deep Adit's leakage feeds the regional thinning. A later return finds the town worse.
