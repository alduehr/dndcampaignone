## AI Use

Load when the player travels the East Road between Kettle Bridge and Hartfell and reaches the contested toll-hamlet at a ruined Concord toll-station — the heart of the Tollwood's toll-war. A bandit-run chokepoint that's also a useful, dangerous waypoint and the entrance to the Greenward Toll-Station dungeon. ~2 days east of Kettle Bridge.

## One-Sentence Identity

A ramshackle road-hamlet squatting in a ruined Concord toll-station, where the East Road bandits levy their "toll" and everyone — travelers, the Ledger, the woodfolk — pays one way or another.

## First Impression

The East Road runs up to a great broken arch — a ruined Concord toll-station, half-swallowed by the wood — and through it, where the causeway pinches between bog and bramble. A clutter of lean-tos, a barred gate of lashed timber, a chain across the road, and hard-eyed folk with bows in the ruin above. A painted board reads the toll. Behind the bandit-front, an actual little community scrapes by: this is the only stop on three days of road, and they know it.

## Public Overview

Tollstone Cross grew up around an old Concord toll-station at the East Road's worst chokepoint. For a generation it's been run by the **road-bandits** — the **Tollmen** — who levy a "toll" on every cart and call it protection (which, against the wood and rival bandits, it half is). The **Compact** can't dislodge them and the **Ledger** finds it cheaper to pay than fight, so the toll endures. A handful of honest folk — a smith, an ale-wife, a Mourner — live alongside the Tollmen because there's nowhere else for three days of forest.

## DM-Only Truth

The Tollmen are **more sympathetic and more divided than they look.** Their boss, **Toll-Boss Cady Renn**, is a deserter from a failed Compact road-watch who turned to the toll when the Compact abandoned the road — she runs a hard but real protection racket and keeps the worse predators (and the deep dead) off the road. Her lieutenant **Skell** wants to escalate to outright robbery and murder; Renn is one bad season from losing control to him. The ruined toll-station below the hamlet (the dungeon) is a minor Concord road-node — drowned and leaking faintly (an M2/M5 *fragment* echoing the Reach's Sunken Tollhouse) — which is why the dead drift unusually near this stretch. **Cap reveals at R1; the node is a fragment-site, not the keystone.**

## Local Leadership

**Toll-Boss Cady Renn** rules by force and a grudging fairness; her authority is fraying as her lieutenant **Skell** agitates for blood. The honest residents have no power but the Tollmen's tolerance.

## Important NPCs

- **Toll-Boss Cady Renn** (secondary) — bandit leader; ex-road-watch; hard but not cruel; turnable.
- **Skell** (secondary) — Renn's lieutenant; wants murder and plunder; the real threat.
- **Smith Doune** (minor) — honest resident; mends gear; quietly hates the toll.
- **Ale-wife Pell** (minor; naming note: a distinct "Pell" from Saltmargin/Vale — common frontier surname) — runs the road's only ale; knows everyone's business.

See `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `../08_npcs/MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

The Tollmen (local bandit faction — a minor regional group, not a major faction; registered), Reachward Compact (would clear them but can't), Cinder Ledger (pays the toll, resents it). The deep wood presses on all sides.

## Shops and Services

- The **Honest Corner** offers a hot meal, ale, and a smith's repair (for coin, on top of the toll).
- Rough shelter (pay the Tollmen); no inn proper; a Mourner for rites.
- The Tollmen sell *passage* and rough "protection" — and information on the road ahead.
- No banking, no healing magic.

## Laws and Customs

- The Tollmen's law: pay the toll, don't cross the bandits, don't go down into the Drowned Vault (they're afraid of it). Renn keeps a rough order; Skell would not.
- The honest residents keep their heads down and the Mourner keeps the road's dead.
- Custom (real): leave a coin at the Broken Arch for "the road" — half superstition, half Tollman-tax.

## Current Tensions

- **The toll-war** — the Compact wants the Tollmen gone; the Ledger wants the road cheap; Hartfell wants the trade flowing; the Tollmen want to survive.
- **Renn vs. Skell** — the boss losing her grip to a lieutenant who wants blood (a powder-keg the player can tip either way).
- **The leaking node** — the dead drifting near this stretch; the Tollmen blame the wood and fear the Drowned Vault.
- **The honest residents** — caught between the bandits and the wood, with nowhere to go.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- Renn is ex-road-watch and keeps the worse dangers off the road (she's a grey ally, not a simple villain).
- Skell is plotting to overthrow Renn and turn the toll into outright murder.
- The Drowned Vault is a leaking minor Concord road-node (M2/M5 fragment; the local dead-drift's cause).
- The Tollmen are afraid of the vault — exploitable leverage.

## Possible Scenes

- The chain across the road, Renn naming a toll, Skell itching behind her for an excuse.
- A standoff the player can defuse, pay, fight, or flip — and the Tollmen splitting on which way to jump.
- The cold drift of the dead near the arch at dusk, and the Tollmen's fear of the stair below.
- Renn, alone, admitting she'd quit the toll for a way back to honest work.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Bandit response:** the Tollmen are **6–10 bandits** (**Bandit/Scout-like**, AC 12–13, ~11–16 HP), Renn a **Veteran/Spy-like** leader (AC 15, ~45 HP), Skell a **Thug/Berserker-like** (AC 13, ~40 HP). They prefer the toll to a fight and break on morale — *unless Skell is in charge*, when they turn lethal. Telegraphed; the toll is the default, not an ambush.
- **Social DCs:** haggle/refuse the toll DC 13; turn Renn against the racket DC 15 (DC 12 with an honest-work offer); expose/exploit Skell's plot DC 14; learn the Drowned Vault's secret DC 13.
- **Rest/recovery:** rough, paid shelter (uneasy); the Honest Corner for food/repair. **Not** a safe long-rest by default (the Tollmen, the wood) — Hartfell or Kettle Bridge are the real havens.
- **Solo danger:** moderate — a real bandit chokepoint, but heavily weighted toward social/negotiated outcomes; lethal mainly if Skell takes over or the player forces a fight.

## Consequences If Ignored

If the toll-war is ignored, Skell eventually ousts Renn and the Tollmen turn from a toll-racket into murderous road-pirates, choking the East Road to Caradril and the wood's trade; the leaking Drowned Vault draws more dead to the stretch; and Hartfell, starved of trade, weakens. If Renn is supported, the road stabilizes; if the player simply kills the Tollmen, the road is briefly open but the wood (and rival bandits) fill the vacuum.
