## AI Use

Load when the player needs a safe waystation on the Concord roads, seeks the Ashen Wardens directly, or wants a healing/resupply/sanctuary point in the wilderness between towns. The Ashwalk Rest is a fortified Warden waystation at the crossroads of the central Concord road; ~1 day from Hollowmere, roughly central to the road network. It is the Reach's primary solo-safety anchor outside the towns.

## One-Sentence Identity

A fortified Ashen Warden waystation at the great Concord crossroads, the one reliably safe walls-and-hearth in the open Reach, where the rite-keepers rest, resupply, and lay the road's dead.

## First Impression

Where four Concord causeways meet on a low rise stands the Rest: a squat, ash-grey hall of old Concord stone, re-roofed and walled with a turf rampart, a star carved deep over the gate and a perpetual thin smoke of ritual ash. Travelers shelter in the yard. A few Wardens come and go in their grey cloaks. Inside it is warm, spare, and quiet, smelling of woodsmoke and the bitter herbs the Wardens burn. After the fens, it feels like the first place in days you can put your back to a wall.

## Public Overview

The Ashwalk Rest is the Ashen Wardens' main waystation in the Reach — not a town, but a crossroads sanctuary maintained by the order for centuries. Wardens base out of it; travelers shelter under its protection; the failing-rites crisis has it busier than it has ever been, a constant churn of grey cloaks heading out to answer calls and stumbling back exhausted. A **Mourners' Circle** keeper shares the hall. It charges nothing but accepts labor, food, and donations. It is the safest roof between any two Reach towns.

## DM-Only Truth

The Rest is built on the **central crossing of the Concord road-network** for a reason the Wardens no longer remember: the roads were harvest-conduits, and this junction was a relay. The Rest's deep cellar holds the order's Reach archive — including, in a locked rite-room only Othetha may open, fragments that hint at the **seal and the true purpose** (a late, trust-gated path to M6/M9 / Secret 8, gentler than Caradril's archives). Warden Pell is often here drinking and brooding. The Rest is where Othetha will, if the Wardens Break clock advances far enough, finally break her oath.

## Local Leadership

**Hospitaller Warden Sirrin** runs the Rest day-to-day — a stout, motherly, no-nonsense Warden who manages the hall, the stores, and the endless flow of exhausted brethren. **Warden Sister Othetha** (major NPC) uses the Rest as her base when in the Reach; **Warden Brother Hale** and **Warden Pell** (secondary) pass through constantly.

## Factions Present

Ashen Wardens (home base), Mourners' Circle (shared keeper). The Wardens keep Gravecallers and Ledger relic-buyers off the premises. The Compact respects the Rest's neutrality.

## Shops and Services

- **Sanctuary** — safe lodging in the yard or hall (free / donation); the most reliable safe long rest in the open Reach.
- **Rite-aid** — the Wardens will help with a failed rite, lend rite-salt, and bless the road ahead.
- **Healing** — Warden folk-medicine and minor divine healing (Priest-like; *Cure Wounds, Lesser Restoration*) — a genuine recovery point.
- Basic resupply (salt, herbs, trail food, lamp-oil); no weapons or armor.
- A Warden guide or warning before dangerous stretches of road (solo-safety).

## Laws and Customs

- The Rest's rule: no violence within the rampart, no relic-trafficking, the dead come first.
- Custom: leave the hall as you found it; burn a pinch of ash for the road-dead before you leave.
- All are sheltered regardless of faction — even a Gravecaller may sleep in the yard, unarmed and watched.

## Current Tensions

- **Overload** — the Rest is overwhelmed; Wardens are breaking; Sirrin is rationing aid.
- **Pell's bitterness** — the veteran openly questions the order's "don't ask why," unsettling younger Wardens.
- **Refugee strain** — fen families sheltering in the yard, straining stores.
- **Othetha's dread** — the senior Warden is quietly alarmed and watchful of any newcomer asking about the shrine.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The Rest sits on a Concord road-relay (M2 background).
- Othetha will break her oath here if the Wardens Break clock peaks.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch response:** the Wardens are noncombatant-leaning but not helpless — Sirrin and 2–3 Wardens use **Priest/Acolyte-like** profiles (AC 13, ~22–33 HP, protective/healing magic). They expel troublemakers rather than fight.
- **Social DCs:** earn Sirrin's resupply-trust DC 10; get Pell to share his suspicions DC 12; reach the locked archive — **gated, Othetha only**, trust-gated and act-gated (Act 3; do not open early).
- **Rest/recovery:** the Reach's best non-town safe rest and minor magical healing. This is the intended solo-safety pillar of the open region.
- **Solo danger:** none within the walls. The Rest is a sanctuary by design.

## Consequences If Ignored

The Rest cannot fail in the way a town can, but if the player never engages the Wardens here, the Wardens Break clock advances unopposed — Sirrin's people keep dying of exhaustion, the Rest's protection over the roads thins, and the open Reach grows more dangerous to travel. If the clock peaks unaided, the Rest empties and the roads go un-tended, isolating the towns.
