## AI Use

The starting settlement and Act 1 hub. Load every session set in or near Hollowmere. This is the player's home base. Minor NPCs and supporting locals now live in `08_npcs/MINOR_NPCS.md` and `SECONDARY_NPCS.md`; regional hooks and rumors in `09_quests/hooks_and_rumors/SUNDERING_REACH_HOOKS.md` and `SUNDERING_REACH_RUMORS.md`.

## One-Sentence Identity

A grey, close-knit frontier town built on the rim of a drowned ruin, where the failing of the dead has just stopped being a rumor and started being personal.

## First Impression

Hollowmere rings a wide hollow of dark, still water. Stilt-houses and peat-smoke crowd the firm ground; planked walkways cross the boggy edges; and out in the flooded basin, when the fog thins, you can see broken grey shapes under the surface — the tops of something old and enormous. The town smells of peat, salt, and cold water. People are kind but watchful, and every doorway has salt at the sill and a small star carved above it.

## Public Overview

Founded ~AQ 12, Hollowmere grew because the basin's edge offered firm ground, peat to cut, fish to net, and ruins to scavenge. It became the Reach's largest town and the seat of the **Reachward Compact**. Its people are farmers, peat-cutters, fishers, coopers, and salvagers. The town is governed by **Reeve Marda Coalmont** and the Compact council, served by the **Mourners' Circle** at its cemetery and visited by **Ashen Wardens** when rites fail. The **Cinder Ledger** keeps a counting-house here. Lately the town is frightened: rites are failing, and now one of their own — the midwife Wren — has come back wrong.

## DM-Only Truth

Hollowmere sits directly atop the **drowned shrine**, the keystone of the Concord's harvest, beneath which the **Hollow Court** sleeps. The "broken grey shapes" in the basin are the shrine's upper structure. The **basin-drainage scheme** Councilor Reke is pushing is secretly an effort to expose and reopen it. The town is, unknowingly, the front line of the whole campaign. See `../03_canon/DM_ONLY_CANON.md`.

## Local Leadership

**Reeve Marda Coalmont** (honest) and the Compact council, including **Councilor Aldous Reke** (secret Court agent). See `../08_npcs/MAJOR_NPCS.md`.

## Important NPCs

Sefra Quick (fixer/first contact), Halla Coalmont (innkeeper/hub), Grandmother Wend & Iola (Mourners), Othetha & Hale (Wardens), Domic Sael (Ledger), Coalmont & Reke (Compact), Wren & Tomas Brevin (the opening crisis), Old Sashe (fen-guide, at the edge of town). Full profiles in `MAJOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

All major Reach factions have a face here (see region file table). The Gravecallers operate hidden; the Hollow Court only through Reke and the steering of Sefra.

## Shops and Services

- The Drowned Lantern (lodging, food, rumors).
- The Counting-House (loans, salvage-buying, hired work).
- A general chandler, a cooper (Tomas Brevin), a fishmonger, a peat-merchant, a hedge-healer, the Mourners' Green for rites. See `08_npcs/MINOR_NPCS.md` for named locals (Pim Ostry, Goodwife Harl, Edda Cole, Sexton Marrow, Drell, and others).

## Laws and Customs

- Compact law: no grave-robbing, no Gravecaller worship (outlawed), watch keeps order.
- Customs: salt at the sill, a star at the door, mirrors covered in a death-house, the dead "named once and released."
- Outsiders are tolerated and quickly put to work; trust is earned by how one treats the dead.

## Current Tensions

- **Wren's return** — the opening crisis; the town is split between pity, fear, and demands to "deal with it."
- **The basin-drainage scheme** — Reke and the Ledger push it; the Mourners' Circle is furious; Coalmont is uneasy.
- **The failing rites** — every funeral is now a gamble.
- **Quiet Gravecaller recruitment** among the grieving.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The drowned shrine and the Hollow Court beneath the basin.
- Reke's true allegiance and the real purpose of the drainage scheme.
- Sefra was hired to steer the player.
- The Ledger vault holds a relic that "remembers" the harvest.
- Tomas has secretly sought the Gravecallers.
- Wend's old songs encode the truth.

## Possible Scenes

- A funeral on Mourner's Green that fails before the player's eyes.
- Sefra offering "an easy job: just go talk to the Brevins."
- A tense council meeting over the basin scheme.
- Wren standing in her doorway at dusk, calling Tomas's name.
- A Gravecaller's knock at a grieving family's door.
- Old Sashe warning, at the basin shore, that "the dead are walking the wrong way — toward the water."

## Consequences If Ignored

If the player leaves Wren's crisis unresolved, the town fractures: a mob forms, or Tomas turns to the Gravecallers, or the Wardens are forced to act, and the basin clock keeps ticking regardless. Hollowmere can curdle from a warm base into a frightened, hostile town. See `../02_runtime_state/WORLD_CLOCKS.md`.
