## AI Use

Load when the player descends beneath Caradril: the flooded lower Concord level under the city, a dungeon-style sub-district and the city's M2/M3 *cousin-site* (a minor, dormant relic-level — NOT the keystone). The exploration/danger zone of the city, levels 6–14, gated behind the Sealgate (the Sill) or the Remnant's secret way (Lantern Reach). Use dungeon mechanical standards. **Guardrail:** the Wards faintly touch the Court's network but must never *name* the Hollow Court or reveal the apex truth — give fragments and unease, not the answer.

## One-Sentence Identity

The drowned older city beneath Caradril — a quiet, dormant Concord relic-level the Remnant has secretly mapped and the city has forgotten it is standing on.

## What The Player Notices First

Down the Sealgate's worn stair, the trade-noise fades into dripping dark. The Sunken Wards are the bones of an older Caradril: Concord stonework, half-flooded halls, sealed bronze doors, channels of still black water reflecting a lantern back at you. The air is cold and patient. Star-marks and Concord Script run along the lintels. It is *quiet* — not dead, but waiting, the way the Reach's leaking nodes are loud and these are merely... listening.

## What Is Actually Going On (DM-Only)

The Sunken Wards are a **minor, long-dormant cousin of the Reach's harvest-network nodes** — a lower Concord level that was part of the same continental system but was never a major node, and has lain still through the Stilling. As the master harvest reawakens far off in the Reach, the Wards **faintly warm**: more still-water "listening," a few drifting Remembrances, Concord Script that now reads as part of the network (C-M2/C-M6 fragments — *fragments*, not the apex). **The Concord Remnant has secretly mapped them** from the Lantern Reach and uses a hidden back-way (and the Sealed Archive connects to the upper Wards). The Wards offer: readable Concord Script in situ (M2/M6 fragments, language-gated), a dormant relic or two (M3 demonstration), and a *back route* into the Remnant's Sealed Archive (an alternative to a front-door break-in). They are dangerous but **not** lethal-by-ambush: hazards, a few telegraphed Remembrance-apparitions, flood-risk, and thin-touch — with constant retreat to the Sill above. This is a city-scaled exploration site, deliberately a *smaller echo* of the Reach's Deep Adit, never the keystone.

## Hazards

- **Flooded passages / cold water:** Athletics/Con DC 13 to cross flooded halls; failure = exhaustion risk, dropped gear, or a forced retreat. Some halls flood on a slow cycle (a telegraphed, escapable rising-water hazard, DC 14 to read the cycle).
- **Collapsing Concord stone:** Perception DC 13 to spot; Dex save DC 14 or 3d6 bludgeoning + a blocked path (reroutable).
- **Thin-touch zones:** near a warming relic or apparition, brief over-exposure to raw Remembrance imposes **thin-touch** (reversible: disadvantage on Death saves until a true rite / shrine long-rest — the Stage-3 custom mechanic). Telegraphed by cold, whispering, lantern-dimming. Never a cheap kill.
- **Drowning-dark / getting lost:** without a guide (Old Pater Dunk) or the Remnant's map, Survival DC 14 to navigate; failure costs time and may wake an apparition.

## Creatures / Adversaries

- **Drifting Remembrances (apparitions):** flavor as **specter/ghost-like** (AC 12, ~22–40 HP, incorporeal, life/Remembrance drain), **telegraphed and often resolvable without combat** (a true rite, a Mourner's blessing, naming the dead, or simply withdrawing). 1–2 at a time, never a swarm. Solo-danger: moderate, scalable; built for avoidance and parley as much as fighting. (Reference the Reach encounter tables' Remembrance entries in `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md`; city-tuned variants in `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/CARADRIL_ENCOUNTERS.md`.)
- **Reclaimer guards (if the player nears the Archive back-way):** **Spy/Mage-like** Remnant agents (AC 13–14, ~40–60 HP; Hold Person, Counterspell, Remembrance one-offs) — they prefer to *turn back* an intruder, not kill; telegraphed, escalating.
- **Smugglers / Syndicate (upper Wards):** the Salt Syndicate uses the dry upper Wards for drops — **Thug/Bandit-like** (AC 13–14); avoidable, bribable, or a low-stakes fight.

## Level Range And Solo Danger

Levels 6–14 (core 8–12). Solo danger: **moderate, telegraphed, retreat-rich.** This is the city's real exploration challenge, but it is a *minor* node by design — every hazard is signposted, every apparition can be avoided or parleyed, the Sill above is always a retreat, and a guide (Dunk) or the Remnant's map removes the navigation risk. Resting: possible in dry upper chambers (risky) or by retreating to the Sill (safe). No forced descent — the Wards reward the prepared and punish only the reckless.

## NPCs Present

**Old Pater Dunk** (the Sill; the delver-guide who knows the dry routes), occasionally **Wessel Dree** or Reclaimer agents near the Archive back-way, and Syndicate runners in the upper Wards. No permanent inhabitants.

## Factions Present

Concord Remnant (secret map, back-way to the Archive, Reclaimer guards); the Hush and Salt Syndicate (upper Wards drops/routes); the Hollow Court (faint network touch only — never named).

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- A relic-level cousin of the Reach nodes; faintly warming as the master harvest wakes (C-M2 fragment — proof the network is *continental*, reinforcing M5/M2 without naming the Court).
- Readable Concord Script in situ (C-M6 fragments; language-gated; Act-paced — fragments only).
- A dormant relic or two (C-M3 demonstration).
- A back route into the Sealed Archive (an alternative to the Lantern Reach front-door — see `Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE`).
- The Remnant's secret map proves Quorrin's reach and intent (a Reclamation clue).

## Clues

- **C-M2 (city fragment):** the Wards' Script + warming "listening" show the harvest-network is continental, not just a Reach phenomenon (language-gated; fragment).
- **C-M6 (city fragment):** in-situ Concord inscriptions describing "the keeping of the dead's pattern" (Act-paced; fragments, never the apex).
- **C-M3:** a dormant relic that, studied, stores a person.
- **Reclamation evidence:** the Remnant's hidden map and back-way.

## Hooks

`Q_BELOW_THE_STILLING` (descend the Sunken Wards — explore, read the Script, or use the back-way to the Archive; levels 6–10). Plus: the Archive back-route (links `Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE`); recover a Wards relic; clear a Syndicate drop for the Watch; map the Wards for the Remnant (with strings) or against them. See `../../09_quests/hooks_and_rumors/CARADRIL_HOOKS.md`.

## Treasure / Resources

Concord salvage and 1–2 genuine Remembrance relics (M3; handle with thin-touch care); coin and goods from old Syndicate caches; a portable Concord Script rubbing (fragment clue); and — the real prize — *access*: the back-way to the Sealed Archive, worth more than gold to an investigator.

## How It Changes After Interaction

- **If the player reads/maps it:** they gain the city M2/M6 fragments and possibly the Archive back-way; the Remnant notices an intruder in "their" Wards and tightens security (Reclamation reacts).
- **If the player loots a relic:** an M3 demonstration in hand; thin-touch risk; the Ledger/Remnant may want it.
- **If the player exposes the Wards:** the city is forced to acknowledge the ruin beneath it (a civic shock; reformers gain ground; the conspiracy scrambles).
- **If ignored:** the Wards keep warming slowly; the Remnant keeps its quiet back-way; nothing is lost permanently (M2/M3/M6 reachable elsewhere).

## Consequences If Ignored

The Wards stay forgotten and the Remnant keeps its hidden advantage and Archive back-way. The faint warming continues unobserved — a missed early confirmation that the crisis is continental. No mystery dead-ends on the Wards; they are a *bonus* path and an exploration set-piece, not a required gate.

## Mechanical Summary (Dungeon Standard)

- **Recommended level:** 6–14 (core 8–12). **Solo danger:** moderate, telegraphed, retreat-rich.
- **Encounters:** 1–2 Remembrance apparitions (avoidable/parley-able), optional Reclaimer guards (turn-back, not kill), Syndicate runners (avoidable).
- **Traps/hazards:** flooded crossings (DC 13), collapsing stone (DC 13/14), rising-water cycle (DC 14 to read), thin-touch zones (reversible).
- **Resting:** dry upper chambers (risky) or retreat to the Sill (safe).
- **Boss/climax:** none fixed — the "climax" is reaching the Archive back-way or a warming relic-chamber; a Reclaimer confrontation is social/avoidable first, combat last.
- **Retreat:** always available (the Sealgate stair up to the Sill).
- **Scaling:** L6 — fewer/weaker apparitions, simpler hazards; L12+ — more apparitions, a Reclaimer ambush attempt near the Archive, a flooded lower hall (the deepest reach) with a stronger apparition guarding a better relic. Never a damage-race; always a non-combat path.
