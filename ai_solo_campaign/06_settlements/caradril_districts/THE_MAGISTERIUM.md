## AI Use

Load for Caradril government, law, charter politics, civic disputes, and any scene at Magisters' Hall or the Wardmoot. This is the district of *power and procedure* — the place to win votes, secure charters, and feel the city's complacency. Pairs with the Counting-Quays (money buys magisters) and the Lantern Reach (the conspiracy the council can't see).

## One-Sentence Identity

The serene upper-city seat of the Tidewater Council, where the dead are someone else's problem and a charter is worth more than a life.

## First Impression

Wide swept steps, clean stone, and a hush enforced by money and habit. The Magisterium sits on the high north bank: colonnaded halls, the great bronze doors of Magisters' Hall, a fountain that runs even in drought because the city can afford it. Liveried doorwards, charter-tokens on every important chest, criers reading the day's rulings. After the Reach's peat-smoke and salt-sills, it smells of beeswax and ink. Everyone here is busy being important and faintly bored.

## Public Overview

The government quarter. **Magisters' Hall** houses the Tidewater Council and its courts; the **Wardmoot** building beside it holds the (toothless) commoners' assembly. Around them cluster the charter-registry, the city treasury, the Tide-Watch's central barracks, notaries, advocates, and the townhouses of the senior Charter Houses. The **Pale Star** inn sits at the district's edge toward Lampgate — the recommended player base, neutral ground where every faction's people drink and watch one another.

## DM-Only Truth

The council is not corrupt so much as *asleep*. Its complacency is the campaign's quiet villain in this district: Caradril's leaders cannot imagine the crisis is real because the city has been safe for a century (and that safety is, secretly, the harvest's pause — see `CARADRIL.md` DM truth). **Councilor Reke corresponds with someone seated here** — the DM should treat this as an unsolved thread (a sealed letter, a name half-glimpsed, a magister who asks oddly specific questions about the Reach basin), NOT a named second Court agent. Let the player investigate it; do not pre-resolve it. The honest reformer **Sefa Dann** is the player's lever to make the council *act*; **Esren Tolm** and the old Houses are the inertia and the Ledger's rivals.

## Local Leadership

**First Magister Halloran Voss** (chair; conflict-averse), the eight other magisters of the Charter Houses, with **Sefa Dann** (reform) and **Esren Tolm** (old money) the active poles. The **Tide-Watch** under **Captain Yorell Kade** answers to the council. Full entries in `../../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` (Caradril).

## Important NPCs

Halloran Voss, Sefa Dann, Esren Tolm, Yorell Kade (secondary). Minor: Steward Maell (Pale Star), Crier Bosh (reads rulings; knows gossip), Doorward Sile (registry gatekeeper). See `../../08_npcs/MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Tidewater Council and Charter Houses (rule here); Tide-Watch (enforce here); Cinder Ledger and Concord Remnant both *lobby* here constantly (their agents haunt the Pale Star); the Hollow Court's interest runs through the unidentified correspondent.

## Shops and Services

Notaries and advocates (legal services; contracts, charters, wills), the charter-registry (records access — legitimately or not), bail and fines at the barracks, fine lodging and dining at the Pale Star, message-couriers, and access — the district's true commodity is *introductions*.

## Laws and Customs

- A charter is sacred; the council's word is law; appeals are expensive.
- The Wardmoot may petition but not compel.
- Charter-tokens worn openly; refusing a magister's summons is a serious matter.
- The upper city is heavily watched and unfriendly to obvious frontier roughness — armor and visible weapons draw the Watch (DC 12 to talk past, or a charter-sponsor waves it off).

## Current Tensions

- **An open charter:** a trade-charter is coming vacant (a House failing, a death, a scandal). The Ledger, an old House, and a reform-backed newcomer all want it — the player can broker, win, or expose the contest (`Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER`).
- **Reform vs. inertia:** Sefa Dann pushes the council to heed the Reach's warnings (and to investigate relic-trade); Tolm and the comfortable majority refuse.
- **The Reach question:** petitions and frightened letters from the Compact pile up unanswered; the council debates whether the frontier crisis is real or exaggerated.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The Reke correspondent thread (lead only; never auto-reveal a second Court agent).
- A misfiled charter-registry record quietly ties Ledger funding to "Reach water-rights/survey" works — a Caradril-side echo of M4 the player can dig up here (Investigation DC 15 in the registry, or via a turned clerk).
- Voss has privately taken Ledger money and is compromised on the relic-trade vote (a pressure point, not the conspiracy itself).

## Quest Hooks

`Q_THE_OPEN_CHARTER` (the charter contest; political fulcrum). Plus: carry a Wardmoot petition; investigate the registry record; secure a council hearing for the Reach's warnings (links to Sefa Dann and, later, to the Wardens). See `../../09_quests/hooks_and_rumors/CARADRIL_HOOKS.md`.

## Possible Scenes

- A charter-hearing where the player's testimony or evidence tips a vote.
- Sefa Dann recruiting the player over watered wine at the Wardmoot, sincere and outmatched.
- Esren Tolm "befriending" the player to use them against the Ledger.
- Captain Kade offering deputization for a discreet job the Watch can't be seen doing.
- A frightened Compact courier in the Pale Star, ignored by everyone but the player.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch:** Guard-like patrols (AC 16, ~11 HP); Veteran-like sergeants; Kade is Knight/Veteran-like (AC 18, ~90 HP, Leadership) — never a default fight; provoking him is flee/talk/bribe.
- **Social DCs:** secure a hearing DC 15 (or sponsor); sway a magister DC 17 (lower with evidence/favors stacked); read/extract a registry record DC 15; talk past the Watch about weapons DC 12.
- **Danger:** social/political. Crossing the council means charter-blocks, fines, outlawry, or a House's economic war — telegraphed, escalating, never cheap death.
- **Rest:** the Pale Star is a premier safe long-rest hub (lodging, food, a locked room, neutral ground).

## Consequences If Ignored

The council stays asleep; the relic-trade and Reach warnings go unanswered; the open charter falls to the Ledger or an old House by default, strengthening whichever the player did not check. Sefa Dann's reform stalls and she may be quietly ruined. None of this dead-ends a mystery, but it cedes the city's *politics* to the conspiracy's patrons.
