## AI Use

Load for Concord scholarship: learning Concord Script (Briss/Tamsin Orr), the Lamplighters' Hall, the Sealed Archive (the campaign's clearest written proof of the harvest — C-M6-1, C-M9), and Quorrin Vane's inner circle. This is the **knowledge gate** of the mid-game and the Act-3 truth cache. Player-safe scholarship vs. DM-only conspiracy must be kept strictly apart. Pairs with Highmourn (the clergy's covered-up failures) and the Counting-Quays (Quorrin's deal with Vyre).

## One-Sentence Identity

The river-college district of green lamps and old books, public home of the Concord Remnant — and, behind a sealed door, the place where the world's worst secret is written down.

## First Impression

The lamps here burn green — a scholars' affectation and the district's pride, lit at dusk along terraced libraries and lecture-halls above the Verdance. Students argue in the streets; copyists' fingers are stained with ink; bells call lectures. The **Lamplighters' Hall** dominates the terrace, its doors open to anyone curious about the lost Concord's "golden age." It is warm, learned, and faintly self-satisfied — a place that reveres the past without suspecting it.

## Public Overview

Caradril's scholars' quarter and the seat of the **Concord Remnant**. The **Lamplighters' Hall** is their public college (the **Lampwrights' Collegium**), where the only fluent readers of **Concord Script** teach, translate, and preach the restoration of the Concord's lost wisdom. Lectors and sub-lectors take students; relics and texts are catalogued; the curious and the credulous gather. **Lector Briss** lectures here when not in the Reach; **Archivist Quorrin Vane** is the revered head scholar. Deeper in, behind locked doors, lies the **Sealed Archive** — said to hold the Remnant's most precious recovered texts, accessible only to the inner circle.

## DM-Only Truth

The Lantern Reach is the conspiracy's *library*. The public Collegium is a sincere idealist front (Briss's world). The **Sealed Archive** holds the clearest written proof that the Concord **harvested the dead** and that the **Quietfall was deliberate** (C-M6-1, C-M9) — guarded by **Keeper-Archivist Wessel Dree**, a Reclaimer who knows exactly what it contains. **Quorrin's inner circle (the Reclaimers)** works from here, deciphering the shrine's location and negotiating with Vyre (Counting-Quays). Quorrin suspects the Hollow Court exists and is racing it. The Remnant has also secretly **mapped the Sunken Wards beneath the city** from here (their relic-cousin site). Reaching the Archive's truth is gated: the player needs Concord Script (taught by Briss/Tamsin Orr), inner-circle trust *or* a break-in, and the act-pacing guardrail (Act 3 / level ~9–11 — never surface the apex Court truth here in Act 1–2; give fragments). Disillusioning Briss with Archive proof can **shatter the Remnant** (Secret 13).

## Local Leadership

**Archivist Quorrin Vane** (major NPC; inner circle) presides over scholarship; **Lector Briss** (major; idealist public face) and **Sub-Lector Tamsin Orr** (secondary; Briss's student, a willing Script tutor) run public teaching; **Keeper-Archivist Wessel Dree** (secondary; Reclaimer) holds the Sealed Archive.

## Important NPCs

Quorrin Vane, Lector Briss (major); Tamsin Orr, Wessel Dree (secondary). Minor: Copyist Hale-of-Lampgate, Page Wenna, Binder Oslo, Lamplighter Tib. See `../../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Concord Remnant (rules; public Collegium + hidden Reclaimers); Tidewater Council (charters the college, ignores its danger); Cinder Ledger (Quorrin's secret partner via Vyre); Hollow Court (a rival the Reclaimers race, present only as Quorrin's growing suspicion).

## Shops and Services

Research and translation (the campaign's main scholarly resource), Concord Script tutelage (a downtime "reward," the key clue-gate), book and map copying, identify/lore services, scribes, and lodging for visiting scholars. The Stacks are open and safe; the Archive is not.

## Laws and Customs

- The Collegium polices its own; the Tide-Watch rarely enters.
- Scholarship is currency; a recommendation from a lector opens doors, a feud closes them.
- Custom: green lamps lit personally by lecturers; texts are "lent to the world," relics "kept for study."
- The Sealed Archive is forbidden to outsiders — entering it without leave is a serious offense *and* the Reclaimers will respond directly.

## Current Tensions

- **Idealists vs. Reclaimers** (DM): Briss's sincere scholars vs. Quorrin's hidden inner circle. The player's wedge.
- **The race to the shrine** (DM): Quorrin presses to decipher the keystone's location and beat the Court (and the player).
- **The relic question:** the Collegium debates whether buying Reach salvage is preservation or grave-robbery (echoing the city-wide tension).

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The Sealed Archive's proof (C-M6-1, C-M9) — Act 3 cache; Script + trust/break-in gated.
- The Reclaimers' true aim and Quorrin's race (M6/M7 feeder; never name the Court early).
- The Remnant's secret map of the Sunken Wards (links to that district).
- Briss is a turnable ally if shown Archive proof (Secret 13).
- Tamsin Orr is honest and can be the player's *teacher* without the inner circle's strings.

District rumor table at the Lamplighters' Hall common room — see `../../09_quests/hooks_and_rumors/CARADRIL_RUMORS.md` (Lantern Reach block).

## Quest Hooks

`Q_THE_LANTERN_AND_THE_LAMP` (learn Concord Script with Briss/Tamsin; the clue-gate on-ramp, levels 3–6), `Q_THE_SEALED_ARCHIVE` (reach the proof — trust, theft, or disillusioned-Briss, levels 9–11). Plus: translate a Reach inscription; expose a Reclaimer; protect Briss. See `../../09_quests/hooks_and_rumors/CARADRIL_HOOKS.md`.

## Possible Scenes

- Briss lighting up explaining the Concord's lost roads, then troubled when the player asks the wrong question.
- Tamsin Orr teaching Script late by green lamp, generous and unaware of the danger.
- Quorrin receiving the player in his study — courteous, reading them, offering "fascinating work."
- Wessel Dree barring the Archive stair with a polite, immovable smile.
- The Archive itself: a Concord page that says, in the player's now-readable Script, what the Concord *did* (Act 3, the floor dropping out).

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Reclaimer response:** if provoked, Quorrin sends scholar-agents — **Spy/Mage-like** (AC 12–14, ~30–55 HP; Charm Person, Hold Person, Counterspell at higher tiers; Remembrance one-offs). Quorrin himself is Tier-1 boss-class (Mage/Archmage-like) and **never a cheap ambush** — he prefers to win by knowledge and to have the player *not* enter the Archive. Telegraphed, escalating.
- **Social/skill DCs:** befriend Briss DC 12 (he's warm); learn Script — downtime, no single roll; gain inner-circle trust DC 17 (or fake it); break into the Sealed Archive DC 18–20 (multi-path: Dree's keys, a forged warrant, the Sunken Wards back-way, or disillusioned-Briss letting you in); disillusion Briss DC 15 + hard proof.
- **Danger:** mostly social/intellectual; the Archive break-in is the district's real risk (L9+). Retreat is always available; the Stacks are a safe fallback.
- **Rest:** safe lodging in the scholars' quarter; the Pale Star is a short walk via Lampgate.

## Consequences If Ignored

Without the player, Quorrin's Reclamation advances: he deciphers the keystone and secures Vyre's relics to race the Court. The clearest written proof stays locked — but M6/M9 remain reachable by Othetha's confession, the Tallow Man's witness, and Reach sites (three-clue rule holds). Briss is never disillusioned and the Remnant never fractures, leaving a strong, hidden rival for the endgame.
