## AI Use

Load for Caradril's temples, funerals, clergy, healing services, and the city's own buried evidence that the dead are failing here too. The district of faith, death-rites, and the cover-up of raise-dead failures (Secret 7). A safe-rest and healing hub, and a quiet M-thread the player can crack inside the "untouched" city. Pairs with the Ashmarket (the clergy protest the relic trade) and the Sill (where the failures are hardest to hide).

## One-Sentence Identity

The grand temple-and-cemetery hill where Caradril buries its dead beautifully, expensively, and — lately, secretly — not quite successfully.

## First Impression

A green hill above the city crowned by the **Threshold Cathedral**, its three great doors for Birth, Living, and the Last Door. Terraced cemeteries fall away below it — the **Quiet Houses** — ordered, well-tended, carved with the names of charter families. Bells toll the hours of mourning. Incense, cut flowers, cold stone. After the Reach's failing graves, Highmourn looks like the way death is *supposed* to work: dignified, professional, paid-for. The cracks are kept out of sight.

## Public Overview

Caradril's faith-and-death quarter. The **Threshold Cathedral** is the city's great temple to the Three Thresholds, led by **Mother Ysarra**. The **Quiet Houses** below are the civic cemetery and funeral establishment, run by **Grave-Magister Lon Quayle**, where the dead are washed, named, and laid to rest for a fee scaled to one's charter. The Mourners' Circle here is wealthier and more institutional than the Reach's, but keeps the same old customs. A small, ignored **Ashen Warden** chapter-house sits at the hill's foot. Caradrese come here to grieve, to pray, to bury, and — for steep coin and council license — to seek *raise dead*.

## DM-Only Truth

Highmourn holds Caradril's *own evidence* that the boundary is failing: **raise-dead rites are quietly failing here too, and Mother Ysarra is covering it up** (Secret 7) to keep the city calm and the Cathedral's authority intact. A few recent civic funerals "did not take"; one or two raised dead came back *wrong* and were hushed away (a Caradril echo of Wren — never as central, but a real, local M1/M5 demonstration inside the city). The institutional Mourners here carry the same buried folk-memory as Grandmother Wend's people — that "the Concord ate the dead" — but it is genteel, half-forgotten, and politically dangerous to voice. The ignored Warden chapter (the only people who *know* the rites are a deliberate fragment) is the player's truest local ally and an under-used early warning. **Grave-Magister Quayle** is troubled by the failures and can be turned into a witness.

## Local Leadership

**Mother Ysarra** (secondary; Cathedral high priest, keeper of the cover-up), **Grave-Magister Lon Quayle** (secondary; funeral official, troubled witness). The Warden chapter has a lone overstretched keeper (use the minor Sister Onae or a generic warden; Warden Pell or a Reach warden may visit).

## Important NPCs

Mother Ysarra, Lon Quayle (secondary). Minor: Sister Onae (Warden chapter), Bell-ringer Tomm, Bone-clerk Vass. See `../../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Three Thresholds clergy (rule the Cathedral); Mourners' Circle (institutional, on the terraces); Ashen Wardens (a thin, ignored chapter); Tidewater Council (licenses and funds civic funerals; pressures Ysarra to keep calm).

## Shops and Services

Paid healing (cure wounds, lesser restoration, gentle repose; remove curse and raise dead by council license, steep cost, and quiet risk), funeral rites and burial, blessings and consecration, religious research (Three Thresholds lore), and sanctuary. **A premier solo-safety hub:** safe rest, real healing, and an honest ally in the Warden chapter.

## Laws and Customs

- The dead are the Quiet Houses' business; private/frontier rites in the city are discouraged.
- Raise dead requires a council license and a Cathedral rite; failures are *not* discussed.
- Custom: eyes of the dead are covered; mourning-bells by station; a coin "for the Door" left at the tomb.
- The clergy's authority is civic as well as spiritual — embarrassing the Cathedral is a political act.

## Current Tensions

- **The failures** (DM): rites and raisings going wrong; Ysarra suppressing it; Quayle's conscience straining.
- **Clergy vs. the relic trade:** Highmourn's protest against the Ashmarket's grave-robbery (a Magisterium vote and a moral fault line).
- **The ignored Wardens:** the chapter's warnings about the Reach crisis dismissed by Cathedral and council alike.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- Ysarra's cover-up of raise-dead failures (Secret 7; a Caradril-internal M1/M5 demonstration).
- A recent civic "wrong-come-back" hushed away (a tragic local clue, parallel to Wren).
- The institutional Mourners' buried harvest folk-memory (a softer C-M6-4 echo; earnable with trust).
- The Warden chapter's true (fragmentary) knowledge of the rites as a deliberate release-only technique (links to Othetha's secret; an Act-2/3 lead).
- Quayle can be turned into a witness to the failures (Persuasion/Insight DC 14 + safety).

## Possible Scenes

- A grand civic funeral that falters — and the clergy smoothly, chillingly, covering it.
- Mother Ysarra, gracious and immovable, insisting "Caradril's dead rest well."
- Grave-Magister Quayle showing the player, by lamplight, a grave that "won't keep."
- The lone Warden at the chapter-house, grateful anyone will listen, warning of the Reach.
- A charter family begging a raise-dead, and the player watching the price — and the risk — up close.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Threat:** low and social. The clergy fight with authority, not arms (Priest-like if cornered, AC 13, ~33 HP; noncombatant). The real danger is political — embarrassing the Cathedral brings council pressure, not violence.
- **Social/skill DCs:** notice a failed rite DC 13 (Insight/Religion); get Ysarra to admit anything DC 18 (she won't, easily — better to turn Quayle); turn Quayle DC 14 + safety; earn the institutional Mourners' folk-truth DC 15 + demonstrated respect for the dead.
- **Rest/healing:** abundant and safe — the district's defining service. A reliable solo-safety pillar in the city.
- **Level framing:** safe to use at any level; its clue-thread targets levels 4–8, feeding the deeper M6 work at the Lantern Reach.

## Consequences If Ignored

Ysarra's cover-up holds; Caradril stays in denial; the failing rites worsen unremarked until the crisis is undeniable. The Warden chapter is shuttered or ignored into irrelevance, and a quiet ally is lost. The city's own proof of the crisis goes unused — though the same truths are reachable elsewhere. No dead-end; a missed humane lever and a missed early warning.
