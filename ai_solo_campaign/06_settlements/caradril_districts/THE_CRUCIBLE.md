## AI Use

Load for craft, smithing, magic-item and gear commerce, guild politics, and the **relic-smelting** thread — where Concord salvage is melted down for profit, destroying evidence and unleashing strange effects. The district of making, mending, and a guild dabbling in what it doesn't understand. Pairs with the Ashmarket (its relic supply) and the Lantern Reach (which wants the relics intact, for opposite reasons).

## One-Sentence Identity

The smoke-and-hammer guild district where master crafters turn Concord salvage into Caradrese coin — and one foundry has started smelting relics that scream.

## First Impression

Heat and noise: forge-glow through open doors, the ring of hammers, the roar of smelters, guild-banners over fortress-like halls. The Crucible never fully sleeps; the night-shift glows red across the river. Crafters here are proud, clannish, and skilled — the best smiths, jewelers, gearwrights, and alchemists between the Reach and the sea. The air tastes of iron, char, and something faintly *wrong* near one foundry, where the relic-smelting is done.

## Public Overview

Caradril's industrial and craft quarter: foundries, smithies, guildhalls, workshops, and the alchemists' row. The craft guilds are a real power bloc — they make the city's wealth tangible and sit in the Wardmoot with more voice than most commoners. **Foundress Beck Harrow** leads the largest foundry-guild, which has lately taken to **smelting Concord salvage** for its rare metals and old glass — lucrative, controversial, and quietly dangerous. The Crucible is where a player buys quality gear, commissions work, and finds out what relics are really worth as *material*.

## DM-Only Truth

Relic-smelting destroys Remembrance relics — and the harvest-evidence they carry — for profit, which suits the Ledger (covering its tracks) and infuriates the Remnant (which wants them whole). But melting raw Remembrance is *not* inert: Harrow's foundry has had "incidents" — a smelt that wept a voice, a worker gone thin-touched, a casting that holds a dead man's last word. **The Crucible is an unintended M3 demonstration and a danger site**: proof that relics store the dead, delivered by industrial accident. Beck Harrow is profit-driven but increasingly frightened; she can be a witness, a warning, or a reckless rival. The Ledger pays the guild to smelt the "embarrassing" relics; the Remnant bribes a foreman to divert the best ones to the Sealed Archive — a small proxy war the player can exploit.

## Local Leadership

**Foundress Beck Harrow** (secondary; leads the foundry-guild and the relic-smelting). The other craft guilds (smiths, gearwrights, jewelers, alchemists) are led by guild-stewards; **Guild-steward Phane** is the player's commerce contact.

## Important NPCs

Beck Harrow (secondary). Minor: Smelt-master Ordo, Guild-steward Phane, Soot the apprentice. See `../../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Craft guilds (rule the district; a Wardmoot bloc); Cinder Ledger (pays to smelt evidence); Concord Remnant (bribes to divert relics); Salt Syndicate (supplies grey-market salvage to the foundries).

## Shops and Services

Quality weapons, armor, and gear (the best mundane and low-magic commerce in the campaign so far), commissions and repairs, jewelers and fine work, alchemy and reagents (potions, oils, antitoxins, some scrolls via the Collegium), and metallurgy/appraisal of metals and old glass. The place to *upgrade* between adventures.

## Laws and Customs

- Guild law governs craft; non-guild work for hire is restricted (the player can commission, not compete).
- Relic-smelting is *technically* licensed under the relic-charter; its risks are unregulated.
- Custom: a maker's-mark is sacred; the night-shift "keeps the fires honest"; the slag-yards are off-limits to outsiders.

## Current Tensions

- **The smelting controversy** (DM): profit vs. the incidents; the Remnant vs. the Ledger over the relics; Harrow's growing fear.
- **Guild politics:** the foundry-guild's rise resented by the older smiths' guild; a Wardmoot seat in contest.
- **Worker unrest:** thin-touched workers and an "accident" the guild covered up; the Sill (where the workers live) is restless.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The screaming smelt and the thin-touched worker (C-M3 demonstration by accident; thin-touch hazard).
- A casting that "holds" a dead man's last word — a portable, eerie M3 clue.
- The Ledger pays to destroy embarrassing relics; the Remnant bribes a foreman (Ordo) to divert the best — exploitable proxy war.
- Beck Harrow is a turnable witness, frightened by what she's caused.

## Possible Scenes

- A night-shift smelt that wails in a human voice as the metal runs.
- Beck Harrow, soot-streaked and scared, asking the player to "find out what we've been melting."
- A slag-yard cast that, picked up, murmurs (thin-touch warning).
- Ordo quietly diverting a crate to a Remnant courier — a tail-able lead.
- A guildhall commission scene: the player upgrading gear while overhearing the controversy.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Threat:** mostly social/industrial. Guild toughs are Guard/Thug-like (AC 13–16, ~11–30 HP); the real danger is the **smelting hazard** — thin-touch from raw relics (reversible, telegraphed) and burn/heat hazards in the foundry (DC 13 Dex/Con to avoid; 2d6 fire if careless).
- **Social/skill DCs:** commission/buy — coin + DC 10–12; learn what's being smelted DC 14 (Investigation/Insight); turn Beck Harrow DC 14; spot Ordo's diversion DC 13; safely handle a raw relic DC 14 (Arcana/Religion) or risk thin-touch.
- **Danger:** the foundry interior is a hazard zone (heat, slag, thin-touch), not a combat gauntlet; retreat is easy; no cheap death.
- **Rest:** safe lodging adjoins; the district is busy-safe by day, hot and watchful at night.

## Consequences If Ignored

The smelting continues: relics (and harvest-evidence) are destroyed, the Ledger's tracks covered, workers thin-touched, and an M3 demonstration lost — though M3 is reachable elsewhere. Beck Harrow's fear curdles into denial or disaster; the Sill's unrest grows. The Remnant or the Ledger wins the relic proxy-war by default. No dead-end.
