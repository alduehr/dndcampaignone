## AI Use

Load for anything involving the Cinder Ledger's *headquarters*: Mother Calla Vyre, loans, salvage-buying, the Ledger Keep, the Vyre–Quorrin monopoly deal, and Ledger work. This is the city's money-heart and the Ledger's home ground. The richest single faction-clue site outside the Sealed Archive (the deal can be exposed here without the Archive). Pairs with the Ashmarket (where Ledger relics move) and the Magisterium (where Ledger money buys votes).

## One-Sentence Identity

The harbor-lake banking district where the Cinder Ledger rules from the Ledger Keep, and where, in a back office, two factions are quietly bargaining to sell the dead.

## First Impression

The Stillwater opens before you, crowded with masts; stone quays run with porters, tally-clerks, and the clack of counting-frames. Warehouses stand like fortresses. Above them all rises the **Ledger Keep** — not a castle but a vast, severe counting-house of grey stone, its windows lamplit at all hours, its door flying the Ledger's cinder-mark. The air smells of lake-water, tar, spices, and money. Nobody is idle; everybody is owed, or owing.

## Public Overview

Caradril's commercial engine: the docks on the Stillwater, the great warehouses, the moneychangers, and the **Ledger Keep**, headquarters of the Cinder Ledger and seat of **Mother Calla Vyre**. The Ledger runs its frontier financing, caravan trade, and salvage-buying from here through its guild-arm, the **Quay Charter**. The **Last Tally** tavern serves the clerks and captains; deals are struck there that the Magisterium only ratifies later.

## DM-Only Truth

The Ledger Keep is where **Vyre is negotiating the Remembrance monopoly with Quorrin Vane** (M3/M6 access; the Monopoly clock's engine). Vyre means to be the house that *sells immortality*; she does not yet grasp the full horror. The deal-papers exist — and **Underfactor Coll Riis has copied incriminating ledger-pages** out of conscience and fear, which is a clean, Archive-free clue path. Vyre's Caradril deputy, **Provost Anneth Vell**, runs the Keep ruthlessly and knows more than she says. The Keep's deepest vault holds Caradril's counterpart to the Hollowmere "relic that remembers" — a refined relic the Ledger is studying (M3 demonstration site, gated, levels 8+). The Ledger is *also* unknowingly funding the Remnant's relic-supply via the deal, intersecting the Monopoly and Reclamation clocks.

## Local Leadership

**Mother Calla Vyre** (matriarch; major NPC, splits time Caradril↔Hollowmere) and her Caradril deputy **Provost Anneth Vell** (secondary). The **Quay Charter** guild governs the docks under Ledger control. Tide-Watch presence is strong and Ledger-friendly here.

## Important NPCs

Calla Vyre (major), Anneth Vell, Coll Riis (secondary). Minor: Tally-clerk Vinn, Wharf-boss Drenn, Moneychanger Oons, Pelt the runner. See `../../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Cinder Ledger (rules); Quay Charter (its guild-arm); Tidewater Council (licenses the trade); Salt Syndicate (smuggling skims the legitimate trade); Concord Remnant (present only through Quorrin's secret visits to Vyre).

## Shops and Services

Banking (loans, letters of credit, deposits, debt), salvage and relic *buying* (the Ledger pays best, with strings), caravan and ship bookings onward, warehousing, hireling-muscle, and information for sale. The single best money source in the campaign — and the single best way to fall into debt-leverage.

## Laws and Customs

- Charter law is strictest here; unlicensed trade on the Quays is swiftly punished.
- The Ledger's word carries near-legal weight; a Ledger blacklist can ruin a newcomer citywide.
- Custom: deals sealed with a "cinder-token"; debts are tracked obsessively and never forgotten.

## Current Tensions

- **The monopoly deal** (DM): Vyre and Quorrin circle each other; each plans to cheat. The player can detect it (via Riis, the Last Tally, tailing a courier), exploit it, or shatter it.
- **Ledger vs. old Houses:** Esren Tolm's bloc resents the Ledger's rising power; the open-charter fight (Magisterium) is partly their proxy war.
- **Smuggling:** the Salt Syndicate skims the Quays; "Tidewife" Sorrel and the Ledger have an uneasy, profitable truce the player can disrupt.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The Vyre–Quorrin deal-papers (M3/M6; the Monopoly+Reclamation intersection).
- Coll Riis's copied pages (Archive-free clue path; turn him with safety, coin, or conscience — Persuasion DC 14 + a promise of protection).
- The deep-vault refined relic (M3 demonstration; gated, L8+; heist or turned-Vell access).
- The Ledger funds the Remnant's relic supply through the deal (links clocks 6 and 7).

## Quest Hooks

`Q_THE_QUAY_CHARTER` (Ledger work that escalates into the deal); plus: collect a frontier debt; appraise/sell Reach salvage; turn Coll Riis; the deep-vault job (L8+). See `../../09_quests/hooks_and_rumors/CARADRIL_HOOKS.md`.

## Possible Scenes

- An audience with Vyre in the Keep: warm, precise, a generous offer with a clause.
- Coll Riis, sweating in the Last Tally, almost confessing what he's copied.
- A Remnant courier slipping into the Keep after dark (a tail-able lead).
- Provost Vell testing the player with a "simple" collections job that is anything but.
- The deep-vault relic, behind glass, "remembering" a name in a dead woman's voice (M3, late).

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch & muscle:** Tide-Watch (Guard/Veteran-like) plus Ledger hirelings (Thug/Spy-like, AC 13–14, ~30–40 HP; intimidation over killing — Tallytooth Ren's Caradril counterparts). Vell can field a half-dozen; not a stand-up fight for a solo PC — scout, talk, or steal.
- **Social DCs:** Vyre audience DC 13 (she *wants* to meet a useful newcomer); turn Riis DC 14 (+ protection); spot the courier DC 14 (Perception) / tail DC 13 (Stealth); deep-vault infiltration DC 17–20 (multi-path heist, L8+).
- **Danger:** economic and social first — debt, blacklist, framing, hired pressure. Violence is a last resort the Ledger prefers to avoid. Telegraphed; no cheap death.
- **Rest:** the Last Tally and nearby inns; the Quays are safe by day, watchful by night.

## Consequences If Ignored

The Monopoly clock advances: the deal matures, the Ledger begins selling small death-cheating to the rich, and relic-supply flows to the Remnant's race. Riis loses his nerve (or his life). The deep-vault relic — a clean M3 demonstration — goes unstudied, though M3 is reachable elsewhere (Gravecallers, Reach relics). No dead-end; a ceded advantage.
