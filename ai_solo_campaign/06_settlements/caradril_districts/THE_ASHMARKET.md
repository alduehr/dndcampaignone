## AI Use

Load for relic and salvage trade (legal and black-market), curio-shopping, appraisals, fences, and the **quiet-coin** thread — Caradril's open marketplace for things that should not be sold. The district where M3 (what is Remembrance?) can be touched commercially and where the relic-boom's moral cost is visible. Pairs with the Counting-Quays (where Ledger relics originate) and the Crucible (which smelts them).

## One-Sentence Identity

A loud, crowded relic-bazaar where Reach salvage becomes Caradrese fortune, and where, in the back rooms, someone is quietly selling the dead.

## First Impression

A roofed-over warren of stalls between the Quays and the Crucible: lamplight on grey Concord stone-scraps, brokers crying lots, the smell of old metal, dust, and incense burned to "settle" uneasy goods. Appraisers squint through lenses; haulers wheel crates fresh off frontier caravans; a clergyman stands at the edge muttering against the whole trade. Legitimate licensed dealing up front — and, through a curtain, the *other* market.

## Public Overview

Caradril's salvage and curio quarter. Licensed dealers buy, sort, and resell **Concord salvage** flooding in from the failing Reach — stone, metal, old glass, oddments. It is regulated by the Tidewater Council (a relic-charter is required) and supplied largely through the Cinder Ledger. Appraisers, antiquarians, smiths' agents, and collectors crowd it. The clergy of Highmourn and the reformers call it grave-robbery; the city calls it prosperity. Beneath the legal trade runs the black market in **quiet-coin** — relics rumored to "hold" the dead — controlled by the **Salt Syndicate** under **"Tidewife" Sorrel**.

## DM-Only Truth

The Ashmarket is where M3 becomes *visible and purchasable*. "Quiet-coin" relics are **raw Remembrance relics** (old glass), and a few genuinely store the dead. The Syndicate sells them to the desperate and the curious without understanding what they are — a horror hidden inside a hustle. The Ledger feeds the legal trade and quietly skims the best relics toward its deep vault and the Quorrin deal. The clergy's protests (Highmourn) are partly a buried folk-memory of the harvest. A player can buy, fence, appraise, or seize a relic here and — with Arcana/Religion study or a Gravecaller/Remnant reading — confirm it *stores a person* (C-M3 demonstration, an accessible early path to M3 that doesn't require the Reach delves or the Archive).

## Local Leadership

Officially the relic-charter wardens (Tide-Watch + a council inspector); really **"Tidewife" Sorrel** (secondary), the Salt Syndicate boss who controls the back market. The Ledger's buyers set the prices.

## Important NPCs

"Tidewife" Sorrel (secondary). Minor: Relic-monger Sabb, Fence Greel-of-the-Ash, Appraiser Mooren, Hawker Liss. See `../../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Salt Syndicate (controls the black market); Cinder Ledger (supplies and skims); Crucible guilds (buy salvage to smelt); Mourners' Circle (protest at the edges); Tide-Watch (light, bribable enforcement).

## Shops and Services

Buying and selling salvage and curios, relic appraisal and authentication, fencing of "questionable" goods, mundane antiques and oddments, and — under the curtain — quiet-coin and smuggled goods. A place to *acquire a Remembrance relic* (legitimately or not) and to learn what one is.

## Laws and Customs

- A relic-charter is required for legal dealing; unlicensed relic-trade is a Syndicate-controlled crime the Watch mostly ignores for a cut.
- Custom: incense burned over "uneasy" lots; a buyer never asks where a relic was dug; the clergy's protest is tolerated as background noise.
- Quiet-coin is an open secret — everyone knows, no one says.

## Current Tensions

- **The relic boom:** supply from the dying Reach surges; prices and rumors rise; the clergy and reformers push to ban the trade (a Magisterium vote in play).
- **Syndicate vs. Ledger:** Sorrel and the Ledger share the trade uneasily; the player can play them against each other.
- **Quiet-coin fear:** more "haunted" relics surface; a buyer has gone strange; the Syndicate wants it hushed.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- Quiet-coin relics are raw Remembrance; some store the dead (C-M3 demonstration — an early, commercial M3 path).
- The Ledger skims the best relics toward its deep vault and the Quorrin deal.
- A specific quiet-coin relic in Sorrel's keeping "remembers" a recent Reach death — a portable, chilling clue if the player obtains and studies it.
- The clergy's protest hides a folk-memory of the harvest (links to Highmourn's Mother Ysarra cover-up).

## Quest Hooks

`Q_QUIET_COIN` (trace, buy, or shut down the relic that holds the dead — a portable M3 path, levels 4–7). Plus: appraise a relic for a desperate buyer; fence Reach salvage; run a Syndicate errand; expose the trade to the clergy/reformers. See `../../09_quests/hooks_and_rumors/CARADRIL_HOOKS.md`.

## Possible Scenes

- An appraiser's lens trembling over a relic that "shouldn't read like that."
- A back-room quiet-coin sale where the relic murmurs a dead man's name.
- Sorrel sizing up the player over a job: discreet, dangerous, well-paid.
- A clergy protest the crowd jeers — and one old voice the player should listen to.
- Buying a relic, studying it that night, and learning it holds a person (M3, the conspiracy turning concrete).

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Syndicate muscle:** Thug/Spy-like (AC 13–14, ~30–45 HP), Bandit-like crews; Sorrel is Spy/Assassin-like (AC 15, ~55 HP) but prefers leverage to blades. A solo PC can handle a back-alley scuffle; crossing the Syndicate's *operation* is a flee/negotiate situation.
- **Social/skill DCs:** find the quiet-coin market DC 13 (Streetwise/Persuasion or a contact); buy without trouble — coin; honest appraisal of a relic DC 14 (Mooren) or Arcana/Religion DC 15 to read it yourself; fence goods DC 12; get a Syndicate job DC 12.
- **Danger:** criminal/social; telegraphed. The relic itself can carry **thin-touch** risk if mishandled (the Stage-3 reversible mechanic) — a warning, not a kill.
- **Rest:** the district adjoins safe inns (Pale Star, Last Tally a short walk); the Ashmarket is busy-safe by day, risky in the back rooms by night.

## Consequences If Ignored

The relic trade swells, feeding the Ledger's monopoly and the Remnant's supply; quiet-coin spreads, and a buyer or two comes to harm — a Caradril-side symptom of the spreading crisis the city denies. M3 remains reachable elsewhere; nothing dead-ends. The Syndicate grows richer and bolder.
