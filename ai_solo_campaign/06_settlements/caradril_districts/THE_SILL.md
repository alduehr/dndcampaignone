## AI Use

Load for Caradril's underside: the poor riverside undercity, information brokers (the Hush), the thin-born refugees the city denies, smuggling, and the only soil where the Gravecallers *could* take root in Caradril. The district of the unseen — where the city's denial is hardest to maintain and the best secrets are bought. Pairs with the Counting-Quays (the wealth that ignores it), Highmourn (the failures that wash downhill), and the Sunken Wards (whose stairs open here).

## One-Sentence Identity

The crowded, half-flooded south-bank undercity where Caradril keeps its poor, its refugees, and its secrets — and where the campaign's crisis has quietly arrived ahead of everyone's notice.

## First Impression

The river's low side: tenements built on older stone, walkways over backwater channels, washing-lines, smoke, and the constant slap of water under the boards. Poorer, louder, and more alive than the upper city. The **Brass Ferry** tavern leans over the water; ferrymen call for fares; children run the planks. Newcomers from the failing Reach huddle in doorways — thin-faced, quiet, unwelcome. Everyone here knows things, and in the Sill, knowing is currency.

## Public Overview

Caradril's poorest district, on the low south bank where the Verdance backs into channels and old flooded foundations. Home to dockhands, ferrymen, washerfolk, the destitute, and a swelling tide of refugees fleeing the Reach's failures. It is under-policed and self-governing in the gaps — held loosely together by neighborhood figures like **Renna Sill** and exploited by two criminal networks: the **Salt Syndicate** (smuggling, sharing the Ashmarket trade) and **the Hush** (information and blackmail). The **Brass Ferry** is its heart and its rumor-mill.

## DM-Only Truth

The Sill is where Caradril's denial breaks down. **Thin-born refugees** from the failing Reach are gathering here (M10 background, made local and human) — proof the thinning is spreading, hidden in plain sight among "just poor folk." Some Sill-folk have seen relics that hold the dead (quiet-coin washes down here); a few quietly practice Reach-style grief-rites the city outlaws — **the only ground in Caradril where the Gravecallers could spread** if the crisis worsens (a latent cell, not yet active — a clock-driven possibility, not a present fact). **The Hush**, led by **the Bellman**, brokers the city's real secrets — including, for a steep price, fragments of the Vyre–Quorrin deal, the Magisterium correspondent thread, and the way into the Sunken Wards (the **Sealgate** opens from the Sill). The Bellman is a clue-merchant: a fair, dangerous shortcut. **Renna Sill** advocates for the thin-born and is the player's humane lever here.

## Local Leadership

No formal rule. **Renna Sill** (secondary; community leader, thin-born advocate) speaks for the boards; **the Bellman** (secondary; the Hush) and the **Salt Syndicate** run the underworld; the Tide-Watch barely enters.

## Important NPCs

Renna Sill, the Bellman (secondary); Old Pater Dunk (secondary; Sunken Wards guide, lives at the Sealgate). Minor: Ferryman Quill, Net-girl Bree, "Old Soak" Marrin, Widow Tace. See `../../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

The Hush (information/blackmail; rules the shadows here); Salt Syndicate (smuggling); Mourners' Circle (a poor, devout presence among the boards); Gravecallers (latent — could spread; not yet a cell); Tide-Watch (minimal).

## Shops and Services

Cheap lodging and food (the Brass Ferry), ferries and quiet passage, smuggled goods, fences, and — the district's prize — **information from the Hush** (the best shortcut-clues in the city, for coin or favor) and **a guide into the Sunken Wards** (Old Pater Dunk). Also: the player's best place to *recruit* desperate, capable, deniable help cheaply.

## Laws and Customs

- The Watch is sparse; the Sill polices itself by reputation and the Hush's leverage.
- Custom: a fare paid honestly buys a ferryman's silence; refugees are sheltered by the boards even as the city scorns them; "you don't ask the Bellman where he heard it."
- Reach-style rites are technically illegal but quietly tolerated among the grieving poor.

## Current Tensions

- **The refugee tide** (DM): thin-born Reach folk arriving; the city wants them gone; Renna fights for them; the Hush profits from the fear.
- **Hush vs. Syndicate:** two underworlds, uneasy neighbors; the player can play them off each other.
- **The latent cult** (DM): if the crisis worsens (clocks), grief-rites here could harden into a Gravecaller cell — a possibility the player can prevent, ignore, or, darkly, seed.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The thin-born refugees (M10 made local and human; an early, quiet proof the thinning spreads).
- The Bellman brokers fragments of the Vyre–Quorrin deal, the Magisterium correspondent thread, and the Sealgate route (paid shortcut-clues — fair, costly).
- The Sealgate down to the Sunken Wards opens from here (Old Pater Dunk guides).
- The latent Gravecaller possibility (clock-gated; not yet real).
- Renna Sill quietly shelters a "wrong-come-back" refugee — a tragic, humane M1/M5 echo inside the city.

## Possible Scenes

- The Brass Ferry at night: ferrymen, refugees, and a Hush-runner sliding into a booth.
- The Bellman, never quite seen plainly, naming a price that is never only coin.
- Renna Sill pleading the refugees' case to a player who could carry it upward.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Threat:** street-level and criminal. Cutpurses and Syndicate runners (Thug/Bandit-like, AC 12–14, ~11–30 HP); the Hush threatens with knowledge, not blades. A solo PC can handle an alley scuffle; crossing the Hush means *exposure*, not assassination.
- **Social/skill DCs:** earn the boards' trust DC 13 (+ kindness to refugees); buy a secret from the Bellman — coin/favor + DC 14 to bargain the price down; find a Wards guide DC 12; navigate the Sill safely DC 11 (Streetwise) or take a guide.
- **Danger:** social, criminal, and the threshold to the Sunken Wards (a real dungeon, level 5–12, gated below). The Sill itself is risky-but-survivable; telegraphed; no cheap death.
- **Rest:** the Brass Ferry offers cheap, watchful rest; safer rest is a short walk to the upper city.

## Consequences If Ignored

The refugees are driven out or perish unremarked; the thinning's spread goes unwitnessed by the one person (the player) who could connect it; the Hush and Syndicate tighten their grip; and if the clocks turn, a Gravecaller cell roots in the Sill's grief. The Sealgate route below goes unused. None of this dead-ends a mystery, but it lets the city's denial — and its underworld — win.
