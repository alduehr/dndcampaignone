## AI Use

Load when the player reaches the Ashgarden Vale's hub — the orchard- and cider-town on Orchardmere lake, seat of the Vale's harvest-moot. The Vale's main town: services Tilbrook lacks, the moot's politics, the Ledger's Vale factor, and the gateway south to Caradril. ~3–4 days south of Candlewick on the South Road.

## One-Sentence Identity

A fat, devout cider-town that has kept the old rites for a century and would rather rebury its dead twice than admit the rites are starting to fail.

## First Impression

The South Road crests a low rise and the Vale opens below: orchards in red-gold autumn rows, a broad bright lake, and a town of mellow stone and cider-presses with woodsmoke and the sweet rot-smell of pressing apples on the air. Bells ring the harvest-hours. People are well-fed, well-dressed for a frontier, and unhurried; they nod to strangers and watch them a beat too long. Garlands of dried apple-blossom hang over every door — and, if you look, a small clay star pressed into the lintel above each one, freshly made.

## Public Overview

Orchardmere is the richest town the Reach-side traveler is likely to have seen — cider, nuts, wool, and grain flow south to Caradril and the fees flow back. It governs the Vale through the **harvest-moot**, a council of orchard-masters and shrine-keepers chaired by the **Moot-Reeve**. The **Mourners' Circle** here is the oldest and most powerful chapter in this part of Orrun, and the town defers to its elder in all matters of the dead. The town is proud that "the rites hold in the Vale" — and increasingly, quietly, terrified that they no longer do.

## DM-Only Truth

Orchardmere's leadership is running a **soft cover-up.** The Moot-Reeve and the senior shrine-keepers know failures are creeping up from the south road and the orchard-shrines, and they are suppressing reports, quietly reburying, and leaning on families to keep silent — to protect the pilgrim trade and the Vale's self-image (mirrors Caradril's denial at a smaller scale; M1/M5 Vale echo). Mother Ezrith Combe, the Mourners' elder, **opposes the cover-up** and holds the fullest version of the "the Concord ate the dead" folk-truth (M6, Act 3 gated). The Ledger's Factor Wymar Pell is buying "saint's relics" (Remembrance relics; M3) and quietly funds the cover-up because panic is bad for trade. **Cap player-facing reveals at R1.**

## Local Leadership

**Moot-Reeve Halsa Tindle** chairs the harvest-moot and speaks for the Vale (a capable, anxious administrator running the cover-up "for everyone's good"). In matters of the dead, the town defers to **Mother Ezrith Combe** of the Old Circle, whose moral authority rivals the Reeve's political one — the central Orchardmere tension.

## Important NPCs

- **Moot-Reeve Halsa Tindle** (Compact-adjacent; secondary) — runs the moot and the soft cover-up.
- **Mother Ezrith Combe** (Mourners; secondary) — eldest Circle elder; opposes the cover-up; M6 folk-truth holder.
- **Factor Wymar Pell** (Ledger; secondary) — buys "saint's relics"; funds the silence.
- **Press-master Goodwife Orrel** (minor) — cider-yard mistress; knows who's been reburied.
- **Innkeep Dab Hollith** (minor) — Cider Star gossip hub.

See `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `../08_npcs/MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Mourners' Circle (dominant, via the Old Circle), harvest-moot/Reachward Compact (governs), Cinder Ledger (Pell's counting-yard), Ashen Wardens (pass through; resident chapter is at Saint Veddow's). Concord Remnant scholar-pilgrims lodge at the Cider Star en route to the shrine. No Gravecaller cell yet.

## Shops and Services

- **The Cider Star** — lodging (6 sp/night), good food and cider, gossip; the Vale's best inn.
- **Pell's Counting-Yard** — Ledger banking, loans, and the relic-buying counter; carts and credit south to Caradril.
- Full market: a smith, a cooper, a fletcher, a fine chandler, a healer's house (folk + one minor-spell hedge-priest of the Old Circle), horses and carts for the South Road.
- The Old Circle offers funeral rites and grief-counsel; no resurrection.

## Laws and Customs

- Harvest-moot law; the **moot-wardens** (a half-dozen, Guard-like) keep order. Justice is communal and reputation-driven — exile from a shrine-town is the real punishment.
- Custom: a fresh clay star at every lintel, renewed at each new moon; speaking too loudly of "failed rites" is considered ill-bred and bad luck, and earns cold stares.
- Grave-meddling and relic-theft are gravely punished by custom (the gibbet at Marrow Cross), though the *town's own* relic-selling is conveniently overlooked.

## Current Tensions

- **The cover-up** — Tindle vs. Combe over whether to admit the failures; families pressured to stay silent; a reburial that won't stay buried.
- **Relic-fraud** — Pell selling "saint's relics" the Mourners increasingly suspect are blasphemy.
- **Pilgrim trade vs. truth** — the whole Vale economy depends on Saint Veddow's reputation; admitting the rites fail there would ruin it.
- **Debt** — Tilbrook and the granges resent the fat shrine-towns.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The Moot-Reeve's suppressed failure-reports (M1/M5 Vale echo) — a paper trail (Investigation DC 14).
- Combe's full folk-memory of the Concord's harvest (M6; Act 3 gated; reveal only fragments early).
- Pell's relic-buying ledger lists "saint's relics" by grave (M3 — proof the relics are the dead).
- A reburied town matriarch keeps surfacing toward the lake (M5 drift, made local and gentle).

## Possible Scenes

- A harvest-moot session where the Reeve smoothly buries an alarming report and Combe quietly seethes.
- A late-night reburial by the lake that the player stumbles onto.
- Pell, all warm hospitality, making the player a generous offer for "anything old they dig up."
- Mother Combe, in the Old Circle at dusk, deciding whether to trust the player with a song she shouldn't sing.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch response:** moot-wardens (6, **Guard-like**, AC 16, ~11 HP) — competent but few; their real power is social (exile, the gibbet for grave-crimes). Crossing the moot openly gets the player run out of the Vale, not stabbed.
- **Ledger enforcement:** Pell can field **2–3 hired toughs** (**Thug-like**, AC 11, ~32 HP) for debt-collection on the granges; telegraphed, avoidable.
- **Social DCs:** get a family to admit a failed rite DC 14 (DC 10 with Combe's blessing); pry into the suppressed reports DC 14; win Combe's trust DC 15 (she tests for respect toward the dead); haggle Pell DC 13.
- **Rest/recovery:** the Cider Star (safe long rest); the Old Circle's healer's house (folk healing + one minor-spell hedge-priest, no resurrection).
- **Solo danger:** low. The Vale hub's danger is reputation and entanglement, not death.

## Consequences If Ignored

If the cover-up is ignored, the Vale clock **"The Harvest-Moot's Silence"** advances: more failures are hidden, the reburied matriarch's drift draws other dead toward the lake, Pell ships more Remembrance relics south to the Ledger (feeding the Monopoly clock), and eventually a public, undeniable failure at a harvest festival shatters the Vale's calm — bringing in Remnant scholars and the first Gravecaller recruiters. Mother Combe, unsupported, may take the truth south to Caradril herself or break with the moot.
