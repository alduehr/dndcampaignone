## AI Use

Load when the player travels the western Concord road toward the Pale Coast, follows the salt-trade or smuggling threads, or needs a second town with services Hollowmere lacks. Saltmargin is the Reach's western gateway town and the chokepoint for the rite-salt the whole region depends on. ~2 days west of Hollowmere on the Pale Road.

## One-Sentence Identity

A windburnt salt-trade town on the Reach's western edge where the price of keeping the dead down is set, gouged, and quietly smuggled.

## First Impression

The road climbs out of the fens onto a low salt-grass shelf, and the air changes — brine instead of peat. Saltmargin is a sprawl of drying-sheds, salt-pans, and tar-black timber halls crusted white at the eaves. Gulls. The constant grind of the salt-mill. Stacked barrels everywhere, each chalked with a buyer's mark. People here squint, bargain hard, and wear charms of knotted salt-grass at the throat. The whole town smells of the sea it can't quite see.

## Public Overview

Saltmargin grew where the Pale Coast's salt-carts meet the Reach's peat-roads. It refines and sells the rite-salt that every Reach household pours at the sill — and since the rites began failing worse, demand has tripled and so has the price. The **Cinder Ledger** runs the weigh-house and sets the rate. The **Reachward Compact** keeps a thin watch here. A **Mourners' Circle** chapter blesses each shipment. The town is prosperous, anxious, and resented by inland villages who think Saltmargin is bleeding them dry.

## DM-Only Truth

The salt shortage is **real but secondary** — rites fail because the harvest is reawakening (M5), not because salt is scarce. The Ledger knows salt only *slows* a failing rite and lets the panic inflate its margins anyway. More importantly, Saltmargin's wharf-factor is **quietly skimming Concord salvage** out of the Pale Coast trade before it reaches Hollowmere's counting-house — a thread that, pulled, exposes the Ledger's relic-hunger (M3) and Vyre's monopoly clock. A smuggler ring here also moves "grave-goods" the Mourners would kill to recover.

## Local Leadership

No reeve of its own; governed at arm's length by the Compact from Hollowmere through a **salt-warden**, **Warden of Weights Pell Oster** (a tired, honest minor official — not the Ashen Warden Pell). In practice the **Ledger's Wharf-Factor Iss Marlow** sets policy through pricing.

## Important NPCs

- **Wharf-Factor Iss Marlow** (Ledger; secondary) — sets the salt rate; secretly skims salvage.
- **Pan-foreman Decca Rull** (secondary) — runs the salt-pans; furious at the gouging.
- **"Gull" Heddwyn** (secondary; hidden) — smuggler who moves grave-goods and contraband relics.
- **Sister-of-salt Onna** (minor) — Mourners' blessing of shipments.
- **Innkeep Brannoch** (minor) — gossip hub at the Knotted Hand.

See `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `../08_npcs/MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Cinder Ledger (dominant), Reachward Compact (thin watch), Mourners' Circle (Brine Chapel), Ashen Wardens (pass through for salt). Gravecallers have no cell here but buy smuggled grave-goods through Gull.

## Shops and Services

- **The Knotted Hand** — lodging (5 sp/night), food, gossip.
- **The Weigh-House** — rite-salt (current price: **2 gp/lb**, triple the old rate), bulk goods, Ledger loans.
- A ropewalk and cooperage; a salt-grass charm-maker; a hedge-surgeon for pan injuries; carts and a guide for the Pale Road.
- No temple healer of note; the Brine Chapel offers folk blessing only.

## Laws and Customs

- Compact law applies but lightly enforced; the watch is two bored guards and the salt-warden.
- Custom: every shipment is salt-blessed before it leaves; breaking a salt-barrel is bad luck and a finable offense.
- Smuggling is an open secret tolerated as long as the Ledger gets its cut.

## Current Tensions

- **The salt price** — inland villages (and the Mourners) accuse the Ledger of profiteering on the dead.
- **Skimmed salvage** — Pale Coast relics vanishing before they reach Hollowmere; Domic Sael in Hollowmere has noticed shortfalls.
- **Smuggling** — Gull's grave-goods trade enrages the Brine Chapel.
- **Worker unrest** — Decca Rull's pan-crews want fairer wages or they'll strike, choking the whole Reach's salt.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- Marlow skims Concord salvage (M3 thread; Ledger monopoly clock).
- Salt only *slows* failing rites; the shortage panic is partly manufactured value.
- Gull's smuggling line reaches a Gravecaller buyer and a Pale Coast fence.
- A crate in the Weigh-House cellar holds a mislabeled Remembrance relic Marlow doesn't dare ship.

- **"The Salt Run"** (Hook 2 destination) — recover or escort a rite-salt shipment.
- **"Light on the Scale"** — investigate Marlow's skimming for Domic Sael or Decca Rull.
- **"Grave-Goods"** — the Brine Chapel hires the player to find who is selling the town's dead.

## Possible Scenes

- A shipment arrives spoiled or short, and a near-riot forms at the Weigh-House.
- A pan-crew downs tools while the Reach's salt supply hangs in the balance.
- A night meeting on the Tar Wharf where Gull offers the player contraband — or a job.
- Sister-of-salt Onna begging the player to recover a stolen burial-shroud.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch response:** minimal — 2 guards (**Guard-like**, AC 16, ~11 HP) plus the salt-warden. Crossing the Ledger brings economic ruin (debt, blacklist) before violence.
- **Ledger enforcement:** Marlow can summon **2–3 toughs** (**Thug-like**, AC 11, ~32 HP) for "collections," telegraphed and avoidable.
- **Social DCs:** haggle the salt price down DC 15; get Marlow to admit skimming DC 20 (or DC 13 with evidence); win Gull's trust DC 15.
- **Rest/recovery:** the Knotted Hand offers safe long rests; the Brine Chapel offers folk healing (no magical restoration).
- **Solo danger:** low. Threats are economic, social, and smuggler-flavored; no lethal ambush by default.

## Consequences If Ignored

If the salt threads are ignored, the price climbs further, inland rites fail worse (advancing the Mourners' clock and feeding the dread), and Marlow's skimming hands the Ledger another relic toward its monopoly. If Decca's crew strikes unaddressed, the whole Reach's salt dries up for weeks — every town's rites get more dangerous.
