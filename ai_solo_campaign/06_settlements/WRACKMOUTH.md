## AI Use

Load when the player reaches the Pale Coast's hub — the cliff-and-harbor town around a deep-water cove, the Coast's main port and salt-market, the Ledger's coastal stronghold, and the departure point for the water-route to Caradril. ~3 days west of Saltmargin on the Pale Road.

## One-Sentence Identity

A salt-crusted cliff-harbor town that runs the frontier's salt, takes the sea's tithe of drowned men with grim familiarity, and quietly profits from things the sea — and the Concord — left behind.

## First Impression

The Pale Road climbs a last headland and the sea opens out, grey and endless, and below in a deep cleft of cliff sits Wrackmouth: a steep town of slate roofs and salt-white timber tumbling down to a stone harbor packed with fishing-boats and the Ledger's salt-hulks. Gulls and the reek of fish and brine. Nets, salt-sheds, and the constant boom of surf on the harbor-wall. Every house has a knot of salt-grass and a bit of sea-glass at the door, and a tide-table chalked by the lintel. The folk are weathered, blunt, and watchful, and they all glance, once, at the broken lighthouse on the far headland.

## Public Overview

Wrackmouth is the Pale Coast's port and capital — where the fish, the salt, and the frontier's trade all pass. The **Cinder Ledger** runs the salt-works, the weigh-house, and the harbor-credit, and sets the salt-price the whole frontier pays. The town governs itself through a **harbor-moot** under a **Harbor-Reeve**, with the Compact a thin presence. A strong **Mourners' Circle** keeps the salt-and-tide death-rites — vital here, where the sea returns its dead constantly. It is prosperous, hard, independent, and increasingly frightened by a drowned-tide that won't stay down.

## DM-Only Truth

Wrackmouth is the **upstream heart of the salt thread** the player may know from Saltmargin: the Ledger's coastal Factor **Maris Cole** controls the salt-price gouge and runs the quiet trade in Concord "sea-relics" (Remembrance relics; M3) salvaged from the drowned shrines and wrecks, feeding Vyre's Monopoly clock. The town's harbor-moot half-knows the drowned-tide is worsening and, like Caradril and the Vale, manages the fear rather than facing it. The **Drowned Lamp** on the far headland is a coastal Concord node (the dungeon), and Cole has been quietly buying up the salvage that comes off it. A Gravecaller current runs through the wreckers. **Cap reveals at R1; the maritime node and the Lamp's true nature stay gated.**

## Local Leadership

**Harbor-Reeve Ond Falk** chairs the harbor-moot and speaks for the Coast (a weathered, pragmatic ex-skipper, managing fear over facing it). In practice the **Ledger's Factor Maris Cole** sets policy through the salt-price. The Mourners' tide-faith commands moral authority on all matters of the drowned.

## Important NPCs

- **Factor Maris Cole** (Ledger; secondary) — runs the salt-gouge and the sea-relic trade; the coastal Vyre-arm.
- **Harbor-Reeve Ond Falk** (Compact-adjacent; secondary) — chairs the moot; manages the drowned-tide fear.
- **Skipper Wenna Roke** (secondary) — honest boat-skipper; the player's best lift to Caradril and guide to the coves.
- **Innkeep Sella Vane** (minor) — the Gull & Anchor's hub; hires out skippers and guides.
- **Fence "Cuttle" Dree** (minor) — the Wrack's sea-relic fence; a Gravecaller-current contact.

See `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `../08_npcs/MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Cinder Ledger (dominant; salt and sea-relics; Cole), harbor-moot/Reachward Compact (governs; thin), Mourners' Circle (strong; the Tide-Bell), Gravecallers (a current through the Wrack and the wreckers), Ashen Wardens (rare; the drowned are many). Concord Remnant survey-scholars lodge at the Gull & Anchor, eyeing the Drowned Lamp.

## Shops and Services

- **The Gull & Anchor** — lodging (5 sp), good fish and sea-gossip, skippers and guides; the town's hub.
- **The Weigh-House** — Ledger banking, loans, the salt-market (current price gouged, as at Saltmargin), ship-supplies; **passage to Caradril by sea** (the Coast's distinctive route).
- A full harbor market: chandler, sailmaker, smith, a healer's house (folk + one minor-spell tide-priest), boats and skippers for hire, the salt-works.
- The Tide-Bell offers salt-and-tide rites; no resurrection.

## Laws and Customs

- Harbor-moot law; the **harbor-watch** (a dozen, Guard/Veteran-like) keep the quays; the wild coast and the Wrack are barely policed.
- Custom: salt-grass and sea-glass at the door; read the tide-table before any business; never sail on the drowned-bell's toll (it tolls for the newly drowned); leave the sea its due.
- Wrecking and sea-relic smuggling are officially crimes and unofficially everywhere; the Ledger gets its cut and looks away.

## Current Tensions

- **The salt-gouge** — Cole's price enrages the salt-workers, the fisher-folk, and the inland towns who pay it (the whole frontier's grievance, concentrated here).
- **The drowned-tide** — the sea giving back its dead worse than ever; the rites overwhelmed; the moot managing the fear.
- **Wreckers vs. the harbor** — the wrecking trade and sea-relic smuggling vs. the lawful port; who controls the coves and the Lamp.
- **The Remnant's interest** — scholars angling to study the Drowned Lamp and the sea-shrines; the moot wary.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- Cole's sea-relic trade (M3; the Monopoly clock's coastal arm) — a salvage-ledger of "sea-relics" by wreck/shrine.
- The Drowned Lamp is a coastal Concord node, warming (M2 fragment); Cole buys its salvage.
- The drowned-tide is the maritime harvest leaking (M5 oblique); the moot hides how bad it is.
- The Gravecaller current runs through the Wrack and the wreckers (M3/M6 oblique door).

## Quest Hooks

- **"The Salt-Price"** (PC-H1) — the salt-workers or the moot hire the player to break or expose Cole's gouge and sea-relic trade (M3; the Monopoly clock's coastal arm).
- **"Passage to the City"** (PC-H2) — take ship with Skipper Roke down the coast and up the Verdance to Caradril (the water-route).
- **"What the Lamp Holds"** (PC-H3) — investigate the Drowned Lamp for the Remnant, the moot, or the player's own curiosity (M2 fragment; the dungeon).

## Possible Scenes

- A near-riot at the Weigh-House over the salt-price as a fishing crew comes home empty.
- The drowned-bell tolling at the Tide-Bell, and the town falling silent for the newly drowned.
- Cole, smooth and generous, offering the player good coin for "anything off the Lamp."
- A night deal in the Wrack for a sea-relic — and the drowned face it shows (M3, experiential).

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch response:** harbor-watch (12, **Guard-like** AC 16 ~11 HP; a **Veteran-like** captain) — keeps the quays, ignores the Wrack and the coves. Crossing the Ledger brings ruin and blacklist before violence.
- **Ledger enforcement:** Cole can field **3–4 salt-toughs/wreckers** (**Thug/Bandit-like**, AC 11–13, ~32 HP) — telegraphed, avoidable.
- **Social DCs:** haggle the salt-price DC 16; get Cole to admit the sea-relic trade DC 20 (DC 13 with a salvage-ledger); hire/win Skipper Roke DC 13; win a wrecker's trust in the Wrack DC 14.
- **Rest/recovery:** the Gull & Anchor (safe long rest); the healer's house (folk + minor tide-magic); the Tide-Bell for rites.
- **Solo danger:** low *in town*; the danger is the sea, the coves, and the Wrack.

## Consequences If Ignored

If ignored, the Coast clock **"The Drowned Tide Rises"** advances: Cole's salt-gouge tightens (the whole frontier's rites worsen — feeding the Reach's dread and the Monopoly clock), the drowned-tide grows beyond the rites' control, the wreckers and the sea-relic trade flourish, and the Drowned Lamp warms further, pulling the drowned "home." Eventually the salt the entire frontier depends on becomes scarce and ruinously dear — a continent-wide squeeze the player could have eased.
