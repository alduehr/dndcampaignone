## AI Use

Load when the player travels the cliff-road and reaches the shingle-cove fishing village under the headland of the Drowned Lamp — the Coast's window into its sea-faith, its drowned-tide, and the ruined lighthouse. The honest, poor, drowned-haunted counterpoint to Wrackmouth, and the staging-point for the Drowned Lamp dungeon. ~1–2 days from Wrackmouth on the Pale Road.

## One-Sentence Identity

A poor, devout shingle-cove village in the shadow of a ruined Concord lighthouse, where the sea returns its dead so often that the living and the drowned share the strand.

## First Impression

The cliff-road drops to a grey shingle cove and a huddle of tarred cottages with upturned-boat roofs, fish-racks, and a little salt-and-tide shrine at the high-tide line. Above, on the headland, the broken stump of an old Concord lighthouse — the Drowned Lamp — stands black against the sky. The folk are lean, salt-cured, and grave, and they keep an empty place at every hearth "for the one the sea sends back." At the strand's edge, a row of small cairns marks the drowned, and someone has left fresh salt on each.

## Public Overview

Cobble Strand fishes the cove and works the near salt-pans, scraping a hard living under the Ledger's salt-price. Its faith is the **Mourners' Circle** in its purest coastal form — the **salt-and-tide rites** that settle the sea's drowned dead, kept by the **Salt-Mother**. The village has always lived close to the dead, because the sea is always giving them back; lately it gives them back faster than the rites can settle them. The Strand reveres and fears the Drowned Lamp above it — the old light that, the songs say, "called the drowned home."

## DM-Only Truth

Cobble Strand is the **honest M5/M2 window into the Coast's maritime harvest**, the way Tilbrook is the Vale's honest window. Too poor to manage its fear, the Strand simply lives with the worsening drowned-tide and says plainly that "the Lamp is waking." The Salt-Mother **Bryd** holds the fullest coastal version of the old songs — that "the Lamps kept the drowned for the Concord" (an oblique M5/M6 fragment) — and keeps the salt-and-tide rites that are the only thing settling the rising dead. The **Drowned Lamp** (the dungeon) is a coastal Concord node at the cliff-foot, drowned and warming. A Gravecaller current reaches the Strand through the grief of the drowned, and the wreckers use the cove's caves. **Cap reveals at R1; the Lamp's node-nature stays gated.**

## Local Leadership

**Salt-Mother Bryd**, keeper of the tide-shrine, leads the village in all that matters — the rites, the drowned, the old songs. **Headman Corl Strand**, the eldest fisher, speaks for the village's worldly affairs (and to Wrackmouth's moot), but defers to Bryd on the dead.

## Important NPCs

- **Salt-Mother Bryd** (Mourners; secondary) — keeper of the salt-and-tide rites and the fullest coastal old songs (M5/M6 oblique; the honest window).
- **Headman Corl Strand** (secondary) — eldest fisher; the village's worldly voice; weary of the salt-price and the drowned-tide.
- **Net-wife Senna** (minor) — sharp-eyed, blunt; the Strand's bluntest gossip; lost a son to the sea.
- **Young Ferec** (minor) — a fisher-lad tempted by the Gravecallers after his father drowned (cult-seed).

See `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `../08_npcs/MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Mourners' Circle (dominant; the tide-shrine; Bryd), Gravecallers (a current through the village's grief and the cove caves), Cinder Ledger (the salt-price and salvage-buying reach from Wrackmouth). No watch; the village polices itself. Concord Remnant scholars pass through toward the Lamp.

## Shops and Services

- No inn; a respected guest is given hearth-room and fish-stew (real, earned hospitality).
- Fish, salt, and tide-lore to trade; net-mending and boat-repair; a folk-healer (and Bryd's salt-and-tide rite-aid — the Coast's best non-Wrackmouth healing).
- Boats and a guide for the coves and the near sea (essential for the caves and the Skerries).
- The Tide-Shrine for rites; no resurrection.

## Laws and Customs

- Custom is law; Bryd and Corl settle disputes. No militia; the sea is the great authority.
- Salt-and-tide custom: a cairn and fresh salt for every drowned; an empty hearth-place "for the one the sea sends back"; never take from a wreck without the Salt-Mother's rite (the wreckers break this, to the village's grief).
- The village shelters its own and distrusts the wreckers, the Ledger, and outsiders who scoff at the tide.

## Current Tensions

- **The drowned-tide** — the sea returning its dead faster than the rites can settle them; Bryd stretched to breaking.
- **The salt-price** — the Strand gouged like all the Coast, and bitter about it.
- **The wreckers** — using the cove caves, breaking the salt-rites, smuggling sea-relics; the village hates and fears them.
- **The Gravecaller temptation** — the grieving (like young Ferec) reaching for "a word with the drowned."

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The honest M5/M2 truth Wrackmouth manages: the Lamp is waking, the drowned-tide is the node leaking.
- Bryd's old songs: "the Lamps kept the drowned for the Concord" (M5/M6 oblique; the coastal folk-truth).
- The cove caves are the wreckers' and the Gravecaller current's route (M3/M6 door).
- A drowned-cairn whose occupant keeps coming back marks the freshest local node-pull (M5, gentle).

## Quest Hooks

- **"The Salt-Mother's Burden"** (PC-H8) — help Bryd settle a drowned-cairn that won't stay quiet (the Coast's gentle, honest M5/M2 delve; salt-and-tide rite).
- **"What Sings in the Lamp"** (PC-H9) — Bryd or the player's curiosity about the waking Lamp leads up the Lamp Stair (M2 fragment; the dungeon).
- **"The Cove Caves"** (PC-H10) — clear the wreckers from the caves, or find the Gravecaller current and young Ferec.

## Possible Scenes

- A drowned man returning on the tide to his own cairn, and the village's grave, unsurprised grief.
- Bryd, at the tide-shrine by lamplight, deciding whether to sing the player the old songs.
- Young Ferec slipping toward the cove caves and a whispered promise after his father drowns.
- The Drowned Lamp on the headland "lighting" faintly in the fog — though no one tends it.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **"Watch" response:** none — the village and the sea are the watch. The wreckers in the caves are the real local threat (Thug/Bandit-like).
- **Social DCs:** earn the village's trust DC 12 (respect the rites, help with the drowned); earn Bryd's old songs DC 15 (DC 12 with deep trust / a drowned settled); learn the salt-and-tide rite DC 12 (Bryd teaches the worthy).
- **Rest/recovery:** safe hearth-rest for an honored guest; Bryd's salt-and-tide rite-aid + folk healing (the Coast's best non-Wrackmouth healing); the tide-shrine for rites. **The Coast's main solo-safety sanctuary outside Wrackmouth.**
- **Solo danger:** low *in the village*; the caves, the Lamp, and the sea are the danger.

## Consequences If Ignored

If ignored, the drowned-tide overwhelms the Strand's rites, the cairns "won't hold," and the village is slowly haunted into abandonment; the wreckers take the cove; young Ferec and others turn to the Gravecallers; and the Drowned Lamp warms unwatched, pulling more drowned "home" (the Coast clock advances). The Coast's clearest, gentlest M5/M2 window — and Bryd's old songs — are lost to grief and the cult.
