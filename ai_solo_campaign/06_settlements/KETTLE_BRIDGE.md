## AI Use

Load when the player travels the eastern Concord road toward the Tollwood, follows the Mirewend River, or needs the Reach's second-largest town and main river crossing. Kettle Bridge is the bridge-town that controls who and what crosses the Mirewend; ~1.5 days east of Hollowmere.

## One-Sentence Identity

A bustling, quarrelsome bridge-town astride the only good Mirewend crossing for days, where everything and everyone passing through the Reach must pay the toll.

## First Impression

The Concord road lifts onto a great old causeway and crosses the peat-dark Mirewend on a bridge older than the town — three massive Concord arches with a newer timber span patched over a collapsed fourth. The town clusters at both ends: mills turning on the current, a market that never quite closes, the smell of river-mud and frying eel. A toll-chain stretches across the road. Boatmen shout from below. It is the liveliest place in the Reach, and the most watched.

## Public Overview

Kettle Bridge grew on the toll. Whoever crosses the Mirewend overland passes here, and the Compact funds half its budget from the bridge-fee. It is a river-port: peat barges, salt-carts, Tollwood timber, and pilgrims all pass through. The **Reachward Compact** governs it directly through a resident bailiff. The **Cinder Ledger** runs the toll-farm and the grain-mill. **Ashen Wardens** and the **Mourners' Circle** both keep houses here because the failing rites strike travelers and locals alike. It is prosperous, factional, and full of strangers — which makes it the best place in the Reach to hear news and the worst to keep a secret.

## DM-Only Truth

The collapsed fourth arch did not fall to age — it was **a Concord harvest-conduit**, and when the Quietfall severed the network it cracked and sank, taking a small toll-house shrine down with it. The **Sunken Tollhouse** (see dungeon file) lies in the river-mud beneath the bridge: a minor Concord node, drowned, leaking, and now stirring like everything else. The bridge's recurring "river-drownings" near the broken arch are early Remembrance leakage (M2/M5). The Ledger's toll-farmer has been quietly fishing salvage out of the river and selling it upstream.

## Local Leadership

**Bailiff Aldwin Cray** of the Reachward Compact — a by-the-book, ambitious official who wants Hollowmere's reeve job and resents being posted to "a bridge." Honest enough, but more interested in his career than in the river's strangeness.

## Important NPCs

- **Bailiff Aldwin Cray** (Compact; secondary) — ambitious, rule-bound, dismissive of "ghost stories."
- **Toll-farmer Quenna Bly** (Ledger; secondary) — runs the toll and quietly fishes salvage from the river.
- **Bargemaster Tibb** (secondary) — knows the river and its drownings; frightened.
- **Warden Pell** (Ashen Wardens; secondary — the registered veteran Warden) — bitter, suspects something is wrong with the bridge.
- **Bann Oester** (Mourners' Circle; secondary — registered grave-digger) — buries the river-drowned, uneasy.
- **Old Marrock** (minor) — fishmonger and gossip.

See `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Reachward Compact (governs), Cinder Ledger (toll + mill), Ashen Wardens (rest house, Pell), Mourners' Circle (house, Oester). Gravecallers have a hidden contact among the boatmen. Concord Remnant agents pass through toward Caradril.

## Shops and Services

- **The Drowned Kettle** inn (6 sp/night, busy, good food, the town's loudest rumor table).
- Toll-house (crossing fee 2 cp foot / 5 cp cart); a grain-mill; chandlers; a boatwright; a competent surgeon (Compact-paid); a Warden rest and a Mourners' house for rites.
- River passage downstream toward the Pale Coast or upstream toward the Heights edge (boat hire).

## Laws and Customs

- Strict Compact law here (the bailiff enforces): no weapons drawn on the span, toll paid in full, lockup for brawlers.
- Custom: a coin dropped in the river for safe crossing; never name the drowned on the bridge.
- Gravecaller activity is hunted harder here than anywhere but Hollowmere.

## Current Tensions

- **The river-drownings** — boatmen are dying near the broken arch; Cray calls it carelessness, Tibb calls it the dead.
- **Toll resentment** — barge crews and Tollwood traders chafe at Bly's rising fees.
- **Cray vs. Coalmont** — the bailiff angles to discredit Hollowmere's reeve and take her post.
- **Salvage upstream** — Bly's quiet relic-fishing is drawing Gravecaller and Remnant interest.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The Sunken Tollhouse under the broken arch (M2 node; dungeon).
- The drownings are Remembrance leakage, worse by the month (M5).
- Bly fishes and sells Concord salvage (M3 thread).
- A Gravecaller cutout works among Tibb's bargemen.

## Quest Hooks

- **"The Broken Arch"** — investigate the river-drownings (leads to the Sunken Tollhouse).
- **"Toll and Trouble"** — mediate or exploit the toll dispute; expose Bly's salvage trade.
- **"The Bailiff's Ladder"** — Cray hires the player against Coalmont, a political trap with a moral choice.

See the regional quest files.

## Possible Scenes

- A barge-crew hauls a drowned man from the broken arch as the player arrives.
- Cray refusing to investigate, then quietly asking the player to do it off the books.
- A tense toll standoff between Bly and angry Tollwood traders.
- Warden Pell, drunk and bitter, half-confessing his suspicions about the bridge.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch response:** moderate — the bailiff commands **4–6 guards** (**Guard-like**, AC 16, ~11 HP) and a sergeant (**Veteran-like**, AC 17, ~58 HP). Drawing steel on the span means the lockup.
- **Ledger enforcement:** Bly avoids violence; uses fees, fines, and the bailiff as leverage.
- **Social DCs:** get Cray to authorize an off-books investigation DC 15; pry the river-truth from Tibb DC 12; expose Bly's salvage DC 18 (DC 13 with evidence).
- **Rest/recovery:** the Drowned Kettle (safe long rest); a Compact surgeon for wounds; Warden Pell for a failed-rite emergency.
- **Solo danger:** low in town; the river and the Sunken Tollhouse are the real hazards (see dungeon).

## Consequences If Ignored

If the drownings go uninvestigated, they spread upriver and the Sunken Tollhouse's leakage grows (advancing the master/basin clocks regionally), travelers begin avoiding the crossing (choking Reach trade), and Bly's unchecked salvage-selling hands relics to whichever faction reaches her first. Cray, ignored, may simply blame and arrest the wrong person to close the matter.
