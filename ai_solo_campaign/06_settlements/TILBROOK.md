## AI Use

Load when the player first enters the Vale from the Reach (Candlewick → South Road), needs a gentle level-1–2 waypoint, or follows the farm-debt / village-resentment thread. Tilbrook is the Vale's working-poor mill village — the plain counterpoint to the fat shrine-towns, and the player's likely first Vale stop. ~1–2 days south of Candlewick on the Ammet.

## One-Sentence Identity

A hard-working, hard-up mill village on the Ammet that grinds the Vale's grain, carries the Vale's debt, and bitterly watches the shrine-towns grow fat on the dead.

## First Impression

After the fens, the Ammet valley feels almost gentle: a brown river turning a big mossy mill-wheel, a huddle of thatch and daub, geese, and the steady knock of the mill. But the gentleness is thin. The cottages need mending, the children are lean, and there's a row of empty houses at the village edge with Ledger marks chalked on the doors. The folk here are blunt where the shrine-town people are smooth, and they size up a stranger by what work they can do.

## Public Overview

Tilbrook mills the Vale's grain and grows what the orchard-towns don't — a working village of millers, ploughmen, and a few weavers. It owes most of its land to the **Cinder Ledger**, which has been foreclosing as harvests tighten. The **Mourners' Circle** here is small and plain — no grand shrine, just a grave-field and an old woman who sings the passings. Tilbrook is tied to the Compact in name and resents Orchardmere's moot in fact. Folk here say what the shrine-towns won't: *the rites are failing in the Vale, same as everywhere, and the fat keepers are lying about it.*

## DM-Only Truth

Tilbrook is where the Vale's denial **breaks down** — too poor to bribe silence, too plain to care about the pilgrim trade, the villagers simply admit their dead aren't resting. This makes Tilbrook the **honest M1/M5 window into the Vale** that Orchardmere hides. The Ledger's foreclosures here are partly engineered: Factor Wymar Pell forecloses farms near old field-shrines so the Ledger can quarry their "saint's relics" (Remembrance relics; M3) unobserved — the Pellow grange is the clearest case. A frightened Tilbrook widow has already sought out the rumor of "people who can give you a last word" — the thin leading edge of a future Gravecaller cell. **Cap reveals at R1.**

## Local Leadership

No reeve; Tilbrook is led informally by **Miller Sael Brunt**, who holds the mill and most of the debt and speaks for the village to the moot and the Ledger (and is slowly being crushed between them). **Old Mam Tace**, the grave-singer, holds the village's spiritual authority.

## Important NPCs

- **Miller Sael Brunt** (secondary) — village leader; mill-master; drowning in Ledger debt.
- **Old Mam Tace** (Mourners; secondary) — grave-singer; will tell the player plainly what Orchardmere hides (M1/M5).
- **Ale-wife Genna Brunt** (minor) — the Plough & Star; the Vale's bluntest gossip.
- **Foreclosed widow Hessit** (minor) — lost her farm; tempted by "a last word" (Gravecaller seed).

See `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md` and `../08_npcs/MINOR_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Cinder Ledger (creditor and quiet relic-quarrier; Pell's reach from Orchardmere), Mourners' Circle (small plain chapter), Reachward Compact (nominal). No Gravecaller cell yet, but the recruiting begins here. Concord Remnant passes through toward the granges and field-shrines.

## Shops and Services

- **The Plough & Star** — cheap lodging (3 sp), plain food, ale, news; the only "inn."
- A smith, the mill (flour, basic repair), a hedge-healer; carts south to Orchardmere or back north to Candlewick.
- No banking but the Ledger's debt-book; no healing magic; the grave-field for rites.

## Laws and Customs

- Compact law in name; in practice Miller Brunt and custom settle disputes. The moot-wardens rarely come.
- Custom: plain rites, no grand shrines; a loaf left on a new grave for the dead's journey. Lately the loaves are found moved.
- The village quietly shelters its own from Ledger enforcers when it can.

## Current Tensions

- **Debt and foreclosure** — Pell's marks creeping across the village; Brunt one bad season from losing the mill.
- **The failing rites, admitted** — Tilbrook says aloud what the Vale denies; the dead won't lie still in the grave-field.
- **Resentment of the shrine-towns** — fury that Orchardmere and Saint Veddow's grow rich while Tilbrook starves and buries its dead twice.
- **The Gravecaller temptation** — desperate folk like Widow Hessit reaching for forbidden comfort.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The honest M1/M5 truth Orchardmere hides, freely available here from Mam Tace.
- Pell forecloses near field-shrines to quarry Remembrance relics (M3; the Pellow grange).
- Widow Hessit's contact is the leading edge of a Gravecaller cell forming in the Vale.
- A loaf-on-the-grave that moves each night marks the freshest local drift (M5, gentle).

- **"The Loaf That Moves"** (AV-H11) — help Mam Tace settle a grave that won't stay closed (a gentle, honest Vale M1/M5 delve; level 1–2).
- **"The Marks on the Doors"** (AV-H12) — help Miller Brunt fight (or expose) Pell's foreclosures; leads to the Pellow grange (M3).
- **"A Last Word in the Vale"** (AV-H13) — find and help (or stop) Widow Hessit before the Gravecallers reach her.

## Possible Scenes

- Mam Tace, at the grave-field at dusk, telling the player plainly: "Don't let them tell you it's only the Reach. It's here too. We just can't afford to lie about it."
- Pell's enforcers nailing a fresh mark to a cottage door while the village watches, helpless and furious.
- Widow Hessit slipping out at night toward a whispered promise.
- A grave the player helps Mam Tace re-settle — the Vale's gentlest brush with a true rite.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch response:** none resident; moot-wardens (Guard-like) come slowly from Orchardmere if summoned. Village justice is communal.
- **Ledger enforcement:** Pell's **2–3 hired toughs** (**Thug-like**, AC 11, ~32 HP) enforce foreclosures — telegraphed, avoidable, and deeply unpopular (the village will quietly back a player who faces them down without bloodshed).
- **Social DCs:** win the village's trust DC 12 (do honest work, side against the Ledger); get the full failing-rites picture from Mam Tace DC 10 (she wants to tell someone); talk Brunt into resisting Pell DC 13.
- **Rest/recovery:** the Plough & Star (safe long rest); hedge-healer (folk only); the grave-field for rites with Mam Tace.
- **Solo danger:** very low. Tilbrook is a soft landing and a moral on-ramp into the Vale.

## Consequences If Ignored

If ignored, Pell forecloses the mill and the shrine-adjacent farms, the village scatters or empties, and the Ledger gains quiet control of several field-shrine relic sources (M3 / Monopoly clock). The grave-field's drift worsens unattended (advancing the Vale clock), and Widow Hessit's contact matures into the Vale's first Gravecaller cell — meaning the desperate, not the player, become the truth-seekers here.
