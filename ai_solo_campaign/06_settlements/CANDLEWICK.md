## AI Use

Load when the player travels the southern Concord road toward the Ashgarden Vale, follows the Concord Script / archive-cairn / Remnant threads, or needs a tidy farming-and-rushlight village with a scholarly streak. Candlewick is the Reach's gentlest, most prosperous village and the place a visiting scholar would lodge; ~1.5 days south of Hollowmere.

## One-Sentence Identity

A prim, candle-making farm-village on the firm southern ground, proud of its tidy graves and its little Concord library — and the first place outsiders' eyes turn toward the old language.

## First Impression

South of the fens the ground firms into real pasture, and Candlewick sits among hedged fields and rush-ponds, a village of whitewashed cob and thatch that actually looks *cared for*. The air smells of tallow and beeswax — Candlewick makes the Reach's candles and rushlights, including the Mourners' funeral tapers. The graveyard is the neatest in the Reach, every stone straight, every plot salted. On the green stands a curiosity: a roofed-over Concord **archive-stone**, half a wall of carved old script that the village has protected for generations without being able to read it.

## Public Overview

Candlewick is the Reach's breadbasket-in-miniature and its candle-works. It sits on firm Vale-edge ground, so its rites still mostly *hold* — which has made it a refuge for families fleeing the deep fens and a point of quiet pride bordering on smugness. The **Reachward Compact** governs through a respected hayward. The **Mourners' Circle** is strong and traditional here. Lately the village has drawn an unusual visitor: scholars from Caradril come to study the archive-stone, and the **Concord Remnant** has taken an interest. Candlewick is wary of the attention but flattered by it.

## DM-Only Truth

Candlewick's archive-stone is a genuine **Concord record-marker** — and one of its panels, if read, is a fragment of the truth (a clear M6/M9 seed under a language gate). Lector Briss lodges here when in the Reach; this is the natural place for the player to begin **learning Concord Script** (Hook 8 / the key clue gate). The village's rites "hold" not because Candlewick is blessed but because it sits at the *outer edge* of the shrine's pull — proof, read the right way, of M5's geography. The hayward has quietly received a Remnant offer to "purchase and protect" the archive-stone, which would strip the Reach of its most accessible Concord text.

## Local Leadership

**Hayward Tomas Greel** of the Reachward Compact — a careful, prosperous farmer-official, proud of Candlewick's order and quietly tempted by the Remnant's coin for the archive-stone. He is honest but provincial and slow to believe the crisis will ever reach his tidy fields.

## Important NPCs

- **Hayward Tomas Greel** (Compact; secondary) — village head; tempted by the Remnant's offer.
- **Lector Briss** (Concord Remnant; major — already in MAJOR_NPCS) — lodges here; teaches Concord Script; honest dupe.
- **Chandler-master Yorrin Tay** (secondary) — runs the tallow works; supplies funeral tapers Reach-wide.
- **Mourner Edda** (minor) — keeps the Straight Yard; defensive of village tradition.
- **Old Beeman Crick** (minor) — gossip, mead, and floor-space for travelers.

See `../08_npcs/SECONDARY_NPCS.md`.

## Factions Present

Reachward Compact (governs), Mourners' Circle (strong, traditional), Ashen Wardens (welcome, rarely needed yet), Concord Remnant (scholarly interest; Briss). No Gravecaller cell — Candlewick is too orderly. The Ledger buys candles but keeps no house.

## Shops and Services

- **The Beeman's table** — informal lodging (4 sp), good food, honey-mead.
- The Tallow Works (candles, rushlights, lamp-oil, funeral tapers; bulk rates).
- A blacksmith and a competent herbalist-midwife; a chapel of the Three Thresholds with a real (if minor) clergy healer — the only reliable magical healing between Hollowmere and the Vale.
- **Concord Script tutelage** from Briss when present (the key clue gate; a downtime activity).

## Laws and Customs

- Compact law, gently enforced by the hayward and two part-time wardmen.
- Strong customs: graves kept perfectly straight; funeral tapers from the local works only; outsiders judged by their manners.
- The archive-stone is village-sacred; defacing or "selling" it is the great local taboo (which is why Greel's temptation is secret).

## Current Tensions

- **The Remnant's offer** — Greel weighing whether to let scholars take the archive-stone "for safekeeping."
- **Refugee strain** — fen families crowding in; old residents resent it.
- **Smugness vs. dread** — the village insists the crisis won't reach it; the wiser folk aren't sure.
- **Briss's questions** — the Lector's reading of the stone is making some villagers uneasy without knowing why.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- A panel of the archive-stone is a fragment of the harvest-truth (M6/M9 seed; language gate).
- Candlewick's rites hold because it's at the edge of the shrine's pull (M5 geography).
- Greel has a secret Remnant purchase-offer for the stone.
- Briss is a sincere dupe; disillusioning him here can begin to shatter the Remnant (Secret 13).

- **"The Scholar's Request"** (Hook 8 destination) — escort/assist Briss; begin learning Concord Script.

## Possible Scenes

- Briss on the green, tracing the carved script, lighting up as he half-translates a line that troubles him.
- The hayward, over honey-mead, fishing for the player's opinion on "an offer from Caradril."
- A fen-refugee family turned away, exposing the village's cold edge.
- The first Candlewick rite to *wobble* — the crisis arriving at the tidy graveyard at last.

## Law And Threat Mechanics

- **Watch response:** light — the hayward and **2 part-time wardmen** (**Guard-like**, AC 14, ~11 HP). This is a safe, orderly village.
- **Social DCs:** persuade Greel against the sale DC 14 (DC 18 to push him toward it); convince Briss to teach you DC 10 (he is eager); read the archive-stone — gated by knowing Concord Script (no Script = no read; with Comprehend Languages, DC 13 Investigation for the meaningful panel).
- **Rest/recovery:** the Beeman's table (safe long rest); the Threshold chapel offers real low-level divine healing — the player's best non-fen recovery point.
- **Solo danger:** very low. Candlewick is a deliberate "safe village" / clue-and-services hub.

## Consequences If Ignored

If the player never visits, the Concord Script gate is still reachable through Briss elsewhere, but Candlewick's archive-stone may be sold to the Remnant (removing the most accessible M6/M9 text from the Reach and strengthening the Reclamation clock). The village's false security eventually cracks as the pull spreads south — a later, grimmer scene if the player returns. Briss, unmet, remains a dupe.
