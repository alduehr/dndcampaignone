## AI Use

**Mixed secrecy.** The five recurring adversary profiles a solo PC meets again and again in levels 1–4. Use these as the standard Act 1 "stat vocabulary" so the DM never improvises a major early threat. Each profile is **D&D 5e/2024-compatible** (no copied stat blocks; reference baselines + custom notes) and is built for **one player character**: single foes or small telegraphed groups, morale, escape routes, and a noncombat alternative every time. Stat references match `SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md` and the NPC mechanical profiles. **Player-Safe** lines may be narrated; **DM-Only** lines (hidden faction links, the harvest cause) must be earned through play and capped at R1 per `../11_mysteries_and_secrets/REVELATION_MAP.md`. Act 1 has **no boss**; its antagonist is the quiet, faceless leakage and the human pressures around it.

## How To Use These Profiles

- Pick the threat that fits the scene/zone, then scale by the **Scaling** line to the PC's level.
- **Always telegraph** before any fight (cold, fog, a shifting casket-lid, raised voices, a chalked mark).
- **Always offer a noncombat out** (a true rite, parley, a bribe, a retreat, an ally fetched).
- Recurring named NPCs (Cole Ashby, Tallytooth Ren, Veska Dunn) use their own NPC entries; this file gives the *generic* recurring threats plus the two named radicals most likely to fight early.

## Threat 1 — The Restless Remembrance (the failed-rite dead)

> The signature Act 1 threat. Mostly a roleplay/rite scene, not a fight. A sorrowful dead person who will not pass.

- **Type:** Undead (Remembrance-leakage). **Size:** Medium. **Role:** lone haunting / clue-bearer.
- **CR/level band:** ~1/2 (L1–2) rising to ~2 (L3–4).
- **AC:** 12. **HP:** ~17 (L1–2) → ~30 (L3–4). **Speed:** 0 ft., fly 30 ft. (hover); drifts toward the basin.
- **Ability emphasis:** Cha and Wis (a lingering will); weak Str/Con (incorporeal).
- **Saves:** Wis +2. **Skills:** Insight +2 (it remembers faces). **Senses:** darkvision 60 ft.; passive Perception 11. **Languages:** understands what it knew in life; speaks haltingly, half a beat behind.
- **Damage:** resistant to nonmagical bludgeoning/piercing/slashing; vulnerable to a **true rite** (see below); immune to poison/exhaustion; can move through walls but is *pinned to a place or grief*.
- **Actions:**
  - *Sorrow-Touch* (only if provoked/cornered): melee spell attack +4, ~2d6 cold (necrotic flavor), and the target feels the dead's last memory.
  - *Cold of the Quiet* (recharge): a 10-ft. chill; Con save DC 12 or briefly chilled (–10 ft. speed, telegraphed).
- **Traits:**
  - *Pinned by Grief* — it lingers over an unfinished thing (a person, a place, a word). Resolve the grief and it can pass without a blow.
  - *Layable* — a **true rite** (Religion DC 13; DC 11 with a Warden assisting) lays it to rest; this is the intended resolution, not damage.
  - *Drift* — left alone, it drifts waterward (a visible **C-M5** clue).
- **Tactics:** It does **not** ambush. It pleads, repeats, mistakes the living for the dead it loved. It only lashes out if attacked, dragged, or if its grief is mocked.
- **Morale:** Will not "flee" — it dissipates when laid, or withdraws into fog/stone if overwhelmed and re-forms later (an unresolved-failure consequence).
- **Where/when it appears:** any failing funeral (`Q_THE_FAILING_FUNERAL`, Hook 9); roadside and fen drifts; **worse and tougher the nearer the basin** (Greywater Holm worst). Wren is a unique, stronger version (see `MAJOR_NPCS.md` #17) — never a default fight.
- **Faction links:** Ashen Wardens lay them; Mourners mourn them; Gravecallers *speak* with them.
- **DM-Only:** these are harvest leakage drawn to the keystone shrine (M5/M1). Their drift and the geography-of-failure are field-proof of R1 — never explain the cause in Act 1.
- **Noncombat alternatives:** true rite (Religion), parley (Insight/Persuasion DC 12–13), resolve the unfinished thing, fetch a Warden (Hale).
- **Retreat:** always — they are slow and place-bound; walk away and they stay.
- **Solo danger:** low (low-moderate basin-adjacent). A telegraphed, mournful encounter; cheap death is impossible if the DM follows the telegraph rule.
- **Scaling:** L1–2 ~17 HP, soften the rite DC by 1, Hale does most of the work. L3–4 ~30 HP, may need two rite successes or have a relic complication; basin-adjacent versions upgrade toward Threat 2.

## Threat 2 — The Wrathful Remembrance (a provoked or stirred haunt)

> What a Restless Remembrance becomes when desecrated, mishandled, or stirred by a radical. The "this one is actually dangerous" version — always telegraphed first.

- **Type:** Undead (Remembrance-leakage, angered). **Size:** Medium. **Role:** telegraphed solo combat.
- **CR/level band:** ~1 (L2) rising to ~3 (L4).
- **AC:** 13. **HP:** ~22 (L2) → ~45 (L4). **Speed:** fly 40 ft. (hover).
- **Ability emphasis:** Cha (rage-will), Dex. **Saves:** Wis +3, Cha +3. **Skills:** Stealth +4 (fades into fog). **Senses:** darkvision 60 ft.; passive Perception 12. **Languages:** as Threat 1, but it has stopped listening.
- **Damage:** resistant to nonmagical physical and to cold; immune to poison/charm/exhaustion; **a true rite still works** but is harder (Religion DC 15) once it is wrathful.
- **Actions:**
  - *Grave-Reach*: melee spell attack +5, ~3d6 cold/necrotic; on a hit the target makes a Wis save DC 13 or is frightened until end of next turn.
  - *Wail of the Unmade* (recharge 5–6): 15-ft. cone; Wis save DC 13, ~2d8 psychic and frightened (half/no-fright on success). **Telegraphed** by a rising, doubled keening.
- **Traits:**
  - *Incorporeal Drift* — moves through creatures/objects (1d10 force if it ends inside something).
  - *Still Layable* — calming or out-ritualing it (Religion DC 15, or addressing the grief that enraged it) ends the fight without killing.
  - *Sunset on Mercy* — if reduced below half and offered the true rite, it can *accept* (Insight/Persuasion DC 13) and pass — a built-in off-ramp.
- **Tactics:** Strikes the nearest living thing, uses fog and walls to break line of sight, keens when grouped against. It is grief turned to fury, not a tactician.
- **Morale:** Fights until laid or until offered rest; recedes into fog at low HP if mercy is refused (re-forms later — a consequence).
- **Where/when:** the Greyfens deep (Drift-Line if provoked), basin-adjacent failures, sites Cole Ashby's radicals have stirred, the Barrow's edge. Rare in L1–2; a L3–4 escalation.
- **Faction links:** Gravecaller radicals (Ashby) provoke them; Wardens dread them.
- **DM-Only:** a Remembrance the reawakening pull has frayed past sorrow into anger (M5/M1). Do not narrate the cause.
- **Noncombat alternatives:** lay it (harder), talk it down at low HP, address the desecration, withdraw and return with a Warden.
- **Retreat:** possible but it pursues within its haunt; leaving the haunt ends pursuit.
- **Solo danger:** moderate — the genuine Act 1 combat threat. Telegraphed by keening/cold; the mercy off-ramp prevents cheap death.
- **Scaling:** L2 ~22 HP single foe; L3 add the Wail; L4 ~45 HP and a chilled-ground hazard. Never field two of these against a lone L1–2 PC without an ally (Hale/Sashe) or escape route.

## Threat 3 — Frontier Toughs (bandits, claim-jumpers, enforcers, smugglers)

> The recurring *human* low-stakes threat: people who want coin, salvage, or to scare the player off — not to murder. Covers road toll-bandits, Heights claim-jumpers, Ledger collections muscle, and edgy smugglers.

- **Type:** Humanoid. **Size:** Medium. **Role:** small telegraphed group; mostly social/intimidation.
- **CR/level band:** 1/8–1/2 each; field 2–4 vs. a solo PC only with terrain, a toll demand, or an escape route.
- **AC:** 12 (leather) / 13 (a leader). **HP:** ~11 (rank-and-file) / ~22–40 (a named leader, e.g. Tallytooth Ren). **Speed:** 30 ft.
- **Ability emphasis:** Str or Dex. **Saves:** none notable (leaders +Str or +Dex). **Skills:** Intimidation +2, Stealth +2 (bandits), Survival +2 (Heights). **Senses:** passive Perception 10. **Languages:** Common.
- **Damage:** none special.
- **Actions:**
  - *Weapon* (scimitar/club/light crossbow): +3 to hit, ~1d6+1.
  - *Shake You Down* (social): a demand, not a blow — "the toll's gone up, friend."
  - Leader: *Grapple & Threaten* (Tallytooth-style): grapple (Athletics +5) to intimidate, rarely to harm.
- **Traits:**
  - *Here For Coin, Not Corpses* — they prefer a toll, a bribe, or a scare to a real fight.
  - *Morale-Prone* — break at half group HP or when the leader falls/flees (morale check; usually flee).
- **Tactics:** Surround and bluster; loose a warning shot; collect and leave. If the player pays, talks, or looks tough, they melt away.
- **Morale:** Low — flee or parley once it costs them.
- **Where/when:** Pale Road and causeways (toll-bandits), Sunder Heights (claim-jumpers, scavengers), Hollowmere/Saltmargin (Ledger muscle — Tallytooth Ren), fen back-routes (Gull's smugglers).
- **Faction links:** Cinder Ledger (enforcers/collections — Hook 10); independent bandits; smuggler network (Gull).
- **DM-Only:** Ledger muscle is squeezing families over failed-rite debt (Secret 12 seed); a turnable enforcer (Tallytooth) hates the work.
- **Noncombat alternatives:** pay the toll, intimidate back, talk, deputize the watch, expose the debt-paperwork (Hook 10), simply leave.
- **Retreat:** easy — they are not committed killers.
- **Solo danger:** low (low-moderate if 3+ with terrain). The social-first design keeps it safe.
- **Scaling:** L1 — 2 bandits or a lone enforcer, soften DCs. L3–4 — a leader + 2 toughs, or a smuggler crew that fights smart with terrain; a named leader (Ren ~40 HP) catches-and-threatens rather than kills.

## Threat 4 — The Cult Radical's Hand (Cole Ashby and stirred Gravecallers)

> The *gentle* Gravecallers prefer to talk (see `Q_ACT1_GRAVECALLER_A_LAST_WORD`). This profile is the **radical wing** — Cole Ashby and the recruits he hardens — the one early human threat that can actually mean harm, and only if the player crosses them or races them to the fens.

- **Type:** Humanoid (zealot cell). **Role:** telegraphed confrontation; rival, not assassin.
- **CR/level band:** Ashby ~1 (lite Tier-1); recruits 1/8–1/4.
- **Cole Ashby:** **AC 13, HP ~30, Speed 30 ft.** (matches `MAJOR_NPCS.md` #11). Ability emphasis Str/Cha; **Skills** Religion +3, Intimidation +3; passive Perception 11; Common + the Gravecaller Knock.
  - *Actions:* two melee strikes (handaxe/breaking-bar) +5, ~1d6+3 each; *Zealot's Cry* (1/day): allies/recruits gain advantage and ignore fear for 1 round.
  - *Trait — Conviction:* advantage on saves vs. fear/charm; he believes utterly.
  - *Trait — Breaking-Tools (DM):* he carries Concord breaking-tools and a partial shrine map (Clock #5). In Act 1 these are a *foreshadow and a danger*, never used to climactic effect.
- **Recruits (1–2):** **AC 11–12, HP ~14**, Commoner/Acolyte-like; club or knife +3, ~1d4+1; flee if Ashby falls.
- **Tactics:** Ashby talks first — fast, righteous, brittle — then fights only if blocked from the fens/shrine or if "the dead's enemy" stands in his way. He targets whoever threatens the cell or the Breaking.
- **Morale:** Ashby does **not** flee easily (Conviction); recruits break at half. **Built-in off-ramp:** he can be *talked down* (Persuasion/Insight DC 14, addressing the catastrophe he denies) — turning a fight into a foreshadow.
- **Where/when:** the Greyfens (Drowned Blind, Mother Tongue Pool), the shrine's fen approaches, a botched cult contact, a purge gone loud.
- **Faction links:** Gravecallers (radical wing); opposed by the gentle wing (Sister Knell) and the Compact (purge).
- **DM-Only:** Ashby races toward shattering the keystone (catastrophe ending seed); he does not grasp the consequence (M10 foreshadow). Never let him reach the keystone in Act 1.
- **Noncombat alternatives:** talk him down, ally with Knell against him, expose him to the watch, out-pace him to a site, warn the cell.
- **Retreat:** the player can always withdraw from the fens (with Sashe, safely).
- **Solo danger:** low-moderate (Ashby + 2 recruits). Telegraphed by his ranting; the talk-down off-ramp prevents a forced lethal fight.
- **Scaling:** L2 — Ashby alone, mostly a scene. L3–4 — Ashby + 2 recruits, or a stirred Wrathful Remembrance (Threat 2) at his side; he hits harder and the breaking-tools loom.

## Threat 5 — Rival Salvage Crews (Remnant & Ledger field agents)

> The recurring *rival* threat: armed scholar-salvagers and buyers racing the player (and each other) for relics in the Heights and at Concord sites. They win by maneuver and money, not murder — recurring faces, not cannon fodder.

- **Type:** Humanoid (mixed crew: a leader-caster + hired bravos). **Role:** telegraphed rival encounter; chase/standoff/negotiation.
- **CR/level band:** leader ~1–2; bravos 1/4–1/2; field a leader + 2–3 bravos.
- **Crew leader (Veska Dunn / a Ledger buyer's agent):** **AC 13, HP ~30, Speed 30 ft.** (matches `SECONDARY_NPCS.md` Veska Dunn). Int/Dex; **Skills** Arcana +4, History +4, Investigation +4, Stealth +3; passive Perception 12; Common + Concord Script.
  - *Actions:* dagger/light crossbow +4, ~1d6+1; **spells (Mage-lite):** *Mage Armor, Misty Step, Shatter (1/day), Mage Hand, Light*.
  - *Trait — Wins By Maneuver:* prefers to secure the relic and *leave* via Misty Step rather than trade blows.
- **Bravos (2–3):** **AC 13, HP ~16**, Scout/Thug-like; shortsword/shortbow +4, ~1d6+2; guard the dig, block the exit, flee at half if the leader is gone.
- **Tactics:** Claim the site first, post bravos, parley with veiled threats ("we can *hire* you, or we can race you"). They grab the relic and withdraw; they fight defensively only if cornered.
- **Morale:** Leader flees (Misty Step) rather than die — **a recurring rival, never a cheap death**; bravos break when the leader's gone.
- **Where/when:** the Sunder Heights and Deep Adit approaches (`Q_RACE_NORTH`), Concord cairns and the Whispering Cairn, the Candlewick archive-stone, any "old glass" find.
- **Faction links:** Concord Remnant (Veska Dunn / Reclamation, Clock #7) and Cinder Ledger buyers (Nessa Coyle / Monopoly, Clock #6) — often racing *each other* too.
- **DM-Only:** the Remnant crews serve the inner circle's aim to seize the harvest (M6/M7 foreshadow); the Ledger crews feed the monopoly. Neither names the deeper truth in Act 1.
- **Noncombat alternatives:** out-race them, negotiate a split/hire, take the job for one side, expose one to the other, beat them to the site by stealth.
- **Retreat:** easy — they want the relic, not the player.
- **Solo danger:** moderate (leader + 3 bravos with a dig as terrain). Telegraphed approach and parley-first design keep it safe; flee/negotiate always works.
- **Scaling:** L2 — a lone buyer + 1 bravo, social. L3–4 — Veska Dunn + 2–3 bravos at a dig, a real standoff; the leader's Shatter/Misty Step make it a *clever* fight, not a slog.

## Solo-Play Rules (apply to every threat here)

- **Telegraph, then offer an out.** No Act 1 threat ambushes a level-1 PC to death before a meaningful choice.
- **One foe, or a small group with terrain/stealth/escape.** Never a party-sized mob against a lone PC without allies (Hale, Sashe, the watch) or a way out.
- **Morale and goals.** The dead want rest; toughs want coin; radicals want the Breaking; crews want relics. Almost no Act 1 foe fights to the death.
- **Allies on tap:** Brother Hale (rites/companion), Old Sashe (fens), the Hollowmere watch (Sergeant Hesk), Warden Pell/Sirrin (the dead). See `ACT_1_NPC_GUIDE.md`.
- **Retreat always exists** from every Act 1 site (zone tables in `SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md`).
- **Reveal cap R1.** Narrate cold, drift, and dread — never the harvest, the Concord's sin, or the Court.
