## AI Use

Solo-tuned encounter tables for the Tollwood, by depth-zone. **Designed for ONE player character.** The Tollwood is the most dangerous Ring 1 region — real predators and bandits near the road, hard-to-lay old dead and a gated ancient power in the deep wood. **Danger rises with depth; telegraph it.** Roll d12 by zone or pick to fit the scene/clocks. Stat references are D&D 5e/2024-compatible baselines (no copied stat blocks); scale per Scaling Notes. Always telegraph; always offer a non-combat option.

## Encounter Type Legend
- **[Dead]** = a Remembrance/old forest dead — near-wood dead are layable (Religion DC 13–15); deep dead resist laying (offering/parley/retreat matter).
- **[Beast]** = a real forest predator; telegraphed by sign; morale-driven.
- **[Folk]** = NPCs (Tollmen, loggers, woodfolk, Gravecallers, travelers); social or combat.
- **[Hazard]** = environmental; a check, not a fight.
- **[Clue]** = a breadcrumb (deliver, don't over-explain; cap Tollwood reveals — the deep secret is gated/oblique, never the Court).

## Scaling Notes

- **Lvl 2–3:** stay in Zone 1; soften predators; the toll is social; the deep wood is rumor and dread, not reached.
- **Lvl 4–5 (center of mass):** Zone 2; old dead and predators are real; the Hanging Oaks open.
- **Lvl 6–7+:** Zone 3 is reachable but **stays gated** — the presence is never a kill-target, the Root-Dark never opens, reveals capped at the oblique M5/M6 fragment.
- **Always:** the deep wood pushes the unworthy out *alive* via dread and misdirection; lethal outcomes only follow the player escalating against a vastly superior power, heavily telegraphed. Near-wood dead are layable; deep dead are honored, not slain. **Never name the Hollow Court or the harvest mechanism here.**
