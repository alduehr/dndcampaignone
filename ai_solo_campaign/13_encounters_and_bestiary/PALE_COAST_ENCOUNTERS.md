## AI Use

Solo-tuned encounter tables for the Pale Coast, by zone. **Designed for ONE player character.** The sea is the great antagonist — tides, storms, and cliffs over monsters. Wreckers and the drowned dead are the recurring threats; the offshore Skerries are the deadliest reach. Roll d12 by zone or pick to fit the scene/clocks. Stat references are D&D 5e/2024-compatible baselines (no copied stat blocks); scale per Scaling Notes. Always telegraph the tide and weather; always offer a non-combat option.

## Encounter Type Legend
- **[Dead]** = a drowned Remembrance — near-shore ones layable by a salt-and-tide rite (Religion DC 13–15) or parley; the Lamp/Skerries dead resist easy laying.
- **[Folk]** = NPCs (fisher-folk, wreckers, smugglers, Ledger men, scholars); social or combat.
- **[Sea]** = a sea-beast or maritime predator; telegraphed; morale-driven.
- **[Hazard]** = environmental (tide, storm, cliff, fog); a check, not a fight.
- **[Clue]** = a breadcrumb (deliver, don't over-explain; cap reveals — the maritime node is gated/oblique, never the Court).

## Scaling Notes

- **Lvl 2–3:** stay in Zone 1; soften sea-beasts and wreckers; the Skerries are rumor and dread, not reached; the tide is the main teacher.
- **Lvl 4–5 (center of mass):** Zone 2; wreckers, the drowned, and the Lamp are real; the cove caves open.
- **Lvl 6–7+:** Zone 3 (the Skerries) is reachable but **stays gated** — the worst drowned and the Skerry-Keeper are not low-level kills, the Sea-Throat never opens, reveals capped at the oblique fragment.
- **Always:** the tide and weather push the unworthy off the sea *alive* (a built-in valve); lethal sea-outcomes follow ignored tide-tables and weather, all telegraphed. Near-shore drowned are layable by the salt-and-tide rite; the Lamp's and Skerries' dead are honored/escaped. **Never name the Hollow Court or the harvest mechanism here.**
