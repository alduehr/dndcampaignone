## Summary

Faith in Orrun centers on the **Three Thresholds** rather than a crowded pantheon: three divine principles governing Birth, Living, and Death. There is no grand church anymore — the Concord was the closest thing, and it fell. Today faith is fragmented between the Mourners' Circle (folk death-faith), threshold shrines, and scattered clergy. Clerics, paladins, and divine casters function normally in 5e terms; the gods are real but distant.

---

## The Three Thresholds (Player-Safe)

| Threshold | Domain | Symbol | Common Title |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Opening** | Birth, beginnings, the unborn, hope | An open ring | "the First Threshold" |
| **The Hearth** | Living, kinship, oaths, the everyday world | A flame within a square | "the Threshold We Stand In" |
| **The Last Door** | Death, memory, passage, the dead | A closed door with a single star | "the Last Threshold" |

- Most people honor all three at different life moments; few are exclusive devotees.
- 5e clerics/paladins draw from these as appropriate domains (Life, Light, Grave, War, Peace, etc. all fit under one Threshold or another).
- The **Last Door** is the most actively worshipped in the Reach because of the death-rite crisis.

## Clergy and Practice

- There is no unified church. Clergy are local: a village rite-speaker, a shrine-keeper, a wandering preacher.
- The **Mourners' Circle** (see factions) is the largest organized faith in the Reach — folk death-keepers, not a temple hierarchy.
- The **Ashen Wardens** are quasi-religious: rite-keepers who lay the restless dead. Many think of them as holy; they are technically an order, not a church.
- Divine magic works normally. A "failed rite" (the campaign's signature horror) is *not* a failure of the priest's faith — it is the world failing, which terrifies clergy who cannot explain it.

## Folk Beliefs (Player-Safe)

- "Salt at the sill, a star at the door" — common funeral protections against a Remembrance.
- The dead should be **named once and released**, never called back. Speaking a dead name three times "opens the door wrong" (folk fear of the Gravecallers).
- Mirrors are covered in a house of the recently dead so the dead "don't see a way to stay."

## DM-Only Truth About the Gods

- The Three Thresholds are **real but distant** divine principles. They do not micromanage the world and did not cause the Quietfall.
- The **Last Door is partly a mechanism the Concord tapped** (see `COSMOLOGY.md`). The gods did not sanction the harvest and are not its authors; the Concord exploited the Last Door without divine permission.
- No god in this setting is secretly the villain. The campaign's evil is **institutional and human** (the Concord/Hollow Court), which is thematically important: the horror is made by people, not gods.
- Clerics who pray for guidance about the crisis receive *grief and silence*, not answers — fuel for the mystery, not a shortcut to the truth.
