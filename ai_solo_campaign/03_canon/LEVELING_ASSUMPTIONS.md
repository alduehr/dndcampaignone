## AI Use

Load when deciding whether to level the player up. Use **milestone leveling** tied to the triggers below. Communicate level-ups at natural rest/reflection beats.

## Leveling System

**Milestone leveling.** Chosen because it keeps pacing in the AI DM's hands, rewards narrative achievement, and avoids grind in a solo game. No XP tracking required.

## Level Pacing (Anticipated)

| Level Range | Act | Approx. Sessions |
|---|---|---|
| 1–4 | Act 1 — The Reach and the failing rite | 8–15 |
| 5–8 | Act 2 — Factions and the spreading harvest | 10–20 |
| 9–12 | Act 3 — The truth of the Concord | 10–20 |
| 13–16 | Act 4 — The Hollow Court | 10–15 |
| 17–20 | Act 5 — The fate of the Remembrance | 5–10 |

## Milestone Triggers (Act 1 Concrete Examples)

| To Level | Trigger |
|---|---|
| 1 → 2 | Resolve the opening failed rite (Wren's situation) one way or another. |
| 2 → 3 | Complete a first real quest and pick a faction thread to pull. |
| 3 → 4 | Reach and explore a Concord site (first dungeon) and survive its truth. |
| 4 → 5 | Confront the first faction-level decision point and the Act 1 threat; the harvest's reality becomes undeniable. |

Later acts' triggers are defined in `../12_campaign_arc/LEVEL_1_TO_20_PROGRESSION.md`.

## Solo-Play Leveling Notes

- Lean toward the faster end of pacing; a solo PC has no party to share the load.
- After any near-death or major revelation, check whether a milestone is appropriate.
- Avoid long flat stretches with no growth, especially levels 1–4 where survivability is thin.
- Pair level-ups with downtime/rest so the player can manage resources.
