## Purpose

Defines how leveling works in this campaign — milestone vs. XP, pacing expectations, and what level milestones mean narratively. Keeps the AI DM consistent about when to level the player up and how to communicate it.

## Level Pacing (Anticipated)

| Level Range | Narrative Stage | Approximate Sessions |
|---|---|---|
| 1–4 | Act 1 — Establishing the world and early conflict | 8–15 sessions |
| 5–8 | Act 2 — Rising stakes, faction involvement deepens | 10–20 sessions |
| 9–12 | Act 3 — Major revelations, high-stakes decisions | 10–20 sessions |
| 13–16 | Act 4 — Confronting the hidden truth, endgame begins | 10–15 sessions |
| 17–20 | Act 5 — Resolution, final confrontation, epilogues | 5–10 sessions |

*These are estimates. Player agency may accelerate or slow pacing.*

## Level-Up Triggers (to be established during Stage 1)

*What events, accomplishments, or moments trigger a level-up?*

*Examples of good milestone triggers:*
- Completing a major quest
- Defeating a significant villain
- Major revelation achieved
- Significant faction choice made
- Surviving a defining crucible event

---

## Solo-Play Leveling Notes

- Solo characters may need to level slightly faster than a standard party to compensate for lack of party support.
- Resource management is more critical solo — healing items and short rest mechanics matter more.
- Avoid long stretches with no level-up if the campaign is brutal.
