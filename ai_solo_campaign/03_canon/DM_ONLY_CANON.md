## The Hidden Truth (Campaign Core Secret)

The Quietfall was not a disaster that *happened to* the Custodian Concord. **The Concord caused it on purpose, and a hidden remnant is repeating it deliberately, right now, in the Sundering Reach.**

Layered truth, innermost last:

1. **Surface (public):** The Last Door "sticks" and the dead linger. Nobody knows why.
2. **First truth:** The lingering of the dead (the Remembrance) is not a curse or divine punishment. It is a **harvestable resource**. A dead person's memory and identity can be drawn out, stored, refined, and used — to extend life, to power great works, to resurrect, to rule.
3. **Second truth:** The Custodian Concord never "kept the dead at peace" for the dead's sake. The Concord was a vast, benevolent-seeming **memory-harvesting engine**. Its shrines quietly siphoned a thin tithe of the dead's Remembrance to sustain the Concord's leaders and its golden age. The afterlife (the Quiet Country) was being slowly drained.
4. **Third truth (the Quietfall):** A century ago, the inner Concord realized the Quiet Country was nearly empty — that they had been consuming the dead so thoroughly that souls were beginning to fail to form, and the *living* were starting to be born "thin." To hide the crisis and reset the system, the Concord's innermost circle, the **Custodians**, deliberately collapsed their own order in a controlled demolition (the Quietfall), severing the great shrines so the harvest would pause and the Quiet Country could refill. They sacrificed their own age to buy time. Almost all of them died doing it. The public remembers only catastrophe.
5. **Innermost truth (the live threat):** A small group of original Custodians **survived the Quietfall by storing themselves as Remembrance** — they are not alive and not dead; they persist as preserved memory-beings. This is the **Hollow Court**. They have been dormant for a century, refilling. Now they judge the Quiet Country "full enough," and they are **restarting the harvest** in the Sundering Reach, beginning with the great drowned shrine beneath Hollowmere. The accelerating death-rite failures are not decay — they are the **machine waking up**.

**The central campaign question the player will gradually uncover:** Should the harvest be restarted (controlled, the Hollow Court's plan), permanently destroyed (freeing the dead but ending easy resurrection and possibly civilization's stability), seized (handed to a single faction), or transformed into something consensual and just? The endgame turns on this choice.

## Villain True Identity and Agenda

There is no single mustache-twirling villain. The campaign has an **antagonist hierarchy**, each "right" from their own view:

- **Primary hidden power: The Hollow Court.** The surviving Custodians, persisting as preserved Remembrance-beings beneath the Reach. Agenda: restart the harvest "responsibly" to save civilization from the slow thinning, ruling as immortal stewards. They are not cruel; they are utilitarian and certain. Their leader is **Custodian Veyl** (see major NPCs). They cannot easily act in the living world and so work through agents who do not know whom they serve.
- **Active field antagonist (Acts 1–2): the reawakening shrine itself**, and the Hollow Court's unwitting and witting agents in the Reach — chiefly a faction leader who has been contacted (see Faction Hidden Agendas).
- **Rival claimant: the Concord Remnant's inner circle**, who want to seize the harvest to rebuild the Concord's empire — they think they are the heroes restoring order.
- **The tragic mirror: the Gravecallers**, who learned a fragment of the truth and are trying to *free* the dead — but their method (shattering the shrines) would collapse all resurrection and possibly the boundary between living and dead entirely.

The player can ally with, oppose, expose, or replace any of these.

## Cosmological Secrets

- **The Quiet Country (afterlife) is finite and was nearly emptied.** It is refilling slowly. This is why resurrection is dangerous and why souls were beginning to fail.
- **Remembrance is the raw substance of identity** — what makes a soul a *someone*. Gods do not control it; the Concord learned to. This is closer to industrial alchemy of the soul than divine power.
- **The gods of the Three Thresholds are real but distant**, and the Last Door is partly a *mechanism* the Concord learned to tap, not purely a divine gate. Clergy do not know this.
- **Undeath in this setting is leaked or stolen Remembrance** given form. This reframes standard 5e undead as symptoms of the harvest where thematically relevant (the DM may flavor, not re-stat).
- **"Thin-born" people** — those born slightly empty since the Quietfall — exist and are a quiet, growing phenomenon. The player may be one (optional hook; player's choice).

## Stage 3 Additions (Region Deep Build — DM Context)

These extend, never contradict, the core secret. The Hollowmere shrine remains the **keystone**; the additions show it is the *center* of a network, not the only node.

- **The Concord roads and Star-Stones are the harvest network's conduits and relay-markers.** As the harvest reawakens, they "warm up": more Remembrances appear along them, cracked Star-Stones leak, and the river carries leakage downstream (linking Kettle Bridge → Reedford → the Pale Coast). The roads the player travels are literally the machine's arteries.
- **Minor and secondary nodes exist.** The **Sunken Tollhouse** (under Kettle Bridge) is a minor relay-node, leaking and drowning boatmen (M2/M5). The **Deep Adit** (under Harrowgast) is a *secondary* harvest node — a smaller cousin of the keystone — which the **Concord Remnant (Veska Dunn's crews) is racing to claim** for relic-supply and proof (the Reclamation clock made visible; M6/M7 foreshadow). The **Barrow of Nine Doors** (Greyfens) is a pre-Concord burial the network was built over and tapped; the drift jams against it.
- **"Old glass" = raw Remembrance relics** dug from the Heights; demonstrably store the dead (a direct M3 demonstration).
- **The geography of failure is the clearest field-proof of M5:** rites fail worse the nearer the basin (Greywater Holm worst; Candlewick "holds" only because it sits at the edge of the pull). Sashe's mapped drift converges on the basin.
- **The "thin" miners of Harrowgast** are early, local evidence of the thinning (M10 background) from over-near a leaking node. Do not over-explain in Act 1–2.
- **Thin-touch** is a custom, *reversible* mechanic (disadvantage on Death saves until a true rite / shrine long-rest) representing brief over-exposure to raw Remembrance — a telegraphed danger, never a cheap kill. See `../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md`, `../10_dungeons_and_ruins/THE_DEEP_ADIT.md`.
- **Reke walks the basin shore alone at night** (C-M4-3); the Surfacing Ruin carries readable Concord Script (C-M2-1). The shore is reachable safely in daylight (Hook 4); the deep shrine/under-shrine stays endgame-gated.

All Stage 3 clue access points feed the existing M1–M9 chains and the three-clue rule; **no new central mystery, faction, god, cosmology, or artifact was created.**

## Player Steering Note (DM Caution)

The PC is being subtly steered toward the shrine by a Hollow Court cutout (via Sefra Quick), but **the campaign must never railroad**. The steering is a hook, not a track. If the player ignores or resists it, the world clocks (`WORLD_CLOCKS.md`) advance the harvest anyway, and other factions act. The steering should feel like opportunity, never compulsion.
