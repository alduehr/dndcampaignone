## The Hidden Truth (Campaign Core Secret)

The Quietfall was not a disaster that *happened to* the Custodian Concord. **The Concord caused it on purpose, and a hidden remnant is repeating it deliberately, right now, in the Sundering Reach.**

Layered truth, innermost last:

1. **Surface (public):** The Last Door "sticks" and the dead linger. Nobody knows why.
2. **First truth:** The lingering of the dead (the Remembrance) is not a curse or divine punishment. It is a **harvestable resource**. A dead person's memory and identity can be drawn out, stored, refined, and used — to extend life, to power great works, to resurrect, to rule.
3. **Second truth:** The Custodian Concord never "kept the dead at peace" for the dead's sake. The Concord was a vast, benevolent-seeming **memory-harvesting engine**. Its shrines quietly siphoned a thin tithe of the dead's Remembrance to sustain the Concord's leaders and its golden age. The afterlife (the Quiet Country) was being slowly drained.
4. **Third truth (the Quietfall):** A century ago, the inner Concord realized the Quiet Country was nearly empty — that they had been consuming the dead so thoroughly that souls were beginning to fail to form, and the *living* were starting to be born "thin." To hide the crisis and reset the system, the Concord's innermost circle, the **Custodians**, deliberately collapsed their own order in a controlled demolition (the Quietfall), severing the great shrines so the harvest would pause and the Quiet Country could refill. They sacrificed their own age to buy time. Almost all of them died doing it. The public remembers only catastrophe.
5. **Innermost truth (the live threat):** A small group of original Custodians **survived the Quietfall by storing themselves as Remembrance** — they are not alive and not dead; they persist as preserved memory-beings. This is the **Hollow Court**. They have been dormant for a century, refilling. Now they judge the Quiet Country "full enough," and they are **restarting the harvest** in the Sundering Reach, beginning with the great drowned shrine beneath Hollowmere. The accelerating death-rite failures are not decay — they are the **machine waking up**.

**The central campaign question the player will gradually uncover:** Should the harvest be restarted (controlled, the Hollow Court's plan), permanently destroyed (freeing the dead but ending easy resurrection and possibly civilization's stability), seized (handed to a single faction), or transformed into something consensual and just? The endgame turns on this choice.

## Villain True Identity and Agenda

There is no single mustache-twirling villain. The campaign has an **antagonist hierarchy**, each "right" from their own view:

- **Primary hidden power: The Hollow Court.** The surviving Custodians, persisting as preserved Remembrance-beings beneath the Reach. Agenda: restart the harvest "responsibly" to save civilization from the slow thinning, ruling as immortal stewards. They are not cruel; they are utilitarian and certain. Their leader is **Custodian Veyl** (see major NPCs). They cannot easily act in the living world and so work through agents who do not know whom they serve.
- **Active field antagonist (Acts 1–2): the reawakening shrine itself**, and the Hollow Court's unwitting and witting agents in the Reach — chiefly a faction leader who has been contacted (see Faction Hidden Agendas).
- **Rival claimant: the Concord Remnant's inner circle**, who want to seize the harvest to rebuild the Concord's empire — they think they are the heroes restoring order.
- **The tragic mirror: the Gravecallers**, who learned a fragment of the truth and are trying to *free* the dead — but their method (shattering the shrines) would collapse all resurrection and possibly the boundary between living and dead entirely.

The player can ally with, oppose, expose, or replace any of these.

## Cosmological Secrets

- **The Quiet Country (afterlife) is finite and was nearly emptied.** It is refilling slowly. This is why resurrection is dangerous and why souls were beginning to fail.
- **Remembrance is the raw substance of identity** — what makes a soul a *someone*. Gods do not control it; the Concord learned to. This is closer to industrial alchemy of the soul than divine power.
- **The gods of the Three Thresholds are real but distant**, and the Last Door is partly a *mechanism* the Concord learned to tap, not purely a divine gate. Clergy do not know this.
- **Undeath in this setting is leaked or stolen Remembrance** given form. This reframes standard 5e undead as symptoms of the harvest where thematically relevant (the DM may flavor, not re-stat).
- **"Thin-born" people** — those born slightly empty since the Quietfall — exist and are a quiet, growing phenomenon. The player may be one (optional hook; player's choice).

See `COSMOLOGY.md` for the full DM-only cosmology.

## Stage 3 Additions (Region Deep Build — DM Context)

These extend, never contradict, the core secret. The Hollowmere shrine remains the **keystone**; the additions show it is the *center* of a network, not the only node.

- **The Concord roads and Star-Stones are the harvest network's conduits and relay-markers.** As the harvest reawakens, they "warm up": more Remembrances appear along them, cracked Star-Stones leak, and the river carries leakage downstream (linking Kettle Bridge → Reedford → the Pale Coast). The roads the player travels are literally the machine's arteries.
- **Minor and secondary nodes exist.** The **Sunken Tollhouse** (under Kettle Bridge) is a minor relay-node, leaking and drowning boatmen (M2/M5). The **Deep Adit** (under Harrowgast) is a *secondary* harvest node — a smaller cousin of the keystone — which the **Concord Remnant (Veska Dunn's crews) is racing to claim** for relic-supply and proof (the Reclamation clock made visible; M6/M7 foreshadow). The **Barrow of Nine Doors** (Greyfens) is a pre-Concord burial the network was built over and tapped; the drift jams against it.
- **"Old glass" = raw Remembrance relics** dug from the Heights; demonstrably store the dead (a direct M3 demonstration).
- **The geography of failure is the clearest field-proof of M5:** rites fail worse the nearer the basin (Greywater Holm worst; Candlewick "holds" only because it sits at the edge of the pull). Sashe's mapped drift converges on the basin.
- **The "thin" miners of Harrowgast** are early, local evidence of the thinning (M10 background) from over-near a leaking node. Do not over-explain in Act 1–2.
- **Thin-touch** is a custom, *reversible* mechanic (disadvantage on Death saves until a true rite / shrine long-rest) representing brief over-exposure to raw Remembrance — a telegraphed danger, never a cheap kill. See `../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md`, `../10_dungeons_and_ruins/THE_DEEP_ADIT.md`.
- **Reke walks the basin shore alone at night** (C-M4-3); the Surfacing Ruin carries readable Concord Script (C-M2-1). The shore is reachable safely in daylight (Hook 4); the deep shrine/under-shrine stays endgame-gated.

All Stage 3 clue access points feed the existing M1–M9 chains and the three-clue rule; **no new central mystery, faction, god, cosmology, or artifact was created.**

## Stage 4 Additions (Caradril — DM Context)

These extend, never contradict, the core secret. Caradril is the mid-game **library, bank, and battlefield** — the place where the conspiracy lives in daylight.

- **The "Stilling" is the harvest's pause.** Caradril's century of blessed prosperity is a *side-effect of the Quietfall pause* — with the harvest stopped, the region's drain eased, and the relief read as good fortune. **The city is, unknowingly, living inside the success of the very crime the player is uncovering.** This is a late, earned thematic key (M6/M9), never an early reveal.
- **The Vyre–Quorrin deal is negotiated in Caradril.** The Cinder Ledger's HQ (the Ledger Keep) and the Concord Remnant's true vault (the **Sealed Archive**) are both here; Vyre and Quorrin are bargaining their Remembrance monopoly *in this city*, each planning to betray the other. The city clock **C1 The Deal Closes** drives this; it feeds the existing Monopoly (#6) and Reclamation (#7) clocks.
- **The Sealed Archive holds the clearest written proof** of the harvest (C-M6-1) and the deliberate Quietfall (C-M9). It is the Act-3 truth-cache — Script-gated and act-paced (L9–12). **It points toward M7 but must never name the Hollow Court;** the apex stays for convergence elsewhere.
- **The Sunken Wards are a minor, dormant cousin of the Reach's harvest-network nodes** — a lower Concord level beneath the city that was part of the same continental system but was never a major node and has lain still through the Stilling. As the master harvest reawakens far off, the Wards *faintly warm* (C-M2 fragment: proof the network is continental). The Remnant has secretly mapped them. **They are NOT the keystone** — a deliberate smaller echo of the Reach's Deep Adit.
- **The Magisterium correspondent thread:** Councilor Reke corresponds with someone seated in the Tidewater Council. **This is a lead, never a named second Court agent.** Let the player investigate it; do not pre-resolve it into a reveal.
- **Caradril's own crisis is hidden in plain sight:** Highmourn's raise-dead failures are covered up by Mother Ysarra (Secret 7 — a city M1/M5 echo); thin-born refugees gather in the Sill (M10 made local); quiet-coin relics and the Crucible's smelting demonstrate M3 commercially and by accident. The city clock **C3 The City's Denial Cracks** tracks this.
- **No new central mystery, faction, god, cosmology, or artifact was created in Stage 4.** All city clue access feeds the existing M2/M3/M4/M6/M8/M9 chains and the three-clue rule. M3/M6/M9 remain reachable in the Reach too, so a player who never visits Caradril is never blocked.

## World Map Authority Pass (Geographic Truths — DM Context)

These extend, never contradict, the core secret. They record the **hidden geography** so the AI DM keeps the underground/endgame map consistent. See `../04_world_atlas/WORLD_MAP_AUTHORITY.md`, `WORLD_MAP_LAYERS.md` (Layers 2 & 7), and `WORLD_MAP_COORDINATES.md` (DM-flagged rows).

- **The mapped land's geography is the machine's geography.** The whole NW quarter tilts and drains toward the **Hollowmere basin**, which is the **lowest point and the keystone**. This is not coincidence — the Concord sited its central node at the natural sink. The "dead walk toward the water" because the keystone pulls them there. This spatial tell is player-observable (M5) but its cause is gated.
- **The Concord Deep** (new DM-only name) is the **buried node-network** — the "map beneath the map." Its hub is the keystone basin; its tapped nodes are the already-established sites: the **Deep Adit** (Harrowgast), the **Sunken Tollhouse** (Kettle Bridge), **Saint Veddow's Tomb** (Vale), the **Drowned Lamp** and **Skerry Shrine** (Coast), the **Sunken Wards** (Caradril), and the relay Star-Stones along the Concord roads. The roads the player walks literally trace the network's arteries. **This names nothing new about the cosmology or the Court — it is the spatial layout of the harvest network already established in Stage 3–7.**
- **Pre-Concord sites were built OVER, not into, the network:** the **Barrow of Nine Doors** (Greyfens) and the **Tollwood's Old Mast** are older than the Concord; the network bends around them. They are *not* nodes — they are why parts of the map (the deep wood) the Concord could not tame.
- **The endgame is vertical, not lateral.** The campaign's apex place — the **Under-Shrine / Drowned Keystone**, where the Hollow Court sleeps — is **straight down beneath Hollowmere**, the deepest level of the drowned shrine. The "forbidden region" is therefore placed at the **center-and-down** of the map. There is no distant forbidden continent; the arc keeps the climax under the starting town. Telegraph it as lethal (drowning, cold, dread) at every low level so a PC never suicides into it early.
- **Off-map / future Orrun:** the broader continent (the **Verdance Reaches** SE, the **Highmark Spine** passes far N) exists but is unbuilt and arc-optional. The Verdance Reaches is the natural Ring 2 corridor (richer inland Orrun, level 8–12). None of this is required to reach the endgame.
- **Map secrecy enforcement:** the **player-safe world map** (Layer 1 / Prompt 1) and the **regional map** (Prompt 3) must NEVER show the node-network lines, the Concord Deep, the Under-Shrine, or warming-node marks. The **DM-only map** (Layer 2/7, Prompt 2) carries them. Never hand a DM-layer map to the player.
- **No new central mystery, faction, god, cosmology, or artifact was created in this pass.** The new names (Pale Sea, Highmark Spine, Verdance Reaches, Concord Deep) are geographic features supporting the existing world; the Concord Deep and Under-Shrine are spatial descriptions of the already-established harvest network and keystone.

## Full-World Cartographic Expansion (Continental Geographic Truths — DM Context)

These extend, never contradict, the core secret. They record the **hidden truth about the whole continent's shape** so the AI DM keeps the world consistent as play (far in the future) opens beyond the NW frontier. See `../04_world_atlas/FULL_WORLD_MAP_AUTHORITY.md`, `FULL_WORLD_MAP_LAYERS.md` (Layers 2/4/7), and `FULL_WORLD_MAP_COORDINATES.md` (DM-flagged rows).

- **The continent is large; the campaign is its NW corner; the endgame is still vertical.** Opening the full map does **not** move the climax. The **Concord Deep** (buried harvest-network) and the **Under-Shrine / Drowned Keystone** stay **beneath the NW cluster, straight down under Hollowmere.** Never draw the network out across the continent; never relocate the Hollow Court's seat to a far land. The far map is breadth; the climax is depth-in-place.
- **The far Concord ruins are SURFACE history, not the machine.** The **Concord Heartlands / the Ruin'd Crown** (central-SE, astride the Greatspine) is the order's **fallen surface capital country** — proof of how vast the Concord was *above ground*, and a late-game treasure many factions covet. It is **NOT** a node, **NOT** the keystone, and **NOT** where the Court sleeps. The Concord Remnant covets it openly (a natural Act 3–4 ambition). Use it to corroborate M6/M9 (the Concord's true scale and sin) **without** ever implying the harvest-heart is there.
- **Pre-Concord echoes exist far off, too.** The **Saltmere drowned towns**, the **Drowned Steps** (submerged sea-causeway), and the **Cindern Waste's** buried works are *older-than-Concord* or surface echoes that thematically rhyme with the Reach's pre-Concord sites (the Barrow of Nine Doors, the Old Mast). They deepen the "the world has fallen and drowned before" motif. They are **flavor and far corroboration**, never required, never the keystone.
- **The Cinder Ledger and Concord Remnant have continental reach; the Reach factions do not.** The Ledger's true heartland is the central river-cities (the **Glassmere League**) down the Verdance/Glasswater to the southern ports — the campaign sees only its frontier arm. The Remnant's ambition reaches toward the Heartlands. By contrast the **Ashen Wardens, Mourners' Circle, Reachward Compact, and Gravecallers are essentially NW-frontier-bound**; do not give them far-continental power. The **Hollow Court has no surface territory anywhere** — only the basin and its cutouts.
- **Other surviving Custodians, if any, are off-map and undefined.** Do **not** invent a second Hollow Court, a rival Custodian faction, or a foreign keystone in narration. The far regions have their own local powers (the Emberfell fire-priests, Wender clans, Karran warlords, Glassmere magnates, Hollow Gulf port-lords) — none of which is part of the harvest conspiracy. Keep the conspiracy local and singular.
- **Map secrecy enforcement (full-world):** the player-safe full-world map (Layer 1 / Prompt 1), the parchment artifact (Prompt 3), and the campaign inset (Prompt 5) must NEVER show the node-network, the Concord Deep, the Under-Shrine, warming marks, or any "the far ruins are the machine" implication. The DM-only full-world map (Layer 2/7, Prompt 2) carries them — and keeps them in the **NW corner**, vertical.
- **No new central mystery, faction, god, cosmology, or artifact was created in this pass.** The new continental names are geographic/placeholder-political features supporting the existing world; the far fallen lands corroborate the Concord's scale and the world's pattern of drowning falls, but the singular live machine remains under Hollowmere.

## Player Steering Note (DM Caution)

The PC is being subtly steered toward the shrine by a Hollow Court cutout (via Sefra Quick), but **the campaign must never railroad**. The steering is a hook, not a track. If the player ignores or resists it, the world clocks (`WORLD_CLOCKS.md`) advance the harvest anyway, and other factions act. The steering should feel like opportunity, never compulsion.
