## AI Use

Load when the player researches history, consults a sage/archive, visits Concord ruins, or asks what happened a century ago. Use the **Public Timeline** for anything the player can casually learn. Use the **True Timeline (DM-only)** only to adjudicate discoveries and never reveal it directly.

## Summary

Orrun's recorded history is split by the **Quietfall** (~100 years ago). Before it: the age of the Custodian Concord. After it: the frontier age. The public version is a story of glory and sudden catastrophe. The true version is a story of a benevolent-seeming order that harvested the dead and then destroyed itself to hide it.

---

## Calendar Note

Dates use **After Quietfall (AQ)**. The Quietfall is AQ 0. Current year is **AQ 101**. Pre-Quietfall dates use **Concord Reckoning (CR)**; the Quietfall falls at roughly CR 900 / AQ 0. See `CALENDAR.md`.

## Public Timeline (Player-Safe)

| Era | Date | Event |
|---|---|---|
| Concord Age | CR 1 (legendary) | Founding of the Custodian Concord, an order said to tend the Last Door and keep the dead at peace. |
| Concord Age | CR 200–800 | Golden age. The Concord builds great shrines, roads, and archives across Orrun. Long lives, stable harvests, little plague. |
| Concord Age | CR ~880 | Faint later memory of "thin years" — poor crops, strange births. Mostly forgotten. |
| The Quietfall | AQ 0 | In one season the Concord collapses. Shrines fall silent. Fires, floods, the death of nearly all Custodians. The cause is never officially explained. |
| Frontier Age | AQ 0–40 | Resettlement. Independent towns and councils form. Hollowmere founded (~AQ 12) on the shore of a flooded shrine-basin. |
| Frontier Age | AQ ~60 | Death-rites begin failing more often. First widely reported "Remembrances." |
| Frontier Age | AQ 80–100 | Failures spread. The Mourners' Circle grows; the Gravecallers appear; the Concord Remnant forms. |
| Now | AQ 101 | Failures accelerate sharply in the Sundering Reach. The campaign begins. |

## True Timeline (DM-ONLY — never reveal directly)

| Date | What Actually Happened |
|---|---|
| CR 1–200 | The Concord's founders discover **Remembrance** — that the dead's identity can be drawn out and used. They build the shrines as a hidden harvesting network, telling the world they "keep the dead at peace." |
| CR 200–800 | The golden age is *powered by the harvest.* A thin tithe of every death sustains Concord leaders and great works. The **Quiet Country (afterlife) slowly drains.** |
| CR ~850 | The inner **Custodians** discover the afterlife is nearly empty: souls are starting to fail to form; "thin-born" children appear. |
| CR ~900 / AQ 0 | The Custodians **deliberately destroy the Concord** to halt the harvest and let the afterlife refill. They stage the **Quietfall** as a disaster. Most die; the public never learns it was intentional. |
| AQ 0 | A handful of Custodians **store themselves as Remembrance** in the deepest under-shrine beneath what becomes Hollowmere — the origin of the **Hollow Court**. They go dormant to wait. |
| AQ 0–60 | The seal holds. The Wardens (secretly seeded by survivors) keep the shrines quiet. The world heals slowly. |
| AQ ~60 | The under-shrine begins faintly to stir; minor leakage causes the first death-rite failures. |
| AQ ~95 | The Hollow Court awakens enough to judge the afterlife "full enough" and begins to **restart the harvest** and recruit unwitting living agents. |
| AQ 101 | The restart accelerates. The drowned shrine beneath Hollowmere is the keystone. The campaign begins. |

## Concord Ruins (What Remains)

- **Shrine-stones**: standing markers across the Reach, now cold.
- **The drowned shrine of Hollowmere**: the great central shrine, flooded when the basin filled after the Quietfall; the town sits on its shore. (Public: a curiosity and hazard. DM: the keystone of the harvest and the Hollow Court's seat.)
- **Old Concord roads**: well-built, overgrown, still the safest routes through the Reach.
- **Archive cairns**: sealed stone vaults of Concord records; most looted or empty, a few intact and dangerous. (Clue sources.)
