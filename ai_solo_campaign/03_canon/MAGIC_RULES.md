## AI Use

Load when the player casts notable magic, attempts resurrection, studies relics, encounters Remembrance phenomena, or asks how magic works. Apply the lightweight wrinkles below on top of standard 5e/2024 rules. Do not invent a new system.

## Summary

Magic in Vael is standard D&D 5e/2024 — arcane, divine, primal, and the usual classes all function normally. The campaign adds **one** setting force: **Remembrance**, the harvestable substance of the dead's identity. Remembrance is not a class or spell list; it is flavor plus a small set of consistent rulings that mostly affect death, resurrection, and certain relics/adversaries.

---

## Standard Magic (Player-Safe)

- All standard 5e classes, subclasses, spells, and magic items work normally unless noted.
- Magic is uncommon but accepted in the Reach. Hedge-casters, shrine-keepers, and a few trained mages exist. Casters are not hunted, but death-magic and "calling the dead" are taboo (see Gravecallers).

## The Remembrance Wrinkles (Apply These Consistently)

These are lightweight rulings, not new mechanics. They preserve 5e usability.

1. **Hard resurrection.** *Revivify, Raise Dead, Resurrection,* etc. still exist, but in the Reach each casting requires a **DC 12 spellcasting-ability check** (rising with target's days-dead) *in addition* to normal costs. On a failure, the spell is not wasted in slot/material terms narratively, but the soul does not return *clean*: the DM may rule a temporary flaw, a missing memory, or (on a natural 1 or repeated failure) a **wrong-come-back** (see below). Telegraph this danger before the player commits. This is a clue, not a gotcha.
2. **Talkative dead.** *Speak with Dead* works unusually well near Concord sites — the dead are *closer* than they should be. This is a clue source. Restless dead may answer even without the spell, briefly, if approached respectfully.
3. **Wrong-come-back revenants.** A botched resurrection or a leaking shrine can produce a creature that looks like the person but is a partial Remembrance: it remembers some things, wants something unfinished, and is not fully the same person. Statline: flavor a **commoner/ghost/specter/revenant** as appropriate to the danger; do not create a new stat block. These are central horror imagery and clue-bearers.
4. **Remembrance relics.** Some Concord salvage stores Remembrance (a "kept memory" in stone, glass, or bone). Such relics can: replay a dead person's memory (clue delivery), power a one-use effect, or be "spent" by certain adversaries. Treat as plot/clue items; mechanical relics live in `/14_treasure_and_artifacts/` (Stage 14).
5. **Thin-born.** A character born thin-souled (optional, player's choice) takes no mechanical penalty by default; it is a narrative/clue hook. If the player wants a mechanical expression, use a minor roleplay trait, not a stat change.

## Forbidden / Dangerous Magic

- **Calling the dead** (necromantic divination, animate dead, soul-binding) is socially taboo and, in the Reach, genuinely risky: it can draw a shrine's attention or attract leaked Remembrances. Use this to escalate, not to punish casters arbitrarily.
- **Animate Dead / Create Undead**: function, but in the Reach the undead created are unusually "aware" (DM flavor), unsettling allies even to a necromancer PC. A necromancer PC is *fully playable* and thematically rich — do not block the class; lean into the moral tension.

## DM-Only: Magic and the Hidden Truth

- Remembrance is the same substance the Concord harvested. A clever caster who studies it can *deduce* the harvest. Investigation/Arcana/Religion at DC 15–20, with a relic in hand, is a valid clue path.
- The Hollow Court and the reawakening shrine are the "source" the wrinkles point back to. Every wrinkle above is a breadcrumb. Never confirm the full truth from one ruling.
