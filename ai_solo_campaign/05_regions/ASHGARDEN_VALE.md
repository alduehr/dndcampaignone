## AI Use

Load when the player travels **south** out of the Sundering Reach (via Candlewick and the South Road), takes Vale work, or pursues the older-Concord / shrine-town threads. The Vale is the Reach's gentle southern neighbor — warmer, richer, older, and quieter in its dread. It is the **safest of the three Ring 1 regions** and a natural early outward step (levels 2–5), and it sits on the southern overland approach to Caradril. Use the player-facing overview freely; keep the DM-only truth hidden.

## One-Sentence Identity

A mellow southern breadbasket of orchards, shrine-towns, and Concord ruins half-buried under crops, where the failing rites are felt not as frontier terror but as a slow, denied grief — and where the oldest standing Threshold shrine hides something the Concord buried under a pilgrimage.

## Player-Facing Overview

South of the Reach the fens give way to firm, rising ground: hedged fields, old cider-orchards, drystone walls, and tidy shrine-towns clustered around the **oldest surviving Threshold shrines on this part of Orrun.** The Vale is Orrun's nearest breadbasket and one of the few places that came through the Quietfall with its faith and its farms more or less intact. Folk here are devout, proud, prosperous by frontier standards, and a little smug about it. They keep the death-rites the old way, and for generations the rites *held* — which is exactly why the recent failures frighten them so badly. The Vale would rather not talk about it.

The Vale is loosely tied to the Reachward Compact but governs itself through **harvest-moots** and the authority of its shrine-keepers. Its hub is the orchard-town of **Orchardmere**, on the lake the same name; its famous pilgrimage is the hill-shrine of **Saint Veddow's Rest**.

## DM-Only Truth

The Vale's "the rites have always held here" pride is **the most dangerous denial in Ring 1.** The Vale held longer than the Reach for a simple reason: it sits *farther* from the keystone, so the harvest's reawakening leakage reached it later. Now it is arriving — and because the Vale is unpracticed at fear, it copes by **covering up, reburying, and silencing** rather than confronting, which lets the failures fester worse.

The deeper secret: **Saint Veddow's Rest is a major Concord shrine the harvest network was built around, capped and disguised as a saint's tomb after the Quietfall.** It is a *secondary node* — smaller than the keystone drowned shrine, but the largest Concord installation outside the Reach, and the clearest place in Ring 1 to learn what the Concord *built* (M2/M6 fragments). The Mourners' Circle here is older and deeper than the Reach's and holds the most complete version of the folk-truth that "the Concord ate the dead" (M6 path, R5-gated to Act 3). The Concord Remnant covets the shrine; the Cinder Ledger is quietly buying up "saint's relics" that are really Remembrance relics (M3). **Never reveal the harvest, the Court, or the deliberate Quietfall in Vale Act-1-level play; cap at R1.** See `../03_canon/DM_ONLY_CANON.md`.

## Geography

- **Terrain:** rolling farmland and low green hills; cider- and nut-orchards; hedgerows, drystone walls, water-meadows; a few standing **Concord field-shrines** ploughed around for a century; the broad, slow **Ammet** river and **Orchardmere lake**.
- **Climate:** temperate and kind by Orrun standards — true summers, mild winters, autumn harvest the year's great event. Campaign starts in late autumn (Greyfall), so the player arrives at the tail of harvest, with cider-pressing and the dead-time (the Vale's funeral season) just beginning.
- **Boundaries:** Sundering Reach (N), Tollwood (E, across the Ammet headwaters), Caradril and its hinterland (SE/S), open downs and unbuilt country (W/SW).
- **Defining features:** **Saint Veddow's Rest** (the pilgrimage hill); **Orchardmere lake**; the **Ammet** river; the **South Road** Concord causeway; scattered field-shrines and orchard-buried ruins.

## Major Settlements

- **Orchardmere** — the Vale's hub: an orchard- and cider-town of ~1,400 on the lake, seat of the Vale's harvest-moot and its richest shrine-keepers. (`../06_settlements/ORCHARDMERE.md`)

## Minor Settlements

- **Saint Veddow's Rest** — the pilgrimage shrine-town: a small, devout town wrapped around the famous saint's hill-tomb; inns, relic-sellers, and pilgrims. (`../06_settlements/SAINT_VEDDOWS_REST.md`)
- **Tilbrook** — a working farm-and-mill village on the Ammet, plainer and poorer than Orchardmere, resentful of the shrine-towns' wealth; a good gentle level-1–2 waypoint and the player's likely first Vale stop coming from Candlewick. (`../06_settlements/TILBROOK.md`)
- **(Hamlets, in the wilderness file):** Nettlecombe (orchard hamlet over a field-shrine), Marrow Cross (crossroads gibbet-hamlet), Pellow Grange (Ledger-mortgaged farm-estate). See `wilderness/ASHGARDEN_VALE_SITES.md`.

## Wilderness Zones

One zone file holds the Vale's named sites (`wilderness/ASHGARDEN_VALE_SITES.md`): **Saint Veddow's hill and pilgrim road**, **the orchard ruins (Nettlecombe field-shrine, the Buried Cloister)**, **the Ammet water-meadows and Marrow Cross**, **the southern downs toward Caradril**, and the **Pellow grange**. Lvl 1–5; the Vale is the gentlest Ring 1 wilderness — the danger is social and slow-creeping, not predatory.

## Travel Routes

See `../04_world_atlas/TRAVEL_ROUTES_RING1.md`. The **South Road** (Concord causeway) runs Candlewick → Tilbrook → Orchardmere (~3–4 days from Candlewick; ~6 from Hollowmere) and continues south/southeast toward Caradril (~5–6 more days). A pilgrim-track climbs from Orchardmere to Saint Veddow's Rest (half a day). The Vale roads are the safest in Ring 1: maintained, patrolled by harvest-moot wardens, and busy.

## Regional Conflicts

- **The denial vs. the failures** — the Vale's shrine-keepers suppress and rebury failed rites to protect the pilgrimage trade and their pride; the failures worsen for it. *The master Vale tension.*
- **Shrine-town wealth vs. farm-village poverty** — Tilbrook and the granges resent Orchardmere and Saint Veddow's living fat off pilgrim coin and relic sales while the working villages carry Ledger debt.
- **Relic-selling** — the Ledger and Saint Veddow's relic-trade quietly sell Concord "saint's relics" that are really Remembrance relics (M3); the Mourners are starting to suspect blasphemy.
- **The Remnant's interest** — scholar-pilgrims pressing for access to Saint Veddow's inner tomb; the shrine-keepers stalling.

## Regional Secrets (DM-Only)

- Saint Veddow's Rest is a capped major Concord shrine / secondary harvest node (M2/M6).
- The Vale Mourners hold the fullest "the Concord ate the dead" folk-memory (M6; Act 3).
- "Saint's relics" sold to pilgrims and the Ledger are Remembrance relics (M3).
- The Vale held longer only because it is farther from the keystone — proof the failures spread *outward from the Reach* (M5 corroboration).

## Recurring Threats

- **Quiet failures** — Remembrances that the Vale hides rather than fights: a "saint" that weeps real tears, a reburied grandmother who keeps surfacing, pilgrims who came back wrong. Telegraphed, tragic, usually layable by a true rite (Religion DC 13–15).
- **Social danger** — being run out of a shrine-town, accused of grave-robbing or heresy, mobbed by frightened pilgrims; the Vale's threat is reputation and the harvest-moot's authority more than monsters.
- **Ledger debt and pressure** — Factor Wymar Pell's mortgages and enforcers (Thug-like).
- **Marrow Cross** — a gibbet-crossroads where the Vale's hangings draw the worst leakage; the one genuinely dangerous spot.

## Encounter Themes

Pastoral folk-horror: harvest festivals with a wrong note, devout towns hiding their dead, relic-fraud, debt and quiet desperation, pilgrim crowds, the slow arrival of a terror the Reach already knows. Combat is rare and usually avoidable; the Vale rewards social play, investigation, and patience.

## Level Range And Solo Danger

- **Region level range:** 1–6; **center of mass 2–5.** The Vale is the gentle outward step — a player who finds the Reach too grim can come south and breathe.
- **Low-danger core (Lvl 1–4):** Tilbrook, Orchardmere, the South Road, the pilgrim road, most orchard-country. Threats are social and tragic, not lethal.
- **Moderate (Lvl 3–5):** the Buried Cloister, the deeper Saint Veddow's tomb levels, Marrow Cross at night, the Pellow grange haunting.
- **Higher / gated (Lvl 5–6+):** the **inner shrine** beneath Saint Veddow's (M2/M6 fragments) is the Vale's most dangerous site — telegraphed as cold, deep, and Concord-built, and explicitly capped so a low-level dive yields fragments and danger, not the apex truth.
- **Solo safety:** the Ashen Wardens' resident chapter at Saint Veddow's (healing, rite-aid, sanctuary), Orchardmere's well-kept inns, and the Vale's busy patrolled roads are the region's solo-safety pillars. The Vale is forgiving by design.

## Encounter And Hazard Mechanics

- **Encounter table (stat-referenced, solo-tuned):** `../13_encounters_and_bestiary/ASHGARDEN_VALE_ENCOUNTERS.md` — three sub-tables (Orchard-Country & Roads, Saint Veddow's & Pilgrims, Marrow Cross & the Downs), D&D 5e/2024-compatible references, non-combat-default.
- **Dead:** Remembrances here are flavored gently — grieving "saints," reburied kin, lost pilgrims (Specter/Ghost-like, AC 12–13, ~17–30 HP); usually layable by a true rite (Religion DC 13–15) or parley (Insight/Persuasion DC 12). The wrathful exception (Marrow Cross gibbet-dead) is a Wraith-like (AC 13, ~36 HP), telegraphed.
- **Hazards/DCs:** mill-race / weir drowning ground (Athletics DC 13), cider-cellar foul air (Con DC 12), collapsing orchard ruin (Dex DC 13 / Perception DC 13 to spot), pilgrim-crush in a panicked crowd (Athletics/Acrobatics DC 13), the inner-shrine cold and **thin-touch** near raw relics (Con DC 14, reversible). Social "hazards": heresy accusation, being barred from a shrine-town (Persuasion/Deception DC 13–16 to recover standing).
- **Resting:** safe long rests in every settlement and the Saint Veddow's Warden house; the orchard ruins and Marrow Cross are poor rest sites (haunting/leakage); the inner shrine forbids safe rest.
- **Scaling:** each sub-table and dungeon carries Lvl 1–2 / 3–4 / 5–6 scaling; named foes never die cheaply.

## What Changes If Ignored

If the player ignores the Vale, its denial deepens on a clock (`../02_runtime_state/WORLD_CLOCKS.md`, Vale regional clock **"The Harvest-Moot's Silence"**): more failures are reburied and hidden, the pilgrimage trade keeps selling Remembrance relics, the Ledger forecloses Tilbrook and the granges, and eventually a failure too big to hide breaks the Vale's calm into panic — handing the Concord Remnant and the Gravecallers an opening at Saint Veddow's. The Vale's slow corruption is also a mirror that, witnessed, confirms the failures spread outward from the Reach (M5).

## Regional Clocks

Two Vale regional clocks (also summarized in `../02_runtime_state/WORLD_CLOCKS.md` under "Ring 1 Regional Clocks"). Both start at Stage 1 at campaign start and advance with time and player inaction. **DM-only causes (the warming node, the harvest) must never be narrated as such.**

### Clock V1 — The Harvest-Moot's Silence
- **Type:** regional/political (denial). **Driver:** Moot-Reeve Halsa Tindle's cover-up; the pilgrim-trade's interest; Factor Pell's funding.
- **What advances it:** time; the player ignoring Vale failures; a new failure successfully hushed; the player aiding the cover-up; the Ledger keeping the moot soothed.
- **Stage 1:** Isolated failed rites quietly reburied; families pressured to silence; the trade protected.
- **Stage 2:** The cover-up strains — Bell-clerk Wessa's reports pile up; Mother Combe and Mam Tace push back; whispers spread to Tilbrook.
- **Stage 3:** A failure too public to hide (a "weeping saint," a surfacing grandmother); panic flickers; the Gravecallers find an opening at Marrow Cross; the Remnant presses for tomb access.
- **Stage 4:** The Vale's calm breaks into open fear; the harvest-moot loses its grip; Saint Veddow's becomes contested ground.
- **Final consequence:** The Vale tips from denial into panic — the worst footing to meet the crisis on; the Remnant and Gravecallers exploit the breakdown at the node.
- **How the player can slow/stop/redirect/exploit it:** break the silence honestly via Wessa/Sergeant Frost/Combe (`Q_THE_FUNERAL_DONE_TWICE`, AV-H2); settle failures with true rites so fewer must be hidden; expose Tindle's orders to force reform; or *exploit* the denial to move unseen — at the cost of letting it worsen.

### Clock V2 — The Saint's Relics Sold
- **Type:** regional/faction (Monopoly's Vale arm). **Driver:** Factor Wymar Pell and the Saint Veddow's relic-trade (feeds master clock #6 Monopoly).
- **What advances it:** time; the player ignoring the relic-fraud; the Ledger foreclosing more shrine-land; pilgrims buying "saint's relics"; the player selling relics to Pell/Tamm.
- **Stage 1:** Pell quietly buys and quarries Remembrance relics from foreclosed field-shrines (the Pellow Grange); Coyle Tamm sells tainted "saint's-bones" to pilgrims.
- **Stage 2:** Pell forecloses Tilbrook and more granges over field-shrines; the relic-supply south thickens; the Mourners begin to suspect blasphemy.
- **Stage 3:** The Vale's shrine-land is cornered; tainted relics spread among pilgrims and up the Ledger chain to Caradril; Combe's Mourners move to resist.
- **Stage 4:** The Vale becomes a steady relic-quarry for Vyre's Monopoly — its dead, dug up and sold.
- **Final consequence:** The Vale's Concord ruins are stripped for the Monopoly; Remembrance relics flow to Caradril and the rich; the Mourners are driven toward militancy.
- **How the player can slow/stop/redirect/exploit it:** stop or expose Pell's grange-quarrying (`Q_SAINTS_BONES`, the Pellow Grange, AV-H3/H12); save Tilbrook's mill from foreclosure (Brunt; `Q_THE_MARKS_ON_THE_DOORS`); rally Combe's Mourners against the relic-trade; or trace the chain south to leverage Vyre (feeds #6).
