## AI Use

Load when the player travels **east** out of the Sundering Reach (via Kettle Bridge and the East Road), takes Tollwood work, or pursues the deep-forest / old-road / pre-Concord threads. The Tollwood is the **most dangerous and most mysterious** Ring 1 region — a vast old-growth forest threaded by overgrown Concord toll-roads, where the deep trees hold something older than the Concord. It is the eastern overland route to Caradril and a level 2–7 region (the deep wood pushing toward the upper band). Use the player-facing overview freely; keep the DM-only truth hidden.

## One-Sentence Identity

A vast, ancient eastern forest of toll-roads, woodcutter villages, and bandit-tolls, where the Concord's roads run straight through trees that were old before the Concord — and where the deep wood keeps a quiet, watchful presence the Concord never tamed and never fully understood.

## Player-Facing Overview

East of the Reach the fens dry and rise into the Tollwood: a sea of old oak, ash, and black pine, so deep and old that the Concord's great toll-roads — raised stone causeways with their crumbling toll-stations — vanish under leaf-mould and moss for miles at a stretch. People live here in clearings: woodcutters, charcoal-burners, pannage-hogs, a few road-towns living off the Caradril trade. They keep to the roads. They pay the tolls — to the road-towns, to the bandits, and, the old folk say, to the wood itself. Everyone who lives here knows the same rule: *don't go deep, don't go off the road after dark, and don't take from the old trees.*

The Tollwood is loosely Compact-claimed and Ledger-exploited (logging, charcoal, the road-trade), but in practice it governs itself road-town by road-town. Its hub is the road-town of **Hartfell**; its heart is the deep wood the locals call **the Old Mast.**

## DM-Only Truth

The Tollwood holds the campaign's clearest **non-Concord** mystery-thread, and it is deliberately *adjacent* to the main arc rather than central. **Something ancient sleeps in the deep wood — a pre-Concord power tied to the land and the dead long before the harvest** — and the Concord *built its roads around it, not through it,* because even the Concord could not master it. (Mechanically and thematically this echoes the Reach's Barrow of Nine Doors — the network was built *over* older things.) This deep presence is **not** the Hollow Court and **not** the harvest; it is an older, slower, more neutral thing — the closest the setting comes to a primal/fey-flavored woodland power, expressed through D&D 5e fey/plant/beast and old-dead references, never a new god or cosmology. As the harvest reawakens far to the west, the deep wood **stirs and resents it** — making the Tollwood a place where the player can learn, from a very old witness, that the dead were being *taken* (an oblique M5/M6 corroboration) without going near the keystone. The Gravecallers shelter cells here (the wood hides them and the deep dead will *answer*); the Cinder Ledger's logging is quietly waking what should be left sleeping (a regional clock). **No new central mystery, god, faction, or artifact: the deep presence is a regional landmark-power, gated and oblique, that points back at M5/M6 and never names the Court.** See `../03_canon/DM_ONLY_CANON.md`.

## Geography

- **Terrain:** deep old-growth forest — oak/ash/black-pine; mossed Concord causeways and ruined toll-stations; charcoal-clearings and coppice; bramble-choked ravines; cold forest streams feeding the Ammet headwaters; the deepest, oldest stand (the Old Mast) at the forest's heart.
- **Climate:** cool, damp, deep-shaded; little wind under the canopy; mist in the hollows; the forest swallows weather and sound. Campaign starts in late autumn — leaf-fall, charcoal-season, the wood at its most disorienting.
- **Boundaries:** Sundering Reach (W, across Kettle Bridge and the Mirewend), Ashgarden Vale (S, across the Ammet headwaters), Caradril and its hinterland (SE), unmapped deeper forest and hills (N/E).
- **Defining features:** the **East Road** (the great Concord toll-causeway to Caradril); the ruined **toll-stations**; the deep **Old Mast**; the locals' **toll-shrines** (offering-stones at forest crossings).

## Major Settlements

- **Hartfell** — the Tollwood's hub: a stockaded road-town of ~900 on the East Road, living off the Caradril trade, the toll, and timber; the only place in the wood that feels like a town. (`../06_settlements/HARTFELL.md`)

## Minor Settlements

- **Coldhearth** — a charcoal-burners' clearing-village deeper in the wood, half-feral, devout to the old forest-customs; the player's window into the wood's true rules. (`../06_settlements/COLDHEARTH.md`)
- **Tollstone Cross** — a tiny road-hamlet at a ruined Concord toll-station where the East Road bandits levy their "toll"; contested, dangerous, useful. (`../06_settlements/TOLLSTONE_CROSS.md`)
- **(Hamlets/sites, in the wilderness file):** the woodcutter camps, the Coppice Shrines, the Hanging Oaks, the sunken stretch of road called the Green Mile. See `wilderness/TOLLWOOD_SITES.md`.

## Wilderness Zones

One zone file holds the Tollwood's named sites (`wilderness/TOLLWOOD_SITES.md`): **the East Road & toll-stations**, **the Coppice Shrines & woodcutter camps**, **the Green Mile (the lost road)**, **the Hanging Oaks**, and **the edge of the Old Mast** (the deep wood; gated). Lvl 2–7; the danger climbs sharply the deeper one goes, and the deep Old Mast is explicitly higher-tier.

## Travel Routes

See `../04_world_atlas/TRAVEL_ROUTES_RING1.md`. The **East Road** (Concord toll-causeway) runs Kettle Bridge → Tollstone Cross → Hartfell → on toward Caradril (~4 days Kettle Bridge to Hartfell; ~5–6 more Hartfell to Caradril). The road is the *only* safe travel — off-road forest travel doubles or triples time, courts getting lost, and risks the deep wood. Bandit-tolls and washed-out causeway stretches (the Green Mile) are the road's main hazards.

## Regional Conflicts

- **The logging vs. the wood** — the Ledger's cutting pushes deeper toward the Old Mast; the deep presence stirs and the forest grows dangerous; the old-custom folk are terrified. *The master Tollwood tension.*
- **The toll-war** — the East Road bandits at Tollstone Cross vs. Hartfell's road-trade vs. the Compact's thin watch; who controls the road controls the wood.
- **The Gravecallers in the wood** — sheltered cells learning from the deep dead; persecuted but harder to root out here than anywhere.
- **The old customs vs. outsiders** — woodfolk who keep the toll-shrines and forest-rules vs. Ledger men and travelers who scoff and pay the price.

## Regional Secrets (DM-Only)

- A pre-Concord power sleeps in the Old Mast; the Concord built its roads *around* it (M5/M6 oblique corroboration; never the Court).
- The deep wood's old dead *answer*, and one is a very old witness to "the taking" (an M5/M6 fragment-source, gated, oblique).
- The Ledger's deep-cutting is waking what should sleep (a regional clock).
- The toll-shrines are pre-Concord offering-stones the Concord co-opted, not built.

## Recurring Threats

- **The deep wood itself** — getting lost, the canopy-dark, the disorienting silence; the forest is the primary antagonist (Survival-heavy, telegraphed).
- **Old forest dead** — Remembrances older and stranger than the Reach's, plus the deep presence's watchers (flavor fey/plant/old-undead; AC 12–14). Often avoidable; rarely simply layable (these are *older* than the harvest).
- **East Road bandits** (toll-takers; Thug/Scout-like) and the toll-war.
- **The Ledger's loggers and enforcers** pushing deeper; their cutting is the real danger.
- **Predators** — the Tollwood holds real apex beasts (dire wolves, a bear, a giant boar; CR 1–3) the Reach lacks.

## Encounter Themes

Deep-woods folk-horror and survival: the road as a thin safe thread through old dark, offering-shrines and forest-rules, bandit-tolls, the slow waking of an ancient thing under the logging, dead older than the Concord, and the dread of going off the road. Combat is more present here than the Vale — real predators and bandits — but the deepest danger is *the wood*, best handled by respect, woodcraft, and not going too deep.

## Level Range And Solo Danger

- **Region level range:** 2–7; **center of mass 3–6.** The Tollwood is the *step up* from the Reach and the Vale — meaningfully more dangerous, and built to grow with the player.
- **Low-moderate (Lvl 2–4):** the East Road, Hartfell, the near woodcutter camps, Tollstone Cross (the toll-war is mostly social/bandit-level).
- **Moderate (Lvl 4–6):** Coldhearth and the mid-wood, the Coppice Shrines, the Hanging Oaks (Gravecaller cell + old dead), the Green Mile.
- **Higher / gated (Lvl 6–7+):** the **Old Mast** (the deep wood and its ancient presence) — explicitly telegraphed as a place even the woodfolk and the Concord would not go, capped so a low-level intrusion yields dread, fragments, and a warning, not the apex. The deep presence is *not* a low-level fight.
- **Solo safety:** Hartfell's stockade and inn, the East Road's (relative) safety, the woodfolk guides of Coldhearth (the Tollwood's Sashe-equivalents), and the forest-custom toll-shrines (offering = safe passage) are the region's solo-safety pillars. **The wood punishes carelessness; it rewards respect and woodcraft.**

## Encounter And Hazard Mechanics

- **Encounter table (stat-referenced, solo-tuned):** `../13_encounters_and_bestiary/TOLLWOOD_ENCOUNTERS.md` — three sub-tables (the East Road & Tolls, the Mid-Wood & Camps, the Deep Wood & Old Mast edge), D&D 5e/2024-compatible references, with the deep-wood table the region's most dangerous.
- **Dead:** old forest Remembrances and the deep presence's watchers (Specter/Ghost/Will-o'-wisp/Dryad-or-Awakened-flavored, AC 12–14, ~22–60 HP by depth); the *near* dead are layable by a true rite (Religion DC 13–15); the *deep* dead are older than the harvest and resist easy laying — parley, offering, and retreat matter more than rites.
- **Hazards/DCs:** getting lost off-road (Survival DC 14, DC 16 deep; failure loops/strands), canopy-dark (disadvantage on sight Perception; light needed), bramble-ravine (Athletics DC 13), washed-out causeway (the Green Mile; Dex/Athletics DC 13–15), forest-cold night (Con DC 12–13), breaking a toll-shrine taboo (Wis DC 13 dread + the wood "marks" the offender), and the deep-wood dread near the Old Mast (Wis DC 14, cumulative).
- **Resting:** safe long rests in Hartfell, Coldhearth, and at honored toll-shrines (offering given); dangerous everywhere off-road; **no safe rest in the deep wood / Old Mast.**
- **Scaling:** each sub-table and dungeon carries Lvl 2–3 / 4–5 / 6–7 scaling; the deep presence and its site stay gated regardless of level.

## What Changes If Ignored

If the player ignores the Tollwood, its regional clock **"The Old Mast Stirs"** advances: the Ledger's deep-cutting wakes the ancient presence further; the forest dead spread toward the road; the bandit-toll tightens and the road-trade to Caradril chokes; and the Gravecallers, sheltered and learning from the deep dead, grow bolder. Eventually the deep wood becomes actively dangerous and the East Road — the overland link to Caradril — becomes perilous, cutting a travel artery. The Tollwood's stirring is also an oblique warning: even an ancient power *resents the harvest's reawakening* (M5/M6, gated).

## Regional Clocks

Two Tollwood regional clocks (also summarized in `../02_runtime_state/WORLD_CLOCKS.md` under "Ring 1 Regional Clocks"). Both start at Stage 1 at campaign start. **The deep presence is a gated regional landmark-power, never the Court; never narrate the harvest as the cause.**

### Clock T1 — The Old Mast Stirs
- **Type:** regional/world (the deep presence). **Driver:** Factor Hesk Dorr's deep-cutting (breaking the forest-bargain) and the Gravecaller cell learning from the deep dead.
- **What advances it:** time; the player ignoring the Tollwood; the Ledger cutting marked trees; the cell working the Hanging Oaks; the player defiling Coppice Shrines or trespassing past the Old Mast edge.
- **Stage 1:** The cutting pushes east; crews that cut marked trees come back short; the old dead grow restless near the burns.
- **Stage 2:** The roused dead drift toward the camps and road; the dread thickens at the Sentinel Oaks; the cell grows bold at the Hanging Oaks.
- **Stage 3:** The deep wood turns actively hostile; the watchers range nearer the edge; the East Road's mid-wood stretches grow dangerous.
- **Stage 4:** The presence is roused enough to make the deep wood lethal and the East Road perilous; the cult holds the wood's oldest knowledge.
- **Final consequence:** The deep Tollwood seals; the East Road — the overland artery to Caradril — becomes perilous; the wood's ancient knowledge passes to the Gravecallers, not the player.
- **How the player can slow/stop/redirect/exploit it:** stop or expose Dorr's deep-cutting (`Q_THE_MARKED_OAKS`, `Q_THE_LOST_CREW`, TW-H1/H15); honor the bargain and atone broken trees at the Coppice Shrines; check the Hanging Oaks cell (`Q_THE_HANGING_OAKS`); learn the bargain from Sennet (`Q_THE_BARGAIN`) to keep the presence asleep; never trespass past the marked edge.

### Clock T2 — The Toll Turns to Plunder
- **Type:** regional/faction (the Tollmen). **Driver:** Skell's bid to oust Toll-Boss Cady Renn and turn the toll into outright robbery and murder.
- **What advances it:** time; the player ignoring Tollstone Cross; Renn losing face or resources; the player siding with Skell or fighting Renn; the road-trade to Caradril faltering.
- **Stage 1:** Renn runs the toll as a grim protection-racket that still keeps worse dangers off the road; Skell agitates among the gang.
- **Stage 2:** Skell's faction grows; tolls turn rougher; honest road-folk (Smith Doune, Ale-wife Pell) are squeezed and threatened.
- **Stage 3:** Skell moves on Renn; the toll tips toward robbery and killing; the Greenward node's leak goes untended; travelers avoid the road.
- **Stage 4:** Skell takes the Tollmen; they turn road-pirate and choke the East Road to Caradril.
- **Final consequence:** The East Road's overland trade strangles under murderous bandits, isolating Hartfell and cutting the Caradril artery from the east.
- **How the player can slow/stop/redirect/exploit it:** back Renn or break the gang (`Q_THE_TOLL_WAR`, TW-H2/H4); turn Renn to honest road-keeping with Captain Vey (TW-H8); protect the honest corner (TW-H13, Doune/Wenny); remove Skell; or escort trade to keep the road alive (TW-H9/H22).
