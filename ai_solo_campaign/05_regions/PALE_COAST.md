## AI Use

Load when the player travels **west** out of the Sundering Reach (via Saltmargin and the Pale Road) to the sea, takes Coast work, or pursues the salt-trade / coastal-ruin / sea-legend threads. The Pale Coast is the Reach's wild, independent, sea-facing western neighbor — fishing towns, salt-works, weather-battered cliffs, sea-caves, and old Concord lighthouses. **Saltmargin (built in the Reach) is the eastern gateway — reference it, do not recreate it.** A level 2–7 region with the sea as its great hazard, and the coastal/river approach to Caradril. Use the player-facing overview freely; keep the DM-only truth hidden.

## One-Sentence Identity

A storm-battered, independent western coast of fishing villages, salt-pans, and cliff-top Concord lighthouses, where the sea is feared and honored in equal measure — and where old legend half-remembers that the Concord used the sea, and the drowned, for something it never explained.

## Player-Facing Overview

West of the Reach the salt marshes give onto the cold sea: a coast of grey cliffs, shingle coves, fishing villages, and the salt-works that supply the whole frontier's rite-salt. The Coast folk are their own people — independent, weather-hardened, and deeply superstitious about the sea, which gives them everything and takes its tithe in drowned men every season. They keep the death-rites with a salt-and-tide flavor all their own, and they have always lived close to the dead, because the sea is always giving the dead back. Old Concord lighthouses and sea-shrines stand on the headlands, half-ruined, and the Coast tells stories about them the Reach has forgotten.

The Coast is loosely Compact-claimed and heavily Ledger-worked (the salt monopoly, the harbors), but governs itself harbor-town by harbor-town. Its hub is the cliff-and-harbor town of **Wrackmouth**; its great landmark is the ruined Concord lighthouse the Coast calls **the Drowned Lamp.**

## DM-Only Truth

The Pale Coast holds the campaign's **maritime echo of the harvest** — and, like the Vale and Tollwood, points back at M2/M3/M5 without touching the apex. **The Concord built sea-shrines and "lighthouses" along this coast that were really maritime harvest-nodes — settling and "keeping" the sea's drowned dead, of which a hard coast supplied an endless tithe.** The Coast's legend that "the Lamps called the drowned home" is a half-memory of this (M2/M5/M6 oblique). The **Drowned Lamp** is a coastal Concord node — a smaller cousin of the keystone — drowned at the cliff-foot and faintly warming (M2 fragment; the network is coastal too). The Cinder Ledger's salt monopoly here is the upstream end of the Saltmargin thread the player may already know, and the Coast's smugglers move Concord "sea-relics" (Remembrance relics; M3) the same way Gull moves grave-goods. A Gravecaller current runs strong here — the drowned *answer* readily — and the Coast is the **water-approach to Caradril** (down the coast and up the Verdance). **No new central mystery, god, faction, or artifact: the maritime node is a regional cousin-site, gated and oblique, feeding existing M2/M3/M5; never the Court.** See `../03_canon/DM_ONLY_CANON.md`.

## Geography

- **Terrain:** grey sea-cliffs, shingle coves, headlands, salt-marsh (toward Saltmargin), tidal salt-pans, sea-caves, offshore stacks and skerries; cold, fish-rich sea; the Verdance river-mouth toward the south (the water-road to Caradril).
- **Climate:** cold, wet, wind-scoured; frequent storms and sea-fog; brutal in winter. Campaign starts in late autumn — the season of gales, wrecks, and the Coast's drowned-tide.
- **Boundaries:** Sundering Reach (E, via Saltmargin and the salt-marsh), the cold sea (W/N), the Verdance mouth and Caradril's coastal hinterland (S/SE), open wild coast (N).
- **Defining features:** the **Drowned Lamp** (the great ruined lighthouse); the **sea-shrines** on the headlands; **Wrackmouth** harbor; the **salt-pans**; the **Skerries** (offshore stacks where wrecks and worse gather).

## Major Settlements

- **Wrackmouth** — the Coast's hub: a cliff-and-harbor town of ~1,100 around a deep-water cove, the Coast's main port, salt-market, and seat of its harbor-moot; the Ledger's coastal stronghold. (`../06_settlements/WRACKMOUTH.md`)

## Minor Settlements

- **Cobble Strand** — a shingle-cove fishing village under the headland of the Drowned Lamp; poor, devout, drowned-haunted; the player's window into the Coast's sea-faith and its ruin. (`../06_settlements/COBBLE_STRAND.md`)
- **Saltmargin** — *(already built; in the Sundering Reach)* the salt-marsh gateway town between the Reach and the Coast; the player's likely entry point. **Reference `../06_settlements/SALTMARGIN.md`; do not recreate.**
- **(Hamlets/sites, in the wilderness file):** the salt-pan camps, the sea-cave coves, the wreckers' strand, the Skerries. See `wilderness/PALE_COAST_SITES.md`.

## Wilderness Zones

One zone file holds the Coast's named sites (`wilderness/PALE_COAST_SITES.md`): **the cliff-road & headland sea-shrines**, **the sea-caves & smuggler coves**, **the Drowned Lamp headland**, **the Skerries & the wreckers' strand**, and **the salt-pans & marsh edge** (toward Saltmargin). Lvl 2–7; the sea and the cliffs are the great hazards, and the offshore Skerries are the region's most dangerous reach.

## Travel Routes

See `../04_world_atlas/TRAVEL_ROUTES_RING1.md`. The **Pale Road** runs Saltmargin → Cobble Strand → Wrackmouth along the coast (~3 days Saltmargin to Wrackmouth; ~5 from Hollowmere to Saltmargin already established). From Wrackmouth, the **coastal/river route to Caradril** runs down the coast and up the **Verdance** (~6–7 days by ship/boat, weather permitting — often *faster and safer* than the overland routes, and a distinctive Coast advantage). Coastal travel means tides, storms, and the sea's own dangers.

## Regional Conflicts

- **The salt monopoly vs. the Coast** — the Ledger sets the salt-price the whole frontier pays; the salt-workers and fisher-folk are gouged and furious (the upstream end of the Saltmargin thread). *The master Coast tension.*
- **The wreckers vs. the harbor-towns** — a wrecking trade (luring or salvaging ships) on the wild coast vs. the lawful harbors; smuggling of sea-relics and grave-goods.
- **The drowned-tide** — the sea giving back its dead worse than before; the Coast's rites overwhelmed; the Gravecallers thriving on the grief.
- **The Drowned Lamp** — who controls the ruined lighthouse and the sea-shrines (Ledger, Remnant, wreckers, the drowned themselves).

## Regional Secrets (DM-Only)

- The lighthouses/sea-shrines were maritime harvest-nodes; the Drowned Lamp is a coastal cousin-node, faintly warming (M2 fragment; the network is coastal).
- The legend that "the Lamps called the drowned home" half-remembers the maritime harvest (M5/M6 oblique).
- The Coast's smugglers move Remembrance "sea-relics" (M3; links to Saltmargin/Gull and the Ledger monopoly).
- The drowned answer the Gravecallers readily here — a strong Coast cult-current and M3/M6-oblique door.

## Recurring Threats

- **The sea and the cliffs** — storms, tides, drowning, cliff-falls, sea-fog; the Coast's primary, ever-present hazard (Survival/Athletics-heavy).
- **The drowned dead** — Remembrances of the sea's countless drowned, often rising with the tide (Specter/Drowned-one-flavored; AC 12–14); many, telegraphed, usually layable by a salt-and-tide rite.
- **Wreckers and smugglers** — a real, dangerous criminal current (Thug/Bandit/Spy-like) on the wild coast.
- **The Skerries** — offshore stacks where wrecks, the worst drowned, and the wreckers' worst secrets gather; the region's most dangerous reach.
- **The Ledger's salt-enforcers** and the salt-price's slow violence.

## Encounter Themes

Maritime folk-horror and survival: the sea giving back its dead, salt-and-tide rites, wreckers and drowned-haunted coves, the gouging salt-trade, ruined lighthouses that "called the drowned," and the dread of the Skerries. Combat is more present than the Vale (wreckers, the worst drowned) but the sea itself is the great antagonist; seamanship, salt-lore, negotiation, and respect for the tide matter most.

## Level Range And Solo Danger

- **Region level range:** 2–7; **center of mass 3–6.** Comparable to the Tollwood in danger — the sea replaces the deep wood as the great hazard.
- **Low-moderate (Lvl 2–4):** Wrackmouth, Cobble Strand, the cliff-road, the near salt-pans, the headland sea-shrines.
- **Moderate (Lvl 4–6):** the sea-caves and smuggler coves, the Drowned Lamp headland, the wreckers' strand, coastal sailing in weather.
- **Higher / gated (Lvl 6–7+):** the **Skerries** (offshore, the worst drowned and the wreckers' secrets) and the **drowned lower levels of the Drowned Lamp** (the coastal node proper) — telegraphed as storm-cut, tide-locked, and lethal, and capped so a low-level reach yields fragments and danger, not the apex.
- **Solo safety:** Wrackmouth's harbor and inn, Cobble Strand's Salt-Mother (salt-and-tide rite-aid and sanctuary), the cliff-road's (relative) safety, and honest boatmen/guides who know the tides are the region's solo-safety pillars. **The sea punishes the careless; it spares those who read the tide and keep the salt-rites.**

## Encounter And Hazard Mechanics

- **Encounter table (stat-referenced, solo-tuned):** `../13_encounters_and_bestiary/PALE_COAST_ENCOUNTERS.md` — three sub-tables (the Cliff-Road & Salt-Pans, the Coves & the Drowned Lamp, the Skerries & Open Sea), D&D 5e/2024-compatible references, with the Skerries table the region's most dangerous.
- **Dead:** drowned Remembrances and sea-shrine dead (Specter/Ghost/Will-o'-wisp/"drowned-one"-flavored, AC 12–14, ~22–52 HP by reach); the near-shore dead are layable by a salt-and-tide rite (Religion DC 13–15) or parley; the Drowned Lamp's and the Skerries' dead are older/worse and resist easy laying — salt-lore, offering, and tide-timing matter.
- **Hazards/DCs:** storm and high sea (Survival/seamanship DC 14–16; capsize/drowning on fail), tide-traps in sea-caves and on causeways (Survival DC 14 to time; Athletics DC 13 to escape a flooding cave), cliff-climb/cliff-fall (Athletics DC 14, fall risk), sea-fog (lost at sea/shore, Survival DC 14), cold-water immersion (Con DC 13 or exhaustion/worse), wreckers' lures (Wis DC 13, false lights on the Skerries), and **thin-touch** near sea-relics (Con DC 14, reversible).
- **Resting:** safe long rests in Wrackmouth, Cobble Strand, and at honored sea-shrines; dangerous on the open coast, in tide-caves, and at sea; **no safe rest on the Skerries.**
- **Scaling:** each sub-table and dungeon carries Lvl 2–3 / 4–5 / 6–7 scaling; the coastal node and the Skerries stay gated regardless.

## What Changes If Ignored

If the player ignores the Coast, its regional clock **"The Drowned Tide Rises"** advances: the salt monopoly tightens (the whole frontier's rites worsen — directly feeding the Reach's dread and the Ledger's Monopoly clock); the drowned-tide grows and the Coast's rites collapse; the wreckers and smugglers grow bold; and the Drowned Lamp / sea-nodes warm further as the keystone reawakens, pulling the drowned "home" (M5 made maritime). Eventually the Coast turns dangerous and the salt — which the entire frontier's death-rites depend on — becomes scarce and ruinously dear, a continent-wide consequence.

## Regional Clocks

Two Pale Coast regional clocks (also summarized in `../02_runtime_state/WORLD_CLOCKS.md` under "Ring 1 Regional Clocks"). Both start at Stage 1 at campaign start. **The warming nodes and the maritime harvest are DM-only causes — never narrate them; the player sees a worsening tide and a tightening price.**

### Clock C-PC1 — The Drowned Tide Rises
- **Type:** regional/world (the coastal nodes). **Driver:** the warming Drowned Lamp and sea-shrines pulling the drowned "home"; the overstretched salt-and-tide rites.
- **What advances it:** time; the player ignoring the Coast; the master harvest clock (#1) turning; the player disturbing/warming the Lamp or sea-shrines; the rites going unsupported.
- **Stage 1:** The drowned come home faster than the rites can settle them; the Tide-Bell rings more each tide; the first "wrong-come-backs" appear.
- **Stage 2:** The salt-and-tide rites fall behind; drowned-cairns open; a stretch of cliff-shore turns haunted; the Gravecaller current swells where the rites fail.
- **Stage 3:** The Lamp and sea-shrines warm visibly; the drowned gather toward them; whole coves turn dangerous; Bryd and the wandering Wardens are overwhelmed.
- **Stage 4:** The drowned-tide overruns the Coast's rites; the outer shore and the Lamp grow lethal; the cult thrives on the grief.
- **Final consequence:** The Coast's death-rites collapse; the outer coast, the Lamp, and the Skerries turn deadly; the maritime drowned-home pull becomes an open, region-wide horror.
- **How the player can slow/stop/redirect/exploit it:** support the rites (`Q_THE_DROWNED_BELL`, `Q_THE_SALT_MOTHERS_BURDEN`, `Q_THE_EMPTY_HEARTH`); settle the cairns and sea-shrines; learn the salt-and-tide rite from Bryd; investigate and avoid disturbing the Lamp (`Q_WHAT_THE_LAMP_HOLDS`, gated); check the Gravecaller current (PC-H12). Only stopping master clock #1 truly halts it.

### Clock C-PC2 — The Salt Price Closes
- **Type:** regional/faction (Monopoly's coastal arm). **Driver:** Factor Maris Cole's salt-gouge and sea-relic trade, fed by the wreckers (feeds master clock #6 Monopoly).
- **What advances it:** time; the player ignoring the salt-trade; the wreckers flourishing; the player selling salvage/relics to Cole's chain; the moot staying cowed.
- **Stage 1:** Cole sets a gouging salt-price and quietly runs sea-relics (M3) through the wreckers and Cuttle Dree; honest skippers (Roke) are squeezed.
- **Stage 2:** The salt-price climbs; the salt-pan workers are ruined and drift to wrecking or the cult; the wreckers grow bold on the wild coast.
- **Stage 3:** Cole corners the coastal salt and sea-relic trade; the wreckers wreck freely; the whole frontier's rite-salt grows scarce and dear (feeds the Reach's dread and #6).
- **Stage 4:** The salt the entire frontier's death-rites depend on becomes ruinously scarce; Cole's coastal Monopoly is locked; the wreckers rule the wild coast.
- **Final consequence:** A continent-wide salt-and-rite squeeze; the Coast's wealth and dead alike funnel into Vyre's Monopoly.
- **How the player can slow/stop/redirect/exploit it:** break or expose Cole's salt-gouge and sea-relic trade (`Q_THE_SALT_PRICE`, PC-H1/H5); free honest skippers from the trap (`Q_THE_SKIPPERS_DEBT`); break the wreckers (`Q_THE_WRECKERS`, `Q_THE_COVE_CAVES`); ease the salt-pan gangs' lot (PC-H13); or carry proof of the chain south to leverage Vyre (feeds #6).
