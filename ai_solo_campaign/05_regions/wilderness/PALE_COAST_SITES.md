## AI Use

Load when the player travels the Coast's cliff-road, coves, headlands, salt-pans, and offshore Skerries. Holds the Coast's named wilderness sites. The Pale Coast wilderness is **sea-and-survival-first** — tides, storms, and cliffs are the antagonists; the offshore Skerries are the region's deadliest reach. Use the Pale Coast encounter table; a skipper or tide-wise guide is strongly advised for the coves and the sea.

## One-Sentence Identity

A cold, storm-cut coast of cliffs, coves, salt-pans, and ruined Concord lights, where the sea gives back its drowned dead with the tide and the danger climbs from the safe cliff-road out to the killing Skerries.

## What The Player Notices First

The sea — grey, vast, never quiet — and how everything bends to it: the wind-stunted cliff-grass, the upturned-boat roofs, the tide-tables chalked on every wall. The ruined lighthouses and sea-shrines on the headlands, older and stranger than anything the fisher-folk built. The drowned-cairns above the tide-line. And, in the fog, how the broken Drowned Lamp sometimes seems to *gleam* though no one tends it.

## What Is Actually Going On (DM-Only)

The Concord's coastal lighthouses and sea-shrines were **maritime harvest-nodes** — settling and "keeping" the sea's endless drowned (M2/M5/M6 oblique). As the keystone reawakens, the coastal nodes (chiefly the Drowned Lamp) **warm and pull the drowned "home"** — the drowned-tide the Coast fears. The Ledger salvages and trades the nodes' "sea-relics" (Remembrance relics; M3) through the wreckers and Cole's weigh-house, feeding the Monopoly clock. The Gravecallers thrive on the readily-answering drowned. **None of this is the Court or a new mystery: it is the harvest's coastal cousin-network, gated and oblique, feeding existing M2/M3/M5.**

## Named Sites

### 1. The Cliff-Road & Headland Sea-Shrines
The Pale Road along the cliff-tops, dotted with small ruined Concord sea-shrines on the headlands where, custom says, "the drowned were settled." Offering at one is the Coast's safe-passage rite; the shrines are minor leaking nodes (M5 oblique). The road is the safe thread.

### 2. The Coves & Sea-Caves
Shingle coves and tidal sea-caves below the cliffs — fishing, smuggling, and the wreckers' haunts (the dungeon `THE_WRECKERS_CAVES.md`). Tide-trap hazards; the Gravecaller current; sea-relic smuggling (M3).

### 3. The Drowned Lamp Headland
The great ruined Concord lighthouse on its headland above Cobble Strand — a coastal harvest-node, drowned at the cliff-foot and warming (the dungeon `THE_DROWNED_LAMP.md`). The Coast's M2 fragment-site and the source of the drowned-tide's worst pull.

### 4. The Salt-Pans & Marsh Edge
Tidal salt-pans toward Saltmargin — the salt-works the whole frontier depends on, hard wet labor under the Ledger's price. A travel zone linking the Coast to Saltmargin and the Reach; salt-worker unrest; the upstream end of the salt thread.

### 5. The Wreckers' Strand & the Skerries
A wild stretch of coast with a wreckers' strand and, offshore, the **Skerries** — sea-stacks and skerries where wrecks pile, the worst drowned gather, and the wreckers keep their darkest secrets (the dungeon `THE_SKERRY_SHRINE.md`). The region's most dangerous reach; boat-only; tide- and storm-locked.

### 6. The Verdance Mouth (south)
Where the coast meets the Verdance river-mouth and the water-road to Caradril begins — the Coast's distinctive route to the city (often faster and safer than overland). A gateway zone south.

### 7. The Drowned-Cairns (the tide-line burial shore)
- **One-sentence identity:** The long shingle above the tide-line where the Coast cairns its drowned, and where, this season, the cairns will not stay shut.
- **What the player notices first:** Mile on mile of stone cairns crusted with salt and weed, fresh ones bright among grey, the Tide-Bell's note carrying down the wind — and, lately, stones rolled aside from the inside and drag-marks toward the water.
- **What is actually going on (DM):** As the drowned-tide rises, the cairned dead are "called home" toward the warming sea-shrines and the Lamp (oblique, maritime M5); the salt-and-tide rite is failing to keep them down. The honest, public-facing heart of the Coast's M5 brush.
- **Hazards:** rising tide cutting off the shore (Survival DC 13 to time; stranded on a spit otherwise); cold-wet immersion (Con DC 12); a cairn-fall (Dex DC 12).
- **Creatures/adversaries:** drowned Remembrances rising from opened cairns (specter/"drowned-one"-like, AC 12–13, ~22–36 HP; layable by the salt-and-tide rite, Religion DC 13). Telegraphed by the dragged stones and the bell.
- **Level range / solo danger:** Lvl 2–4; **low-moderate** (rite-work and tide-timing, not a clearance). Retreat = up off the shore before the tide.
- **Connected to:** Salt-Mother Bryd and the Tide-Bell Mourners; quests "The Drowned-Bell" (Q_THE_DROWNED_BELL) and "The Empty Hearth" (Q_THE_EMPTY_HEARTH); clue C-PC-M5-1; the Coast clock. Re-settling a stretch of cairns quiets that shore (a real win the warming nodes slowly undo).

### 8. The Salt-Pan Flats & the Workers' Camp
- **One-sentence identity:** The bleak tidal salt-pans and the gouged-poor workers' camp where the whole frontier's rite-salt is raked — and where the salt-price's slow violence is felt first.
- **What the player notices first:** Mirror-flat brine pans striped to the horizon, raking gangs bent under the Ledger's quota, a mean huddle of shacks, and a Ledger tally-clerk's hut with a guarded door.
- **What is actually going on (DM):** This is the upstream end of the salt thread the player may know from Saltmargin and the Reach's Salt Run: Cole's quota and price ruin the workers, some of whom drift to wrecking or the cult out of desperation. No undead here — the danger is human (debt, hunger, Ledger toughs) and the slow continental squeeze on the death-rites.
- **Hazards:** brine-burns/exposure on the flats (Con DC 12); sucking pan-mud (Athletics DC 12); a Ledger enforcer's beating if the player agitates the gangs (telegraphed).
- **Creatures/adversaries:** Ledger salt-toughs (Thug/Bandit-like, AC 12–13, ~22–32 HP; morale-driven, turnable); desperate workers (Commoner-like; allies more than foes). See `PALE_COAST_ENCOUNTERS.md`.
- **Level range / solo danger:** Lvl 2–4; **low-moderate** (social/human). Open flats; easy retreat.
- **Connected to:** Factor Maris Cole, Harbor-Reeve Falk, the moot; quests "The Salt-Price" (Q_THE_SALT_PRICE), "The Skipper's Debt" (Q_THE_SKIPPERS_DEBT), hook PC-H13; the Monopoly/Coast clocks; links west to SALTMARGIN. Easing the gangs' lot checks the Monopoly locally and dries up the cult/wrecker recruiting pool.

### 9. The Lantern Stacks (the false-light reef)
- **One-sentence identity:** A near-shore cluster of sea-stacks off the wreckers' strand where false lights are shown to lure ships onto the rocks — the wrecking trade's killing ground.
- **What the player notices first:** Jagged black stacks white with gull-mess and spray, a wreck's ribs jammed in a gut between them, and — on a foul night — a steady "harbor" light burning where no harbor could be.
- **What is actually going on (DM):** The wreckers show false lights here to wreck ships, then strip them (and the drowned) and feed the salvage and sea-relics up Cole's chain (M3). The nearer, lower-stakes approach to the gated Skerries; a clue-and-justice site.
- **Hazards:** false-light lure if sailing (Wis DC 13 to see through it; wreck on failure); tide-trap among the stacks (Survival DC 14; a gut floods, Athletics DC 13 to escape); cliff/stack climb (Athletics DC 14, fall risk).
- **Creatures/adversaries:** wreckers (Bandit/Thug-like, AC 12–13, ~22–32 HP; a Wreck-Master, Veteran/Spy-like, AC 15, ~45 HP; morale-driven, turnable); the worst drowned only out at the true Skerries (gated). See `PALE_COAST_ENCOUNTERS.md`.
- **Level range / solo danger:** Lvl 3–5; **moderate** (the wreckers and the sea). Retreat = back to shore on the tide.
- **Connected to:** the wreckers' strand and the Skerries; quests "The Wreckers" (Q_THE_WRECKERS) and "The Cove Caves" (Q_THE_COVE_CAVES); Fence "Cuttle" Dree and Cole's chain (M3); Skipper Wenna Roke (who knows the safe water). Disabling the false-lights saves ships and chokes the salvage-chain.

### 10. The Weeping Light (a minor headland sea-shrine)
- **One-sentence identity:** A small ruined Concord sea-shrine on a lonely headland that has begun to glimmer untended and "weep" salt-water down its old stones.
- **What the player notices first:** A squat, roofless stone light on the cliff-edge, offering-shells heaped at its foot, and — at dusk, in fog — a faint cold gleam from the dark lamp-room and water beading down the carved walls though no rain has fallen.
- **What is actually going on (DM):** A minor coastal node — a small cousin of the Drowned Lamp — warming as the keystone reawakens (an M2/M5 fragment-site, the accessible "what is happening to the lights" brush short of the gated Lamp). The "weeping" is the node sweating Remembrance-cold; a drowned one or two keep vigil here.
- **Hazards:** thin-touch near the warming stone / handling its shells (Con DC 13, reversible); cliff-edge fall (Athletics DC 13); fog stranding (Survival DC 13).
- **Creatures/adversaries:** one or two vigil-drowned (specter-like, AC 12, ~22 HP; layable by the salt-and-tide rite, Religion DC 13, or parley). Telegraphed by the cold and the gleam.
- **Level range / solo danger:** Lvl 2–4; **low-moderate.** Lonely but escapable; retreat back along the cliff-road.
- **Connected to:** the cliff-road sea-shrines; the Drowned Lamp (the great node it foreshadows); Concord Remnant survey-scholars (hook PC-H14 "The Lights on the Headland"); clues C-PC-M5-1/M2-1; hook PC-H9. Investigating the Weeping Light is the low-risk on-ramp to "What the Lamp Holds."

## Hazards

- **Storm & high sea (boats):** Survival/seamanship DC 14 (DC 16 in a gale); failure swamps/capsizes (cold-water immersion: Con DC 13 or exhaustion; risk of drowning, recoverable by a skipper/rope/shore).
- **Tide-traps (sea-caves, causeways, the Skerries):** Survival DC 14 to read the tide; a cave or skerry can flood (Athletics DC 13 to escape; a missed tide strands the player on a stack — telegraphed by tide-tables and the locals' warnings).
- **Cliff-climb / cliff-fall:** Athletics DC 14; falls are serious (cliff height); telegraphed.
- **Sea-fog:** Survival DC 14 to hold course on shore or sea; failure strands or loops the traveler; wreckers' false lights worsen it.
- **Wreckers' lures (the Skerries):** Wis DC 13 to see through false lights luring boats onto the rocks.
- **Thin-touch (sea-relics, the nodes):** Con DC 14 near raw Remembrance relics; reversible.

## Creatures / Adversaries

- **Drowned Remembrances (cairns, shrines, the Lamp)** — flavor **specter/ghost/"drowned-one"-like** (AC 12–13, ~22–36 HP); rise with the tide; near-shore ones layable by a salt-and-tide rite (Religion DC 13–15); the Lamp's and Skerries' drowned are older/worse and resist easy laying (offering, salt-lore, tide-timing matter).
- **The Skerries' worst drowned** — flavor **wraith/drowned-spirit-like** (AC 13–14, ~45–60 HP); telegraphed; the region's deadliest dead; gated.
- **Wreckers & smugglers** — **Bandit/Thug/Spy-like** (AC 12–14); a real, dangerous criminal current; social-or-combat; morale-driven.
- **Sea-beasts** — a giant crab/octopus, hunting seals, a reef-shark-flavor threat (CR 1–3); telegraphed; rare.

See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/PALE_COAST_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Level Range And Solo Danger

Level 2–7; **low-moderate on the cliff-road and near coves, high in the open sea and at the Drowned Lamp, gated-lethal at the Skerries.** Retreat = get back to shore/the road and read the tide. Resting is safe in settlements and at honored sea-shrines; never at sea, in tide-caves, or on the Skerries. **Telegraph the tide and the weather constantly** — tide-tables, the locals' warnings, the drowned-bell, and the storm-sky are all built-in. The Skerries and the deep Lamp are never a low-level reach.

## NPCs Present

- **The cliff-road/shrines:** fisher-folk, Mourners tending the sea-shrines, Warden Brother Sef (rare).
- **The coves/caves:** wreckers, smugglers, the Gravecaller current, Skipper Wenna Roke (honest guide).
- **The Drowned Lamp:** Remnant survey-scholars, Cole's salvage-buyers, the warming node's drowned.
- **The Skerries:** the wreckers' worst, the drowned; no living dwell there.

## Factions Present

Cinder Ledger (salt-pans, sea-relic salvage), Mourners' Circle (sea-shrines, the drowned-rites), Gravecallers (the cove/wrecker current; the answering drowned), Reachward Compact (the thin harbor-watch, not the wild coast), Concord Remnant (eyeing the Lamp and sea-shrines), Ashen Wardens (rare; the drowned are too many).

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The lighthouses/sea-shrines are maritime harvest-nodes; the Drowned Lamp is a coastal cousin-node, warming (M2 fragment).
- The drowned-tide is the nodes pulling the drowned "home" (M5 oblique made maritime).
- The Ledger's sea-relic trade (M3; the Monopoly clock's coastal arm).
- The Skerries hold the wreckers' worst and a sea-shrine the drowned guard (gated).

## Clues

- **C-PC-M5-1** — the drowned "come home" toward the Lamp/sea-shrines with the worsening tide (Survival/Insight DC 13, or witnessed). M5 oblique, maritime.
- **C-PC-M3-1** — a "sea-relic" off the Lamp or a wreck stores a drowned person (Arcana/Religion DC 13, or experiential). M3; links to the Ledger trade.
- **C-PC-M2-1** (the Drowned Lamp; gated) — Concord Script and node-architecture proving the lighthouses were harvest-nodes (Script-gated; M2/M6 oblique fragment, act-paced; never the Court).

## Hooks

- **"What the Lamp Holds"** (PC-H3) — investigate the Drowned Lamp (M2 fragment; the dungeon).
- **"The Cove Caves"** (PC-H10) — clear the wreckers; find the Gravecaller current.
- **"Out to the Skerries"** (PC-H15) — brave the offshore Skerries (gated; the wreckers' worst and a drowned-guarded shrine).

## Treasure / Resources

Salt, fish, and sea-lore to trade; salvage and sea-relics off the wrecks and nodes (M3 hooks; moral choice); rare shore-herbs and shellfish (Survival DC 12, healing-kit/antitoxin components); the wreckers' plunder (at a cost); the old songs and the drowned's testimony (the Coast's true treasure; gated). The Skerries and the deep Lamp yield nothing safely.

## How It Changes After Interaction

Settling the drowned-cairns and sea-shrine dead (with the salt-and-tide rite) quiets a stretch of coast — a real, visible win the warming nodes slowly undo. Breaking the wreckers' hold on the coves reopens honest fishing and checks the sea-relic trade (easing the Monopoly clock locally). Investigating the Drowned Lamp delivers the coastal M2 fragment and, if disturbed, warms it further. Braving the Skerries with respect earns the worst drowned's testimony and the wreckers' secrets; trespassing recklessly strands or drowns the careless (telegraphed by the tide).

## Consequences If Ignored

The coastal nodes warm, the drowned-tide rises, the wreckers and the sea-relic trade flourish, the Gravecaller current strengthens, and the Drowned Lamp and the Skerries grow actively deadly — sealing the outer coast, choking the salt the whole frontier needs, and handing the Coast's maritime truth (and Bryd's old songs) to the cult and the Ledger. The Coast clock advances toward a continent-wide salt-and-dread squeeze.
