## AI Use

Load when the player travels the Tollwood's roads, camps, and deep wood. Holds the region's named wilderness sites and the path inward toward the Old Mast. The Tollwood wilderness is **survival-first and the most dangerous Ring 1 wild** — the road is a thin safe thread; off it, the wood is the antagonist. Use the Tollwood encounter table; the deep-wood sub-table is the region's deadliest. Guide (Brann) strongly advised past the near wood.

## One-Sentence Identity

A vast, ancient forest where the Concord's straight roads run through trees that ignore them, and the danger rises with every mile from the causeway until, at the heart, the wood is awake.

## What The Player Notices First

How *old* it is — oaks like towers, no undergrowth in the deep stands, just brown shade going back forever. How the Concord road, so confident in the fens, here runs cracked and mossed and *bends*, more than once, around stretches of wood it plainly chose not to cross. The offering-marks on the trees at every crossing. And, off the road, how fast you stop being able to tell which way you came.

## What Is Actually Going On (DM-Only)

The Tollwood holds a **pre-Concord power** in its deepest stand (the Old Mast) — older than the harvest, tied to the land and its oldest dead, neutral and slow. The Concord built its roads *around* it (the bends are deliberate) because it could not be mastered (echoing the Reach's Barrow). As the harvest reawakens far west, this deep presence **stirs and resents it**, and its old dead grow restless — which is why the Gravecallers shelter here (the deep dead *answer* and remember "the taking" — an oblique M5/M6 fragment-source) and why the Ledger's deep-cutting is dangerous (it breaks the forest-bargain and wakes what sleeps). **None of this is the Court or a new god; it is a gated regional landmark-power expressed via D&D 5e fey/plant/old-undead references.**

## Named Sites

### 1. The East Road & the Toll-Stations
The great Concord toll-causeway and its ruined stations (Tollstone Cross is the inhabited one; the Greenward station is the drowned dungeon). The road is the safe thread; the stations are landmarks, shelters, and — where drowned — leaking minor nodes (M2/M5 fragments).

### 2. The Coppice Shrines
Offering-stones at forest crossings, kept by the woodfolk — pre-Concord stones the Concord co-opted. Offering at one grants safe passage by custom (and, the DM may rule, eases the wood's hostility). Skipping or defiling one "marks" the traveler. The wood's rules made tangible.

### 3. The Woodcutter Camps
Temporary Ledger logging-camps, pushing east toward the Old Mast each season. Rough shelter, hard men, and the front line of the logging-vs-wood conflict. Camps that cut too deep come back short of men.

### 4. The Green Mile
A long stretch of East Road causeway sunk and drowned where a forest stream broke it — a washed-out, bog-flanked passage where carts mire and the dead drift (the road-node leakage from the Greenward station). A travel hazard and an M5 fragment-site.

### 5. The Hanging Oaks
A grove of ancient oaks where, the woodfolk say, the old wood "hung" offerings (and worse) since before the Concord — now where the Gravecaller cell meets and the old dead gather (the dungeon `THE_HANGING_OAKS.md`). Forbidden-knowledge and danger; the mid-wood's dark heart.

### 6. The Edge of the Old Mast
The boundary of the deepest, oldest stand — where the road bends hardest away, the offering-marks grow thick and old, and the dread begins (Wis DC 14, cumulative). Beyond lies the Old Mast itself (the gated dungeon). The woodfolk will guide a traveler *to* the edge and no further.

### 7. The Charcoal Burns (deep-wood camp belt)
- **One-sentence identity:** The smouldering belt of charcoal-clamps and slash where the Ledger's cutting meets the deep wood — the front line of the logging-vs-wood war.
- **What the player notices first:** A reek of smoke and sap, blue haze hanging in the trees, stripped ground and stacked cordwood, and hard, jumpy men who go quiet whenever the wood creaks past the cut.
- **What is actually going on (DM):** Each season the burns push east toward the Old Mast, breaking the forest-bargain a little more; crews that cut a marked tree come back short of men, and the roused dead drift toward the clamps at night. The engine of the "Old Mast Stirs" clock made visible.
- **Hazards:** clamp burns/smoke (Con DC 12 in the haze; burn 1d6 near a breached clamp); falling/rolling timber (Dex DC 13); getting lost off the cut-lines (Survival DC 14); deep-wood dread eastward (Wis DC 13, climbing).
- **Creatures/adversaries:** roused old forest dead at night (specter/ghost-like, AC 12–13, ~22–36 HP; harder to lay — Religion DC 15 or retreat); Ledger loggers/enforcers (Thug/Scout-like, AC 12–13, ~22–32 HP; human, telegraphed). See `TOLLWOOD_ENCOUNTERS.md`.
- **Level range / solo danger:** Lvl 3–5; **moderate** (rising at night/east). Retreat = back down the cut-lines to Hartfell.
- **Connected to:** Cutter-foreman Vask, Factor Hesk Dorr, Brann; quests "The Lost Crew" (Q_THE_LOST_CREW) and "The Marked Oaks" (Q_THE_MARKED_OAKS); clue C-TW-M5-1; the Old Mast Stirs clock. Stopping the deep-cutting quiets the burns.

### 8. The Withy Bog & the Drowned Causeway (off the Green Mile)
- **One-sentence identity:** A black alder-bog flanking the sunk stretch of the East Road, where carters who leave the causeway vanish and the road-node's leak pools.
- **What the player notices first:** Black still water under bare alders, the broken causeway running straight into it, will-o'-wisp glimmers after dusk, and a cart axle-deep and abandoned just off the firm road.
- **What is actually going on (DM):** The Greenward toll-station's node-leak drains here (an M2/M5 fragment-site, cousin to the Reach's Drowned Mile); the bog's lights lure the strayed, and a few road-dead drift in the shallows. A travel hazard and a quiet clue-site, not a dungeon.
- **Hazards:** bog-suck/mire (Survival DC 13 to keep the firm line; Athletics DC 13 to pull free; failure = mired/lost gear); will-o'-wisp lure (Wis DC 13); cold-wet night (Con DC 12).
- **Creatures/adversaries:** a will-o'-wisp-flavored light or two (AC 19, ~22 HP, but avoidable — it lures, not charges); drifting road-dead (specter-like, AC 12, ~22 HP; layable by rite DC 13). Telegraphed.
- **Level range / solo danger:** Lvl 2–4; **low-moderate** (the bog, not a fight). Retreat = back to the causeway.
- **Connected to:** the Greenward Toll-Station and "The Drowned Vault" (Q_THE_DROWNED_VAULT); clue C-TW-M2-1; the Green Mile. Settling the road-dead and marking the safe line eases the East Road here.

### 9. The Mast-Beasts' Range (deep-wood predator country)
- **One-sentence identity:** The deep-wood reaches where the Tollwood's real apex beasts den — the dire wolves, the bear, the great boar the road-folk fear as much as the dead.
- **What the player notices first:** Kill-sign and scrape-trees, a den-stink on the wind, deer fleeing the wrong way, and Brann going still and pointing out a track twice the size of a wolf's.
- **What is actually going on (DM):** As the wood grows uneasy (the cutting, the stirring), its beasts range nearer the road and the camps; this is the region's *natural* danger, distinct from the dead — best handled by woodcraft, avoidance, or honest hunting. Telegraphed by sign, never an ambush.
- **Hazards:** beast ambush if sign is ignored (Perception/Survival DC 13 to read it first); a treed climb to escape (Athletics DC 13); deep-wood cold (Con DC 13).
- **Creatures/adversaries:** dire wolves (pack of 2–4, CR 1 each), a forest bear (CR 2), a giant boar (CR 2); morale-driven, telegraphed by sign; the Tollwood's true predators. See `TOLLWOOD_ENCOUNTERS.md`.
- **Level range / solo danger:** Lvl 3–5; **moderate-high** if sign is ignored (a pack can overwhelm a solo PC — telegraphed, with retreat/avoidance built in).
- **Connected to:** Brann (reads the sign; the solo-safety guide), hook TW-H18 "Wolves on the Road"; the East Road's safety. Clearing or avoiding a den-pack reopens a stretch of road; reckless travel courts the one fight the wood will give freely.

### 10. The Sentinel Oaks (offering-road into the deep)
- **One-sentence identity:** A double row of ancient marked oaks lining a lost forest track toward the Old Mast, hung with generations of offerings — the woodfolk's holy approach and the last clear warning.
- **What the player notices first:** Two ranks of vast oaks, bark scarred with old marks, the branches heavy with bound tokens, bones, and ribbons gone grey with age — and the absolute, listening silence between them.
- **What is actually going on (DM):** The Sentinel Oaks are pre-Concord offering-trees marking the *honoured* way toward (never into) the Old Mast; keeping the offerings here is the bargain's oldest form. The deep presence's watchfulness begins along this row. The clearest place to *feel* the gate without crossing it — an oblique M5/M6 brush, hard-capped at "dread and fragment."
- **Hazards:** cumulative deep-wood dread (Wis DC 14); the "mark" for defacing an oak or taking an offering (Wis DC 13 + disadvantage on Stealth/Survival in the wood until atoned); getting lost leaving the row (Survival DC 16 without a guide).
- **Creatures/adversaries:** the deep presence's watchers if the row is defiled (dryad/awakened-tree/ancient-undead-flavored, AC 13–14, ~36–60 HP; they warn and turn back, not hunt; **handled by respect and retreat, not combat**). Gated.
- **Level range / solo danger:** Lvl 5–7; **high/gated.** This is the warning, not the apex. Retreat = back down the row to the marked edge.
- **Connected to:** Goodwife Sennet and Brann (who bring respectful travelers here and no further); quests "To the Edge and No Further" (Q_TO_THE_EDGE) and "The Bargain" (Q_THE_BARGAIN); the Old Mast (gated dungeon). Leaving an offering here earns a fragment and safe passage out; defiling it rouses the watchers — a telegraphed, survivable mistake.

## Hazards

- **Getting lost off-road:** Survival DC 14 (DC 16 deep wood) to hold direction each leg; failure strands or loops the traveler (and, deep, draws the dead). **Brann or a Coppice-Shrine offering greatly eases this.**
- **Canopy-dark:** deep stands are night-dark by day; disadvantage on sight-based Perception without light; light draws attention in the deep wood.
- **The Green Mile (drowned road):** mire/washout, Athletics/Survival DC 13–15; carts may be lost; the dead drift here (M5).
- **Bramble-ravines:** Athletics DC 13 to cross; falls 1d6.
- **Forest-cold night:** Con DC 12 (DC 13 deep) or 1 level exhaustion; no safe fire deep in.
- **Taboo-breaking:** defiling a Coppice Shrine or cutting a marked tree = Wis DC 13 dread and the wood "marks" the offender (disadvantage on Stealth/Survival in the wood until atoned at a shrine).
- **Old Mast dread:** Wis DC 14 cumulative near the deep stand; telegraphed; the wood wants the unworthy gone.

## Creatures / Adversaries

- **Old forest dead (mid-wood, Hanging Oaks)** — flavor **specter/ghost/will-o'-wisp-like, older and stranger** (AC 12–13, ~22–36 HP); harder to lay than Reach dead (true rite Religion DC 15; some resist laying entirely — offering and retreat matter more).
- **The deep presence's watchers (Old Mast edge)** — flavor **dryad/awakened-tree/fey/ancient-undead** (AC 13–14, ~36–60 HP); not mindless — they warn, test, and turn back intruders; **best handled by respect, offering, and retreat, not combat.** Gated.
- **East Road bandits (the Tollmen)** — **Bandit/Scout/Veteran-like** (AC 12–15); social-first; see Tollstone Cross.
- **Real predators** — dire wolves (pack), a forest bear, a giant boar (CR 1–3); telegraphed by sign; the Tollwood has the apex beasts the Reach lacks.
- **Ledger loggers/enforcers** — **Thug/Scout-like** (AC 12–13); human; telegraphed.

See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/TOLLWOOD_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Level Range And Solo Danger

Level 2–7; **moderate near the road, high in the deep wood, and gated-lethal at the Old Mast.** Retreat = get back to the road and follow it. Resting is safe only in settlements and at honored Coppice Shrines; never in the deep wood. **Telegraph the depth-danger constantly** — the offering-marks, the road's bends, the dread, the woodfolk's refusal to go further are all built-in warnings. The deep presence is never a low-level fight.

## NPCs Present

- **The road/camps:** Tollmen (Tollstone Cross), Ledger loggers, Brann the Guide, road-travelers.
- **The Coppice Shrines/Coldhearth wood:** woodfolk, Goodwife Sennet's people, the Mourners.
- **The Hanging Oaks:** the Gravecaller cell; Wren-of-the-Wood (drawn deeper than she should be).
- **The Old Mast edge:** the deep presence's watchers (gated); no living NPCs go further.

## Factions Present

Cinder Ledger (logging the camps, breaking the bargain), Gravecallers (Hanging Oaks cell; learning from the deep dead), Mourners' Circle (Coppice Shrines, Coldhearth), Reachward Compact (the thin road-watch), Ashen Wardens (rare; the deep dead resist them).

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- A pre-Concord power sleeps in the Old Mast; the roads bend around it (gated; M5/M6 oblique).
- The Coppice Shrines are pre-Concord; the Concord co-opted, not built them.
- The deep dead remember "the taking" (M5/M6 fragment; via the Gravecallers or Sennet's songs).
- The Ledger's deep-cutting wakes the presence (regional clock).

## Clues

- **C-TW-M5-1** — the old forest dead "drift the wrong way" and grow restless as the harvest wakes far off; even the deep wood feels it (Survival/Insight DC 13; oblique M5).
- **C-TW-M2-1** — the drowned road-stations (Greenward, the Green Mile) are leaking minor Concord nodes (M2 fragment; mirrors the Reach's Sunken Tollhouse).
- **C-TW-M6-1** (gated, Act 2–3; oblique) — a pre-Concord witness (the deep dead via the Gravecallers, or Sennet's songs) testifies that "the grey lords took the dead before the roads fell" — corroborates M6 from outside the Concord. **Deliver as fragment/dread; never the harvest's full truth, never the Court.**

## Hooks

- **"The Marked Oaks"** (TW-H1) — stop/expose the Ledger's deep-cutting (the master Tollwood quest).
- **"The Hanging Oaks"** (TW-H6) — confront or learn from the Gravecaller cell and the old dead (M6 oblique; the dungeon).
- **"To the Edge and No Further"** (TW-H7) — guide-led approach to the Old Mast edge; dread, fragments, and a hard line not to cross.

## Treasure / Resources

Charcoal, rare deep-wood herbs and fungi (Survival DC 13; potent healing-kit and antitoxin components), good timber and furs, the occasional Concord road-relic at the drowned stations (M2 fragment), and the wood's true treasures — the forest-rules, the old songs, and the deep dead's testimony (gated). The Old Mast itself yields nothing safely takeable.

## How It Changes After Interaction

Stopping the deep-cutting and honoring the bargain *quiets* the wood (a real, visible win the harvest's distant pull slowly undoes). Clearing or co-opting the Tollmen reopens the road. Exposing or aiding the Hanging Oaks cell shifts the Gravecaller thread (and the wood's restlessness) either way. Going to the Old Mast edge with respect earns a fragment and a warning; trespassing past it "marks" the player and rouses the watchers — a telegraphed, survivable mistake.

## Consequences If Ignored

The Ledger cuts deeper, the Old Mast stirs further (regional clock), the old dead spread toward the road, the Tollmen turn pirate, and the Gravecallers grow bold and reckless in the deep wood — sealing the deep Tollwood, endangering the East Road to Caradril, and handing the wood's oldest knowledge to the cult rather than the player.
