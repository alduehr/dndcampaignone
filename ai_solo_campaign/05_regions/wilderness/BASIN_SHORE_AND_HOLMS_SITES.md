## AI Use

Load when the player explores the Hollowmere basin's shore, the surfacing ruins, or the small holms (firm islands) ringing the drowned hollow. **The keystone shrine itself is DM-only and built as a dungeon in Stage 12 — this file covers the approaches and shore only, which the player can reach early (Hook 4).** Telegraph the deep basin as too dangerous yet. Level 1–5 at the shore; the under-shrine is far higher.

## One-Sentence Identity

The eerie shore of the drowned basin and the firm holms around it — where, when the fog thins, the broken grey shapes of something vast and old show beneath the water.

## What The Player Notices First

The basin is a wide hollow of dark, still water, fog clinging to it even in sun. Hollowmere rings its near shore. Out in the water, broken grey shapes break the surface — and on a clear day you can see they continue *down*, far down, far larger than any building should be. The shore is salt-marked, half-shunned; the Mourners leave offerings at the waterline; and the water is unnaturally calm, as if it is listening.

## What Is Actually Going On (DM-Only)

The basin drowns the **great Concord shrine — the harvest keystone** — and beneath it sleeps the **Hollow Court** (M2/M7; the campaign's center). The "broken grey shapes" are the shrine's upper structure, now slowly *surfacing* as the basin's level subtly drops (the reawakening, accelerated by Reke's drainage scheme). The shore is where the player can first see the shrine (M2 shape), find Concord Script (language-gated fragments), and witness the leakage at its source — but the deep shrine and the Court must stay gated until late arc. Reke walks the shore alone at night (C-M4-3).

## Named Sites

### 1. The Basin Shore (Hollowmere side)
The shunned waterline below the town. Offerings, salt, and the best view of the surfacing shapes. Where Old Sashe sometimes warns travelers, and where Reke can be tailed at night (M4 clue).

### 2. The Surfacing Ruin
A piece of the shrine's upper structure now breaking the surface near the shore — reachable by boat or, increasingly, on foot at low water. **Carries Concord Script (C-M2-1; language-gated).** Eerie, leaking, telegraphed as a place not to linger.

### 3. The Drainage Works
Where the Compact/Ledger drainage scheme is (or will be) cutting channels to lower the basin. Workers, Ledger overseers, and — secretly — Reke's true purpose (M4). A site that changes as the Basin clock advances.

### 4. The Offering Holm
A small firm island where the Mourners' Circle has tended a shore-shrine for generations, leaving the dead's tokens. A Mourner clue-site (folk-memory; M6 seed) and a safe-ish foothold.

### 5. The Reed Holms
A scatter of small inhabited firm islands in the basin's shallows — fisher-families, a few cottages each, reachable by punt. Low-stakes community scenes and the people most directly endangered by the drainage scheme.

### 6. The Lantern Shallows
Where the basin's leakage is densest near shore: the water glows faintly at night and drowned Remembrances drift just below the surface. A telegraphed danger-line: *do not swim out*.

## Hazards

- **Cold deep water:** the basin is deep and frigid; swimming out = Con DC 14 (cold) and risk of being drawn down at the Lantern Shallows. **Strongly telegraphed** as lethal — this is the "not yet" gate to the shrine.
- **Low-water mudflats:** at low water the shore exposes treacherous mud; Survival DC 13 or be mired.
- **Leakage (Lantern Shallows / Surfacing Ruin):** Wisdom save DC 13 near the leak or be "grave-touched" (as the Star-Stone effect); lingering invites a Remembrance.
- **Drainage works:** unstable cut-banks and machinery (Dex DC 12 hazard) once the works begin.

## Creatures / Adversaries

- **Basin Remembrances** — flavor **specter/drowned-ghost-like** (AC 12, ~22 HP), more numerous and more *pulled* than elsewhere (this is the source). Telegraphed; layable; dangerous in numbers if provoked.
- **Ledger overseers & guards** at the drainage works — **Guard/Thug-like** (AC 11–16, ~11–32 HP); social/legal first.
- **The deep shrine's defenders / the Hollow Court** — **far above Act 1–2 level; do not deploy at the shore.** The shore telegraphs their presence (cold, dread, the surfacing shapes) without combat.

See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md` (Basin table).

## Level Range And Solo Danger

Shore and holms: level 1–5, **low-to-moderate** (and a key M2/M4 clue site that can be visited *safely in daylight* — Hook 4 is designed for this). The **deep basin / under-shrine is endgame-tier and explicitly off-limits early** — telegraph drowning, cold, and dread to keep a level-1 player from suicidally swimming out. Retreat: the shore is minutes from Hollowmere. Safe rest in town; never on the Lantern Shallows.

## NPCs Present

- **Councilor Reke** (major) — walks the shore at night (C-M4-3).
- **Old Sashe** (major) — sometimes warns travelers here (Secret 20).
- **Ledger overseers** at the works; **Mourner-keepers** at the Offering Holm; Reed Holm fisher-families.

## Factions Present

Reachward Compact & Cinder Ledger (the drainage scheme), Mourners' Circle (shore-shrine; furious opposition), Hollow Court (DM-only; the sleeping center). Gravecallers watch the basin hungrily from the fen side.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The basin drowns the keystone shrine; the Hollow Court sleeps below (M2/M7; gated).
- The basin is *already* dropping; the drainage scheme accelerates the reawakening (M2/M4).
- Reke's night-visits reveal his true allegiance (C-M4-3).
- The Surfacing Ruin's Script is an M2 clue (language-gated).

## Clues

- **C-M2-1** — Concord Script at the Surfacing Ruin.
- **C-M4-3** — tail Reke to the shore at night.
- **C-M5 (source)** — the densest, most clearly basin-ward leakage in the Reach, witnessed at the Lantern Shallows.
- **Gate:** the under-shrine clues (C-M7-1, C-M9-4) are **Act 3+** — the shore only hints.

## Hooks

- **"The Councilor's Errand"** (Hook 4) — survey the surfacing ruins (designed safe-daylight M2 intro).
- **"The Reeve's Trust"** (Hook 5) — tail Reke; expose the scheme (M4).
- **"The Reed Holms"** — protect the basin-island families from the drainage land-grab (Ledger debt thread; Hook 10 link).
- **"What Lies Beneath"** — a forbidden, telegraphed-dangerous push toward the shrine (saved for later levels).

## Treasure / Resources

Minor Concord salvage at the Surfacing Ruin (Ledger will buy — but the Mourners will hate it); fishing rights at the Reed Holms; the M2/M4 clues (the real prize). The deep shrine's treasure is endgame.

## How It Changes After Interaction

If the player stops or slows the drainage scheme, the basin stays high and the shrine stays drowned (slows the master clock — a major win). If the scheme proceeds, the basin drops, more ruin surfaces, leakage spikes, and the shore grows openly dangerous. Exposing Reke at the shore changes the political map of Hollowmere.

## Consequences If Ignored

The Basin Drains clock advances on schedule: the shore floods with the dead, the Reed Holm families are displaced or drowned, the shrine surfaces, and the keystone becomes breachable — driving the master Harvest clock toward Stage 3. The basin is the campaign's heart; ignoring it is the worst-case default.
