## AI Use

Load when the player enters the Greyfens — the Reach's most dangerous wetland and the densest gathering of the drifting dead. This file holds the fen's named sites; use Old Sashe (major NPC) as the solo-safety guide and the encounter table for the open fen. The Greyfens lie southeast of Hollowmere, reached via Greywater Holm. **Higher solo danger; telegraph it.**

## One-Sentence Identity

A vast, fog-drowned peat-wetland where the ground lies, the dead drift the wrong way, and only a fool or a guided traveler walks far from the firm paths.

## What The Player Notices First

Fog that never fully lifts. Black water between hummocks of red-brown peat, dotted with drowned trees and the white gleam of old bone. Sound carries strangely — a voice from one direction, footsteps from another. Will-o'-wisp lights. And, more than anywhere else in the Reach, the pale, sorrowful shapes of Remembrances standing in the water, all of them facing the same way: toward the Hollowmere basin.

## What Is Actually Going On (DM-Only)

The Greyfens are where the drowned shrine's pull is strongest above ground. Leaked Remembrances gather here and **drift slowly toward the basin** (M5; Sashe's mapped drift, Secret 20) — the single clearest field-evidence of the reawakening harvest. The Gravecallers hide their main Reach cell here (around the Drowned Blind). The fog and sinking ground are natural hazards made worse by the leakage; the dead are not malevolent by default, but a frightened or aggressive traveler can turn a haunting into a fight.

## Named Sites

### 1. Sashe's Crossing
The marked firm-path Old Sashe maintains from Greywater Holm into the fen — the only reliably safe route. Lose Sashe or his markers and the fen becomes lethal.

### 2. The Drift-Line
A stretch where dozens of Remembrances stand in the water, all facing the basin. Witnessing it (with Sashe, or surviving alone) is **clue C-M5-1/C-M2-2** made visceral. Non-hostile unless provoked.

### 3. The Drowned Blind
A sunk fowler's blind, hidden, where the Tallow Man's main Gravecaller cell meets. A forbidden-knowledge door (M3/M6) and a danger.

### 4. The Bone Weir
A natural snag where the current piles drowned debris — and bodies, and grave-goods washed from upriver. Salvage and grim clues here; also where Gull's smugglers (from Saltmargin) collect contraband.

### 5. Mother Tongue Pool
A still, perfectly round pool the Gravecallers consider sacred; the Tallow Man channels the dead at its edge. Eerie, powerful, dangerous (the channeled Remembrance has its own agenda).

### 6. The Lantern Maze
A region of dense will-o'-wisp activity where countless travelers have been led to drown. Pure environmental hazard; a test of navigation and nerve.

## Hazards

- **Sinking ground / bog:** off the firm path, DC 13 Wisdom (Survival) to read safe footing each stretch; failure = mired (Str DC 13 to escape, half speed) or, on a hard fail, begin to sink (Athletics DC 15 or 1d6 stages toward drowning; an ally or rope ends it). **Telegraphed** by the ground's "give."
- **Killing fog:** without a guide or marked path, DC 14 Survival to hold direction; failure wastes hours and may loop the party to a hazard. Disadvantage on Perception beyond 30 ft.
- **Will-o'-wisp lures (Lantern Maze):** Wisdom save DC 13 to disbelieve a false light; failure draws the traveler toward deep water. Telegraphed (the lights "want" to be followed).
- **Cold exposure:** a night in the open fen without shelter = 1 level of exhaustion (Con DC 12 to avoid).

## Creatures / Adversaries

- **Drifting Remembrances** — flavor **specter/ghost/will-o'-wisp-like** (AC 12, ~17–22 HP). Non-hostile by default; drift, mourn, and ignore the respectful. Provoking, attacking, or grave-meddling makes them dangerous. A true rite (Religion DC 13) lays one peacefully.
- **Fen predators** — flavor **giant constrictor snake / giant frog / swarm-like** (CR 1/4–1). Real but mundane; telegraphed by tracks and disturbed water.
- **Gravecaller cell (Drowned Blind)** — the Tallow Man (T1, see MAJOR_NPCS), Cole Ashby (T1 lite), and 2–4 grief-mad recruits (**Cultist/Commoner-like**). Prefer to talk or flee; deadly only if cornered.

See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md` (Greyfens table).

## Level Range And Solo Danger

Level 2–5. **Moderate-to-high solo danger** — primarily from terrain and getting lost, not from monsters. **With Old Sashe as guide, danger drops to low** (this is the intended solo-safe approach and a core reason to befriend him). Retreat: follow Sashe's markers or the drift-line back toward firm ground. Resting in the open fen is dangerous (no safe long rest; exposure); Greywater Holm and Sashe's hut are the recovery points.

## NPCs Present

- **Old Sashe** (major) — fen-guide; the safe-passage lever and the M5/Secret 20 clue-source.
- **The Tallow Man, Cole Ashby, Sister Knell** (major) — Gravecallers, around the Drowned Blind / Mother Tongue Pool.

## Factions Present

Gravecallers (main cell), Ashen Wardens (won't enter past the drift-line if their clock advances), Mourners' Circle (loose ties via Sashe). The Hollow Court's pull is the invisible cause of everything here.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The drift toward the basin is the harvest reawakening (M5; the field-proof).
- The Drowned Blind hides the Gravecallers' truth (M3/M6).
- The Bone Weir collects upriver grave-goods (links to Kettle Bridge / Saltmargin smuggling).
- Mother Tongue Pool is where the Tallow Man's channeled Quietfall-witness speaks (M6/M9 path; dangerous).

## Clues

- **C-M5-1 / C-M2-2** — the drift-line, witnessed (via Sashe).
- **C-M3-2 / C-M5-3** — Gravecaller teaching at the Drowned Blind.
- **C-M6-3 / C-M9-3** (act-gated, Act 3) — the channeled witness at Mother Tongue Pool. **Do not surface the apex truth in Act 1–2; give fragments.**

## Hooks

- **"What the Fens Carry"** (Hook 6) — recover a lost peat-cutter with Sashe; witness the drift.
- **"A Knock in the Night"** (Hook 7) — the door to the Gravecallers leads here.
- **"The Bone Weir"** — recover stolen grave-goods or salvage for the Mourners or Gull.

## Treasure / Resources

Salvage and grave-goods at the Bone Weir (a low-value Remembrance relic possible — M3 hook); rare fen-herbs (Survival DC 13, useful for healing kits); the Gravecallers' forbidden knowledge (the real "treasure" of the fen).

## How It Changes After Interaction

If the player lays Remembrances or witnesses the drift, the fen's character shifts from menace to tragedy. If the Gravecaller cell is exposed to the Compact, it scatters deeper or relocates (and resents the player). If the drift-line is disrupted (rite-mass-laying), the harvest's leakage briefly eases — a small, visible win that the Hollow Court will counter.

## Consequences If Ignored

The drift thickens; Remembrances pile toward the basin; the Gravecaller cell matures toward "the Breaking"; and the Greyfens become impassable even to Sashe by mid-Act 2, sealing off the southeast and the clearest M5 evidence.
