## AI Use

Load for travel between Reach settlements — the Concord roads (raised causeways, the safe-ish routes) and the Mirewend River (the water artery). This file holds the named travel sites, road hazards, and shrine-stones the player passes. Use with `TRAVEL_PROTOCOL.md` and the regional encounter table. Level 1–4; the connective tissue of the Reach.

## One-Sentence Identity

The raised Concord causeways and the slow black Mirewend that knit the Reach together — the safest ways to travel, and themselves slowly going wrong.

## What The Player Notices First

The Concord roads are unmistakable: broad stone causeways lifted above the bog on ancient piers, dead straight, crumbling at the edges, marked at intervals by **shrine-stones** carved with the old star. The Mirewend runs peat-dark and slow beside and beneath them. Travel here feels safe compared to the open fen — until a shrine-stone is cracked, or a Remembrance stands on the road ahead, or the fog rolls up off the river.

## What Is Actually Going On (DM-Only)

The Concord roads were **harvest-conduits** — the network that carried the dead's Remembrance to the great shrines — and the shrine-stones are relay-markers. As the harvest reawakens, the roads and stones are "warming up": more Remembrances appear along them (drawn to the old conduits), and cracked stones leak. The river carries leakage downstream (linking Kettle Bridge → Reedford → toward the Pale Coast). The roads are still the safest travel, but they are the harvest's own arteries, and the player is literally walking them.

## Named Sites

### 1. The Star-Stones (shrine-stone chain)
The carved relay-markers along every Concord road. Intact ones still faintly "settle" passing dead; cracked ones (more each season) leak. A cracked Star-Stone is a recurring travel-encounter and a quiet M2/M5 clue (Concord Script; "the road remembers").

### 2. The Long Causeway
The main Hollowmere–Kettle Bridge road via Reedford: the Reach's spine. Mostly safe, patrolled loosely by the Compact and the Wardens; the default travel route.

### 3. The Pale Road
The western causeway to Saltmargin and the Pale Coast: salt-carts, smugglers, and a stretch where the causeway has half-collapsed (the **Broken Span**, a climbing/detour hazard).

### 4. The South Road
The causeway to Candlewick and the Vale: the gentlest road, firm ground, the safest in the Reach.

### 5. The Heights Road
The northern causeway to Harrowgast: it climbs out of the fens and degrades into a rough track; cold, exposed, scavenger-risk.

### 6. The Drowned Mile
A stretch of the Long Causeway where the road dips below the autumn flood and must be waded or boated — flood hazard, and a place the river-dead gather.

### 7. The Mirewend Landings
Boat-landings at Hollowmere, Kettle Bridge, and Reedford; river travel is faster but carries the river's leakage and the occasional drowned Remembrance.

## Hazards

- **Crumbling causeway:** at marked spots (Broken Span, Drowned Mile) DC 12 Dex/Athletics to cross safely; failure = a dunking in the bog (mire rules) or minor fall.
- **Autumn flood (Drowned Mile, Reedford ford):** rising water can close routes; Survival DC 13 to ford safely, or detour (costs time, advances clocks).
- **Road fog:** DC 12 Survival to keep pace in fog; failure adds travel time and a chance of an encounter.
- **Cracked Star-Stone leakage:** approaching a cracked stone, Wisdom save DC 12 or be "grave-touched" (disadvantage on next save vs. fear) until a short rest; a true rite (Religion DC 13) re-settles the stone.

## Creatures / Adversaries

- **Road / river Remembrances** — flavor **specter/ghost-like** (AC 12, ~17–22 HP), drawn to the conduits; telegraphed, usually avoidable or layable.
- **Toll-bandits & road-robbers** — flavor **bandit/scout-like** (CR 1/8–1/2), on the Pale and Heights roads where the watch is thin; prefer threats and robbery to murder.
- **Smugglers** — Gull's people on the Pale Road (social, not combat, by default).
- **Fen predators** straying onto the causeway at night (telegraphed).

## Level Range And Solo Danger

Level 1–4. **Low-to-moderate.** The roads are the intended safe travel layer (with the Ashwalk Rest as a mid-route sanctuary). Danger rises on the Pale and Heights roads (bandits, cold) and at flood-stretches. Retreat: the roads always lead to a known settlement or the Ashwalk Rest. Safe rest at any settlement or the Rest; risky in the open at the Drowned Mile or in flood.

## NPCs Present

Passing Wardens and Compact wardmen; Ledger salt-carts; Gull's smugglers (Pale Road); pilgrims and refugees; the occasional lone Mourner walking the dead home. Old Sashe never uses the roads (he prefers the fen) — a small character note.

## Factions Present

Reachward Compact (loose patrols), Ashen Wardens (walk the roads laying the dead), Cinder Ledger (cart traffic), Gravecallers (use the river quietly). The Hollow Court's conduits are the roads themselves (invisible).

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The roads and Star-Stones are the harvest network (M2; "the road remembers").
- Cracked stones leak and draw the dead — proof the system is waking (M5).
- The river carries leakage downstream, linking the river-towns' hauntings.

## Clues

- **C-M2-1-adjacent** — Concord Script on the Star-Stones (language-gated; fragments).
- **C-M5-2-adjacent** — more dead appear along the conduits each season (a pattern a watchful player or a Warden notices).

## Hooks

- **Travel complications** feed every cross-Reach journey (use the encounter table).
- **"The Cracked Stone"** — re-settle a leaking Star-Stone for a frightened village (Warden-trust; M5).
- **"The Broken Span"** — escort a Ledger cart past the collapsed Pale Road stretch (Saltmargin link).
- **Bandit / smuggler** encounters open into Saltmargin's Gull thread.

## Treasure / Resources

Minor salvage at cracked stones and the Broken Span; bandit loot; the roads themselves as a resource (fast, relatively safe travel — worth protecting). A fully-read Star-Stone chain is a slow M2 clue path.

## How It Changes After Interaction

Re-settling Star-Stones briefly calms a road stretch (a repeatable small win the Court counters). Clearing bandits reopens the Pale/Heights roads. Letting flood/leakage spread closes routes, forcing dangerous fen travel.

## Consequences If Ignored

As clocks advance, more Star-Stones crack, more dead walk the roads, floods close the Drowned Mile and Reedford ford, and bandits seize the thinned Pale/Heights roads — until cross-Reach travel itself becomes dangerous and the towns isolate. The safe travel layer erodes on a schedule.
