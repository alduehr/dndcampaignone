## AI Use

Load when the player travels north into the cold highland ruins above the fens. This file holds the Heights' named sites; Harrowgast is the staging town and the Deep Adit is the major dungeon here. **Higher level range (2–6) and genuinely dangerous — the "go north when ready" frontier.**

## One-Sentence Identity

A cold, bare-boned highland of broken Concord ruins and abandoned mines where the old order dug deepest — and where what it dug for still bleeds into the rock.

## What The Player Notices First

The fens fall away below and the world goes grey and hard: scree, heather, wind, and the great sundered ridges that gave the Reach its name — cliffs split as if by a single ancient blow. Concord ruins stand more *intact* here than anywhere, because the highland never drowned: roofless halls, snapped aqueducts, the black mouths of mine-adits. It is colder, clearer, and lonelier than the fens, and the silence has a weight.

## What Is Actually Going On (DM-Only)

The Concord mined the Heights for stone and for **deep Remembrance ("old glass")**, and the sundered ridges are scars from the original harvest-network's construction, not natural geology. A **secondary harvest node — the Deep Adit** (dungeon) — lies beneath the old delvings near Harrowgast, leaking into the rock. The **Concord Remnant** is racing here for relic-supply and to reach the Adit before rivals (the Reclamation clock made visible; M6/M7 foreshadow). The "thin" miners are early evidence of the thinning (M10 background). The Heights are the place to see that Hollowmere is not the *only* node — just the keystone.

## Named Sites

### 1. The Sundered Ridge
The signature landmark: a ridge cleft to its root. From its lip, on a clear day, you can see the whole Reach — and the Hollowmere basin gleaming at its heart. A place for revelation-scenes and orientation.

### 2. The Roofless Hall
The most intact Concord ruin in the Reach: a great hall open to the sky, walls dense with Concord Script. A prime **reader's site** (M2/M6 inscriptions, language-gated) and a haunt of highland Remembrances.

### 3. The Abandoned Adits (Upper)
The worked mine-tunnels above Harrowgast where salvage is dug. Scavenger territory, rockfall hazard, and the gateway down toward the Deep Adit.

### 4. The Whisper Scree
A slope where the leaked Remembrance in the rock makes the wind "talk." Unnerving; a Wisdom-save hazard; and a subtle clue that the rock itself holds the dead.

### 5. The Snapped Aqueduct
A broken Concord aqueduct spanning a gorge — a dangerous climbing route and a shortcut, with a Remembrance-haunted span.

### 6. The Cold Cairns
Highland archive-cairns (like Candlewick's stone, but exposed and looted). Concord Script, scavenger ambush risk, and the destination of Hook 8's variant.

## Hazards

- **Cold & altitude:** a day's exposure without proper gear = Con DC 13 or 1 level of exhaustion; worse at night. The Heights punish the unprepared (telegraphed at Harrowgast — buy gear).
- **Rockfall / unstable ruin:** DC 14 Perception to spot, DC 13 Dex save to avoid (2d6 bludgeoning) in the adits and ruins.
- **Climbing (aqueduct/scree):** DC 13–15 Athletics; failure = slip (fall damage scaled to height, telegraphed handholds).
- **Whisper Scree:** Wisdom save DC 13 each crossing or be unnerved (disadvantage on next Perception/Insight) by the talking wind.

## Creatures / Adversaries

- **Highland Remembrances** — flavor **specter/wraith-like** (AC 12–13, ~22–45 HP), colder and angrier than fen ghosts (the Heights dead are old Concord miners). Telegraphed; a true rite or avoidance works; combat is harder than in the lowlands.
- **Scavengers / claim-jumpers** — flavor **bandit/scout/thug-like** (CR 1/8–1), rival salvagers who'll rob a lone traveler.
- **Remnant salvage-crews** — **scouts + a hired bravo** (AC 13–14, ~16–30 HP) guarding dig-claims; prefer to warn off, not kill.
- **Heights predators** — flavor **giant eagle / wolf-pack-like** (CR 1–2); telegraphed.

See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md` (Heights table).

## Level Range And Solo Danger

Level 2–6. **Moderate-to-high.** Exposure, falls, and tougher dead make the Heights the most physically demanding wilderness in the Reach. **Telegraph hard:** Harrowgast folk and the Wardens warn that the Heights are no place for the green or the unsupplied. Retreat: back down to Harrowgast (a day or less from most sites). Resting: dangerous in the open (cold); Harrowgast is the only safe rest, with no magical healing — arrive prepared.

## NPCs Present

- **Reclaimer-agent Veska Dunn** (secondary — Remnant) and her salvage-crew, roving the ruins.
- **Ledger buyer Nessa Coyle's** scouts (from Harrowgast).
- Solitary scavengers; occasionally a lone Warden laying a Heights rite.

## Factions Present

Concord Remnant (the live presence — racing for the Adit), Cinder Ledger (salvage-buying), Ashen Wardens (rare, struggling with the harder dead). No Mourners, no Gravecallers up here.

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The Deep Adit is a secondary harvest node (dungeon; M6/M7).
- The sundered ridges are harvest-network scars, not natural (M6 background).
- The Roofless Hall and Cold Cairns hold readable harvest-truth fragments (M6/M9; language-gated).
- The Remnant is winning the race north if the player ignores it (Reclamation clock).

## Clues

- **C-M2-1 / C-M6-4-adjacent** — Concord Script at the Roofless Hall / Cold Cairns (language-gated; fragments in Act 1–2, fuller meaning Act 3).
- **C-M3-1 / C-M3-3** — "old glass" relics dug here (a relic in hand for study).
- **M10 background** — the "thin" miners as thinning-evidence (do not over-explain early).

## Hooks

- **"The Scholar's Request"** (Hook 8 Heights variant) — escort a reader (Briss or a Remnant scholar) to the Roofless Hall or Cold Cairns.
- **"Old Glass"** (Harrowgast) — recover a relic from the upper adits.
- **"The Crew That Won't Dig"** — into the Deep Adit.
- **"Race North"** — beat or sabotage the Remnant's push for the Adit.

## Treasure / Resources

"Old glass" Remembrance relics (M3; valuable and dangerous); Concord stone and metal salvage; the best climbing/mining gear (bought at Harrowgast); the orientation-revelation from the Sundered Ridge (narrative treasure).

## How It Changes After Interaction

Clearing scavengers or laying the Roofless Hall's dead opens safer reader-access. Reaching the Deep Adit first denies the Remnant its relic-supply (slows the Reclamation clock). Looting "old glass" recklessly spreads relics — and may leave the player "thin-touched" if careless (a custom risk; see MAGIC_RULES Remembrance handling).

## Consequences If Ignored

The Remnant reaches and exploits the Deep Adit, arming Quorrin with relics and pushing the Reclamation clock toward Act 3; the "thin" phenomenon spreads from Harrowgast; and the Heights' tougher dead, un-laid, begin descending toward the fens, worsening the whole Reach.
