## AI Use

Load when the player travels the Vale's orchard-country, pilgrim road, water-meadows, or southern downs. Holds the Vale's named wilderness sites and its three hamlets (Nettlecombe, Marrow Cross, Pellow Grange). The Vale's wilderness is **gentle and social** — the danger is slow leakage and human cruelty, not predators. Use the Vale encounter table for travel.

## One-Sentence Identity

A kind, hedged farm-country of orchards and water-meadows, dotted with Concord ruins the ploughs have politely ignored for a century — and where the dead are only now beginning to refuse the soil.

## What The Player Notices First

Order and plenty: red-gold orchard rows, drystone walls, sheep on the downs, a slow bright river. Then the odd note — a standing carved stone the orchard has grown around, a field-shrine used as a sheep-byre, fresh flowers on a grave nobody will discuss, a gibbet at a crossroads with a too-fresh occupant. The Vale is beautiful and it is hiding something, and a careful traveler feels both at once.

## What Is Actually Going On (DM-Only)

The harvest's reawakening leakage is arriving in the Vale from the north, **later and weaker than in the Reach because the Vale sits farther from the keystone** — direct corroboration that the failures spread outward (M5). The Concord's old field-shrines and the great shrine under Saint Veddow's are beginning to warm. The Vale copes by denial and reburial, which lets the leakage fester at the field-shrines (Nettlecombe), the gibbet (Marrow Cross), and the foreclosed granges (Pellow). The Ledger quietly quarries Remembrance relics from these ruins (M3). The Concord Remnant maps them. None of this is malevolent ground — it is sorrowful, slow, and very human in its concealment.

## Named Sites

### 1. Nettlecombe (orchard hamlet over a field-shrine)
A tiny orchard hamlet built around an old Concord field-shrine the locals call "the Sheep Stone" and use as a byre. The shrine is a minor harvest-node, leaking gently: the hamlet's dead are restless, and a sorrowful Remembrance walks the orchard at dusk. A soft, low-level haunting and an easy M3/M5 brush.

### 2. The Buried Cloister
A larger Concord ruin — a collapsed wayside cloister — sunk under a hillside orchard south of Orchardmere. Partly explorable (the dungeon `THE_BURIED_CLOISTER.md`); the Remnant's scholar-pilgrims have begun digging here. Concord Script and minor relics; the Vale's accessible "what did the Concord build" site, short of the great tomb.

### 3. The Ammet Water-Meadows
Lush flood-meadows along the river between Tilbrook and Orchardmere; mills, weirs, and pilgrim-ferries. Mostly a gentle travel zone — but the weirs are a drowning hazard, and a drowned pilgrim's Remembrance haunts one mill-pool (M5, gentle).

### 4. Marrow Cross (gibbet crossroads hamlet)
A bleak crossroads hamlet where the Vale hangs its criminals (and, by old custom, its grave-robbers). The gibbet-dead, denied proper rites, draw the worst leakage in the Vale — this is the **one genuinely dangerous wilderness site**, a wrathful-dead hotspot and the place a future Gravecaller cell recruits. Telegraphed: crows, the creak of chains, cold even in sun.

### 5. The Pellow Grange
A Ledger-foreclosed farm-estate over a field-shrine, now a quiet relic-quarry (the dungeon `THE_PELLOW_GRANGE.md`). Pell's men work it after dark. The clearest case of the Ledger's relic-greed in the Vale (M3) and a haunted, human-villainy site.

### 6. The Southern Downs
Open sheep-downs rolling away toward Caradril's hinterland; the South Road runs through them. Mostly safe travel — shepherds, drovers, the odd standing stone — and the player's gateway onward to the city. A few barrows on the downs are pre-Concord and best left alone.

### 7. The Cider-Wake Orchard (Orchardmere outskirts)
- **One-sentence identity:** An old cider-orchard where the Vale's harvest-festival "wake" for the year's dead is held — and where, this autumn, the wake will not settle.
- **What the player notices first:** Lantern-strung rows, the press-smell of crushed apples, trestle tables for the funeral-feast — and an uneasy hush where a wake should be loud, the older folk glancing at one row no one will walk down.
- **What is actually going on (DM):** A field-shrine at the orchard's heart is warming; the year's grave-wake (a Vale Mourners' rite) keeps failing in that row, and Tindle's people quietly move the feast each year to hide it. A gentle, public-facing M5 brush dressed as festival folk-horror.
- **Hazards:** drunken festival crush (Athletics DC 12); a press-cellar's foul air (Con DC 12); the cold "dead row" (Wis DC 12 unease, telegraphed).
- **Creatures/adversaries:** at most one sorrowful Remembrance walking the dead row (specter/ghost-like, AC 12, ~17 HP; layable by rite, Religion DC 13, or parley, Insight DC 12). Non-hostile.
- **Level range / solo danger:** Lvl 1–3; **low.** Retreat trivial (open, peopled orchard).
- **Connected to:** Mother Ezrith Combe and the Old Circle Mourners; Moot-Reeve Tindle's cover-up (AV-H2); clue C-AV-M5-1; hooks AV-H1/H2. Settling the wake earns Mourner goodwill; ignoring it lets the cover-up deepen.

### 8. The Drowned Lane (Ammet flood-road)
- **One-sentence identity:** A low stretch of the South Road causeway that floods each autumn, where a pilgrim-ferry once capsized and the drowned still wait at the ford.
- **What the player notices first:** The road dipping into standing water, a broken pilgrim-ferry's posts, votive ribbons tied to a leaning waystone, and carters arguing over whether to risk the lane or take the long way round.
- **What is actually going on (DM):** A small Concord culvert-shrine under the lane leaks gently; a drowned pilgrim-party's Remembrances linger at the ford, worse in flood-season (gentle M5; the grave-drift toward water mirrors the Reach).
- **Hazards:** flood-ford crossing (Athletics/Survival DC 13; failure = swept, recoverable); mired carts (Strength check or lost time); cold-water (Con DC 12).
- **Creatures/adversaries:** drowned pilgrim Remembrances (specter-like, AC 12, ~22 HP; rise in flood; layable by rite, Religion DC 13). Telegraphed by the ribbons and the cold.
- **Level range / solo danger:** Lvl 2–4; **low-moderate** in flood. Retreat = the long dry way round (a known travel choice).
- **Connected to:** Old Mam Tace and the Tilbrook Mourners; clue C-AV-M5-1; the Ammet water-meadows; hooks AV-H11. Settling the ford reopens the short road and quiets a patch.

### 9. The Beacon Tump (downs hill-fort)
- **One-sentence identity:** A turf-covered pre-Concord hill-fort on the southern downs that the shepherds use as a fold and the Remnant covets as the oldest thing in the Vale.
- **What the player notices first:** A green ringed hill crowned with weathered stones, sheep grazing its banks, a shepherd's bothy in the ditch — and a Remnant survey-stake driven into the turf that the shepherds keep pulling out.
- **What is actually going on (DM):** The tump is older than the Concord (like the Reach's Barrow); the Concord built *near*, not on it. It is not a node and holds no harvest-secret — but the Remnant (Oneth Vael's people) wrongly hopes it does, and digging it would only anger the downs-folk. A fair red-herring that teaches "older things predate the Concord" (supports M2 by contrast; not M6).
- **Hazards:** collapsing ditch/rampart (Dex DC 12, fall 1d6); a territorial ram (CR 1/4); getting turned around on the open downs at dusk (Survival DC 12).
- **Creatures/adversaries:** none undead (the tump is *quiet* — a deliberate contrast to the warming Concord sites); mundane downs threats only (feral dogs, a ram, a drover's quarrel; CR up to 1).
- **Level range / solo danger:** Lvl 1–3; **low.** Open country; easy retreat.
- **Connected to:** Sub-Lector Oneth Vael (Remnant overreach) and the downs shepherds; the South Road to Caradril; a fair false-lead that disproves cleanly (the tump is empty of nodes). Aiding the dig sours the shepherds; defending the tump earns downs-folk goodwill.

### 10. Saint Veddow's Spring (the holy well)
- **One-sentence identity:** The famed healing spring below Saint Veddow's hill, where pilgrims drink and wash their dead's keepsakes — and where the water has begun to taste of cold iron.
- **What the player notices first:** A walled well-court thick with pilgrims, crutches and votive tokens hung on the rails, a relic-seller's stall — and, lately, pilgrims murmuring that the holy water has turned cold and metallic and that the cures have stopped.
- **What is actually going on (DM):** The spring rises through the capped Concord works beneath Saint Veddow's; as that secondary node warms, the water carries a faint thin-touch taint (the "cold iron" taste). The shrine-keepers (Mother Sennet) hush it to protect the pilgrimage; relic-mongers still sell "blessed" spring-tokens that are mildly tainted. An accessible outer-works brush with the Tomb's secret (M2/M3, gated; never the node's true purpose).
- **Hazards:** thin-touch from drinking deeply / handling tainted tokens (Con DC 13, reversible — disadvantage on Death saves until a true rite/shrine rest); pilgrim-crush at the rails (Athletics DC 12).
- **Creatures/adversaries:** none directly; a relic-monger (Coyle Tamm) and pilgrims; the danger is the taint, not a foe.
- **Level range / solo danger:** Lvl 2–4; **low** (the taint is reversible and telegraphed by the taste).
- **Connected to:** Mother Sennet and the Saint Veddow's shrine-keepers; Relic-monger Coyle Tamm (M3 tokens); Saint Veddow's Tomb (the inner works); clues C-AV-M2-1/M3-1; hooks AV-H8/H9. Investigating the spring's taint is a low-risk thread into the Tomb mystery.

## Hazards

- **Weir / mill-race drowning (water-meadows):** crossing a flood-weir, DC 13 Athletics; failure = swept (1d6 + risk of going under, Athletics DC 13 or an ally/rope to recover). Telegraphed by the roar of water.
- **Collapsing orchard ruin (Nettlecombe, Buried Cloister):** DC 13 Perception to spot rotten timber/undercut floor; DC 13 Dex save or fall (1d6, climb out Athletics DC 10).
- **Gibbet-cold / wrathful dead (Marrow Cross):** the leakage here imposes a Wis DC 13 (fear/chill) on the disrespectful; a wrathful Remembrance is a real threat (see below).
- **Cider-cellar / pit foul air (granges):** Con DC 12 or minor incapacitation; trivial but telegraphed (guttering flame).
- **Thin-touch (deeper ruins / relic-quarries):** Con DC 14 near raw Remembrance relics; reversible (disadvantage on Death saves until a true rite / shrine rest).

## Creatures / Adversaries

- **Sorrowful Remembrances (Nettlecombe, water-meadows, granges)** — flavor **specter/ghost-like** (AC 12–13, ~17–30 HP); non-hostile, grieving; layable by a true rite (Religion DC 13) or parley (Insight DC 12).
- **Wrathful gibbet-dead (Marrow Cross)** — flavor **wraith-like** (AC 13, ~36 HP); telegraphed; angry at being denied rites; can be calmed by giving the denied dead a true rite (Religion DC 15) rather than fought.
- **Ledger toughs (Pellow Grange, foreclosures)** — **Thug-like** (AC 11, ~32 HP), 2–4; human, telegraphed, prefer intimidation, break on morale.
- **Mundane country threats** — a bad-tempered bull, feral dogs, a drover's quarrel (CR 1/8–1); rare and minor.

See `../../13_encounters_and_bestiary/ASHGARDEN_VALE_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Level Range And Solo Danger

Level 1–5; **low solo danger** across most of the Vale, **moderate** at Marrow Cross and inside the grange/cloister ruins. Retreat is always easy — the Vale is open, settled country with frequent roads and farms. Resting is safe except at Marrow Cross and inside the ruins. Telegraph the gibbet site clearly; it is the one place that can hurt a careless low-level PC.

## NPCs Present

- **Nettlecombe:** Goodman Orrick (hamlet elder; minor), the dusk-walking Remembrance.
- **Marrow Cross:** Gibbet-keeper Sorle (minor; grim, lonely), and the future Gravecaller recruiter who haunts the crossroads.
- **Pellow Grange:** Pell's foreman Drust (minor; Ledger), the grange's bound dead.
- **The Downs:** drovers and shepherds; a Remnant survey-pair near the Buried Cloister (Oneth Vael's people).

## Factions Present

Cinder Ledger (relic-quarrying the granges and field-shrines), Concord Remnant (mapping/digging the Buried Cloister), Mourners' Circle (tending the country graves), Ashen Wardens (pass through from Saint Veddow's), Gravecallers (recruiting at Marrow Cross — the leading edge).

## Secrets (DM-Only)

- The field-shrines and the great tomb are warming as the keystone reawakens (M2/M5).
- The Vale leaks *later and weaker than the Reach* — geographic proof the failures radiate from the keystone (M5).
- The Ledger quarries Remembrance relics from foreclosed shrine-land (M3).
- Marrow Cross's denied dead are the Vale's seed of both wrathful hauntings and the Gravecaller cell.

## Clues

- **C-AV-M5-1** — the Vale leaks later/weaker than the Reach (compare with the player's Reach experience; Survival/Insight DC 12, or simply observed). Corroborates M5.
- **C-AV-M3-1** — a Remembrance relic quarried from a field-shrine (Nettlecombe/Pellow) stores a dead person (Arcana/Religion DC 13, or experiential). M3.
- **C-AV-M2-1** (Buried Cloister / Saint Veddow's outer works) — Concord Script and shrine-architecture proving the Vale held a real Concord installation (Script-gated; M2/M6 fragment, Act 2–3 paced).

## Hooks

- **"The Sheep Stone"** (AV-H15) — settle Nettlecombe's dusk-walker (gentle M3/M5).
- **"What's Under the Orchard"** (AV-H16) — explore the Buried Cloister with or against the Remnant.
- **"The Crows of Marrow Cross"** (AV-H17) — lay the gibbet's wrathful dead (the Vale's toughest fen-style job) and find who's recruiting there.

## Treasure / Resources

Minor Concord salvage and the occasional Remembrance relic (M3 hooks) at the ruins; good Vale cider, wool, and grain to trade; rare orchard-herbs (Survival DC 12, healing-kit components); the Remnant pays for Script-rubbings from the Buried Cloister.

## How It Changes After Interaction

Settling Nettlecombe's or the water-meadow's dead earns Mourners' goodwill and quiets that patch (a small, visible win the harvest slowly undoes). Exposing or stopping Pell's grange-quarrying checks the Ledger's Vale relic-supply (eases the Monopoly clock locally). Laying Marrow Cross's wrathful dead — and giving the denied criminals true rites — closes the Gravecaller recruiting window there. Digging the Buried Cloister with the Remnant advances their Vale interest (and their Reclamation clock) whether the player means it to or not.

## Consequences If Ignored

The field-shrines and granges leak unchecked; the Ledger quietly corners the Vale's relic supply; Marrow Cross becomes a wrathful-dead danger and a Gravecaller seedbed; and the Remnant works the Buried Cloister and presses harder for Saint Veddow's inner tomb. The Vale's slow corruption accelerates on its regional clock.
