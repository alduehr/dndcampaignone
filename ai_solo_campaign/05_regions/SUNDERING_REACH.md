The starting region. Load for any travel, geography, or "what's out there" question in Acts 1–2. Stage 1 establishes its identity and frame; Stage 3 fills it with settlements, wilderness sites, dungeons, and the regional quest/encounter/rumor tables.

## One-Sentence Identity

A cold, ruin-haunted frontier of fens and broken highlands where the dead no longer rest — and where the worst of it is centered on a drowned shrine the locals barely understand.

## Player-Facing Overview

The Sundering Reach is Orrun's northwestern frontier: peat moors, slow black rivers, fog, and the bones of the old Custodian Concord everywhere underfoot. Settlers came after the Quietfall to farm the firmer ground, cut peat, and pick over the ruins. Life is hard, neighborly, and superstitious. For decades the Reach has suffered the failing of death-rites worse than anywhere else — and in the last year it has become a crisis. People bury their dead with salt and a star at the door and pray the rites hold. Often, now, they don't.

The region is governed loosely by the **Reachward Compact** from the town of **Hollowmere**, which sits on the rim of a wide, flooded hollow over a great drowned ruin.

## DM-Only Truth

The Reach is the **keystone of the Concord's harvest.** The drowned shrine beneath the Hollowmere basin is the central node of the machine that consumed the dead — and the dormant **Hollow Court** sleeps in its deepest under-shrine. The Reach's exceptional rate of failed rites is **leakage from the reawakening harvest.** Everything in the campaign radiates from this region; the player begins, by design and by the Court's quiet steering, almost on top of the answer. See `../03_canon/DM_ONLY_CANON.md`.

## Geography

- **Terrain:** peat fens, moorland, slow rivers, scattered firm "holms" (islands of dry ground) where villages sit; broken highlands (the Sunder Heights) to the north.
- **Climate:** cold, wet, foggy; brutal winters; campaign begins in late autumn (Greyfall).
- **Boundaries:** Sunder Heights (N), Pale Coast (W), Ashgarden Vale (S), Tollwood (E).
- **Defining features:** the **Hollowmere basin** and its drowned shrine; the **Mirewend River**; the fog-bound **Greyfens**; raised **Concord roads**; scattered **shrine-stones** and **archive cairns**.

## Wilderness Zones

- **The Greyfens** — the most dangerous wetland; fog, sinking ground, and the densest gathering of Remembrances. Old Sashe guides travelers through.
- **The Sunder Heights** — cold highland Concord ruins and abandoned mines to the north.
- **The Mirewend banks** — flood-prone, dotted with shrine-stones.
- **The Concord roads** — raised causeways; safest travel, but haunted and crumbling.

## Regional Conflicts

- The **basin-drainage scheme** (Compact + Ledger push; Mourners' Circle fury). *The master tension of Act 1–2.*
- The **failing rites** overwhelming the Wardens and Circle.
- The **outlawed Gravecallers** spreading as the desperate seek answers.
- **Relic-buying** by the Ledger inflaming grave-reverence.

## Regional Secrets (DM-Only)

- The drowned shrine is the harvest's keystone; the Hollow Court sleeps below it.
- The Wardens unknowingly guard the seal.
- The Mourners' old songs encode the harvest's truth.
- The player was steered here.

## Recurring Threats

- **Remembrances** (wrong-come-back dead) — leakage from the shrine; flavor as ghosts/specters/revenants. Telegraphed, often resolvable without combat (a true rite).
- Fen hazards (drowning ground, fog, exposure, fen predators).
- Gravecaller radicals; Ledger enforcers; Compact watch.

## Encounter Themes

Eerie folk-horror: restless dead, fog and getting lost, frightened communities, forbidden knowledge, frontier law vs. mob, ruins that remember. Combat is real but never assumed; stealth, ritual, negotiation, and investigation are first-class.

## What Changes If Ignored

If the player ignores the region's troubles, the **master clock advances** (`../02_runtime_state/WORLD_CLOCKS.md`): the basin drains, the shrine surfaces, the harvest restarts, the Wardens break, the Circle turns militant, and the Gravecallers move on the shrine. The Reach gets darker and more dangerous on a schedule — the world does not wait.
