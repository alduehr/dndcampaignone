## AI Use

The starting region. Load for any travel, geography, or "what's out there" question in Acts 1–2. Stage 1 establishes its identity and frame; Stage 3 fills it with settlements, wilderness sites, dungeons, and the regional quest/encounter/rumor tables.

## One-Sentence Identity

A cold, ruin-haunted frontier of fens and broken highlands where the dead no longer rest — and where the worst of it is centered on a drowned shrine the locals barely understand.

## Player-Facing Overview

The Sundering Reach is Orrun's northwestern frontier: peat moors, slow black rivers, fog, and the bones of the old Custodian Concord everywhere underfoot. Settlers came after the Quietfall to farm the firmer ground, cut peat, and pick over the ruins. Life is hard, neighborly, and superstitious. For decades the Reach has suffered the failing of death-rites worse than anywhere else — and in the last year it has become a crisis. People bury their dead with salt and a star at the door and pray the rites hold. Often, now, they don't.

The region is governed loosely by the **Reachward Compact** from the town of **Hollowmere**, which sits on the rim of a wide, flooded hollow over a great drowned ruin.

## DM-Only Truth

The Reach is the **keystone of the Concord's harvest.** The drowned shrine beneath the Hollowmere basin is the central node of the machine that consumed the dead — and the dormant **Hollow Court** sleeps in its deepest under-shrine. The Reach's exceptional rate of failed rites is **leakage from the reawakening harvest.** Everything in the campaign radiates from this region; the player begins, by design and by the Court's quiet steering, almost on top of the answer. See `../03_canon/DM_ONLY_CANON.md`.

## Geography

- **Terrain:** peat fens, moorland, slow rivers, scattered firm "holms" (islands of dry ground) where villages sit; broken highlands (the Sunder Heights) to the north.
- **Climate:** cold, wet, foggy; brutal winters; campaign begins in late autumn (Greyfall).
- **Boundaries:** Sunder Heights (N), Pale Coast (W), Ashgarden Vale (S), Tollwood (E).
- **Defining features:** the **Hollowmere basin** and its drowned shrine; the **Mirewend River**; the fog-bound **Greyfens**; raised **Concord roads**; scattered **shrine-stones** and **archive cairns**.

## Major Settlements

- **Hollowmere** — the de facto capital; the starting settlement; rim of the drowned basin. (`../06_settlements/HOLLOWMERE.md`)
- **Kettle Bridge** — the second-largest town; the Mirewend crossing and toll-town to the east. (`../06_settlements/KETTLE_BRIDGE.md`)
- **Saltmargin** — the western salt-trade gateway town toward the Pale Coast. (`../06_settlements/SALTMARGIN.md`)

## Minor Settlements

- **Candlewick** — prim candle-and-farm village on the firm southern ground; a Concord archive-stone and a Script gate. (`../06_settlements/CANDLEWICK.md`)
- **Greywater Holm** — shrinking Greyfens-edge fen-village where rites fail worst and the Gravecallers spread. (`../06_settlements/GREYWATER_HOLM.md`)
- **Harrowgast** — half-ruined Concord mining town in the Sunder Heights; rougher, higher-level (2–5). (`../06_settlements/HARROWGAST.md`)
- **Reedford** — tiny reed-cutting ford-hamlet on the central road; a gentle level-1 waypoint. (`../06_settlements/REEDFORD.md`)
- **The Ashwalk Rest** — fortified Ashen Warden waystation at the central crossroads; the open Reach's main solo-safety sanctuary. (`../06_settlements/THE_ASHWALK_REST.md`)

## Wilderness Zones

Four zone files hold ~25 named sites (`../05_regions/wilderness/`):
- **The Greyfens** (`GREYFENS_SITES.md`) — the most dangerous wetland; Sashe's Crossing, the Drift-Line (M5 field-proof), the Drowned Blind (Gravecaller cell), the Bone Weir, Mother Tongue Pool, the Lantern Maze. Lvl 2–5; low with Sashe.
- **The Sunder Heights** (`SUNDER_HEIGHTS_SITES.md`) — cold highland Concord ruins; the Sundered Ridge, the Roofless Hall, the abandoned adits, the Whisper Scree, the Cold Cairns. Lvl 2–6.
- **The Mirewend River & Concord Roads** (`MIREWEND_AND_ROADS_SITES.md`) — the Star-Stones (relay-markers), the Long/Pale/South/Heights causeways, the Drowned Mile, the river landings. Lvl 1–4; the travel layer.
- **The Basin Shore & Outer Holms** (`BASIN_SHORE_AND_HOLMS_SITES.md`) — the shore, the Surfacing Ruin (M2), the Drainage Works (M4), the Offering Holm, the Reed Holms, the Lantern Shallows. Lvl 1–5; **the deep basin/under-shrine is endgame-tier and off-limits early.**

## Regional Conflicts

- The **basin-drainage scheme** (Compact + Ledger push; Mourners' Circle fury). *The master tension of Act 1–2.*
- The **failing rites** overwhelming the Wardens and Circle.
- The **outlawed Gravecallers** spreading as the desperate seek answers.
- **Relic-buying** by the Ledger inflaming grave-reverence.

## Regional Secrets (DM-Only)

- The drowned shrine is the harvest's keystone; the Hollow Court sleeps below it.
- The Wardens unknowingly guard the seal.
- The Mourners' old songs encode the harvest's truth.
- The player was steered here.

## Dungeons And Ruins

Six built sites (`../10_dungeons_and_ruins/`); the keystone drowned shrine itself is built in Stage 12.
- **The Peat Chapel** (Lvl 1–2) — a half-sunk fen wayside chapel; intended **first delve**; a Remembrance relic (M3). (`THE_PEAT_CHAPEL.md`)
- **The Sunken Tollhouse** (Lvl 2–3) — a drowned Concord node under Kettle Bridge's broken arch; M2/M5 clue site. (`THE_SUNKEN_TOLLHOUSE.md`)
- **The Whispering Cairn** (Lvl 1–3) — a roadside archive-cairn; the Concord Script gate; M6/M9 seed. (`THE_WHISPERING_CAIRN.md`)
- **The Ledger Vault** (Lvl 2–4) — a social/stealth **heist** for the vault relic (C-M3-3); turn-Domic path. (`THE_LEDGER_VAULT.md`)
- **The Barrow of Nine Doors** (Lvl 3–5) — a pre-Concord barrow in the Greyfens; a parley-boss and deep M5/M6 testimony. (`THE_BARROW_OF_NINE_DOORS.md`)
- **The Deep Adit** (Lvl 3–5) — a secondary harvest node under Harrowgast; M3/M6 + the Reclamation race. (`THE_DEEP_ADIT.md`)

## Recurring Threats

- **Remembrances** (wrong-come-back dead) — leakage from the shrine; flavor as ghosts/specters/revenants/will-o'-wisps. Telegraphed, often resolvable without combat (a true rite). Worse nearer the basin (M5).
- Fen and Heights hazards (drowning ground, fog, will-o'-wisps, cold/exposure, rockfall, predators).
- Gravecaller radicals (Cole Ashby); Ledger enforcers (Tallytooth Ren); Compact watch; Remnant salvage-crews (Veska Dunn); road bandits/smugglers (Gull).
- The custom **thin-touch** risk near raw Remembrance relics (Deep Adit; reversible). See `SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md`.

## Encounter Themes

Eerie folk-horror: restless dead, fog and getting lost, frightened communities, forbidden knowledge, frontier law vs. mob, ruins that remember. Combat is real but never assumed; stealth, ritual, negotiation, and investigation are first-class.

## Level Range And Solo Danger

- **Region level range:** 1–6 (Acts 1–2). The Reach is built so the player can begin at level 1 near Hollowmere and work outward as they grow.
- **Low-danger core (Lvl 1–3):** Hollowmere, Reedford, Candlewick, the central/south Concord roads, the Ashwalk Rest (sanctuary). Gentle on-ramps; failures are telegraphed; non-combat resolutions abound.
- **Moderate (Lvl 2–4):** Kettle Bridge & the river, Saltmargin & the Pale Road, the basin shore (daylight), Greywater Holm.
- **Higher (Lvl 3–6):** the Greyfens deep (the Barrow), the Sunder Heights & Harrowgast (cold, falls, tougher dead, the Deep Adit), and the Reed Holms/basin politics.
- **Off-limits early (endgame-tier):** the deep basin / drowned shrine / Hollow Court — **explicitly telegraphed as lethal** (drowning, cold, dread) to keep a low-level PC from suiciding into it. Built in Stage 12.
- **Solo safety:** Old Sashe (Greyfens guide), the Ashen Wardens/Hale (the dead), the Ashwalk Rest (sanctuary + minor healing), Candlewick's chapel (the south's healing) are the region's solo-safety pillars.

## Encounter And Hazard Mechanics

- **Encounter tables (stat-referenced, solo-tuned):** `../13_encounters_and_bestiary/SUNDERING_REACH_ENCOUNTERS.md` — four zone tables (Roads/River, Greyfens, Heights, Basin), all D&D 5e/2024-compatible references, non-combat-default.
- **Dead:** Remembrances are specter/ghost/wraith/will-o'-wisp-like (AC 12–14, ~17–60 HP by tier); usually layable by a true rite (Religion DC 13–15) or parley (Insight/Persuasion DC 12–13).
- **Hazards/DCs:** sinking ground (Survival DC 13 / Athletics DC 15), killing fog (Survival DC 14), will-o'-wisp lures (Wis DC 13), cold exposure (Con DC 12–13), rockfall (Perception DC 14 / Dex DC 13), crumbling causeway (Dex/Athletics DC 12–15), cracked Star-Stone / leakage grave-touched aura (Wis DC 12–13), and the reversible **thin-touch** (Con DC 14 near raw relics).
- **Travel checks** per `../01_runner_protocol/TRAVEL_PROTOCOL.md`; see the Roads & River wilderness file for route-specific DCs.
- **Resting:** safe long rests at any settlement and the Ashwalk Rest; dangerous in the open fen/Heights; minor magical healing at the Ashwalk Rest and Candlewick's chapel only.
- **Scaling:** each table and dungeon includes Lvl 1–2 / 3–4 / 5–6 scaling; recurring named foes never die cheaply.

## What Changes If Ignored

If the player ignores the region's troubles, the **master clock advances** (`../02_runtime_state/WORLD_CLOCKS.md`): the basin drains, the shrine surfaces, the harvest restarts, the Wardens break, the Circle turns militant, and the Gravecallers move on the shrine. The Reach gets darker and more dangerous on a schedule — the world does not wait.
